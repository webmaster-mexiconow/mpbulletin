NAFTA talks put on hold until May 7 for consultations

Negotiations on a new NAFTA will stall until May 7, allowing time for consultations with the auto industry in Mexico, and for U.S. Trade Representative Robert Lighthizer to visit China, Reuters reported. According to auto industry officials, the U.S. currently demands 75 percent of NAFTA content in vehicles built in North America to qualify for duty-free status, with production of certain components specified in areas that pay higher wages. The Mexican team will use the timeout for consultations with auto industry executives on the proposals. More details here: bit.ly/2HGp8qf
Stricter rules of origin could hurt US auto sales, exports: study

A new study by the Michigan-based Center for Automotive Research (CAR) suggests that adjustments proposed by the U.S. team that renegotiates NAFTA will impact auto sales in the U.S. market as well as auto exports by assembly plants that operate in U.S. soil. By increasing minimum percentages on rules of origin, some U.S. automotive and parts manufacturing companies would prefer to move their plants to lower-cost regions outside North America. Just in 2017, 31 countries each imported more than US$ 100 million in automotive parts to the United States. CAR also estimates the current U.S. proposal could result in Most Favored billion tax on U.S. consumers. More details here: bit.ly/2Kp6957 Nation tariffs that add at minimum a US$ 2.1 to 3.8
Chinese automaker BAIC readies to reveal location of US$ 2 billion plant in Mexico

Officials of Chinese state-owned automaker Beijing Automotive Industry Holding Co., commonly known as BAIC, prepare the announcement of an investment of at least US$ 2 billion for the construction of an assembly plant in Mexico. It would be tentatively in May when the company announces where it will be installed, reported Expansión, citing anonymous sources within the company. Currently the company makes the final assembly of its models in Veracruz, where the vehicles arrive from China in the form of CKD kits and then are put together at a facility owned by Mexican company AT Motors, which also assembles commercial vehicles for other Chinese brands like Foton. More details here: bit.ly/2r3dRcj
Ford likely to build SUVs in Sonora, says state official

After Ford Motor Co. announced that it will cease manufacturing of sedan cars for the U.S. market, Jorge Vidal Ahumada, Sonora's secretary of Economy, said the company's officials informed him that in the coming months, it will be decided which vehicle will be manufactured at the Hermosillo plant, where the Fusion and Lincoln MKZ are currently produced. Vidal affirmed that it will be in two or three months when they will reveal which new project will be developed in the capital of Sonora, but it is possible that it will be an SUV or a crossover. This version coincides with those of local media which affirm that the facility is already being adapted for the production of a compact SUV. More details here: bit.ly/2KoNbvJ
Electric car company Zacua opens assembly facility in Puebla

After a series of delays caused by the earthquake last September, the Mexican electric car brand, Zacua, inaugurated in Puebla the facility where it will assemble the M2 and M3 models that will be launched in the coming weeks. Zacua is an initiative from Motores Limpios SAPI de CV, a company that commissioned Dynamik Technological Alliance, of Spain, and Automobiles Chatenet, a France-based auto designer and manufacturer, the task of developing an electric car for the Mexican market. Zacua invested a little more than US$ 4 million in the facility located at Parque Industrial de Puebla 2000, where it will receive the vehicles in the form of CKD kits (Complete Knock-down) for its final assembly. More details here: bit.ly/2Kp7h8R
Heavy truck production in Mexico up 20% in February; exports hike 43%

Manufacturers of commercial vehicles produced 12,125 units in Mexico during the second month of the year, that figure represents an increase of 20.8% when compared to the 10,038 units manufactured in February 2017, according to data from the National Association of Bus, Trucks and Tractor Trucks Producers, (Anpact). Total production in the first two months of 2018 saw an increase of 15 percent, with 8,120 trucks and 12,693 truck tractors, Anpact said. As for exports, these totaled 10,798 units in February, a 42.8 percent hike compared to 7,500 units in the same month of 2017. Total exports in the first half of the year saw an increase of 29.5 percent, totaling 18,887 units.
Bosch tech to reduce emissions of nitrogen oxides to one-tenth of 2020 limits

German manufacturer Bosch announced that a series of tools it has developed for diesel engines could enable vehicle manufacturers to reduce emissions of nitrogen oxides (NOx) so drastically that they already comply with future limits. "Even in RDE (real driving emissions) testing, emissions from vehicles equipped with the newly premiered Bosch diesel technology are not only significantly below current limits but also those scheduled to come into force from 2020," said the company in a statement. A factor that's worth highlighting, is that Bosch engineers achieved these results by just refining existing technologies. There is no need for additional components, which would drive up costs. More details here: bit.ly/2JBNFxg
Nuevo Leon-based Katcon to open three plants in China and US

Mexican company Katcon plans to open at least three production plants during this and next year; two in China and one more in the United States, said Carlos Turner Gonzalez, president of Katcon Global. "We want to continue growing in China, we have expansion plans to open two plants there in the next two years. Now we are preparing the opening of one and next year we will prepare another one," Turner explained. Katcon is a family-owned company based in Monterrey, Nuevo Leon that designs and manufactures catalytic converters and diesel after-treatment devices, as well as exhaust modules and systems for diesel and gasoline. It also operates an advanced materials division for high technology lightweight components. More details here: bit.ly/2r9dE8f
Mergers and acquisitions in automotive industry more than double in first quarter of 2018

Mergers and acquisitions (M&A) in the automotive industry more than doubled globally during the first quarter of 2018, with overall deals valued at US$ 29.8 billion, representing a 272 percent hike when compared to same period of 2017. Volume increased 7 percent to 217 deals while average value of disclosed deals jumped 282 percent to US$ 455 million, according to the Q1 2018 Automotive deals report from PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP (PwC). The increase in value and average deal size is primarily driven by two megadeals in Q1 2018, compared to zero in Q4 2017. The two megadeals were by Parts and Component Manufacturers accounting for US$ 20 billion of deal value, or 66 percent of all disclosed deal value in the quarter. More details here: bit.ly/2vQDjr8
Continental may spin-off car engine components business

Continental said it might spin-off its business that makes car engine components, potentially paving the way for a stock market listing for parts of the division, Reuters reported. The auto parts manufacturer has been in talks with banks about a possible structural overhaul to become more flexible and adjust to far-reaching shifts in the auto industry towards electric and self-driving technologies. Continental will expand the "Powertrain Division," which accounts for less than a fifth of its 53.17 billion in annual sales and makes components for battery and combustion engines, said Reuters citing Chief Executive Elmar Degenhart as source.
