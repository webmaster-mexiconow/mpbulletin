Volvo Buses Mexico will close 2019 with record sales

By the end of this year, Volvo Buses Mexico will have sold 1,000 units to national clients, which represents a record for the brand and reflects the importance that the country has on the company's global production, which amounts to almost 10%. This was discussed as part of Volvo Buses Leadership Meeting, where experiences were shared and the strategic direction of the business was discussed. In the meeting, Rafael Kisel, general director of Volvo Buses Mexico, delivered to the president of Volvo Buses, Håkan Agnevall and to the senior vice president of Volvo Americas, Ralph Acs, two pieces that document the brand's history in Mexico.

Environmental verification programs are already considering the use of EPA 10 and Euro VI

Tomas Martinez Lopez, normativity consultant of the National Trucking Chamber (CANACAR), said that the current programs of environmental verification of the Megalopolis already consider the use of EPA 10 and Euro VI technologies in the engines of cargo trucks. "They're taking for granted that carriers are acquiring vehicles with this technology and are considering what's included in the NOM-044-SEMARNAT-2017 in the verification programs, and they're also assuming that there's availability of ultra-low sulfur diesel," he said.

Mexico saw the production of 300,292 cars in November

This November 300,292 cars were produced in Mexico, 13% less than those registered in the same month of last year, reported the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This is the biggest contraction for an eleventh month of the year in 16 years. The fall was caused because eight out of the twelve companies assembling vehicles in the country reported a reduction in their assembly lines, resulting from changes in their production lines and the low demand for vehicles worldwide.

Toyota Mexico hopes to close 2019 with sales superior to 100,000 units

Toyota Motor Sales of Mexico expects to close this year with sales superior to 100,000 units, which would mean the fourth consecutive year with sales surpassing 100,000 vehicles; this was said by Tom Sullivan, general director of the automotive company. México accepts for 70% of the steel used in vehicle production to be from the region: T-MEC

The United States, Mexico and Canada are expecting for the negotiations of the new commercial agreement between them (USMCA) to come to an end soon, according to the latest developments presented on Sunday by Marcelo Ebrard, minister of Foreign Relations. Ebrard specified that there's only two "red lines" in the negotiation: the generalized use of labor inspectors from one country in plants of another country and an automotive clause that would tighten the origin rules for steel and aluminum products. Mexico made one allowance: it's accepting the use of 70% of steel from the region, for the production of vehicles, as long as this requirement begins five years after the USMCA goes into force. As for the aluminum, Mexico rejected that requirement.
