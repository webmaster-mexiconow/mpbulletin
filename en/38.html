30 March, 2017
Spain-based automaker Seat plans to build cars in Mexico

Spain-based automaker Seat considers building cars in Mexico using facilities owned by Volkswagen, its parent company. Seat aims to expand its business in Latin America in the next decade as part of a plant to stabilize earnings by reducing its reliance on Europe. "Making Seat vehicles in VW Group production facilities in Mexico could provide access to more Central and South American markets," said Luca de Meo, CEO of Seat, at the brand's annual earnings press conference.
Joint venture led by Carlos Slim introduces first two built-in-Mexico SUVs

Jianghai Automobile Company (JAC) introduced two SUVs built in Mexico by a joint venture where Carlos Slim's financial company Inbursa will be the principal shareholder. Production of both models (SEI 2 and SEI 3) started last month at Giant Motors assembly plant located in Sahagun city, in the central state of Hidalgo. JAC expects sales to reach 10,000 units annually over the next five years in the country, but also aims to take advantage of Mexico's free trade agreements to export its products to the rest of Latin America.
Mexico increases auto production share in NAFTA region

Mexico's share of North American automotive production exceeded 20% during the first two months of 2017, gaining considerable regional share amid falling output in the US and Canada over the same period, revealed data by WardsAuto. Mexico's vehicle output rose 10% over the first two months of the year compared with January and February 2016, while Canada slipped 9% and the US fell 2.9%, WardsAuto.com said. The data reveals General Motors, Fiat Chrysler Automobiles, Nissan and Volkswagen scheduled significantly more Mexican light-truck production over the period.
Reynosa plant to remain under Kongsberg Automotive's ownership

On newsletter #37 MexicoNow stated Kongsberg Automotive sold its North American headrest and armrest business to Futuris Automotive. However, we wrongfully named a Reynosa plant as being part of the sale instead of Kongsberg's factory in Milan, Tennessee. The Reynosa remains under Kongsberg's ownership.
Puebla state auto workers union to demand 7% salary rise

Leobardo Soto Martinez, leader of the Puebla chapter for CTM, the biggest Mexican union, said his organization will demand a 7% salary rise for workers in 50 automotive companies located in the state. The new round of talks comes in a moment when automakers face uncertainty due to the coming renegotiation of NAFTA. Volkswagen's biggest production facility outside of Germany is located in Puebla, as well as the new Audi plant which started operations last September.
Continental opens US$ 167 million plant in San Luis Potosi

Germany-based auto supplier Continental started operations at a new brake components plant located in San Luis Potosi after a year of construction work and US$ 167 million invested. The company hired 97 workers for the initial phase and expects to increase its workforce up to 250 by next year. By 2019 the plant will have a 518 workers staff and is expected to reach 990 employees once it achieves its full capacity. The facility will manufacture hydraulic brake systems and turbo chargers.
Recreational vehicles manufacturer BRP reports strong fourth quarter profits

BRP reported a stronger-than-expected fourth quarter which ended on January 31, 2017. The company obtained a US$ 102.07 million profit compared with a loss of US$ 21.45 million during the same three-month period last year. Revenue for the quarter was up 17.7% to US$ 980 million. BRP employs 3,600 at three facilities in Mexico, two of them are located in Ciudad Juarez which built all-terrain vehicles and a Queretaro facility that manufactures Ski-Doos.
Ford reminds state officials US$ 1.2 billion investment in 3 Michigan plants

Ford Motor Co. announced is investing US$ 1.2 billion in three Michigan facilities, including an engine plant where it plans to add 130 jobs. Ford announced plans to upgrade some of its Michigan plants in November 2015 as part of a new contract with the United Auto Workers union. However, Ford added US$ 350 million in new investments on top of previously pledged spending. The announcement was timed to a state meeting where Michigan officials approved nearly US$ 31 million in grants and 15-year tax exemptions for Ford.
Ford issues three safety recalls in North America

Ford issued a safety recall for approximately 230,000 2013-15 Ford Escape, Ford Fiesta ST, Ford Fusion and Ford Transit Connect 1.6-liter GTDI vehicles for under-hood fire risk. In the affected vehicles, a lack of coolant circulation could cause an engine to overheat, causing a crack in the cylinder head that may result in a pressurized oil leak. Oil that comes into contact with a hot engine surface increases the risk of a fire. Ford is aware of 29 reports of fire associated with this issue in the United States and Canada. No injuries have been reported.
Continental starts Head-Up Display production in Guadalajara

Continental announced its Head-Up Display (HUD) production has started at a facility located in Guadalajara. The HUD shows the vehicle's information on the windshield to the driver, including speed readings, to help drivers keep their eyes on road. The new process required installation of state-of-the-art clean rooms and hundreds of hours in development by a team of engineers from U.S., Germany and Mexico. A staff of 40 workers were hired for the endeavor adding to the 2,842 employees already working at the facility.
