Decrease in automotive exports and production is expected to continue in 2020

The production and exports of vehicles in Mexico could fall again in 2020 due to weakening local and global vehicle demand, as expected by the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA). "I think that we will have a production of around 3.5 million units (in 2020) and exports that are going to be at around 80% of this amount," said Eduardo Solis, outgoing president of AMIA, in a press conference.

KIA Motors Mexico has a successful end of the year

KIA Motors Mexico sold 8,319 units in December, number with which it's closing a year of great results despite the market's fall; thus consolidating itself as the fifth most sold brand in the country with a market participation of 7.3% by selling 95,539 vehicles this year, a record number for the South Korean company. Once again Mexican consumers showed their trust in the products produced in the Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon plant, with KIA Rio and KIA Forte sedan, leading the sales list.

JAC Mexico closes the year with a growth of 63.5%

At the close of the January- December 2019 period JAC Mexico, which is commercialized and produced by Giant Motors Latin America, reported a sales increase of 63.5% with more than 4,500 placed units, thus becoming the brand with the most growth in the Mexican automotive market. "We're very satisfied and very thankful with the great acceptance JAC has experienced, being the brand with the most growth in 2019," commented Elias Massri, CEO and director of Giant Motors Latin America.

Automotive financing experiments a reduction of 7.7%

Between January and December of 2019 there were a total of 805,741 vehicles through credit in Mexico, this number is 7.7% less to that registered in the same period of 2018, informed the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). This the biggest reduction presented by the industry in this rubric in the last 10 years, on top of being the lowest volume in the last four years. Guillermo Rosales, deputy general director of AMDA, said in conference that this is due to the deterioration of the population's purchasing power and in the power to undertake new debts.

Volkswagen produces 443,414 units in 2019

In 2019 Volkswagen made 443,414 units in its Cuautlancingo, Puebla plant, 1.8% more than the 435,373 units produced in 2018, according to numbers from the Administrative Register of the Light Vehicle Automotive Industry from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). Of the production total, 221,731 vehicles were Tiguan, 146,630 Jetta, 54,473 Golf and Golf Variant and 20,580 Beetle/Cabrio. As for exports, the company sent 415,921 units abroad in 2019, 10.1% more than what was requested in 2018.

Hyundai Motor of Mexico has a successful 2019

Hyundai Motor of Mexico closed the year with 45,607 sold vehicles. Of which, the Accent was the clients' favorite by reaching 10,775 units throughout 2019. It was followed by Creta with 9,144 sold units. On the other hand, Grand i10 sold 8,002 vehicles. In total Hyundai has sold 216,759 units since it arrived to the country in 2014. Hyundai Motor of Mexico was recognized as the best volume brand in J.D. Power's Vehicle Dependability in Mexico 2019 (VDS) study.
