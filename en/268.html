September 24th, 2019
Chinese company JAC sees potential in Mexico

JAC Motors has ambitious plans, in which Mexico is a key player. The brand operates in Mexico with Giant Motors Latin America and has a five vehicle offer. All of these units are made in the company's Sahagun City plant, exclusively for their sale in the national market. However, this complex is strategic when we speak of a plan to branch out into exporting vehicles to other markets within the region.
CMIC will invest US$8.7 million in logistic center

The Mexican Chamber of Construction Industry (CMIC) informed that a logistic center between the municipalities of Amozoc y Acajete will be built, with an investment of US$8.7 million, to give better fluidity to vehicles assembled by Volkswagen of Mexico and Audi, as well as supply demanded by the auto parts sector. Jose Antonio Hernandez Gonzalez, CMIC Puebla's president, commented that construction is expected to begin in November.
The new T-Cross will be available for pre-order online through Volkswagen system

Volkswagen will begin a new 100% online pre-order phase for the new T-Cross. The online platform can be found at: t-cross.com.mx. The platform will walk the client through the pre-order process every step of the way and all versions will be available for pre-order. The available versions are: manual and automatic Trendline, automatic Comfortline and the automatic Limited Edition.
GM announces the arrival of Buick Encore GX, GMC Acadia, Cadillac XT4 and Cadillac XT5 to México

General Motors announced the arrival to the Mexican market of the models Buick Encore GX, GMC Acadia, Cadillac XT4 and Cadillac XT5. Buick confirmed the arrival of the new Encore GX, for which it didn't give any engine specifications, but said that it'll be available at the beginning of next year. GMC confirmed the arrival to Mexico of the 2020 Acadia, which has a V6 3.6L engine coupled to a new 9 speed transmission. The 2020 Cadillac XT4 will be available in all Buick-GMC-Cadillac distributors in Mexico starting September 30. The 2020 Cadillac XT5 in all its versions will be available at the end of 2019.
Lamborghini presents Hurracán Evo in its coupe and Spyder versions

The Commercial Director of Lamborghini Mexico, Manuel Sainz, presented the Hurracán Evo model with coupe bodywork and remarked that this car is the result of an evolution where the already existing characteristics are consolidated, while at the same time, there's a solution development in matters of presentation and design. He highlighted that the Spyder version, which starts at US$390,000, doesn't lose its torsional rigidity and its aerodynamic design with an aluminum frame and carbon fiber bodywork with an aluminum outer shell, electrically foldable soft top roof and front splitter with integrated wing.
Tresguerras Cargo Trucking inaugurates distribution terminal 140

Tresguerras Cargo Trucking began operations of its 140th national distribution terminal. The installation comprehends 18,000 square meters, of which the area of concentration for product distribution compromises practically 10,000 square meters. It's been adapted with 15 truck unloading areas (located in the posterior part of the building) and five direct dispatch platforms in the front.
Production and export of transport vehicles showed an increase in July

The National Association of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks Producers (ANPACT) reported that, during July, the production of transport vehicles reached a total of 20,006 units, which means an increase of 6.06% compared with the same month of last year. At the same time, in July, there was an export of 18,140 units, which meant an increase of 11.21%, in relation with the same month of 2018.
