Hanon Systems will invest US$4 million in Queretaro expansion

The Korean automotive company, Hanon Systems, will expand its production center in the Queretaro, project for which it will invest US$4 million, with the projection of adding 374 new jobs. Hanon Systems plant director, Yonas Sirgew, reminisced that the complex was first established in the state in 2014 on a 3,000 square meter surface, while the expansion plan entails reaching 15,000 square meters in El Marques Industrial Park, where it generates smart thermal solutions.
Mercedes-Benz Buses presented award to 11 supplier companies

A total of eleven supplier companies for the Mercedes-Benz Buses plant in Villa de Garcia, Nuevo Leon, received the "Masters of Quality" award, which praises qualitative performance, delivery programs, work and engineering development of the companies that supply systems, parts and components to the firm's assembly lines, as well as service, delivery times and acknowledgement of non-productive material.
Amazon registered its first home delivery sale in México

The company announced that it delivered the first Beetle Final Edition unit in Mexico State through their Twitter account. The car arrived packaged in a cardboard box with the respective Amazon and Volkswagen logos, as well as a plastic window that allowed visibility of the unit, as if it were a miniature toy car. This was the first time in Amazon Mexico's history in which the company sold a car through the online platform.
Toyota's 2020 Supra arrives to Mexico

The development of the Toyota Supra saw BMW as a partner. As such, practically every mechanical component of the Japanese coupe is shared with the Z4, although the programming and tuning are different. It arrives to Mexico with one engine option, the range's most potent engine: a turbo charged propellant of six cylinder inline and 3.0 liters, capable of generating 355 hp and 365 lb-foot. It's operated through an automatic eight speed transmission with launch control mode. The price for Toyota's 2020 Supra in Mexico is of MXN$1,149,900.
Leoni will invest US$25 million towards expansion of Chihuahua plant

Leoni announced that it will build a new section of 700 square meters (with an option of increasing it to 300 more meters) in its manufacturing plant in Cuauhtémoc, Chihuahua, for which it will invest more than US$25 million. With this, production capacity for EV charging cables and high voltage Hivocar cables for alternative activation, will be increased. Production is scheduled to begin in the first trimester of 2020, although initial testing will be conducted by the end of December 2019.
CONALEP and Mercedes-Benz Mexico sign education agreement

Mercedes-Benz's Mexico president and general director, Jaime Cohen Benitez; and the general director of the National College of Technical Professional Education (CONALEP), Enrique Ku Herrera, signed the "General Collaboration Agreement." It's a formative agreement whose objective is to conjunctly develop programs on a national level for CONALEP's students to fulfill their career apprenticeships in accordance with their profile. Through this alliance, both institutions will develop the following actions: graduate placement, job center of related careers, and technical equipment donations.
Sales of Mexican vehicles in the United States increased 14.3%

In the first seven months of the year, the sales of Mexican vehicles in the United States increased 14.3% adding up to a total of 1.5 million units. Kristin Dziczek, researcher for the Center for Automotive Research (CAR), considers that the increase of Mexican exports in a decreasing American marker responds to two factors. The first is related to what the specialist considers an increase in automotive production in the country. The second one has to do with the "demand of some vehicles specifically produced in Mexico."
