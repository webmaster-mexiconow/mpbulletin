Matcor Matsu Group invests US$90 million in Coahuila

The Canada-based firm Matcor Matsu Group inaugurated the expansion of its facilities in the southeast of Coahuila. The manufacturer of metal structures, panels, and seats for the automotive industry arrived almost two years ago at the Parque Amistad Industrial Park with an investment project of US$90 million. GM, Chrysler, LEAR, Yang Feng, and Adient are among its main clients. In July of last year, the Canadian group generated 550 jobs once it commenced operations; nevertheless, the expansion is set to increase its workforce to 950 strong.
Sales of heavy vehicles increase in May

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) reported that, during the month of May, the wholesale sale of heavy vehicles in Mexico reached a total of 4,682 units, which, compared to 2,817 registered in May 2018, represented an increase of 63.1%. During the same month, retail sales reached 3,947 units, which compared to those sold during the same month last year, meant an increase of 14.3%.
There could be a 6.5% drop in the sale of vehicles by late 2019

By adding 24 months of contraction, vehicle dealerships negatively adjusted their car sales expectation within the Mexican market by the end of 2019, dropping from 5 to 6.5%, informed Guillermo Rosales, general director of the Mexican Association of Auto Dealers (AMDA). With May's sales numbers, the industry registered the worst sales' drop since 2014. In this sense, the dealerships represented within AMDA made the adjustment to 2019's forecast placing the market, by the end of the year, at 1 million 331,186 units.
Without tariffs, exports won't be negatively impacted: Miguel Elizalde

With the suspension of tariffs on Mexican products by the United States, the effects on the exports of the automotive industry, as well as the entire value chain, will be immediately avoided, said Miguel Elizalde Lizarraga. The executive president of the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) added that, in the medium and long-term, it will be possible to maintain and boost the competitiveness of North America as a region before other commercial blocks in other regions of the world.
Audi Mexico celebrates the fifth edition of Audi Girls' Day

With the aim of promoting the inclusion of women in the automotive sector and reducing the gender gap that exists in the industry, Audi Mexico celebrates the Audi Girls' Day for the fifth time at their Specialization Center in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla. With several interactive workshops, more than 120 girls of around 14 and 17 years old joined the training activities that take place within the center, where they can learn about the components of an engine, the mechanics of tools, quality control, painting, and automotive mechanics.
Nissan Leon joins the NREDI 2.1 image standard

Nissan Mexicana, alongside its business partner Grupo Torres Corzo, renewed the Nissan Leon dealership, in Guanajuato, which adds to the expansion of the NREDI 2.1 image standard of the brand's distributor network in Mexico, which began in 2017. In addition to continuing to strengthen the brand in the Bajio area, the renewal of Nissan Leon seeks to provide a service focused on complete customer satisfaction while offering an experience based on innovation values.
Tariff agreement between Mexico and the United States is good news: AMDA

The automotive industry would have been one of the most affected sectors had the United States executed their tariffs on Mexico, due to the level of regional integration that has been achieved in the last three decades, said the general director of the Mexican Association of Auto Dealers (AMDA), Guillermo Rosales. "However, we must be aware that the US electoral process will continue to be a source of pressure that will generate instability." He considered the announcement of the agreement between Mexico and the United States as "very good news", since it cancels out the threat of President Trump to impose tariffs on Mexican exports.
