24 November, 2016
Toyota breaks ground for new Mexican plant

Toyota held a groundbreaking ceremony in Apaseo el Grande for its new Toyota Motor Manufacturing de Guanajuato (TMMGT) facility. The US$ 1 billion factory will be Toyota's 15th manufacturing plant in North America and its second in Mexico, complementing a Baja California facility that builds the Tacoma pickup. The Guanajuato factory will focus solely on the Corolla, which claims the title of best-selling nameplate across the globe.
Ford vows to work with Trump after criticism of Mexico investments

Ford Motor Co., which faced harsh criticism from Donald Trump over its Mexican investments, congratulated him on his election victory and vowed to work with the president-elect. "We agree with Mr. Trump that it is really important to unite the country, and we look forward to working together to support economic growth and jobs," Ford spokeswoman Christin Baker said. Trump repeatedly said during the campaign that if elected he would not allow Ford to open a new plant in Mexico and would slap hefty tariffs taxes on Ford vehicles.
Frisa opens US$ 150 million specialty steel facility

Steel products manufacturer Frisa inaugurated its specialty mill in Garcia, Nuevo Leon. The project opened 250 jobs directly. The US$ 150 million facility has capacity to manufacture up to 350,000 metric tons of specialty steel to supply the company's units that produce forged parts for aerospace, oil, heavy machinery and automotive industries. The new plant will be able to produce 600 different combinations of specialty steel.
KIA reaches new incentive agreement with Nuevo Leon authorities

Even though the KIA facility in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon is up and running, the Government of the State of Nuevo Leon and the Korean company had several negotiation meetings to reach a new incentive agreement. The new signed agreement includes some incentives such as the reduction of 95% of the Payroll Tax for 5 years, as well as the donation of the land in which the KIA facility was erected and additional land for future growth. Also, KIA will receive a property tax reduction of 50% for 10 years. Both parties will build additional infrastructure such as a railroad spur and the relocation of a gas pipeline.
Nelson Global and Tube Specialties merge businesses; two Mexican plants involved

Nelson Global Products Inc., a global leader in design and manufacture of tubular and exhaust products for commercial vehicle markets and Tube Specialties Co., Inc, an industry leader of tube forming for many of the world's largest transportation original equipment manufacturers (OEM's), have combined their "On Highway" operations into one organization. The newly combined company will have a broader product offering, expanded capacity, and global reach. Terms of the deal were not disclosed. Tube Specialties owns a 70,000 square feet production site in Saltillo, Coahuila, while Nelson Global Products operates a manufacturing and distribution facility in Monterrey, Mexico, just 54 miles east of Saltillo.
GM to lay off 2,000 workers as it reduces small-car production

General Motors Co. will permanently lay off around 2,000 hourly workers in January at two car plants to cut bloated inventory as sales of small cars continue to slow. The automaker also would invest more than US$ 900 million at three plants for future products. The company plans to cut the third shift at its Lansing Grand River Assembly Plant and Lordstown Assembly Plant in Ohio beginning in the first quarter to match supply with demand. GM has said it remains committed to small car production in the U.S. but also is investing US$5 billion in Mexico over six years. It has been relatively quiet about what vehicles and engines it will build in Mexico, though some analysts believe some next-generation Chevrolet Equinox and GMC Terrain small crossover production will shift from Canada.
Solvay Group plans nylon compounding facility in San Luis Potosi

Belgium-based Solvay Group is building a nylon compounding unit in San Luis Potosi, Mexico, with initial annual capacity of 10,000 tons aimed at the automotive market. This new facility is expected to become operational in July 2017 to serve the region's and America's growing automotive and consumer goods markets. The new plant will help the company to support its fast growing Technyl® polyamide business in North America, a compound for under-hood engine parts such as air induction hoods.
PPG Industries completes first phase of expansion at Mexican facility

Pittsburgh-based PPG Industries announced the completion of the first phase of an expansion project at its facility in Tepotzotlan, State of Mexico. The completed expansion, which adds production and distribution capacity for automotive adhesive and sealant products, is part of a two-phase project that will help PPG meet increasing demand in the growing Mexican car manufacturing industry. "This investment reflects our commitment to supporting automotive-manufacturing customers in Mexico and throughout the region, ensuring they have access to PPG's adhesives and sealants portfolio as well as all our world-class automotive coatings products," said Juanjo Ardid, PPG global general manager for adhesives and sealants division, in a press release.
Eaton to expand two facilities in Juarez

Electric components manufacturer Eaton will expand operations in two out of its three plants located in Juarez, Chihuahua, during late 2016 and early 2017, to produce fuses and transformers while adding new products to its lines. Peter Lugo, Vice President and General Manager of Bussmann division, announced that regarding fuses, a new line of products for electric vehicles will be introduced, with smaller components which protect the system of these units.
Season Group's Reynosa facility achieves TS16949 certification

The electronics manufacturing services company Season Group announced that its operation in Reynosa, Mexico, has been formally awarded TS16949 approval. The ISO/TS16949 is an ISO technical specification aimed at the development of a quality management system that provides for continual improvement, emphasizing defect prevention and the reduction of variation and waste in the automotive industry supply chain. "The timing of the award is also helpful, as we have a stand at the upcoming Electronica event in Munich and are keen to promote Reynosa's credentials to European automotive companies," said Allen Bennink, Operations Manager at the Reynosa plant.
