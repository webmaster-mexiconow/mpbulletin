October 4th, 2019
Ford's technological center aims to finish construction in December

Ford's Research and Development Technological Center (CTIDIA) might finish construction in December, after starting construction two years ago. More than 3,000 people will work in this center. The center's design was work of the architectural firm JON International. This building is a smart building with 41,000 square meters for offices and 20,000 square meters for testing and Ford product development.
Volvo inaugurates new Olmann Motors dealership in Chiapas

Volvo Group Mexico celebrated the opening of a new Olmann Motors dealership in Tuxtla Gutierrez, in Chiapas, before clients, business partners and press which gathered at the dealer's installations. With Olmann Motors' start of operations, Volvo Group's brands sold in Mexico give a step forward in the Mexican South West to strengthen the brand's presence through its robust dealer network.
South Korean ambassador to Mexico gives award to KIA Motors Mexico

South Korea's ambassador to Mexico, Song Il Kim gave the Corporate Social Responsibility award to Jong Kun Lee, president of KIA Motors Mexico, for the company's work in support of education, health, corporate development and sport. "For KIA Motors Mexico it's a matter of pride to be recognized by the embassy of South Korea in Mexico, but it's also an incentive to continue with the commitment and work on its various social programs," said the company in a press release.
SEAT Arona and Ibiza receive award from Latin NCAP

SEAT's vehicles obtained the maximum safety rating in Latin NCAP's tests. Both SEAT Ibiza and the Arona obtained the maximum possible safety rating in the series of tests organized by the consumer protection organism for the Latin American market. SEAT Mexico's director, Edgar Casal, received Latin NCAP's award for the Ibiza and Arona models, which was delivered by Alejandro Furas, secretary general of Latin NCAP.
GM announces idling of Silao plant due to strike in the United States

The strike of more than 50,000 workers of General Motors (GM) in the United States, is taking its toll on Mexico. The complex in Silao, Guanajuato announced that it would be idled due to the lack of components coming from the plants on the other side of the border. "For now, our complexes located in Ramos Arzipe, San Luis Potosi and Toluca will operate as usual," said the firm in a press release.
September registers the sale of 100,734 units

Car sales in the Mexican market registered their worst September since 2014 with the sales of only 100,734 units. This result also implied the second weakest month for automotive companies in 2019 so far, only above April, month in which only 98,346 cars were sold. This sales total in Mexico meant a fall of 12.3% in its annual comparison, according to numbers reported by the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) and disseminated by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI).
No one will be fired despite idling in GM's Silao plant

The plant in Silao underwent an idling due to the strike of almost 50,000 workers in the United States. The decision to idle Silao's plant will mean that a part of the plant's 6,000 workers will stop receiving their full salary, some will get a percentage of it and others will be covered as vacations, informed GM Mexico's Communications and Public Relations department. The company chose to not reveal any other details, but they reaffirmed that no one would be fired throughout this process.
AT&T and Fleet Complete announced the release of their services in México

AT&T, one of the largest telecommunications providers in the United States and Fleet Complete, are officially announcing their launch of cost-reducing, safety-oriented fleet solutions in Mexico. "We are thrilled to be offering our next-generation fleet management technology in Mexico, helping their businesses – big and small – be safe, productive and cost-effective," says Tony Lourakis, founder and CEO of Fleet Complete.
Continental Plant in Chihuahua celebrates its 20th anniversary

Continental Chihuahua, dedicated to the production of transmission power bands, celebrated its 20th anniversary. Nowadays, the plant has 400 employees, and has grown production wise every year. Additionally, this growth has been accompanied by high quality standards with awards and recognitions from the industry like ISO 9001-2015 e ISO 14001-2015, as well as the ContiTech Excellence Award, which make Continental Chihuahua a worldwide class plant.
FCA Mexico reported the sales of 4,961 units in September

In September, FCA Mexico reported the sales of 4,961 units. RAM had sales of 2,121 units. On the other hand, Dodge managed to sell 1,376 units in the Mexican market. Dodge Attitude continued being the top seller with 1,030 units. Dodge Journey had sales of 130 units, while Dodge Neon sold 82 units. Jeep sold 958 units, while Fiat sold 457 units. Finally Chrysler registered the sales of 25 units and Alfa Romeo registered the sale of 24 units.
