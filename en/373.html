GMC begins selling the Acadia Black Edition in Mexico

GMC announced the beginning of the commercialization of the Acadia Black Edition in the Mexican market, which incorporates an exclusive design and unique technology to complete the current portfolio offered by the AT4 and Denali versions. The 2021 GMC Acadia incorporates a 3.6L V6 engine, which delivers 310 hp and 271 lb-ft of torque, as well as a Stop/Start function with a disable feature, coupled to a 9-speed automatic transmission electronically controlled by means of Electronic Precision Shift button shifting. It will be available at all Buick, GMC and Cadillac dealers in Mexico in 7 exterior and 4 interior colors.

Impro begins construction of a new production base in San Luis Potosi

In order to align with the Group's localization strategy, Impro Precision began to build a new production base for North America in San Luis Potosi, Mexico (Impro Mexico SLP Campus) on land acquired on August 2019, in order to better serve the needs of the end markets for automotive equipment, agricultural engineering, high-powered engines, hydraulic systems and aerospace industry in the USMCA region. The total investment for the development of phase one was more than US$100 million, of which the estimated expenditure for this year in the construction of two plants is US$56 million.

Ford develops their purchasing and supplier development strategy in Mexico

Ford's plants in Mexico are supported by a purchasing and supplier development strategy, with a focus on product quality, innovation and location, in order, above all now, to comply with the precepts established in the USMCA. Pete Hockey, Director of Purchasing and Supplier Technical Assistance for Ford Mexico, states that the company's business vision for its supplier base is supported by taking advantage of technological advances. He points out that there are multiple opportunities for the Mexican supplier base to grow in the field of developing production components and tools of different types.

Guillermo Alejandro Díaz is Vice President of Toyota Motor Sales of Mexico

With the responsibility of continuing to strengthen Toyota Motor Sales of Mexico’s commercial strategy within the perspective of the changes and trends shown by the demand for new vehicles nationwide, Guillermo Alejandro Diaz Arana, who since November 2013 was the firm's director of operations, assumed the vice presidency of that area as of October 1. Kangoo Z.E. the first utility vehicle of its kind arrives to Mexico

Renault announced the arrival in Mexico of Kangoo Z.E. (Zero Emissions), an electric utility vehicle, the first of its kind in the country and the best-selling utility vehicle in Europe. The vehicle offers a range of 200 km, with a battery that has a charge capacity of 33 kWh, and a 7.4 kWh (32A) charger, with three years of free preventive maintenance, which allows for a complete charge in 5 hours and 15 minutes. The unit will be marketed in Mexico with a 5-year guarantee, in two versions: Cargo Maxi and 5 passenger Maxi, with a starting price of MXN$699,999.

The SE is working on the renewal of the decree that regulates the import of used vehicles

The head of the Ministry of Economy (SE), Graciela Marquez, said that she is working on the renewal of the decree that regulates the import of used vehicles from the United States and Canada, since it is valid until December 31, 2020. In this regard, Guillermo Rosales Zarate, deputy director general of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), explained that the current decree has achieved an orderly market, however, it is necessary that the new one has sufficient control elements to ensure that "garbage" is not introduced into the vehicle fleet.

ZF Chihuahua receives Volvo Quality of Excellence recognition

The ZF Chihuahua plant, which manufactures air bags, received the Volvo Quality of Excellence "VQE Award 2019″ from Volvo Car Corporation, due to the continuous quality of its products and "excellent service". This recognition, created in 2011 at the company's headquarters in Gothenburg, Sweden, is awarded to suppliers that provide products and services of optimum quality. The relevance of the recognition also lies in the fact that it is the first time that Volvo Car Corporation has given it to a supplier company based in Latin America.
