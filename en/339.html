New car sales drop in Chihuahua

New car sales in the state of Chihuahua continue at a low due to the effects of COVID-19, which is why the month of May became the third month with a downward trend. In AMDA's report it is shown that, while in May 2019 2,688 vehicles were sold in Chihuahua, in the same month of this year that number decreased to 1,043. From one year to the next 1,645 units stopped selling due to the economic stagnation throughout the country.

Guillermo Prieto is ratified as AMDA's president

Guillermo Prieto Treviño, who has been the head of the organization for nine years now, was ratified as president of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). With a digital assistance of 38% of the almost 3,300 distributors throughout the country of 42 brands, the vote was unanimous for him to continue, given the difficult moment the industry is going through at the moment. The main challenge for Prieto and his team is to reactivate operations. With the traffic light system already in orange the country has been opened about 30% with the following states opening sales floors: Jalisco, Michoacán and Veracruz, and Aguascalientes and Durango doing so partially.

Motorcar Parts of America will invest US$11 million in Mexico in 2021

Auto parts manufacturer, Motorcar Parts of America, presided by Selwyn Joffe, plans to expand its operation in Mexico to move part of its productive capacity previously installed in other markets (mainly Asia) to North America. Since last year the auto parts manufacturer has invested millions for this project in national soil and for the 2021 fiscal year they expect to inject other US$11 million in Mexico, as commented by David Lee, the company's finance director, in a meeting with analysts. Motorcar Parts of America currently has installations in New York, California, Mexico, Malaysia, China and India.

The INA PAACE Automechanika Mexico is postponed to July 2021

After an extensive debate with the automotive community and considering the current environment in Mexico, INA PAACE Automechanika Mexico's organizers made the decision to postpone the 2020 edition. The event, originally programmed for July and then switched over to September, will be carried out from July 21 to July 23, 2021 in the Centro City Banamex in Mexico City. The July 2021 edition will offer a new approach adapted to the "new normality," considering that this will include a more spacious distribution or a more personal environment, designed for executive contacts and business networking.

Audi Mexico will return to operations

Audi will return to operations starting Monday June 22 with 30% of its workforce, informed last Thursday the assembly plant's union to its workers. Through a release, the plant's union made the return to productive activities known in Audi Mexico, highlighting that it will be carried out by meeting all the health requirements and recommendations established by the health authorities, which have been implemented in the company's work and service areas.

General Motors of Mexico begins producing N95 masks in the State of Mexico

General Motors of Mexico announced that it has begun the production of more than 2 million N95 masks in the manufacturing complex located in Toluca, State of Mexico, as part of the efforts to help combat the pandemic caused by COVID-19. Around 90% of the more than 2 million N95 masks will be donated to public hospitals in which General Motors is present (Mexico City, Coahuila, State of Mexico, Guanajuato and San Luis Potosi) and about 10% will be delivered to professional health teams working in GM’s medical area.
