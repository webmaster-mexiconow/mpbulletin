Automotive companies will be allowed to renew operations once the US industry is reactivated: AMLO

Andres Manual Lopez Obrador, president of Mexico, said that he will allow automotive and auto parts companies restart their operations in the country once this industry is reactivated in the United States, as to not affect the highly integrated North American supply chain. The executive explained in his daily press conference that he made this decision to attend to a petition made by firms from the so called "Grupo Monterrey" in a meeting held at National Palace.

Audi Mexico extends the halt of operations in its Puebla plant

Audi Mexico will extend the halt of operations in its plant located in the municipality of San Jose Chiapa, Puebla, in keeping with the federal government's disposition in the frame of the pandemic caused by the coronavirus, with which they expect to restart operations at the beginning of May with the guarantee of fully supporting the 5,200 job positions during the time of the contingency. Initially, the halt in activities was considered to last from March 23 to April 13. However, the automotive company extended the halt of operations before the federal decree to the 30th of this month.

Mexican automotive industry asks again to be considered as essential

The Mexican automotive industry asked the federal government's General Health Council to be considered as an essential industry for the economy before the COVID-19 pandemic, to restart operations as soon as possible. In a letter addressed to Jorge Carlos Alcocer Varla, minister of Health and president of said council, and signed by the main automotive associations, it is established that they are doing this because of the words spoken by President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador, who said they would be allowed to return to operations sooner.

Illegal vehicle imports increase 14.2%: AMDA

The Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) informed that illegal used vehicle imports during this year's first two months added to 28,803 units, which means an increase of 14.2% when compared to the same period of 2019, when 25,227 units were registered. According to AMDA one of the factors that increases the entry of illegal vehicles to Mexico is product of the initiative from Baja California, which has as an objective to regularize illegal vehicles giving them plates and registration.

Brose donates 650 masks to the General Hospital of Queretaro

Due to the global situation we're going through, Brose, one of the biggest automotive suppliers worldwide, has decided to join forces with the Aditiva por Mexico initiative to support the General Hospital of Queretaro, where all of the state's COVID-19 cases are being treated. It is for this reason that the three Queretaro Brose plants made a donation of 650 shield-type masks, which help prevent infection in medical personnel.

Nemak suspends activities in some of its plants

Nemak announced that it suspended activities in some of its plants and production lines before the contingency cause by the coronavirus pandemic. The automotive sector engine component manufacturer didn't specify which plants halted operations, but did say that they have implemented a series of measures in the regions it operates in to adapt to industry changes caused by the coronavirus epidemic, which is hitting the global economy.

KIA is implementing the KIA Promise program in Mexico

"KIA Promise" is a worldwide campaign launched by KIA Motors Corporation to give benefits to every vehicle commercialized by KIA Motors Mexico and its distributors in Mexican territory. The benefits apply for clients whose guarantee ends between March 23 and May 31 of 2020 or whose service appointment was scheduled for said period. The program is extending the coverage period for such vehicles until June 30, 2020. The benefit is for all KIA clients in Mexico.
