Daimler Trucks reactivates operations in Coahuila and State of Mexico

Daimler announced the implementation of new protocols and safety measures for the reactivation of operations of the plants in Saltillo, Coahuila and Santiago, State of Mexico, on May 18. The objective of this safety campaign is protecting the health and integrity of their workers and their families in the process of going back to work, in alignment with manufacturing plants in the United States, thus supporting the continuity of the supply chain and the national economy.

The national automotive industry speeds up for a safe return to operations

The automotive sector is committed to speeding up the restart of operations and the reactivation of the economy with support from federal authorities. The Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) announced that with the sector's return to productive activity, "Mexico will begin an unprecedented period in its history," and with the promotion of integral solutions it will counter COVID-19's impacts. "We have taken actions and fundamental precautions (and we will keep doing it with strict adherence to the law) to mitigate the pandemic's effects, with which we will keep showing the sector's firm commitment in the adoption of health prevention measures," informed the automotive industry.

The Fiat Ducato 2020 is now available in Mexico

Fiat Ducato, one of the most sold vans worldwide, is the ideal vehicle for work and it's now available in Mexico. Ducato is equipped with the MultiJet Diesel four cylinder engine with a turbo compressor and intercooler, of 2.3 liters that performs at 126 horse power and 236 pounds-foot of torque at only 2,000 rpm. This engine is associated to a six speed manual transmission. Fiat Ducato is available in three options of volumetric capacity: 9.5 m3, 11.5 m3 and 15 m3.

Companies will be able to restart operations as long as they meet health requirements

Companies from the automotive, aerospace, mining or construction industries will be able to restart operations this week, as long as they meet with the requirements in the process of protocols for safe distancing and labor protection, and these requirements are endorsed by federal authorities, defined the Ministry of Health. As an extraordinary measure, the agency headed by Jorge Alcocer referred that if "the process is successfully concluded and approved before June 1st, 2020, the corresponding company or industry will be able to start its operations."

Jose Luis Valls leaves his position as president of Nissan North America

Jose Luis Valls, resigned to the presidency of Nissan North America and the executive vice presidency due to family and personal commitments. This is an important change, which will affect operations in Mexico. The end is to facilitate "a smooth transition," as stated in Nissan's United States official newsletter. After Valls' resignation there's expected changes in the brand's North America management, which is preparing a deep change within its transformation plan for its business model in the region, which includes the United States, Canada and Mexico. Jérémie Papin will assume Valls' obligations and will be in charge of supervising the region as vice president of Nissan North America.
