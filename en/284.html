Heavy vehicle production and exports grow in the first nine months of the year

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), informed that during the first nine months of the year a total of 158,431 units were produced, number which compared with the 129,795 units produced during the same period of 2018, represents an increase of 22.06%. As for accumulated exports, these reached a total of 133,182 units, which represents an increase of 20.93% in comparison with 2018.
Porsche will install charging infrastructure in Mexican distributors

Porsche's race toward electrification is outlined for the next few years with the investment of US$6.6 billion, the brand expects that half the vehicles delivered worldwide in 2050 will be wholly electric or plug-in hybrids, a plan in which Mexico plays a role and for which it will need adequate charging infrastructure. "In Mexico, our distributors will have charging infrastructure (...) We don't want to wait a long time, which is why we're beginning on our own in Mexico and we'll think of alliances on the long-tern," said Detlev von Platen, Porsche board member for sales and marketing, during the 2019 LA Auto Show.
Kia hopes to reach 300,000 produced units in 2019

Kia Motors Mexico informed that from January to October 2019 it has manufactured 244,800 vehicles in its Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon plant. They hope to close the year with an annual production of 300,000 units, said Luis Lerma, Production Director. This would mean an accumulation of 868,400 assembled units since the start of production in May 2016. Victor Aleman, the company's Public Relations manager, said that the production goal for 2020 is of 300,000 units, but that it will depend on demand.
Asian companies show interest in Nuevo Leon

Factors like the increase in national content if the USMCA were ratified and the threat of a commercial war between China and the United States have encouraged Asian companies to explore the industry in the state for their installation, said Manuel Montoya Ortega, general director of the Automotive Cluster of Nuevo Leon. "If the USMCA is ratified there would be an increase of 12% in the region's content," he said. Additionally, auto parts suppliers from Japan, Germany and South Korea would arrive to not lose their business with American carmakers.
UPSL inaugurated the Training Center for the Automotive Industry

The Polytechnic University of San Luis Potosí (UPSL) inaugurated the Training Center for Human Capital of the Automotive Industry and its Supply Chain (CCIA) in the presence of San Luis Potosi's companies and state authorities. The CCIA is made up by 6 laboratories that will contribute to the training and/or upgrading of human capital in relevant areas for the automotive industry like: hydraulics, electrohydraulic, pneumatics, electro pneumatics, programmable logic controllers (PLC) and sensors, industrial robotics, electricity and electronics and Computer Numerical Control (CNC).
AMOPSA acquires 10 DINA buses

The company Autotransportes Metropolitanos Oriente-Periférico (AMOPSA) put ten 2020 Runner DINA buses into operation, which are substituting some 2009 buses that are outside of current normativity. These vehicles are the first ones in a renewal process the company is planning to carry out in the next few years until it can modernize its bus fleet by 100%. In addition to the delivery of the units, DINA's commercial training area certified 23 operators so that they could get familiar with the functions and advantages of the new buses.
JAC Mexico announced the opening of a new JAC Store in Metepec

Giant Motors Latin America and JAC Mexico announced the opening of a JAC Store in Metepec. It belongs to CEVER Group, JAC Store Metepec will be the first in showing the "EV Corner" of the brand's pure electric line. JAC Store Metepec will sell the brand's diesel models that include de J4 sedan, the medium-sized Frison T6 pickup, the SUV family made up by the Sei2, Sei3, and Sei7 as well as the brand's new release: the Sei4 SUV. Additionally, it will also sell the JAC x200, the brand's first light truck and commercial vehicle; and JAC Frison T8, a medium sized pick up. Starting with its opening JAC Store Metepec, will have the first "EV Corner" which will show a vehicle of the brand's electric line so that interested customers can get to know the new products.
