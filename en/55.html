Firsts Audi SQ5 units start rolling out of Puebla facility

The firsts Audi SQ5 SUVs started to roll out of the new Mexican plant earlier this month making their way to perform test drives for media outlets, among them New York Daily News, which called it "impressive". The sport member of the Q5 family is powered by a 354 horsepower, 369 pound-feet of torque, turbocharged 3.0-litre petrol V6. It features an eight-speed automatic transmission and all-wheel-drive. While air suspension was not available for the first-generation vehicle, it is standard for the new model. The Audi SQ5 is base priced at US$ 55,095.
Carlos Slim's Giant Motors develops electric taxi to reduce pollution in Mexico City

Giant Motors, an automotive startup partially owned by Mexican billionaire Carlos Slim, is working to develop an electric taxi to help battle pollution in Mexico City. The company has partnered with electric vehicle maker Moldex, a unit of Mexican bread maker Grupo Bimbo and four Mexican universities to produce an environmentally friendly car that will eventually replace part of the city's more than 130,000 registered cabs, said chief executive Elias Massri. Over the last few years Giant Motors and Moldex have developed 500 light-weight, electric cargo trucks capable of handling one ton load. Massri said the experience has given them a platform for the taxi project.
MD Elektronik opens US$ 20 million plant in Guanajuato

Germany-based corporation MD Elektronik opened its first manufacturing site in Mexico at the Colinas industrial park, in Leon, Guanajuato, with a US$20 million investment, expecting to generate up to 400 jobs. This firm is dedicated to assembling automotive cables for data transmission and it will supply electronic components to clients in the Bajio region and the U.S. Automakers like BMW, Volkswagen and Mercedes Benz, among others are included in its customers portfolio. The plant will have a production capacity of 10 million pieces per year at full capacity.
Monterrey-based Lodi Group acquires US aftermarket parts distributor

The Lodi Group, a Monterrey-based family of auto parts companies, has acquired the assets of ATP Automotive Inc. a Chicago-based company strove to provide aftermarket replacement parts for automatic transmissions. Under new ownership, the company started operations as Automotive Technology Products LLC, according to a press release. No financial terms of the acquisition were provided. The Lodi Group owns manufacturing facilities in North America as well as automotive aftermarket supply companies specializing in powertrain and power transmission components.
Trump: Too many German cars sold in the U.S.

Der Spiegel and other German media outlets covering President Donald Trump's trip to Europe last week reported he called Germany "very bad" because it runs a trade surplus with the U.S., citing trade figures about the auto industry. Trump said during a private meeting he would stop German automakers from selling "millions of cars" in the U.S., Der Spiegel reported. American media then criticized the President for ignoring that much of German autos sold in the U.S. come from factories the German automakers have built in this country, like BMW and Mercedes-Benz, which own massive plants in South Carolina and Alabama, respectively.
GM faces new accusations of cheating on diesel emissions test

A year after a class-action lawsuit accused General Motors of cheating on emissions-testing with its Cruze Diesel, a new lawsuit charges the automaker with similar acts involving diesel-powered pickups. The class-action suit filed in Detroit federal court covers a total of 705,000 2011-16 Silverado and Sierra heavy-duty models equipped with Duramax diesel engines. Plaintiffs are represented by the same law firm that targeted Volkswagen Group for emissions cheating, which generating billions of dollars in settlements and penalties for the automaker. The suit charges that with the Duramax engine emit two to five times the levels of nitrogen-oxide levels allowed by law.
Bosch tied once again to diesel-cheating claims, this time against GM

Robert Bosch GmbH, the world's largest auto-parts supplier, was linked to emissions-cheating allegations against GM, raising questions about the German company's role in the scandal gripping the car industry. Bosch and General Motors Co. are co-defendants in a lawsuit filed by owners of diesel-powered pickups who accused the automaker of using illegal software, known as a defeat device, to pass U.S. emissions tests. The technology was so sophisticated that it could recognize when a car was being tested in a lab or smog station to feign clean emissions and compliance with pollution standards, according to U.S. regulators.
Software update can fix diesel emissions, FCA lawyer says

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles NV believes a software update can address U.S. regulators' contention that its diesel vehicles are producing excess emissions, a lawyer for the company said at a court hearing. The lawyer admitted no wrongdoing by the Italian-American automaker, however. The Justice Department filed a civil suit on Tuesday accusing Fiat Chrysler of illegally using software to bypass emission controls in 104,000 2014-16 diesel and labeled the software "defeat devices."
Jeep Renegade makes its way to Mexican market

The 2017 Jeep Renegade, the smallest crossover you can buy from Fiat Chrysler's off-road brand, made its way into the Mexican market this May. Just two trim levels are being offered for the Renegade so far, Sport and Latitude, none of them feature all-wheel drive. Again, contrary to the U.S. market, there's only one engine available for Mexico, a 1.8 litter E-TorQ EVO with 130 horsepower, 134 pound-feet of torque and a 6-speed automatic transmission. Units for the Mexican market are supplied by the FCA plant located in Goiana, Pernambuco, Brazil, while models for the U.S. market are made in Italy.
Nelson Global Products to close two facilities in North America

Nelson Global Products Inc., announced a restructuring plan which includes the closing of facilities in Clinton, Tennessee, and Cambridge, Minnesota. The decision affects approximately 300 employees. The company designs and manufactures exhaust, tubular, and emissions related products for on and off-road vehicles. The company's operations include corporate offices in Stoughton, WI and manufacturing operations in Fort Wayne, IN; Arcadia, Black River Falls and Viroqua, Wisconsin; Troutdale, OR; Statesville, NC; and Mexican operations in Monterrey, Nuevo Leon and Saltillo, Coahuila.
