Bulletin #39
3 April, 2017
Trump administration draft shows modest NAFTA tweaks

Far from the sweeping trade overhaul that Donald Trump promised on the campaign trail, his administration is considering a surprisingly modest revamp of NAFTA, according to a draft letter provided to Congress first leaked by The Wall Street Journal. The objectives outlined would bolster Trump's emphasis on "Buy American," including giving greater preferences for U.S. companies in government procurement. But the draft includes none of the harsh, punitive measures or steep tariffs he once threatened against Mexico.
Borg Warner sees eco-friendly tech growth despite U.S. uncertainty

Auto supplier Borg Warner said it expects to increase sales for its global hybrid and electric vehicle parts, even as a U.S. review of fuel economy standards threatens to slow emissions improvements. The company expects to boost revenue from eco-friendly technology to around 16% of total by 2023, from around 1% currently. Borg Warner owns production facilities in San Luis Potosi, Juarez, El Salto, Ramos Arizpe and Irapuato.
German auto supplier ZF, with 11 plants in Mexico, reports nearly US$ 1 billion profit

Germany-based auto supplier ZF reported its profits for 2016 were slightly lower compared to 2015 results. In the first full year after ZF acquired TRW in May 2015, an operation that included manufacturing plants in Queretaro and Jalisco, sales rose 21% to US$ 37.7 billion, but its net profits after taxes were US$ 992 million, down from US$ 1.07 billion in 2015. ZF has 43 locations in North America, including 29 in the U.S., 11 in Mexico and three in Canada and employs 32,000 people, two thirds of them in Mexico.
Nissan's global production increases for the 13th consecutive month

Nissan's global production in February increased 11.1% year-on-year to 460,256 units, the 13th consecutive month of increase and a record for the month. Production in Japan increased 14.2% year-on-year to 95,715 units, the seventh consecutive month of increase. Production outside Japan increased 10.3% year-on-year to 364,541 units. In the U.S., production decreased 5.1% year-on-year to 83,899 units. In Mexico, production increased 3.4% year-on-year to 73,922 units, a record for the month of February.
VW pays another US$ 157.45 million to settle environmental claims

Volkswagen AG has agreed to pay US$ 157.45 million to settle environmental claims from 10 U.S. states over its excess diesel emissions, as the world's largest automaker looks to move past the scandal. The settlement covers Connecticut, Delaware, Maine, Massachusetts, New York, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Vermont and Washington, as well as some consumer claims. In 2016, the German automaker reached a US$ 603 million agreement with 44 U.S. states, but that settlement did not cover claims in the new announcement. So far, the scandal has cost VW US$ 25 billion.
French firm Sarrel builds US$ 50 million plant in Guanajuato

French auto parts manufacturer Sarrel broke ground in recent days to build a plant in Apaseo el Grande, Guanajuato, with a US$ 30 million investment in its first phase and the creation of 290 direct jobs by 2022. The project includes a second phase in which an additional US$ 20 million will be invested. Sources at the breaking ground ceremony claimed the company will supply components for the Toyota plant currently under construction nearby, but company officials refused to confirm.
Ford recalls 52,600 F-250 pickups that could roll while in park

Ford Motor Co is recalling about 52,600 F-250 pickup trucks sold in the U.S. and Canada because the vehicles could roll after the driver moves the automatic transmission lever into park position. The recall, the third announced by Ford in a week, affects 2017 model year F-250 vehicles powered by 6.2-liter gasoline engines and built in its Louisville, Kentucky, truck plant. Days before the company recalled 211,000 vehicles in North America to replace potentially faulty side door latches and another 230,000 units that present a fire risk under the hood.
ZF acquires stake in German radar sensing tech firm

German supplier ZF Friedrichshafen AG has acquired a 45% stake in Astyx Communication & Sensors GmbH, which develops and produces ultra-high-frequency radar sensors and modules. ZF, a leading supplier of transmissions, chassis parts and steering and safety systems, characterized the stake acquisition as a significant step in its move into advanced autonomous technologies. Radar is a crucial technology in object recognition that will aid future vehicles in accident avoidance and autonomous driving.
Nissan's global sales rise for the seventh consecutive month

Nissan's global sales increased 5.9% year-on-year to 443,281 units during February, the seventh consecutive month of increase. Sales in Japan, including mini-vehicles, increased 4.4% year-on-year to 65,645 units, the fourth consecutive month of increase. Sales outside Japan increased 6.1% year-on-year to 377,636 units, a record for the month of February, after decreasing the previous month. In the U.S., sales increased 3.7% year-on-year to 135,740 units, a record for the month of February. In Mexico, sales increased 4.8% year-on-year to 30,078 units, a record for the month of February.
Daimler steps back from fuel-cell car development

Daimler AG head Dieter Zetsche said at an automotive summit in Germany this week that hydrogen fuel cells are no longer a major part of the automaker's plans for the future. Engineering news site Smart2Zero reports that Zetsche said declining battery costs have made fuel cell vehicles uncompetitive with electric cars. Daimler has also declared that it will accelerate its development of battery-electric vehicles by three years.
