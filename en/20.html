Bulletin #20
23 January, 2017
UK firm predicts Mexico's Auto Industry will grow despite tariffs

Auto market analysis and forecasts firm LMC Automotive predicts automakers will continue to invest in Mexico over the next four years despite tariff threats and social media bashing from President Donald Trump. According to the study, Mexican production from Ford and GM will increase through 2020, while Fiat Chrysler's is expected to fall.
Audi produces in Mexico its 8 millionth vehicle with quattro technology

Audi is celebrating a special milestone: rolling off the production line at the new plant in San José Chiapa is the 8 millionth Audi with quattro drive –the technology that delivers a big, built-in boost in safety and driving performance, even on snow and ice. "The milestone car is a garnet red Audi Q5 2.0 TFSI quattro," revealed the company.
Hyundai, Kia to invest US$ 3.1 billion in U.S. over the next five years

Hyundai Motor Co. and Kia Motors Corp. said they will spend US$ 3.1 billion in the U.S. over the next five years. Hyundai is even considering building a new factory in the U.S. to produce the Genesis and a U.S.-specific SUV. Meanwhile Kia may add a new SUV model to the plant in the Mexican state of Nuevo Leon, though the decision isn't final. Kia is targeting to export 80 percent of cars made in Mexico.
Yanfeng choses Michigan over Queretaro to build US$ 8.45 million technical center

Yanfeng Automotive Interiors plans to invest US$ 8.45 million to build a new technical center in Michigan and move its North America headquarters. About 280 employees are expected to move in and additional 60 jobs will be created over the next couple of years. The company considered to build the facility in Queretaro for a while before making a final decision.
GM resolves ignition switch investigation

General Motors announced that it has resolved an investigation arising from the company's 2014 ignition switch recalls without admitting or denying any wrongdoing. The settlement does not call into question any of GM's current or prior financial statements or its disclosures. GM will pay a civil penalty of US$ 1 million as part of the settlement.
Ford's US$ 1.1 billion expansion in Chihuahua remains on schedule

Ford Motors officials in Mexico confirmed that a new production line will start in November at the company's Chihuahua facility, creating 1,200 jobs and becoming the country's largest engine plant. The automaker plans to invest US$ 1.1 billion at the site to produce a new gasoline-powered engine aimed to the U.S., Canada and South America markets as well as the Asia-Pacific region.
Japan-based BMW supplier builds US$ 47 million plant in San Luis Potosi

Japan-based Hirotec Corporation broke ground in San Luis Potosi to build a plant which will provide components and services for BMW. The company will invest more than US$ 47.6 million to supply stamping products for the German automaker. This will be Hirotec's third facility in the country since it already operates manufacturing sites in Silao, Guanajuato, and Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila that supply parts for GM, Mazda, Daimler, Nissan Motor Co. and Fiat Chrysler.
Nexteer Automotive joins Continental in automated driving endeavor

Nexteer Automotive and Continental announced their agreement to form a joint venture focused on the advancement of motion control systems and actuator components for automated driving. This joint venture will combine Nexteer's advanced steering and driver assistance (ADAS) technologies with Continental's portfolio of Automated Driving and advanced braking technologies to accelerate advancements in vehicle motion control systems.
Toyota, Ford to expand Takata air bag safety recall

Toyota and Ford announced they're expanding a recall involving Takata front and side passenger air bag inflators. This expansion involves approximately 543,000 additional Toyota, Scion and Lexus vehicles in the U.S. and a total of 816,309 Ford, Lincoln and Mercury units built in North America. Depending on the model, dealers will replace either the front or side passenger air bag inflator or air bag assembly at no cost. Neither Ford or Toyota are aware of any injuries associated with the inflators included in this safety recall.
EPA accuses Fiat Chrysler of using emissions cheating software

The Environmental Protection Agency accused Fiat Chrysler of using cheating software that changes the way the emissions system operates. Now the company faces a potential fine which could equal a third of its total market value, US$ 4.62 billion. The software is included in the 3.0-liter EcoDiesel engine available in 2014-16 model-year Jeep Grand Cherokee SUVs and Ram 1500 pickups. The EPA says 103,828 vehicles are affected.
