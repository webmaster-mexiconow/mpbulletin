Mazda of Mexico will stop operations for 10 days

The productive plant of Mazda in Mexico will close for approximately 10 days starting March 25, as a result of the COVID-19 crisis, informed Mazda Motor Corporation. "In response to the worldwide spread of the coronavirus, a lot of countries have quickly and widely strengthened their infection prevention measures. As a result, Mazda has chosen to adjust production in its installations worldwide due to the difficulties in acquiring parts, the fall in sales in foreign markets and the uncertainty of future sales," said the Japanese automaker.

FCA will produce protective masks to help combat coronavirus

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) informed that it will help those who are on the front lines of this pandemic, which is why it will make and donate more than 1 million protective masks a month. Starting this week the necessary equipment for the making of masks will be installed and in the next few weeks, the company will begin production with an initial distribution in the United States, Canada and Mexico. The production will be donated to the police and the fire department, as well as hospitals and medical attention clinics.

Ford reevaluates the start of operations of its North America plants

Ford's priority is the well-being and safety of its workers, distributors, clients, suppliers and society at large. As a result of the indications of several governments of staying at home and working remotely, in Ford they have decided that operations in the plants located in the United States, Canada and Mexico will not restart operations on March 30 as it was originally planned. The American automaker is evaluating several options and working with union leaders, to find the optimum moment to restart vehicle production, taking the well-being of their workers into consideration.

Car sales are expected to drop 25% in 2020: AMDA

Before the drop in vehicle demand projected after the arrival of the coronavirus COVID-19 the National Automotive Dealer Association (AMDA) estimates a fall of up to 25% in the sale of new models at the close of this year. "This sanitary crisis is going to deepen the fall. We expect a decrease in sales between 16 and 25% in 2020," said Guillermo Rosales, deputy director general of AMDA. Gerardo San Roman, president of the automotive market analysis firm Jato Dynamics, confirms a deep fall in vehicle sales for this year, which he projects will be at 20%.

Hyundai offers sales and service alternatives before COVID-19

Before the contingency experienced because of the COVID-19, the South Korean brand in Mexico is offering test driving, maintenance service and new vehicle delivery to their clients' house. Likewise, it's offering online quotations and showcasing their products remotely, through the Hyundai Live program. Hyundai Live is available for any device with an Internet connection, Monday through Friday from 10:00 to 13:00 and from 14:00 to 18:00 hours.

Daimler Trucks Mexico is also stopping operations in its Mexico plants

Heavy vehicle maker Daimler Trucks Mexico informed that, with the intention of avoiding the possibility of infection of COVID-19, they will be stopping production at their two plants in Mexico. According to information provided by the company, the Saltillo plant will operate at 100% up to March 27, while the Santiago plant will do so until April 3rd, with both of them returning to normal operations on April 14. "This measures are meant to avoid the possibility of COVID-19 infection and safekeeping the health and integrity of our workers and commercial partners," they said.

Mercedes-Benz Buses suspends operations in Mexico

Due to the epidemiologic contingency caused by the arrival of the COVID-19 to Mexico and as part of the effort to limit the spread of this sickness, Mercedes-Benz Buses has implemented several sanitary measures in all its locations. From a few days ago, the company's administrative personnel is working from home under the home office scheme. In the same way, starting March 30 and through to April 22, operations at the company's plant in Garcia, Nuevo Leon, which is best known as "The Bus House."
