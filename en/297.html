Jeep Wrangler's limited edition arrives to our country

Mexico sees the arrival of another special edition of the Jeep Wrangler, this time it's the 2020 Rubicon Xtreme-Trail Rated, which will be limited to 100 units. This models is distinguished by its Apicula Solar color, special to this edition. The engine is a 3.6 liter Pentastar V6 with 285 hp and 260 pounds foot of torque with an automatic 8 speed transmission. The 2020 Jeep Wrangler Rubicon Xtreme-Trail Rated is available at MXN$$998,900.

Mercedes-Benz Buses' plant in Nuevo Leon celebrates its 26th anniversary

With a focus on the production of buses and Boxer chassis designed by Mexican, Brazilian and German engineers, Daimler Buses Mexico's manufacturing plant located in the municipality of Garcia, Nuevo Leon, celebrated 26 years from its start of operations. In 2015 the plant saw the production of the complete 30,000 unit and the 75,000 chassis; while in 2018 there were more than 85,820 units produced including the Mercedes-Benz City, Intracity and BRT models. Additionally the plant has the quality certifications ISO 9000, ISO 14001, ISO TS-16949, Clean Industry, Socially Responsible Company and Family-friendly Company.

For more information click here: http://bit.ly/35XBh5y
Audi Mexico celebrates the production of 500,000 Audi Q5 in Puebla

For the third year in a row, Audi Mexico exceeded the production goal for 2019, reaching a production volume of 156,995 Audi Q5 and has now exported 500,000 units. The automotive plant from Audi Group in Mexico is one of the most modern ones in America, capable of producing 150,000 annual units. "Half a million Audi Q5s made in Mexico, in a period of three and a half years, is only a demonstration of the strategy and performance we have as a plant," said Thomas Slupik, director of the plant in Audi Mexico.

The Honda CR-V renews itself for the new year

The Honda CR-V has proven to be successful in Mexico and Honda has given it an update. As for what will propel the 2020 Honda CR-V we find that it'll no longer use the atmospheric 2.4 liter engine and will now have a 1.5 liter turbo engine with 188 hp and 179 lb-foot torque which is connected to a CVT transmission for all its versions. The CR-V will have a price of MXN$489,900 in its Turbo version, MXN$538,900 in its Turbo Plus version and MXN$589,900 in its Touring version.

Heavy vehicle retail sales experience upturn in December

Retail sales for heavy vehicles, excluding integral buses, in December were of 3,705 units, 5.7% more when compared to December of 2018, as stated by the report Evolution of the Commercialization of Heavy Vehicles edited by the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). As for the accumulated sales for the January-December 2019 period, distributors to the general public sold 40,214 units, 1.4% more when compared to the same period of 2018.

CIQA develops a plastic nanocomposite that can be used by the automotive industry

In order to develop safer and environmentally friendly materials and offer a solution to the complex flammability plastic problem, the Center for Research in Applied Chemistry (CIQA) developed a plastic nanocomposite resistant to fire that could be used in the automotive industry. The material has an application in the making of accessories for the automotive industry such as dashboard parts and A/C conducts, among others.
