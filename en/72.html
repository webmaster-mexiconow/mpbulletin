There's no plans to build next-gen Ford Fiesta in Mexico, company official says

According to Romanian auto news site 0-100.ro, the seventh generation of the Ford Fiesta will be manufactured exclusively at a facility in Koln, Germany, to be launched only in Europe, the Middle East, and Africa. This decision opens a big question to what's going to happen at the Ford plant in Cuautitlan, Mexico, where the company builds current generations of the Fiesta 4 and 5 doors, and the Fiesta ST for several markets. Ford Fiesta sales in the U.S. market fell 0.9% in June to 4,026 units and dropped 3.8% during the first half of 2017 to 24,580 vehicles, according to the automaker.
GM considers to end production of up to six sedans by 2019

As sales of sedans continue to plummet in the U.S. due to consumers increasingly demanding SUVs and pickups, General Motors keeps six of its cars under review, including its Chevrolet Volt plug-in hybrid, to decide whether to cancel them in 2019, according to a report from Reuters. Besides the Volt, other vehicles believed to be under consideration for elimination are the Buick LaCrosse, Cadillac CT6, Cadillac XTS, Chevrolet Impala and Chevrolet Sonic, says the article. Out of those vehicles, the Sonic was the only model built in Mexico, but according to workers at the Ramos Arizpe plant, GM ended the sedan production at this facility last June.
Nissan introduces the Sentra Nismo to the Mexican market

Nissan announced plans to introduce the Nismo version of its Sentra model in the Mexican market. The Japanese automaker builds the unit for the U.S. and Canada at its A2 plant in Aguascalientes since last December. The unit features an aluminum 1.6-litre, four-cylinder engine which is built in the Yokohama plant with turbocharging and direct injection. It develops 188 horsepower and 177 pound-feet of torque, thanks in part to a 10.5:1 compression ratio, variable valve timing and a variable-pressure oil pump. The engine is matched either a six-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable automatic.
Martinrea invests US$ 35 million to expand capacity at Silao plant

Canadian manufacturer Martinrea inaugurated the expansion of its facility in Silao, Guanajuato, where the company invested US$ 35 million to install a new Tandem press to supply stamping products to several automakers. The production site now has five presses of 5,900 tons highly automated to manufacture stamping chassis components for Nissan, GM, Volkswagen, Mercedes Benz and BMW. Martinrea's Silao facility is dedicated to the engineering and manufacturing of automotive parts, such as fuel tanks, steel and aluminum stamping and assemblies.
Sumitomo Electric Bordnetze opens US$ 17 million plant in Puebla

German auto supplier Sumitomo Electric Bordnetze (SEBN), a subsidiary of Japan-based Sumitomo Electric Industries, opened earlier this week a production facility in Puebla to manufacture harnesses. The plant, located in the municipality of Atlixco, was built in just six months and required a US$ 17.6 million investment to start operations. Company officials revealed this endeavor created 400 direct jobs in its first stage but the figure will triple over the next years if everything goes according as planned. This is SEBN second manufacturing site in Mexico. The company operates another facility in the neighbor state of Tlaxcala.
Meritor plant in Nuevo Leon achieves 10 or fewer defective parts per million milestone

Heavy truck manufacturer PACCAR has designated its supplier Meritor, Inc. as recipient of the 2016 10 PPM Quality Award for work done at the Escobedo, Nuevo Leon plant. The distinction from PACCAR recognizes excellent suppliers that meet or exceed its rigorous, near-perfect standard of 10 or fewer defective parts for every 1 million shipped to the OEM. To be considered for the award, suppliers must also meet performance criteria for on-time delivery and support of PACCAR's operating divisions and its customers. Meritor manufactures front and rear axles as well as drivelines and brakes for PACCAR from its plant in Escobedo.
Rassini's net income up 19% in second quarter

Mexican auto supplier Rassini reported a sales increase of 12% in the second quarter, totaling $ 4,489 million Mexican pesos, around US$ 249.4 million under current exchange rate. For the first half of 2017 the company's sales climbed to $ 9,369 million Mexican pesos or US$ 520.5 million, a 20% hike compared to the result from the first half of 2016, according to its unaudited results. The company's net income totaled $419 million pesos or US$ 23.3 million in the second quarter, up 19%. For the first half of 2017 Rassini posted a net income of $876 million pesos, around US$ 48.6 million, up 3% compared to the same period of the previous year.
Lear sales grow faster than auto industry production in second quarter 2017

Lear Corporation, the global supplier of automotive seating and electrical systems, reported record sales in the second quarter 2017 of US$ 5.1 billion, up 8% from a year ago, up 10% excluding the impact of foreign exchange. The result outpaced worldwide automotive production growth of 3.0% according to IHS Automotive. The company's net income for the second quarter 2017 totaled US$ 312 million, up 10% from US$ 282 million in the prior year, while its core operating earnings reached US$ 439 million with a margin of 8.6%, up from 8.4% a year ago. Lear operates 46 plants with more than 50,000 employees in Mexico.
Faurecia's earnings hike 28% in the first half of 2017

France-based global auto supplier Faurecia performed during the first half of 2017 a solid growth in value-added sales of 8.5% to €8.6 billion, around US$ 10.10 billion according to current exchange rates. The company saw a two-digit increase of 20% in operating income, to €587 million or some US$ 689.14 million, while its net income hiked 28% to €314 million, around US$ 368.64 million. Faurecia is on track to open in Guanajuato its 16th plant in Mexico by the end of year. Two Research & Development centers located in Puebla and Mexico City respectively are also part of the company's footprint in the country.
US reviews allegations that German automakers colluded

U.S. Justice Department officials are looking into allegations that German automakers colluded on technology, strategy and parts to gain an advantage over rivals, reported Bloomberg this week citing as source a person familiar with the matter. There's no indication that the department has opened a formal investigation. The European Commission and Germany's cartel office said days before they have received information about possible collusion and are studying the matter. According to German news outlet Der Spiegel, the biggest German car manufacturers - Daimler, BMW and Volkswagen Group - may have colluded for decades.
