Nippon Express of Mexico's logistics center in Guanajuato begins operations

Nippon Express (NE) of Mexico built its Guanajuato-Apaseo Logistics Center inside the Colinas de Apaseo Industrial Park in Guanajuato. This new center was designed based on NE of Mexico's accumulated knowledge in automotive-related logistics and built especially to accommodate such logistics, allowing the center to provide high value-added services for customers' production plans. The consolidation of the existing locations in Queretaro and Salamanca also generates greater operational efficiency.

Beat launches Beat Tesla service

Beat announced the launch of Beat Tesla, a service that, as its name explains, will allow users to choose whether they want to make their trip in an electric car from the U.S. brand, although it will only be available in certain areas of Mexico City and the State of Mexico. Beat Tesla will use several Tesla Model 3's belonging to the company, and they will be operated by drivers specially trained in the use of these cars and who can also offer an experience more in line with Beat's premium level of services.

Kia manages to position itself as the fourth bestselling brand in Mexico

Kia Motors Mexico closed September with 6,803 units sold in the country, a figure that places it in fourth position with an 8.7% market share, a new achievement for the Korean automaker, which has been the result of the decisions made to reach its objectives towards the end of the year. These results would not be the same without the brand's microsite, Apartatukia.com, which also shows positive results, since it ended September with a record never seen before, since in only 30 days it saw 1,086 vehicles being set aside for purchase.

Toyota seeks to strengthen its supplier base in Mexico to comply with USMCA rules

Toyota will seek to strengthen its supplier base in the country in order to adapt to the new commercial agreement in North America. The rise in regional content due to the USMCA is calling on automotive companies to relocate their suppliers, especially those from Europe and Asia. "Mexico is a strategic market to go to and of course, we will continue to expand our suppliers here. Over the past 20 years we have worked with many of you and we want to work with more of you on long-term relationships and are looking for partners to maintain our competitiveness," said Greg Laskey, vice president of purchasing for Toyota North America.

Vehicle production and exports decline during September

Mexico produced 301,426 light vehicles during September, which meant a decrease of 5.5% when compared to the same month of 2019, mainly affected by the 98% reduction in Ford's assembly; although this translates into its second drop after the reopening of activities due to the COVID-19 pandemic, revealed INEGI. Car exports in September totaled 14 months of consecutive drops, reporting a 13% decrease during the ninth month, with the shipment of 247,126 units, and the cessation of sales of 37,117 vehicles.

Mazda celebrates 15 years of presence in our country

The Japanese brand is celebrating 15 years in our country and 100 years since its foundation. The brand arrived in our country in October 2005 as an independent brand, and nine years later inaugurated one of its most important assembly plants globally in Salamanca, Guanajuato. The brand, directed by Miguel Barbeyto, employs more than 5,000 people directly at its 260-hectare plant in Salamanca, which today produces the Mazda 3 hatchback and sedan, Mazda 2 hatchback and sedan, and the brand's most recent SUV in the Americas, the CX-30.

Heavy vehicles sales face a year of declines

After nine months of 2020, the sale of heavy vehicle units faces a drop of 40.9% in the retail market and 49.6% in the wholesale market, according to figures released by the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT). In the wholesale market, unit sales reached 15,862 units, while a year earlier they had reached 31,492. In the retail market, sales reached 16,266 vehicles, which is far from the 25,619 that were sold in the country during the previous year.

INA adjusts its turnover forecast for 2020 upwards

Due to the increase in demand from the United States, the auto parts industry in Mexico has adjusted its turnover expectations upwards for the end of 2020. The new figure is close to US$77,000 million, against the June forecast, when the reopening of activities occurred, which was US$66,594 million, informed Oscar Albín, president of the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA). Although the estimated production value of US$76,996 million at the end of 2020 would be 21% lower than in 2019, the projected figure is higher than the one estimated a few months ago, when the pandemic impacted productive activity.

Honda sells 4,671 units during September

Honda of Mexico continues recovering with the sale of 4,671 units during the month of September and accumulated sales for the year of 34,233 units. These figures represent a 14.5% drop compared to September 2019, but considering that the industry on average had a 22.8% drop, Honda de Mexico had a growth in its market share, with which it reaffirms its sixth position in sales in the country. Accumulated sales have fallen by 37.1% compared to January-September 2019, although there is a recovery.

SUVs and pickups dominate Mexican production

During the January-September 2020 period, 2,062,533 light vehicles were produced in Mexico, of which 65.41% were SUVs and pickup trucks, or 1,349,213, according to data from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). However, the total production of pickup trucks and SUVs during the first nine months of 2020 showed an annual drop of 26.95% in contrast to the same period of 2019, when 1,847,127 units were produced.
