New North American trade deal makes Mexico more attractive to R&D, says top negotiator

The new USMCA trade agreement will benefit Mexico "if it plays its cards right" because of cost advantages, said Jesus Seade, President-elect's chief negotiator in an interview with Reuters. Although the official acknowledged that the new deal increases protectionism, he said those restrictions, especially on the auto industry, will bring more production and engineering into the region from Asia and Europe. The new deal, he said, "forces companies to do more business in North America, more engineering, a lot of which is done in Germany or Korea. If they are going to do it in North America, where are they going to do it? The cheapest place is Mexico," he said. More details here: bit.ly/2yni7b5
Mexico-made Volkswagen Jetta enters the Brazilian market

The all-new Volkswagen Jetta began to be marketed this month in Brazil, where it will face a tight market in which 14 brands are betting on the compact sedans segment, according to a report by Just-auto.com. The main rivals with which the vehicle manufactured in Puebla will have to compete are the Toyota Corolla, Honda Civic, Chevrolet Cruze and the Ford Focus. Initially, the Jetta will be offered in only two versions: Comfortline for approximately US$29,300 and R-Line with a price tag of US $32,000. The vehicle enters the Brazilian market free of tariffs under a scheme of limited quotas between both countries.
Next-gen Mazda3 to be debuted next month in Los Angeles, the automaker confirms

Japanese automaker Mazda Motor Corporation released a 15-second video showing a glimpse of what is likely the next-generation Mazda3. The ad says the vehicle will make its debut next month, which backs up previous reports that the unveiling will take place at the Los Angeles Auto Show. The vehicle clearly resembles the Kai Concept revealed at the 2017 Tokyo Motor Show. A brief shot of the vehicle's side shows that the door handles match those in the images revealed by AutoExpress back in June, of what it claims to be the production model. It is worth noting that the video shows two vehicles in different colors, red and gray, which suggests that both the sedan version and the hatchback will be unveiled. Fibra Nova to build US$13 million facility in Queretaro for major automotive manufacturer

Industrial Real Estate developer Fibra Nova recently announced it has signed a lease agreement for the construction of a 22,500 square meter facility in Queretaro state capital for a 10-year initial term. The new facility includes 6,000 square meters of office space for software design and 450 parking spaces. Fibra Nova's investment in this project will be of US$13.3 million. The lessee is a major U.S.-based manufacturer of components for the automotive industry listed in the New York Stock Exchange with more than 10,000 employees in Asia, North America and Europe, said Fibra Nova in a statement, but it declined to identify the company.
Mobility ADO places order of 144 Volvo 9800 coaches

Volvo Buses division announced it has received an order for 144 Volvo 9800 coaches from Mobility ADO, one of the largest coach operators in Mexico. The vehicles will be delivered in 2019. The new Volvo coaches will join an already existing fleet of almost 350 Volvo 9800 coaches at Mobility ADO. The company declined to reveal the financial terms of the deal, but considering that last year ADO announced a similar purchase of 200 coaches at a price of MX$1 billion (US$52 million), the new acquisition could be of around US$37 million. The new coaches will be equipped with features such as Collision Warning and Emergency Break and Lane Keeping Support. More details here: bit.ly/2ErBV2K

Antitrust authorities clear Grammer AG's acquisition of Toledo Molding & Die

Germany-based Grammer AG, a manufacturer of interior automotive components announced it has completed the acquisition of the U.S. automotive components supplier TMD Toledo Molding & Die Inc. The US$271 million deal includes a production facility in Celaya, Guanajuato. The transaction has been cleared by the antitrust authorities and all other regulatory bodies; all closing conditions have been fulfilled. The deal is part of Grammer's strategy to expand its market position in North America by acquiring access to additional process and production expertise for interior components. Grammer AG also owns two manufacturing plants in Mexico. More details here: bit.ly/2pSDjkO
