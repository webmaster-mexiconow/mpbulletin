Toyota Mexico sold 3,177 2020 Sienna units

Toyota published that it sold 3,177 2020 Sienna units in their distributors in Mexico during the first half of 2019, which represented a 44.9% increase of the total sales in the segment. The vehicle, which is offered in five different versions within the country: CE, LE, XLE, XLE Leather and Limited, has a 296hp engine with a 263 lb-foot torque, 8 speed transmission with normal driving mode and sport driving mode, as well as VVT-I Dual, ETCS-i, SEFI and D4S technology.
Mexico saw a 13% increase in automotive industry exports

Mexico led in growth among the top 10 automotive product exporters in 2018, according to the World Trade Organization. Mexican goods exports of the automotive industry added up to US$123 billion, a year-to-year increase of 13%. By doing so, Mexico placed itself like the fourth global exporter in this sector, behind the European Union, Japan and the United States. As for the value related only to auto export, Mexico climbed in 2018 from the sixth to the fourth position among the global leaders, placing itself above the United Kingdom and Canada.

Nissan Mexico makes organizational changes

Nissan Mexico recently announced changes to its organizational structure in the direction and finance departments. Rodrigo Centeno was named Senior Marketing Director for Nissan Mexico, after his two year stay in the headquarters in Yokohama, Japan. Lucia Herrmann will take over as Corporate Communication Director for Nissan Mexico, after Herman Morfin left the post to take over the directive of the Nissan Mexico Foundation. Additionally, Diana Torres was named Senior Finance Director for Nissan Mexico; she will be taking over for Alejandro Talonia, who was promoted to Finance Director for Nissan Latin America.

The regularization of chocolate cars is still up in the air

During his morning conference on August 1st, President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador was asked about "chocolate cars" in Northern Mexico and said that it's being discussed whether or not they should be regularized, since there's complaints from vehicle distributors about it being unfair trade. He said that a review on this matter was held in presence of the Minister of Finance and Public Credit, the Minister of Economy and the security cabinet and a course of action is expected to be taken soon.
