Trump, Peña Nieto and Trudeau sign the USMCA trade deal

U.S. President Donald Trump, Mexican President Enrique Peña Nieto and Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau signed the new U.S. Mexico Canada Agreement, or USMCA , in Buenos Aires past Friday, using the G-20 Summit as frame to resolve a trade dispute between the United States and its two closest neighbors. "It is probably the largest trade deal ever made," Trump said of the agreement, adding that all three countries will benefit from the deal. "This has been a battle, and battles sometimes create great friendships." The USMCA, which will be replacing the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA), will require ratification by all three countries' legislatures in order to take effect. Next-gen Mazda3 to be built in Guanajuato, the automaker confirms

Mazda executives confirmed to Mexican media that the production of the next generation Mazda3 will take place in Mexico, although the details are not yet clear. El Economista pointed out that its production will begin in mid-2019, while Motorpasión assured that the first units will roll out of the plant located in Salamanca, Guanajuato, between February and March. For his part, columnist Carlos Mota revealed that the Mexican facility initially will only produce the sedan while the hatchback versions for the North American market will be imported from Japan, as happened with the outgoing generation for several years. BMW CEO confirms Mexico plant will start up with expanded production capacity

Alexander Wehr, BMW CEO for Latin America and the Caribbean, confirmed to local media in San Luis Potosi that the assembly plant will begin operations with a production capacity of 175,000 vehicles per year, not 150,000 units as originally planned, while its initial staff will be of 2,000 people instead of the 1,500 previously stated. Such figures were first reported last September, when managers of the plant announced a job fair in the state capital to recruit 500 additional people since, according to the company at the time, the facility would start operations with a greater capacity. More details here: bit.ly/2zDNDCz
The state of Chihuahua develops industrial corridor to attract electric vehicles manufacturers

Authorities of the state of Chihuahua visited the headquarters of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry in Mexico City to publicize the development of an industrial corridor with which they seek to attract companies interested in producing electric cars in that state, which borders Texas and New Mexico. For this project, said the delegation headed by Governor Javier Corral, there is a large area near Ciudad Juarez with train tracks, utilities and, due to its location in a free zone, it features certain tax exemptions. Officials in Chihuahua said the project has already generated interest from companies based in China, Japan and the United Kingdom, although there are still no formal negotiations. More details here: bit.ly/2Qv8Gkh
Continental opens training center in San Luis Potosi

German-based manufacturer Continental recently inaugurated the Continental Training Center (CTC), located in its San Luis Potosi plant where the company allocated an investment of US$600,000. This campus is aimed at preparing its current and future employees in specific technical skills such as pneumatics, electronics, hydraulics, robotics and automation, among others. The CTC covers an area of approximately 280 square meters and is equipped with a management simulation system, as well as pneumatic simulators, tables for electronic courses, numerical control simulators, robots, control cabinets, training rooms, as well as collaboration spaces focused on quality issues and staff development. Auto parts industry expects US$2-billion worth of Chinese investments in 2019

The new rules of origin of the USMCA, coupled with the trade disputes in the United States, will cause Mexico to receive about 30 Chinese auto parts plants next year that together will invest nearly US$2 billion, assured Óscar Albín Santos, President of the National Auto Parts Industry. According to the official, Nuevo Leon, Coahuila and Chihuahua will be the main destinations for these investments, due to their proximity to the U.S. market. "There is a strong interest from Chinese companies to come to manufacture in Mexico to supply the North American market from here. For the coming year we are foreseeing that, in the beginning, some 30 companies will enter with an investment of at least 2 billion dollars," said the official to Reforma paper.
GM San Luis Potosi plant to shut down for two weeks in December

General Motors announced that its plant in San Luis Potosi will shut down operations for two weeks during December. These measures are intended to perform maintenance tasks and are not related to the closure of five plants in North America announced by the company last week, said GM Communication and Public Relations Manager in Mexico, Kathia Muñoz Peña, via a statement. "There will be two technical stoppages: from December 9 to December 12 for the assembly plant, and from December 24 to January 1 for both the assembly and transmission plants, due to major maintenance issues scheduled since the beginning of the year," she said. VW Golf family makes it to Car and Driver ‘10Best' list for the 13th year in a row

Car and Driver magazine has named the Volkswagen e-Golf, Golf, Golf Alltrack, Golf GTI, Golf SportWagen and Golf R to its 2019 "10Best" list. This marks the 13th consecutive "10Best" award for a Golf family model. "We love all Golfs, and how could we not when the basic package is this good?" noted the editors of Car and Driver in the article. "The lineup's broadening range of talents brings the basic car's inherent goodness into clear relief. Without its brilliant packaging, chassis integrity, and clarity of purpose, none of the many variants could do what they do so well." BMW now considers building a plant in the US for engines, transmissions

Just a few days after BMW officials in Mexico revealed that the German premium automaker would consider to build its engines in the country, global CEO Harald Krueger said the company now mulls opening a second U.S. manufacturing plant that could produce those engines, but also transmissions. Interviewed at the Los Angeles Auto Show, Krueger said BMW is considering changes to U.S. operations as sales in the region grow. "We're at the range where you could think about a second location" in the United States, he said as reported by Reuters, adding that such a factory would provide a natural currency hedge. Ford cuts work shifts at two US plants, but no layoffs are expected

Ford Motor Co. announced it will eliminate shifts at assembly plants in Michigan and Kentucky but give all affected workers jobs at other locations nearby. About 500 workers will move from the Louisville Assembly Plant, which makes the Ford Escape and Lincoln MKC crossovers, to instead make Super Duty pickups and full-size SUVs at the Kentucky Truck Plant, also in Louisville. The Escape plant will go from three shifts to two in the spring. Around the same time, Ford's plant in Flat Rock, Mich., which makes the Mustang and Lincoln Continental southwest of Detroit, will go from two daily shifts to one. Most of those workers will be transferred to the Livonia Transmission Plant, about 30 miles away, and to other Ford facilities.
