BMW gets loan from three German banks for Mexican plant

BMW has received a loan from three mayor German banks for the construction of a plant in central Mexico. Neither the banks nor the automaker disclosed the amount of the credit. The financing has a tenor of 10 years. KfW Ipex-Bank, DZ Bank and BayernLB each contributed an equal share. Rassini to supply brakes for sixth-generation Ford Mustang

A new Ford contract has enabled Mexican brake and suspension supplier Rassini to increase 10% jobs at a factory near Flint, Michigan. The company will provide 380mm diameter high carbon brake rotors for the sixth-generation Mustang, which will offer precise tolerance to ensure ultimate performance while increasing overall longevity of the parts.
Maflow Mexico will provide parts for Volkswagen's new full-size SUV

Maflow Mexico, a subsidiary of Poland's largest auto parts manufacturer, Boryszew SA, just started supplying car parts for the Atlas, the new full-size SUV from Volkswagen to be released on Spring 2017. Maflow's auto pipes production started at the new 4,712-square-meter greenfield plant located in Silao, Guanajuato, where the company invested US$ 13.8 million to manufacture air conditioning parts and assemblies. The facility also supplies tubing and assemblies for the Audi Q5 model, which is built at an assembly plant 300 miles south.
Visteon taps former Daimler executive to lead its driver assistance development

Michigan-based Visteon Corp. appointed Matthias Schulze of Daimler AG to lead its next-generation advanced driver assistance system (ADAS) development. Schulze, 54, worked for Daimler for more than 20 years, most recently leading the group research and advanced engineering department. The unit deals with autonomous driving, vehicular communication and environment perception efforts.
Mercedes-Benz beats in 11 months last year's total sales figure

Mercedes-Benz increased its unit sales during November by 12.7% to 182,602 vehicles, thus achieving its 45th consecutive record month. With more than 1,893,000 vehicles delivered since the beginning of the year -an increase by 11.8%- the three-pointed star already surpassed its unit sales of full-year 2015 in the first eleven months of this year. Mexican sales grew 40% during last month.
Polymer compounds supplier Hexpol invests US$ 9.2 million at Queretaro facility

As a result of business growth in the automotive industry, polymer compounds supplier Hexpol A.G. invested US$ 9.2 million in a 135-liter rubber mixing line, adding about 12,000 metric tons of capacity to its Queretaro facility, which now stands at 53,000 metric tons per year. Hexpol also operates a site in Aguascalientes, which has three rubber mixing lines and 27,000 metric tons of capacity per year.
Ineos Styrolution adds specialty resin capacity in Altamira

Frankfurt-based materials supplier Ineos Styrolution is increasing specialty resin production capacity at its plant in Altamira, Mexico, to support growing demand in the automotive sector. The company will add 20,000 metric tons of annual capacity for ABS and ASA copolymer resins, increasing the firm's total capacity in Altamira to 180,000 metric tons.
Korean-Japanese joint-venture SPM Auto Parts opens plant in Escobedo

Korean-Japanese joint-venture SPM Auto Parts opened a new production plant at Vynmsa industrial park in Escobedo, Nuevo Leon. Korean firms Sunil dyfas and Pung Kang, along with Japanese Mizuno, invested US$ 18 million and hired 200 workers to manufacture 13 million pieces a month such as bolts, screws, fasteners and other products in a 7,000 square feet facility. SPM will supply to automotive clients all over Mexico, including Kia, Toyota, Nissan, Mazda, Honda and General Motors.
Bosch's footprint will continue to grow in Juarez next year

The German company Robert Bosch will continue to expand its footprint in Juarez at a 10% annual rate, said Friedrich Dreier, technical plant manager for Bosch in this city. The workforce at Bosch Juarez will also increase and by the end of 2017, the company forecasted 500 new jobs to a total staff of 4,500. Recently, Bosch Juarez started operations in its 203 building to manufacture the sensors that allow airbags to open when needed. These sensors will be supplied to Ford, Chrysler and Nissan, the company informed.
Monterrey-based Nemak receives product, process innovation awards

Nemak, S.A.B. de C.V., a provider of innovative light weighting solutions for the global automotive industry, has received double honors from the National Technology and Innovation Award (PNTi), which is overseen by Mexico's Economy Ministry and National Council of Science and Technology (Conacyt). PNTi focuses on supporting the development of businesses and other organizations through the recognition, promotion, and facilitation of successful technology and innovation management processes. Nemak participated in the categories of Process Innovation and Product Innovation, winning both of them.
