KIA Motors Mexico registers a 4.8% growth in June

KIA Motors Mexico reported the sale of 7,704 units in June, which showed a 4.8% growth in comparison with the same month of 2018, and granted them a 7.4% participation share in market participation. The most requested model in June was Rio, which sold 2,710 units. Forte came in second place, with a sale of 1,800 units; SUV Sportage followed with 1,259 units; and following it came Soul with 681 units and Sedona with 527.
Nissan Mexico adapts Versa for users with motor disabilities

Nissan released to the Mexican market the first applied adaptation to its vehicles, so that people that have motor disabilities can drive them. Roberto Lerma, subdirector of Marketing for the company, said that as part of their Nissan GO plan, two additional devices for people who can't use their legs will be offered. The first device is a hand set, which will make it so that with a lever the user can use the accelerator and break, while the steering wheel has other controls that aid in the driving of the car. The second one is on the co-pilot's side and it allows the seat to turn 90 degrees, so that a person with a disability would be able to get in directly from their wheelchair.
Mercedes-Benz Buses, 25 years creating mobility solutions for Mexico

Mobility in Mexican cities has transformed in the last 25 years, because of these technologies, roads and urbanism have caused public transportation to transform. In that sense, Mercedes-Benz Buses has created buses especially for Mexico and its streets. To design, modify and make special chassis for Mexico, the German manufacturer, through a specialized team of engineers, urban planners and mechanics, analyzed mobility behaviors and user behaviors, transforming this data and findings into passenger transport units. It's like this that in the 25 years that Mercedes-Benz Buses has been in Mexico, it has released distinct passenger vehicles to the market.
Nissan will renew 60% of its product portfolio

To maintain sales leadership in Mexico, Nissan Mexico will renew 60% of its product portfolio in the next two years, which will include model changes, innovations, and the release of vehicles with the highest demand in the domestic market, said Roberto Lerma, subdirector of Marketing of the Japanese brand. Nissan possesses 20.6% participation of the total sale of vehicles in Mexico, positioning itself as the market leader, and it's expected to increase the brand's presence. "To do that, we have worked closely with Nissan Mexico and with Japan to see about strategies on facilities for costumers, with portfolio and credit accessibility," said the directive.
Auto Transportes Alanis acquires 40 Kenworth tractor units

A total of 40 model T680 tractor units were acquired by Auto Transportes Alanis, which specializes in the movement of domestic cargo, door to door import-export and transfer in the dealership Kenworth del Rio Bravo, which is why the delivery ceremony took place in the company's headquarters in Nuevo Laredo, Tamaulipas. With the acquired units, the company incorporates a 440 unit fleet, of which 80% is Kenworth.
BP starts operation in five service stations in Chihuahua

BP announced the beginning of operations in Chihuahua with the initial opening of five service stations in the municipalities of Camargo, Delicias and Ojinaga, in which it offers high-quality services and guarantees the complete dispatch of fuel. As in every service station that the company has throughout the country, clients in Chihuahua will be able to load fuel unique to BP with ACTIVE technology, which with continual use, helps protect the motor of harmful deposits, thus reducing its wear and giving it the performance that the client expects.
Neohyundai produces their 1,000 unit in Queretaro

Neohyundai Mexico assembled their 1,000 unit in their plant located in El Marques, Queretaro, since its initial opening in 2016 with an initial investment of US$14 million. Gino Saer, general director of the assembly plant, commemorated this achievement by revealing the 1,000 unit plaque (same that is located inside the vehicle) and talked about the importance of the results generated by the whole Neohyundai Mexico team in moments that involve new challenges and opportunities in the market. "The 1,000 unit is synonymous with the commitment that Hyundai's Trucks and Buses has with Mexico and its people," informed the company in a press release.
Financing for used cars grew from January to May

From January to May of this year 362,648 vehicles were financed, 8.1% less (32,021 vehicles) in comparison with the same period of 2018. Of that total, 320,443 were new units and 42,205 were used units. This last segment showed a growth of 5.9% in comparison with the same period of 2018, when 39,872 units were sold, according to the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). As far as vehicle financing through an institution, 65% was done through brand's financial arms, 30.2% through banking and 4.2% through auto financing.
Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico opens branch in Zacatecas

In presence of the secretary of Economy of Zacatecas, Carlos Barcenas, Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico, together with Euro Centro Camionero, opened a modern Authorized Center of Spare Parts and Service in Zacatecas, with which they reaffirm their presence in the entity, after four years of entering this market. Moreover, for the development of their infrastructure, the center required a US$576,850 investment and became a source of jobs in the city by generating 25 direct jobs, 15 remote ones and 120 indirect ones.
Queretaro's automotive sector will create more than 2,500 jobs during the second semester of 2019

The automotive and auto parts sector in Queretaro would be generating more than 2,500 new jobs in the second semester of this year, according to preliminary figures of Automotive Cluster of Queretaro (CAQ). The president of the corporate body, Renato Villaseñor Mendoza, said that, while there are adjustments in the automotive industry worldwide, the sector in the entity is looking for alternatives to maintain competiveness and productivity of the organizations. Which is why the creation of future new job openings can be considered.
