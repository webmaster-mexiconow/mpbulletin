Kyungshin Cable invests US$65 million in Durango and La Laguna

Propelled by the USMCA and the growing demand of its product, Kyungshin Cable expanded its fabric in Durango and plans to open another in La Laguna, with an investment of US$65 million. Kyungshin Cable, South Korean company which produces cable harnesses for the automotive industry, expanded the capacity of its production plant in Durango and doubled the labor force to 400 employees. This happened because the company is transferring production from South Korea to Mexico to meet the USMCA's new requirements.

Queretaro universities boost automotive innovation

The Council for Science and Technology of the State of Queretaro (COCYTEQ) and the Polytechnic University of Queretaro (UPQ) will support the development of six projects for the automotive industry. Martha Elena Soto Obregon, dean of UPQ, revealed that the selected projects will be developed by researchers from the institution as well as students from different engineering degrees. The projects will be led by César Isaza Bohórquez, Marcos Zea Pérez, Patricia Torres Falcón, Brenda Dublan Barragánen and Enriqueta Ortiz Moctezuma.

As part of the organization's strengthening and transformation plan and answering to talent development, Nissan announced the naming of Brenda Rodriguez as Aftersales and Supply Chain Management director of Nissan Mexico. Mercado Libre will give certification courses in digital sales for vehicle sellers

Mercado Libre will give certification courses in digital sales for car sellers affiliated to the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), with which it pretends to impact more than 5,000 people dedicated to this sector. Directors and experts from the company in Mexico will show during the training changes in trends, basic marketing concepts, techniques and available resources, as well as better professional practices in the industry, so that they can track their e-commerce strategies, implement them and follow-up.

MPA will invest US$200 million in the expansion of its plant in Tijuana

The company Motor Car Parts of Mexico (MPA) leader in automotive components, will invest US$200 million in the expansion of its Tijuana plants, announced Mario Escobedo, minister of Sustainable Economy and Tourism (SEST). In a gathering with executives from the corporation, they explained that the investment is in its final stages, with an estimated job creation superior to 300 jobs, additional to the 3,700 already existing ones within the company.
