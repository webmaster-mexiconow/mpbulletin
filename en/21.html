Bulletin #21
26 January, 2017
Nissan dismisses closing Mexican plants

Nissan's CEO Carlos Ghosn said that automakers would have to "adapt" now that newly sworn-in U.S. President Donald Trump's vowed to renegotiate the North American Free Trade Agreement, but he dismissed the idea that the company would close its Mexico plants, saying the company also exports to Canada and South America from there.
Hyundai's plans to build the Accent in Mexico remain ahead

Hyundai's plans to build the Accent small car at Kia's factory in Nuevo Leon remain ahead, but the company stays alert to how new trade policies develop under Donald Trump's presidency, two people familiar with the matter have told Reuters in recent days. It would be the first Hyundai car to be made in Mexico. The company plans to start production in July at a pace of 20,000 units a year.
Trump talks to Detroit Big 3, pushes for new American plants

During a meeting at the White House, U.S. President Donald Trump urged the chief executives of the Big Three U.S. automakers to build more cars in the country, pressing his pledge to bring jobs to America and discourage the car industry from investing in Mexico. In return, the new Republican president vowed to cut regulations and taxes to make it more attractive for businesses to operate in the United States.
GM cuts less jobs than expected at Chevy Cruze assembly site

General Motors started cutting-off workers at its Lordstown, Ohio, facility, which builds the Chevy Cruze sedan. Originally, 1,202 hourly workers were expected to be laid-off, but the figure ended up in less than 900. The facility is idled to prepare for the transition to two shifts from the three shifts the plant had since it started production of the Cruze in 2010. The company builds a Hatchback version in Ramos Arizpe, Mexico.
Toyota to add 400 jobs at Indiana facility

Toyota Motor said it would add 400 jobs to build more SUVs at an Indiana plant, highlighting its expansion plans just as U.S. President Donald Trump calls on manufacturers to build more cars in the country. The Japanese automaker said the jobs were part of a US$ 600 million upgrade of its plant in Princeton, and were included in its plans announced earlier this month to invest US$ 10 billion in its U.S. operations over the next five years.
Mexico-made Nissan Kicks gets SUV of the Year award

Compact SUV Nissan Kicks, the only vehicle made in Mexico with 90% of national contents, was named the "2017 SUV of the Year" by FIPA, a group made up by specialized automotive journalists who represent the entire American continent. According to FIPA, qualities that helped Kicks take victory include innovative technology, safety features, environmental efficiency, relevance in the markets where it's sold, and projected sales volume.
Automated welding solutions supplier starts operations in Queretaro

AWL-Techniek, a Dutch producer of automated welding machines, announced its Mexican branch, located in Queretaro, is now operational and looking to develop opportunities to serve the demanding automotive industry. "The opportunities and possibilities are there, and we are ready for a new chapter in our company's history," said Van 't Hof, CEO of AWL.
Accuride manufactures new advanced aluminum alloy wheel in Monterrey

Accuride introduced its new Quantum 99 aluminum alloy, which the company claims to be the strongest, most advanced alloy in the commercial vehicle industry. The first product to incorporate the Quantum 99 alloy will be its most popular aluminum wheel -the standard 22.5 x 8.25 model- manufactured in Monterrey, Mexico. According to the company, Quantum 99 is 20% stronger than Accuride's previous alloy, enabling to reduce the weight of its most popular standard lightweight aluminum wheel by 11%.
Norma Group invests research and testing resources in Mexico

Norma Group announced investments to expand its research and testing resources in laboratories for lighter exhaust, powertrain and electric vehicle parts. To achieve this goal, the company currently runs a components development program at facilities located in Auburn Hills and St. Clair, Michigan, as well as Apocada, Nuevo Leon, where has a test stand for urea transport systems for diesel engine.
Toyota production in North America exceeds 2 million again in 2016

In 2016, Toyota achieved another record year of North American vehicle production with 2,124,608 vehicles built at its North American vehicle assembly plants. Production volume was up 4.4% over the previous year total of 2,035,028 vehicles. Toyota produces 13 models in North America including: Avalon, Camry, Corolla, Highlander, RAV4, Sequoia, Sienna, Tacoma, Tundra, Lexus RX 350, Lexus ES 350, Yaris and Yaris iA. The company directly employs more than 44,000 people.
