2020 Jaguar XE arrives to Mexico

Jaguar XE has evolved in a notable manner. Its 2020 model presents a striking design, both inside and out, full of architecture and mechanical characteristics that strengthen its sporty and dynamic character, as well as a bigger technological equipment. Aluminum makes up 75% of the new XE’s bodywork. This minimizes fuel consumption and CO2 emissions, without affecting its durability and reliability. The weight saving technology allows it to reach a maximum speed of up to 250 km/hr.
Daimler Trucks opens two E-CAS, one in Mexico City and one in Tijuana

The eighth and ninth Applied Connectivity in Solutions Stations (E-CAS) of Freightliner, in Mexico City and Tijuana were inaugurated days apart. With this Daimler Trucks extends to carriers in key regions, the benefits of strategic consulting, through the analysis of comprehensive fleet and unit indicators for business decision making. Among the main services offered in E-CAS are fleet and unit performance, action plans for poor performance, intelligent programming for preventive maintenance and immediate action to fault codes.
Kia Motors Mexico is able to sell 8,045 vehicles in August

Kia Motors Mexico keeps up its performance by placing 8,045 vehicles, with which it asserts itself in fifth place in national sales, with a 7.4% market participation; being the only brand in the top five with an accumulated growth of 2.9%. These results were achieved with Kia Rio in first place. The second model in the list is Kia Forte, with 1,701 sold vehicles, followed by Sportage with 1,412 units. Fourth place in the internal ranking is Soul, with 798 cars and Sorento rounds up the top five with 568 sold units.
BTMS Technology inaugurates plant in Coahuila for which a US$100 million investment was made

With a US$100 million, the Ramos Arizipe Plant of BTMS Technology was inaugurated in an event headed by the minister of Government of Coahuila, Jose Maria Fraustro Siller. The company produces escape turbochargers for automobiles, commercial vehicles and off-highway applications. “These new investments, like BTMS’s, bring not only job generation, but also encourage the specialization of labor, productivity increase and the creation of better work conditions,” declared Siller, in representation of governor Miguel Riquelme.
Investments for US$556 million from Chinese companies will reach Jalisco

Jalisco secured investments for US$556 million from Chinese companies, mainly from ones that provide for the electric automotive industry, the telecommunications sector and software development. At the time of his return from China, where he made an investment promotion trip, the Minister of Economic Development (Sedeco) of Jalisco, Ernesto Sánchez Proal, said that, with these providers, Jalisco will embed itself in the electric automotive industry. The minister didn’t disclose the name of the companies investing, which asked for confidentiality until their respective representatives make the announcements.
Toyota Mexico sold a total of 9,106 vehicles in August

With the total sales of 9,106 new vehicles through their distributor network in the country this August, Toyota Motor Sales of Mexico reported the best month since their start of their operations. This represents a 5.5% growth when compared to the same month of 2018. With this, the Japanese brand’s vehicles positions themselves in the fourth spot of the national automotive market, by capturing 7.9% participation in the annual aggregate.
Kia Plant in Nuevo Leon celebrates its third anniversary

Three years ago, Kia Motors Mexico officially inaugurated its production plant in Pesqueira, Nuevo Leon. In this short time, the Kia production center accumulated a series of achievements that elevate Mexican manufacturing quality worldwide. Since the start of operations in 2016 and to date, there’s been more than 817,000 units produced in the plant for their distribution in more than 50 countries. From the production, 62.5% corresponds to the compact model Forte sedan and 37.5% to the subcompact Kia Rio.
BMW Group and VECSA Group inaugurate new dealership in Veracruz

BMW Group renews its presence in Veracruz State with the inauguration of a new sales point from VECSA Group for the brands BMW, MINI and BMW Motorrad, located in Punta Tiburon Riviera Veracruzana. The renewal of their installations has the objective of being close to the region’s clients and offering Premium service that distinguishes the Distributor Network of BMW Group worldwide.
Nissan plant in Aguascalientes will be responsible for the assembly of the 2020 Versa

With the investment of US$278 million, Nissan Mexicana started the global production of the 2020 Versa, a redesigned unit with added technology and security, thus distinguishing it from the previous version, with the bet that it’ll keep being the most sold vehicle in Mexico and the world’s favorite sedan, affirmed Jose Roman, CEO of the Japanese automaker in Mexico. Thus far, Aguascalientes 1 will be the only plant in which the Versa 2020 will be produced, to later include the launch in Thailand and India.
Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico inaugurates third training center in Veracruz

Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexcio (DVCM) with its brands Mercedes-Benz Buses and Freightliner Trucks, inaugurated their third training center, in the gulf region housed by the distributor Gruver Trucks Veracruz. In this Regional Training Center technicians and engineers that work for DVCM distributors in the cargo trucks and buses areas will be trained. This will allow distributors and clients to have a nearby training center, thus avoiding wasting time and resources in the movement of personnel.
