Alexandre Augusto Nogueira is the new CEO for Daimler Buses Mexico

Mercedes-Benz Buses announced the arrival of Alexandre Augusto Nogueira to fill the CEO position for Daimler Buses Mexico. Alexandre Augusto Nogueira is the current Planning and Industrial Engineering Director for Chasis Bus in Mercedes-Benz do Brasil. He will act as CEO for Daimler Buses Mexico starting on September first of this year. "With Alexandre Nogueira we have obtained a leader with great experience, who will continue the development and strategic growth of our bus business in Mexico," said Daimler Buses.
Volkswagen sold 80,101 units in January-July 2019

Volkswagen sold 80,101 vehicles in the January-July 2019 period, which shows a decrease of 8.6% when compared to the figure given by AMDA during the same period of 2018. It should be pointed out that when you gather the sales of the brands Audi, Bentley, Porsche and SEAT, Volkswagen Group Mexico managed to sell 102,259 units of its different models during January-July 2019, which represented 7.2% less than in a similar period of last year.
Waldaschaff Automotive opens plant in Leon

This is the first Waldaschaff Automotive plant, which is located in the VYMSA Industrial Park, it had an investment of US$20 million and will generate 250 new jobs; they provide for BMW and Audi in Mexico. This plant of the German company Waldaschaff Automotive is dedicated to the manufacturing of structures with a high degree of rigidity and very low weight for premium vehicles and has a 6,000 square meters area.
KIA Mexico sees a 1.4% increase in July sales

The Korean automaker saw a 1.4% growth in sales, in relation to the same period of last year. With a total of 7,615 sold units, KIA Motors Mexico has another successful month, achieving a 7.4% market participation, which cements its place in fifth place in the national market. However, the increase hasn't been monthly only, since with this figure they reach 54,717 sold units so far this year, which translates to a 3.3% increase in relation to the same period of 2018, when they sold 52,976 units.
Kamax opens plant in Leon

Kamax of Mexico opened its first plant in Mexico, the complex is located at Leon Hills Industrial Park; bolts and fasteners for critical applications in automotive safety will be produced there. Kamax is a family group from Germany, and it's also a leader in the manufacturing of high resistance fastening auto parts with high innovation, quality and profitability standards. Said complex will have potential clients like Audi, BMW, Volkswagen, Nissan, Ford, among others.
Automotive industry in Jalisco expects moderate growth for the rest of 2019

Despite the 1% decrease in automobile production in Mexico during July, the automotive provider sector in Jalisco predicts moderate growth for the closing of 2019 and bigger opportunities for the next years. The Automotive Cluster of the State of Jalisco, Ruben Resendiz Perez, said that once the United States–Mexico–Canada Agreement is ratified by the three countries, provider development will be imminent due to the specifications of the agreement regarding regional content.
Giant Motors opens JAC Store in Queretaro

Giant Motors, alongside Grupo Zapata, opened a new JAC Store in Queretaro, thus making that 29 JAC Stores, which allow an almost total coverage in terms of sales and service within México. In this way, JAC México moves forward with its goal in expanding its presence to 38 JAC Stores by the end of 2019 with the best automotive distributor groups, guaranteeing total coverage and attention to every JAC user.
JFE Shoji Steel Service Center Bajio will invest US$50 million

The ground breaking ceremony for JFE Shoji Steel Service Center Bajio in the industrial park FIPASI was held recently. Silao's mayor, Jose Antonio Trejo Valdepeña, participated in this invent to welcome the Japanese investments that keep adding to Silao's development. JFE Shoji Steel will supply to the demand of steel sheets for cars, with high-resistance steel and panels for the automotive industry. A US$50 million investment is foreseen and operations will begin in October 2020.
Agility Tooling of Martinrea will invest US$22 million in Queretaro

Agility Tooling of Martinrea, the company's third plant started operations in Queretaro, and will manufacture steel auto parts injected to low or high pressure. The company of Canadian equity made an initial investment of US$8.1 million and will grow to US$22 million, and will give generate 190 jobs. This plant is dedicated to the manufacturing of steel auto parts injected to low or high pressure, its main products are motor housing, transmission housing, structural pieces and Knuckles.
Vesuvius invests US$20 million in Coahuila

The British company Vesuvius arrived to Coahuila's southwest where it's investing US$20 million and will generate 200 jobs. The company based in the United Kingdom is expanding its operations in Mexico and is installing its subsidiary Foundry Services Limited (FOSECO) in the 360 Industrial Park, located on the federal highway Saltillo-Monterrey 54, in the municipality of Ramos Arizpe. The British company is currently a provider for automakers like KIA, Audi, Mercedes-Benz, BMW and Nissan.
