Bulletin #14
2 January, 2017
All of GM plants in Mexico are now landfill-free facilities

General Motors achieved a record number of landfill-free facilities in 2016. The automaker now operates 152 global facilities that recycle, reuse or convert to energy all waste from daily operations, including 100 manufacturing sites and 52 non-manufacturing sites. Adding its Toluca foundry, which melts and molds metal for auto components, to the list this year means that none of GM's manufacturing operations in Mexico send waste to landfills.
Honda in talks with Google unit to develop autonomous cars

Honda Motor said it is talking to Waymo, the autonomous driving unit of Google parent Alphabet Inc., to try to strike a deal that would put its self-driving technology into some of the Japanese automaker's cars. Both companies stressed that the talks are about research, rather than full production vehicles, at this point. If all goes well, Honda may provide Waymo with vehicles that are modified to run the self-driving system, and those cars would join the existing Waymo fleet currently being tested in four U.S. cities.
VW worldwide deliveries rose by 7.9% in November

Worldwide deliveries by Volkswagen rose by 7.9% to 899,400 units in November. The company delivered 9.38 million new vehicles from January to November 2016, an increase of 3.1%. During last month, deliveries in the U.S. and Mexico contributed decisively to the positive trend, with 16.5% and 7.7% increases respectively. From January through November, deliveries in Mexico rose 13.8%.
New features on Chevy Trax to be revealed at Detroit Auto Show

General Motors will reveal some changes for the built-in-San Luis Potosi Chevy Trax SUV next month at the North American International Auto Show (NAIAS) in Detroit The Trax enters 2017 with a fresh look, turbocharged performance and 10 standard air bags, among other features. Deliveries of the compact SUV from January to November in the U.S. market have climbed 23% to 71,009 units.
Hyundai fires its U.S. CEO allegedly for failing to meet sales targets

Korean automaker Hyundai has fired its U.S. CEO, Dave Zuchowski, reportedly for failing to meet internal sales targets, as the company's car-heavy lineup struggles in a market currently dominated by sport-utility vehicles, crossovers and pickup trucks. Jerry Flannery, who was serving as executive vice president and general counsel, will take over temporarily as CEO of the division, known as Hyundai Motor America.
Ford issues safety recall in North America for its 2017 Super Duty pickup

Ford is issuing a safety recall for approximately 8,069 2017 Ford Super Duty to inspect and install a fuel tank strap reinforcement. In the affected vehicles, over time, a missing reinforcement could lead to a fuel tank strap separating from the frame, resulting in the fuel tank moving out of position and potentially contacting the ground, increasing the risk of a fuel leak and consequently of fire. Ford is not aware of any fires, accidents or injuries associated with this issue. Affected vehicles include certain 2017 Ford Super Duty built at Kentucky Truck Plant, from August 10, 2016 to September 17.
Kia sales grow 143% during November in Mexican market

Kia Motors saw its sales in Mexico more than double in November. The Korean automaker sold 5,460 vehicles in November, a 143 percent increase from the same month a year earlier. In the first 11 months of this year, Kia shipped a cumulative 52,082 units, more than five times the 8,906 vehicles sold during the same period last year. The Sportage SUV was the top-selling brand with sales of 16,909 units in the January-November period, followed by the Rio subcompact sedan with 11,356.
Auto parts manufacturer GKN Driveline to expand 4 plants in N.C.

GKN Driveline confirmed plans to expand four plants in Sanford, Maiden, Mebane and Timberlake, North Carolina, after state economic development leaders approved two incentive grants worth more than US$ 3 million over the next 12 years. The company will invest US$ 179 million in the expansion projects across the state to add a total of 302 jobs. GKN Driveline considered locating those jobs in Ohio, Sweden, Japan, Brazil and Mexico before turning to North Carolina. BMW, Maserati, Ford, Chrysler and Audi are among its clients.
Tsubaki choses Massachusetts over Tennessee, Mexico for expansion

Japan-based automotive parts manufacturer Tsubaki is constructing a 100,000-square-foot addition to its manufacturing plant at the Westover Industrial Park located in Chicopee, Massachusetts. The US$ 12 million expansion will add a new production line that will create 35 jobs. To entice Tsubaki to expand, the city approved a 10-year property tax abatement worth almost US$ 250,000 and the state approved US$ 525,000 in tax credits. The Japanese-headquartered company had looked at other sites in Tennessee and Mexico for the new production line.
Pinal County plans tax to fund US$ 35.8 million land deal for Lucid Motors

The Pinal County Board of Supervisors will hold a meeting next month to consider raising taxes to fund a multimillion-dollar purchase of land for would-be electric-car maker Lucid Motors. The estimated financing cost for the land is US$ 31.8 million in principal, plus US$ 4 million in interest. Besides the Pinal land deal, Lucid Motors will attempt to collect up to US$ 46.5 million over the next five years in various subsidies offered by the state.
