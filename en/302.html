Continental Nogales Plant receives "Sonora Environmental" certificate

Continental Nogales Plant obtained the "Sonora Environmental, Responsibility, Regulation and Certification" given by the Environmental Attorney of the State of Sonora. This certification was given to the Nogales Continental plant in recognition of its efforts as environmental advocate, for its actions in favor of the environment and for complying with the state'ws normativity indexes. The Continental Nogales Plant is dedicated to the manufacturing of connectivity and infotainment devices as well as the making of security devices for the automotive industry, and currently houses 2,400 employees.

Yazaki invests US$3 million in Chihuahua

With an investment of US$3 million, Japanese automotive company Yazaki inaugurated a new design area, which allowed for the generation of 27 new jobs in the state's capital as informed by the head of Economic and Touristic Development, María Angélica Granados Trespalacios. In this sense, the municipal official added that these 27 persons received intensive training in both the United States and Chihuahua.

Nissan Mexicana announces appointments in its Corporate Communication team

Nissan announced a series of appointments in its Corporate Communications team to strengthen the brand's operations in the country. Martha Diaz Serrano, previous subdirector of Corporate Communication for Nissan Latin America, is now subdirector of Corporate Communication for Nissan Mexicana. At the same time, Israel Aguilar Limas, is stepping in as senior manager for Nissan Mexicana's Corporate Communication team. Martha and Israel will report to Luciana Herrmann, director of Nissan Mexicana's Corporate Communication.

Valeo begins expansion of its Tamaulipas plant

Valeo, a French company with presence in Mexico since 1990, and that has 10 plants in the country, began the expansion of its plant in Rio Bravo, Tamaulipas, where automotive switches and sensors are made. The expansion consists of more than 23,000 square meters in diverse areas within the manufacturing complex and it's projected to generate 624 new jobs to add up to almost 2,000 jobs generated by the company in Rio Bravo.

FCA Mexico sells 4,689 units in January

FCA Mexico informed that in January of this year it sold 4,689 units. By brand, RAM sold 1,822 units, an increase of 17% when compared to January of 2019. On the other hand, Dodge sold 1,262 units in January. Jeep commercialized 928 units; while Fiat reported the sales of 616 units for the year's first month. Chrysler sold 51 units, a growth of 6% when compared to January of 2019. Finally, Alfa Romeo registered the sale of 10 units, an increase of 43% when compared to January of 2019.

Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles made changes to its executive team

Giovanni Juarez Suarez announced that he'll be leaving Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles starting February 29. The former director will instead be responsible for MAN Truck & Bus Mexico's commercial area, which belongs to Volkswagen's Trucks and Buses division. In his place, Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles will be led by Luis Rojas, industrial engineer from the Ibero-American University of Puebla and who began working at Volkswagen Group in 1998, by working in Volkswagen of Mexico in the after-sales area.

Nidec will invest US$450 million in Mexico

Nidec, Japanese engine supplier, will invest US$1.8 billion to produce electric vehicle motors in Poland, China and Mexico, with the goal of increasing its production capacity to 10 million units. It's expected for Mexico to receive an investment of approximately US$450 million. The plant will have a maximum capacity of 2.4 million units a year and will attend to the orders from European and American automakers. The production is expected to begin around 2023 in Mexico.

104,832 light vehicles were sold in January

During January 2020 total sales for light vehicles reached 104,832 units, which meant a fall of 6% when compared to the sales registered for the same month of 2019. Japanese brand Nissan was the brand with the biggest market share, followed in second and third place by General Motors and Volkswagen. This result adds another month to the negative trend experienced by light vehicle sales in Mexico, according to numbers from INEGI, there's almost five years with a low dynamism.

The fourth generation of the Toyota Highlander arrives to Mexico

The fourth generation of Toyota's 2020 Highlander joins the market and is now built in accordance with the Toyota New Global Architecture (TNGA) platform, manufacturing method based on the utilization of modular platforms. This new generation highlights safety factors, which is why it's the first vehicle from Toyota in Mexico to incorporate Toyota Safety Sense 2.0 technology. The 2020 Highlander is equipped with a V6 3.5 liter engine and can generate 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft torque. It arrives to the country in four versions: LE, XLE, Limited PR and Limited BR.

MAN Truck & Bus Mexico will implement new distributor excellence program

One of MAN Truck & Bus most important pillars is its distributor network, present throughout the country with 12 headquarters, eight branches, two MAN Center and five MAN Bus Centers, and this year won't be an exception. Which is why, this year the brand is implementing a new Excellence Program that will help develop their distributor network to better business profitability, standardization of processes, bettering operations and customer service, and they will receive constant training and will be certified.
