Retooling of Mazda plant in Guanajuato will enable the facility to build crossovers

Mazda Motor Corporation confirmed to AutoNews that retooling its plant in Salamanca, Guanajuato, will begin in early 2019 to build the next-generation Mazda3. What's even more interesting here is that the upgrade will enable the assembly facility in Central Mexico to manufacture crossovers if required. However, Mazda officials wouldn't say if there's plans to actually build crossovers in Mexico. But the upgrade simplifies future decisions. It also would help Mazda lift utilization rates at its Mexico plant, one of its few production centers operating below capacity. More details here: bit.ly/2Eo5ikO
Ford Cuautitlan to cease Fiesta production in August; retooling for electric car comes next

In the second half of 2019, Ford Motor Co. will stop producing the Fiesta model at the Cuautitlan plant in the State of Mexico. This is in order to retool the facility and be able to start the production of an electric vehicle in 2020, Notimex reported. The president and CEO of the company in Mexico, Hector Perez, said it is expected that, by August 2019, the production of the Fiesta will stop to start the modification of tools and the production line for the new electric unit, whose nameplate is still unknown. The manager said that down times and payment terms have been agreed for workers who will remain inactive during the reconfiguration of the plant and will be reincorporated into production in the first half of 2020. More details here: bit.ly/2C94ej6
KIA to cease production work of Forte hatchback in Nuevo Leon

KIA Motors México will cease production of the hatchback version of the Forte in its Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon plant, to focus on the production of the Rio, both in sedan and hatchback, and in the revamped version of the Forte, which is currently built only in the sedan version. According to a report by Notimex that quotes as a source Horacio Chávez, general manager of the South Korean automaker in Mexico, the company plans to launch a hatchback version of the new Forte generation, but it will be imported from South Korea. The manager stressed that, at the end of 2018, the automaker maintains its expectation of exceeding the production of 300,000 units. More details here: bit.ly/2ChD8pX
Volkswagen to build new 1.4-liter engine at Silao plant

Volkswagen will manufacture a new four-cylinder engine at its plant in Silao, Guanajuato, starting in 2020, which will contribute to increase production volume at the facility to up to 40%. Julio Cesar Marin Leal, VW director of environmental management and sustainability in Mexico, said to Periódico AM that, with the new 1.4-liter EA-211 engine, the plant will require expansion work and adding new job positions, but he offered no concrete figures. "We seek to make engines much more efficient; we look for engines that are of lower hydrocarbon consumption. For this reason, we will start making the EA-211 engine from the Silao plant, a smaller, more efficient engine," he added. More details here: bit.ly/2Gpu6v8
Most Mexico-made vehicles made it to 2019 Consumer Guide Best Buy Awards

Nearly every Mexico-made vehicle that is marketed in the U.S. made it to the 2019 Consumer Guide Automotive Best Buy Awards. In fact, only the Volkswagen Beetle, the Fiat 500 and the Dodge Journey were left out, while the rest made way to their respective categories. Each year Consumer Guide Automotive editors select the best vehicles from 20 segments to receive Best Buy Awards, with vehicle price and value being major factors in their selection process. Consumer Guide evaluates numerous vehicles during the year to determine which ones prove their value and available features. Vehicles selected offer a combination of features and options plus the dynamic qualities that make for safe, enjoyable and reliable choices. More details here: bit.ly/2GnzP4Q
Volkswagen widens voluntary leave options for Puebla plant workers

A few weeks after Volkswagen offered a two-year voluntary leave plan to workers at the assembly plant in Puebla, the company unveiled new options to reduce the workforce during 2019, local press reported. In addition to a new early retirement option, Volkswagen is offering to workers from the Cuautlancingo facility to be transferred to the engine plant in Silao, Guanajuato, where in the near future more personnel will be recruited to start a production line for a new engine. VW is also offering the option of being transferred to the Audi plant located in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla. Offers to exercise rights on any of the proposals will be valid until March 2019. More details here: bit.ly/2GdQgAF
Chinese manufacturers consider Queretaro to build industrial park

The mayor of the municipality of Corregidora, Queretaro, which borders the state capital, revealed to local media about negotiations with a group of Chinese companies that are interested in installing manufacturing operations in that region. According to Roberto Sosa Pichardo, these companies––between 10 and 12––are dedicated to the manufacture of automotive parts, and they're planning to become suppliers of the plant that Toyota currently builds in the neighboring municipality of Apaseo el Grande, Guanajuato. The official stressed that the reason for negotiating with the companies in bulk is because they intend to develop an industrial park from where they can operate together. More details here: bit.ly/2zWGhdk
Volkswagen Trucks and Buses to expand production capacity in Queretaro

Volkswagen Trucks and Buses revealed plans to expand its operations in Mexico. For this purpose, the company plans to inaugurate a new production line in its factory in Queretaro in the first quarter of 2019. The new structure will serve to resume the assembly of MAN bus chassis with specifications for the Mexican market. In addition, the company has just installed a new logistics center with a view to increasing the sales of special vehicles to meet the Mexican customer. The new assembly line will occupy an area of 1,500 square meters. For greater space optimization, it will share quality inspection areas with the other models assembled in Queretaro. More details here: bit.ly/2UQUtxp
Auto industry contributed 3.7% of the national GDP in 2017

In 2017, the automotive industry in Mexico contributed 3.7% of the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and 20.2% of manufacturing GDP, being the second economic activity with the highest participation in the sector, after the food industry. All this in accordance with the document "Knowing the automotive industry," published by Inegi and AMIA, which aims to present the general public and national and international specialists relevant indicators about one of the most important industries in the country. In a statement, AMIA highlighted that from 2010 to 2017, the GDP of the industry grew 12.3% on average in real terms, impacting 90 economic activities in the secondary sector and 70 in trade and services. More details here: bit.ly/2QzYp73
Porsche, BMW unveil charging tech three times faster than Tesla's

A research group with members including Porsche and BMW has unveiled a new 450 kW charging station prototype in Germany, which has a capacity of three times that of Tesla's existing Superchargers. Supplying that much power means that the new station, built by the FastCharge research group, could supply around 100 km (62 miles) of range in just three minutes, or charge a BMW i3 from 10 to 80 percent in 15 minutes. In contrast, Tesla's Superchargers currently have a maximum capacity of 145 kW. However, FastCharge is unlikely to maintain this same lead for long: Tesla has already upgraded its network from 120 kW in the past, and it plans to increase the maximum capacity of its network to 250 kW next year.
