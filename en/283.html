November 22th, 2019
Bimbo will incorporate 4,000 electric vehicles to its fleet in the next four years

As part of its commitment with the environment, Bimbo Group announced that during the next four years starting in 2020 it will grow its fleet by 4,000 electric delivery vehicles in Mexico, which represents an investment of MXN$2.8 billion. To achieve this goal, Moldex, a subsidiary of Bimbo Group, which since 2012 has been working in the engineering and production of electric vehicles, will be in charge of providing 1,000 annual units during four years, which will be incorporated to different delivery routes in the country.
Volkswagen Financial Services Mexico is offering "Full Service Leasing" program

With the goal of simplifying, controlling and helping to administer the operation of company vehicles, Volkswagen Financial Services Mexico developed and is currently offering the "Full Service Leasing" (FSL) program with which it aims to contribute in reducing costs, offering integral solutions from one single point, like personalized attention. For that purpose a digital platform was created that allows to permanently monitor every fleet (starting at five units), thus managing captured requirements of every vehicle.
The Mustang Mach-E will be produced in Ford's Cuautitlan plant

For the first time in 55 years, Ford is expanding the Mustang family, bringing the famous pony to the electric era with Ford Mustang Mach-E, a completely new and electric SUV born from the same American ideals that inspired the best-selling sports coupe in the world. This new model will be sold in two versions at the end of 2020 and it will be produced in Ford's plant in Cuautitlan, State of Mexico.
Beat arrives to more Mexican cities

Mobility app Beat, which is directed in Mexico by Carlos Lieja, expanded its service to Durango, Villahermosa, Queretaro, Saltillo, Leon, Aguascalientes and La Laguna. "We're looking to offer an affordable and convenient transport service for many other passengers and drivers. By doing this we contribute to urban development in more cities, while we offer an effective alternative to have an extra income," said Nikos Drandakis, CEO of Beat.
Profeco and Toyoya recall three models because of air bags

The Federal Consumer Protection Office (Profeco) extended the alert that Toyota in Mexico issued for the following vehicles: Matrix (models 2003-2008), Corolla (models 2003-2008) and Yaris (model 2004-2005) to replace air bags. The automotive company said that the involved vehicles have air bag inflators on the passenger's side produced by Takata, might have been assembled with a propellant that, after being exposed to climates with high humidity and variation of high temperatures, runs the risk of degrading.
BMW and MINI were rewarded by J.D. Power Mexico

BMW Group Mexico and MINI, obtained first and third place respectively, in the category of "Vehicle Quality and Dependability" given by J.D. Power Mexico, global leader in consumer knowledge, consulting services and information analysis. According to the Vehicle Dependability Study Mexico 2019 (VDS), BMW Group Mexico reached the highest performance in the industry with 81 PP100 (problems per every 100 vehicles), while MINI obtained third place with 88 PP100.
Kia awards winners of Kia University contest

Sánchez Burgueño and Lupercio Gonzalez came out as winners between more than 70 projects presented by 35 universities, both public and private, that participated in Kia University, that is framed within the project "Kia toward the future." Kia University consisted of a creativity contest directed to university students, whose objective was to create a creative strategy starting from the concept "Kia toward the future" including key messages and products, as well as making an execution proposal
Automotive investments await USMCA approval

Automotive companies from Japan, Europe and the United States are waiting on the approval of the USMCA to define investment projects in Mexico, informed Luz Maria de la Mora Sanchez, undersecretary of Foreign Trade of the Ministry of Economy . She also added that they're keeping an eye on the negotiations of the agreement in the Senate of the United States and are only awaiting its resolution. Luz Maria de la Mora affirmed that the public policy that they'll follow to attract more investments is one of openness, transparency and combating corruption.
Scania opens its 51st shop and delivers 40 units in Aguascalientes

With the sale of 40 Scania units acquired by Flecha Amarilla, which will be incorporated to the bus line Primera Plus, the Swedish brand opened its 51st shop in the client's installation in the city of Aguascalientes. The objective of this business model is for the client to feel backed up with an integral and personalized service that has maintenance and repair services, a remote diagnostic system, and spare parts, as well as brand certified technicians and assistance.
Guanajuato's automotive industry strengthens itself in the face of Toyota's start of operations

With the start of operations of the fifth vehicle assembly plant in Guanajuato, Toyota began producing pilot units of the Tacoma, in its plant located in Apaseo el Grande; mass production will begin in December of this year, informed the president of Toyota Apaseo, Juan Francisco Garcia Lopez. Other 10 Japanese companies will install themselves in Guanajuato to provide for the Japanese automotive company, confirmed the Japanese Consul in Guanajuato, Osamu Houkida.
