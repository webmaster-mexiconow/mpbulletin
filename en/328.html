Auto parts industry will bill US$57.6 billion in 2020: INA

After the close of operations during 40 days due to the COVID-19 health emergency and the fall in demand of light vehicles in North America, the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA) expects a fall of 40% in the production of parts and automotive components, which would go from billing US$98 billion in 2019 to US$57.6 billion in 2020. The forecast for the production of light vehicles in North America would go from 16.78 million units to 12 million.

The Mexican automotive industry expects a fall of 28% in auto parts production and 35% in vehicle sales

The Mexican automotive sector will have a fall of 28% in the production of auto parts and 35% in vehicle sales this 2020 because of the coronavirus crisis, estimated on Monday several industry representatives. The auto parts production value would close the year "in the best of cases" with MXN$26.95 billion less than in 2019 to add up to a total of MXN$70.87 billion, warned Oscar Albin, executive president of the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA). On the other hand, the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) updated their previous forecast of an annual drop of 25.5% to one of 35%.

Automotive financing drops during the first quarter of 2020

Data recently published by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI) states that at the close of the year’s first quarter 296,677 new vehicles were sold in the country, of which 62.5% or 185,503 were acquired through financing, but this last number represented a decrease of 7.3 percentage points when compared to the same period of 2019 (when it totaled 200,126 units), also in accordance to data from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA).

The automotive industry is now considered essential

The General Health Council (CSG) approved the return of construction, mining and the manufacturing of transport equipment (which considers the automotive, aerospace, railway and shipping industries) as essential activities within the Mexican economy, after being paralyzed during the quarantine to prevent the spread of COVID-19 in the country. All companies and establishments will apply sanitary measures in an obligatory manner in the workplace, which will be released by the Ministry of Health, the Mexican Social Security Institute (IMSS) and the Ministry of Labor and Social Security.

Due to COVID-19 only 6% of Mexicans are thinking about acquiring a vehicle in the next year

Only 6% of Mexicans have an intention of buying a vehicle in the next 12 months, according to a survey made by J.D. Power called "Automotive consumer trust in Mexico during the COVID-19 emergency." Gerardo Gomez, the brand's general director in Mexico, said that 37% of consumers consider that the impact to family economy in the next 3 months will be worse, while 26% considers it'll be the same. From those who do decide to buy a vehicle in 2020, 17% will decide based on the brand's reputation and 14% by vehicle quality.
