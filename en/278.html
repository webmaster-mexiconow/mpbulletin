2020 Audi A1 will be available in our country in four different versions

One of the most expected subcompacts for Mexico is the 2020 Audi A1. Its first generation was a success in our country and in its segment. It's now preparing another offensive with its second generation that is broader, more refined and much more equipped. The brand announces that it will be available for pre-sales for now, since the units will start delivery in December of this year. The available versions will be: Urban, Cool, Ego and S Line with a price range of MXN$399,900 to MXN$549,900.
Golden Star 4000 in alliance with Zhongtong Bus Holding will invest US$325 million in Nuevo Leon

Nuevo Leon governor, Jaime Rodriguez Calderon informed that the first assembly plant for ecological buses will be installed in the municipality of El Carmen, which will have a capacity to produce 4,800 annual units, based on natural gas. For this, the company Golden Star 4000 made an alliance with the Chinese company Zhongtong Bus Holding Co. Ltd and will invest US$325 million. The ecological bus assembly plant will have natural gas as its main technology.
Claudia Marquez, the new CEO of Hyundai Mexico

Hyundai Motor Mexico named Claudia Marquez like its new CEO, starting November. Marquez will be responsible for the administration of all business aspects of Hyundai in Mexico, including product strategy, sales, marketing, client relations and distributor development, among other responsibilities. The executive joins Hyundai with more than 24 years of global automotive experience and will report directly to Jose Muñoz, president and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America.
Car sales will fall 9% this year: AMDA

The executive president of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) Guillermo Prieto Treviño affirmed that they expect to close this year with the sales of a million automobiles, which would represent a fall of 9% when compared to last year. The uncertainty in investment and consumption are some factors that cause this reduction in sales; banks too are increasing requirements because of the recovery of guarantees.
JAC X200 arrives to Mexico with a price of MXN$370,000

JAC announced the arrival to Mexico of the X200, the brand's first commercial vehicle that will compete in the light distribution truck segment. JAC X200 will be available in one version, chasis diesel cabin with a capacity of 1,800 kg. "It's a vehicle that can easily be adopted into the fleet of big companies, or an ally in the day to day of small and medium enterprises," said Elias Massri, president of the Board and CEO of Giant Motors Latin America.
Mazda in Mexico's sales drop 7%

The sales of Mazda in Mexico in October of this year were of 4,645 vehicles, a decrease of 7% in comparison to last year. The following comes from the monthly sales report of Mazda North America, in which it's also noted that in Mexico the sales from January to October increased 4.1% with 47,981 sold vehicles. It should also be noted that in the United States, Mazda had a sales total of 19,520 vehicles in October, an increase of 4.5% when compared to October of 2018; sales from January to October showed a decrease of 10.3% with 227,687 sold vehicles.
Business sector trusts that the Senate will keep rejecting the regularization of chocolate cars

The business sector trusts the Senate will keep refusing the regularization of used vehicles and that it will freeze the law that pretends to legalize the contraband of 18 million units. The Corporate Coordinating Counsel (CCE), the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) and the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), explained that Congress has until November 5th to define if it will or will not accept the reservation of the fifteenth transitory article, or otherwise it will be frozen until a new ordinary period.
