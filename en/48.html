Delphi creates independent Powertrain company; four Mexican plants involved

Delphi Corp. plans to separate its Powertrain program into a publicly traded company. Once the transaction is completed, by March 2018, Delphi shareholders will own shares of both companies. The move is part of a wider Delphi's strategy to focus on developing technology for electrically powered and self-driving vehicles. Delphi's Powertrain division has 20,000 global employees, 5,000 engineers and revenues of approximately US$ 4.5 billion in 2016. This business unit operates four production facilities in Mexico, located in the cities of Juarez, Chihuahua, Torreon and Saltillo.
Automakers report lower-than-expected sales in US market during April

Six of the biggest automakers reported lower sales in the U.S. market during April when compared to the same month from 2016. The latest figures provide further evidence the auto industry is receding its pace after seven straight years of growth, according to analysts. Overall the industry reported a 4.7% sales decline to 1.43 million units, according to Autodata Corp. Fiat Chrysler's sales fell 6.6% to 177,441 vehicles, GM sales totaled 244,506 units, down 5.8%, Ford's sales also fell 7.2% to 214,695 vehicles. VW was the only major automaker to avoid the fall with a 1.6% increase to 27,557 vehicles.
Canada's auto sales fall 1.6% in April after record breaking first quarter

After a record-breaking first quarter, auto sales in Canada fell in April compared with a year ago for the first time this year. DesRosiers Automotive Reports says overall sales fell 1.6% to 197,203 vehicles last month compared with a year ago when April sales totaled a record 200,384. The consulting firms says passenger car sales retreated 8.7% last month while light truck sales increased 2.5%. For the first four months of the year auto sales totaled 618,721 units, 2.5% above the 603,364 sold by the end of April 2016. GM topped the sales charts for April with 30,948 vehicles sold, followed by Ford and FCA with 30,385 and 27,373 units, respectively.
China-based metal stamping supplier Cheersson opens plant in Queretaro

China-based Suzhou Cheersson Precision Metal Forming Co. announced the opening of its plant in Queretaro to supply components for the automotive industry. Cheersson invested US$ 8 million in its facility to manufacture metal stamping products for Tier 1 suppliers located in the region. The company arrived with a robust customers' portfolio which includes Valeo, Continental, Harman, Bosch, Magna and Delphi. According to government officials, Cheersson recruited 200 workers for its endeavor.
SSI Schaefer starts packaging production in San Luis Potosi

The Packaging Systems Division of SSI Schaefer Systems International started production at a new manufacturing facility in San Luis Potosi. The plant will service existing and new clients located within Mexico. The new 8,500 square meter facility opened March 1, 2017, but held a grand opening celebration in April 26. The new packaging fabrication plant will provide turn-key packaging solutions servicing the automotive industry, among other segments.
Sumitomo Riko opens its second plant in Queretaro, third in Mexico

Japan-based Sumitomo Riko (SumiRiko), one of the world's top suppliers of anti-vibration automotive rubber products, announced the opening of its second plant in Queretaro. The company invested US$ 20 million and recruited 300 workers to manufacture rubber components that reduce vibration and noise caused by the engine and road surface. The new facility will supply parts for Mazda, Honda and Infiniti. SumiRiko estimates sales of US$ 20 million a year out of this plant. This is SumiRiko's third production site in Mexico, the company also operates a hose manufacturing facility in Chihuahua city.
Germany-based Willi Elbe Manufacturing starts operations in Guanajuato

Germany-based steering technology supplier Willi Elbe Manufacturing started operations in recent days at a 4,800 square-meters production facility in Valle de Santiago, Guanajuato. The company invested US$ 12 million and recruited a few dozen workers for its starting phase. According to company officials the investment will reach eventually US$ 25 million and a workforce of 200 in the coming years. The company manufactures steering components for automakers from the premium segment. BMW, Mercedes Benz and Volkswagen -including its Porsche and Audi subsidiaries- figure among its customers. This is the company's first plant in the Americas.
Bosch sales its starter motors and generators division

Bosch announced the sale of its subsidiary Robert Bosch Starter Motors Generators Holding GmbH, including all subsidiaries, to the purchaser consortium ZMJ (Zhengzhou Coal Mining Machinery Group Co., Ltd.), and CRCI (China Renaissance Capital Investment). The purchase agreement was signed by Bosch, ZMJ, and CRCI on May 2, 2017. It has been agreed that the purchase price will not be disclosed. Consummation of the transaction is subject to approval by the antitrust and other official authorities. The business unit has under 7,000 associates and 16 locations in 14 countries, including Brazil, China, Hungary, India, Mexico, South Africa, Spain and the US.
Pricing for 2018 GMC Terrain to start at US$ 25,970

General Motors announced that pricing for the all-new 2018 Terrain will start at US$ 25,970 when it goes on sale this summer. Three all-new turbocharged engines, leading with a 2.0L at launch -followed later with a 1.5L and an available 1.6L turbo-diesel- will provide more choices when it comes to performance, efficiency and capability. The automaker expects the market for small SUVs will expand 10% during the next five to six years, that's why the Terrain production was shifted from Canada to not one, but two facilities in Mexico.
Porsche Cayenne sets new Guinness World Record by towing an Airbus A380

A completely standard Porsche Cayenne has successfully towed an Air France Airbus A380 weighing 285-tons over a distance of 42 meters at Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport, to set a new Guinness World Records title for Heaviest aircraft pull by a production car. The Porsche Cayenne S Diesel with 385 horsepower (measuring 4.8 meters in length) was connected to the most sophisticated and largest passenger aircraft in the world, the Airbus A380 (73 meters) via a special towing attachment that sat on the Cayenne's standard tow bar.
