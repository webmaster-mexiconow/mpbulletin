Giant Motors Latin America together with JAC Mexico announced the opening of the last two JAC Stores of 2019. "We're very happy to close this year with news that have a positive impact on Giant Motors Latin America, JAC, and of course, Mexico," commented Elias Massri, CEO and president of the Board of Giant Motors Latin America.

Nidec invests US$35 million and Tokai Rica invests US$40 million in Nuevo Leon

Nidec Corporation will open its sixth plant in Nuevo Leon with an investment of US$35 million, while Tokai Rica will have an expansion of US$40 million. Nidec Corporation, Japanese maker of electric motors for the industry will acquire a refrigeration compressors business from Embraco, a Brazilian maker with presence in Apodaca. At the same time, the expansion for US$40 million of the auto parts company Tokai Rica, part of Toyota, that arrived in 2016 was confirmed and will create another 760 direct jobs in the state.

Meeting the modifications made to the steel content in the USMCA will be a challenge: AMIA

The Mexican automotive industry recognized that the last modifications to the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) put pressure on the steel industry to be able to meet a 70% of regional content in a lapse of seven years; although, if this is not met there's a chance in the next five years to request an extension in the period. Eduardo Solis, president of the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA) indicated that the steel industry will have to make an effort to attract investments and make this input, which will provide for the Mexican automotive sector.

SUVs and pick-ups lead Mexican automotive exports

Between January and November of 2019, Mexico exported 3,104,359 automobiles, from this amount almost 70% were SUVs and pick-ups, the biggest participation registered in the industry's national history, shown by data from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). In the eleven months of this year, Mexico has sent abroad 2,077,512 units of this type, an increase of 2.5% when compared to that registered from the same period of 2018. Additionally, this happened despite the fact that total automotive exports fell 2.2%.

Worldwide vehicle demand will impact production in Mexico: AMIA

Growth expectations for the automotive industry will continue to decrease due to the pressures of global vehicle demand, the uncertainty surrounding the USMCA and the new origin rule for steel sheets, mentioned Eduardo Solis, president of the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA). In this sense, Eduardo Solis explained that 88% of the units made and exported in Mexico depend on the worldwide market, and he assured that there will be no significant growth for the industry by the end of the year.

The Golf Variant exits Volkswagen Puebla's production lines

Volkswagen of Mexico informed that after 840,000 units and 12 years of production of the wagon variant of the Volkswagen Golf, Golf Variant, its cycle has come to an end in the Puebla plant. The Golf Variant began being produced in Mexico in 2006 when it still had the brand's PQ35 platform. After several years, updates and even a new generation that was briefly sold in Mexico, it is saying goodbye to our country and also starting the general production exit from the Golf in general from our territory.

Daimler Commercial Vehicles put into operation four Regional Training Centers

Daimler and its brands Mercedes-Benz Buses and Freightliner Buses, are celebrating 21 years from the creation of its Regional Training Centers, to support the Distributor Network of Daimler Commercial Vehicles of Mexico (DVCM).
