Toyota presents new driving assistance systems for Mexico

Safety systems in cars are quickly evolving worldwide. In Mexico, Toyota is joining the trend with the announcement of the inclusion of the Toyota Safety Sense 2.0 systems. In total, Toyota will introduce four systems to Mexico: pre-collision system (PCS), lane departure alert, dynamic radar cruise control (DRCC) and automatic high beams.

Mercedes-Benz anticipates the sales of 8,500 buses in 2020

Mercedes-Benz is expecting bus sales to increase 8.6% this year, propelled by city growth and the renewal of unit fleets for urban and suburban transport, said Raul Gonzalez, the brand's vice president of Sales, Marketing and After-Sales. The executive anticipated that bus sales throughout the country might reach a growth of 2.40% to reach 8,500 units, compared to the 8,300 units sold last year.

Mexican auto parts makers hope to surpass US$102 billion in production value

Mexico's auto parts industry hopes to surpass the US$102 billion mark in production value in 2020, under the expectation that more sophisticated vehicles will be assembled in the United States and the approval of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA). It's estimated that the 2019 auto parts production value in Mexico was of US$99 billion, 2.2% higher to that registered last year. Oscar Albin, president of the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA) highlighted that the USMCA's approval generates certainty for the national industry, since the exportation of Mexican manufacturing will continue with the certainty that no tariffs will be applied.

Tiguan is the most produced vehicle in Mexico in 2019

The SUV Tiguan was the most produced model in Mexico's automotive industry in 2019, with 221,731 made units, which meant an increase of 13% when compared to 2018. According to data from the Administrative Register of the Light Vehicles Automotive Industry published by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI), the German brand's SUV placed 16,510 units over its closest competitor in the production cumulative from January to December 2019.

Heavy vehicles production and exports decrease in November

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) informed that, during the month of November 2019, the production of heavy vehicles reached a total of 12,972 units, which meant a fall of 17.6%, when compared to the same month of 2018. At the same time, in November, there was an export of 11,869 units, which meant a decrease of 4.2% in relation to the same month of 2018.
