Production of new small Ford truck to be allocated at Hermosillo, says report

A new report by car enthusiast website Jalopnik says that production of the new small unibody truck that Ford Motor Company is developing will begin at Hermosillo, Sonora, towards the end of 2021, using updated engines and powertrains already featured in the Ford Fusion, which is currently built at the same facility. Citing multiple sources, the report says the pickup will share the same powertrain and wheelbase of another crossover also under development. This rumor coincides with previous reports that the Ford Fusion will be turned into a crossover, but will keep the same nameplate. More details here: bit.ly/2AXBrgo
Sales of hybrid cars hike 70%, but demand of electric vehicles drops 17%

Sales of electrified vehicles in Mexico have increased at a two-digit pace, although demand of electric cars is growing at a lower rate than that of hybrid units and, in some cases, it actually has dropped. From January through October, auto dealers in Mexico sold 13,925 electrified units, which compared to the 8,288 vehicles sold during the same period of 2017, represents a 68% increase. On the same year-to-date basis, sales of hybrid cars were of 13,765 units, a skyrocketing 70% hike compared to 8,095 units in the first 10 months of 2017. Meanwhile, sales of electric vehicles decreased to 160 units, which is 17% lower from the 193 electric vehicles sold during the same 10-month period of the previous year. More details here: bit.ly/2DopxOe
HBPO to supply modular front-end systems for FCA in Saltillo

HBPO, a supplier of modular front-end systems, announced its new plant in Saltillo, Coahuila, is completed and ready to support a new program by Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA). The company did not disclose the date the new plant will start operations or which FCA program it will support, although it is very likely that it will be the Ram Heavy Duty, which debuted last week at the Detroit Auto Show and whose first units are slated to reach U.S. dealerships in March. The company did not disclose the amount of investment allocated at the new plant or job positions it opened in order to start operations. More details here: bit.ly/2RGEUup
Mexican plastic molder Fischer to open new plant in Celaya

Fischer S.A. de C.V., a Mexican manufacturer of plastic components with operations in the state of Veracruz, plans to build its second production facility in the state of Guanajuato, Plastic News reported. Construction work will start by March at the Amistad Chuy María industrial park in the municipality of Celaya. With the new plant, Fischer aims to increase its business as supplier of components for the automotive industry. The company has provided plastic components such as thermostat housings for Tier 1 suppliers that serve automakers like General Motors, Ford, Fiat Chrysler Automobiles, Volkswagen and Mazda.
Ohio-based chemical company to shift production work to Guanajuato

Ferro Corp., an international chemical company based in Mayfield Heights, Ohio, has announced plans to move some of its U.S. operations to Mexico. The company's manufacturing plant in Canton Township, Pennsylvania, will be closed soon, while the Cleveland site will discontinue porcelain enamel production. These operations will be moved to a more modern facility in Villagran, Guanajuato, the company said in a press statement. The Mexico transition will start in the first quarter of the year and continue through late 2019, or possibly into early 2020. The company provides functional coatings and color solutions. Coahuila-based Grupo Server develops new industrial park in San Luis Potosí

With almost 500 hectares to host companies from any sector, Coahuila-based Grupo Server is building a new industrial park in the municipality of Villa de Zaragoza, San Luis Potosí, which will be added to the 18 parks that the state currently has. "Grupo Server arrived a couple of years ago to San Luis Potosí and bought between 75 and 78 hectares first on Highway 57 in Villa de Zaragoza, and later bought almost 400 hectares near the road to Rioverde, becoming a strategically located and very important park. It would be difficult to say how many plants can be accommodated because there may be some companies that need less or more space," said Gustavo Puente Orozco, head of the state Economic Development Department. More details here: bit.ly/2ATv1Pt
Nissan cuts extend to Mississippi plant

Nissan Motor Co. announced plans to cut up to 700 contract workers due to production changes at its Canton, Mississippi, plant. The Japanese automaker plans to cut the production of NV cargo and passenger vans and Titan and Frontier pickup trucks. The move comes one month after it announced plans to cut 1,000 jobs at three production sites in Mexico. The cuts at Canton will become effective March 18. "Nissan is adjusting production capacity at its Canton manufacturing facility to match market demand and maintain healthy inventory levels," said a spokesman to local media. "While affected Nissan employees will be redeployed to other areas of the plant, we anticipate that there will be some associates (contract workers) who will remain unassigned."
FCA recalls 1.4 million vehicles to replace Takata airbag inflators

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles is recalling an estimated 1.4 million vehicles in the U.S. to replace passenger-side airbag inflators that may, on deployment, send shrapnel into the vehicle cabin. The Company is unaware of any injuries or accidents associated with this vehicle population. This recall is the fourth and final phase of a coordinated schedule established by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, and developed to capture defective airbag inflators produced by the former Takata. Affected vehicles include: 2010 Ram 3500 pickups, 2010 Ram 4500/5500 chassis cab, 2010-2011 Dodge Dakota pickup, 2010-2014 Dodge Challenger sport coupe, 2011-2015 Dodge Charger sedan, 2010-2015 Chrysler 300 sedan and the 2010-2016 Jeep Wrangler SUV.
Senators revive bill to delay 232 auto tariffs

Legislation that would delay the imposition of additional Section 232 tariffs on automobiles and automotive parts imported by the United States has been reintroduced by two US Senators. The Automotive Jobs Act, reintroduced by Senators Doug Jones (Democrat, Alabama) and Lamar Alexander (Republican, Tennessee), "would require the International Trade Commission (ITC) to conduct a comprehensive study of the well-being, health and vitality of the United States automotive industry before tariffs could be applied," the senators said in a statement. The Section 232 investigation into whether imported automobiles, trucks, and parts threaten U.S. national security and, if so, to subsequently levy tariffs, is expected to end and make its recommendation next month.
Mercedes-Benz A-Class sedan to star 60-second Super Bowl commercial

Mercedes-Benz confirmed that its latest model, the new A-Class sedan, will be the star of a 60- second Super Bowl LIII commercial. The ad will be aired on Sunday February 3 during the game, which will take place at the Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta, one of two NFL facilities the German automaker owns naming rights, the other one being the Mercedes-Benz Superdome in New Orleans. The commercial will also feature rapper Ludacris, an Atlanta native and long-time Mercedes-Benz brand ambassador.
