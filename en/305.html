IUSA Group releases vehicle line made in Mexico

IUSA Group, maker of electric products in the country and energy supply, officially released in Mexico four vehicles: a compact car, a SUV, a van and an urban bus. Agustin Ortega, sales manager, commented that these units will be produced in Pasteje, State of Mexico, next to the brand's solar panel plant. The IIK model, the company's compact vehicle, has a 440 kilometer capacity in constant speed, while the SUV, called ATL, can reach 310 kilometers. The van is a hybrid urban transport bus called Balaam.

Renault forecasts sales growth of 3.3% for 2020

Renault forecasts a sales growth of approximately 3.3% for Mexico, with which it would register a deceleration, since last year its growth rate was of 18.4%. In a reunion with press, Magda Lopez, president and general director of the company in Mexico, said that the commercialization of new vehicles in 2020 will be of 34,000 units. The director commented that the growth increase numbers for this year are positive, which shows an increase before the expected contraction for the entire automotive industry.

Gunter Daut assumes the presidency of San Luis Potosi's Automotive Cluster

Gunter Daut, Commercial vice president of BOSCH in San Luis Potosi was elected as the cluster's president, while Alejandro Veraza occupies the organization's general direction. The Cluster will soon share the details for the 2020 Annual Operative Program. The Automotive Cluster of San Luis Potosi has more than 49 associates among which are the two assembly plants present in the state, GM and BMW; and educational institutions in the academic helix.

Imports for used cars registered a total of 159,446 vehicles in 2019

The Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) indicated that the number of used vehicles legally entering the country in 2019 increased 12.1% when compared to last year, since there were 159,446 registered vehicles, the highest number reported by the industry since 2015, while at the close of 2018 there were a total of 141,752 imported vehicles. "It's important to keep used vehicle imports under control and to strengthen the growth of the internal automotive market," commented Fausto Cuevas Mesa, general director of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA).

Renault Mexico achieves participation of 2.58% in the national market

Renault Mexico reported the sales of 2,700 vehicles from the ten different models offered in the market, which meant an increase of 15.6% when compared to the same month of 2019, when through its distributor network the brand sold 2,335 units. For Magdalena Lopez Estrada, president and general director for the French company in Mexico, which sells the Logan, Stepway, Duster, Captur, Koleos, Kwid, Oroch, Sandero RS, Twisy and Kangoo models, this is relevant because the brand achieved a participation of 2.58% in the national market.

SUVs represent 26.13% of sales in January

In January 2020 there was a commercialization of 104,832 light vehicles in Mexico, of which 26.13% belongs to the sales of SUVs, segment which has gained a 1.17% participation in the new vehicle market when compared to its participation with the same month of 2019, according to data from the National Institute of Geography and Statistics (INEGI). Honda is the leader in this segment, since at the close of January it sold 3,640 vehicles.

Transport SUVI acquires 20 Kenworth T680 units

Kenworth Mexicana, through its distributor Kenworth of el Bajio delivered 20 Kenworth T680 tractor trucks to Transports SUVI, company belonging to Traxion Group. The units are incorporated to the logistic and transport services offered by the group throughout the country. The 20 T680 units strengthen Trasnports SUVI's and Traxion Group's objective to maintain their leadership in innovation for the benefit of its clients, operational efficiency, road safety, environmental responsibility and the compromise they have with their collaborators.
