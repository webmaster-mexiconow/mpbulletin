Volkswagen Mexico production, export figures drop while Audi's duplicates

Volkswagen and its sibling Audi posted mixed results in the first quarter of 2018 concerning production and exports, with the first one facing challenges to adjust inventories and to launch the all-new Jetta, while the latter benefits from high demand of SUVs in the North American market. Volkswagen exports from Mexico dropped 20% during the first quarter totaling 68,224 units while its production fell 33.5% to 78,219 vehicles. Also, during the first quarter, Audi built 45,897 Q5 SUVs, of which 44,529 units were exported. Such figures represent increases of 97.2% and 101.2% respectively, compared to same period of 2017. Production idles at two Nissan plants in Aguascalientes

A local auto workers union confirmed that a new production stoppage will take place this week at the two auto plants Nissan Motor Corp. operates in Aguascalientes state in order to adjust inventories in both Mexico and the U.S. market. It is the second time that production idles at both A1 and A2 Nissan plants so far this year. A new stoppage will occur in July to make some necessary updates on production lines for the manufacture of the 2019 year models, according to local media in central Mexico. Nissan builds four models at the Aguascalientes plants, but only the Sentra and the Versa are made for the U.S. market. More details here: bit.ly/2HjMcig
Mazda considers building a new model in Guanajuato plant

Another Japanese automaker that has already stopped production in El Bajio, central Mexico, is Mazda, who kept its plant in Salamanca, Guanajuato idle during the past week, although its managers say it was just a routine maintenance procedure in the production lines. On the contrary, the rumor among the Guanajuato media that the Hiroshima-based company plans to produce a new model at its Mexican plant is getting stronger. For some time now, several news outlets have reported about the possibility of producing in Salamanca the CX3 SUV, which is based on the same platform as the Mazda2 and the Yaris sedan that Mazda builds for Toyota in the same facility. Asian brands account for more than half of Mexico's auto sales in the first quarter

During the first quarter of 2018, auto sales of Asian brands in Mexico accounted for the first time for more than half of total, grabbing a 54.6% of market share, according to figures of AMDA. Auto sales in Mexico totaled 337,229 vehicles in the first three months of the year, a 10.8% decline compared to those sold over the same period in 2017. Also, in the first quarter, 110,546 vehicles were imported from Asia, equivalent to 63% of total imports, a year ago the figure were of 50.8%. At the end of March, market share of Japanese and Korean automakers in Mexico was as follows: Nissan 23.8%, Toyota 7.6%, KIA 6.9%, Honda 6.8%, Mazda 3.9%, Hyundai 3.5%, Suzuki 1.6% and Infiniti 0.2%, while Acura, Isuzu and Subaru each obtained 0.1%. Japanese manufacturer Ashimori opens its second plant in Guanajuato

Ashimori Industry Co. Ltd., a Japanese manufacturer of automotive safety systems, last week opened its second plant in Mexico in the Guanajuato Puerto Interior zone, near Silao, Guanajuato. The company opened its first facility in Mexico in the end of 2012 with a US$ 10.2 million investment and the opening of 190 job positions to manufacture seat belts, airbags and interior parts for Mazda, Honda and Nissan. With the second plant the overall investment in the country has grown to US$ 17.5 million with more than 415 jobs created, while the production output has tripled. More details here: bit.ly/2qQHoWD
Audi fulfills commitment of 5,200 jobs in Puebla

A little more than a year after formally starting operations in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla, German premium automaker Audi has fulfilled its commitment to add 5,200 employees to its workforce, the company said in a statement. According to Alejandro Pliego, director of HR Marketing and Employee Engagement, during the time that the company has been in operation, it has sent 750 Mexican collaborators to Germany to be trained for more than a year in the language and the productive process of the company. This measure is part of the EMA program, which aims to recruit engineering students. More details here: bit.ly/2HEeF1z
US manufacturers create lobby group to fight steel, aluminum tariffs

A group of small- and medium-size manufacturers gathered last week in Washington D.C. to announce a new group - the Coalition of American Metal Manufacturers and Users - to fight the steel and aluminum tariffs proposed by the Trump administration, the Associated Press reported. Hundreds of companies are asking the Commerce Department to exempt them from the 25% steel tariff and the 10% aluminum tariff. Other companies are weighing their options. Jody Fledderman, CEO of Batesville Tool & Die in Indiana, says American steelmakers have already raised their prices since Trump's tariffs were announced last month. Fledderman says he may be forced to shift some production to a plant in Mexico in response to demands from his customers.
WABCO to set up Americas headquarters in Michigan

WABCO Holdings, a Belgium-based supplier of safety, efficiency and connectivity technologies for commercial vehicles, announced it will move its Americas headquarters to an Auburn Hills, MI, facility currently under construction. WABCO has significantly expanded its presence in the region since its launch in 2007. Over the past two years alone, WABCO has tripled its workforce as it acquired four high-tech manufacturing businesses: MICO Inc., Laydon Composites Ltd., R.H. Sheppard Co. Inc., and the buyout of Meritor WABCO Vehicle Control Systems, the 50-50 joint venture with Meritor. WABCO's workforce in the Americas is comprised of more than 1,800 members at 11 locations, including two Mexican sites in Empalme, Sonora and San Luis Potosi.
NAFTA officials to meet this week amid intensified push for deal

Senior trade officials from the U.S., Canada and Mexico will meet again in Washington next Tuesday in an intensified push to reach a NAFTA agreement in the next few weeks. Mexican Economy Minister Ildefonso Guajardo said last week that after seven rounds of talks in as many months, rotating between the three countries, the three sides have entered a concentrated phase. Chrystia Freeland, Canada's minister for foreign affairs, said Friday that negotiators have been making good progress on updated rules for cars, which she said will be at the heart of any eventual updated deal. "We are certainly in a more intense period of negotiations, and we are making good progress," Freeland added.
