Kia Motors Mexico donates 16 vehicles to Anahuac University

Kia Motors Mexico joins Anahuac University with the aim of supporting and fostering academic values, which is why, it donated 16 vehicles to the institution. Said vehicles will be used by engineering students, to further their education and for them to help the development of mobility in the country. The units will be distributed between the North campus, which will get seven units; Queretaro will get five and South campus will get four vehicles.
Mexican sport vehicles VUHL will be sold in the United States

VUHL's new 05RR model was presented in California to begin its entry into the US market, where it'll be available in states like Florida, Nevada, New York and Texas. VUHL is the only Mexican super sports car brand. VUHL's cars are not simple sport cars, they are true ultra-light racing cars that can be used in the streets. This makes them incredibly attractive in the US automotive culture. VUHL's first client deliveries began in 2015; this same year VUHL's plant was officially established in Queretaro.
Bosch San Luis receives Clean Industry certification for the fourth time

The Federal Attorney for Environmental Protection (Profepa) certified Robert Bosch Mexico Automotive Systems in San Luis Potosi as a clean industry for the fourth time, this by complying with environmental legislation and by demonstrating that the conditions under which it was certified are being maintained or improved. The company has been part of the National Environmental Audit Program for more than 8 years and it received the certification for the first time in 2013.
Continental and UAQ presented dual education program

Technology company, Continental, and authorities of the Autonomous University of Queretaro (UAQ) presented the first "Dual Education Program", which began this semester and will allow for an education program, with direct support of professors-mentors and the state's productive sector for students, to exist. This pilot program will begin with the Engineer of Automatization major, from the School of Engineering in its terminal lines in Mechatronic Systems, Instrumentation and Control, as well Electronic Systems.
Toluca II obtains Kenworth's Red Total certification

Kenworth Anahuac's Toluca II shop obtained Kenworth's Red Total certification, which endorses its superior level in service and freight carrier service, based on the knowledge and abilities of specialized personnel within the company, as well as facilities and post-sales programs. By successfully crediting all of Kenworth's Mexicana requirements in this level in the facilities of the concessionary, Alejandro Armenta, service director of the company, said that the concessionary achieved certification of the second shop in Toluca II, at the same time that it recertified its head shop.
Volkswagen Mexico bets on e-commerce

E-commerce is ever growing in Mexico, a tendency that German automaker Volkswagen wants to take full advantage of, by beginning sales on-line in the midst of a complicated landscape for the automotive industry. "We believe in new tendencies, in the ever growing digital consumer behavior and in the possibility of selling cars as iconic and emblematic as the Beetle," said Juan Pablo Gomez, Marketing Director of the company in México.
Automotive industry contributes seven times more currency than the agricultural and agribusiness sector

Mexico registered a trade surplus of US$3 billion in the first semester of this year, which is the highest value presented by the country since 2011 and contrasts with the deficit reported in the same period of last year, according to numbers from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). The automotive sector reported a historical surplus of almost US$44 billion during the first half of this year. With this, automobiles now contribute five times more currency in Mexico than the tourism sector and seven times more than what is generated by the agricultural and agribusiness sector.
Mazda will produce its new CX-30 model in Guanajuato

Mazda is betting strongly on SUVs and one of these products is the new Mazda CX-30, which was presented in 2019 Geneva's Motor Show, and is mounted on the Mazda 3 base, It is a smaller crossover than the CX-5 and with a coupe style. The Japanese automaker confirms that the car will be produced in its Guanajuato plant. Mazda already produces the new generation of the Mazda 3 in the Salamanca, Guanajuato plant, and the CX-30 will now join its production line.
Hyundai's Ioniq 2020 arrives to national territory

After two years in the country, Hyundai Motor of Mexico is bringing a renewed Ioniq, which seeks to take a step further with its futuristic design and premium technology, thus remaining like the same hybrid mobility option that takes care of the environment. Ioniq 2020 presents a renewed exterior and technological developments in its equipment, from which we can highlight the diverse driver assistance tools offered to the driver.
Ford recalls 8,148 vehicles in Mexico

Ford announced four recalls in North America, which affect a total of 665,154 vehicles because of problems with seats, direction systems, brakes and batteries. The biggest recall affects 550,186 vehicles in the United States, Mexico and Canada of the models 2018-2020 F-150, 2019-2020 F-Series Superduty, 2018-2020 Explorer, 2019-2020 Expedition and the 2020 Lincoln Aviator. In all of these vehicles, a necessary component to maintain the seat's back rigidity was not installed.
