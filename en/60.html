VW reveals base price for the all-new 2018 Tiguan

When it arrives in dealerships later this summer, the new 2018 Volkswagen Tiguan will cost US$ 26,245 to start, which includes $900 for shipping, the automaker announced. A fully loaded Mexico-made VW Tiguan can cost US$ 40,445. The SUV would inherit the warranty that the new, bigger 2018 Volkswagen Atlas received: a 6-year/72,000-mile bumper-to-bumper warranty, which also includes powertrain issues. The warranty can be transferred if the car is sold before the warranty expires. The base models will be equipped with three rows of seats, 17-inch wheels, cloth seats, a rearview camera, Bluetooth, one USB charger, and a 6.5-inch touchscreen.
Honda adds driver assistive technology to the Fit hatchback

Honda announced the addition of a Sport trim and the availability of Honda Sensing on its popular Fit hatchback. The unit has an updated look at the front and rear and two new colors were added. Furthermore, the availability of Honda Sensing brings the suite of advanced safety and driver assistive technologies to the subcompact Fit. This makes Fit the most affordable car in America with such a robust suite of available advanced safety and driver-assistive technologies and the only vehicle within its segment to do so. The Honda Fit for the North American market is built in Celaya, Guanajuato.
Auto sales in South America hike 14.1% in March, 9% for the first quarter

Based on strong growth in production and sales of automobiles in the first quarter of 2017 in most of the key regions around world, there is optimism that this year will see continued gains for the global automotive industry. According to Ward's Auto, some 23.92 million vehicles were sold globally in the three months to March 2017, up 5.3% on the same period in 2016. A surprising result came from South America. Despite many countries suffering economic downturns, total South American vehicle sales soared by 14.1% to 339,000 units in March 2017, bringing a first quarter total of 899,000 units, up 9% on the same quarter of 2016.
BMW sales in Mexico and Latin America grow 17.3%

The BMW Group announced it continued in May the positive sales trend of the past months, with sales totaling 208,447 vehicles worldwide, a 5.1% increase year-on-year. With its three premium brands, BMW, MINI and Rolls-Royce, the BMW Group sold 988,185 vehicles in the first five months, up 5.7%. Sales in the Americas were significantly influenced by the declining market in the United States. Sales in the U.S. totaled 137,916 units, down 4.8% compared to the first five months of 2016. Meanwhile, sales figures in Mexico and Latin America developed very positively, with growth of 17.3% to 14,558 vehicles.
Infiniti sales increase 53% in the Americas during May

Nissan's premium brand Infiniti posted its 13th consecutive global sales record. The company sold 19,565 vehicles globally in May, an increase of 9% compared to last May, and sold 104,512 vehicles so far, an increase of 37% compared to same period last year. This was the best May sales month in the Americas and in Taiwan. Infiniti sold in the Americas 73,015 vehicles year-to-date, a 53% increase; in United States, 66,872 vehicles, a 55% increase; in Canada, 4,938 vehicles, a 34% increase; and in Mexico, 822 vehicles, a 26% increase. In Latin America Infiniti sold 383 vehicles this year, a 46% increase.
GM produces first round of self-driving Chevrolet Bolt EV test vehicles

General Motors announced that it completed production of 130 Chevrolet Bolt EV test vehicles equipped with its next generation of self-driving technology at its Orion Assembly Plant located in Orion Township, Michigan. The vehicles will join the more than 50 current-generation self-driving Bolt EVs already deployed in testing fleets in San Francisco; Scottsdale, Arizona; and metro Detroit. GM became the first company to assemble self-driving test vehicles in a mass-production facility when its next generation of self-driving Chevrolet Bolt EV test vehicles began rolling off of the line at Orion Township in January.
Arizona Governor shows Lucid Motors endeavor as example of benefits on trade with Mexico

Arizona Gov. Doug Ducey visited Washington, D.C., to take part in a discussion on the state's relationship with Mexico at the Wilson Center's Mexico Institute, where he talked about his efforts to boost trade with its Southern neighbor. Ducey explained the case of electric car company Lucid Motors and its plans to build an assembly plant south of Phoenix, with a supply chain that will extend down to Sonora. The governor said this endeavor is an example on how the state's relationship with Mexico has led to more jobs and prosperity in Arizona.
GM extends summer shutdowns at two US sedan assembly plants

General Motors is extending the normal two-week summer shutdowns at two U.S. assembly plants to as many as five weeks amid a slump in car demand and high inventories. The Lordstown, Ohio factory, which builds the Chevrolet Cruze compact sedan, would stop production for the last two weeks in June and another three weeks in July. The Fairfax, Kansas plant, that assembles the Chevrolet Malibu midsize car, will be idled for five weeks starting in late June. GM declined to comment on the reasons for the extended downtime and whether more temporary shutdowns are scheduled.
Ford honors Thyssenkrupp steering division as one of its top global suppliers

Thyssenkrupp has been honored by automaker Ford as one of its top global suppliers in 2016. The "World Excellence Award" in the "Special Recognition" category went to the company's steering business. The award recognizes suppliers who deliver outstanding performance in customer support and development. In Mexico, Thyssenkrupp operates three plants, two in Puebla, one in San Luis Potosi, that manufacture a complete range of coil springs, stabilizer bars, steering shafts, steering columns and all its corresponding assemblies for a variety of customers in the U.S., Mexico and Canada like Volkswagen, Mercedes, BMW, Ford, GM, Chrysler and Renault Nissan.
Investment firm acquires Dynamic Technologies, deal includes a Mexican plant

Ardian, an independent private investment company, announced it will acquire Italy-based Dynamic Technologies S.p.A. (DT), a global designer and manufacturer of automotive fluid-handling systems and precision aluminum parts. Once the acquisition is completed, Ardian will merge Huron Inc., an existing portfolio company, with DT. Huron is also a designer and manufacturer of automotive fluid-handling systems based in Lexington, Michigan. The combined entity will have ten manufacturing facilities across worldwide. Dynamic Technologies Mexican facility opened in San Luis Potosi in 2012 to build aluminum fluid-handling components.
