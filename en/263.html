Chevrolet Equinox's Midnight edition arrives to our country

Chevrolet announced the arrival of the special edition Equinox Midnight to Mexico, this special edition is based on the LT version of the Chevrolet Equinox. There will only be 185 units available for sale in our country. The special edition of Chevrolet Equinox Midnight stands out by offering exclusive elements in black such as the Chevrolet bowtie, grill, leather interiors and black 19" aluminum rims. It also has xenon headlights and LED lights for safer daylight driving and the trunk offers automatic opening and closing with an obstacle censor and programmable height.
Nissan Mexicana will invest US$278 million in Aguascalientes

Automaker Nissan Mexicana will invest US$278 million in the production of a new car in Aguascalientes. In conference, Armando Avila, vice-president of Manufacturing of Nissan Mexicana, said that this is part of the brand's transformation into new technologies. "It is part of our Nissan Intelligence Mobility strategy," he described. Jose Roman, president and general director of Nissan Mexicana, commented that in light of the turbulent landscape of the global and Mexican car market, they want to be a flagship brand that takes advantage of the trust the market has deposited on them.
Suzuki Mexico's sales saw 22.3% increase in August

Suzuki Mexico informed that, in August of this year, their sales saw an increase of 22.3% in respect to the same month of last year. Suzuki sold 28.3% more in the first eight months of this year in comparison with last year. The brand highlighted that, the increase in sales, occurred in an environment in which the whole industry is exhibiting downwards numbers.
Volkswagen Mexico celebrated the production of six million Jetta units

Volkswagen Mexico celebrated the production of six million units of the Jetta model, which has been manufactured in the automaker's Puebla plant for 40 years now. A tornado red GLI, 2.0L, 230hp, made for the Mexican market was the vehicle that marked this new record. It was presented by Steffen Reiche, president of Volkswagen Mexico's executive board; management of the Puebla plant, Edgar Estrada, director of Volkswagen Mexico, and technical personnel that works in segment one of the German automaker's plant.
Mazda of Mexico sold 4,853 vehicles in August

Mazda Motor of Mexico sold 4,853 vehicles in August of this year, number that translates to a 4% drop when compared to the same month of 2018. This comes from Mazda's North America monthly sales report, which informs that, from January to August of 2019, Mazda sold 39,037 units in Mexico, which meant a 7% increase over the same period of last year.
Array Plastics will create a Mexican subsidiary in Queretaro

The Commission of Financing for the Internationalization and Development of Spain (COFIDES) will invest 1.5 million euros so that the Catalan company Array Plastics, which produces plastic pieces for the automotive sector, can establish a Mexican subsidiary. The total investment for the project, along with the contribution of FONPYME for small and medium Spanish enterprises, goes up to 2.15 million euros and it will be destined to machinery acquisition and the financing of capital. The plant where the Mexican subsidiary will be located will begin operations in September 2019 and is located in an industrial park in the state of Queretaro.
Volkswagen's T-Cross will be available in Mexico

Most of the information regarding Volkswagen's T-Cross arrival to Mexico is still under wraps, but the arrival date has been confirmed; it's September 17. The T-Cross is part of the German company's global alignment. The Latin American version was presented a day after the premiere of the European version, with minor modifications to adapt to the taste and needs of the Latin American market. There's practically no design changes. The most important modification is in its bodywork, which is 88 mm longer in Latin America.
The Fiat 500 will no longer be produced in Toluca

FCA confirmed that the vehicle in its Fiat 500 2019 and 500e versions will stop production in North America, although inventory will last until 2020. The decision was made due to market trends, in which the Fiat 500 was declining. The time has come to bid farewell to the only Italian vehicle produced in Mexico, the Fiat 500, which will stop being assembled in FCA's Toluca plant, although the available inventory will be sold up to mid-2020.
Mexican new car auto market experiences 9.7% sales drop in August

In August 2019 Mexico registered the sales of 107,651 new cars, 9.7% less to that registered in the same month of last year, informed the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This decline is the biggest one seen by the industry for an eight month of the year in 10 years, besides being the lowest sales volume since 2014. Guillermo Rosales, deputy general director of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) said that there's still economic factors within the market that prolong low vehicle demand.
FCA Mexico sold 5,106 vehicles this August

FCA Mexico sold a total of 5,106 vehicles, through their distributor network, in August from the brands Alfa Romeo, Chrysler, Dodge, FIAT, Jeep and RAM. Bruno Cattori Alonso de Florida, president and CEO of FCA Mexico, said that this is based on the fact that the company is interested in always being at the forefront to offer high-performance vehicles. RAM reported the sale of 2,090 units; Dodge sold 1,420 units in the market; Jeep registered 1,030 units; FIAT reported the sale of 505 units; Chrysler registered the sales of 47 units in August and Alfa Romero reported the sale of 14 vehicles.
