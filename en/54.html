Bulletin #54
25 May, 2017
Ford's CEO is replaced by head of smart mobility division

Ford Motor Company announced the retirement of CEO Mark Fields, who spent less than 3 years in the position and 28 years overall at the company. Fields' replace is Jim Hackett, who joined Ford's board in 2013 and, until last weekend, was head of the company's Smart Mobility unit, a position he took over in March of last year. The month Fields took over, Ford shares closed at US$ 17.41. The company's stock prices have declined almost 40% since then. But also under Fields, Ford achieved a record pretax profit of US$ 10.8 billion in 2015.
New Ford CEO defends cancellation of Mexican plant

Ford's new CEO Jim Hackett said at a press conference he had nothing to do with the decision to cancel a US$ 1.6 billion auto plant the company was building in central Mexico. At his former job as CEO of office furniture manufacturer Steelcase Inc., Hackett oversaw the opening of two plants in Mexico in 2011. Now at the helm of Ford, the new CEO defended the cancellation calling it a business decision. According to The Wall Street Journal, Ford's board executives blamed ousted CEO Mark Fields for putting the automaker on the political radar by announcing plans to move production on the Ford Focus from Michigan to San Luis Potosi in 2015.
Mexico, US automakers groups push to keep rules of origin intact

Auto industry groups of the U.S. and Mexico agree that rules of origin, which demand that products must meet minimum NAFTA-region content to be tariff-free, shouldn't be changed during the renegotiation. "Our position is that the trade agreement has been a success, and we shouldn't be touching something as important as the rules of origin," said Eduardo Solis, president of AMIA. A similar answer gave Annemarie Pender, spokeswoman for the Association of Global Automakers, a group that includes most of foreign automakers in the U.S. "We believe the NAFTA rule of origin, which establishes the highest threshold of any free trade agreement the U.S. has ever negotiated, should remain intact," she said.
Storm damages customs facilities at U.S.-Mexico border, disrupts supply chains

Auto parts deliveries to manufacturers and suppliers in all three countries of NAFTA have been delayed at the Laredo, Texas, port of entry after heavy storms and flash floods caused power outages and structural damage to customs operations on both sides of the U.S.-Mexican border. Cargo operations at the World Trade Bridge, which carries the most commercial vehicles of any port of entry on the southern border, remained suspended as custom authorities from both sides vowed to work as much as possible to have it reopened by the weekend.
Mexico-made 2018 Tiguan to feature new turbocharged engine

Volkswagen announced the Mexico-made all-new 2018 Tiguan is set to arrive in dealerships this summer powered by "the most advanced version ever of our EA888 four-cylinder engine". "The updated version of the benchmark EA888 four-cylinder, turbocharged and direct-injection engine uses an innovative modification to the conventional four-stroke cycle to offer an improved combination of power, efficiency and responsiveness," said the company. The new 2.0-liter EA888 Generation 3B is expected to offer improved fuel efficiency along with a 20% boost in maximum torque to 221 pound-feet.
Bosch ended 2016 with 32,800 employees, US$ 450 million investments in NAFTA region

Investments of Bosch in the NAFTA region totaled US$ 450 million in 2016. Projects in Michigan, South Carolina, San Luis Potosi, Aguascalientes and Chihuahua received the most significant amounts, according to a financial results the global auto supplier reported. The Germany-based manufacturer concluded its 2016 fiscal year with US$ 13.7 billion in consolidated sales in North America, slightly below from 2015's record-breaking level of US$ 14 billion. The number of associates employed throughout the U.S., Canada and Mexico in 2016 reached nearly 32,800, a year-over-year increase of more than 5%. This number is expected to increase slightly in 2017.
Magna International net income increases 19% during first quarter

Magna International Inc., a Canadian auto supplier with 31 operations and more than 24,000 employees in Mexico, reported a 19% hike in net income to US$ 586 million during the first quarter of 2017. The company totaled sales of US$ 9.37 billion for the period, an increase of 5% over the first quarter of 2016. The strong year over year growth was achieved despite North American light vehicle production declining by 1% and European light vehicle production increasing only 2% compared to the first quarter of 2016, said the company in its report. Income from operations before taxes was US$ 806 million, up 19% compared to the first quarter of 2016.
U.S. government sues Fiat Chrysler over excess emissions

The U.S. government accused Fiat Chrysler Automobiles NV of illegally using software to bypass emission controls in 104,000 diesel vehicles sold since 2014. The U.S. Justice Department lawsuit, filed in U.S. District court in Detroit, is a procedural step that may ramp up pressure on Fiat Chrysler and comes amid growing scrutiny of diesels by regulators around the world. The lawsuit could ultimately help lead to a settlement, as in an earlier probe of Volkswagen AG that will cost VW up to $25 billion, but which affected a much larger number of vehicles. VW admitted to intentionally cheating while Fiat Chrysler denies wrongdoing.
Bosch obtains final approval of diesel vehicle settlement program in the U.S.

Bosch announced it has obtained the final approval of a settlement agreement between the company and civil claimants in the United States. The agreement resolves the most substantial part of the civil law proceedings pending against Bosch in connection with Volkswagen, Audi and Porsche diesel vehicles that were sold in the U.S. The settlement covers Volkswagen and Audi diesel vehicles with 2.0L engines (model years 2009 – 2015) and Volkswagen, Audi, and Porsche diesel vehicles with 3.0L engines (model years 2009 – 2016). For this purpose, Bosch agreed to pay US$ 327.5 million, without acknowledgement or acceptance of any liability.
Ford recalls more than 3000 trucks and SUVs over safety issues

Ford Motor Company announced a safety compliance recalls in North America for certain 2017 pickups and SUVs for inadequate welds in driver seat back frames. The recall involves approximately 2,500 2017 Ford F-150, Explorer and Super Duty. Ford is not aware of any accidents or injuries associated with this issue. Another recall involves certain 2015-17 Ford F-150 and 2017 F-250 to F-550 vehicles equipped with aftermarket chrome exterior door handle covers which may unlatch during a side impact, increasing the risk of injury in a crash. Ford has identified 551 sets of accessories sold through dealerships, which will be removed.
