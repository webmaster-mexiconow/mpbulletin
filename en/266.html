Nissan announces new general director for Nissan Renault Finance Mexico

Nissan announced changes to its management team with the appointment of Andre Medeiros as general director of Nissan Renault Finance Mexico. Medeiros will be responsible for the operation and business strategy of Nissan's financial subsidiary in Mexico. Previously, Medeiros worked as director of Nissan Motor Acceptance Corporation Consumer Services in Dallas, Texas. He joined Nissan in 2013 and has assumed high responsibility roles in treasury and financing in the United States and Europe.
The renewed Hyundai Elantra is already in Mexico

The updated Hyundai Elantra has been among us only a year, but the Korean brand continues perfecting its offer, this time with novelties on a mechanical level. The 2020 model, which already has a price in Mexico, replaces the automatic six-speed transmission for a new infinitely variable transmission, called IVT. The 2020 Hyundai Elantra line is available in Mexico with a price range that goes from MXN$297,000 to MXN$395,700.
Euzkadi presents its new Overlander AT2 line

Mexican brand Euzkadi, announced to its whole distributor network the release of its new Overlander AT2 product line. Its technology was developed and tested for Mexico's roads. Its excellent performance both in and outside the road with 15% more wear resistance make it the best option for the Mexican professional of the All-Terrain segment.
Jorge Martinez is named president of IdeaLease's Board

Jorge Martinez Madero, general director of FULTRA Group (which is made up by the companies Sierra Norte Truck Center, concessionary focused on the sale and post-sale of International buses, trucks and tractor units, the spare parts wholesalers Promare, as well as Amparts Truck & Trailers and TruckMax) was elected president of IdeaLease's Incorporated board for the 2020-2021 period.
Italian brands find Queretaro an attractive investment destination

In the Bajio region, Queretaro is the most attractive entity for Italian brands, where there's five on-going investment projects. These would imply a total capital of US$5.7 million and the creation of 1,300 jobs in Queretaro, said the director of the Bajio Chapter of the Italian Commerce Chamber in Mexico, Antonio Galliani, who added that these projects will develop in a period of three years. These projects are dedicated to the manufacturing industry, specifically the automotive sector, being the Italian investment rubric with the most presence in the Bajio.
GM in Mexico has enough components to continue operations despite US strike

General Motors in Mexico has enough components to keep working, despite the strike taking place in the Unites States. They added that so far they have enough component stock, which is why there's no expected affectation for the General Motors assembling plants in Toluca, Ramos Arizpe, Silao and San Luis Potosi. Sunday night, workers from United Auto Workers initiated a strike in the brand's plants in the United States after failed negotiations on a collective contract.
Dina delivers 5 Runner10 units in Coahuila

Logisticas Odel in Coahuila acquired five 2020 Runner10 buses from Dina to provide corporate transport in Piedras Negras and Ciudad Acuña. With the delivery of these buses, whose operation was financed by Mercader Financial, Dina confirms its commitment to society to contribute safer, more profitable and comfortable transport, with the best technology and an excellent cost-benefit ratio, added Roberto Coronado, regional manager of Dina for the north zone.
