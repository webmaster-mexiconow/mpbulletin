Queretaro's automotive industry expects to work at 70% during the last week of June

The reactivation of Queretaro's automotive industry will be gradual, until it's able to reach the production levels it had before the pandemic; in the first three weeks back, starting on June 1st, the companies within this sector began operating with 35 to 40% of their productive capacity, and expect to reach 60 to 70% during the last week of June. The president of the Automotive Cluster of Queretaro, Renato Villaseñor Mendoza, explained that the reactivation of the sector will be carried out in strict adherence to health requirements.

Audi and Volkswagen gradually return to operations

Starting on Monday, the Volkswagen and Audi plants in Puebla gradually returned to operations, with 1,120 and 1,170 technical workers, respectively, and with permission from the federal government, although the governor, Miguel Barbosa Huerta, insisted that there's no conditions yet to operate because the state is in "maximum alert" due to COVID-19 contagion. In the case of auto parts suppliers, at least 15 plants began training work and protection equipment delivery to about 1,500 technicians to restart operations.

Heavy vehicle production and exports fell during April

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), informed that, during the last month of April, the production of auto transport vehicles reached a total of 2,538 units, which meant a decrease of 85.1% when compared to the same month of last year. At the same time, during April, there were a total of 2,362 units exported, which meant a fall of 82.5%, in relation to the same month of last year. On this regard, Miguel Elizalde, ANPACT's executive president, said that "the fall in both production and exports is due to the decrease in demand in the United States, Canada and Mexico markets."

Ford is celebrating 95 years in Mexico

In 1925, Henry and Edsel Ford decided to set up operations in Mexico with the help of Adrian Lajous, who became the company's first general director and they founded Ford of Mexico in Bucareli 13. Months after, they opened the first assembly and automobile finishing plant in Balbuena, in Mexico City, which would produce the impressive amount of 25 T models a day. "After 95 years of which we are very proud it's evident now more than ever, that we cannot stop looking toward the future," said Hector Perez, CEO and director of Ford of Mexico.

At least 2% of Volkswagen's workers give positive to COVID-19 testing

2% of workers in Volkswagen's plant have tested positive to fast COVID-19 tests, informed the German company. This is to say that of the total 11,400 employees that the company has at this plant, 5,100 took the test and around 100 employees tested positive. The company added that the fast tests applied to their personnel have an effectiveness, specificity and sensitivity superior to 90%, "which is interpreted like the capacity to identify healthy individuals and virus carriers respectively, allowing to incorporate COVID-19 free personnel into activities."

Traxi begins operations in Guadalajara

Traxi, Traxion's new mobility app, will start operations with the objective of transporting the inhabitants of Guadalajara in safe and totally sanitized units. The app will be available for download in Android and iOS with two available routes: Centro Sur-Andares Corporativos and San Agustin-Andares Corporativos. However, the company is planning on consolidating 10 routes by September, which will mobilize people both in the interior of Guadalajara as well as connecting to neighboring cities. Antibacterial gel will be available for users; once inside the unit, passengers will have access to completely free Wi-Fi.
