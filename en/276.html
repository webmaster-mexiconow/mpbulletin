Bulletin #276
October 22th, 2019
TIP Mexico names Oscar Andrade commercial director

Within the framework of the Expo Transporte celebrated in Puebla City, TIP Mexico, leading transport and car leasing and fleet administration company, announced the naming of Oscar A. Andrade like the company's new commercial director; who after more than twenty-two years of experience in Ford Motor Company, is now assuming this new position, previously occupied by Mauricio Medina, who is now the company's CEO.
ExxonMobil already offers ULSD in its service stations

Carlos Rivas, ExxonMobil Mexico's fuel director, presented the introduction of ultra-low sulfur diesel (ULSD) to the Mexican market, same which is already offered at the company's service stations and is produced in and imported to Mexico by the company, with the goal of giving clients a combined last generation Delvac (for diesel engines) lubricant offer and support by the Mobil Serv service platform.
Navistar hopes to increase its presence in Mexico by 4%

Navistar claimed to be prepared to face the world's economic environment and confirmed its expansion strategy for the next five years, which is to increase its presence by four percentage points in the Mexican market, which would imply additional production of 10,000 vehicles. This was confirmed by Persio Lisboa, executive vice president of Navistar Corporation, by stating that 2019 will be taken as a production record year after a 35% growth when compared to last year.
FCA presents its 2020 Jeep Wrangler Mild-Hybrid line for Mexico

FCA Group is working hard on the releases for some its brands, like important presentations that announce the integration of some models to the era of semi-hybrid vehicles, such is the case of the 2020 Jeep Wrangler Mild-Hybrid. Thanks to the support of the 48 volt battery, the system takes charge of turning the vehicle on, of the electronically assisted direction, extended injection cut and regenerative breaking.
Renault will offer the Zoe's new generation in Mexico

Finally, and after great efforts from Renault Mexico, the Zoe's new generation, the brand's 100% electric vehicle will circle through our country starting next year and will become one of alternative mobility's most attractive options for the national market. The confirmed version for Mexico will have fabric seats made with recycled materials, rear and front sensors, High Beam Assist, rear camera, wireless phone charger, AppleCar Play and Android Auto, steering wheel with leather detailing and an electrochromic rear view mirror.
BMW Group Latin America has new corporate offices

Starting on October 21st of this year BMW Group Latin America will have a new location for its corporate offices in Mexico City, in Park Plaza Torre III in Santa Fe; country that has consolidated itself like a key market and where operations for Mexico and Latin America are currently located. Matthias Schlesiger, CEO of BMW Group Financial Services for Mexico and Latin America and leader of this project, affirmed that "the new offices reflect the dynamism, innovation and, above all, the commitment that the company has with Mexico and Latin America."
The door for the legalization of chocolate cars is open

The Chamber of Deputies, meeting in plenary, endorsed two proposal from the Morena caucus that were added to the 2020 Federal Revenue Law (LIF) as transitory articles, which consist of the Federal Electricity Commission (CFE) "forgiving" debt to states and municipalities and the regularization of "chocolate cars" through a tax payment. Coparmex lamented this action because "at the last minute in Congress, without control or deliberation with the private sector of the economic consequences, the regularization of chocolate cars was authorized in the Revenue Law. There will be harmful consequences for the automotive sector," they warned.
