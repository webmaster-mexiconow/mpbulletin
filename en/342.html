30 auto parts companies in Puebla return at 30% of their capacity

Starting on Monday, 30 auto parts supplying companies returned to productive activities at 30% of their productive capacity and personnel, after almost 12 weeks of suspension due to the COVID-19 pandemic, after receiving permits from local and federal authorities. The general secretary from the Confederation of Mexican Workers (CTM) in Puebla, Leobardo Soto Martínez, explained that the companies needed to restart operations because automotive plants in the United States, Asia and Europe restarted their work and were asking for supplies to be able to produce.

Edgar Casal Alvarez is Audi Mexico's new general director

Audi Mexico announced that it's changing directors. After 12 years of leading the brand, Walter Hanek will leave his position due to retirement and will be returning to Germany. Because of this, starting on July 1st 2020, Edgar Casal Alvarez, will become Audi Mexico's general director. "The most important thing in a transition is that the successor is the right person. I'm glad that Edgar Casal, after successfully leading Porsche and SEAT, has decided to take the position of director at Audi Mexico," commented Walter Hanek.

SEAT and Volkswagen make organizational changes

Starting on July 1st, Alfonso Chiquini will assume Volkswagen's marketing direction in Mexico, while Juan Pablo Gomez Macfarland will now helm SEAT and Cupra in Mexico as general director. These are key moves for Volkswagen's optimal growth both as a brand and as a Group. Alfonso Chiquini has more than 24 years of experience in the automotive industry, during which he's had directive responsibilities within the Group.

Volkswagen bets on digital commercialization

Due to social confinement as a health measurement to prevent the spread of the COVID-19 pandemic, Volkswagen has faced the challenge of commercializing its vehicles through digital schemes after sales floors throughout the country closed during April and May. According to Edgar Estrada, the brand's director, possible vehicle buyers will be able to acquire a unit in five simple steps through their website, getting information on different models through digital channels and with the guidance of an advisor make payments and receive a new vehicle all the way to their doorstep.

General Motors presents new Smart Driver service

OnStar, General Motors' high technology service which provides safety and connectivity services presented "Smart Driver," a new service within the mobile app which allows clients with an active OnStar subscription to take advantage of the data generated by their vehicle with the objective of making their driving more efficient. Smart Driver uses a great part of the OnStar technology to measure in every drive the times the driver brakes and accelerates suddenly, traveled distance, fuel economy, average speed and time driven at night, among other variables.

The heavy vehicles industry seeks to affirm its leadership with the USMCA: ANPACT

The USMCA entered into vigor on Wednesday and is substituting de North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA). "With the USMCA we're going to affirm our global leadership, which boasts Mexico as the first tractor truck exporter, fourth exporter and sixth producer of cargo vehicles worldwide," said Miguel Elizalde, executive president of ANPACT. He admitted that the new requirements and their way of applying them, "add complexity and imply a cumbersome process for companies. However, having a treaty and uniform regulations is a positive thing, since it gives the sector legal certainty and definition."

Japanese automotive manufacturers will keep their operations in Mexico

According to Asian Nikkei Review, "Japanese automobile manufacturers are choosing to keep their operations in Mexico and either pay Mexican workers more or even just pay tariffs," contrary to what Donald Trump hoped to achieve with the USMCA, attracting said manufacturing sites to the United States. However, Mexico has other things going for it: one of the main ones is how costly it is to move production, especially when the plants located in Mexico have proven to be highly efficient and have great quality in their vehicles.
