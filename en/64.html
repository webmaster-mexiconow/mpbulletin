BMW plant triggers US$ 398 million investments in San Luis Potosi

Since the announcement of its construction in 2014, the BMW plant in San Luis Potosi has triggered foreign investments for US$ 398 million from auto suppliers in the state. Eduardo Nunez, purchasing manager for the German firm in Mexico, said suppliers currently develop 39 green fields in the central state to meet the premium automaker needs. "We have more than 20 years buying parts in Mexico, more than 100 suppliers produce for us (for all of our plants in the world) and that is why we continue to grow. We see the potential for suppliers is enormous in the North American region. Many OEMs have many years here and that helps development," he said.
Bridgestone to supply every new assembly plant opening in Mexico in next two years

Tire manufacturer Bridgestone Corporation announced it will supply every new assembly plant opening in Mexico during the next two years, that means Toyota, Infiniti, Mercedes-Benz and BMW will all be added to its customers portfolio. The Japanese firm currently supplies nearly every automaker in Mexico, including Mazda, Nissan, FCA, Audi, Volkswagen, Honda and even premium brands such as BMW and Mercedes-Benz receive at their plants overseas run flat tires (RFT) made in Cuernavaca, Morelos. In Mexico, Bridgestone manufactures more than seven million tires a year through its plants in Cuernavaca, Monterrey, and Leon.
Mexican automotive sector boosts manufacturing exports during May

Mexican exports continued to grow last May due to the dynamism of manufacturing sales abroad, especially in the automotive sector, according to the latest official data. The value of exports in May 2017 was US$ 35.4 billion, 12.9% higher than same period from the previous year and second highest rate since October 2012. The increase in Mexican sales abroad was a result of a 13.8% increase in non-oil exports and a 3.5% drop in oil-related shipments. Manufacturing exports reported an annual increase of 12.9%, driven by shipments in the automotive sector which reported a 13.8% hike in May totaling US$ 10.7 billion.
Nachi-Fujikoshi opens US$ 13 million production facility in Queretaro

Japanese machining tools and systems and machine components manufacturer Nachi-Fujikoshi Corp. officially opened in the state of Queretaro its first production site in Mexico. The company revealed it invested US$ 13 million to build the 107,000 square-feet facility located at the Aerotech Industrial Park in the municipality of Colon. On full deployment, the plant will employ 200 workers to manufacture bearings. Nachi expects to reach a 7 million pieces output during 2017, with plans to increase it up to 10 million in 2018.
J.D. Power highlights the Mexico-made Kia Forte in its Initial Quality Study

For second year in a row Kia topped J.D. Power's Initial Quality Study, a distinction granted to the automaker with the least problems reported by new-car owners in the first 90 days of ownership. Kia finished with 72 problems per 100 vehicles, while another South Korean make – Genesis, which is Hyundai's new luxury brand and is ranked for the first time – came in second with 77 problems per 100. The Kia Forte, built in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon, was recognized with the best initial quality in the compact car segment, outperforming models such as the Chevrolet Cruze, the Toyota Corolla and the Volkswagen Jetta.
Panalpina considers Queretaro for a frequent cargo destination

A carrier Boeing 747-8 landed at the Queretaro International Airport last week as part of a flight test from Swiss logistic solutions provider Panalpina, which is planning to add the terminal as a weekly destination. The chartered flight supplied goods for two Panalpina clients with automotive operations in the Queretaro area. Panalpina operates two weekly flights from Luxemburg to Mexico City and Guadalajara. This first landing in Queretaro helped the company to get the proper certifications in order to operate at the airport, which has all the requirements and specifications to host a Boeing 747-8 and its up to 140 tons of cargo.
NXP, Harman expand collaboration for connected car solutions

NXP Semiconductors N.V., a global leader in car infotainment semiconductors and automotive semiconductors and Harman International, a wholly owned subsidiary of Samsung focused on connected technologies, announced plans to expand their 15-year collaboration to speed up time-to-market for connected car solutions. The two companies will further expand their partnership in technologies supporting the connected in-car experience, including tuners, digital signal processors, amplifiers and applications processors. NXP operates a facility in Tlaquepaque, Jalisco, while Harman owns three productions sites in Mexico, located in Tijuana, Juarez and Queretaro.
Schaeffler profit warning sparks sector worries

A profit warning from German manufacturing group Schaeffler sent tremors through the industry on Tuesday after the car parts supplier complained of "pricing pressures" from carmakers. Shares in Schaeffler, a leader in the supply of ball bearings and other essential car parts, fell 11.8% as it cut its 2017 outlook for operating profit margins. In Germany, parts supplier Continental fell 3.5% and car cables supplier Leoni fell 3.6%. In New York, shares of propulsion technology systems maker BorgWarner opened 4% lower. Every car manufacturer in the world has a relationship with Schaeffler, and a typical car contains US$ 114 of its parts inside, the company said.
Adient develops removable seat covers for ride-sharing market

With an eye in the growing market of ride-sharing services, Adient, the global leader in automotive seating, has developed removable seat covers to maintain comfort, cleanliness and individualization for drivers and passengers. With an integrated zipper, Adient's seat covers are easy to remove and clean. Covers are washable and removing the seat cover will not damage the foam in the structure. Adient foam is protected by a lining underneath the removable covers, eliminating the need to be restored or replaced. The company owns 13 facilities located in the states of Chihuahua (3), Coahuila (5), State of Mexico, Tamaulipas, Puebla, Queretaro and Tlaxcala.
GM settles hundreds of ignition switch lawsuits

General Motors has agreed to settle federal lawsuits by as many as 203 plaintiffs over defective ignition switches in its vehicles, court filing shows. Settlement terms are confidential, but the accord could also resolve hundreds of state court claims, as well, lawyers for the automaker said in the filing in Manhattan federal court. GM had been defending against hundreds of lawsuits over faulty ignition switches that could cause engines to stall and prevent airbags from deploying in crashes. The defect has been linked to 124 deaths and 275 injuries, and prompted a recall in February 2014. GM has paid about US$ 2.5 billion in settlements related to the defect.
