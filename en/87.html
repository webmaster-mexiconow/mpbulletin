Volkswagen production at Puebla facility increases 10% from January through August

From January through August, Volkswagen built 322,831 vehicles at the Puebla assembly plant, an increase of 10.1% compared to the 293,285 units from the same eight-month period from a year ago. Last month alone, production reached 46,970 vehicles, up 3.5% compared to August 2016, revealed local media last week citing figures from the AMIA. Exports from the Puebla facility totaled 256,164 vehicles during the first eight months of 2017, a 12.4% hike compared to the 227,886 exported from January through August 2016. However, last month exports saw a double-digit drop of 18.9% to 29,339 vehicles, compared to 36,155 units in August 2016.
Mexican auto dealers lower sales forecast for 2017

The Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) slightly adjusted its sales forecast for this year from 1,665,000 units to 1,615,000 vehicles. AMDA general director, Guillermo Rosales Zarate, said that this adjustment still leads to a positive result compared to the closing of 2016, when it totaled 1,603,000 units, although the figure is far from the initial forecast for 2017 which had been located at 1.7 million units. The official stressed that sales of 131,000 vehicles are expected for the month of September.
Germany-based Voss Automotive inaugurates its new plant in Arteaga, Coahuila

After 14 months of construction work, Germany-based manufacturer Voss Automotive officially opened last week its new US$ 10 million plant at the Server Industrial Park in Arteaga, Coahuila. The company started operations in 2011 at a leased facility before it moved operations to its own 148,000-square-foot site. This plant manufactures rubber pipes, connectors, valves, air suspension components and assemblies for emission, fluids and refrigeration systems for commercial and passenger vehicles. Eduardo Vultorius, VOSS CEO for the NAFTA region, said the company has invested US$ 79 million since its arrival to Mexico.
Korean brakes supplier builds plant in Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila

Korean manufacturer Sangsin Brake Automotive builds since las July its first manufacturing facility in Mexico at the Server Industrial Park in Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila, with plans to begin operations by the end of this year or early 2018. This will be the fifth Korean company to operate at this industrial development. For that end, the company acquired 4 hectares of greenfield in order to place its 18,000 square-meter facility. From this facility, Sangsin plans to supply brakes to the Kia assembly plant in the neighbor state of Nuevo Leon but it also expects to gain additional market share among other original equipment manufacturers (OEM).
Korean-based Hyosung GST opens production facility in Torreon, Coahuila

Korean-based textile manufacturer Hyosung Global Safety Textiles (GST) officially opened its second plant in Mexico, this time in Torreon, Coahuila, to supply fabric and airbags for the automotive industry. The company invested US$ 18 million to build a 25,000 square-feet facility at the PYMES Industrial Park. The plant is expected to create 700 jobs once it reaches its full capacity with the addition of automotive floor carpet manufacturing. Hyosung GST broke ground to build this facility in June 2016 and started partial operations nine months later, in March 2017. The company also operates a production site in Ensenada, Baja California.
Kia assembly plant in Nuevo Leon celebrates first anniversary of its inauguration

Kia Motors assembly plant in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon, celebrated the first anniversary of its official inauguration operating at full throttle to reach a total 242,000 vehicles built since it began production. According to company officials, 107,000 units rolled out of the facility from day one in May 2016 through December 2016. An additional 135,000 vehicles have been built at the plant from January through August 2017. Kia has built hatchback and sedan versions of the Rio and the Forte models from day one at this site, adding production of the Accent for its sister brand Hyundai starting last July.
Honda adds new top-of-range trim level for the 2018 HR-V compact SUV

Even though Honda does not offer aesthetic changes for the 2018 Honda HR-V compact SUV, except for new wheels, now the Japanese automaker presents the Touring trim as top of range, with a more complete and luxurious equipment. The Honda HR-V 2018 remains unchanged in terms of the engine, which is the 1.8-liter inline 4-cylinder, 16-valve SOHC and i-VTEC system that develops 141 hp at 6,500 rpm, as well as a torque of 127 foot-pounds at 4,300 rpm. The 2018 HR-V for the North American market is built at Honda's assembly plant located in El Salto, Jalisco.
New Jeep Compass receives maximum safety rating from EuroNCAP

The new Jeep Compass has attained the maximum five-star EuroNCAP rating after scoring positive ratings in all categories -including adult protection, child protection, pedestrian protection and driving assistance safety systems. The result is even more significant considering that it meets the 2017 severe, ever increasing EuroNCAP standards. Making a major contribution to the Jeep Compass's maximum five-star rating are the effectiveness of its 'safety cage' -65% of which is constructed of high-strength steel, plus the availability of more than 70 active and passive safety systems. The Jeep Compass is built at four global locations, including the FCA's Toluca plant.
Daimler Trucks celebrates Freightliner and Western Star anniversaries

Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA) is celebrating two significant anniversaries: 75 years of Freightliner Trucks and 50 years of Western Star Trucks, respectively. Since their founding days in 1942 respectively 1967, Freightliner and Western Star have put a total of about 3,200,000 trucks on the road. In the first half of 2017, Daimler Trucks has brought 42,300 units to market. DTNA leads the market in classes 6-8 with a market share of 40.2% in the NAFTA region. DTNA produces vehicles of the Freightliner, Western Star, and Thomas Built Buses brands. The DTNA production network encompasses nine locations, including two facilities in Coahuila and Mexico State.
Workers go on strike against GM at Ingersoll, Ontario, assembly plant

Members of Unifor, the largest private sector union in Canada, have gone on strike against General Motors Co. at the company's assembly plant in Ingersoll, Ontario, a move that cuts off the main supply of the auto maker's hot-selling Chevrolet Equinox vehicles. Negotiators for the auto maker and Unifor, the union that represents about 2,800 workers, were unable to reach a deal on a new contract before a 10:59 p.m. Sunday deadline. GM officials refused to designate Cami as the lead plant for future Equinox production. The crossover is also assembled in small numbers at the Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila, and San Luis Potosi plants in Mexico.
