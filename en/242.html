June 7th, 2019
Volkswagen Mexico publishes its production results

Volkswagen Mexico published its production results for May, in which 41,786 vehicles were manufactured at its plant in Puebla, a number that represented a decrease of 5.6% compared to the same period of 2018, when 44,311 vehicles were produced. On the other hand, in the accumulated amount from January to May, the Puebla factory produced 190,180 units, which represented a growth of 22% compared to the same period of the previous year, when it reported 155,835 manufactured vehicles.
Faurecia Mexico invests US$20 million in Puebla

The auto parts firm Faurecia has invested US$20 million in Puebla, where it's set to construct a building in which specialized services for the automotive industry will be created, designed, and provided. Francisco Maciel, CFO of Faurecia Mexico, explained that in the new facilities, in which more than 700 people will be employed, specialized services will be provided to the United States, Europe, and Mexico.
Nissan Mexicana reaches a milestone within the industry by achieving 10 consecutive years as a sales' leader

Nissan Mexicana finished May 2019 off by establishing a historic milestone, as it achieved 10 consecutive years of sales leadership, a goal that no other automotive firm in Mexico had ever achieved before. "These 10 years of leadership in Mexico would not be possible without the confidence and preference of the public for our products, services, and technologies, as well as the joint work of an extraordinary team of more than 17,000 collaborators," said Mayra Gonzalez, president and CEO of Nissan Mexicana.
BMW Group inaugurates its US$1 billion production plant in San Luis Potosi

BMW executives are opening the first automotive assembly plant in Mexico, a week after President Donald Trump struck a blow to their plans with a new tariff threat to Mexican products. BMW invested close to US$1 billion in the factory, which is located in the vast arid plains of San Luis Potosi. It is one of the three facilities -the other two are located in Germany and China- that will be producing the next generation Series 3 sedan for its sale within the United States' market.
Production and domestic sales of light vehicles in Mexico decrease in May

In May of this year, the production of light vehicles in Mexico fell by 1.47%, while exports showed an increase of 5.61%. Nevertheless, domestic sales fell by 11.18%, the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI) informed. According to the monthly report of the administrative record of the light vehicle automotive industry for May, 102,081 new vehicles were sold in Mexico, which compared to 114,933 in the same month of the previous year, resulted in a decrease of 11.18%.
Tariffs on Mexican goods could impact one in five companies

The imposition of 5% tariffs on Mexican goods by the United States government can impact the income of one in five companies qualified by the Fitch Ratings agency, especially those related to the automotive, manufacturing, and energy industries, the company warned in a press release. "[The tariff] could have a direct negative effect on the income of 20% of companies with a Mexican rating at Fitch Ratings. One in five Mexican issuers with a Fitch rating exports to the United States," said Jay Djemal, head of Latin American credit research corporations at Fitch.
Uber resumes its operations in Cancun

Just a day after the mobilizations of taxi drivers against digital transport platforms, Uber Mexico announced its return to Cancun, Quintana Roo, on Wednesday. "Starting tomorrow, June 6, Uber will restart its operations in Cancun, offering more than 500,000 citizens a reliable and accessible mobility option for their trips at the touch of a button," said Saul Crespo, communications manager at Uber Mexico.
