Mexico to remain an automotive force after Trump, report says

Mexico will remain a force in the automotive industry even as President Donald Trump looks to revamp NAFTA, PricewaterhouseCoopers said in a report about the industry. "Mexico has leveraged its low wages, free trade agreements and proximity to US markets to attract millions of units of new capacity, as well as a growing supply chain to support it," cites the PwC report. The consulting company estimated Mexican light-vehicle capacity will expand 37% from 2016 to 2023, to 4.8 million units, according to the paper.
Exedy-Dynax opens new production facility in Aguascalientes

Japan-based drivetrain systems manufacturer Exedy Corporation completed a US$87.8 million investment at its subsidiary Exedy-Dynax Mexico, bringing about 647 new jobs to its production site located in central state of Aguascalientes. This is the third capital investment the Japanese clutches and torque converters manufacturer makes in Mexico, this time meant to expand the facilities in the plant by 136,000 square meters. This plant serves clients such as Jatco, Nissan, Isuzu, Suzuki, Ford, General Motors, Toyota, Mitsubishi, Hyundai and Honda.
FCA recalls more than 1 million Ram trucks due to air bag, software issue

FCA is recalling more than 1 million Ram pickups in North America due to a software glitch that could prevent side air bags and seatbelts from deploying during a rollover. The company said that it is aware of one death, two injuries and two accidents that may be related to the problem. The recall includes some 2013-16 Ram 1500 and 2500 pickups and 2014-2016 Ram 3500 pickups. In addition to vehicles in the U.S., it also affects about 216,007 vehicles in Canada, 21,668 in Mexico and 21,530 outside the NAFTA region. The company plans to reprogram the software in affected vehicles free of charge. The recall is expected to begin June 23.
Faurecia to open new seating production site in June

French auto supplier Faurecia plans to open next month new production lines at a plant located in the municipality of Huejotzingo, Puebla, central Mexico. The ceremony will take place on June 5. The company is investing US$ 200 million in Mexico to update and expand most of its 15 facilities around the country, being the expansion at Huejotzingo the most significant. This project will require around 300 employees to manufacture seats for several automakers, including Audi. All three Faurecia's divisions have operations in Mexico, that means a total 1,800 jobs will open in the coming months due to expansion plans for the exhaust systems, interiors and seating business.
Ford lays off 130 workers at Ohio plant

Ford Motor Co. has temporarily laid off 130 workers at its plant in Avon Lake, Ohio. The company had hoped to preserve those jobs by moving production there from Mexico in 2015. The layoffs started May 8 and will run through the end of September, most of them are voluntary. Laid off employees with at least one year of seniority will receive pay while they don't work, equal to roughly 75% of what they would be making at the plant. Ford announced in 2014 that it was bringing the trucks back from Nuevo Leon to the factory near Cleveland after a joint venture in Mexico with Navistar International Corp. disbanded.
Koller Group starts operations in Puebla to supply parts for Volkswagen

Bavaria-based Koller Group started operations in Puebla to manufacture injection-molded parts. The company invested US$ 6.5 million so far and recruited 100 workers. The Koller Group, a family-managed technology company has established itself as an automotive industry supplier for virtually all European car manufacturers. The company will supply lightweight trunk floors for the Golf sedan and Tiguan compact crossover, which are built in the Volkswagen assembly located just a few miles from this new facility.
Built-in-Mexico Jeep Compass receives top honors at 23rd annual NWAPA ‘Mudfest'

The all-new 2017 Jeep Compass, built in Toluca, Mexico, was named the Best Compact Utility Vehicle at the 23rd annual "Mudfest" competition hosted by the Northwest Automotive Press Association (NWAPA). Twenty-eight NWAPA automotive journalists spent two days testing 27 vehicles through a mix of on-road handing and off-road courses. Testing took place at The Ridge Motorsports Park in Shelton, Washington, where a custom off-road course was built to push the limits of the vehicles.
Japanese auto supplier Samtech builds US$ 27 million plant in Guanajuato

Japanese auto supplier Samtech broke ground to build a manufacturing plant at Leon-Bajio Industrial Park (PILBA), located in the municipality of Leon, Guanajuato. The company plans to invest US$ 27 million and recruiting of 75 workers to manufacture gears and bearing hubs for automotive applications. Samtech will be the second firm to establish operations at PILBA, where Michelin also builds its US$ 510 million facility. At the breaking ground ceremony, Governor Miguel Marquez said 71 Japanese companies have started operations under his term, creating 23,479 direct jobs.
Visteon's net income more than triples during first quarter

Michigan-based electronics auto supplier Visteon Corporation reported that its first-quarter net income more than tripled to US$ 63 million from US$ 19 million in the same period of 2016, reflecting higher electronics production volume and new business. During the first quarter, global vehicle manufacturers awarded Visteon new business of US$ 1.5 billion in lifetime revenue, with infotainment systems accounting for 44% of it. The company owns manufacturing plants in Reynosa and Chihuahua, where it also operates a Technical Center.
Japan Climate Systems opens facility in Salamanca to supply Mazda auto plant

Japan Climate Systems Corporation (JCS) opened a production site in Salamanca, Guanajuato, where the company invested US$ 4.7 million to build HVAC units for Mazda. This is JCS's second production site in Mexico, the company first started operations in the country by the end of 2013, manufacturing heater and blower units at a facility in El Salto, Jalisco. The company recruited 100 workers for its new plant located at the Mazda Suppliers Park, next to the assembly plant where the Japanese automaker builds the Mazda2 and Mazda3 models.
