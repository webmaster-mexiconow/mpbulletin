Bulletin #43
17 April, 2017
Mitsubishi Steel Manufacturing starts operations in Aguascalientes

Mitsubishi Steel Manufacturing (MSSC) started operations at a new production facility in Aguascalientes, after a US$ 20 million investment. The new plant located at the Finsa Industrial Park recruited 95 workers on its first phase to manufacture stabilizer bars for Nissan. The company expects to produce 400,000 pieces a year once the facility runs at full capacity. A second investment will be made in the near term to manufacture coil springs at the same facility.
Slovenian firm TBP invests in Tlaxcala to supply bowden cables for Audi

After a US$ 3.5 million investment, Slovenian company Tovarna Bovdenov in Plastike (TBP) is set to start operations in the municipality of Tlaxco, central state of Tlaxcala. TBP recruited 40 workers to manufacture Bowden Cables for Audi's Assembly plant, located just 20 minutes away in the neighbor state of Puebla. TBP customers' portfolio includes some of the biggest names in the automotive industry, including Volkswagen, BMW, Audi, Skoda, Seat, Lamborghini, Porsche, Mercedez-Benz, Bentley, Rolls Royce and Bugatti.
Chinese firm to build a new plant in Indiana instead of expanding Mexican facility

BeijingWest Industries Co. Ltd., whose customers include Audi, BMW, GM and Porsche, will invest more than US $80 million to build a 276,000 square-foot plant in Greenfield, Indiana, creating up to 441 high-wage jobs by 2021. An executive said high demand forced the company to decide between expanding its current facility in Chihuahua, Mexico, or building a new one. The Indiana Economic Development Corp. plans to offer BWI up to US$ 4.5 million in conditional tax credits and up to US$ 200,000 in training grants. The city of Greenfield is considering additional tax incentives as well.
Border tax is not dead, executives warn at New York Auto Show

Chances for a tax on goods crossing the border into the U.S. from Mexico to become effective stirred up debate at the NADA/J.D. Power Automotive Forum in the New York Auto Show. While Nariman Behravesh, chief economist for IHS Markit, said, "that's dead", Mitch Bainwol, CEO of the Alliance of Automobile Manufacturers, said he thought the border tax has a "less than 50-50" chance of passing. On the other hand, John Bozzella, CEO of the Association of Global Automakers, said, "I'm from the school of politics that it's never dead until it's actually dead."
Tesla overtakes GM as the most valuable U.S. car maker

Silicon Valley's Tesla overtook General Motors to become the U.S. car maker with the largest market capitalization. It's the first time the most valuable U.S. car maker is not based in Detroit. Helped by an analyst's recommendation, Tesla rose 3.26% to a record high of US$ 312.39 last week. Its market value of US$ 50.887 billion exceeded GM's by about US$ 1 million. Last year Tesla sold 76,230 units, missing its target of at least 80,000 vehicles. By comparison, GM sold 10 million cars and Ford sold 6.7 million.
Lucid makes its first auto show appearance in New York

The luxury electric car known as the Lucid Air made its auto show debut in New York. The vehicle has long been considered a potential competitor to the Tesla Model S. The Lucid Air's price will start at US$ 52,000 with federal tax credits for a unit with 240 miles of range. The car can go from zero to 60 miles per hour in "less than 2.5 seconds," according to the company. The Lucid Air is scheduled for production in 2019 at a production facility in Arizona, currently under construction, with supply chain components from Sonora, Mexico.
BMW Group sales perform best-ever March

BMW Group sales achieved their best ever March, with sales in the month totaling 254,862, a 5.9% increase. It was also a record first quarter with sales increasing by 5.3% to a total of 587,237 vehicles delivered. In the U.S. market the company sold 36,002 units in March and 81,933 vehicles in the first quarter, 3.5% and 0.6% increases respectively. In Mexico and the rest of Latin America 3,086 units were sold in March and 8,533 vehicles in the first quarter, reflecting 22% and 19.3% increases respectively.
Seat sales grow by 14% in the first quarter

Spain-based automaker Seat closed the first quarter of the year with its highest sales figure since 2001. Worldwide deliveries grew by 14.0% compared to the same period in 2016 to stand at 117,300 vehicles. Seat delivered 53,200 cars in March, which represents an increase of 14.4% over the same month last year. In Mexico, the company sold 6,800 units over the first quarter, a 11.0% increase. In January Seat updated the Leon, the brand's best-selling model. The fifth-generation Ibiza entered the market in the first week of April.
California grants Apple permit for autonomous vehicles testing

California's Department of Motor Vehicles added Apple to the list of companies allowed to test self-driving light vehicles on public roads. The company joins a list of 29 automakers, suppliers and tech companies, including fellow Silicon Valley titan Google, allowed to perform such tests in the state. Apple registered three 2015 Lexus RX 450h crossovers, and six drivers for this endeavor.
VW to build a new SUV in Chattanooga, TN

Volkswagen announced at the New York International Auto Show it would produce a second SUV at its Chattanooga plant. VW's new Atlas SUV is already being assembled there and is expected to be in showrooms around the country in about a month. The company already produces the Touareg and the Tiguan, both of which are SUVs. In addition, the company said it would offer an all-new, larger Tiguan later this year (built in Mexico), but continue to sell the current Tiguan as the Tiguan Limited. Thus, the new, Chattanooga-produced vehicle, expected to be a five-seater, would be a fifth SUV line.
