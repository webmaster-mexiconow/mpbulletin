Audi Mexico is named Socially Responsible Enterprise for the third time

Audi Mexico was named, for the third consecutive occasion, a Socially Responsible Enterprise by the Mexican Center for Philanthropy (Cemefi) and the Alliance for Corporate Social Responsibility (AliaRSE). "From the inauguration of the Audi Mexico plant, we have undertaken the task of meeting all the requirements to be a socially responsible enterprise," declared Niels Bosse, vice president of Human Resources and Organization. "We know that our initiatives such as Green Energy or our commitment to society, are the right path to be recognized for the third consecutive year with this distinctive."

MAN Truck & Bus Mexico opens the doors to its new Tabasco distributor

A new member of the MAN Truck & Bus Mexico family was added in Tabasco for the commercialization of the MAN-Volkswagen Truck and Bus products. Camiones Modernos de Tabasco belonging to Grupo HM Automotriz, is opening its doors on the Vista Hermosa-Cardenas highway kilometer 8,600 in Villahermosa, Tabasco where they will be attending to the cargo and passenger segment in a 1,000 square meter distributor, which will be integral with a showroom for unit sales, spare parts sales for both brands and aftersales service.

Mexico's automotive industry will see recovery in 2022: S&P

American agency S&P Global Ratings estimated that Mexico's automotive industry could recover from the strong economic contraction, as well as the fall in consumption, by 2022, however a resurge in the pandemic caused by the novel coronavirus would generate a greater delay in its recuperation. "The lack of a material economic stimulus or an aggressive weakening of the social distancing measures that would lead to a spike in COVID-19 cases would delay the recovery," said the firm.

Alfa begins process to split from Nemak

Mexican conglomerate Alfa informed on Friday morning that it called for a stockholders meeting, which will be held mid-August to propose the split from its subsidiary Nemak, which manufactures components for the automotive industry. The split of the companies implies the constitution of a firm named "Controladora Nemak" which would be enrolled in the Mexican Stock Exchange (BMV), and to which Alfa's total participation in the already existing Nemak will be transferred.

The automotive industry rebuilds its logistic chains after the pandemic

After the halt of operations in automotive manufacturing plants as a health measure to avoid the spread of the pandemic caused by COVID-19, the automotive industry has had to accelerate the reconstruction of its logistic chains. "For the restart of operations national transport was easily activated because we luckily had the capacity and were only awaiting the restart, as for the mobility of merchandise that arrived to ports we had to administer that so our containers would not fall into abandonment," assured Evelin Nava, director of logistics of FCA Mexico.
