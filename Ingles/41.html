Mexico's auto production, exports reached record figures in March

Mexico's auto production boosted 36.2% in March compared to same month last year, while exports were up 32.7% in the same period. Auto plants established in the country produced a record 363,687 cars and light trucks last month and exported 297,571 of those vehicles, AMIA said. Exports to the United States, Mexico's top trading partner, hiked 37.8% to total 225,741 units. Meanwhile, exports to Canada were down 0.8% to 24,426 units. During first quarter 2017 Mexico produced a record 943,704 vehicles, a 17.1% hike compared to same period of 2016. Exports totaled 750,162 units, also a record figure and a 14.2% increase.
Kia to invest US$ 150 million in Nuevo Leon this year

Hyundai Motor Co. and its affiliate Kia Motors Corp. will invest US$ 983.9 million in facilities overseas during this year, including US$ 149.91 million for the plant Kia opened last year in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon. Most of their production bases overseas have already surpassed 100% of operating rates, making inevitable for them to expand their facilities. The average operating rate of Kia Motors'global production facilities is overloaded with 100.5%, the Mexican plant alone operates at 105.1%, according to figures released by Korean media last week.
Kolbenschmidt gains contract to supply steel pistons from its Celaya facility

German auto components manufacturer KS Kolbenschmidt GmbH (KSPG), a member of the Rheinmetall Group, has been awarded a contract for commercial vehicle steel pistons worth over US$ 74.6 million. The product will be manufactured at the company's facility located in Celaya, Guanajuato. The ultramodern steel pistons will be supplied over a period of six years as from the fourth quarter of 2019. The Mexican location of KS Kolbenschmidt manufactures steel pistons for the North American market since 2013. Besides making the actual pistons, the manufacturer completes the assembly with rings and pins.
Aeromexico starts new daily, non-stop Detroit-Mexico City route

Aeromexico started a new route in the United States, Detroit-Mexico City with a daily non-stop flight on April 1st. This flight adds to the recent announcement of the Monterrey-Detroit route, starting on May 1st. The Mexico City flight is operated with Boeing 737-700 and 737-800 aircraft. And the Monterrey flight will be operated with Embraer 190 aircraft. "Convenient connections will be available to Aguascalientes, Queretaro, Bajio, Hermosillo and other key cities with a strong influence on the automotive industry," said Jorge Goytortua, Senior VP Global Sales.
Spark Minda KTSN opens US$ 20 million facility in Queretaro

Automotive component supplier Spark Minda KTSN opened its new manufacturing plant located at Queretaro Industrial Park. The company invested US$ 20 million in its new facility to manufacture interior automotive components such as Glove Box and Panel Parts for Volkswagen. The new plant has a total area of 8,500 square meters and started operations with 130 employees. The plant has an additional 5,000 square-meters space in the similar premises for further growth. Once fully operational, the facility's workforce is expected to reach 350 workers.
Erich Jaeger opens facility in Coahuila to supply Daimler truck plant

After a US$ 6 million investment and one year of construction works, electric components supplier Erich Jaeger opened a new plant at the border municipality of Piedras Negras, Coahuila. The company will hire 500 workers to manufacture sockets, connectors and adapters for heavy trucks. The plant will supply its products to the Derramadero truck plant located in Saltillo, the state capital, where Daimler builds Mercedes Benz and Freightliner Class 4-8 units. Germany-based Erich Jaeger develops and manufactures connector systems for the automobile, commercial, agriculture and military vehicle industries worldwide. This is Erich Jaeger's first production facility in Mexico.
American Axle completes acquisition of Metaldyne Performance; three Mexican plants included

American Axle & Manufacturing Holdings, Inc. (AAM) announced that it has completed the acquisition of Metaldyne Performance Group Inc. (MPG). The transaction was unanimously approved by the boards of directors of both companies and recently approved by stockholders of AAM and MPG. MPG operates Mexican three facilities located in Aguascalientes; Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila and El Carmen, Nuevo Leon. AAM owns two production sites in Silao, Guanajuato.
Tesla passes Ford in market value, reaches quarterly sales/production record

Tesla surpassed Ford in market value for the first time, illustrating the growing investors'optimism over new players versus traditional carmakers from Detroit. Tesla shares surged as high as US$ 304.81, which put its market capitalization around US$ 49.5 billion, ahead of Ford's US$ 44.4 billion. GM remains on top with US$ 51.3 billion. Tesla delivered just over 25,000 vehicles in the first quarter, a new record and a 69% increase over same period of 2016. Quarterly production totaled 25,418 vehicles, also a record. Tesla expects to deliver 50,000 vehicles in first half of 2017.
Ford ranked as leading company in self-driving development by Navigant Research

Ford Motor Company ranks first in the strategy and execution of self-driving cars, according to a new independent Leaderboard Report from Navigant Research. The players were rated on 10 criteria: vision; go-to market strategy; partners; production strategy; technology; sales, marketing, and distribution; product capability; product quality and reliability; product portfolio; and staying power. The complete ranking goes as follows: 1. Ford, 2. GM, 3. Renault-Nissan Alliance, 4. Daimler, 5. Volkswagen Group, 3. BMW, 7. Waymo, 7. Volvo/Autoliv/Zenuity, 9. Delphi, 10. Hyundai Motor Group.
March marks Mercedes-Benz strongest-ever sales month

March 2017 was the strongest sales month in the history of Mercedes-Benz with growth of 14.8% and 228,296 vehicles delivered. The first quarter of 2017 also surpassed all previous quarters, with sales rising by 16% to 560,625 units. Mercedes-Benz achieved best-ever sales in March also in the NAFTA region, where 34,922 automobiles were sold, an increase of 6.3%. Quarterly figures were also the highest ever, with a total of 94,142 vehicles delivered in the region, a 7.1% increase. New records for the first quarter were also set for each of the individual NAFTA countries: USA, Canada and Mexico.
