May 31th, 2019
Automakers' shares fall in NY due to tariffs on Mexican products

Shares of major US automakers fell in the New York Stock Exchange after President Donald Trump's announcement of its administration's tariffs on Mexican products until undocumented immigration is absolutely avoided. Through his Twitter account, the US president said that the tariff measures against Mexico are both to stop undocumented migrants and drug trafficking.
Samtech Automotive opens US$27 million plant in Guanajuato

The arrival of Samtech Automotive Mexico to Guanajuato confirms the consolidation of Japanese investment in the entity, as well as the importance of the automotive industry in the state's economy. The governor of the entity, Diego Sinhue Rodriguez Vallejo, said that Guanajuato is consolidating as the largest automotive cluster in Latin America with the inauguration of the Samtech Automotive Mexico plant, a company that made an investment of US$27 million. "The plant located in the Leon-Bajio Pilba Industrial Park is generating 60 jobs, but with a growth perspective to reach 219 jobs by next year," Gov. Sinhue said.
CVG invests US$5 million in Coahuila for the production of automotive harnesses

The governor of Coahuila, Miguel Angel Riquelme Solis, announced the arrival of the manufacturer of automotive harnesses, CVG (Commercial Vehicle Group), in the municipality of Morelos. The firm invested US$5 million and, consequently, is to generate up to 1,200 new positions. Gov. Riquelme explained that Morelos will now work with an American company with a presence on four continents and more than 5,000 employees. He also indicated that a factory of this magnitude had not been installed in the municipality in over 10 years.
Marcelo Ebrard travels to Berlin and meets with automotive giants

The implementation of the rules of origin of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) was one of the main topics of the meeting with representatives of the German automotive industry, said Marcelo Ebrard, secretary of Foreign Affairs for Mexico's federal administration. Ebrard pointed out that, during his visit to Berlin, he had a meeting with Volkswagen, BMW, Audi, and Mercedes-Benz representatives, as well as Siemens. "What they were interested in is knowing what the 'road map' of Mexico is for the implementation of the rules of origin, how they are going to be measured, how they are going to be valued," he explained.
HellermannTyton Manufacturas continues investing in Nuevo Leon

The English-German company, HellermannTyton Manufacturas, will carry out an expansion in the infrastructure of its plant located in the municipality of Escobedo which, since its installation in Nuevo Leon in 2017, will reach an investment of US$30 million by the end of 2019. The company is a manufacturer and supplier of products for the connection, isolation, protection, and identification of electrical installations and data network infrastructures, while also developing parts with special measures for industrial applications. Victor Sanchez, manager of the plant in Mexico, explained that, within the next three years, they will have an installed capacity of 80%, which means that they will invest around US$10 million more.
FCA inaugurates dealership with a "mall" concept in Mexico City

FCA Mexico carried out the inauguration of the new Surman La Villa dealership in the northern part of Mexico City. Bruno Cattori, president and CEO of FCA Mexico, commenced operations of this kind of dealership that presents the concept of an automotive mall for the brands Chrysler, Dodge, Fiat, Jeep, and Ram. The opening of Surman La Villa is part of the ambitious growth and consolidation plan of the FCA Mexico Distributors Network, as well as the strategy for the renewal of the group's corporate image in its sales floors.
Continental's Research and Development Center Queretaro celebrates its first year of operations

The Research and Development Center of Continental celebrated its first anniversary of operations in Queretaro. During its first year, the center has established itself as a key part of Continental's technological capacity for innovation from Mexico to the whole world. This campus, located in the south of the city of Queretaro, was designed under a concept of open spaces and collaborative areas, allowing an interaction between the teams, which fosters an environment of innovation and creativity.
Daimler strengthens its footprint in northern Mexico with new distributor

Daimler Trucks Mexico inaugurated a new distributor in the northern part of ​​Tijuana, alongside its business partner Camiones y Autobuses del Noroeste (CANORSA). Said distributor strengthens the presence of both companies in Baja California. The opening of Canorsa Tijuana is part of the comprehensive strategy and expansion of the Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico (DVCM) network, whose infrastructure presents modern facilities with the aim of maintaining high standards of quality, while providing the best shopping experience, orientation, and service to the thousands of transporters on the border.
Giant Motors Latinoamerica and RZ Motors open new JAC dealership in Puebla

RZ Motors, alongside Giant Motors Latinoamerica (GML), inaugurated a new JAC vehicle distribution agency in the municipality of Teziutlan, Puebla. "In 2017, we placed the first JAC Store in Puebla and, today, we add one more branch. This way, we reaffirm our confidence in the vehicles of the JAC firm, which are proudly assembled by a Mexican workforce in Ciudad Sahagun, in the Hidalgo plant," said Jaime Aportela, director of RZ Motors.
Marie Djordjian, the new general director of TOTAL Mexico

As soon as June 2019 begins, Marie Djordjian will assume the general direction of TOTAL Mexico, replacing Alexandre Duret-Proux, who will be responsible for other assignments within the group. The new designated executive for Mexico has been collaborating with the firm for over 19 years, a time period in which she performed various functions, both in the service station network business and in mergers and acquisition negotiations in Paris, France.
