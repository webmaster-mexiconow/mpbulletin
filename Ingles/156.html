Mexico's auto production up 3.9% in May

Mexico's auto production grew 3.9% last May to 352,860 units, a record for the month, according to the latest data from the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA). Production of automakers installed in the country totaled 1,607,057 vehicles in the first five months of the year, to a slight increase of 0.6% compared to same period of previous year. The AMIA revealed that Nissan, Volkswagen, and Honda no longer provide their export figures. However, when comparing the figures from year to year, excluding the aforementioned companies, it results in a 9.9% growth in exports during last month and a 11.1% increase from January to May. Forecast: US tariffs on imported vehicles would cost automakers one million annual sales

Should the Trump administration impose tariffs on imported vehicles, U.S. auto sales could drop by somewhere between one and two million vehicles this current year alone, according to a forecast by analysis firm LMC Automotive. The projection assumes that if automakers absorb at least half the cost of a tax on imported vehicles, the loss would be of one million units, said Jeff Schuster, senior vice president of forecasting. But, if companies pass the full 25% cost on to consumers, it could wipe out about two million sales, or more than 10% of annual U.S. deliveries, he said. More details here: bit.ly/2sTPHTq
Steel, aluminum tariffs have already driven up General Motors costs, says CEO

Tariffs on aluminum and steel have already driven up costs in General Motors, CEO Mary Barra acknowledged Tuesday during a press conference ahead of the company's annual shareholders meeting, local media in Detroit reported. Since trade negotiations are still incomplete, it is difficult to have the complete picture of the impact that tariffs will have on the General Motors or the automobile industry in general, hence the company still hasn't delayed or modified any current plans, Barra told reporters. "Clearly there are a lot of moving pieces in trade, and the auto industry is a very complex business. We need to let the discussions proceed to a conclusion," the official stated. American Axle to supply power transfer units for 2019 Ford crossovers

American Axle & Manufacturing will supply Power Transfer Units (PTU) for Ford Motor Co. crossover vehicles with all-wheel drive (AWD) starting with the 2019 Ford Edge and Lincoln Nautilus. AAM will manufacture the components in Three Rivers, Michigan, Silao, Guanajuato, Changshu, China, and Świdnica, Poland. Officials from Turkey-based auto parts supplier Odelo-Farba confirmed their interest in investing on the state of Aguascalientes to start manufacturing operations of exterior lights for premium segment vehicles in the medium term. Bendix celebrates 30 years of manufacturing operations in Acuña, Coahuila

Bendix Commercial Vehicle Systems LLC and Bendix Spicer Foundation Brake LLC celebrated its 30th anniversary of operations in the municipality of Acuña, Coahuila. First came Plant I, in May 1988, focused on OEM assembly and machining for the Controls business unit. Plant II followed, in 2000, for remanufacturing and OEM service as part of the Charging unit. In 2010, Plant III opened to manufacture actuator products. The fourth facility, a bustling logistics center, joined the operation last fall. The Bendix's Acuña operation employs more than 2,000 workers and encompasses 521,000 square feet. More than a dozen employees have been with the operation since it started. Kia recalls more than half a million cars in the US for airbag issues

Kia Motors Corp announced the recall of more than 507,000 vehicles in the United States because an electronic glitch may prevent airbags from deploying in a crash. The recall follows an announcement in March by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) that was investigating why some air bags had failed to deploy in Kia vehicles and its affiliate Hyundai Motor Corp after crashes in which four people were killed and another six were injured. Kia's recall issued covers 2010-2013 Kia Fortes, 2011-2013 Kia Optimas, and 2011-2012 Kia Optima Hybrid and Sedona vehicles. The company said it does not yet have a fix, but is working with its supplier, the German manufacturer ZF, on the issue.
Tesla to cut nine percent of its workforce in ‘difficult but necessary' reorganization

Tesla told employees Tuesday it will cut about nine percent of its workforce, trimming mostly salaried positions. Since the start of the year, Tesla has added roughly 8,000 jobs and currently employs approximately 46,000 workers. Through this reorganization, it will shed about 4,100 jobs. "To be clear, Tesla will still continue to hire outstanding talent in critical roles as we move forward and there is still a significant need for additional production personnel," Tesla CEO Elon Musk said in a letter to employees. "I also want to emphasize that we are making this hard decision now so that we never have to do this again."
