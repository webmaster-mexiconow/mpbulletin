Trump plans to withdraw from Paris climate deal, shakes up the auto industry, again

Various news outlets have reported that President Donald Trump has already made the decision to withdraw the U.S. from the Paris climate agreement, a landmark deal in which nearly every country volunteered to curb its greenhouse gas emissions to limit global warming. In terms of the auto industry and transportation, the measure puts pressure on countries to develop alternative fuels, cars with better gas mileage and less emissions. The announcement prompted Tesla chief Elon Musk, an avowed supporter of climate change initiatives, to warn he'll quit as Trump advisor if the President does not comply with the climate accord.
Mexican automotive trade surplus up 9.8% during first quarter

During the first quarter of 2017 Mexican automotive trade surplus reached US$ 16 billion, a 9.8% increase compared to same period of 2016, according to figures by the Statistics National Institute (INEGI) and the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA). Mexican automotive exports rose 9.6% over the three-month period, totaling US$ 29 billion. Shipments to the U.S. market increased 14.2%, while exports to Central and South America went up 16.1%. On the other hand, automotive imports reached US$ 13 billion during the first quarter, a 9.3% increase compared to the same period of 2016.
In a year, Japanese automakers built 6.45 million cars in the NAFTA region

Japanese automakers produced together a combined 5,003,000 units in U.S. and Canada during fiscal year 2016, which ended in March 31, 2017, a 1% increase from the previous year. It was also the fifth straight year of record production. Out of those 5 million units, 3.97 million were produced in the U.S. During the five-year span, the five automakers' production in the U.S. - Canada region increased by 1.36 million units. Mexico is not left behind, over the same five-year period Japanese production in the southern hub has roughly doubled. During fiscal 2016, they finished some 1.45 million cars in Mexico, almost 40% of what they produced in the U.S.
Bancomext partners with Japan's Mizuho bank to create line of credit

Banco de Comercio Exterior (Bancomext) announced it has signed a memorandum of understanding with Japan's Mizuho bank for the creation of a line of credit for Japanese companies investing in Mexico. The peso-denominated line of credit would provide Japanese companies with both short and long-term financing for projects in the country. Mizuho Bank, the third-largest bank in Japan, launched multi-bank operations in Mexico in March, serving Japanese companies doing business in the country by providing commercial loans and other services. Mizuho focuses on the automotive industry, especially on giant automakers and their suppliers.
APL Logistics reports strong results boosted by auto parts trade between Mexico, US

Singapore-based APL Logistics reported an operating profit of US$ 23.7 million in fiscal year 2016, ending March 31, boosted by its US-Mexico cross-border business serving the auto parts industry, according to the annual report by its parent company, Japan-based Kintetsu World Express (KWE). Uncertainty about trade is making it difficult for auto industry executives to make decisions about their global and North American manufacturing needs, but the favorable exchange rate for U.S. importers is driving more cross-border business between Mexico and the United States, the company said in its report.
Mercedes-Benz vows to include more US content in cars

Mercedes-Benz announced its interest in adding more local content on the vehicles it produces at its Alabama facility, a move perceived as response to reports about President Donald Trump highlighting the German car maker's role in a US$ 15.4 billion automotive trade deficit with Germany. "Having more local suppliers increased overall flexibility, reduced transport times and improved manufacturing and logistics costs. Overall, this results in considerably increased efficiency," the company said. The C-Class sedan, built in Alabama, uses among the highest content supplied by U.S. or Canadian components makers at 80%, according to NHTSA.
BMW seeks compensation from Bosch for disrupting its auto production

BMW AG will seek compensation from Robert Bosch GmbH after missing parts for several models, including the bestselling 3-Series sedan, caused production stoppages in Germany, China and South Africa. The disruption was caused by a bottleneck at an Italian company that supplies the casings for Bosch's electronic-steering systems. Bosch dispatched employees to Italy to help solve the problem as soon as possible and deliveries resumed a couple days later. Now BMW is trying to eliminate the manufacturing backlog in coming weeks by adding extra shifts to the affected assembly facilities.
Ford sued by 7,000 Fiesta, Focus owners over dual-clutch transmissions

Nearly 7,000 Fiesta and Focus owners are suing Ford Motor Co. over an alleged defect in the PowerShift transmissions featured in some small cars, a problem that has plagued the automaker for years. According to Autonews, the suit claims that 2012-16 Focus and 2011-16 Fiesta sedans come with faulty dual-clutch transmissions prone to "slipping, jerking, premature internal wear, delays in downshifting and, in some cases, sudden or delayed acceleration". Ford builds in Cuautitlan, Mexico the Ford Fiesta for the North American market. The Ford Focus is built in Wayne, Michigan, but the company currently works on shifting production to Hermosillo, Sonora.
PMP Auto sells its wiping systems business; deal includes a plant in Puebla

India-based automotive components manufacturer Pricol Ltd has agreed to acquire the wiping systems business of PMP Auto Components Pvt. Ltd. for an undisclosed amount. The operation includes a manufacturing facility in Puebla, Mexico. The acquisition will help Pricol diversify its product portfolio and access to manufacturing facilities in Europe and North America where it doesn't have a footprint, the company said in a stock-exchange filing. The Mexican facility is located in Cuautlancingo, Puebla, where PMP manufactures and assembles wiper systems for auto plants located in Mexico and overseas.
Honeywell opens US$ 300 million automotive refrigerant plant

With an eye on growing global demand for eco-friendly automotive air conditioners, Honeywell International Inc. started production of its Solstice yf refrigerant in a new US$ 300 million plant in Geismar, Louisiana. "Half the cars manufactured in the U.S. today are using this product and that will convert to 100 percent by 2021," said Honeywell Vice President Ken Gayer. In 2013, only half a million vehicles on the road worldwide used the eco-friendly refrigerants. The figure has now climbed to more than 20 million and Honeywell says the number will surpass 40 million by the end of this year.
