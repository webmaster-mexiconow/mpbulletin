Cie Automotive acquires two Mexican firms so as to complement its operations in the country

Spain-based Cie Automotive announced the acquisition of the Mexican companies Maquinados de Precisión de México y Cortes de Precisión de México, valued at US$65.5 million, with the aim of complementing its operations in the country "and allowing their physical and commercial expansion in the future." The acquired facilities are located in the city of Celaya, in the state of Guanajuato, which Cie qualifies as "the largest automobile corridor in Mexico" and where suppliers of "Tier 1 companies within the automotive sector supply a wide range of components," the company explained.
ANPACT and its partners carry out a reforestation day within the Expo Transport ANPACT 2019 framework

With the assistance of around 150 volunteers from the 14 brands associated with the National Association of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks Producers (ANPACT), a reforestation day was held within the framework of the Expo Transport ANPACT 2019. The plantation of a 1,000 white cedar and white oak trees, Puebla's native vegetation, was carried out in the surroundings of the Puebla Expositor Center, headquarters of the biggest heavy vehicles fair within the continent.
Grupo Cruces opens JAC Stores in Veracruz and Tabasco

Giant Motors Latin America announced that, alongside JAC Mexico, it inaugurated two JAC Stores, one in Coatzacoalcos, Veracruz, and another in Villahermosa, Tabasco. Both JAC Stores belong to Grupo Cruces, led by Sergio Cruces Galan and Sergio Cruces Fernandez. "For Giant Motors Latin America and JAC Mexico, it is a pleasure to receive Grupo Cruces, one of the most important distributors within the region with more than 30 years of experience," said Elias Massri, CEO and director of Giant Motors Latin America.
Ratification of the USMCA will strengthen competitiveness within North America: ANPACT

The ratification of the commercial treaty between Mexico, the United States and Canada (USMCA) will strengthen the competitiveness of North America before other regions of the world, said the executive president of the National Association of Bus, Truck and Tractor Truck Producers (ANPACT), Miguel Elizalde Lizarraga. "The agreement provides a context of greater stability for the region, which translates into greater legal certainty for the industries. We have to work to make these possibilities a reality," he said in an interview with Notimex.
Renault to fortify its sales due to the Kangoo model

Renault Mexico will reinforce its light vehicles sales growth with the launch of its commercial vehicle Kangoo, which focuses on serving the entrepreneur market in the country. In this sense, Renault is committed to close 2019 off with a sales increase of 16%. This was announced by Carlos Rodriguez, sales director of Renault Mexico, who highlighted the level of acceptance of the French brand among Mexican consumers, showing an 11.7% growth from January to May of this year, which contrasts with the 5.4% drop in sales of the automotive industry in the same period.
The USMCA is a milestone in the history of North America: Coparmex

The Employers Confederation of the Mexican Republic (Coparmex) indicated that, with the approval of the Senate on the commercial agreement between Mexico, the United States and Canada (USMCA), there's a great step toward the ratification of the agreement and that it will be a milestone in the history of North America. The organization led by Gustavo de Hoyos Walther recognized and supported the positioning of President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador regarding the new treaty, as well as the work of the various private agencies that achieved a successful negotiation.
Grupo JC to invest US$6.5 million in Nuevo Leon

The Monterrey-based company Grupo JC will invest US$6.5 million over the next two years to install steel stamping and aluminum injection plants for the automotive sector, said Francisco Montalvo, the company's director of operations. The manager said that these projects are for the short term, between a year and a half and two years. "We are focusing on projects for the automotive sector and we are in the process of being authorized to enter the stamping area of ​​steel and aluminum."
