The 2021 Honda Insight arrives to Mexico

Honda of Mexico announced the arrival to Mexico of the 2021 Honda Insight. It has an exterior red color and a new rim finishing. Its arrival to Mexican distributors is immediate, in one version with a price of MXN$609,000. The Insight is propelled by the third generation of Honda's two engine hybrid system. It has a very efficient 1.5 liter Atkinson cycle engine and a potent electric motor, combined both engines offer up to 151 horsepower and 197 lb-ft of torque.

Mazda presents new equipment for its Mazda2 and CX-30 models

On top of the introduction of a turbo engine for the Mazda 3, the Japanese brand is announcing an extra dose of equipment in different versions on two of its most sold vehicles in Mexico, the Mazda2 and CX-30. Mexico's government seeks steel investments from Asia

Mexico's government has spoken to several foreign companies, particularly from the steel sector, in an effort to attract business from Asia to capitalize on the new United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA), said on Monday the minister of Economy, Graciela Marquez. The minister added that President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador's government has maintained conversations with foreign steel makers, among which are POSCO from South Korea, Nippon Steel Corp and Mitsubishi Corp, from Japan and Ternium about investing in Mexico to produce steel for the automotive sector.

Automotive production in the U.S. pushes recovery in Mexico

The automotive industry appears to spearhead Mexico's economic recovery, as reflected by INEGI indicators and the U.S. Federal Reserve. First, vehicle production in the United States reached in June 74.1% of the level corresponding to last February, which was the highest point in 2020, to then plummet due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The recovery of automotive production in the U.S. is pushing forward Mexican exports, since Mexico is its main auto parts supplier.

The 2020 Audi A4 is now available in Mexico

The Audi A4 disappeared from the country a few months ago, as it waited for its facelift's homologation process to be authorized, but now it's back. Underneath the chest, the entire range has a 2.0 liter turbo engine, capable of performing at 190 hp and 236 lb-ft, associated to an S-Tronic seven speed transmission. It also uses a mild-hybrid system of 12 V, with which it can save 0.3 liters every 100 km/h. Audi reports a combined consumption of 16.3 km/l. The 2020 Audi A4 is available in Mexico in three versions: Dynamic, Select and S-Line. Prices range from MXN$609,000 to MXN$749,000.

Volkswagen expects recovery during the second semester of 2020

With the start of the second semester of 2020, Volkswagen of Mexico is observing a push in vehicle sales through digitalization, when compared to previous months, with which there's a perception of more certainty for the stabilization of the automotive industry, after facing the worst registered falls in 25 years, affirmed Edgar Estrada, director of the German brand in our country. "The second semester is beginning with a bit more certainty and industry stabilization", he said, "maybe it's not where we want it to be, but we're seeing a small recovery when compared to the months of April and May".

Railroad blockage in Sonora is affecting Ford's operations

Ford Mexico said on Wednesday that a more than a week long blockage of railroad tracks in Sonora is affecting their operations, which mainly exports to the United States. For about 10 days now, the Yaqui pueblo has been blocking the railroad, which transports grains, steel and several automotive pieces from Sonora, with impacts in the Mexicali-California and Nogales-Arizona border crossings. "We ask for the support of all involved parties (...) since the exit to the Mazatlán port is stopping the exports of our products currently being manufactured in our Hermosillo plant," Ford Mexico told Reuters.
