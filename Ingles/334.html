Nissan Mexico is the first one to renew operations in North America

Nissan Mexico became the first operation from the Japanese brand to restart operations in its manufacturing installations in the North American region. After meeting all safety protocols and inspections required by the Mexican authorities to prevent the spread of COVID-19, and receiving the federal certification for the restart of operations, Nissan Mexico reaffirmed its position as a benchmark manufacturing operation for the corporation worldwide. At the same time, the export operations to the United States were reactivated and will gradually continue to the more than 50 export destinations around the world.

The Volkswagen Nivus will arrive to Mexico in 2021

The Nivus is developed on the MQB platform and its dimensions give us an ideal vehicle for the city because of its easy maneuverability. The foreseen engine for the Nivus is a TSI three cylinder one that develops at 128 hp, which is associated to a six speed automatic transmission. This vehicle will be assembled in the company's Sao Bernardo do Campo plant, and will be sold in Brazil in less than a month, while it's expected to begin sales in Argentina at the end of this year's second semester. Its sale for the rest of the Latin American markets, Mexico included, is expected to start at the beginning of 2021.

MAN Truck & Bus Mexico with its two commercial brands, MAN and Volkswagen Trucks and Buses, along with its distributor network prepared for a safe return to the "new normality" starting on June 1st. Queretaro's automotive companies return to activities at 35% of their productive capacity

After almost two months of halted activities, automotive companies in the state are returning to operations with 35% of their workforce and between 35 and 40% of their productive capacity. These are the operative percentages companies expect to implement at least during this first week of reactivation, which means the return of 18,000 to 20,000 active workers, explained the president of Queretaro's Automotive Cluster, Renato Villaseñor Mendoza. The productivity level at which companies are starting is in line with the behavior of the automotive market.

Automotive financing falls 18.1% during the year's first four months

During the first four months of 2020 there were 331,580 sold light vehicles in Mexico, of which 62.34% were acquired through financing, according to the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). Financing in the first four months of the year represented a decrease of 18.1% when compared to the same period of 2019, which meant 52,980 less units. According to type of financing in the first fourth months, Brand financing had a market participation of 67.56% with 139,653 units, an annual decrease of 16.6%.

New vehicle sales in Mexico sees a recovery of 20.4% in May

During May of this year there was a registered total of 42,028 new commercialized vehicles in Mexico, which implied a modest improvement in relation to the sales level reached last month. In April there barely were 34,903 commercialized units in the internal market, according to numbers reported by AMIA and disseminated by INEGI. This situation implied a recovery of 20.4% on a month to month comparison, after the health crisis led the industry to an almost complete halt of operations during April and May of this year.
