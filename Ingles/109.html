Auto sales in the US market up 1% during November

Boosted by Black Friday deals and strong consumer confidence, U.S. sales of new cars and light trucks rose 1% in November to nearly 1.4 million, according to Autodata Corp. Out of the Detroit Big 3, Ford was the only automaker to post an increase in sales, of 6.7% to 210,771 units due to strong demand for trucks and SUVs. Ford sold 72,769 F-Series pickups, marking that vehicle's best November since 2001. Out of the Japan Big 3, Toyota was the only automaker whose sales fell with a 3% drop to 191,617 units. Honda sales rose 8% to 133,156 vehicles. Nissan didn't report its sales due to a computer problem. However, Autodata estimated a 14% increase to 131,138 vehicles.
Auto sales fall 8.5% in Mexico during November

Mexican auto dealers sold 141,434 cars and light trucks during November, an 8.5% decrease compared to the same month of 2016. From January through November, 1,371,600 were sold in the country, which represents a 2.8% fall compared to the same 11-month period of last year. While Nissan, GM and Volkswagen remain the top three sellers in the country with 24%, 16.7% and 15.4% market shares respectively on year-to-date sales, there's now a tie between FCA and Toyota in the fourth position with a 6.7% share each. Also tied, but in the fifth position, are Honda and Kia with a 5.7% market share each.
Canada's auto sales cool off in November, but still on track for a yearly record

Vehicle sales appeared to cool off slightly in Canada last month, but are still poised to surpass two million for the first time. Auto makers reported sales in November that were about flat with those of a year earlier. Sales for the first 11 months of the year were set to hit about 1.91 million, leaving car companies to sell less than 100,000 vehicles in December in order to reach two million for the year. The lowest total for December since 2010 was 109,000 in December, 2012, so hitting two million is almost certain, said industry analyst Dennis DesRosiers, president of DesRosiers Automotive Consultants Inc.
Infiniti officially starts production of the QX50 in Aguascalientes

Infiniti announced that along with the presentation of the semi-autonomous crossover QX50 in Los Angeles, California, its production officially started for the global market at COMPAS (Cooperation Manufacturing Plant Aguascalientes), the new US$ 1 billion manufacturing facility of the Renault-Nissan and Daimler alliance. The new QX50 will arrive to U.S. showrooms in the first quarter of 2018, while in Mexico it will be available during the summer. At its peak, estimated for 2020, the complex will produce 230,000 vehicles per year for Infiniti. Mercedes-Benz plans to start production in 2018. The QX50 incorporates around 65% of regional components, mainly from Mexico and the United States, while the rest come from Asia.
Swiss manufacturer Komax opens US$ 8.5 million plant in Guanajuato

With a US$ 8.5 million investment, Komax, a Swiss manufacturer of machines for wire processing technology, started operations at the Castro del Rio industrial park in the Municipality of Irapuato, Guanajuato. The company will be dedicated to the production of electromechanical equipment and systems for the testing of wire harnesses mainly for the automotive sector, but also for other industries. With its operation, one hundred direct, highly specialized jobs will be generated, since it requires engineers and technicians in electronics, mechanics, mechatronics, with skills in computer-aided design and manufacturing management and machining operations with numerical control centers. Some of Komax customers in Mexico are Delphi, Condumex, Lear and Sumitomo.
Japanese auto supplier Dowa Metaltech opens US$ 11 million plant in Guanajuato

Japan-based manufacturer Dowa Metaltech officially started operations last week at its new production facility located in Irapuato, Guanajuato, 15 months after the company broke ground with a planned US$ 11 million investment. Dow Metaltech is a subsidiary of Dowa Holdings, a nonferrous metals manufacturer with mining operations in Mexico. In Guanajuato, Dowa Metaltech will supply plating services for automotive components, including controllers for air conditioners and windshield wipers, metal connectors, among others. The project implied the opening of 34 highly specialized job opportunities.
Japanese manufacturer plans US$ 60 million expansion in Aguascalientes

Kurota Seiki-Seisakusho, a Japanese manufacturer of aluminum parts, confirmed plans to expand operations at its production site in Aguascalientes, Mexico. The company will invest US$ 60 million which will increase the manufacturer's total investment in Mexico to US$ 165 million, while its workforce is expected to grow from the current 140 employees up to 200. Kurota provides parts for Tier 1 auto suppliers as well as original equipment manufacturers (OEMs). Bosch, Honda and Nissan are among its customers in Mexico, but it also exports to the rest of North America. The added production capacity will be destined in part to Daimler's new assembly plant in Aguascalientes, where Mercedes-Benz cars will be built by the end of 2018.
Germany-based manufacturer Leoni opens wiring systems plant in Yucatan

Leoni AG, a Germany-based manufacturer of cables and cable systems for the automotive sector opened a new -environment friendly- wiring systems plant in the city of Merida. This is Leoni's third site for wiring systems in Mexico and its fifth location in the country overall since the Wire & Cable Solutions Division also operates two sites. The production scope in Merida comprises harnesses for a series of passenger car models from a U.S.-based customer and a manufacturer from Asia. Today, Leoni employs about 500 people in the new facility. In 2018, when the plant will run at full capacity, the company expects to count up to 2,600 staff. The new location has a total production area of approximately 25,000 square-meters.
BRP to double vehicle production at Juarez facility

Despite the uncertainty surrounding the NAFTA renegotiations, Canadian manufacturer Bombardier Recreational Products Inc. (BRP) announced it will invest US$ 78.6 million over the next two years to expand its global production capacity. Part of the investment will be used to double vehicle production output at its Juarez 2 plant in Chihuahua, Mexico. "We cannot manage our business waiting for the government to finalize negotiations. Right now, we are in a situation where we have more demand for our product than we can produce," CEO José Boisjoli said in a conference call to discuss the company's quarterly results. If NAFTA is terminated, BRP would be subject to WTO tariffs in the range of 2 to 2.5%, said chief financial officer Sébastien Martel.
Ford recalls more than 200,000 pickups, SUVs for issues on front seat

Ford is issuing a safety recall in North America for approximately 202,274 2016 Ford F-150 and 2016 Ford Explorer vehicles equipped with front power seats that may experience looseness of the front seat adjuster assembly, increasing the risk of injury in a crash. Ford is not aware of any accidents or injuries associated with this issue. Affected vehicles include: 2016 Ford F-150 vehicles built at Dearborn Assembly Plant, Jan. 22, 2016 to April 19, 2016; 2016 Ford F-150 vehicles built at Kansas City Assembly Plant, Jan. 22, 2016 to April 19, 2016, and 2016 Ford Explorer vehicles built at Chicago Assembly Plant, Jan. 22, 2016 to April 19, 2016. The recall involves approximately 177,264 vehicles in the U.S, 22,620 in Canada and 2,390 in Mexico.
