Mazda places first in post-sale service according to J.D. Power Mexico

In its 2019 long term client satisfaction study, J.D. Power Mexico acknowledged Mazda for best post-sale service, followed by Chevrolet in second place and Honda and Toyota in a tie for third place. 2,835 people with cars that are 3 to 12 years old were interviewed for the study evaluating the service satisfaction offered by authorized distributors. The sample was taken in accordance with the last 12 months and evaluated five factors: quality of service, advisor treatment, service start, delivery process and facilities.
Mercedes-Benz Buses delivers 15 new buses in Queretaro

Mercedes-Benz Buses, alongside Zapata Buses, delivered 15 new buses to Top Gun Transports, which will mobilize more than 3,000 employees a week in the streets of Queretaro. The delivered units have an OF 1321/44 chassis, Beccar bodywork and capacity for 40 people. Every bus has a cutting edge BlueTec 5 low emission EURO V engine. The passenger transport units will service employees of several companies within Queretaro State.
Heavy vehicles exports see an increase of 9.12% in June: ANPACT

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) informed that during June, the assembly of auto transport vehicles reached a total of 18,249 units, which represents an increase of 14.64% when compared to the same period of last year. At the same time, in the month of June, a total of 14,594 units were exported, which meant an increase of 9.12% in relation to the month of June of 2018. As for cumulative figures, ANPACT said that 104,214 units were produced during the first semester of this year.
Webasto invests US$28 million in Irapuato

The German company Webasto invested US$28 million in the expansion of their operations in Irapuato, where they make sunroofs and panoramic roofs for the automotive industry. The company has been in Guanajuato since 2008, but with the increase in demand these last few years, they decided to build new facilities, that with 32,000 square meters, are three times bigger than the previous ones. With the expansion of the productive area, it's expected for the number of employees to increase from 460 to 580 in the next few months. Additionally, the company will explore new production niches. Some of their main clients are Mazda and Ford.
UAEM and Mexico State Automotive Cluster sign education agreement

In a follow up to the general agreement between the Mexico State Automotive Cluster and the Autonomous Mexico State University (UAEM) celebrated November 7, 2018, the framework for collaboration to establish and begin operations on strategies for the development and certification of human capital in areas that contribute to the strengthening of innovation and productivity of the companies in the automotive sector and the educational institutions of Mexico State in areas of technological and industry 4.0 competence .
Kiekert will make new generation car locks in Mexico

Kiekert, a German company dedicated to the production of car locks, will begin to make a new generation of locks for last generation vehicles in Mexico, beginning with an inertial door lock system. Roberto Pereira, general director of Kiekert in Mexico, said that the plant they have in Puebla is the company's second biggest worldwide and represents annual sales of US$200 million, which is about 20% of the company's overall income. "We have plants to invest US$8 million this year, a similar amount to that pumped in 2018, and for 2020 we would be increasing our investment in the country," he explained
BP opens two service stations in Baja California

BP announced the start of operations in Baja California Sur with the initial opening of two service stations in the state capital of La Paz, in which it offers high-quality service and guarantees the complete dispatch of gas, thanks to its volumetric control integrated system. Just like in every service station that make up the BP network, Baja California Sur consumers will be able to load BP's unique gas with ACTIVE Technology.
HUF will invest US$54 million in Puebla

The German company HUF, maker of keys and electric security systems for brands such as BMW and Rolls Royce, announced the investment of US$54 million for the expansion of its plant located in the municipality of Coronango, with which an additional 237 jobs will be created in addition to the already existing 672. HUF America's president, Carlos Almeida Marques, commented that in 2020, together with a Chinese group they will invest in a tire pressure monitoring system plant, the amount of which was not disclosed.
General Motors opens advanced engineering laboratory in Queretaro

General Motors of Mexico opened in Queretaro an Advanced and Collaborative Engineering Laboratory, a space that will be intended for innovation and investigation to provide specialized services to the automotive industry. Located in Arkansas State University Queretaro (AUSQ), like part of the collaboration agreement between both institutions, the laboratory emerges to provide training to the provider base for General Motors, although it will also service university students and will even extend to the entire engineering industry.
UMC will invest US$65 million in Yucatan

Uchiyama Manufacturing Corporation (UMC) will invest US$65 million for the construction of one plant in Yucatan, which will manufacture plastic pieces for Mazda and Toyota in the United States. Construction is expected to begin in November. The plant will be located in Central Business Park Industrial Park in the Hunucma corridor and will have an extension of six acres. Static and dynamic engine seals or gaskets will be produced in this plant, which will be exported to the United States, South America and Asia by sea from the Progreso port.
