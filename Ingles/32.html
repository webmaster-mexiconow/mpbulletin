6 March, 2017
Nissan Kicks may enter U.S. market in 2018

While Nissan has not officially disclosed plans for the Kicks in the U.S. market, the unit has been spotted near the automaker's Research and Development center in Farmington Hills, Michigan. The unit built in Aguascalientes could reach U.S. showrooms in early 2018, to strengthen Nissan's lineup in order to compete with the Honda HR-V and other models in the compact SUV segment, according to company sources.
GKN will manufacture propshafts in Mexico for the first time

UK-based GKN Driveline announced it has created a new lightweight, high-speed propshaft joint for the all-new Audi Q5. The PVL joint is smaller, lighter and more refined than its predecessor, improving performance and efficiency, and helping to lower vehicle emissions. Production of the PVL joint units for the Audi Q5 will represent the first time that GKN has built propshafts in its Mexico plants.
Mexican auto sales increase 6.5% in February

Auto sales in Mexico increased 6.5% during February, according to the Mexican Auto Dealers Association (AMDA). Dealerships sold 117,976 units last month, the highest figure for a February ever. AMDA however, said auto dealers failed to achieve a goal estimated by the Association in 127,519 units. Accumulated sales for first two months of 2017 are 4.7% up at 241,236 units.
Auto sales drop 1.1% in U.S. market during February

Auto sales in the U. S. market fell 1.1% to 1.33 million vehicles during February, according to Autodata. Among the major domestic automakers, GM was the only one to post an increase, up 4.1%. Ford Motor recorded a 4% decline, its fourth in the last six months, even though the company's luxury Lincoln brand continued its hot streak with an 8.8% increase. Fiat Chrysler reported an overall drop of 10.1%. Among Japanese automakers, Toyota's slump continued with a 7.2% fall, while Nissan sales rose 3.7% and Honda's increased 2.3%.
Canadian auto sales reach all-time record in February

Automakers sold 123,032 new light vehicles in Canada, up 3.2% and a record for a February, according to a media report citing DesRosiers Automotive Consultants and automakers. Light truck sales were 84,865, up 5.7% from February 2016, while car sales fell 2.1% to 38,167, according to the data. Fiat Chrysler was the top seller, moving 19,115 new light vehicles, a 1.6% rise that helped it claim 15.5% of the market.
Audi Q5 scores highest possible rating by Euro NCAP

The new Q5 is the latest Audi to be awarded five stars by Euro NCAP, the organization's highest possible rating. The SUV built in Puebla, Mexico, showed off its strengths in every type of test, scoring points for occupant protection, child safety and pedestrian protection. Euro NCAP is a voluntary vehicle safety rating system originated in the UK, but is now backed by the European Commission, seven European governments, as well as motoring and consumer organizations in every EU country.
Autoneum profits double in 2016

Autoneum, a Switzerland-based manufacturer of vehicle acoustics and thermal management systems reached new highs in 2016 net sales and profitability thanks to the efficiency enhancements in production and the globally diversified customer base. Compared to 2015, net profit virtually doubled to US$ 133 million in 2016 due to operational improvements. Autoneum owns two plants in Hermosillo and San Luis Potosi and operates a joint venture with UGN Automotive in Silao, Guanajuato.
Ford recalls 32,000 Ford Edge, Lincoln SUVs

Ford Motor Company issued a safety compliance recall in North America for or approximately 32,000 2016-17 Ford Edge, 2016-17 Lincoln MKX and 2017 Lincoln Continental vehicles to replace Takata driver frontal airbag module. Ford was notified by Takata that certain driver frontal airbag cushions installed in these vehicles may not inflate as intended. Affected vehicles were built at Oakville and Flat Rock Assembly Plants from November 2014 to February 2017.
Italy-based Ferper Springs starts operations in Tlaxcala

Italian manufacturer Ferper Springs started operations in Tlaxcala state to produce coils and springs for automotive components. The company invested US$ 3 million in its first phase and hired 60 workers. Ferper Springs plans to manufacture 10 million pieces a year as first target.
Chinese manufacturer Sonavox builds plant to supply VW, Audi

China-based Sonavox announced plans to build a production site in Tlaxcala to supply speakers to Volkswagen and Audi. No investment figures or job estimates were given at the news conference. Sonavox operates plants in Czech Republic, Germany, Brazil and China. Since 2006 Sonavox has supplied parts for VW.
