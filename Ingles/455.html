Shortage of semiconductors slows production of 383,000 cars

The Bank of Mexico (Banxico) informed that between January and July, 383,000 vehicles stopped being produced in the country due to the lack of chips worldwide. This figure represents 10% of total production in 2019, when just over 3.7 million units were assembled in Mexico, according to data from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI).
