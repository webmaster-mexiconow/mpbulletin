Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico presents its new engine line

Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico presented its new engine line Detroit DD5, DD8, Mercedes-Benz MBE924 and MBE926 with GHG17 for the Mexican market, a year after their release in the North American region, with which they shorten the gap in the implementation of new technologies developed on a global level and adapted to the national environment. The engines will be an equipment alternative for the M2 truck, the top model in the medium-sized segment of the Freightliner brand, whose development is completely in line with the emission standards EURO V and EPA 10 required by the NOM 044 Semarnar-2017.
Autoliv will expand its plant in Lerma

The top seatbelt and airbag provider, Autoliv, announced that it will be expanding its plant in Lerma; this expansion will generate 1,000 direct jobs and 2,500 indirect ones. Governor of Mexico State, Alfredo del Mazo, was present at the event and remarked on the legal certainty, infrastructure and security that enable these investments, while also noting the 23 years that Autoliv has been present in the state. Autoliv has invested more than US$63 million in expanding their operations in Lerma from 2017, with the goal of doubling their seatbelt production from 25 to 45 million a year.
Nissan's restructuring will not affect Mexico

Automaker Nissan announced that it will be cutting 12,500 jobs on a global scale, this comes as part of a restructuring of their operation. However, the brand commented that no jobs will be touched in Mexico, since the country already saw an operational change. "Nissan has already taken steps to adequate the structure corresponding to North America. While we continue monitoring and reacting to the demands of the market, we have no intention at this moment, of making any additional reductions to our work team for Mexico and the region," the brand said.
Valeo Lighting Systems endorsed its commitment in Queretaro

The Company Valeo Lighting Systems endorsed their commitment with the industry in Queretaro, since they have developed various products and solutions for the automotive sector from their headquarters in the state over the course of two decades. Valeo has a 13,000 square meters occupation, with 52 injection machines, 29 assembly lines (22 OEM lines and 7 OES), 7 coating machines and 21 metallizers. The company has a total of 2,100 employees, of which 80% corresponds to direct labor.
Automotive industry in Queretaro proceeds cautiously

Queretaro's automotive industry proceeds cautiously around the effects that can be seen in the internal market and that could have an effect on companies' operability. The president of the Automotive Cluster of Queretaro, Renato Villaseñor Mendoza, explained that the companies of this industry have begun outlining their budgets for the 2020 fiscal year, and whose figures will be outlined cautiously before the circumstances that could affect national economy. Budget design will look to give certainty to the projects that companies have planned for next year.
General Motors is the Brand that exported more vehicles in June

According to the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) and the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), during the sixth month of 2019 a total of 337,253 vehicles were manufactured, while a total of 320,470 units were exported. General Motors is the band that exported the largest amount of vehicles with 76,690 units, followed by FCA Mexico with 58,845 units and Volkswagen came in third place with 47,527 units.
Hirotec invests US$57 in automotive plant in San Luis Potosi

By holding a meeting with the directors of the Japanese company, Hirotec, San Luis Potosi's governor, Juan Manuel Carreras Lopez, thanked the company for investing in San Luis Potosi and for trusting the talent and capacity of its people for the establishment of the plant that is currently being built in industrial park Logistik I, which will be the provider for the German assembler BMW and companies of the sector in the Center-Bajio-West region. This company will invest US$57 million, which adds to the industrial growth that San Luis Potosi has recently registered.
Mopar Mexico introduces their catalog for the 2020 Jeep Gladiator

To demonstrate the equipment levels to which the new 2020 Jeep Gladiator can reach, Mopar Mexico introduced the Moparized version of this vehicle, incorporating a list of accessories that make it the most potent and capable Jeep pickup of its segment. The Moparized 2020 Jeep Gladiator shows clients how they can leave their mark in the vehicle, by choosing from the great variety of accessories including: lifting kit, tubular doors, storing system, just to name a few, all of them especially designed for the 2020 Jeep Gladiator.
Mexican automotive industry exports US$72.5 billion

Despite the drop in sales in Mexico in the last 25 months, the automotive industry remains as one of the principal engines of export and employment in the country's manufacturing industry. Between January and June of this year the industry reported exports for US$72.5 billion, which represented a 36% of the total of manufacturing sales abroad. The US is the main market for light vehicles exports, with a 78.5% total of the sales abroad, the second place goes to Canada with a 7.1% and Germany takes third place with 4%.
PPAP MGR is selected to participate in Techstar Detroit's acceleration program

The Chihuahua based company PPAP MGR was selected between 600 start-ups on a global level to participate in Techstar Detroit's (TSD) acceleration program; the program's goal is to better different aspects of the automotive industry. PPAP MGR is an online platform that streamlines the administration and approval of the set of digital documents required in the automotive industry for the approval of parts, these documents are called PPAPs. Additionally, this has an application in the aerospace and electronics industries.
