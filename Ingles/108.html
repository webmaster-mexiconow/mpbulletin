Kia reaches 300,000 cars milestone at its Nuevo Leon plant

Kia Motors Mexico in recent days produced the 300,000th car at its Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon plant in a record time of a year and a half, the company said in a statement. The Korean automaker builds at its Pesqueria plant the Forte and Rio models, both on sedan and hatchback versions, as well as the Hyundai Accent, to supply the domestic market and aims to close the year with exports to 68 countries. Horacio Chavez, CEO of Kia Motors Mexico said the automaker produced 110,000 vehicles in 2016, during its first seven months of operations; while for this year it is estimated a production of 250,000 units. It was a Forte sedan of the highest trim level the 300,000th unit that came out of the assembly line, which has capacity to manufacture a car every 53 seconds.
Infiniti unveils in LA the all-new, Mexico-sourced QX50 crossover

Nissan Motor premium brand Infiniti revealed at the 2017 Los Angeles Auto Show, the all-new 2019 QX50, a luxury mid-size crossover with never before seen technologies, such as variable compression ratio engine and the ProPILOT semi-autonomous driver assist system. The unit is manufactured at the new COMPAS (Cooperation Manufacturing Plant Aguascalientes), an assembly facility built by a partnership between Daimler and the Renault-Nissan Alliance. "The 2019 QX50 is the most important vehicle we have ever launched, and it embodies everything the brand stands for: Beautiful design, breakthrough technology, empowering to drive," said Christian Meunier, Infiniti Global Division Vice President, during the presentation.
Mexico-made Nissan Kicks to enter US market by June 2018

Nissan Motor Company unveiled the 2018 Kicks subcompact crossover at the 2017 Los Angeles Auto Show, as it confirmed the unit will arrive to U.S. dealerships in June 2018. The U.S. version of the Kicks will come from Nissan's A2 facility in Aguascalientes, Mexico, equipped only with front-wheel drive and an automatic transmission. The 2018 Kicks will be offered in a range of seven exterior colors - plus five two-tone combinations. Every 2018 Kicks comes with a standard 1.6-liter DOHC 16-valve 4-cylinder with Continuous Variable Valve Timing Control System (CVTCS). The engine is rated at 125 horsepower and 115 lb-ft of torque. The unit is expected to have a fuel economy rating 33 mpg combined.
Volkswagen adds R-Line optional sporty package to the 2018 Tiguan

Volkswagen announced that it will offer the R-Line package on the 2018 Tiguan. "The new trim package augments the all-new SUV's sportiness with interior and exterior design elements that give it an even more dynamic appearance," said the company in a note prior to unveiling the new feature at the 2017 Los Angeles Auto Show. The R-Line package enhances the robust list of equipment in the Tiguan SEL and SEL Premium trims with performance-inspired treatments. The sporty exterior styling details include 19-inch aluminum-alloy wheels for SEL trims and 20-inch aluminum-alloy wheels on SEL Premium trims. Also included unique front and rear bumpers that incorporate stylish air intakes at the front and a diffusor in glossy black paint at the rear.
Daimler introduces two cabover tractor trucks specifically for the Mexican market

Daimler Trucks North American introduced two new Freightliner cabover models designed specifically for Mexico. Flavio Rivera, President and CEO of Daimler Trucks Mexico, cited poor infrastructure, heavy congestion, and narrow, European-style streets as top challenges for the truck industry in the Mexican market. The Class 8 360 2528 is equipped with a Mercedes-Benz engine with 280 horsepower and a Mercedes-Benz G131 manual transmission. An extended cab is available. The 2528 can haul 17 tons and has rear and frontal stabilization bars. The Class 6 360 1217 comes with a Mitsubishi engine with 170 horsepower and a Mercedes-Benz G85 transmission with 7 gears. It offers eight tons of payload.
Detroit automakers ask Vice President Mike Pence to drop NAFTA demands

Detroit automakers pressed Vice President Mike Pence to pump the brakes on proposed changes to NAFTA that they say could add thousands to the cost of a car in the U.S. Pence met Monday with GM CEO Mary Barra, FCA CEO Sergio Marchionne and Ford Executive Vice President and President of Global Operations Joe Hinrichs in Washington. U.S. Trade Representative Robert Lighthizer and National Economic Council Director Gary Cohn also attended. Matt Blunt, president of the American Automotive Policy Council, which lobbies for Ford, GM and Fiat Chrysler, said in a statement that automakers used the face-to-face meeting with Pence to "directly address the industry's concerns" about the proposal for increasing the minimum percentage of U.S. content.
Tristone Flowtech invests US$ 3.5 million to increase production at Chihuahua facility

Tristone Flowtech Group, a Germany-based automotive supplier specialized in tailor-made fluid solutions, announced plans to invest US$ 3.5 million at its new plant in Delicias, Chihuahua, to increase output capacity and broaden its product catalog by 2018. The company earlier this year finalized the move of production to a new 175,000 square-feet facility, which doubles the space of the old facility. Tristone already invested about US$ 7 million in equipment during the latter part of 2016 and throughout 2017 at the site, said in a company update, Guenter Froelich, president and CEO. The plant produces engine cooling, battery cooling and air charge hose systems. Its customers portfolio includes Ford, GM, VW, FCA, BMW and some Tier 1 suppliers.
Lucid Motors opens new headquarters near Tesla's factory

The electric-car startup Lucid Motors, which is developing the 1,000-horsepower Lucid Air, announced it will move its operations from Menlo Park, California, to Newark, one city over from Tesla's factory in Fremont. The new facility will allow for a larger design studio and an expanded space for research and development, the company said in a blog post on its website. The company currently builds its US$ 700 million assembly plant in Casa Grande, Arizona, in order to take advantage of the proximity to Mexico and the highly integrated cross-border automotive supply chain. Lucid aims to start production in 2019.
Honda electric vehicles to charge in 15 minutes starting in 2022

Honda Motor aims to roll out electric vehicles capable of 15-minute charging in 2022, cutting down one of the steepest hurdles to the expansion of battery-powered cars, the Nikkei Asian Review reported. The Japanese automaker plans to release in that year a selection of fully electric cars that can run 240 km on a single 15-minute charge. Most electric vehicles now available take at least twice that long to reach an 80% charge even using a high-speed charger. To do this, the automaker will offer new batteries capable of taking advantage of 350-kW ultra-fast chargers that Japan expects to launch in 2020, up from the 150 kW currently offer. Honda will partner with another company to produce the new batteries, but it has not yet chosen that collaborator.

Volkswagen announced its Golf family made the Car and Driver magazine's "10Best" list for the 12th consecutive time. Woebcken, president of VW of America. The Golf family consists of the e-Golf, Golf, Golf Alltrack, Golf GTI, Golf SportWagen and Golf R. All variants for North America are built in Puebla, except for the e-Golf and the Golf R, which are still Germany-sourced.
