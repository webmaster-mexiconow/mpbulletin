April 12th, 2019
Honda Mexico taps Kazuhiro Takizawa as president

The Japanese automaker Honda announced that, as of April this year, Kazuhiro Takizawa is the new president of the company in Mexico. In a statement, Honda says that the objectives of Takizawa are to increase the volume of sales, not only for cars, but for motorcycles and power products (electric generators) as well. In addition, Takizawa is to maintain and promote the export of Honda products that are manufactured in the Guanajuato and Jalisco plants. Prior to his arrival in Mexico, Takizawa worked in Bangkok, Thailand, as general manager of Asian Honda Motor.
Visteon invests more than US$9.2 million in a design and engineering center in Queretaro

Visteon, an automotive company, has invested more than US$9.2 million in Queretaro for the development of a design and engineering center, which plans to generate 600 highly specialized jobs in the state, said the state governor, Francisco Dominguez Servien. He indicated that more than a year ago, Visteon Mexico began its operations in Queretaro with 160 employees, and ever since then, they have boosted local talent by enriching users' experience of smart, connected, fun and safe cars through their development of cutting-edge technology.
LS Automotive opens US$60.8 million plant in Nuevo Leon

With an investment of US$60.8 million, the first plant of the Korean company LS Automotive in Nuevo Leon was inaugurated, through which it will produce electronic components for the automotive industry in order to serve the North American market. The opening of this plant represents a new source of work for Nuevo Leon's citizens, since LS Automotive will gradually increase its workforce to have a thousand employees, said the undersecretary of Investment and Industrial Development of Nuevo Leon, Lorenzo Aguilar Camelo.
Honda celebrates the production of one million transmissions in Guanajuato

The Honda plant in Celaya, Guanajuato, celebrated the production of one million transmissions in the state. Governor Diego Sinhue Rodriguez Vallejo said that it has been difficult for the Japanese company to reach this figure, as they have had four years of challenges. During the celebration, the president congratulated the executives of the company for their vision and leadership, as well as the 1,063 associates who work within the plant. The ambassador of Japan in Mexico, Yasushi Takase, highlighted the good friendship with Guanajuato in various areas, mainly economic, since there are about 300 Japanese companies in Guanajuato's territory.
DENSO expands its production plant in Nuevo Leon

In order to strengthen the economic growth of the state of Nuevo Leon through the creation of jobs, Governor Jaime Rodriguez Calderon was present at the placement of the first expansion column of the Japanese company, DENSO, located in Apodaca. According to its website, DENSO is "a leading provider of advanced technology, systems and automotive components for all the major vehicle manufacturers in the world".
Cuprum to invest US$30 million in the production of automotive components

The Mexico-based company Cuprum will invest US$30 million this year in strengthening the production of aluminum components for the automotive industry in the country, revealed the company's director, Eugenio Clariond. The executive said that they have full confidence that the good performance and growth of the assemblers installed in the country during the next years will continue. The resources, he said, will be allocated to, "more capacity for extrusion, machinery for added value, as many of these automotive components are nothing more than just a profile, but are delivered with certain additional added values."
Renault reiterates its commitment to its Kwid model for the Mexican market

In the midst of an economic slowdown, the French automaker Renault will attack the Mexican market with a low-cost vehicle born in India, the small SUV Kwid. In this sense, its president and director, Magda Lopez, is confident that 2019 will be a great year for sales growth. Renault Mexico's goal is of 33,000 commercialized vehicles, of which 5,000 would come from the small Kwid. Said model has given great results in emerging markets, as it has been placed as a pillar of growth and profitability within the brand.
