Bulletin #17
12 January, 2017
Ford cancels plan to build US$ 1.6 billion plant in Mexico

Ford Motor Co. announced it is canceling plans for a new US$ 1.6 billion assembly plant in San Luis Potosi, instead choosing to build small cars in like the next-generation Focus sedan an existing factory in Hermosillo, and invest US$ 700 million in a Michigan facility that will build electric vehicles.
Low small vehicle sales, main reason to cancel Mexican plant: Ford CEO

It was market conditions, especially low small vehicle sales, not pressure from US President-elect Donald Trump, behind Ford Motor's decision not to build a new production facility in Mexico, the automaker CEO Mark Fields revealed. "We've seen small cars markedly decline, and it became very clear that we don't need this plant and we can use an existing facility in Mexico," Fields said in an interview.
Ford reveals a new line-up of electric, hybrid vehicles

Ford Motors revealed seven out of 13 new electric vehicles, including hybrid versions of the F-150 pickup and Mustang, along with a fully electric SUV with at least 300 miles range and a plug-in hybrid Transit Custom van. The automaker hope its electric vehicle plans will make its most popular vehicles "more capable, productive and fun to drive," and that electric cars will exceed gasoline-powered vehicles within the next 15 years.
Trump threatens GM with tax for car mostly built in U.S.

President-elect Donald Trump threatened General Motors Co. with a "big border tax for moving its Chevy Cruze model production to Mexico". GM responded in a press release that it makes most of them in Lordstown, Ohio. The automaker sold about 190,000 Chevy Cruze models in the country in 2016, and about 4,500 were the Mexican model, a company's spokesman said.
Fiat Chrysler debuts a semi-autonomous electric minivan

Fiat Chrysler revealed the Portal concept, a minivan-like, semi-autonomous electric vehicle geared toward millennials. The design features pillarless openings to the cabin with articulating front and rear doors, which use glass on both the top and the bottom. Chrysler estimates its Portal concept will have a range of more than 250 miles on a full charge, and that 150 miles of range can be restored in less than 20 minutes using a fast charger.
Here's Mexico's most exported vehicles to U.S., Canada and Germany

Mexico exported 2,551,623 vehicles between January and November 2016, out of which 89.9% had three countries as their main destination: the U.S., Canada and Germany. The most exported vehicle models were as of November 2016: Ford Fusion, Dodge RAM 2500 and Chevrolet Silverado 2500 to the U.S.; GMC Sierra, RAM 2500 and Volkswagen Jetta to Canada; Volkswagen Jetta, Volkswagen Beetle and Mazda 3 Hatchback to Germany.
Hyundai, Kia rely on Mexico to achieve 2017 sales target

South Korean auto giant Hyundai Motor and its affiliated Kia Motors are targeting combined 2017 sales of 8.25 million vehicles worldwide, up from their 7.88 million goal for 2016, which analysts expect the companies to miss. The two companies are counting on new plants in China and Mexico, where they will gradually raise output, and introduction of models including a sport utility vehicle to attract buyers.
Nidec Sankyo builds a US$ 15 million plant in San Luis Potosi

Japanese auto part manufacturer Nidec Sankyo will invest US$ 15 million to build a plant in San Luis Potosi, on a 4-hectares land, with plans for a second stage of expansion. The construction is expected to end by July this year, to start operations in August. The plant is expected to have over 300 employees by 2019. Nidec Sankyo Corporation manufactures electric motors and other components.
Dodge Journey, Jeep Compass, Jeep Patriot recalled to prevent potential stalling

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles is recalling nearly 50,000 Dodge Journey, Jeep Compass, and Jeep Patriot vehicles from the 2016 model year. The underlying problem with the three SUVs is a faulty crankshaft sensor connector, which may work intermittently. Should a connector lose contact, it could prevent a vehicle from starting, or it might cause the vehicle to stall in traffic. Although the connector flaw has the potential to cause serious problems for owners, FCA says that it's received no reports of injuries or accidents linked to the issue.
Jeep Compass, Patriot production ends at Illinois plant, retooling begins

The last Jeep Compass and Jeep Patriot to be made in the United States rolled off last week at the Fiat Chrysler Belvidere, Illinois, Assembly Plant. This week the plant began retooling the assembly line for the much-anticipated production of the Jeep Cherokee, one of the company's top selling vehicles. Last year, 220,000 Jeep Cherokees were sold. The Belvidere plant is expected to ramp up production to more than 300,000 Cherokees per year. A new Compass version in being built now in Toluca, Mexico, while the Patriot was discontinued.
