Mazda unveils its Kai Concept, a preview of the next-gen Mazda 3

Mazda Motor Corporation unveiled the Mazda Kai Concept at the 45th Tokyo Motor Show, a hatchback that offers a preview of what will become the next-generation Mazda 3. The unit features the Skyactiv-X engine, a spark-controlled compression ignition, a never-before-seen combustion method which provides dramatic improvements across a range of important performance indicators. The automaker builds the current Mazda 3 sedan and hatchback versions in Salamanca, Guanajuato, where there's also an adjacent engine production facility. However, there's still no confirmation that either the next-generation Mazda 3 and the Skyactiv-X engines will be manufactured in Mexico.
Heavy truck production in Mexico boosts 25.6% in August, exports up 47%

Production of heavy trucks in Mexico increased 25.6% to 14,978 units during August, compared to 11,927 vehicles in the same month of 2016. Exports totaled 11,515 vehicles, which represents a 47% hike also compared to August 2016, according to the latest figures released by the National Association of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Producers (ANPACT). From January through August, Mexican production of heavy trucks, buses and tractors totaled 97,554 units, a 2.9% decrease compared to 100,471 vehicles manufactured in the first eight months of 2016. During the same period exports totaled 72,684 heavy trucks, a 5.4% drop compared to the 76,805 units from January through August 2016, ANPACT revealed.
Heavy truck sales fall 10.1% during September in the Mexican market

Sales of heavy trucks in the Mexican market totaled 3,285 units, during September, a 10.1% decrease compared to same month of 2016, Mexican Auto Dealers Association (AMDA) reported. It was the fourth month this year with negative figures. Sales in the passenger segment (buses) dropped 14% to 652 vehicles, tractors fell 10.1% to 1,444 units and trucks decreased 7.8% to 1,189 vehicles. September also marked the first month with lower sales on a year-to-date basis. From January through September sales totaled 28,482 units, 100 vehicles less or a 0.3% decrease compared to the same nine-month period of 2016.
Germany-based manufacturer Webasto expands production capacity in Puebla

Webasto, a Germany-based manufacturer of sunroofs, panorama roofs and convertible roofs as well as heat systems, inaugurated the expansion of its plant for convertible roofs in Puebla, Mexico, where the company invested around US$ 3 million. As a result, the company is increasing the production capacities to a total of 11,800 square meters and is reinforcing the location as a competence center for textiles in North America. Webasto currently employs around 220 people in Puebla. Among the products Webasto is currently manufacturing in Puebla are the VW Beetle Softtop and the Jeep ThrowBack roof, as well as textile components for the Chevrolet Corvette and the Ford Mustang.
German auto supplier Kiekert AG develops 'autonomous door' concept

Kiekert AG, a manufacturer of side-door latches and closure systems for the auto industry, announced it is developing an "autonomous door" concept which will intelligently control access into vehicles, preventing unnecessary dents and scratches on vehicle doors, and help them to completely prevent injuries to pedestrians and other vehicles. The company is combining its latest product solutions, which include an electromagnetic brake system with dynamic environment recognition to raise vehicle access to a new level, allowing automatic opening of the vehicle door using a fingertip or even digital devices like smartphones. The company operates engineering and manufacturing centers in Puebla, Mexico, and Wixom, Michigan.
US auto industry to Trump: We're winning with NAFTA

Major automakers, suppliers and auto dealers launched a new coalition to urge U.S. President Donald Trump not to withdraw from NAFTA. Auto trade associations representing major carmakers including General Motors, Toyota, Volkswagen, Hyundai and Ford are part of the coalition dubbed "Driving American Jobs." The group is behind an advertising campaign to convince the White House and voters that NAFTA has been crucial in boosting U.S. automotive sector production and jobs. They pointed to US$ 9.5 billion in new investments announced this year by the auto and auto parts sector and feature the personal stories of auto sector employees throughout America - from plant workers to auto dealership personnel.
GM was days from closing Canadian plant when strike ended, sources say

General Motors was within days of shutting down its Cami Assembly plant in Ingersoll, Ontario, for good when it settled with its striking workers, labor sources said to The London Free Press. The automaker notified Cami suppliers last week to prepare "supply lines" to Mexico, where it planned to shift production, said Tim McKinnon, chairperson of Unifor Local 199 at GM Canada's plant in St. Catharines, which supplies Cami with transmissions. "It is 100 per cent real. We talked to our suppliers and were given notice to get ready to ship parts to Mexico. Once they start going that way, it is hard to turn it around," said McKinnon.
General Motors third quarter earnings down 32%, but better than expected

General Motors earned US$ 2.5 billion before taxes in the third-quarter, down 32% from the US$ 3.7 billion year-earlier pre-tax earnings, but better than most Wall Street analysts were expecting. On a net-income basis, the automaker lost US$ 3 billion, but that reflected US$ 5.4 billion in accounting charges for the sale of its Opel and Vauxhall European brands to PSA Group. GM warned in July that its profits might decline as it cut production of slower selling cars and retooled several plants for production of new crossover and SUV models. Now that GM Europe is off its books, GM made money in all other regions, including US$ 500 million in China and US$ 100 million in South America. The majority of its profit, US$ 2.1 billion, came from North America.
FCA posts record third quarter earnings

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles NV reported a profit of about US$ 659 million (606 million euros) during the third quarter, despite recall costs and lower sales and market share in the U.S. and Canada. "We had our best Q3 ever for adjusted earnings before interest and taxes and our best adjusted net profit ever," said the company in a statement. The carmaker reported an 8% adjusted operating profit margin in North America, up from 7.6% a year earlier. FCA attributed the increase mainly to a heavier sales mix of more profitable pickups and SUVs, as well as pricing gains. FCA said its U.S. market share slipped to 11.3% at the end of the quarter, from 12.5% a year earlier. Canadian share fell to 11.1% from 12.5%.
All-new Jeep Compass earns 2017 Top Safety Pick rating from the IIHS

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles announced the all-new Jeep Compass has earned a 2017 Top Safety Pick rating from the Insurance Institute of Highway Safety (IIHS). The 2017 compact SUV achieves "good" ratings in each of five IIHS tests that evaluate crashworthiness. Its available Automatic Emergency Braking (AEB) technology also earns a grade of "superior." Each of these ratings are the highest possible IIHS rating in its category. The Jeep Compass scored "good" ratings in tests that simulate a side impact with a large SUV or pickup truck, a moderate-offset frontal impact, a small-offset frontal impact, roof-deformation consistent with a rollover; and a rear collision capable of inducing whiplash.
