May 13th, 2019
KIA Motors Mexico rises to fourth place within the Mexican market

For the first time since its arrival, KIA Motors Mexico ranked fourth in sales within the Mexican automotive industry with 7,429 sold units during April, according to data from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This achievement meant a 7.6% share within the national automotive market.
BMW manufactures 709 units in new San Luis Potosi plant

The BMW factory in San Luis Potosi reported 709 manufactured vehicles during April to the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). The firm commenced the plant’s construction two years ago and it began its testing phase at the beginning of 2019. The inauguration of the plant, which state and federal authorities are to attend, is scheduled for June 6th. The factory, where BMW 3 Series vehicles will be assembled, will have the capacity to produce 175,000 cars per year.
Heavy truck production hikes 34.6% in April: ANPACT

The National Association of Bus, Truck and Tractor Truck Producers (ANPACT) reported that, during the month of April, the wholesale sale of heavy vehicles reached a total of 3,865 units, which compared to the 2,871 registered in April 2018, represented a 34.6% increase. During the same month, retail sales reached 3,840 units, which, compared to those sold during the same month last year, represented an increase of 19.0%.
Royal Power Solutions opens an assembly plant in Queretaro

Royal Power Solutions, a supplier of essential components for the distribution of electrical energy and high-precision signals for global automotive and industrial applications, has opened a new assembly plant in the automotive mega-cluster in Queretaro. Royal started production in Queretaro in February and it’s currently assembling terminal systems for automotive batteries, molded plastic covers, and electrical terminals for multiple Tier 1 and 2 manufacturers.
We want to develop a supply chain for the electric automotive industry: Jalisco’s SEDECO

Jalisco is looking to get involved in the production of electric vehicles, which is why it's planning to pull an electric assembly plant from China into the state within the next three years. Ernesto Sanchez Proal, head of the Economic Development Secretariat (SEDECO) of Jalisco, explained that a supply chain focused on electric mobility is also to be developed.
Koichi Yoda, the new CEO of Isuzu Motors Mexico

Hirokazu Maruyama, who served as chairman of the Board and CEO of Isuzu Motors Mexico, has left his position in the hands of Koichi Yoda, who has extensive experience within the firm's subsidiaries around the world. "Mr. Yoda started working for Isuzu in the year of 1980; [he has] almost 40 years of experience working within different markets around the world," explained Isuzu Motors Mexico in a statement.
AMIA forecasts 3.5% growth in automotive production and exports

The volume of Mexican production and exports of vehicles will grow 3.5% in 2019 compared to last year, estimated Eduardo Solis, president of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA). The head of the association said that this is a moderate forecast that could be changed throughout the rest of the year as more companies join in to produce cars in Mexico.
