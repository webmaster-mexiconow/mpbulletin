We support the USMCA: Toyota Mexico

Toyota is positive and confident that ratification will be given to the new trade agreement between Mexico, the United States and Canada (USMCA), which, in the opinion of the automaker, needed to be renewed. "Our position in relation to the [still valid] North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) was that it benefited the automotive industry in Mexico, the United States and Canada; however, we recognized before the government that the agreement was more than 25 years old and needed to be improved and updated," said Bob Carter, executive vice president of sales for Toyota Motor North America.
Coahuila explores automotive opportunities in China

In order to strengthen and expand the automotive industry in Coahuila, a commercial mission was sent to the Auto Shanghai 2019 event, held in China, said state governor Miguel Angel Riquelme Solis. Governor Riquelme explained that the delegation of the entity that attended the automotive event, held from April 16 to 25, had business meetings with 20 companies, mainly Chinese and Japanese. He explained that this fair has become one of the exhibitions of automobiles with greater authority and international influence in China, as it aims to establish cooperation between the Chinese and foreign automotive industries.
The Network of Automotive Clusters of Mexico seeks to attract foreign suppliers

The Network of Automotive Clusters of Mexico aims to attract foreign automotive suppliers to the entities of the country of said sector that currently have buyers in Mexico, but have not decided to install their production plants in national soil as they lack knowledge when it comes to the possible amount of demand within the country. "We call it the location fast track; we know that there are supplier companies abroad that are interested in coming to Mexico; they already sell to one or two companies here, but the volume is not enough for them to consider opening their facilities here," explained Alexandre Burgueño Rodriguez, general director of the network.
Uber seeks Mexican talent for the development of autonomous cars

Uber has more than 100 vacancies in its Advanced Technology Group that specializes in the development of autonomous vehicles. In this sense, the company wants part of said vacancies to be occupied by Mexican engineers. On the other hand, Uber took advantage of the Jalisco Talent Land 2019 event, so as to encourage young people to apply for the vacancies while using Martin Velez as an example, who works as a software engineer for Uber in the Advanced Technology Group. Velez wants more young Mexicans to have similar opportunities, which is why he urged them to apply as well.
Lexus to arrive in Mexico by 2021

With the goal of becoming the best-selling automotive luxury brand, Lexus will arrive in Mexico by September 2021, announced Bob Carter, executive vice president of Toyota Motor Sales North America. In a press conference held in Mexico City, Carter announced that Lexus will start selling vehicles in the last quarter of 2021, with Mexico City, Monterrey and Guadalajara being the first cities that will be able to offer official Lexus distributors.
Mercedes-Benz Buses delivers 33 vehicles in San Luis Potosi

Mercedes-Benz Buses, alongside Tangamanga Urban, Suburban Transports and TULSA, delivered 33 buses for urban transportation purposes in San Luis Potosi. The Boxer buses, which are equipped with a MBO 1421/60 chassis, offer the latest BlueTec 5 generation engines of low emissions, BusConnection telematics and monitoring system, which have a manual transmission. The 33 buses represented an investment of more than US$3.15 million and are to mobilize approximately 20,000 passengers daily.
Volkswagen Mexico finishes second stage of the "Reconstruyamos Puebla" initiative

With the delivery of 42 rebuilt homes and rooms to residents of San Miguel Tecuanipa, Puebla, who happened to be affected by the earthquake of September 19, 2017, Volkswagen Mexico completed the second of three stages of the "Reconstruyamos Puebla" initiative. In the aftermath of this earthquake, which left severe damage to thousands of homes in Puebla, many of which were declared uninhabitable, Volkswagen Mexico launched said reconstruction program in the community whose main means of subsistence is agriculture.
Mopar Mexico to offer new accessories to Jeep Renegade Limited 2019 users

The Mopar brand joins the launch of the Jeep Renegade Limited 2019 with a portfolio of more than 60 accessories to improve the model's potential. Jeep Renegade Limited 2019 arrived in Mexico offering all the refinement, equipment, security and technological advances necessary to meet the needs of customers, offering excellent driving dynamics. The owners of Jeep Renegade Limited can customize it by choosing some of the accessories offered by Mopar, such as: rear and front loaders, luggage rack, bike rack, tents, among others.
Navistar Mexico opens new store in Aguascalientes

Navistar Mexico, alongside CACESA, inaugurated a new spare parts store in Aguascalientes, with the aim of improving the delivery time of the "Express Spare Parts" service, and providing better service to customers in the Bajio area. The store has spare parts from the most representative brands in the market such as Fleetrite, Cummins Alesso, Grote, Bendix, Meritor, Saf Holland and Idealease, among others. "With the opening of this new store we will be closer to the operations of our customers in the southern part of the state," said Martin Amarante, CACESA's CEO.
Ford to produce environment-friendly vehicles in Mexico

In addition to the reconversion process carried out by Ford at its Cuautitlan Izcalli plant, the creation of an Advanced Development and Engineering Center in the state has already been planned, said Enrique Gonzalez Hernandez, general director of the plant. Gonzalez Hernandez announced that the company's goal is to remain a benchmark within the international market; in this sense, the State of Mexico will be developing technologies that use friendlier fuels. The reconversion will be completed in 2020, so that between 2022 and 2023, fully electric units will be produced within the plant.
