German auto supplier Continental to open US$ 62 million R&D center in Queretaro

After municipal officials in Queretaro revealed that an important investment by an automotive company would soon be announced for the installation of a R&D center in the city, state authorities confirmed that it is the German auto supplier Continental who's behind this project. The company invested about US$ 62 million and will open 1,100 positions for highly qualified personnel to start up the technical center, from where it will work on technologies for autonomous vehicles, connected and electric cars, according to Jorge Vazquez Murillo, who will act as director of the facility. He explained that Continental chose the entity for the concentration of universities and the number of researchers per capita, which is among the highest in Mexico.
Auto industry officials hopeful that Canadian proposal can avoid the end of NAFTA

Auto industry officials are now hopeful that a Canadian proposal could avert the end of NAFTA as some key members of the Trump administration have shown interest in the plan. In a bid to keep the talks alive Canada is now suggesting that North American content in autos -known as rules of origin- should be calculated to also include the value of software, engineering and other high-value work, which currently is not counted toward the regional content targets. The plan got a boost from Fiat Chrysler Automobiles Chief Executive Sergio Marchionne on Thursday, who called it a "defensible" concept and a "step in the right direction". "Within the structure proposed by the Canadians, there appears to be the beginnings of a solution to this problem," Marchionne said.
Guajardo hints NAFTA talks could be extended

Mexico on Friday suggested talks to modernize NAFTA could be extended to give officials more time to address disagreements threatening to undermine the US$ 1.2 trillion trade pact. As the negotiations grind on, officials are looking at an extra round in Mexico at the end of February, according to sources close to the process. Although the process was initially scheduled to finish by the end of March to avoid clashing with Mexican presidential elections in July, Economy Minister Ildefonso Guajardo said the timeline could be extended. "This negotiation has a window of opportunity to reach a deal between February and the end of July," he told Reuters.
Boost in automotive exports lessens Mexico's trade deficit to its lowest in three years

Mexican exports ended 2017 with an annual increase of 9.5%, representing the most significant growth since 2011. Such rise contributed to reach the smallest trade deficit in three years as shipments of manufactured goods helped offset a widening gap in the country's petroleum products trade. The country ran up a US$ 157 million deficit in December, bringing the full-year 2017 trade deficit to US$ 10.88 billion, the lowest since 2014. Total 2017 exports rose to US$ 409.49 billion while imports increased 8.6% to US$ 420.37 billion. Full year manufacturing shipments totaled US$ 364.48 billion, which represented an 8.5% increase, boosted in part by an 11.8% hike in automotive exports to US$ 126.67 billion.
Japan-based Toray Industries opens US$ 80 million plant in Jalisco

Officials from Japan-based Toray Industries inaugurated a new plant in El Salto, Jalisco, to manufacture fabrics for automotive air bags, its first production facility related to this product in the Americas. The company invested US$ 80 million and recruited 170 workers in order to start operations with capacity to produce about 10,000 tons of nylon fibers per year. This will increase the Japanese global company's output capacity about 20%. The plant sits on the grounds of a factory operated by Toray's American carbon fiber subsidiary Zoltek. The global market for air bags is growing 8% a year on average and will reach $29.7 billion in 2020, U.S. research firm Transparency Market Research projects.
Fiat Chrysler profit climbs; employees get US$ 5,500 bonus

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles N.V. (FCA) released its earnings for 2017, showing profits up 93% for the year and announcing that eligible employees will get profit-sharing checks averaging US$ 5,500 to eligible UAW-represented employees. Approximately 40,000 employees will receive the payment on February 16, 2018. The company reported pretax profits of US$ 4.4 billion (3.5 billion euro). Adjusted earnings per share of US$ 3 (2.41 euro) were an increase of more than US$ 2 (1.64 euro) during 2016. Revenues were flat at US$ 139 billion (111 billion euro). The earnings results beat the US$ 1.9 billion (1.8 billion euro as released last January) in net profits the company reported for 2016.
Lear Corp. posts record profit boosted by acquisitions, new US tax law

Lear Corp. reported strong quarterly profit and record full year earnings led by higher sales in Europe and Africa and the acquisition of Grupo Antolin's seating business. Its fourth-quarter profit jumped 74% to US$ 400.5 million, or US$ 5.80 a share, an increase from US$ 229.9 million, or US$ 3.24 a share, for the same quarter a year before. Regarding full year 2017 results, the company posted a record net income of US$ 1,313 million and record adjusted net income of US$ 1,178 million, compared to US$ 975 million and US$ 1,026 million, respectively, in the prior year. In the fourth quarter, the company had a one-time net tax benefit of US$ 146 million related to the new tax law. Sales totaled a record US$ 20.5 billion during the full year, up 10% compared to 2016.
Mazda tops EPA fuel economy list for fifth year in a row

Mazda announced that the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency listed the company as having the highest overall Manufacturer Adjusted Fuel Economy for the 2016 model year. Mazda received the first-place ranking for the fifth year in a row. The EPA's report summarizes fuel economy trends by model year for vehicles sold in the U.S. in order to rank automakers. Mazda's overall average fuel economy was 29.6 miles per gallon, improved 0.4 mpg over the previous year. The company said its SKYACTIV technology, an innovative range of engines, transmissions, bodies and chassis, played a key role in this achievement. Since 2014, Mazda builds SKYACTIV engines at an US$ 120 million facility in Guanajuato, with an output capacity of 230,000 units a year.
Plastic recycler specialized in automotive molders to open three facilities in Mexico

SPS Plastics LLC, a Maryland-based recycler specialized in processing scrap plastics from Tier 1 automotive molders, has plans to open three facilities in Mexico, including a 50,000-square-foot site now under construction in San Luis Potosi, which is expected to open this Spring, Plastic News reported. The company is also looking at locations in Queretaro and Monterrey, where the firm is currently outsourcing work to third-party recyclers. Those locations could open next year, according to SPS Plastics Chief Operating Officer Shreyas Naik. According to Plastic News, SPS focus on high temperature polymers, which are used to manufacture components that are placed under the hood.
Toyota aims to increase sales in Mexico by 2% during 2018

After a year of slowdown in sales in the Mexican market, Toyota anticipates that in 2018 sales will reach 107,000 units, which would represent a growth close to 2% compared to 2017, said Tom Sullivan, president of Toyota Motor Sales de Mexico in an interview to local press. Toyota ended 2017 with sales of 105,464 units, which meant a growth of 0.5% compared to 2016 and remained the fourth best-selling brand in the Mexican market by volume. For 2018, Toyota plans to exceed the customers' expectations with the incursion of two new models, the hybrid vehicle Prius C and the C-HR compact SUV. The official said that with the introduction of these two new vehicles the Japanese firm will feature 18 models in its offer for Mexico.
