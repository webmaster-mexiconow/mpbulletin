October 18th, 2019
The 2020 Mazda2 will arrive in November

The subcompact 2020 Mazda2 will arrive to the brand's 60 Mexican dealerships starting in November. Among its features we can highlight the 1.5 liter SKYACTIV engine of 109 hp and 104 lb-ft torque and manual or automatic transmission. The model in its i MT version has a price of MXN$259,900, while the i AT version has a price of MXN$260,900, the i Touring MT costs MXN$279,900; the i Touring AT and Grand Touring AT are placed at MXN$289,900 and MXN$309,900, respectively.
SDEyT of Morelos held a meeting with AMDA to strengthen automotive investment

The head of the Ministry of Labor and Economic Development of Morelos (SDEyT), Ana Cecilia Rodriguez Gonzalez, held a work meeting with members from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), with the purpose of combining efforts that will allow for the reinvestment and strengthening of the automotive sector in Morelos. Cecilia Rodriguez shared with AMDA executive, Sergio Aldana Hinzpeter, the public policies working toward the promotion of companies, domestic consumption, of the textile, automotive and commercial sector with sights set to a better projection for citizens' purchasing power.
INFINITI celebrates its eight year in Mexico

INFINITI has empowered its drivers during these 8 years in Mexico with a new daring design language in its models and concepts, creating a unique driving experience; thus reaffirming its commitment to fulfill the brand's fundamental values on a global scale, and has made great technological advances in its powertrains that give great performance and efficiency when driving, as well as a future vision of semi-autonomous technologies that will give more security and comfort to both drivers and their passengers.
Siemens celebrates 125 years in Mexico

Siemens celebrates 125 years in Mexico within the framework of the first edition of Hannover Messe's Industrial Transformation Mexico. Juan Ignacio Vergara, CEO of Siemens Mexico and Central America, said that they started operations in 1894 lighting 14 kilometers of Paseo de la Reforma. As part of the celebration for its 125th anniversary in Mexico, Siemens signed an agreement with the government of Guanajuato with the purpose of donating software for public schools within the state.
30 units of the MX-5's 30th anniversary edition will reach Mexico

To commemorate the thirtieth anniversary of production and sales of the MX-5 roadster (developed by the Japanese company Mazda), period during which there's been more than a million sold units, placing it like the most accepted model in the world, the brand decided to create 3,000 collection units, with exclusive characteristics and exclusive "racing orange" color. The Mexican market will receive 30 vehicles, which will be auctioned through the website www.mazda.mx, from 17:00 to 22:00 during the 30 days of November.
The Audi R8 V10 Decennium arrives to national territory

Audi is celebrating the successful story of the V10 engine with the Audi R8 V10 Decennium, a limited edition of 222 units. V10 engine of 610 hp, bronze detailing in the exterior and interior. Ten years of fascination on the road and success in racing. Mexico, who will receive 25 units, is one of the countries that will have more units of this model. The price for the R8 Coupe V10 Performance is of MXN$3,269,900, while the R8 Spyder V10 Performance starts at MXN$3,519,900.
ANPACT asks for more financing and the guarantee of ULSD

To increase competiveness of small and medium enterprises in Mexico, the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) urged the minister of Economy, Graciela Marquez, to boost the renewal of the vehicular fleet through financing for businessmen, which will then allow for a respite for the national market. Miguel Elizalde, president of the heavy vehicles industry also asked the Ministry of Energy, through the Ministry of Economy, to accelerate the availability of ultra-low sulfur diesel (ULSD).
Limited edition of the Tiguan arrives to our country

The Volkswagen Tiguan has become one of the most popular C-SUVs for the Mexican market. To improve its product portfolio, the German brand is introducing an alternative for Mexico, called Limited Edition, charged with a good amount of driving assistance tools and availability of a two or three row of seats format. This edition is distinguished by its bodywork in petrol blue-although there's other available colors-with silver mirror shells. The pricing for the five passenger version is of MXN$524,990; the seven passenger version has the price of MXN$539,900.
INFINITI QX50: first class aerodynamics

INFINITI, presents the QX50's new form language that contribute to a pure driving dynamism of the brand's medium proportion SUV. A sporty look and a new structure are the elements that the QX50 reveals at plain sight. Exterior technology that allows air to flow smoothly over the vehicle, reducing wind resistance when driving, making the vehicle more stable when driving at high-speeds or in cross wind scenarios.
The 2020 Renault Koleos arrives to Mexico

From Renault's global headquarters in Boulogne-Billancourt, Paris, France, the automotive company informed of the changes made to the current second generation of its SUV Koleos. Julien Peirano, product chief for Koleos and Espace, said that the main evolution has to do with an exterior reconfiguration, to give it a luxurious image and one of a superior segment, which is why the chromed detailing, is the common factor for the 2020 model. For our country, the 4 cylinder 2.5 liter engine, that develops 171 hp and a torque of 172 lb-ft, is maintained; the transmission is CVT.
