Bulletin #272
October 8th, 2019
Toyota sold 8,258 vehicles in September

Toyota sold 8,258 units during the ninth month of 2019, thus registering its best September in Mexico. With this, the brand managed to secure the fourth place in market participation, with a 7.9% in the annual accumulation. The leading vehicles were Hilux with 1,560 units, followed by RAV4 with 1,171 units; Corolla with 1,061, followed by Avanza with 723 and Sienna with 697 units. Regarding the sales of eco-friendly vehicles, there were 1,850 units sold.
Credit for new cars decreases, but increases for pre-owned cars

Financing for new cars in Mexico droped 12% during August of this year, by placing 65,856 vehicles against the 74,856 units from last year, reported the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). Before the decline in the acquisition of new financed units, consumers have chosen to buy pre-owned cars, via credit, method that increased 9% during the eight month of the year, by placing 9,912 units, compared to the 9,097 cars from 2018.
Hybrid and electric vehicles sales increase in Mexico

During July 2019 the sales of hybrid and electric vehicles in Mexico increased 86% in annual comparison. In the seventh month of the year companies sold 2,084 hybrid engine or electric motor vehicles, a significantly higher result than the one registered for the same month of last year when sales amounted to only 1,113 units, according to a report from the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA).
Automotive production and exports experience decline in September

Mexico produced 318,906 new vehicles in September 2019, 0.4% less than the reported amount in the same month of last year, this being the lowest volume for a similar lapse in three years, informed the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This was also reflected in exports, since in the ninth month of the year a total of 284,243 vehicles were sent abroad, 7.1% less than that reported in September 2018.
Kia Motors of Mexico sold a total of 7,714 units in September

The sales total for the period was of 7,714 units, which represents a market participation of 7.7% and secures the South Korean brand's 5th place on a national level, and additionally, Kia is the only brand within the sales top 5 that's had a 2.1% growth in the accumulated results of 2019 vs 2018. With 3,154 units placed this month, Kia Rio, proudly made in the brand's Pesqueria plant, it consolidated itself like the consumers' favorite brand.
General Motors' Ramos Arizpe plant informed partial halt of operations

Through a press release, General Motors Ramos Arizpe informed the partial halt of technical operations for the GEN engine and the CV7 transmission starting October 7. GM of Mexico informed that the assembly plant where the Chevrolet Blazer and Equinox are produced is operating normally. At the same time, the engine plant keeps producing the CSS engine normally and there's only a minor impact in the production in the engine and transmissions plant, caused by the lack of components coming from one U.S. plant that's not functioning because of the strike.
Mazda CX-30 arrives to Mexico

Produced in the Mazda Motor Manufacturing plant in Salamanca, Guanajuato, the model CX-30 will be introduced to the Mexican market in November in two versions: iSport 2WD and iGrand Touring 2WD, and their price is of MXN$424,900 and MXN$454,900, respectively. In its two versions, the vehicle is equipped with a 2.5 liter engine, for 189 horse power and 186 punds-foot torque. It's a vehicle that will place itself in an intermediate segment between the CX-3 and the CX-5.
