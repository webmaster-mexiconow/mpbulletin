Bulletin #154
7 June, 2018
Sales of Puebla-made Audi Q5 remain flat during May in the US market

Audi of America reported May sales of 19,315 vehicles, a marginal increase of 0.6 percent compared to total sales of same month in 2017. Audi's year-to-date sales through May totaled 88,471 units, an increase of 5.9 percent compared to the same five months of 2017. The Q5 SUV made in Puebla remained as Audi's best-selling model in the United States on sales of 4,918 units, which means more than one in four vehicles sold by the German automaker in May was a Q5, although it represented a flat result compared to the 4,909 units sold in May 2017. Year-to-date sales of the Q5 totaled 24,223 vehicles, an increase of 14 percent compared to the same five-month period of 2017.
US sales of Toluca-made Jeep Compass triple in May

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles US (FCA) reported sales of 214,294 vehicles in May 2018, an 11 percent increase compared to sales of 193,040 vehicles in May 2017. Year-to-date sales are now of 913,212 units, up four percent. FCA sold 17,327 Jeep Compass last month, an increase of 223 percent compared to the 5,363 units the company sold in May 2017, which was the second complete month of sales for the SUV. Year-to-date sales are now of 72,368 vehicles. Sales of the Fiat 500 were of just 264 units in May and 1,908 vehicles on a year-to-date basis, representing drops of 79 percent and 70 percent respectively. Sales of the Dodge Journey hiked 37 percent in May to 10,966 units, but its year-to-date results are still down 19 percent to 41,023 vehicles during the first five months of 2018. All three vehicles are built at FCA's plant in Toluca.
Sales of Ford Fusion, Lincoln MKZ sink at two-digit pace; Ford Fiesta deliveries up 22 percent

Overall Ford Motor Company U.S. sales for May totaled 242,824 vehicles, a marginal gain of 0.7 percent compared to same month of 2017. Year-to-date sales were of 1,047,056 units, down 2.4 percent. The Ford Fiesta saw its sales hike 22.2 percent to 5,110 units in May, while its year-to-date sales totaled 20,559 vehicles, just five units more compared to same five-month period of 2017. Sales of the Ford Fusion sank 29.4 percent to 15,253 vehicles, while its year-do-date sales were of 71,300 units, down 20 percent. The Lincoln MKZ saw a 35.7 percent drop in sales of 1,800 units in May and a decline of 35.1 percent to 7,851 vehicles in a year-to-date basis. Both the Fusion and the MKZ are built in Hermosillo, Sonora, while the Ford Fiesta for the U.S. market is sourced from Cuautitlan Izcalli, state of Mexico.
GM drops monthly sales reports

General Motors announced in March that it would cease monthly sales reports in favor of quarterly reports. Its next sales report will be published in the first days of July.
Sales of both Mexico-made Honda models decline in May

Honda Motor Co. reported U.S. May sales of 153,069 Honda and Acura vehicles, a 3.1 percent increase versus May 2017. Year-to-date sales are down 1.7 percent to 641,261 units. Sales of the Honda Fit totaled 4,976 units in May, down 2.5 percent. Its year-to-date sales are down 7.4 percent to 20,254 vehicles. The HR-V saw an eight percent decrease in sales of 8,773 units. Its year-to-date sales totaled 36,898 vehicles, which represents an 2.3 percent drop. The Honda Fit is built in Celaya, Guanajuato, while the HR-V is manufactured in Celaya but also in El Salto, Jalisco.
Kia Forte is the brand's best-selling vehicle in the US during May

Kia Motors America announced May sales of 59,462 vehicles, a 1.6 percent increase over the same month last year. Year-to-date sales totaled 236,992 units, which represents a 1.08 percent decrease. Its sister brand Hyundai reported May sales of 66,056 units, up 10 percent. Its year-to-date deliveries of 270,996 vehicles represent a 7.1 percent decline. Sales of the Kia Rio totaled 2,181 units, meaning an 83.7 percent hike. Year-to-date sales totaled 9,170 units, up 60.2 percent. The Kia Forte was the brand's best-selling vehicle in May with 11,261 units delivered, up 1.6 percent compared to last year. Its year-to-date sales totaled 44,191 vehicles, representing a 6.7 percent decline. Sales of the Hyundai Accent totaled 2,471 units in May, while the year-to-date figure was of 12,705 vehicles. Since last August, the Accent is built in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon, along with the Kia Rio and the Forte.
Sales of Mazda3 drop 11 percent in May

Mazda North American Operations reported May U.S. sales of 29,980 vehicles, representing an increase of 15.1 percent versus May of last year. Year-to-date sales through May are up 14.9 percent versus last year, with 137,031 vehicles sold. Sales of the Mazda3 in sedan and hatchback versions totaled 5,983 units, representing a drop of 11.7 percent. Year-to-date deliveries reached 30,363 vehicles, which represents an nine percent decline. Both versions of the Mazda3 for the U.S. market are sourced from Salamanca, Guanajuato.
Infiniti delivers 1,859 QX50 SUVs in its second full month of sales

Nissan Group announced total U.S. sales for May of 131,832 units, a decrease of 4.1 percent compared to the previous year. Year-to-date sales totaled 635,599 vehicles, down six percent. Sales of the Nissan Versa, including both sedan and hatchback versions, were 27 percent down to 6,576 units in May. Year-to-date sales decreased 21.6 percent to 37,939 vehicles. The Nissan Sentra saw its sales drop 1.5 percent to 18,103 units in May, while its year-to-date deliveries are still 5.6 percent higher than last year to 95,104 vehicles. With its second full month of sales at Infiniti retailers, the all-new 2019 QX50 built in Aguascalientes totaled 1,859 deliveries in May for a total 3,272 vehicles sold in the year so far.
Sales of Toyota Tacoma hike 21 percent in May

Toyota reported U.S. May 2018 sales of 215,321 vehicles, a decrease of 1.3 percent compared to May 2017. Its year-to-date sales are 2.8 percent up to 979,710 units. Sales of the Yaris iA, which is built by Mazda at its Guanajuato plant, decreased 16.9 percent to 2,861 vehicles. Year-to-date sales are 14.8 percent down to 13,430 units. Sales of the Toyota Tacoma midsize pickup, built in Tijuana and San Antonio, Texas, hiked 21.1 percent to 21,380 units in May, and 20.2 percent in a year-to-date basis to 93,970 vehicles.
The all-new Tiguan is once again Volkswagen's best-selling vehicle in May

Volkswagen reported sales of 31,211 units delivered in May, an increase of four percent over same month of 2017. With 143,957 units delivered year-to-date in 2018, the German automaker is reporting an 7.5 percent increase in year-over-year sales. Sales of the Golf family declined 44.1 percent to 4,027 units, and 36 percent in a year-to-date basis to 20,705 vehicles. All variants of the Golf family are built in Puebla, except for the e-Golf and the R-Line, which are made in Germany. Sales of the Jetta totaled 6,821 units, while year-to-date deliveries were of 25,784 vehicles, down 40.9 percent and 43.3 percent respectively. Sales of the Beetle totaled 1,492 units, down 18.4 percent. Year-to-date sales totaled 6,839 vehicles, which represented a decline of 5.5 percent. Sales of the all-new 2018 Tiguan, which entered the U.S. market last August, totaled 8,579 units and is once again the brand's best-selling model. Year-to-date sales are now of 38,314 vehicles.
