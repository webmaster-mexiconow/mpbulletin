Despite drop in sales Mexico is a key player for BMW

BMW Group managed to increase sales 1.8% worldwide, however, Latin America represented a challenge because of lower sales in the region, and where the Mexican market reported a decrease of 3.2% when compared to 2018, by placing 29,458 units. Despite the numbers, Mexico is a key player in BMW Group's strategy to be the center of operations in Latin America. The decision to place Mexico as the center of operations of BMW Group in Latin America has delivered positive results, said Maru Escobedo, general director of the German automaker in Mexico.

Daimler's Santiago plant celebrates 50 years of operations

The Daimler Santiago plant was inaugurated in 1969 and is successfully operating in the State of Mexico; throughout its operations it has established a provider network that has positioned Mexico as the main tractor truck exporter in the world. It currently has 2,630 direct workers and benefits more than 10,000 people within the region. This plant has the capacity to produce 175 units a day; which is to say, 875 units a week.

Aline Berumen joins GM as manager of Public Relations and Communication

Aline Berumen has joined General Motors of Mexico as manager of Public Relations and Communication, position in which she'll be responsible for the strategy of Product Communication of Chevrolet, Buick, GMC and Cadillac before journalists and opinion leaders. Likewise, she'll be responsible for GM's Internal Communication strategy. Pick-up sales increase 4.8% in 2019

The only segment that saw an increase in sales in the Mexican automotive market last year was the pick-up segment. In total, there were 191,729 sold pick-ups in Mexico, 4.8% more than those sold in 2018, as indicated by numbers from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). The most sold models in 2019 were Nissan NP 300, Toyota Hilux, Ford Ranger, Ford F-150, Volkswagen Saveiro, Renault Oroch, RAM 700, Mitsubishi L200, Chevrolet Silverado 2500 and Toyota Tacoma.

Automotive financing drops 6.9% in 2019

There were 902,026 vehicles sold through credit during 2019 in Mexico, 6.9% less than the ones registered a year before, which represented the biggest fall for the industry in 10 years, according to numbers from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). A total of 790,823 new vehicles were placed last year, 9.2% less than those registered in 2018. On the other hand, credit for used vehicles increased 14% by reaching a sales volume of 111,203 units.

Suzuki hopes to sell 36,000 vehicles in 2020

Amidst the crisis faced by the Mexico's automotive market, Japanese company Suzuki hopes to sell 36,000 vehicles in the country in 2020, which would mean a 16% increase in sales when compared to 2019, said David Hernandez, commercial director for the company. Their bet would be based on vehicle sales that would have the possibility of obtaining the double zero environmental certification and getting it a second time and not in sacrificing dealerships with bonuses or discounts. Currently, the brand has 72 dealerships and expects to open between three to five new ones this year.
