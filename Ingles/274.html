Hyundai Motor of Mexico achieved the sale of 200,000 units

Hyundai Motor of Mexico reached the sale of 200,000 units, with 3,659 units sold in September. After five years in the country, the South Korean brand has firmly placed itself within the top ten when it comes to driver's preference at the moment they're choosing a new vehicle. In total, there's been a total of 203,829 units sold from the South Korean brand in the country, of which 32,602 vehicles have been sold this year. Hyundai Accent, in its sedan and hatchback versions, was the brand's top seller model in September, with a total of 809 units.
Daimler Commercial Vehicles delivers four New Cascadia units in Jalisco

With a productive and commercial headquarters in San Juan de Los Lagos, Jalisco, the company Proteina Animal (PROAN) dedicated to the transformation of natural food inputs, acquired four Freightliner New Cascadia tractor units, in concrete operation with the Jimenez Autocamiones distributor, which is directed by Fernando Jimenez. The tractor units were delivered to the company, which is characterized by its human capital, cutting-edge technology use, implementation of environmentally friendly processes, as well as the quality of their products.
Special edition of Suzuki Vitara reaches our country

Suzuki created a special version of its Vitara model that will have the special name "Crystal." It is the exterior that receives the distinct touches, because while the forms are the same in this generation, there's the addition of 17 inches aluminum wheel rims, lateral chrome strips, a back spoiler in the bodywork's color and a new outline in the back fascia. All of this will be shaded in Iridium White, which will be this edition's special color.
Mexico positioned itself as the 14th market worldwide

In 2018 there was a total of 1,421,458 sold cars in Mexico, amount with which the country placed itself like the 14th market worldwide, the lowest position held by the country since 2014, according to data from the International Organization of Motor Vehicle Manufacturers. In their global car sales report, in 2017 the country was on 13th place and two years before that it was in 12th place, which is why this would be the second year it drops a place.
CUPRA will arrive to Mexico on November 26

CUPRA will arrive on November 26th, 2019 with the CUPRA Ateca, of which there will first be 300 units that will include a special gift for those first few lucky ones to pay MXN$724,200. The version arriving to our country will have a 2.0 TSI engine with 300 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque with a DSG 7 speed box, 4Drive traction and high performance CUPRA brakes, however the Brembo brakes for even greater braking strength will be optional. On a design level it'll have a dual exhaust and a whole bodywork kit to help with cooling down, aerodynamics and will also give it a sportier design.
Series production of the Tarek SUV will begin in 2021

Series production for the new SUV model being produced at Volkswagen Mexico's plant will start at the beginning of 2021, without there being a scheduled hiring of personnel, said the company's worker's union leader, Rey David García Avendaño. "The information we have is that the arrival of the new model will come at the end of 2020. We don't have an established timeline concerning testing or an anticipated series, but series production will begin in 2021," he commented.
Alejandro Delgado is named finance director of General Motors Mexico

Alejandro Delgado assumed the direction of General Motors Mexico's finance department, taking over Juan Carlos Garcia, who since the beginning of September is vice president of sales, service and marketing; Delgado will also form part of the company's Executive Board. Delgado has a Bachelor's Degree in International Relations by Slippery Rock University in Pennsylvania, United States and a PhD in Economy from Temple University, also in Pennsylvania.
