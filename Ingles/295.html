Toyota in Apaseo el Grande starts operations

Toyota of Mexico began the year on a good note, since it started operations in Apaseo el Grande, Guanajuato where the Tacoma is produced. This project began in 2016 with an investment of approximately US$700 million, and now employs around a thousand people. 100,000 units will be produced per year as soon as the production lines reach their full capacity, which is expected to happen soon.

New light vehicle internal market falls 7.6% in 2019

In 2019 there was a total of 1,317,727 new light vehicles sold in Mexico, which meant a fall of 7.6% when compared to 2018, informed the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). In releasing the report on sales to the public of the internal market according to the administrative records of the automotive industry of light vehicles in the month of December of 2019, INEGI pointed out that, in this month there were a total of 130,448 commercialized vehicles, which is to say, an 8.3% less than in the same month of last year.

AMDA proposes program to renew micro entrepreneurs' vehicular park

Before the new vehicle market, which has been falling for 31 months now, the industry proposed a plan that could reactivate it through the renewal of the vehicular park for micro entrepreneurs. The general director of the Mexican Automotive Dealer Association (AMDA), Guillermo Rosales, explained that the program would seek to give support in credit, as well as fiscal stimuli, with the decrease of the Tax on New Cars (ISAN), as well as increasing the deductible allowed in the Income Tax for Cars.

Automotive production and exports decrease

There were 3,750,841 vehicles assembled in 2019, 4.1% less than that registered in 2018, according to data from INEGI. As for exports, 3,333,586 vehicles assembled in Mexico were exported, 3.4% below what was reported in 2018, and this is the first reduction presented by the sector in this area since 2009. Only in December of 2019, 208,073 vehicles were assembled in Mexico, 12.6% below that reported in the same month of last year. While in the last month of last year, there was a total of 229,227 units sent abroad, 16.7% less than what was reported in the same period of 2018.

Suzuki presents sales growth of 18.3% in 2019

In contrast with an industry that is closing the year with a sales decrease of 7.6%, the Japanese manufacturer has managed to assert a new sales record in Mexico. In 2019, it managed to sell 31,211 new units which represents a growth of 18.3% in comparison to an already successful 2018, in which 26,380 cars were sold. With this growth Suzuki manages a 2.4% participation in the Mexican auto market.

Heavy vehicles sales fall in December and 2019

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) informed that, during December, the wholesale sale of heavy and commercial vehicles reached a total of 2,828 units, which compared to the 4,256 ones registered in December 2018, meant a fall of 33.6%. The retail sale of heavy and commercial vehicles reached 3,868 units which compared to the 3,783 ones commercialized during the same month of 2018, meant an increase of 2.2%. As for sales in 2019, ANPACT reported that wholesale sales reached 39,723 units, which meant a decrease of 3.8%.

Eduardo Solis steps down as president of AMIA

Eduardo Solis announced on Wednesday that he would step down from the executive presidency of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) starting January 16, 2020. He added in conference that he is retiring to begin a personal project. He also informed that Fausto Cuevas, who currently is general director of the association, will be in charge while the committee of AMIA decides who will permanently occupy the position, although there's no date yet for the decision.
