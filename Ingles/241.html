June 4th, 2019
BAIC opens a dealership in Guanajuato

The electric car company BAIC opened a dealership in Leon, Guanajuato, the first of its kind in the state. Eduardo Saenz, president and director of BAIC in Mexico, reported that the Chinese car manufacturing firm is the number one automotive brand in Beijing. In addition, he explained that, in the last two years, they have built 35 agencies in Mexico, with the expectation of closing 2019 with a total of 39, as they are targeting the municipalities of Silao, Guanajuato, Celaya, and Irapuato as well.
Spain-based Cepsa to open 150 gas stations in Mexico

The Spain-based oil company Cepsa is to operate within Mexico through the Red Energy brand, with the objective of opening more than 150 gas stations in the next two years and; subsequently, growing until it reaches a market share of 4% in the long term, the firm announced. The first Red Energy station will be located in the city of Tlalnepantla de Baz, State of Mexico, and the forecast is for the stations to progressively extend to other Mexican states.
Japanese companies are finding great opportunities in Mexico

Mexico is consolidating as a base of operations for Japanese companies of the automotive industry, which in line with the strategy of the new federal government, said companies seek diversification of their markets and are focusing on countries such as Brazil, where they could find a niche in their auto parts production. Kimihiko Inaba, general director in the country of the Foreign Trade Organization of Japan (Jetro), said that companies in his country have found great opportunities within Mexican territory. He noted that, "production in Mexico is very specialized; it has the high levels of competitiveness required by Japanese companies."
Increasing Queretaro's regional automotive content is a challenge

Given the regional content rules that the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) dictates, once it enters into force, Queretaro's automotive industry has the challenge of meeting its new requirements. The president of Queretaro's Automotive Cluster, Renato Villaseñor Mendoza, said that one of the industry's priorities is to increase the regional content integration so as to comply with the treaty's new rules, on the eve of the three congresses' ratification of the commercial agreement.
Volvo and MACK Trucks Mexico open the Volvo Group Learning Center in Toluca

With the aim of expanding its training rate for salesmen of vehicle parts, as well as increasing the knowledge of the service area personnel of the distributors of Volvo and MACK trucks operating in Mexico, the Volvo Group Learning Center (VGLC) in Toluca was put into operation. Luz Elena Jurado Soto, general director of Volvo and MACK Trucks Mexico, informed that the company invested MXN$15 million for the equipment and conditioning of the facility, thereby increasing its ability to develop staff skills by 300%.
An automotive cluster will invest US$15 million to prepare engineers in the development of electric vehicles

State of Mexico's Automotive Cluster will invest approximately US$15 million to build a facility that helps with the preparation of specialized engineers in the development of electric car components. Gunther Barajas, president of the cluster, said that the investment seeks to create knowledge and attract companies that produce high-value components for electric cars. In addition, the investment took into account the production line that Ford will operate next year so as to manufacture electric vehicles.
Mazda Mexico increases its sales by 8% during May

In May of this year, Mazda sold 4,642 vehicles in Mexico, representing an increase of 8% over the same month of last year. Said numbers were taken from the monthly sales report of Mazda North America, which also states that, from January to May 2019, the brand sold 25,129 vehicles in Mexico, which translates into an increase of 16% compared to the same period of the previous year. In the United States, Mazda sold 25,192 vehicles in the fifth month of 2019; thus, manifesting 16% less than in the same month of the previous year.
