KIA Seltos to arrive in Mexico by 2020

The South Korean automaker KIA presented its new Seltos SUV, which is to be sold within the Mexican market by the beginning of 2020. The model, which is slated to strengthen the company within the SUV segment, will be manufactured at the company's new plant in India, KIA's executives said during a press conference. Daniel Bengio, marketing manager of KIA in Mexico, explained that, for the time being, the price at which the product will be launched is still being analyzed.
GM Mexico presents its 2018's Social Responsibility Report

For the eighth year in a row, General Motors Mexico presented its Social Responsibility Report, which compiles the automaker's actions implemented throughout 2018 under the three pillars of its social responsibility strategy: environment, quality education, and community support. In addition, the report touches up on the GM Mexico's initiatives that focus on being a good corporate citizen, always seeking to adhere to its "Win with Integrity" code of ethics.
Audi Mexico remains as one of the most attractive companies to work for in the country

According to Universum 2019's ranking, Audi Mexico remains in the top positions as one of the most attractive companies to work for in the country. Moreover, Audi Mexico is considered as one of the companies that arouse the most interest among future engineers and computer scientists. The three main attributes that engineering and IT students associate with Audi the most were: prestige, competitive benefits, and international relocation. The survey was applied to more than 40,000 students from different degrees in 143 educational institutions across the country.
Automotive sales could keep falling throughout the second half of 2019

The sales of new vehicles in Mexico could decrease more during the second half of 2019 due to a lower consumer purchasing capacity, as there's a slowdown in the local economy, the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) informed. "The decline that began in June 2017 accentuated at the end of the first semester of 2019, and this condition is expected to persist throughout the second semester," AMDA's Deputy Director General Guillermo Rosales said in a statement to the media. "The stagnation of investment and the slowdown in job creation are part of the risk scenario that can further deteriorate the commercialization of vehicles," he added.
The USMCA will create a favorable business environment: Automotive Cluster of Queretaro

The automotive and auto parts sector in Queretaro has a positive projection of investment towards the near future, after the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) was ratified in the Mexican Congress, said Daniel Hernandez Camacho, general director of the Automotive Cluster of Queretaro (CAQ). "The sector is turning to see what can happen with the USMCA, and to see if the treaty gets ratified (by the United States and Canada). It will generate a lot of confidence towards the projections that already exist, and that will create a favorable business environment where you can continue to grow," Hernandez Camacho assured.
The USMCA's ratification represents a "complex journey"

The pending ratification of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) by the United States Congress is "complex journey", as the treaty could become a "hostage of the political environment" that is to be experienced in the following months. In an optimistic scenario, there's a 70% chance that the ratification of the agreement will be achieved between November and December of this year, so that the USMCA enters into force on January 1, 2020, Kenneth Smith, former chief negotiator of this commercial agreement, explained.
INFINITI announces organizational changes in its sales division

INFINITI, the Japanese premium vehicle brand, announced the appointment of Carlos Estebaranz as INFINITI's vice president of sales for the Mexican and Latin American markets, effective as of July 1. Estebaranz will be responsible for the sales of the brand, performance, and development of the INFINITI distributors within the markets of Mexico, Panama, Chile, Puerto Rico, and the Dominican Republic. In addition, he will report directly to Philipp Heldt, general director of INFINITI. Carlos joined the company in 2015 and has 13 years of experience in the automotive industry.
