Asian imported cars gain ground in the Mexican market

In 2019 there was a total of 1,317,727 new vehicles sold in Mexico, of which 38.2% correspond to units imported from Asia, which represents the highest percentage reported by the industry in its history, according to data from INEGI. India is the main Asian country exporting vehicles to Mexico, since in 2019 166,396 units reached Mexico from this country; it's followed by Japan with 135,539 and China with 84,664. On a lesser extent there's Thailand with 50,669; South Korea with 46,996 and Indonesia with 15,454 units.

Audi Mexico releases call for apprentice program

The German automaker opened a call for their apprentice program under the dual training model which is developed in collaboration with the Technological University of Puebla (UTP). The dual training program has a duration of three years and is based on the German system of dual education, which consists of 80% practice and 20% theory. The apprentices will be formed in the Specialization Center in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla with the possibility of being employed by the company once they complete their education.

MAN Truck & Bus Mexico and LoJack Mexico form strategic alliance

With the goal of offering advanced telematics and video surveillance services in their entire line of buses and trucks commercialized in the country, MAN Truck & Bus Mexico established a strategic alliance with LoJack Mexico. Said technology will be installed in the MAN trucks and buses assembly line and will be sold on the national market. The first of these will provide real time video surveillance through cameras in the cabin. The second one, focused on telematics, will deliver critical data about the driver's behavior, gas consumption, fault codes and other information on the vehicle's performance.

Virginia Olalde Lopez-Gavito joins ANPACT

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) informed that, Virginia Olalde Lopez-Gavito joined the association as director of Foreign Trade and Economic Studies. Virginia Olalde has a Bachelor's degree in International Business from Tecnologico de Monterrey and has a Master's degree in International Relations by the Instituto de Empresa in Madrid. Olalde has served as director for Canada in the Undersecretary of Foreign Trade of the Ministry of Economy.

The 2020 Tacoma arrives to national territory

The Toyota Tacoma has been among us since 2005. The 2020 model is receiving a slight update and recently began production in Toyota's new plant in Guanajuato. The main changes are a redesigned grill, some fascia changes, new 17 inch rims and a retouch in the taillights. Additionally, this model also includes a towing hook and harness of 7 pin. As for the interior, the main novelty is the introduction of an infotainment system with an 8 inch screen compatible with Android Auto and Apple CarPlay. The prices for the Tacoma are MXN$626,900 for the Sport 4x2 version, MXN$691,900 for the Sport 4x4 version and MXN$766,600 for the Special 4x4 Edition.

Daimler Trucks Mexico names Christian Jullian Basurto as marketing manager

Christian Jullian Basurto was designated as marketing manager for Daimler Trucks Mexico, position in which he is responsible for the development and implementation of marketing and product strategies, focused on satisfying and exceeding the clients' needs. Jullian studied Industrial Engineering and Systems in the Instituto Tecnológico de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey (ITESM) and also has a Master's in Business Administration from Anahuac University. He joined Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico in 2004, where he has led several strategies as director in after-sales, market development and product portfolio.

Toyota announced that it'll move production of the Tacoma to Mexico

Toyota Motor Corp. announced on Friday that it will move the production of the Tacoma from the United States to Mexico as part of a production adjustment in North America. The company has been making the Tacoma in its Baja California plant in Mexico since 2004, and last month, the Toyota plant in Guanajuato began assembling it too. Toyota will move the production of the Sequoia to Texas in 2022, where it will stop producing the Tacoma in 2021. Toyota said that its production capacity for the Tacoma in Mexico will be of about 266,000 units per year.
