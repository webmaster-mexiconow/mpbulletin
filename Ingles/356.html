Chihuahua's auto parts industry works at 60% of its capacity

Chihuahua is the only entity in which the auto parts industry is working at 60% of its installed capacity, while in the rest of the country this sector is working at 100% of its capacity. "We've struggled to meet client demand, both nationally and internationally, because our clients are working at a different rhythm (more accelerated)," said Pedro Chavira, president of Index Ciudad Juarez. Chavira also said that at the moment no contract has been lost and the situation hasn't caused any companies to leave the entity.

Alfa approves Nemak's split

Alfa's shareholders approved the Split from Nemak, a subsidiary operating in the automotive industry. In a statement sent to the Mexican Stock Exchange (BMV), the conglomerate confirmed the fact and said that after the approval there will be a new company called Controladora Nemak, which will be listed on the stock exchange. "Alfa will transfer the totality of its participation in the social capital of Nemak to Controladora Nemak, as well as other assets and capital," detailed the document. Which is why the split process is expected to be completed in the next 60 days.

Drop in tourism affects car rental companies in Mexico

The drop in the tourism sector caused by the COVID-19 pandemic in Mexico made car rental companies park' 75% of their fleet because of the lack of clients, estimated the president of the Mexican Association of Vehicle Leasing Companies (AMAVe), Mauricio Medina. In Mexico there's a vehicular park of 35,000 cars for daily rent, of which only about 8,750 units are active, which means about 26,250 vehicles are out of circulation due to the drop in demand, according to data and estimates from Rentcars and AMAVe.

Stant invests more than US$15 million in Guanajuato

Governor Diego Sinhue Rodriguez Vallejo, led the start ceremony for the expansion works of Stant's plant, where more than US$15 million will be invested and which will generate 500 direct jobs. Volkswagen of Mexico increases its workers salary by 5.46%

After three weeks of negotiations, Volkswagen of Mexico and the Independent Union of Automotive Industry Workers (Sitiavw) reached an agreement through which, the strike programed for Tuesday August 18 is void. Manuel Aburto, spokesperson for the union, announced that with said resolution, which is valid starting on Tuesday, the salary received by unionized workers in the production plant in Puebla, is increased by 5.46% from which 3.62% corresponds to wages and 1.84% to an increase in benefits.

Audi Mexico in San Jose Chiapa has produced 555,555 units of the Audi Q5

The Audi Q5 began production at San Jose Chiapa, Puebla in 2016. Since then, Mexico has imported the model to the world. Which is why, reaching a success such as the following: manufacturing 555,555 units of the Audi Q5, is worthy of celebration. We should also highlight that the celebration isn't only born out of meeting this number, but also because amid the fight against COVID-19 and the country's economic reactivation, keeping at work day to day, with the necessary health measures and going after the Mexican market is a double achievement for the brand.

ANPACT has a new Technical and Engineering Director

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), informed that Miguel Ogazón Del Abrego, was incorporated into the association as Technical and Engineering Director. He is an industrial engineer and has a master's in business administration from ITESM. Miguel Ogazón has more than 16 years of experience working in operative areas of the insurance, logistic, institutional relations and transport normativity sectors. With the incorporation of the new Technical and Engineering Director, ANPACT seeks to foster a normative environment that would boost a greater development of the heavy vehicles industry.
