Marcopolo Mexico implements collective vacations due to COVID-19

Bus and body maker Marcopolo Mexico informed that, due to the global situation experienced because of COVID-19, it has taken the decision to suspend production during two weeks. "Marcopolo Mexico is strengthening its commitment to respect and value the people that are already our inspiration for all we do, maintaining the maximum security to take care of their health and integrity, for this reason, we have made the decision to carry out collective vacations for a period of two weeks, from March 30 to April 13," said Marcopolo.

Toyota will resume operations on April 20

Due to COVID-19 and to lower vehicle demand, Toyota will extend the suspension of its operations in all its automobile and component manufacturing in North America, including Mexico, Canada and the United States. The manufacturing plants will remain closed until April 17, restarting operations on April 20, 2020. Toyota said that Parts Distribution Centers and logistic vehicle centers will continue to operate to satisfy clients' needs.

Concamin asks to postpone the USMCA's entry into force

It will be impossible for the Mexican automotive industry to meet the specifications dictated by the new United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA), which is expected to go into force on June 1st of this year, assured the Confederation of Industrial Chambers (Concamin). "This request stems from the fact that to date, 67 days before June 1st, the industry doesn't yet know the official text of the uniform regulations, through which the methodology that will have to be applied to carry out the calculations of regional content value is established in detail, and thus meet the new USMCA rules," declared the organism.

Daniel Echeverri is the new CFO of Renault Mexico

Daniel Echeverri is joining Renault Mexico as CFO, with the task of conducting the financial activities of the French brand in the country. The new executive will report to Magdalena Lopez, president and general director of the commercialization of the vehicles in Mexico. He joined Renault in 2015 as performance manager and executive secretary. He also worked, from 2018 to January 2020, as executive secretary of the French company for the Americas.

The Yukon and Yukon XL arrive to Mexico

GMC confirmed the arrival to Mexico of the fifth generation of the Yukon, the brand's premium SUV that is highlighted for its unique capacities, bigger proportions, leading technology in its segment and a robust design. GMC also announced that, for the first time in Mexico, it will offer the Yukon XL, which gives more space for passengers and cargo. Both will be sold in the Denali version and will be available during the second semester of this year. Both models have a V8 6.2 liter engine, coupled to a 10 speed transmission, which gives 420 hp and 460 lb-ft of torque.

Ford in Hermosillo will restart operations on April 6

While Ford manufactures necessary medical equipment for the crisis, it's also set the objective of restarting the production in certain plants of North America starting on April 6, so that key plants go back online. The first Ford plant that plans to restart automobile production is the assembly plant in Hermosillo, Sonora on April 6 with only one turn.

BMW and Honda are leaders in Sales Satisfaction in Mexico 2020: J.D. Power

Low consumer trust increases the pressure on distributors to offer a more satisfactory purchase experience, according to results from J.D. Power's Sales Satisfaction Index Study in Mexico 2020. BMW obtained the highest score for luxury brands with a satisfaction index of 901 out of 1,000 points, an increase of 20 points when compared to their 2019 result. Honda obtained the highest score amongst volume brands with an index of 891 points, 42 points above their 2019 score.
