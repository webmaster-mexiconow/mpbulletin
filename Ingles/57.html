Bulletin #57
5 June, 2017
Auto sales fall in the U.S. market during May for the fifth straight month

Automakers posted in May for the fifth straight month a sales decline compared to the same month a year ago, bolstering expectations for the first annual sales decline since 2009. Auto sales fell 0.5% during May in the U.S. market to 1.52 million, despite an extra selling day compared to a year ago, according to Autodata Corp. Year-to-date sales are down 2% through the first five months of the year, prompting analysts to lower their forecasts that now appear will fall short of last year's 17.47 million units record. Passenger cars represented just 38.4% of sales, down from 42.2% in May 2016. Truck sales including SUVs, rose 6% to represent 61.6% of sales.
Ford outsells GM in U.S. market during May

Ford Motor edged its archrival General Motors in May U.S. sales in a surprise turn of events as the Blue Oval automaker seeks to regain its footing following the ouster of its CEO. Ford outsold cross-town rival GM by nearly 4,000 units in May, making it temporarily the largest automaker in the U.S. market. It was the first time Ford outsold GM since March 2016. Ford sales chief Mark LaNeve acknowledged that when Ford tops GM, it's typically because the company sold more vehicles to fleet customers, which include rental car companies. "It feels good for about 10 seconds and then we move on," he said on a conference call.
Auto sales increased 1.1% during May in Mexican market

Mexican auto dealers sold 122,916 vehicles during May, a 1.1% increase compared to the same month of 2016. Sales for the first five months of 2017 are up 4.8% compared to the same period a year before, totaling 615,641 units, according to AMDA. Nissan, the best-selling automaker in the country, reported 29,251 units sold, a 2.1% increase, which help to retain a 24.8% market share. GM sold 20,638 vehicles, down 14.9% from the previous year, ending with a 16.6% share of the market. Volkswagen sold 19,080 units including its Audi, Seat and Porsche brands, VW alone sold 15,465 vehicles, down 7.8% while keeping a 15.5% share of the market.
Canadian auto dealers reach record-breaking sales during May

In Canada auto sales hit a record high in May, with General Motors and Ford reporting double-digit increases, fueled by demand for crossovers and light trucks. Automakers sold 216,861 vehicles in May, a number which "smashed all previous monthly records," and was up 11% compared with the same month a year earlier, Canadian auto analyst Dennis DesRosiers wrote in a note. Year-to-date auto sales in Canada reached 835,582 units representing a 4.7% increase over the previous year, according to DesRosiers.
Sales of hybrid, electric vehicles grow at three-digit pace in Mexico

The Mexican Automotive Industry Association reported that 755 hybrid or electric vehicles were sold by auto dealers across the country during February, up 540% compared to the second month of 2016. Out of total, just 31 cars were full electric while the resting 724 were hybrids. During the first two months of 2017 Mexican auto dealers sold 1,348 eco-friendly units, a 422.5% increase compared to the 258 vehicles sold during the same two-months period of 2016. Just 60 vehicles sold were full electric and the other 1,288 units were hybrid models. Mexico City remains the biggest low-emission vehicles market as 41.3% of sales take place at the country's capital.
Brose begins recruitment for its fourth plant in Mexico

Germany-based automotive supplier Brose announced it will start the recruitment process for its third production site in Queretaro scheduled to start operations in August 2018. The company plans to fill 150 positions for engineers and technicians during the first stage of the process, which will be added to 140 positions already filled. Overall the company expects to create 900 jobs in this operation, which will be the company's biggest in Mexico. Brose plans to invest US$ 190 million to manufacture adjustment systems for front and rear seats. For that end, the company plans to gather metal stamping, welding, painting and electronic assembly processes under the same roof.
GM to cut around 300 jobs at transmission plant in Michigan

General Motors plans to cut more jobs, this time at a Warren, Michigan plant. The automaker said the cuts - which will eliminate one of two shifts that make transmissions - will happen late June, and will help it "maintain more stable production." The company wouldn't say how many jobs would be affected, but CNNMoney reported the cuts could affect about 300 positions. This is the fifth time GM has eliminated a work shift at a US plant since last November, cutting a total of 5,000 jobs. Shifts have been eliminated at the Lordstown, Ohio plant, the Detroit Hamtramck plant, and two plants in Lansing, Michigan.
Automakers sort out supply chain hurdles at Mexico-US border

Cargo operations at the Laredo port of entry will be limited for at least one more week as U.S. and Mexican authorities work to repair damage caused by a devastating storm May 21 that forced auto suppliers and other companies to divert shipments to alternate border crossings. U.S. and Mexican authorities initially closed the World Trade Bridge connecting Nuevo Laredo and Laredo, Texas - a major conduit for auto parts moving back and forth between suppliers and assembly plants on both sides of the border. VW, GM and Ford said that the situation has not affected production but that logistics operations are under pressure to adjust.
AK Steel joins Regal Beloit to develop steels for hybrid, electric vehicles

AK Steel announced that it has accepted an award of up to US$ 1.8 million from the U.S. Department of Energy to develop the next generation of advanced, non-oriented electrical steels (NOES) for motors used in a wide variety of industrial and automotive applications, including hybrid-electric vehicles. The three-year project will be conducted in collaboration with the DOE and Regal Beloit Corporation, the manufacturer of electric motors and motion control products. Both companies own production facilities in Mexico.
Musk to Trump: You quit Paris, so I quit you

Elon Musk, the CEO of Tesla and SpaceX, has quit two of President Trump's business advisory councils after the president announced he will pull the U.S. out of the historic Paris climate agreement. "Am departing presidential councils. Climate change is real. Leaving Paris is not good for America or the world," Musk said Thursday on Twitter, shortly after the president announced from the Rose Garden that he would begin the process of leaving the accord. Musk had vowed to step down from the advisory councils he sits on if the president pulled out of the pact.
