INA sees the need to homologate assembling activities in Mexico with the United States

The Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA) lamented that the Mexican government is keeping the automotive industry shutdown in light of the need to homologate assembling activities with the United States. Oscar Albin, president of INA, warned that, although the industry currently sent workers home with their salary, there's not enough money to keep this going if the current situation continues and that layoffs are not off the table. That's why he asked for the automotive industry to be considered as essential and to be homologated to the needs of the United States.

AMLO is open to restarting activities in the automotive sector

President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador showed willingness to reach an agreement with the United States to restart operations in complementary industrial sectors for both economies, such as the automotive and agricultural, which have been stopped as preventive measures against the spread of the COVID-19. "In due course there's going to be an agreement, when they open, and we have committed, especially with national businessmen, to analyze these openings to little by little return to productive normalcy in the border," said the president in his daily press conference.

The country's automotive sector proposes a rescue plan to the Senate

Manufacturers of vehicles, buses and auto parts proposed to the Senate a rescue plan due to the drop in sales, exacerbated by the national health emergency. In the document they ask for immediate and long-term actions, like the substitution of units with 10 years of antiquity to reactivate production and sales, protection to temporal employment, deductibility of MXN$350,000 for new vehicles, temporary exemption on the payment of the ISAN (tax on new vehicles), and delaying the USMCA's entry into force to 2021, among others.

Volkswagen of Mexico works toward clean production

Volkswagen has as a goal to reduce global carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions by contributing to the fight against climate change and aligning itself with the Paris Agreement, which seeks to accelerate and intensify the actions and necessary investments for a sustainable future. Currently, 70% of the energy used by the German brand in its Guanajuato and Puebla plants is fed through the La Bufa wind park, located in Zacatecas, with which they have been able to reduce emissions in about 215,000 tons of CO2 from 2012 to 2019.

Automotive sector could begin to register positive growth in the second half of 2021

For Janneth Quiroz, subdirector of Economic Analysis of Grupo Financiero Monex, "the automotive sector has presented a contraction for several months, with which the downward trend could be observed even before the COVID-19 pandemic. We expect the sector will begin exhibiting signs of recovery in the next year, which could be accelerated toward 2022, considering that there's a global effort to try and accelerate the global economic recuperation, with which there's been the announcement of extraordinary stimulus measures, we hope the sector could begin to register positive growth rates toward the second half of 2021," she said.

Pick-up sales keep growing in the first quarter of 2020

During 2019 there were 193,332 pick-up type vehicles sold in Mexico, an increase of 4.82% when compared to last year, when there were 184,439 sold units, according to numbers from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This trend continues in 2020 since based off numbers provided by INEGI, by the end of this year's first quarter there were 44,815 pick-up type vehicles sold vehicles, an increase of 0.8% when compared to the same period of last year. The light trucks segment which includes mostly pick-ups, is the only segment that obtained a sales increase during the first quarter.

Honda extends the halt of production in its North America plants

While the unprecedented economic impact caused by the COVID-19 pandemic continues, Honda keeps evaluating the commercial conditions and making temporal adjustments to its production and commercial operations in North America. Honda is extending the halt of production for its vehicle, engine and transmission plants in North America by one week until May 8th. Honda begin the suspension in the production of vehicles in North America on March 23. The Japanese automaker has two production centers in Mexico located in Celaya, Guanajuato and El Salto, Jalisco.
