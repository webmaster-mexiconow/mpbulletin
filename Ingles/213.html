Next generation of Mexico-made HD Ram trucks debut at Detroit Auto Show

The all-new 2019 RAM Heavy Duty pickup was unveiled Monday at the North American International Auto Show (NAIAS) in Detroit, the first of its kind to reach 1,000 lb.-ft. of torque, thanks to an I-6 6.7-liter Cummins High Output Turbo Diesel engine, which allows a towing capacity of 35,100 lbs and a payload capacity of 7,680 lbs. However, the standard engine in the new truck will be a 6.4-liter Hemi V8, mated to an eight-speed automatic, which can deliver 410 horsepower and 429 lb.-ft. of torque. Despite previous announcements that production of HD Rams would be moved from Coahuila to Michigan, the new line-up is still built at the same assembly plant in Saltillo, that makes the outgoing version. Layoffs begin at three Nissan plants, the automaker confirms

The layoffs that Nissan planned at three assembly plants in Aguascalientes and Cuernavaca has already started with a first group of employees that was dismissed last week, the Japanese automaker confirmed in a statement. This process, which will affect 1,000 workers in total, is expected to continue throughout the first quarter of 2019. Last December the company announced this plan to readjust production volumes, in response to new market conditions, mainly in Mexico and the United States. The "workforce adjustment" includes the two plants in Aguascalientes (A1 and A2), as well as the Cuernavaca facility (CIVAC), but excludes the COMPAS facility in Aguascalientes, where the Infiniti QX50 is built. More details here: bit.ly/2CmvOZ1
The Mercedes-Benz A-Class will be the brand's most affordable sedan in the US

Mercedes-Benz USA announced pricing for the all-new 2019 A-Class sedan, arriving to U.S. dealerships in the coming weeks, which will start at just US$32,500 for the front-wheel-drive A 220 version, becoming the brand's most affordable model in that market. Meanwhile, the all-wheel-drive A 220 4MATIC will start at US$34,500. In comparison, the CLA 250, which is the next sedan in the catalog starts at US$33,100, and an additional US$995 destination fee is charged at delivery in every model. The 2019 Mercedes-Benz A-Class sedan for the U.S. market is sourced from the Cooperation Manufacturing Plant Aguascalientes, a car manufacturing site jointly developed by Daimler and the Renault-Nissan Alliance. More details here: bit.ly/2T2InjD
Production of Chevy Cruze at Ramos Arizpe ended last month, GM confirms

General Motors (GM) public relations staff recently confirmed to local media in the state of Coahuila that production of the Chevrolet Cruze at the Ramos Arizpe plant ended in December. The technical and human resources destined to the production of the Cruze are now focused in the new Chevrolet Blazer, hence no layoffs were made, the company announced. Last November GM announced a restructuring plan that involves the end of production of several sedan models in North America, which would involve the closure of several plants. Still in December GM produced 1,536 units at Ramos Arizpe, of which 1,315 vehicles were exported to the United States and 256 to Canada, according to official figures. More details here: bit.ly/2HdA5DS
Volkswagen unveils US$800-million plan to build plant for electric vehicles in Tennessee

Volkswagen confirmed that it will be investing US$800 million in its Chattanooga, Tennessee assembly plant to prepare the location for production of new electric cars using the MEB platform. Back in September (Newsletter 184) it was reported that VW was scouting locations to build the electric car factory and that at one point the assembly plant in Puebla was considered. But now the company has confirmed that it will expand its current location in Chattanooga. The first electric vehicle to be produced in Chattanooga will be an all-electric crossover-based I.D. CROZZ Concept in 2022, and then the I.D. BUZZ electric microbus will follow later.
French EMS provider ALL Circuits opens new plant in Zapopan, Jalisco

French electronic manufacturing services (EMS) provider ALL Circuits opened its new production facility in Zapopan, Jalisco, to serve the North American automotive industry. The 12,000-sq.-mt. facility, located at the Guadalajara Technology Park, currently employs around 40 people, but its total workforce will increase to 400 employees once fully operational. Investment figures were not disclosed. The new plant features the same equipment, software, and consumables set as other company facilities in France and Tunisia. ALL Circuits manufactures circuit boards for automakers such as Audi, BMW, Alfa Romeo, Cadillac, Jaguar, Land Rover and McLaren, but it also serves other Tier 1 automotive suppliers like Continental. More details here: bit.ly/2QQ7W5o
Paint-finishing systems developer Therma-Tron-X opens new plant in Nuevo León

Therma-Tron-X (TTX), a Wisconsin-based designer and manufacturer of industrial finishing systems and industrial water-treatment equipment, announced it has chosen the Mexican market to open its first international subsidiary, TTX Mexico. The company has selected the municipality of San Pedro Garza Garcia, Nuevo Leon, for its Mexico headquarters. "The importance of the Mexican market and a commitment to TTX's existing Mexican customers drove the decision to invest in a permanent operation within Mexico," said the company in a statement. TTX is a custom manufacturer of powder coating systems, electrodeposition systems, liquid paint coating systems and reverse osmosis as well as wastewater treatment equipment. Cummins invests US$7 million in San Luis Potosí for human capital development

Cummins announced that it has allocated US$7 million to improve the infrastructure of its manufacturing and development complex in San Luis Potosí. The Ohio-based manufacturer of engines, filtration, and power generation products said this investment was focused not on expanding its production capacity but on improving the conditions of around 4,000 employees that the company has in that location. The improvements involved a 4,000 sq.-mt. expansion for training centers and areas that encourage communal living among employees. At the end of 2018, company officials announced that Cummins has plans to expand in Mexico, but it was considered important to first provide proper training to its collaborators. More details here: bit.ly/2FzoCx1
KCS to build US$99-million cargo processing facility on US-Mexico border

Kansas City Southern Railway (KCS) plans to develop a US$99-million cargo processing facility on the U.S.-Mexico border this month, aimed to improve traffic between southern Texas and the state of Tamaulipas in northern Mexico. Such infrastructure revamp comes along with procedural changes to smooth cross-border activity. The company estimates it can increase traffic from 24 to 32 trains crossing every 24 hours once the facility is completed. KCS is the main rail freight operator in the US offering services to Mexico, serving 12 ports on the Gulf of Mexico and one on the Mexican Pacific coast. The company handles cars and auto parts, steel, grain and oil-derivative products. More details here: bit.ly/2Da6eIE
Ford-Volkswagen alliance will start by building trucks and vans

Ford and the Volkswagen Group confirmed at the Detroit Auto Show on Tuesday they're combining forces in a global alliance, which will start by making pickup trucks together. The companies will also build commercial vans together, and are exploring collaborations on autonomous and electric vehicles as well as new mobility services. As result of the deal, VW will get access to Ford's midsize Ranger pickup truck platform, in which the next-generation VW Amarok would be based on. Both companies emphasized vehicles built on shared platforms would maintain their own, separate brand identities, including driving dynamics and user interface, but they would use the powertrains native to the platform.
