Volkswagen of Mexico and Sitiavw will negotiate collective contract

The Independent Union of Automotive Industry Workers of Volkswagen of Mexico (Sitiavw) presented the strike summons to the Federal Conciliation and Arbitration Council, with which it begins the legal process for the contractual revision with the company. The secretary of this union, Jose Juan Hernandez, said that in this occasion they will negotiate the Collective Work Contract and that it's premature to speak of the fact that there's no conditions yet for the 7,695 union workers to receive a better salary and benefits. The union asked for a 12% increase in salaries from the German auto maker.

Adval Tech will expand its Queretaro plant

Swiss auto parts manufacturer Adval Tech, dedicated to the molding of metal and plastic components for the automotive sector, began the expansion project of its plant in El Marques, Queretaro, for July 2021. Said expansion is born in an important moment for the automotive industry and for the state, since both are on the road to recovery after the COVID-19 pandemic and with the USMCA's entry into force, working together will be the key to success.

Renault Mexico manages to obtain a participation of 2.56% during the year's first semester

Despite the fact that new vehicle demand in the country contracted by 31.9% during 2020's first semester in comparison with the amount of billed vehicles in the 2019 January-June period, Renault Mexico managed a participation of 2.56% during this period, which meant growth when compared to the same period of last year. Magdalena Lopez Estrada, president and general director of Renault Mexico assured that the company is the second in the sector with less loss of presence in the market since the start of 2020.

Uber expands audio recording in Mexico City and Guadalajara

In line with its commitment to innovation for safe mobility, Uber said that it's expanding the audio recording tool in Mexico City and Guadalajara. The tool, which began its implementation in the country on February of last year, is already available in 38 cities within the country and allows both driving partners and users to activate it during trips with the app, in order to file a report to the Uber support team if something makes them feel uncomfortable during the ride.

72,897 vehicles were sold during July

The Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) and the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) said that in July there were 72,897 new sold vehicles in the country, number which is 10,060 units bigger than those sold in June of this year; 30,869 bigger than those sold in May and 37,994 bigger than those sold in April. According to the Administrative Register of the Light Vehicle Automotive Industry, issued by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI), the July number is 31.3% below the sales of the same month of 2019, when 106,099 units were sold.

Kia Motors Mexico obtains 7.4% accumulated market participation

Kia Motors Mexico depicts effective results to finalize the year's seventh month, since the work carried out by the assembler in Mexico has multiplied to maintain its position in the industry. The South Korean company closed July with 5,606 delivered units in national territory, number which confirms its position in 5th place with 7.4% accumulated participation in the market. Among the most sold vehicles, Kia Rio is the leader with 2,212 sold units. This vehicle, proudly Mexican is the favorite both in sales and the brand's micro site.
