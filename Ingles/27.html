16 February, 2017
Soon-to-be-built in Hermosillo, redesigned Ford Focus is tested in Europe

The fourth-generation Ford Focus hatchback is being tested in Europe. The camouflaged Focus appears to have grown in length and width, and could feature a larger back-end. The Focus is currently built at the Ford Michigan Assembly Plant in Wayne, Mich., but it's production will shift to Hermosillo, Sonora, in 2018.
Thyssenkrupp's future Mexico investments in doubt

German industrial group Thyssenkrupp is watching the new administration of U.S. President Donald Trump carefully to decide whether to make further investments in two automotive components plants in Mexico, CEO Heinrich Hiesinger revealed. Hiesinger said current investments in the Mexican plants were fully covered by orders from carmakers but questions now arose over future investments.
Cruze Diesel Sedan sets 52-mpg benchmark

The 2017 Cruze Diesel Sedan reached up to an EPA-estimated highway mileage of 52 mpg - the highest highway fuel economy of any non-hybrid/non-EV in America. Based upon the EPA highway estimate, Cruze Diesel with the six-speed manual transmission has an estimated range of up to 702 highway miles on one tank of diesel fuel. The 2017 Cruze Diesel Sedan features a new Ecotec 1.6-liter inline four-cylinder turbo-diesel engine offering an SAE-certified 137 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque.
Worldwide sales of Toyota hybrids surpass 10 million units

Toyota Motor Corporation announced cumulative global hybrid vehicle (HV) sales of 10.05 million units as of January 31, surpassing the 10-million-unit mark. The milestone was achieved just nine months after total sales reached 9 million units at the end of April 2016. Toyota launched the Coaster Hybrid EV in August 1997 and the Prius, the world's first mass-produced hybrid passenger vehicle, in December of the same year.
U.S. import tax would hit Jaguar Land Rover hardest, Ford least

Research firm Baum & Associates estimates most automakers would need to raise vehicle prices by thousands of dollars to recoup higher costs incurred by a proposed border-adjusted tax. Ford, would need to set the smallest price hike among major automakers, at about US$ 282 per vehicle, followed by General Motors at US$ 995. VW would have to rise by about US$ 6,800 on average and Jaguar Land Rover would have to make an adjustment of more than US$ 17,000 per vehicle, according to the report.
Japanese auto parts manufacturer Nisshinbo nixes plan for Mexican plant

Nisshinbo Holdings has dropped Mexico as a site for its new plant for automotive brake materials in light of U.S. President Donald Trump's push to renegotiate NAFTA. The company had planned to select the new plant site by the end of 2017, and Mexico was at the top of the list. "With regard to Mexico, we are back on the drawing board," Executive Managing Officer Takayoshi Okugawa said.
American Axle income falls 25% hit by slower demand

American Axle & Manufacturing Holdings Inc. reported lower net income in the fourth quarter of 2016 amid lower parts demand. The Detroit-based supplier reported net income of US$ 46.9 million, down 25% compared with the same period in 2015. The company operates three manufacturing sites in Guanajuato.
Takata sets aside US$ 864 million as part of settlement

Japan's Takata Corp announced it will set aside 96.93 billion yen (US$ 864.4 million) as part of a US$ 1 billion settlement with the United States Justice Department over the world's largest ever auto recall. The company said it will probably report a third consecutive annual loss as part of said measure. The auto supplier also faces class-action lawsuits as well as litigation filed by the states of Hawaii and New Mexico.
Honda starts 2017 CR-V production in Indiana

The 2017 Honda CR-V entered production in Greensburg, Indiana, becoming the first SUV ever built at that facility. In March 2016, Honda Manufacturing of Indiana (HMIN) announced a US$ 52 million investment to support the production of the CR-V. This transitioned CR-V production from their plant in Guadalajara, Mexico to Greensburg and created 100 jobs.
Ford invests in Argo AI, a new artificial intelligence company

Ford Motor Company announced a US$ 1 billion investment during the next five years in Argo AI, an artificial intelligence company, to develop a virtual driver system for the automaker's autonomous vehicle coming in 2021 - and for potential license to other companies. Founded by former Google and Uber leaders, Argo AI is bringing together some of the most experienced roboticists and engineers working in autonomy from inside and outside of Ford.
