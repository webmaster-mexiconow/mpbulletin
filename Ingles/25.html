9 February, 2017
Mexico's auto production grows 4.1% during January, exports fall 0.7%

2016. Meanwhile exports saw a slight drop of 0.7% in January, in part due to lower sales in the U.S., said the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA). AMIA numbers showed that 211,682 cars were exported in January 2017, down from 213,244 in the same period of 2016.
Here are the most popular Mexico-built vehicles in US during 2016

According to WardsAuto, the best selling Mexico-built vehicles in the U.S. during 2016 are: Ford Fusion (Hermosillo) 257,865 units; Ram (Saltillo) 246,000; Chevrolet Silverado (Silao) 222,000; Nissan Sentra (Aguascalientes) 214,709; Nissan Versa (Aguascalientes) 132,214; VW Jetta (Puebla) 121,107; Dodge Journey (Toluca) 106,759; GMC Sierra (Silao) 99,000; Toyota Tacoma (Tijuana) 86,000; Ford Fiesta (Cuautitlan) 48,807.
Slim, Bimbo bakery to build electric vehicle

A made-in-Mexico electric vehicle by Giant Motors -part of Carlos Slim's financial conglomerate Inbursa- could hit the roads in a year's time. The vehicle will be developed through a collaboration with Moldex, a subsidiary of the world's largest bakery, Bimbo, and the Monterrey Institute of Technology (ITESM). Giant Motors expects to have a working prototype ready late this year, and could launch it commercially next year.
GM's unionized U.S. workers will get record bonus checks

General Motors' reported more than US$ 12 billion in pre-tax profits in North America, which translates into record profit-sharing payments of up to US$ 12,000 for union-represented workers. About 20% of GM's North American production is in Mexico, higher than both its Detroit rivals. Plus, 42% of GM's Silverado and Sierra pickup trucks, which are two of the company's highest-revenue vehicles, are made at a plant in Silao, Mexico.
Ford unveils long-awaited redesigned Expedition SUV

Ford unveiled an all new, aluminum-bodied version of its big Expedition SUV that is both bigger and lighter than the aging version that it replaces. Built in Louisville, Kentucky, the unit promises better fuel economy and new features when it goes on sale this fall. The Expedition grows 4.0 inches longer on a 3.0-in. longer wheelbase. The bigger Expedition Max –previously called the Expedition EL– grows one inch. Its wheelbase is unchanged.
FCA Mexico kicks off 2017 with 15 percent sales gain

FCA Mexico reported sales of 9,612 units during last month, up 15% versus same month of 2016 and best January since 2009. The Dodge brand performed the best with 3,726 units sold, followed by Ram (2,110), Mitsubishi (1,610), Fiat (1,108), Jeep (906), Chrysler (114) and Alfa Romeo which delivered 38 vehicles.
Metal stamping supplier opens plant in Nuevo Leon

Michigan-based metal stamping supplier Trans-Matic started operations in recent days at a US$ 12 million plant in Santa Catarina, Nuevo Leon, creating 55 full-time jobs. The new 55,000 square feet facility located at the Finsa Industrial Park houses 23 stamping presses. Some of its customers are Bosch, Denso, Delphi, Ford, Valeo and Visteon.
Japan-based Vuteq announces US$ 30 million expansion in Ramos Arizpe

Just 10 months after it started operations, Japan-based Vuteq Industries announced the expansion of its plant in Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila, with an US$30 million investment and the creation of 195 jobs. Vuteq is a door and interior provider for General Motors. A second production facility is located in San Luis Potosi.
Mercedes-Benz starts year with double-digit growth in sales

Mercedes-Benz started the new year with 18.3% growth in sales. Last month, 178,467 vehicles with the three-pointed star were delivered to customers around the globe, more than ever before in a January. In the NAFTA region, the company reported a 6.4% increase with 30,001 automobiles delivered, 25,527 of them in the U.S. and the rest in Canada and Mexico.
Mexico-built Honda's HR-V, Fit receive Best Cars for the Money Award

Three 2017 Honda models, the CR-V, HR-V and Fit, were named as 2017 Best Cars for the Money by U.S. News & World Report. Two of those units, the HR-V and Fit, are made in Mexico. The HR-V retained its top position in the Subcompact SUV category, that it also won last year, and the Fit took first place in the Subcompact Car segment for the third year in a row. The ranking values a combination of a vehicle's five-year total cost of ownership and the average price paid for the vehicle.
