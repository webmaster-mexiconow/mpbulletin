Uber releases Uber Pass in Mexico

Uber released Uber Pass, a unique monthly subscription which will allow platform users in Mexico to access multiple benefits, have a better control of their expenses and obtain services with a greater cost-benefit. "In Uber we know that memberships play a key role in the activities we carry out daily, since they give us an additional way to plan and administer our expenses, and especially when it's about our mobility and food," explained Eduardo Donnelly, general director of Uber Eats in Latin America.

He began his career studying the global automotive industry at R.L.Polk before joining the University of Michigan Transportation Research Institute (UMTRI), from which he retired in 2018. GM of Mexico announces new Sales, Service and Marketing vice president

General Motors announced the naming of Hector Villareal as new Vice president of Sales, Service and Marketing of Mexico, Central America and the Caribbean, starting on October 1st. He will be responsible of directing the commercial strategy of the Chevrolet, Buick, GMC and Cadillac brands; as well as the relation with the distributor network in Mexico and the 21 countries in Central America and the Caribbean. Hector has more than 30 years of experience, currently he is the President of General Motors in Southeast Asia, based in Thailand and will join the Executive Committee of GM of Mexico led by Francisco Garza.

Shacman will install a new plant in Hidalgo

Chinese truck manufacturer Shacman chose Mexico to install a new plant, taking care of the local market starting this year, and on the mid-term exporting to other Latin American countries and even to the United States, its general director in the country, Carlos Pardo, said to Reuters. Shacman expects to begin assembling in November of this year in the state of Hidalgo with an initial annual capacity of 1,000 units and 30 operatives. During the first five years, the brand hopes to manufacture 5,000 trucks which will be destined to the national market, but the mid-term goal is to export to other countries in Latin America.

Tarek Mashhour will assume Audi Mexico's executive presidency

Starting on November 1st, Tarek Masshour will assume Audi Mexico's executive presidency. Andreas Lehe, actual president, will leave the charge to become Chief of Strategic Planning at Audi AG. Mashhour has an ample trajectory within Audi AG, holding posts like Corporate Chief of Production and Chief Production Planning at Audi's plant in Ingolstadt, Germany. He was in charge of the planning in the production of Audi's plants in Brussels, Belgium and Györ, Hungary, as well as the planning and implementation of Audi's new plant in San Jose Chiapa.

General Motors and Honda announce agreement in North America

General Motors and Honda announced they have signed a non-binding memorandum of understanding following extensive preliminary discussions toward establishing a North American automotive alliance. The scope of the proposed alliance includes a range of vehicles to be sold under each company's distinct brands, as well as cooperation in purchasing, research and development, and connected services. Under the proposed alliance, Honda and GM would collaborate on a variety of segments in North America, intending to share common vehicle platforms, including both electrified and internal combustion propulsion systems that align with the vehicle platforms.

Vehicle exports and production continue to decline in August

In August 293,463 vehicles were produced in Mexico, 13.1% less to that registered last year, this being the lowest volume assembled in the country for a similar period in five years, informed the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This is contrasted with that reported in July; last month production managed to increase production by 0.7% in an annual rate. In the year's eight month 257,610 vehicles were sent abroad, 8.6% less than to that registered in the same month of 2019, which represents the most reduced volume since 2015.

Volkswagen presents Electric Mobility Program in Guanajuato

Volkswagen presented its Electric Mobility Program, which contemplates applying pilot testing of electric units for cargo and transport of personnel in Leon, Guanajuato. Mexico will be the first country outside of Europe to have it. In recent years, Volkswagen has committed to the application of new technologies that allow the transition from combustion engines to electric mobility. On top of applying it to its light vehicles, VW is taking electrification to its commercial vehicles, buses and trucks and Mexico will be the first country outside of Europe to have one of these fleets.
