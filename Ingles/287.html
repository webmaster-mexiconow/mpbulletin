GM begins assembling the Chevrolet Onix in San Luis Potosi

After hitting a speed bump-the worker's strike in the United States that caused partial stops in Mexico-General Motors begins a new race with the manufacturing of the subcompact Chevrolet Onix in its San Luis Potosi plant, that seeks to position itself like one of Mexico's favorites, announced Francisco Garza, president and general director of the automotive brand. Onix will be added to the production lines of the company in San Luis Potosi, where the SUVs Equinox, Terrain and Trax are made.

Peugeot Mexico manages to sell 1,074 units in November

Peugeot Mexico reported that it broke through the barrier of 1,000 units in November of this year, by selling 1,074 units which represents a growth of 20%. As for the accumulated sales volume by the end of November sales reached 9,978 units, thus showing a growth of 13% when compared to last year. Despite the fact that, in 2019, the market has suffered from a constant fall, Peugeot Mexico is looking at a growth of 13%.

The new Mercedes-Benz GLB reaches Mexico

Mercedes-Benz began sales in Mexico of the totally new GLB, the brand's first compact to have three rows of seats. This vehicle will be proudly produced in Mexico; and among its features there's the big interior space, that allows for seven passengers thanks to its optional third row, made up by two individual moving seats (Easy-Entry) that allow for people of up to 1.68 meters to ride comfortably. Mercedes-Benz GLB can be acquired through authorized distributors and its pricing begins at MXN$859,900.

Volkswagen will close 2019 with a 13% growth in the conversion commercial vehicles sector

In the midst of an automotive industry that has exhibited negative results for 29 months, the conversion commercial vehicles (delivery) sector led by Volkswagen (VW) in Mexico will close 2019 with a sales growth of 13%, which places it like a flagship market worldwide with the potential of expansion, assured Giovanni Juarez, director of Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles. VW has five different types of vehicles designed for the needs of clients with fleets, through retail, direct sales, leasing and internet sales.

Vehicular leasing grows to 207,106 units in the year's third quarter

Vehicle leasing grew in the year's third quarter, since there were 207,106 reported units administered by partners among which are cars, trucks and motorcycles, versus the 188,000 reported ones at the closing of the second quarter. 71.8% of these units are financed through pure leasing, which represents a growth of 8% for this scheme. David Madrigal, president of the Mexican Vehicle Leasing Association, mentions that this growth is due to the fact that more companies are realizing there are benefits to be gained from leasing and fleet administration.

124,797 new cars were sold in November

During the penultimate month of the year there were a total of 124,797 new cars sold in Mexico, this number represents the worst performance registered for a November in the last five years. The result of sold cars within the internal market saw a contraction of 7.0% in annual comparison, since in November 2018 there were 134,131 units sold, according to numbers by the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA) and the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI).
