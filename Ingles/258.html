Coahuila produces 19.2% of the auto parts total

Nationally Coahuila leads in auto parts production, including engines and transmissions, with a 19.2% of the total, followed by Chihuahua with 13.5%, Guanajuato and Nuevo Leon with 8.9%, informed Oscar Albin, executive president for the National Auto parts Industry (INA). "Coahuila has the assembly plants General Motors, Ford and Chrysler; Chihuahua has a lot of companies; Guanajuato is now producing more auto parts than Nuevo Leon, but both log the same participation percentage," he explained.
Korean stamping and molding companies are looking to establish themselves in Puebla

Korean businessmen, from the stamping and molding supply sector for the automotive industry, held an encounter with authorities to acquaint themselves with the competitive advantages that the state of Puebla offers, because they are interested in arranging investment projects. The minister of local Economy, Olivia Salomon Vibaldo, welcomed members of the Korean International Commerce Association, as well as molding and stamping companies from the Jeonbuk region, who represent the second exporter of these products in the Asian region, in competition with China and Japan.
Hyundai placed 3,477 units in July

Hyundai Motor of Mexico closed July with a total of 3,447 sold units, to an increase of the brand's vehicles sold in the Mexican market this 2019. Hyundai Accent, in both its sedan and hatchback versions, remains as the biggest seller of the Korean brand, with a total of 897 units sold in July. The accumulated numbers by Accent, represent a 25.53% of the sales total of Hyundai Motor of Mexico so far this year. In total, it amounts to 6,455 vehicles sold, thus confirming that it's one of the most sought out models within the B segment in the national market.
Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico delivers 15 new Cascadia units in Jalisco

Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico, through their Jimenez Autocamiones distributor, delivered 15 new Cascadia units to JSF Transporta y Transportes Temperaturas Controladas (TCC), in Jalisco, with which the transport company strengthens its vehicular park with Freightliner units. The delivered tractor units have a telematics system Enlace Freightliner 2.0, which will allow TTC to monitor the units in real time, to obtain strategic indicators which will allow them to improve their decision making and to protect their assets.
Mercedes-Benz Buses delivers 20 buses in Leon

Mercedes-Benz Buses, together with their distributor Zapata Buses Leon and the transport company Corporativo Transporta, delivered 20 buses of a total of 100, which will be used for the public transport service of the city of Leon. The new units will mobilize around 10,000 people a day, in 3 different routes through the key city in the Mexican Bajio. The estimate investment for the 100 passenger vehicles surpasses US$7 million.
Nissan Mexico achieves the production of 13 million vehicles after 53 years of operations in Mexico

Nissan Mexico began production in 1966, and now has 4 plants in the country that have, in turn, produced 13 million vehicles. It's in this way, that Mexico has placed itself like the fourth most important market for the brand worldwide. After 53 years of manufacturing operations in Mexico, Nissan achieved the production milestone of 13 million units in conjunction with its three assembly plants the Aguascalientes A1, Aguascalientes A2 and CIVAC.
FCA Mexico celebrates the production of 4 million RAM units in Saltillo

The assembly plant in Saltillo celebrated the exit from the production line of the 4 million unit, this unit is a 2019 RAM 2500 Limited with a diesel motor of 6.7 liters. The RAM pick-ups assembled in this plant are exported to more than 30 countries and have been internationally recognized with different awards, from which the following can be highlighted: the Truck of Texas Award (won on more than 4 occasions), Best Technology and the prominent Trend Motor Award, Truck of the Year.
