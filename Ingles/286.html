The totally renewed BMW 1 Series arrives to Mexico

After 2 generations and more than 1.3 million produced units worldwide, the totally renewed BMW 1 Series arrives to the Mexican market, with a powerful and modern design that combines a great driving dynamism and a sensation of great amplitude in the interior. The new generation of the BMW 1 Series arrives to Mexico in three versions that can be adapted to every taste: Sport Line, M Sport, and the M Performance version.
Zacatecas hopes to become a hub of automotive investment

Automotive investments will keep arriving to Mexico, and Zacatecas will be one of the receptors of the new auto parts investments from Eastern Europe coming from Daimler and possibly the Chinese electric car manufacturer GAC, which will impulse the development of the state's automotive cluster, said governor Alejandro Tello Cristerna. The automotive company Daimler will move a group of suppliers from the vehicle and truck production line from Europe to Mexico to then locate the operations in Zacatecas.
BMW bets on service in Mexico

BMW continues making investments in Mexico, especially in the customer service and service areas, despite the uncertainty in the sector due to the drop in sales. "We've invested US$100 million since 2017 (in customer service) and we're working in our service area (...) we have to train more people," said Maru Escobedo, CEO of BMW in Mexico. Currently BMW Group has 42 BMW distributors, 34 MINI ones, and 31 Motorrad ones and an installed service network capacity of 200,000 entered units per year.

Nissan Mexico and KidZania form an alliance to create the future of mobility

Nissan Mexico announced a strategical alliance with KidZania. Under the brand's concept of "Intelligent Mobility", the purpose of the alliance is to inspire new generations to create the future of mobility, which will be more connected, sustainable and one where electric vehicles (EVs) like the Nissan LEAF will be a key element. As part of this alliance, Nissan Mexico will have interactive pavilions, denominated Automotive Innovation Centers, in four of KidZania's locations.
Faurecia received the "Supplier Excellence Award" from GM

Faurecia, one of the leading automotive technology companies in the world, received the "Supplier Excellence Award" from General Motors (GM) given to two of its plants: Clean Mobility in Silao and Clean Mobility in Queretaro, for third and fourth year respectively. GM representatives delivered the award that highlights Faurecia's innovation, quality and performance, as one of its best suppliers. Both plants house the manufacturing of catalysts, silencers, exhaust systems, adaptive valves, among others, in order to give clean mobility solutions.
General Motors confirmed the arrival of the Chevrolet Onix for the first quarter of 2020

Chevrolet will be introducing the medium-sized sedan Onix during the first quarter of 2020, this model will be produced in the firm's industrial complex in San Luis Potosi. Adriana Schutte Richaud, Chevrolet's marketing manager confirmed this in company of Jose Francisco Garza Rodriguez, president and general director of General Motors Mexico, that this sedan will be offered in the LS, LT and Premier versions, and that "it will revolutionize the sedan demand in Mexico, thus elevating Chevrolet to another level."
