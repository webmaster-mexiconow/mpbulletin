Bulletin #19
19 January, 2017
Mercedes-Benz to build up to three models at Mexican plant

Mercedes-Benz sources told Autocar the Mexican plant will start production with the new GLA model, but the company also plans to assemble a four-door sedan, which is set to be previewed in concept form at the Shanghai motor show in April. In addition, a new seven-seat SUV still under development and likely to be named GLB will come out of the Aguascalientes facility.
GM plays along with Trump; announces old plan to move jobs from Mexico to U.S.

After being criticized by president-elect Donald Trump, General Motors announced plans to invest additional US$ 1 billion in several U.S. plants and to move a supplier pick-up axle production from Mexico to Michigan. Some media outlets pointed out the shift of axle work was part of a strategy revealed in June 2015 aimed to produce future vehicle components at GM's own plants.
WWL opens logistic facility to support Audi's Mexican operations

Wallenius Wilhelmsen Logistics (WWL) opened a new processing facility at Audi's new assembly plant in San Jose Chiapa, Mexico, to support the automaker operations. WWL will perform two critical activities in the dispatch of the new Audi vehicles: yard management and railcar shunting. For this purpose, around 80 people will be employed, and two 30-ton railcar movers have been leased to pull and push Automax and Bilevel railcars, covering 20 kilometers of tracks.
Ford works a plan to compensate suppliers involved with canceled plant

Ford Motor works a plan to compensate suppliers that were preparing to serve a plant the company canceled. The company will also return the land to the government of Mexico. Ford officials said the cost to compensate suppliers won't be "too dramatic", but didn't give any figures. They also declined to say, for now, how much Ford had spent on the construction that began last spring.
Delphi acquires software provider to enhance its automated driving program

Delphi Automotive PLC announced the acquisition of Movimento, a Michigan-based provider of software and data management for the automotive sector. Financial terms of the transaction were not disclosed. Movimento's advanced technology platform is designed to allow manufacturers to quickly and efficiently upgrade or fix software at all stages of a vehicle's lifecycle.
BMW Mexican plant will be profitable even without U.S. market, official says

BMW board member Ian Robertson, speaking to the Financial Times, made clear that the new plant in the works at San Luis Potosi can survive without making any sales to the US. "It's a full blown operation to produce cars for anywhere in the world. The plant is capable of making right hand drive, left hand drive, various models, various derivatives," Robertson said. "When asked if the plant could survive without selling any cars to the US, he said: absolutely," remarked the London-based news outlet.
Mexico's tire industry to increase its production by 5%

The tire industry in Mexico expects to raise its production at a 5% rate, to achieve a record of 31.3 million units. These plans are based on the fact that new factories will open in the upcoming months: Goodyear plant in San Luis Potosi will start operations in April and Pirelli is planning to open its second plant in Silao, Guanajuato, midway along 2017.
Toyota chief discusses Mexican plant with Vice President-elect Mike Pence

Toyota Motor Corp. President Akio Toyoda met with U.S. Vice President-elect Mike Pence in Washington after the carmaker announced plans to make $10 billion in capital investments in the U.S. over the next five years. Toyoda is believed to have tried to seek Pence's understanding for the company's plan to build a new factory in Mexico, stressing that Toyota has no plan to cut jobs in the United States, according to informed sources.
Takata pleads guilty over airbag defects; three former executives indicted

The U.S. government charged Takata Corp. with criminal wrongdoing in connection with airbag inflator ruptures linked to at least 16 deaths worldwide. The company agreed to plead guilty to the charge as part of a US$ 1 billion deal. A federal grand jury separately indicted three former Takata executives for fraud and conspiracy over the defective airbag inflators, which triggered the largest automotive safety recall in U.S. history.
VW executive faces possible life sentence over diesel scandal

The Justice Department said Volkswagen executive Oliver Schmidt "faces what would be an effective life sentence" if convicted over the diesel-emissions scandal. Schmidt is charged with eleven felony counts, which could be punished by up to 169 years in prison, the government said. Volkswagen agreed to plead guilty and pay US$ 4.3 billion in civil and criminal fines.
