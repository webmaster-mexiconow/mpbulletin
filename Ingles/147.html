US auto sales drop an estimated 5 percent in April

U.S. auto sales totaled an estimated 1.35 million units in April, down 5 percent from the same period a year ago, reported automotive analysis firm Edmunds. The seasonally adjusted annualized sales rate (SAAR) for April came in at 17.15 million, according to Autodata Corp., marking the eighth straight month the pace of U.S. sales has topped 17 million. The average price of a new car in April was US$ 35,411, up 2 percent from the same month last year, according to Kelley Blue Book. Sales estimates became more complicated to calculate after General Motors announced in March that it would cease monthly sales reports in favor of quarterly reports. Auto sales in Mexico drop during April for the 11th month in a row

Sales of light vehicles in Mexico declined for the 11th month in a row in April, this time to 109,265 units, down 4.6 percent compared to the same month of 2017, according to the latest data from the Mexican Auto Dealers Association (AMDA). Year-to-date sales are now of 446,494 vehicles, a 9.4 percent drop against the same four-month period of 2017, AMDA figures revealed. Given that sales have been in line with AMDA's forecasts, the possibility of improving results during the second half of the year is increasing, but it will depend on a successful renegotiation of NAFTA and the absence of post-electoral conflicts, said Guillermo Rosales, head of the auto dealers lobby. Canada's auto sales down 2.7 percent in April

Canada's auto sales fell 2.7 percent in April to 191,856 vehicles due to weak demand for passenger cars, according to research firm DesRosiers Automotive Consultants. Sales of passenger cars sold fell 12.4 percent in the month to 58,083 vehicles. In contrast, automakers marketed 133,773 light trucks (pickups and SUVs) representing a 2.2% increase. Year-to-date sales through April were of 621,114 units with a marginal gain of 0.4% compared to sales during the same period of 2017. Passenger car sales in the first four months of the year are down 9.6 percent to 179,269 units, while light trucks are 5.1 percent up, totaling 441,845 vehicles. More details here: bit.ly/2HO1UTh
Chevrolet Blazer spotted during test drive in Michigan

Seems like rumors about General Motors' relaunch of its Chevrolet Blazer are about to be confirmed as shown by Trucks.com, who posted in recent days spy shots of the formerly SUV now as a crossover while performing tests on public roads near Troy, Michigan. According to the site, the new Blazer will share its platform with the GMC Acadia and Cadillac XT5 to fit between Chevrolet's two-row Equinox and the three-row Traverse in size. Previous reports have stated that the Blazer will be built in GM's Ramos Arizpe assembly plant and at least one supplier in Coahuila has hinted about being involved in the program. Ford might bring back the Maverick as crossover

Ford has trademarked once again the Maverick nameplate but the iconic car might not be back in the near future at least in form of sedan or coupe, but as a small SUV or crossover. According to Autoblog, who broke the story in recent days, Ford applied for registration of the Maverick trademark in December 2016 and was finally granted last month along with the Timberline name. Given that Ford is currently shifting its catalog to get rid of low-profit sedans, opening more room for pickups and SUVs, Autoblog points out that the Maverick name could fit well on a "baby Bronco".
Leoni builds US$ 37.5 million plant, its second in Yucatan, sixth overall in Mexico

Leoni AG, a Germany-based manufacturer of cable, harnesses and wiring systems, in recent days broke ground to build its second plant in Yucatan, Mexico, less than six months after the company officially started operations of its first facility in the state, fifth in Mexico overall. The new 40,000 square-meter site will imply a USD$ 37.5 million investment, USD$ 20 million to build the facility, and USD$ 15 million for equipment. The company plans to open 2,000 job positions in a two-year period. The site will be built under a sustainability model that includes the use of solar panels and wastewater treatment sources. Korean manufacturer LS Automotive builds USD$ 60 million plant in Nuevo Leon

LS Automotive Technologies Co. (LSAT), a Korean manufacturer of automotive components, announced it broke ground in recent days to build a new plant in the municipality of Guadalupe, Nuevo Leon, in Northern Mexico. LSAT will invest USD$ 60 million in the 40,000 square meter site at the FINSA industrial park to manufacture a wide variety of components that ranges from internal vehicle control systems such as switch modules and interior lamps to electric control system such as sensors and body control equipment. The company plans to use the new facility as an overseas base for the North American region amid growing demand in the continent including the United States and Mexico. American Axle's net income up 14% on record sales

American Axle & Manufacturing Holdings Inc. (AAM) announced its net income rose 14 percent to USD$ 89.4 million during the first quarter of 2018, boosted by record sales resulting from its acquisition last year of Metaldyne Performance Group Inc. First quarter sales of the Detroit-based driveline supplier reached US$ 1.86 billion, compared to US$ 1.05 billion in the first quarter of 2017, the company reported. Through the acquisition of Metaldyne, the presence of AAM in Mexico expanded to nine facilities, two of these are located in Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila, five in Silao, Guanajuato, and one each in El Carmen, Nuevo Leon, and Aguascalientes city. Net income of automotive supplier Dana Inc. hikes 44 percent during first quarter of 2018

Ohio-based automotive supplier Dana Inc. saw its net income hike 44 percent to USD$ 108 million during the first quarter of 2018, on stronger demand across commercial, off-highway and light-vehicle business lines. "In the first quarter, Dana's sales growth outperformed the market with 26 percent higher revenue, including 17 percent organic growth," said James Kamsickas, Dana president and chief executive officer. Dana Holding Corporation operates six manufacturing sites in Chihuahua, Monterrey, Queretaro, Tlaxcala, Toluca and Tlalnepantla, dedicated to the production of axles, driveshafts, suspension and transmission parts, as well as electronic components for both light and commercial vehicles. NAFTA talks resume today in Washington

Talks to update the NAFTA trade deal enter a critical week on Monday, as uncertainty and pessimism blooms because of gridlock on the most critical issues. Discussions will center on rules of origin, the dispute-resolution mechanism and U.S. demands for a sunset clause that could automatically kill the trade deal after five years. Mexico's main auto sector lobby, AMIA, has already said that U.S. proposals regarding rules of origin are unacceptable. Meanwhile, the reveal of a counteroffer by the Mexican delegation is expected. All three countries seek to resolve an impasse in key areas before elections in Mexico and the United States complicate the process.
