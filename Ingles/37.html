GM bets on Made in Mexico Equinox to gain share in SUV market

General Motors will try to increase its SUV market share with the redesigned Chevrolet Equinox. For that end, the automaker will build the model in four plants worldwide, including two factories in Mexico, in an effort to get advantage of the country's free trade agreements with more than 40 countries. GM plans to build about 550,000 of the Equinox and its sibling, the more upscale GMC Terrain, globally starting in 2018. To make room for Equinox capacity, GM is shifting production of the Cadillac SRX from Mexico to Tennessee, where it also builds the XT5 model.
Recently acquired Vitro subsidiary aims for autonomous vehicle market

Pittsburgh Glass Works (PGW), recently acquired by Mexican glass giant Vitro S.A.B. de C.V., has developed technology that when integrated into windshield glass provides a way to mount cameras and sensors in self-driving cars. The company also designs technology to integrate smart communication and antenna connections into windshield glass. Now that PGW has access to a world-class research center also owned by Vitro, there's confidence it will be better positioned to expand its technical and engineering capabilities for self-driving cars.
Ford expects 50% profit fall in Q1 2017

Ford Motor said its profit will fall by about 50% in the current quarter, due to weakness in the auto industry that's already been reported by rivals such as General Motors. Ford expects a decline in sales in Asia Pacific and weaker pricing in many other markets. The company also expects Brexit to hurt its European operations, which finally moved from a loss to a profit in 2016. Ford did say will maintain its full-year forecast of only a modest decline in profits, anticipating earnings of about US$ 9 billion this year after a US$ 10.4 billion profit for 2016.
Kongsberg Automotive completes sale of Reynosa plant to Futuris Group

Norway-based automotive parts manufacturer Kongsberg Automotive announced it has completed the sale of its "North American Headrest/Armrest business" to Futuris Automotive Group for an undisclosed amount. Kongsberg's only plant dedicated to seating components in North America is located in Reynosa, Tamaulipas. Futuris Group is a global seating and interiors supplier which operates two plants located at Acuna and Piedras Negras, in the Northern state of Coahuila.
Uber suspends self-driving vehicle program following Arizona accident

Uber Technologies Inc. halted a pilot program for self-driving vehicles following an accident on Saturday in Arizona. The accident took place in the city of Tempe after the driver of a second vehicle made a turn and failed to yield to a self-driving Uber, police said. Two drivers were sitting in the front seats of the Uber car when the crash took place. Tempe Police said the Uber vehicle, a Volvo SUV, was not responsible for the accident, which caused the car to flip on its side.
Spain-based Gestamp to enter Europe stock market valued at US$ 3.8 billion

Spanish auto parts supplier Gestamp announced it would price its initial public offering (IPO) at 5.6 to 6.7 euros per share, valuing the company at 3.54 billion euros (US$ 3.8 billion) if it prices at the middle of the range. The company aims to list on the Spanish stock exchange on April 7. Gestamp and its subsidiary Edscha operate six plants in Mexico located in Aguascalientes, Toluca, Puebla and San Luis Potosi. (US$ 1 = 0.9269 euros)
French firm Demotechnic builds interior components plant in Guanajuato

Demotechnic, a subsidiary of France-based Safe Group, started the construction of a plant in the state of Guanajuato. During its first phase the company will invest US$ 4.2 million to set up an operation with 94 employees to supply auto interior components. The facility will employ 200 workers once operates at full capacity. Demotechnic operates another facility in Chihuahua. Safe Group Metals Division also owns two Mexican plants located in Nuevo Leon and Mexico City.
Mazda names Tim Olson as Public Relations Program Manager for North America

Mazda North American Operations (MNAO) announced the addition of Tim Olson to its public relations team as Program Manager, Public Relations. In his new position, Olson is responsible for managing day-to-day PR activities and storytelling related to product, corporate, lifestyle and motorsports communications. MNAO is headquartered in Irvine, California, and oversees the sales, marketing, parts and customer service support of Mazda vehicles in the United States and Mexico through more than 600 dealers.
Cummins increases goal to reduce water use

Cummins Inc. announced it has increased its facility water goal to a 50% intensity reduction by 2020 from a baseline of 2010. Cummins released its water conservation goal in 2014, pledging to reduce water use intensity by 33%. As of the third quarter of 2016, the company's water efficiency efforts had resulted in a 42% intensity reduction. Because the Company achieved the initial goal ahead of schedule, it revised its goal to be more aggressive. The revised 50% intensity reduction goal represents a total water savings of 763 million gallons of water since 2010, equivalent to the water needed for 395,000 people for a year.
Investment firm KKR completes acquisition of Calsonic Kansei Corporation

Investment firm KKR announced that it has completed its deal to acquire Japan-based auto supplier Calsonic Kansei Corporation, including the 41% of the company previously owned by Nissan. KKR now owns 95.21% of the existing common shares of Calsonic Kansei, and plans to acquire the remaining common shares. Calsonic Kansei operates 5 plants in Mexico, three in the state of Aguascalientes, one in Durango and one in Mexico City.
