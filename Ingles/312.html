Hitachi Cable invests US$14.2 million in Queretaro

Hitachi Cable announced the installation of a new production center in Queretaro, which forms part of the expansion of the second plant they have in the state; this project is seeing an investment of US$14.2 million and will generate 620 jobs. It's expected to begin operations in October of this year, in the El Marques Industrial Park, as exposed by the director of Hitachi Cable Queretaro, Antonio Vazquez. The company is dedicated to the production of original equipment for Japanese, American and other brands like Nissan, Subaru, Toyota, Mazda, Ford, GM, Tesla, and etcetera.

Mercedes-Benz Mexico is taking precautions against coronavirus

Mercedes-Benz Mexico informed that it's decided to change their event strategy, in such a way that they will be carried out virtually or will be postponed, before the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic. "In this way, the decision to change the format of Mercedes-Benz Fashion Week Mexico City (MBFWMx); the event will be transmitted digitally and will be available online from anywhere. At the same time, Longines Global Champions Tour, will be postponed by the organizers with the intention of resuming in the next few months."

K Tech Mexico invests US$20 million in Guanajuato

The automotive supplier of injection molding and mold manufacturing, K Tech, invested US$20 million in its new plant and will generate 120 direct jobs. The inauguration was attended by Diego Sinhue Rodríguez Vallejo, governor of Guanajuato; Toshiaki Idea, president of Kyoto Platec; Silao Major, Jose Antonio Trejo Valdepeña and the general director of K Tech Mexico, Yoshifumi Ikeda. K Tech Mexico has clients such as BOSCH, U-shin, Toyoda Gosei, Mitsubishi Electric, Furukawa, Jeco, Aisan, Yazaki and Honda Lock, among others.

Honda will suspend operations in North America due to COVID-19

Automotive company Honda announced on Wednesday that it will suspend operations in its car assembly and powertrain plants in North America, among which are the brand's Celaya, Guanajuato plants. In a press release, the company highlighted that this is due to the anticipated decrease in demand from the market related to the economic impact caused by the coronavirus pandemic. "Honda will suspend production during six days starting on March 23, with plans to resume production on March 31," said the company.

Ford and General Motors are also stopping operations due to COVID-19

Ford and General Motors announced that their plants in Mexico, United States and Canada will stop operations through to March 30 to rearrange their plans, take care of the safety of their workers and their families as well as looking for creative solutions to go back into production. While dealing with delicate situations, as is the COVID-19, the great American automakers have decided to decrease their operations to stop the situation and rearrange to go back into production on a scale that's adjusted to the new economic and market forecasts.

Audi Mexico will stop production for two weeks

Audi Mexico announced that it will be closing its Puebla plant during two weeks, as part of the steps to combat the coronavirus, with which it will stop production due to lack of parts needed for the assembly of cars, while adhering to the recommendations made by international health authorities to safeguard workers. Audi Mexico has experienced the lack of inputs for the assembly of the Audi Q5 in San José Chiapa, as well as diverse difficulties in the transport of finished products to their country of destination.

A new Jaguar Land Rover distributor opened in Torreon

Jaguar Land Rover Mexico informed that it opened the doors to its new Jaguar Land Rover Laguna distributor. The distributor has an extension of 1,925 square meters and has a service shop equipped with the most modern technology, on top of washing and vehicle preparation areas. Raul Peñafiel, general director of Jaguar Land Rover Mexico said: "We're excited by the arrival of this new distributor in Torreon, this reflects the commitment that Jaguar Land Rover has to having a solid presence in the country."
