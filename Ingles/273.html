GM strike in the United States also affects Chihuahua

The maquiladora Mahle stopped its operations in Ciudad Juarez due to the fact that no material nor any supplies have reached them due to the strike carried out by the General Motors (GM) workers in the United States and the technical halt of the company in Silao, Guanajuato; besides other 11 manufacturing companies will carry out technical halts if the strike continues, informed Luis Carlos Ramirez Lopez, president of the Association of Maquiladoras and Exporters of Chihuahua (Index).
AMDA considers that there's need for a strengthening program for the automotive market

Guillermo Rosales considered that the moment has come for the federal government to implement a Strengthening Program of the Automotive Internal Market that would allow the main engine of the national economy to recover. AMDA's deputy director explained that this program should consider the next points: credit support to micro and small businesses for the renovation of their vehicular park; control of used car imports; battle the used car contraband; and an extension in the deductibility over income tax in the acquisition of new vehicles.
Sale of the new Acadia 2020 will begin in November

Buick-GMC-Cadillac distributors will start offering Mexican clients in the first few days of November the medium-sized SUV, Acadia 2020 in the Denali and AT4 versions, with prices of MXN$944,600 and MXN$851,600, respectively, informed Yammil Guaida, marketing manager for Buick, GMC and Cadillac. Both versions have a 6 passenger capacity and a trunk, which features hands-free opening, capacity of 362 liters and 1,841 kilo pull capacity.

Heavy vehicle sales decrease in September

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) informed that during September, the wholesale sales of heavy and commercial vehicles reached a total of 2,754 units, which compared with the 3,737 units registered in September 2018, represent a decrease of 26.3%. During said month, the retail sales of heavy and commercial vehicles reached 3,431 units, which compared to the ones sold during the same month of 2018, meant a decrease of 0.9%
Bandag receives Clean Industry certification for the second time

Bandag, business unit of Bridgestone Mexico, dedicated to the research, development and manufacturing of materials and equipment for the renewal of truck tires, received for the second time the environmental certification Clean Industry, which is given by the Environmental and Territorial Order Prosecutor Office of Guanajuato State. The certification was validated by authorities through an environmental audit in which, after making a documented and objective evaluation of the productive process, it was confirmed that Bandag meets with the current standards and laws in the state of Guanajuato, to safeguard the environment.
Queretaro auto parts sector establishes emerging strategies in light of GM strike

Auto part makers that supply for General Motors (GM), have established emerging strategies like moving personnel vacations forward and machine maintenance, in order to alleviate the affectations in the supply chain caused by the strike befalling the company in the Unites States. The head of the Minister of Sustainable Development (Sedesu), Marco Antonio Del Prete Tercero, explained that these are processes taken by companies at other times in the year, but because GM's halt of activities they're choosing to move them forward and are urging workers to go on vacation.
Pxl Automotive Mexico invests US$10 million in Queretaro

With an investment of US$10 million and the creation of 400 jobs, Pxl Automotive Mexico started operations in the Industrial Park Technologico Innovacion in the municipality of El Marques. The Chinese company, dedicated to aluminum machining, will include steel next, as well as bent tube forming for the automotive industry. The company joins 14 other Chinese companies located in the state, of which seven have settled there during the current administration.
Automotive investments of US$100 million reach Durango

The quality of labor in Durango as well as investor's trust, increase growth opportunities in Durango, assured Governor Jose Rosas Aispuro Torres, by inaugurating in La Laguna a new plant for the company ATR Sumitomo and the expansion of Henniges Automotive, both projects represent an investment of US$100 million. Every time a company grows, there's job creation, affirmed the governor, when celebrating both of these openings, which represent more than 2,000 new jobs and he thanked both international consortia for their trust.
UPS Mexico incorporates 80 new units to its fleet

UPS Mexico incorporated to its fleet 67 units of the new Mercedes-Benz Vans Sprinter and 13 M2 Freightliner trucks. This acquisition forms part of the investments carried out by the brand during the year to strengthen their network in Mexico with the goal of meeting export demands for the parcel sector for industries like the automotive, aerospace and high-technology, on top of the demands of e-commerce and small and medium sized enterprises.
