Mexican auto production, exports on the rise in April

Mexican auto production increased 3.2% to 278,173 vehicles in April. Exports hiked 16.1% to 228,810 units, AMIA said. Almost 80% of Mexican auto exports, 179,958 units, headed to the U.S. in April, 22,127 vehicles ended up in Canada (9.7%), 12,770 units were shipped to Latin America (5.6%) and 8,269 cars reached Europe (3.6%). During the first four months of 2017, auto plants established in Mexico produced 1,221,877 vehicles, a 13.6% increase compared to the 1,075,340 cars from the same period in the previous year. Total auto exports during the first four months of the year were up 14.6% to 978,972 units.
Two new industrial parks launch operations in Guanajuato

Last week two industrial parks opened doors in the municipality of Apaseo el Grande, Guanajuato, where Toyota builds its new manufacturing facility. The Amexhe Industrial Park started operations over a 250 hectares' surface where 50 hectares are already fully developed all kind of utilities included. The area has space for 63 facilities y the potential creation of 8,000 jobs, according to officials from Grupo DRT, a Queretaro-based developer that built the complex. Also, a developer based in Coahuila opened doors for its park named El Grande located in the same municipality. Grupo El Grande invested US$ 45 million to develop 28 hectares and the potential creation of 2,400 jobs under shelter programs, which the company also supply.
American Axle achieves record sales during first quarter

American Axle and Manufacturing Holdings Inc. (AAM) reported a record first quarter driven by sales volume, a strong product mix and cost efficiencies. AAM said its net income totaled US$ 78.4 million, up 28% from a year ago. Revenue reached US$ 1.05 billion, up more than 8% and a new quarterly record. AAM reported US$ 16 million of restructuring and acquisition costs related to the acquisition of Metaldyne Performance Group (MPG) and buying a USM Mexico Manufacturing LLC plant. AAM owns two production sites both located in Silao, Guanajuato. MPG operates three facilities in Aguascalientes, Coahuila y Nuevo Leon.
Koyo Joint starts steering shafts production in San Luis Potosi

Japan-based Koyo Machine Industries opened its new facility in San Luis Potosi where the company invested US$ 8 million to manufacture steering intermediate shafts. The production plant is located in the Villa de Pozos Industrial Park. Around 160 workers were recruited by Koyo Joint Mexico in order to start operations. This is Koyo Machine Industries' first manufacturing facility in the Americas.
Dana Inc. reports strong first quarter with sales of US$ 1.7 billion

Ohio-based axle and powertrain manufacturer Dana Inc.'s reported a strong first quarter with sales of US$ 1.70 billion, a 17% increase compared to same period of 2016. Its net income rose 67% to US$ 75 million due to new business gains, higher end-market demand in global light-truck markets, and improved demand in global off-highway end markets. Currency was a slight headwind of US$ 6 million due to strength of the U.S. dollar against the euro, British pound, and Mexican peso. Over the NAFTA region Dana operates 23 facilities, 12 locations in U.S., six in Mexico and five in Canada. Its Mexican plants are located in Chihuahua, Monterrey, Queretaro, Tlalnepantla, Tlaxcala and Toluca.
Kia Motors Mexican output to be launched in 65 new markets

Korean automaker Kia Motors announced that the new Rio sedan built in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon, was selected by the company to be launched over 65 new markets, including countries such Egypt, United Arab Emirates and Jordan. Additionally, the Forte, which is also made in Pesqueria, will be exported to 10 countries. Kia projects 300,000 vehicles output for 2017, of those 40% will be Rio units both sedan and hatchback versions. The other 60% consists of three different models, Forte cars both sedan and hatchback version, and the Hyundai Accent. The facility has now a second shift is prospected to start a third shift by the end of the year, totaling a 3,000-people labor force in the Kia plant.
BMW Mexican facility reports 60% completion, on schedule to start operations in 2019

BMW's Mexican facility, under construction in the central state of San Luis Potosi, reports a 60% completion in its main buildings and remains on schedule to start production of next-generation 3 Series sedans in 2019, Company officials revealed during a press conference. The painting facility is 30% completed, the area for vehicle testing before shipping is 20% and the assembly zone is barely 10%, but it will be one of the last to be finished, said Hermann Bohrer, President and CEO for the Mexican plant, which will build 150,000 vehicles a year. The plant is counting on 180 suppliers to begin production, but 160 of those have already operations in the country, 45 are installed in the state.
Japan-based Midori Auto Leather builds its third plant in San Luis Potosi

Japan-based Midori Auto Leather broke ground in recent days to build its third plant in San Luis Potosi. The company plans to invest around US$ 26 million and hire some 1,100 workers to manufacture leather products for automotive applications. Takashi Akaji, Director for the Company in Mexico, said Midori opted for Mexico over China to expand its footprint. Midori currently employs 2,100 workers in its Mexican facilities to supply seat covers and steering wheels for automakers such as Honda, Toyota, Mazda, Subaru y Nissan. GM, VW and Fiat Chrysler are also part of Midori's customers, but those OEMs are supplied from other facilities.
Certus Automotive opens US$ 22 million plant in Queretaro

Certus Automotive opened in recent days its first production facility in Queretaro to manufacture chrome plated plastic components for automotive applications. The company invested US$ 22 million to establish operations at a facility located in the Aerotech Industrial Park. Some 350 workers were recruited for this endeavor. Certus supplier parts for Lincoln and Volkswagen from other facilities, but is not clear which automaker will supply from the Queretaro site.
Cooper Standard's net income up 33% during first quarter

Michigan-based Cooper Standard reported a strong net income of US$ 41.7 million during the first quarter of 2017, up 33.2% when compared to the same period of 2016. The company reported sales of US$ 902.1 million during the first quarter, up 4.6%. Cooper Standard is a global supplier of rubber and plastic components for the automotive industry. Its catalog includes rubber and plastic sealing, fuel and brake lines, fluid transfer hoses and anti-vibration systems. The company operates eight production sites in Mexico, including two in Juarez, Chihuahua and two more in Guaymas, Sonora. Other facilities are located in Aguascalientes, Atlacomulco, Torreon and Saltillo.
