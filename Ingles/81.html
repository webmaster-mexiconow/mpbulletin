VW unveils the new T-Roc compact SUV; could be built in Puebla

Volkswagen unveiled its new T-Roc compact crossover in a ceremony on the shores of Lake Como in Italy. The move seeks to realign Volkswagen's product strategy to put the red-hot crossover/SUV market at the top of its priorities. In Europe, the T-Roc is expected to go on sale in November for around 20,000 euros with China soon to follow, but there's no plans to introduce the model into the U.S. market until 2019. According to Glenn Brooks from just-auto.com, the T-Roc could be built also in Puebla, since it's based on the same MQB platform as the new Tiguan.
Mexican production of heavy vehicles up 1.9% in June

Mexico's commercial vehicle industry manufactured 13,362 trucks, tractors trailers and buses in June, an increase of 1.9% compared to same month of 2017. Exports totaled 9,753 units, down 2.1% compared to June 2016. During the first half of 2017 heavy truck manufacturers built in Mexico 68,830 units, down 12.1% compared to the same six-month period of 2016. Meanwhile, exports experienced a decline of 17.7% to 50,727 vehicles. Truck trailer production alone, which is the most exported product of the commercial vehicle industry, increased 23.9% during the first half of year. Exports of truck trailers also increased 23.1% from January through June.
PACCAR introduces 12-speed transmission jointly developed with Eaton

PACCAR, a global manufacturer of heavy-duty trucks is introducing the new PACCAR Automated Transmission, the lightest heavy-duty transmission for on-highway commercial vehicles, designed to complement the superior performance of PACCAR MX engines and PACCAR axles. Kenworth and Peterbilt will begin offering the PACCAR Automated Transmission to North American customers in October 2017. The transmission is the result of a three-and-a-half-year development program with Eaton. Production is set to begin in October of this year at Eaton's San Luis Potosi facility.
Adient acquires Futuris Groups for US$ 360 million, two plants in Coahuila included

Adient PLC., the global leader in automotive seating, announced it has signed a definitive agreement to acquire Futuris Group. The purchase price is approximately US$ 360 million including the assumption of approximately US$ 18 million of net debt. Privately held Futuris is headquartered in Oak Park, Michigan, and operates two production sites in Piedras Negras and Acuna, Coahuila, Mexico. Adient expects the transaction to add approximately US$ 500M in revenue on an annual basis. It is expected to be accretive to Adient's adjusted fiscal 2018 earnings per share.
Honda replaces 210,000 Takata airbag inflators in Mexico

Honda has replaced in Mexico more than 210,000 Takata airbag inflators in some 143,000 vehicles free of charge since it started a recall two years ago, the company revealed. The automaker has partnered with auto dealers and insurance companies to reach vehicle owners who no longer take their units to get certified maintenance at the company's service centers. "It has been hard work considering how complicated it can be to track down and contact each customer to let them know that they need to take their car to the nearest dealership. However, when it comes to safety no effort should be spared," said Erich Merckel, Subdirector of Honda de Mexico Aftermarket.
VW relaunches previous generation of the Tiguan in the US market

Because of the unusual interest for the nearly ten-year old SUV, Volkswagen will continue selling its previous generation Tiguan in the U.S. under the Tiguan Limited name alongside the all-new version of the model for two more years. In order to spark more interest, the German automaker has revealed the unit will go on sale with only one trim level starting at US$ 21,995, which includes halogen headlights, 16-inch steel wheels, cloth upholstery, the Composition Color infotainment system (with Bluetooth), plus a rearview camera. The Tiguan Limited will be equipped with a turbocharged 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine that produces 200 horsepower and 207 lb.-ft. of torque.
Firsts units of the all-new GMC Terrain arrive to US auto dealers

The launch of the all-new, second-generation GMC Terrain is well under way with the firsts units, which last month began to roll out of the GM San Luis Potosi assembly plant, starting to arrive at dealer lots across North America. Test drives were offered to several media outlets in recent days at a GM location in Pennsylvania, where the Terrain caught the attention of journalists with its "refinement, comfort and performance," according to Cars.com. The Terrain will be offered with three new turbocharged engines, two gas variants of 1.5 and 2.0-liter four-cylinder, and a 1.6-liter diesel engine.
Analysts expect auto sales to increase in August

Forecasters expect a small increase in U.S. auto sales for August compared the same month a year ago, after seven months of declines. J.D. Power and LMC Automotive expect unit sales of just over 1.5 million in August, an increase of just under 1%. Kelly Blue Book said separately it expects August sales to increase 1.5% year-over-year, also to around 1.5 million for the month. Edmunds.com also had a similar forecast, up 1.3% from August 2016. Through the end of July, U.S. auto sales for 2017 were down 2.9%, to about 9.9 million, according to the Automotive News Data Center. For the month of July, auto sales were down 6.9%, to about 1.4 million.
Ford faces sue over low-quality lug nuts

Ford is being sued from hundreds of Fusion, Escape, Flex, Focus, F-150 and F-350 owners over allegedly defective lug nuts. According to the suit, lugs on millions of vehicles from the aforementioned models can swell and delaminate, making it very difficult to remove the wheels without specialized help and additional expenses. The suit was filed on behalf of the affected owners by law company Hagens Berman earlier this week in the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Michigan. The suit seeks class-action status. The lug nuts in question have a steel core with a chrome, aluminum, or stainless decorative cap.
Hyundai to launch a pickup in the US market to revert sales slide

Hyundai Motor plans to launch a pickup truck in the United States as part of a broader plan to catch up with a shift away from sedans in one of the Korean automaker's most important markets, a senior company executive told Reuters. Michael J. O'Brien, vice president of corporate and product planning at Hyundai's U.S. unit, told the Canadian news outlet that Hyundai's top management has given the green light for development of a pickup truck similar to a show vehicle called the Santa Cruz that U.S. Hyundai executives unveiled in 2015. Hyundai currently does not offer a pickup truck in the United States.
