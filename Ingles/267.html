TILSA adds 40 Kenworth units to its fleet

Tecnica Internacional en Limpieza S.A. (TILSA) acquired a total of 40 Kenworth vehicles, of which it received the first delivery of six 2020 T880 units. The company, whose fleet goes up to about 600 units and has at least seven yards located in strategic zones within the country, bet on the renewal of its tractor units with the most versatile vehicle from Kenworth Mexicana.

Kenworth gives Red Total certification to another shop in Jalisco

Kenworth Jalisco keeps moving forward in its efforts to offer excellent customer service to all customers. In this case it was its Tepatitlan, Jalisco shop that obtained the Red Total certification, which attests to excellence in service of Kenworth shops that meet all requirements. "Red Total is one of its kind and as such, it's our value proposition. It's a sign of our commitment to customers, our distributor network and the region's community," said Ricardo Rizo, general manager of Kenworth Jalisco.
Nissan Versa, almost 10 years of history in Mexico

Nissan shared a recap of some of the key facts of almost a decade of production and sales of the Nissan Versa in Mexico. Nissan Versa is a national production pride that began its manufacturing in 2011 in the Aguscalientes A1 and CIVAC plants. Historically, since the aforementioned year, both plants have produced more than 1.3 million units, from which more than 575,000 have been destined for the domestic market, while more than 786,000 have been destined to the exports market.
Jeep Renegade's limited edition reaches our country

The new 2020 Jeep Renegade makes its arrival to the Mexican market, and it does so by releasing a limited Blue edition, which as it names says, is a special colored edition that will be available in the Latitude and Limited versions. The model offers an exquisite exterior design, reflecting the brand's legacy, but in a vehicle with a more urban hue. In front we can observe the already familiar grill and the round lights, while at the same time, having X shaped rear lights that are the model's trademark.

Light vehicle's retail sales for the January-August period saw a 7.4% increase

Excluding integral buses, the retail sales of heavy vehicles was of 2,954 units in August, 20.1% less when compared to the same period of 2018, according to the report Evolution of the Commercialization of Heavy Vehicles edited by the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). As for accumulated sales from the January-August period, retail distributors sold 27,141 units, 7.4% more when compared to the same period of last year.
Hyundai designs new center side airbag

Despite the fact that the development of new models is focused on the integration of new green technologies, safety is an issue that will never go out of style, which is why this time Hyundai announced a new airbag that will work to separate the existing space between driver and copilot. The brand aims for this new airbag to be integrated progressively to their vehicles.
Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico delivers 100 New Cascadia units

To complement Transportes Refrigerados Galvan's truck fleet which is made up 100% by Freightliner vehicles, the company incorporated more than 100 units of the New Cascadia produced by Daimler Trucks in its Derramadero-Saltillo, Coahuila planta. The trucks acquired by the company headquartered in Zamora de Hidalgo, Michoacan, have a DD13 EUROV engine, as well as the Detroit Assurance and Freightliner Connectivity 2.0.
Chevrolet Onix, proudly produced in San Luis Potosi

The automaker General Motors presented the new Chevrolet Onix, a sedan that it will be produced in its San Luis Potosi. In conference, Francisco Garza, president and CEO of the company in Mexico said that this vehicle is thought mainly for the Mexican market, but that it will also be exported to Central and South America. "We will begin production in San Luis Potosi and it will be released for sale in the first quarter of 2020," he said.
