Bulletin #11
22 December, 2016
BMW reaffirms commitment to build Mexican plant

At the launch of the new MINI Cooper S Countryman, German automotive group BMW reaffirmed its commitment to continue its investments in the country and reiterated the opening of its new plant in San Luis Potosi in 2019. "At the end of the day, in an environment affected by the exchange rate, by any Free Trade Agreement, the road ahead is clear and focused on Mexico", said Alexander W. Wehr, President and CEO for BMW Group Mexico, Latin America and the Caribbean.
Nissan pledges to focus on what's in its control

Nissan Motor has a simple plan to navigate an unprecedented level of uncertainty for the auto industry: focus on what's in its control. "The regulations are not in our control, but what is in our control is that we stay competitive cost-wise, technology-wise, product-wise," Rakesh Kochhar, Nissan's senior vice president of global treasury and sales finance, said at the Bloomberg Asean Business Summit in Hanoi. The executive said he's confident the carmaker will be able to work with any administration in a constructive manner.
Nuevo Leon officials in talks to bring Chinese assembly plant

Nuevo Leon is currently in negotiations with Beijing Automotive International Corporation to install an assembly plant in order to produce cars for both the local and the export market. BAIC is already commercializing two of its models in the national market: a compact car and a SUV.
Sweden-based SKF to supply parts for new Cadillac CT6 from Mexican plant

Swedish bearing manufacturer SKF is supplying General Motors with front and rear wheel hub bearing units for the new Cadillac CT6 sedan from a Mexican plant located in Puebla. The new hub bearing units, which were developed in partnership with GM, use an asymmetric raceway based on SKF's X-Tracker technology, with their geometry contributing to higher corner stiffness.
General Motors CEO will advise Trump on job creation

President-elect Donald Trump appointed General Motors CEO Mary Barra to the Strategic and Policy Forum, a panel of 16 prominent business leaders that will advise him on job creation and economic matters, several U.S. media outlets revealed. Barra will join the CEOs of JPMorgan Chase, Walt Disney Co., Wal-Mart Stores, Boeing and other companies. The group will have its first meeting in February at the White House.
Ford celebrates 30 years building cars at Hermosillo plant

Ford celebrated 30 years since the establishment of its Stamping and Assembly Plant in Hermosillo, Sonora. This facility became the first in Mexico to build luxury vehicles in 2012 when it began production of the 2013 Lincoln MKZ, and it remains the only in the country to produce hybrid vehicles, both the Ford Fusion and Lincoln MKZ.
Fiat Chrysler delays redesign for Ram heavy-duty pickups

Fiat Chrysler will not redesign its current Ram heavy-duty pickups onto its new platform as originally scheduled for 2018. This measure will save money on a changeover of its plant in Saltillo, which is FCA's only heavy-duty pickup factory. Keeping the current truck going with only a freshening will also ensure that Ram heavy-duty pickups remain available to dealers in an uninterrupted supply. In November, Ram pickup sales went up 8.1 percent.
Mexican Class 8 truck market to reach double digit growth for third year in a row

For the third year in a row, the Mexican Class 8 truck market will show double digit growth, beating 2015 sales by 20% if it hits the projected 26,000 units, said Stefan Kurschner, president and CEO of Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico. Forecasting similar growth in 2017, Kurschner said the domestic market here is strong enough to continue growing well beyond 30,000 trucks even following annual sales growth of 12% to 14% over the last three years.
HBPO opens plant in Puebla to supply front-end modules for Audi's Q5

Germany-based HBPO opened a new facility in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla, to support assembly of Audi's Q5 luxury SUV. The 89,000-square-foot facility currently produces front-end modules and center consoles with nearly 70 employees operating on two shifts. The company plans to add a third shift during the first quarter of 2017, creating 50 additional jobs over the next four months.
Plastic Omnium to sale its truck supplying business; one Mexican plant included

Mutares AG made a binding offer to acquire the truck supplying business of the Plastic Omnium Group. creating a world leading truck supplier for cabin components with a total turnover of approximately US$ 425 million and 2,700 employees in 15 plants. The truck supplying business of the Plastic Omnium Group consists of nine plants, five in France, two in China, one plant in Germany and one in Ramos Arizpe, Mexico with more than 1,500 employees in total.
