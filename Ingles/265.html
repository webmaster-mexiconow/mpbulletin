Bulletin #265
September 13th, 2019
Honda Mexico announces production changes

Honda Mexico announced that it will transfer production of the crossover HR-V, which is currently made in the El Salto, Jalisco plant, and concentrate all automobile production on its Celaya, Guanajuato plant, beginning in 2020. The El Salto plant will focus its operations in the production of motorcycles, power products and spare parts. “This decision was taken to optimize the automobile manufacturing structure of Honda Mexico, as well as improving the company’s commercial efficiency,” signaled the company in a press release.
ANPACT reports drop in sales in August

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), informed that, during August, wholesale sales of commercial and heavy vehicles reached a total of 1,668 units, which compared with the 4,301 registered in August of 2018 represents a decrease of 61.2%. During said month, retail sales of commercial and heavy vehicles reached 3,068 units, which compared with the ones sold during the same month of 2018 represent a drop of 19.8%
Car exports drop 12.7% in August

The automotive industry suffered a decline in August, by reporting a drop of 10% in production and 12.7% in exports mainly because of the lower demand of the American market and it’s considered the lowest sales abroad so far this year and not registered since April 2016. According to numbers from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI), Mexico produced 337,462 units in August, which means 35,780 less cars when compared to the same month of 2018, when 373,242 units were produced.
AMDA adjusts light vehicle sales projection to 1.30 million units

The analysts of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) decided to lower the sales projection of light passenger and commercial vehicles for 2019, in such a way that now there’s the projection for the sale of 1.30 million units against the originally expected 1.33 million. Guillermo Rosales Zarate, AMDA’s deputy director general, noted that August represents the 27th month of continuous negative results, and that it’s expected for this trend to continue.
Honda achieves 5.7% market participation in Mexico’s automotive market

According to new vehicles sales numbers from the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA), Honda Mexico confirms its leadership in the SUV segment, where the Honda CR-V keeps first place in accumulated sales. Honda Mexico keeps itself in sixth place amongst the brands that sell their products in the country, with a 5.7% market participation share and keeps looking forward to keep offering its clients the best products.
Mexico State and Nuevo Leon amongst the states that sold more light vehicles

Although automobile sales keep dropping in Mexico, there’s some states that continue boosting this activity. Among them, there’s Nuevo Leon, which places like the third biggest marketer of light vehicles in the country. The most recent data from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) revealed that Mexico City is first place, with 127,291 units sold in the first seven months of the year. Mexico State places second with 81,337 sold units.
Automotive financing exhibits a drop of 8%

Financing for the purchase of cars in Mexico registered a drop of 8% between January and July when compared with the same period of 2018. According to AMDA so far this year there’s been 507,039 units sold through financing. This number was lower by 44,149 units with respect to the January-July 2018 period.
Land Rover hopes to sell 20% more in 2019

The English automotive company Land Rover, hopes to close 2019 with a 20% sales increase in Mexico, number above the increase reported on the first half of this year, which was 15%, explained Raul Peñafiel, the brand’s general director in the country. The directive signaled that they expect this accelerated increase for their van brand because they’ll have a bigger unit volume for the national market.
Navistar Mexico presents developments of its OnCommand platform

Navistar Mexico presented the advances and novelties of its OnCommand platform. Within the novelties presented by this platform there’s a new control panel where customers can see the mileage of every unit and of the entire fleet; security and performance events of every driver; fuel consumption trends, as well as the activity of every unit.
New Mercedes-Benz Vans distributor is inaugurated

Betting on an extremely aggressive expansion strategy, Mercedes-Benz Vans inaugurated its fourth exclusive sales point together with Eurostern. Gonzales Gallo was the chosen location to grow operations in Guadalajara and on a national level. Eurostern has a long history with Mercedes-Benz, it was founded in 1998 and began selling Mercedes-Benz products in Guadalajara. In 2009, it joined the Vans family with the Adolfo Lopez Mateos dealership and 10 years later, it’s opening this new distributor.
