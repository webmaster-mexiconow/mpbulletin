Swedish safety systems manufacturer readies US$120 million expansion in Tijuana

Baja California state authorities announced that a Swedish company, "a world leader in automotive safety components and systems," will invest US$120 million to expand its two manufacturing plants in the border city of Tijuana. Governor Francisco Vega de Lamadrid, said in a statement last week that the company will carry out the expansion through the next four years, a time frame in which more than 200 people will be added to the 3,500-strong workforce already in the company. Although the official omitted the name of the company, it is worth mentioning that Autoliv, the Swedish manufacturer of security systems, specifically air bags, seat belts and steering wheels, has two manufacturing plants in that city. Japanese gasket and seal manufacturer builds US$50 million plant in Yucatan

Uchiyama Group, a Japan-based manufacturer of gasket and seal products for the auto industry, announced in recent days that it has chosen the state of Yucatan, in the Mexican southeast, to establish its first manufacturing plant in the country. According to state authorities, the company estimates an investment of US$50 million over a period of five years to develop its project in order to produce seals and gaskets for dynamic and static engines. Uchiyama will open 200 jobs in order to start operations, but its workforce will continue to grow to up to 1,000 employees when the plant becomes fully operational. More details here: bit.ly/2LGD4m6
Daimler Mexican official confirms production of electric truck by 2021

Flavio Rivera, president and director of Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico, confirmed to the Alto Nivel site that the company is in the preparatory phase for the production of its first electric Class 8 truck in Mexico, which will start in 2021. "In Mexico we are very excited, there's a lot of work ahead in infrastructure for the circulation of these vehicles, such as re-charging stations, regulations and even vehicle maintenance," the official told the business news site in recent days. "This is the new challenge for the industry and Daimler." Daimler recently unveiled the Freightliner eCascadia, a heavy-duty electric truck with a 250-mile range (400 kilometers) whose production is scheduled to begin in Saltillo, Coahuila, by 2021. More details here: bit.ly/2LGDnxg
Manufacturers oppose automotive tariffs at Washington hearings

Representatives from the world's biggest automakers and its suppliers gathered in Washington last Thursday to deliver their remarks at a hearing before the U.S. Commerce Department which is considering to impose tariffs of up to 25% on imported cars and auto parts. The hearings are a required step in the regulatory process described in the Section 232 of the Trade Expansion Act, a law originally intended to protect U.S. capability to produce munitions. Not only manufacturers were among those opposing the trade barriers. A bipartisan group of 150 lawmakers signed a letter urging the administration to drop the plans as bills changing the Trade Expansion Act to require Congressional approval are pending in both houses. More details here: bit.ly/2uX0uwt
Lopez Obrador's chief negotiator to join upcoming NAFTA talks

Marcelo Ebrard, nominated by the next Mexican president to head the Secretariat of Foreign Affairs (SRE), informed that Jesus Seade, chief NAFTA negotiator proposed by Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador (AMLO), will go to Washington, DC. to join the renegotiation of the agreement on July 26. Steelmaker Nucor posts best second quarter ever; US$270 million Mexican plant still in the works

Nucor Corporation announced second quarter of 2018 consolidated net earnings of US$683.2 million, or US$2.13 per diluted share, the highest second quarter earnings in the company's history and above all Wall Street estimates. During the second quarter conference call, CEO John Ferriola said the company is "currently implementing eight exciting growth initiatives, totaling more than US$1.5 billion." Among those projects is a joint venture with Japanese JFE Steel to build in Guanajuato a US$270 million sheet galvanizing plant to support its expansion into Mexico's automotive industry. BMW taps Maru Escobedo, a 19-year experience exec, for CEO job in Mexico

BMW Group Latin America announced that Maru Escobedo has been named new CEO of BMW Group Mexico as of last Thursday. Escobedo will report directly to Alexander W. Wehr, President and CEO of BMW Group Latin America, an area that integrates under his direction the regions of Mexico, Argentina, Brazil and other importing markets. These movements are intended to promote greater integration and strengthening in the region, as well as homologation in all areas. Escobedo has worked at the company for 19 years. She has a degree in Economics from the Universidad Iberoamericana in Mexico City and has a specialty in Economics for Importing and Exporting Markets from the ITAM. More details here: bit.ly/2NA8ifg
Japanese firm acquires Sage Automotive Interiors for US$1.06 billion, including a Mexican plant

Clearlake Capital Group, L.P., a California-based private investment firm, in recent days announced a definitive agreement to sell its global company Sage Automotive Interiors for a cash transaction price of approximately US$700 million plus debt to Asahi Kasei Corporation, a Japanese chemicals and materials manufacturer. Sage is a global supplier of specialty designed, high-performance technical textiles and microfiber suedes for seating, door panel surfaces, and headliners for the automotive industry. The company operates a manufacturing facility in Ecatepec, State of Mexico. Some of the automakers that use Sage Automotive products include, BMW, Jaguar and Mercedes-Benz. Lucid Motors aims to start production of electric vehicles in Arizona by 2020

Lucid Motors recently announced that production of vehicles in Casa Grande, Arizona, will begin in 2020. The two-year timeframe comes from the estimated time and cost it will take to begin producing the Lucid Air model at the Casa Grande facility. In January 2017, the company announced plans to build a manufacturing plant in Casa Grande, supported by a supply chain that would run all the way down to Sonora, Mexico. However, since the announcement, the company has seemed to have a difficult time securing financing. The city was left in limbo for more than 18 months as Lucid sought to secure US$700 million in Series D funding. More details here: bit.ly/2O5S3Yi
Sergio Marchionne is replaced as Fiat Chrysler CEO amid health issues

Sergio Marchionne was replaced last Saturday by Mike Manley, who had been the head of Jeep and RAM brands, as the new CEO of Fiat Chrysler Automobiles amid reports by Italian media that Marchionne was gravely ill in a Swiss hospital. The boards of FCA, Ferrari and CNH Industrial were called to urgent meetings in Italy to find replacements for Marchionne, who holds multiple leadership roles in those companies. Ferrari's board named as new chairman John Elkann, grandson of Gianni Agnelli, former president of Fiat and principal shareholder to his death in 2003. Marchionne had shoulder surgery three weeks ago in Switzerland, but his convalescence is taking longer than expected. The CEO of Fiat since 2004 has long said he planned to step down in 2019.
