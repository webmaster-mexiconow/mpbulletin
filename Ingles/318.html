Automotive production and exports decrease in March

The halt of activities in the industry resulted in a drop of automotive production of 24.6%, by assembling 261,805 light vehicles in Mexico, reported the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). During the first quarter our country assembled 908,393 units, which meant a decrease of 8.6% in respect to the same period of 2019. On the other hand, exports fell 12% during March, by sending 285,075 units abroad, as a reflection in the drop in vehicle demand caused by the coronavirus effect which first damaged the United States.

AMLO's measures aren't enough to face the crisis

Industry leaders from Coparmex, Concamin, Cocanaco, Canacintra and the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) were hoping for President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador to rectify the need for government assistance. Guillermo Rosales, deputy general director of AMDA, described as "incredible" for the Executive to present an emerging program to the reaffirmation of his economic political proposal based on the allocation of public money. And with his message, AMLO is reaffirming his conviction of not recognizing the gravity of the crisis, the worst and deepest one in the last 10 years.

AMIA asks for the USMCA's entry into force to be pushed back to 2021

For the Mexican automotive industry it's not viable to conclude the uniform regulations for the rules of origin of vehicles, auto parts and inputs in a period of 60 to 90 days, since the way the normativity under the United States-Mexico- Canada Agreement (USMCA), and which is expected to entry into force on July 1st, will operate is still unknown, considered the director of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA), Fasto Cuevas. Because of this, the organism is asking for the entry into force of the regulations to be pushed to January 1st, 2021, in order to know and prepare for the upcoming changes.

February sees a decrease in automotive financing and an increase in used vehicle importation

To February 2020, 131,254 new vehicles were sold through the three main credit options in Mexico, 4,698 less when compared to the same month of last year, representing a drop of 3.4%, explained Guillermo Rosales Zarate, deputy director general of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). As for the importation of used units from the United States and Canada to our country, he elaborated that from January to February of this year there's been an internment of 28,803 units, which is to say 3,576 more, which meant an increase of 14.2%.

Car sellers are at risk of losing their jobs

Workers in the automotive sector, but especially those that are involved in the sales of new vehicles, are at risk of experiencing personnel cuts before the drop in new vehicle sales expected for the next few months, as consequence of the health emergency caused by the coronavirus COVID-19. In a press conference the deputy director general of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), Guillermo Rosales, explained that while they will try to maintain job positions, the affectation is imminent.

Heavy vehicle sales drop in March

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), informed that during March, wholesale sales of heavy vehicles reached a total of 2,089 units number which compared to the 3,928 units registered in March 2019, represented a fall of 46.8%. At the same time, during March, retail sales of heavy vehicles reached 2,260 units, which compared to the 3,890 sold ones during the same month of 2019, meant a decrease of 41.9%.

Toyota will restart North America operations on May 4

Toyota announced that due to the perceived scenario, as well as the drop in vehicle demand, the company is expanding the suspension of operations for automotive and component production in all of its North America plants, including Canada, Mexico and the United States. The aforementioned complexes will remain closed until May 1st and will restart production on May 4th, 2020, or at least those are the plans for now since things may change in the blink of an eye.
