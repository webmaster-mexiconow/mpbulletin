Peugeot Mexico releases virtual shop

Peugeot Mexico released its virtual shop meant for the purchase of new vehicles in Mexico, which entails the possibility of asking for a drive test, choosing a vehicle, setting it up and acquiring it, all of this from any device connected to the internet. Regarding the release of the Peugeot e-Store available at www.peugeotstore.com.mx, Gerardo Carmona, Marketing Director of Peugeot Mexico, detailed that this is a tool that will allow customers to acquire a vehicle from their homes. He highlighted that to guarantee their clients' safety in regards to payment transactions, Peugeot e-Store is backed by Mercado Pago.

General Motors of Mexico will gradually restart operations

General Motors of Mexico announced that it will restart operations in its manufacturing complexes in a gradual, programmed and safe manner, starting with the engine and transmission plants in the Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila and Silao, Guanajuaro complexes. This in keeping in line with the recently published government dispositions, where transport equipment manufacturing was included as an essential activity and by having completed the Self-assessment of the Health Security Protocol with a passing result in compliance given by the Mexican Social Security Institute (IMSS).

Automotive plants in the United States halted due to lack of Mexican auto parts

After the Mercedes-Benz and Volvo plants in the United States closed in the last seven days due to lack of auto parts supply, other assembly plants have restarted operations in that country and warned that they too could be in the same situation if supplies from other countries, including Mexico, are not available. In the United States there's already 38 open automotive plants, from companies like General Motors, Ford, Honda, FCA, BMW, Volkswagen, Nissan and Toyota. However, other automobile manufacturers told Reuters that, if Mexico doesn't restart parts manufacturing, they will need to stop their operations.

Sedans gain strength amid pandemic

For many years, the sedan was the family vehicle in Mexico. However, due to the furor for SUVs, vehicle manufacturers focused their resources in expanding their van offer and relegated sedans to the background. Some brands went as far as reducing the offer of this type of vehicle in their sales floors. Now, the coronavirus is tipping the scale back to small vehicles. "I have the theory that towards the second half of the year there will be a boom in smaller vehicle demand, from MXN$200,000 to MXN$400,000," said Jose Roman, president and general director of Nissan Mexicana.

Nuevo Leon's automotive sector invests in health security protocols

Companies from Nuevo Leon's automotive sector are investing around US$4,355 and US$43,554 in health security protocols for the reactivation of the economy after the COVID-19 pandemic, affirmed Manuel Montoya Ortega, general director of the Automotive Cluster of Nuevo Leon (Claut). This spending is not significant for big companies, although it might be costly for a small or medium enterprise, however, companies are aware that this is an investment meant to prevent COVID-19 contagion in plants, he exposed.

Puebla's automotive industry still doesn't have a date to restart operations

Puebla's government released a decree so that the automotive and construction industries don't restart operations on June 1st; these will reopen until further notice, since there's no conditions for their reopening due to the rise in COVID-19 cases in the state. The publication of the decree in the Official Journal of the State of Puebla was carried out on Friday and makes clear that the 159,942 people that work in both sectors, represent a mobility with an exponential risk for infections.

Nissan Mexicana will gradually operate again

Nissan announced that it is preparing to gradually restart its operations under strict security measures to protect the health and well-being of its employees. Nissan has met all of the health protocols necessary to restart operations and will proceed in accordance to authorities. The gradual start will begin with the activities directly related to vehicle and auto parts manufacturing, in specific dates for each installation. In all installations in Mexico City, remote work will continue until further notice.
