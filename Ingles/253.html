July 26th, 2019
Navistar Mexico and Sierra Norte sold Trans-Mex 50 ProStar units

Navistar Mexico reported that they, along with Sierra Norte, have begun the delivery process of 50 ProStar Hi-Rise 73" units to Trans-Mex, a subsidiary of Swift. The units will be used for national and international transport throughout Mexico. This acquisition is part of the company's continuous plan to renew their fleet, which is done every year. Trans-Mex foresees to continue with the acquisition of International units in 2020, like it has done for three years now.
Mexico sees a 42% increase on the production of heavy vehicles

Mexico achieved a historic production of heavy vehicles during the first five months of 2019, by assembling a total of 85,965 units, which represents an increase of 42% in comparison with the same period of 2018; this happened because of a demand in the American market brought about because of the switch to eco-friendly technologies, reported ANPACT. Additionally, the assembly of heavy vehicles from January to May 2019 exceeded the record production, previously achieved by the national industry in 2015 when it manufactured 83,546, by 3%
Manufacturing of transport equipment grew 12.7% in Queretaro

According to data from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI), during the January-May 2019 period the value of manufacturing of transport equipment in Queretaro registered an annual increase of 12.7%, with a total amount of US$1.947 billons. Said growth is more than double in regard to the total growth that the manufacturing industry registered in the state, of 5.7% in that period. In this way, the INEGI states that the subsector represents 25% of the manufacturing industry in Queretaro.
The Beetle Final Edition can be bought through Amazon Mexico

To celebrate Volkswagen Mexico, the company announced that the Beetle Final Edition, which produced its last 65 units exclusively for Mexico, will be available for purchase through Amazon Mexico. This plan, developed for this special occasion, will include a reservation system for those who wish to acquire the vehicle, who will be able to reserve the Beetle Final Edition from the comfort of their own homes and through their cellphone, computer or preferred mobile device, to then be guided through the purchase process by an authorized Volkswagen distributor all the way up to the delivery of the vehicle to their door.
INFINITI names Gabriel Rios Product Marketing Subdirector

INFINITI, the Japanese premium vehicle brand, announced the appointment of Gabriel Rios as Product Marketing subdirector for the Mexico and Latin America markets, effective July 15. Rios will be responsible for marketing for products, prices and the commercial offer for INFINITI distributors for the markets of Mexico, Panama, Chile, Puerto Rico and the Dominican Republic. He will report directly to Philipp Heldt, general director for INFINITI.
Christian Flores is the new delegate for CANACAR in CEDA

Christian Valentin Flores Garcia, assumed the delegate position for the National Trucking Chamber (CANACAR) as representative of this organism in the Central Market of Mexico City (CEDA), thus replacing Gelasio Flores Padron. In his swearing in, he ratified the compromise as propeller of the sector's development through joint action, work schedules and support for union members so that they can keep growing and to strengthen the auto transport sector, as outlined by CANACAR's guidelines.
Despite a drop in sales, Versa stays in first place

Despite a drop in vehicle sales in the Mexican market, Nissan Versa asserts itself as the favorite vehicle of Mexican consumers, by selling 40,777 units during the first semester of 2019, with which it holds 17% of sales of the top 10 selling models in the country. According to AMDA, the top 10 selling models in the Mexican market accumulated a total of 241,754 units sold during the January-June 2019 period, which means a 37.8% coverage of the total sales for light vehicles in the reference period, in which 638,597 cars were sold.
VW will invest US$600 million in Puebla for the production of its new model, Tarek

The assembling of Volkswagen's new SUV model will begin in the second quarter of 2020 in Volkswagen's Puebla plant, for which a US$600 million investment is expected. The minister of the Ministry of Competitiveness, Labor and Economic Development (Secotrade), Jaime Oropeza Casas, confirmed that the Tarek LWB project is foreseen to be exported to North America. Months ago the plant began making technical adjustments and workforce adjustments for the assembling of the new model, of which 700 cars will be produced daily.
Amports reopens car yard in Altamira

American Company Amports reopened their terminal dedicated to the movement of cars, after it obtained a partial rights assignment at the beginning of 2018 in the port area. During an event with authorities, clients and assignees of Altamira port, Ernesto Ibarra, director of Amports Mexico, said that because of this partial rights assignment, which has a duration of 20 years with the possibility of an extension for 20 more years, there is a legal certainty that would allow Amports to invest US$10 million in infrastructure and equipment to better provide care to the automotive industry, providing storage and other added value services.
Traxión sees its operational flow grow 70% during the first semester

By the closing time of the second quarter of the current year, Traxión grew their operational flow (EBITDA) 50.8% by adding US$33 million; meanwhile their income reached US$161 million, an increase of 37.2%, both of them compared to earnings in the same period of 2018. In the quarterly report presented to the Mexican Stock Exchange (BMV), the transport company disclosed that the increase in their consolidated EBITDA happened because of an increase in consolidated income, cost-expenses efficiency, their successful synergy plan and internal improvements in each of their seven subsidiaries.
