Estafeta adds 49 Cascadia units to its fleet

Daimler Commercial Vehicles delivered 49 new 2020 Cascadia units to Estafeta. The new units, which represented an investment of MXN$174 million, are added to the 515 Cascadia units that already form part of Estafeta's fleet, which means Freightliner has an 87% participation in the company's tractor truck fleet. Ingo Babrikowski, general director of Estafeta said that, "with the acquisition of units offered by Freightliner, the company will keep bringing people together and connecting businesses in the country with high levels of efficiency and security for operators, clients, their parcels and the community."

Baja California approves registry and circulation of used imported vehicles

The implementation "of the necessary actions to achieve full identification, location and relation with the owner of vehicles from foreign origin that are within the state, complementing and strengthening vehicular control", was approved by the twenty-third Legislature of Baja California. For this purpose there will be a Census for Vehicular Identity to register used imported vehicles, as well as owners and drivers. Said census, will have a cost of at least MXN$1,000 per unit and interested physical persons, will present documents that legally prove their property, as well as a valid driver's license issued by the state.

Hoffman Group Invests US$12.7 million in Puebla

Hoffman Group consolidated in Puebla an investment of US$12.7 million with the operation of a new corporate office which will allow to meet machinery supply for the country's automotive and aerospace industry. The company, which generates 54 direct jobs in Puebla, supplies from tool 24 to video microscopes for measuring to a thousand active clients in Mexico like Volkswagen, Audi, BMW, Daimler, Toyota, Continental, Benteler, Goodyear, Thyssenkrupp.

Queretaro's automotive industry hasn't been affected by the coronavirus yet

The spread of the coronavirus will generate an impact in the commercial relationship that links the productive base installed in Queretaro with Chinese brands. The state's automotive industry hasn't suffered strong affectations, due to the fact that local production plants still have supply inventory-which usually comes from China-to give support to their production lines, referred the head of the Ministry of Sustainable Development (Sedesu) in the state, Marco Antonio Del Prete Tercero.

Navistar delivers 10 LT International units to Transportadora de Líquidos Azteca

Navistar informed that Transportadora de Líquidos Azteca acquired 10 LT International units to expand and renew their fleet. The new units will be circulating throughout the country and will be dedicated to mainly transporting edible vegetable oil. The LT International units produced for Transportadora de Líquidos Azteca have special characteristics, for example, an APU Kompressor to keep the use of idle speed low and give the operator major comfort, as well as a fifth wheel from Holland which makes the transport of liquids safer.

Leoni's new plant in Cuauhtémoc is inaugurated

Six months after the placing of the first stone, the governor of Chihuahua, Javier Corral Jurado, inaugurated the new Leoni Plant Emomex Project, located in the Cuauhtémoc Industrial Park, which was built with an investment of US$27 million and will create 123 direct jobs. This industrial plant will produce high voltage charging cables for electric and hybrid vehicles like Tesla, GM, Ford, among others. This new building is located in front of the first Cuauhtémoc Leoni Plant.
