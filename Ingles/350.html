January to May automotive exports in Nuevo Leon reach US$4.1 billion

Automotive sector exports in Nuevo Leon fell 34% from January to May of 2020, compared to the same period of last year, they went from US$6.7 billion to US$4.1 billion, according to Nuevo Leon's economic indicators, from the state's Ministry of Labor and Economy (Sedet). The state's total exports fell 19% from January to March, in relation to the same period of last year from US$17.4 billion to US$14.2 billion.

Automotive financing sees a decrease of 26.2% during the year's first semester

The Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) informed that during the year's first semester a total of 319,099 vehicles (new and used) were acquired through financing, a number that's 26.2% less to that registered in the same period of last year. Of these 319,099 units, 271,034 were new units, although this also meant an annual fall of 28.57%, according to AMDA's Report on total light vehicle financing.

Mexico has potential for the utilization of autonomous vehicles

The 2020 Autonomous Vehicle Readiness Index (AVRI), belonging to consulting firm KPMG International, revealed that Mexico holds the 28th place among 30 countries evaluated in their readiness for autonomous vehicles. In the 2019 ranking Mexico placed 23rd among 25 evaluated nations. "Mexico has the potential for the development of the conditions that would boost the utilization of autonomous vehicles due to its closeness to the United States. Sooner or later we will be on par with this country, since we share a border and are one of its main commercial allies," said Ignacio Garcia de Presno, Leading Infrastructure Partner of KPMG in Mexico.

MAN Truck & Bus Mexico presents MAN | Service Telematics platform

With the new generation of the MAN | Service Telematics platform recently launched by MAN Truck & Bus Mexico for fleet administration, transport companies will be able to monitor fuel consumption, identifying fault codes, locating their units through GPS, among others. Auto parts companies face local closures due to union decisions

After two months of suspension of activities in the automotive sector and its supply chain in Mexico, assembly and auto parts plants in the country are now working at 90% of their capacity on average. However, for Patrick Koller, general director of auto parts company Faurecia, there's still a lot of risks and problems in the sector. In conference with analysts, he warned that in Mexico they're facing local closures due to union decisions, who are still worried for the protection of its members in light of the coronavirus pandemic.
