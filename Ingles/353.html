Vehicle production grows in July, while exports fall

Mexico's automotive industry had a respite in July and light vehicle production managed to surpass 2019 levels by obtaining a slight growth of 0.65% when compared to the same month of 2019, with which it recovered numbers previous to the COVID-19 pandemic, although exports showed a slight decrease of 5.4% in said month. According to numbers from INEGI, the automotive sector assembled 294,946 units during the year's seventh month, which is 1,911 more vehicles when compared to July of 2019.

Honda commercializes 3,576 units

As economic and productive activities have restarted both in Mexico and North America, Honda of Mexico has an improvement in sales within the country. According to data from INEGI and AMIA and AMDA, Honda remains as the brand with the most sales in the passenger van segment within the national market reaching 14,050 units in the January-July period. During July, Honda of Mexico commercialized 3,576 units, which meant a growth of 31.4% against sales from June, to reach a total of 25,330 commercialized units during the first seven months of 2020.

AMDA adjusts its sales estimate to a fall of 26.8%

Before a better dynamic in car sales in Mexico during June and July, the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) updated its sales estimate for 2020, expecting only a fall of 26.8%, variation which is smaller than the 30% one set on April. "The sale of 964,400 units is expected for this year, number which is greater than the 922,000 units expected previously, this adjustment is a result of the behavior observed during June and July, which is better than expected," said Guillermo Rosales, deputy director general of AMDA.

The Ford Fusion bids farewell to the production lines

Ford's new product strategy has left no place for sedans. In the last few years, the Fusion had remained in production only awaiting a new SUV to take its place in the plant and range. With the presentation of the Bronco Sport, the Fusion's days are numbered. Ford's original plan was to stop making the Fusion on July 21st, but due to a slight adjustment this date was extended to July 31st, day in which the last unit left the production line in Hermosillo, Sonora.

Despite industry efforts automotive industry jobs are lost due to the crisis

Despite efforts to keep jobs during the crisis caused by COVID-19, the automotive industry lost 5,000 registered jobs in auto dealerships, which represents 4% of the workforce in the retail commerce rubric, informed Guillermo Rosales, director of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). According to numbers from INA, the sector generates around 800,000 direct jobs, so with a base in the estimation of 10% the number of lost jobs would be of 80,000.
