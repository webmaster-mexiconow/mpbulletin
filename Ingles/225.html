Mexico auto production declines 5% in February, INEGI data shows

Global automakers with assembly plants operating in Mexico built 311,833 cars and light trucks in February, which resulted in a 5% decrease compared to the 328,352 units from the same month of 2018, according to official data. Total production figures for the first two months of 2019 was of 645,510 vehicles, still a 2.1% gain compared to the 632,107 units built during the same period of last year. It's worth noting that the data from INEGI does not include output figures from Mercedes-Benz, who has been churning out A-Class sedans since the third quarter of 2018 at the Cooperation Manufacturing Plant Aguascalientes. Auto exports slow down in February, according to official data

Automakers with manufacturing operations in Mexico exported 271,075 light vehicles in February, according to official data from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This figure represents a marginal decrease of 0.1% compared to the 271,228 units shipped in the same month of 2018. Total exports for the first two months of 2019 were of 513,374 units, which is still a 2.2% gain compared to the 502,316 vehicles from the same period of last year. During the first two months of 2019, Mexico auto exports to the United States, its main destination, were of 403,172 vehicles, representing a 7.6% increase. More details here: bit.ly/2ITEkFs
Auto sales in Mexico drop to 2015 levels during February

Auto dealers in Mexico sold during February a total of 103,679 light vehicles, which means a decrease of 5.5% over the same month a year earlier, reported the INEGI. It is the lowest sales figure in Mexico since May 2015, when 102,156 units were marketed. Year-to-date sales in the first two months of the year are of 214,891 units and represent a 1.9% drop. In this regard, the deputy general director of the Mexican Association of Auto Dealers (AMDA), Guillermo Rosales, said the result is attributable to "the slowdown in economic activity since December and the uncertainty of investors regarding the prospects of the Mexican economy," among other factors. US auto sales decline 2.9% in February due in part to severe weather

Auto sales in the U.S. market declined in February primarily due to severe weather that kept consumers away from dealerships in the Northeast region. According to AutoNews, global automakers sold 1,268,571 cars and light trucks last month, a 2.9% decline compared to 1,306,210 units from the same month of 2018. Total sales in the first two months of 2019 were an estimated of 2,401,843 vehicles, 2.6% less than the 2,465,328 units sold over the same period from last year. The seasonally adjusted, annualized sales rate (SAARS) was of 16.61 million, marking the second straight month below 17 million. More details here: bit.ly/2H1bWiQ
Canadian auto sales drop 3.7% marking the 12th month of declines

Last month, auto sales in Canada dropped 3.7% to 120,891 cars and light trucks, down from 125,530 units in February 2018, according to data from DesRosiers Automotive Consultants. This is the 12th month of sales declines from the previous year. The trend away from the sale of passenger cars continued last month with auto sales down 16% for the month while light trucks demand rose 1.2%. Fiat Chrysler had the best sales with 18,461 autos sold but still represented a 2% drop from a year earlier. Ford came in second but with just a 1.4% decline, and GM managed third but with a 9.1% drop from 2018. European luxury brands Audi, Jaguar, Maserati, and Mercedes all posted more than 20% drops.
Japanese manufacturer Sango builds US$17-million plant in Guanajuato

Japanese manufacturer Sango, through its U.S.-based subsidiary Arvin Sango, recently broke ground at the municipality of Valle de Santiago, Guanajuato to build a production facility. The company plans to allocate an initial investment of US$17 million at the 12,000-sq.-mt. plant. An estimated 123 job positions for highly-qualified individuals will be opened in order to start operations. The site will focus in the manufacturing of exhaust systems for Toyota and other customers. The plant was initially slated to supply manifolds and mufflers for the Corolla, but now it will work on the Tacoma program instead, as plans for the Toyota assembly plant in Guanajuato were changed. Japan-based Nitta Corp. expands output capacity at San Luis Potosi plant

Nitta Corporation, a Japanese multi-sector manufacturer that operates in San Luis Potosi since 2007, recently inaugurated the second phase of its expansion plan at the production facility located in the municipality of Villa de Reyes. According to company officials, new assembly and extrusion lines were added in order to increase 30% of its output capacity for piping assemblies for fuel systems. A new dining area, doctor office, dressing rooms and a clean room were also added to the plant's footprint, now expanded to 7,487 square meters. A third expansion phase is already in the works aimed to add electric furnaces and more equipment for pneumatic assemblies. No investment figures were revealed. More details here: bit.ly/2TppICL
California manufacturer Compass Made opens production facility in Chihuahua

Compass Made, a contract manufacturer headquartered in Fremont, California, has officially opened a manufacturing site for fuel injection harnesses in the town of Puerto Palomas de Villa, Chihuahua, just across the border from Columbus, New Mexico. According to a statement, the company plans to invest up to US$80 million and open 300 job positions in the long term. "We are beginning with one production line for fuel injection harnesses and our plans are to increase in the long-term our workforce up to 300 workers," said Salvador Moreno, plant manager. Compass Made has additional manufacturing facilities in Deming, New Mexico, two facilities in Guadalajara and now in Palomas, Mexico. More details here: bit.ly/2TsZr5x
Indorama acquires German airbag fabric manufacturer UTT; deal includes a plant in Puebla

Indorama Ventures (IVL), a Thailand-based global chemical producer, announced it has completed the acquisition of UTT Beteiligungsgesellschaft mbH (UTT), one of the leading suppliers of airbag fabrics, with manufacturing sites in Germany and Mexico and a combined capacity to produce around 70 million square meters of fabrics per year. UTT also produces other highly specialized solutions in the field of technical textiles. The UTT Mexican facility started operations in 2011 at the municipality of Huejotzingo, Puebla, although it was officially inaugurated in January 2012. The facility supplies fabric for airbags for European, Central, and North American markets. More details here: bit.ly/2NUkB7H
Michigan-based firm RJG Inc. to open injection molding training facility in Queretaro

Michigan-based injection molding training and consulting firm RJG Inc. announced is opening its first training facility in Mexico in April, in partnership with auto parts molder Yanfeng Global Automotive Interiors (YFAI). The joint training facility is located at Yanfeng's manufacturing plant in Queretaro. "We are very excited to bring our world-class training to the Mexico area for our current and new customers. For the first time, we will be able to hold a variety of classes in Mexico that were not previously available," said Scott Van Hoven, RJG's product commercial consultant.
