16 March, 2017
Trump announces challenge to Obama-era fuel standards

President Donald Trump announced that his administration will re-examine federal requirements governing the fuel efficiency of cars and trucks, regulations that he says are stifling economic growth. The EPA under Obama promulgated a rule requiring a fleet-wide average of 36 mpg by 2025. Trump's decision has no immediate effect, but requires the EPA to determine no later than April 2018 whether the current standards are appropriate. If the EPA determines they are not, the agency will submit a new proposal next year.
Cities, defying Trump, seek to gather US$ 10 billion order for EVs

Dozens of U.S. cities are willing to buy US$ 10 billion of electric cars and trucks to show skeptical automakers there's demand for low-emission vehicles. Thirty cities including New York and Chicago jointly asked automakers for the cost and feasibility of providing 114,000 electric vehicles, including police cruisers, street sweepers and trash haulers, said Los Angeles Mayor Eric Garcetti, who is coordinating the effort. That would be comparable to about 72% of total U.S. plug-in sales last year.
Hybrid-electric car sales skyrocketed 448.4% in Mexico during 2016

Last year, 8,260 hybrid or electric vehicles were sold in Mexico, a 448.4% increase from 1,842 units in 2015, according to data from the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA). A total of 7,989 vehicles sold were hybrids, while just 271 featured full electric powertrains. The Toyota Prius was the best-selling hybrid car in the country due in great part to a stimulus program for taxi drivers in Mexico City. Nissan Leaf took the lead in the electric cars segment.
VW pleads guilty to charges in U.S. diesel cheating scandal

Volkswagen pleaded guilty to three criminal charges for rigging 590,000 diesel-powered vehicles in the United States to cheat on diesel emissions tests. The judge in the case has set an April 21 sentencing date in Detroit's U.S. District Court. Meanwhile, VW is proceeding with its agreed US$ 22 billion settlement that features compensating U.S. owners of more than 500,000 diesel vehicles with buybacks and cash.
BP opens first gas station in Mexico; plans 1,500 retail sites in 5 years

BP officially opened last week its first retail fuels site in Mexico as part of its plan to invest in the growing Mexican retail fuel and convenience market over the next five years. The new BP retail site located in Mexico City is the first global oil company branded site operating since the country approved deregulation of the fuel retailing market in 2013. BP plans to open around 1,500 retail sites in Mexico over the next five years, 200 of those in 2017, including both dealer and company owned-and-operated.
Trump to CEOs: build modern plants like you've built in Mexico

President Donald Trump pushed domestic and foreign auto executives for more jobs and new manufacturing plants in the U.S. During a roundtable with industry officials, Trump repeatedly stressed to the CEOs and senior leaders of 11 American, German, Japanese and South Korean automakers, three suppliers and the United Auto Workers President that he wants to see automakers build "modern plants, like you're building in Mexico."
San Luis Potosi officials dismiss Mitsubishi plant rumors

San Luis Potosi government officials dismissed speculations about a deal with Japanese automaker Mitsubishi to build a plant on the grounds Ford Motor Company planned its facility, a project the Detroit-based company cancelled earlier this year. Gustavo Puente Orozco, State's secretary of Economic Development, said in an interview he's been on talks with several auto suppliers and carmakers, specially from China, seeking to invest in San Luis Potosi, but Mitsubishi hasn't showed any interest.
Gentherm opens US$ 15 million plant in Guanajuato

Gentherm Corporation inaugurated their new plant located at the Amistad Sur Industrial Park, in Celaya, Guanajuato, after a US$ 15.7 million investment and expecting to generate 1,500 jobs during its first stage. The Michigan-based company develops and manufactures temperature management technologies for central heat systems, refrigeration and temperature control. This new 140,000 square-feet facility is Gentherm third production site in Mexico. The company owns two plants in the border city of Acuna, Coahuila.
Samsung completes acquisition of Harman Industries, three Mexican plants included

Samsung Electronics Co., Ltd. announced it has completed the previously announced acquisition of Harman International Industries, Incorporated. The transaction will immediately give Samsung a significant presence in the large and rapidly growing market for connected technologies, particularly automotive electronics, which has been a strategic priority for Samsung, and is expected to grow to more than US$ 100 billion by 2025. Harman operates three Mexican plants located in Tijuana, Ciudad Juarez and Queretaro.
Bosch announces Artificial Intelligence self-driving computer with NVIDIA

Bosch announced it is working with NVIDIA to develop artificial intelligence self-driving systems for mass market cars. The car computer built on NVIDIA's deep learning software and hardware will enable vehicles to be trained on the complexities of driving, operated autonomously and updated over the air with new features and capabilities. "Using DRIVE PX AI car computer, Bosch will build automotive-grade systems for the mass production of autonomous cars," said Jen-Hsun Huang, founder and CEO, NVIDIA.
