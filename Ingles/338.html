Audi Mexico trains its personnel for the return to operations

Audi Mexico has as its number one priority the health and safety of the more than 5,200 collaborators that contribute to the proper operation of the plant. The entry into the New Normality for Audi Mexico will be carried out with a maximum of 30% of the workforce (which is to say about 1,560 people). First they will be trained with the goal of disclosing all the established guidelines given by the health authorities in hope of avoiding contagion. Afterwards, the workforce will prepare and carry out the necessary activities in the plant's installations to restart production.

Cleaning activities in distributors have become important for clients

According to the seventh wave of J.D. Power's study "Trust of the Automotive Consumer in Mexico (CCAM)" during the contingency caused by the COVID-19, in which the results and trends of its weekly measurement are listed, 2 out of every 5 people are now focusing in the cleaning activities in distributors, over credit extensions for the first time. Gerardo Gomez, general director of J.D. Power Mexico, said that, a relevant point in this 7th wave was that, 34% of those surveyed expect for everything to remain the same in their family situation.

AMDA and Jato Dynamics collaborate on app to sell vehicles online

The Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), in collaboration with Jato Dynamics Mexico will make available without cost the access and use of the app FLEX MX, a tool focused on carrying out digital vehicle sales in the country. FLEX MX pretends to boost car sales, with a wide range of model and brand options to capture costumers' preference, with which they aspire to sell at least 10 units per month additional to those being sold through traditional means.

The reactivation of Puebla's automotive sector could begin on June 22: Canacintra

The National Chamber of Industry (Canacintra) in Puebla considered that a week would be ideal to postpone the reactivation of the automotive sector and that after June 22 this could be gradual, which is why 40 of 130 suppliers have the possibility of starting activities at this date, since they have to meet contracts. Gabriel Covarrubias Lomelí, president of the organism, said that the industries in general have complied with the measures from authorities, both federal and local, due to the rise in COVID-19 cases, but there has to be a deadline to restart operations.

Claugto will support companies in the state with "Claugto's Business Ecosystem" initiative

Automotive companies adjust their processes to meet USMCA

After two months of inactivity, vehicle manufacturers are trying to recover lost time. Not only to restart model production to restock sales floors in North America, but also to adjust their processes to meet with the new origin rule in the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA). Both vehicle and auto parts manufacturers, the latter which didn't previously have to demonstrate a minimum regional content, will have to slowly raise regional content until they reach, in some cases 75%.

Vehicle production in Mexico won't recover until 2024: Deloitte

Consulting firm Deloitte estimates that Mexico will recover the registered vehicle production seen in 2019 of 3.81 million vehicles until 2024 due to the sales reduction in the United States market caused by the industry's impact brought about by the COVID-19 pandemic. In their study "Automotive Industry Landscape," Deloitte referred that the health emergency accelerated the deterioration of vehicle sales worldwide and interrupted production and automotive industry supply chains.
