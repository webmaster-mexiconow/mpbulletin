Five Mexico-made vehicles make the KBB's 10 Best Back-to-School Cars of 2017 list

Automotive research company Kelley Blue Book (KBB), one of the most recognized firms by both consumers and the auto industry, highlighted five Mexico-made vehicles in its 10 Best Back-to-School Cars for 2017 list. The ranking considers models that are practical, affordable and safe, but also fun and good-looking. Number one on the list for the third year in a row is the Honda Fit. The fourth model on the list is the HR-V, also from Honda. On the fifth position KBB places the Mazda 3 sedan, a car that topped this year's list of KBB's 10 Coolest Cars Under US$ 18,000. Number six on the list is the Nissan Versa Note and the Chevrolet Trax is the last car of the 10-position ranking.
India-based automotive supplier Sakthi to open US$ 33 million plant in Durango

India-based automotive supplier Sakthi plans to open a production site in Durango to manufacture aluminum components for braking and suspension systems, Ramon Davila Flores, state Secretary of Economic Development, announced in a press conference. The company will invest US$ 33 million to set up operations at the Durango Logistic and Industrial Center. Sakthi expects to create 600 jobs, 200 of them will be high-paying positions, said the official. Davila Flores pointed out that 20 engineers have been recruited already and sent to Michigan for training in order to work on the installation process. A breaking ground ceremony will take place coming October first.
Autoliv, Seeing Machines join efforts to develop driver monitoring systems

Sweden-based Autoliv Inc., a manufacturer of automotive safety systems, announced a deal with Seeing Machines, a leader in computer vision based human sensing technologies, to develop next generation driver monitoring systems (DMS) for autonomous vehicles. Both companies plan to develop a state-of-the-art DMS that can detect distracted and drowsy drivers by accurately measuring eye and head position, driver attention and fatigue. The DMS will invoke action when a dangerous situation is detected or imminent. Autoliv operates five production sites in Mexico dedicated to manufacture of steering wheels, airbags, seat belts and electronic components.
Swiss packaging solutions supplier Utz Group opens plant in Guanajuato

Swiss packaging solutions manufacturer Utz Group officially opened its production facility in San Miguel de Allende, Guanajuato. The company invested US$ 16 million and recruited 60 workers to start operations. Utz Group develops and manufactures sustainable reusable plastic products using thermoforming and injection molding processes. Even though the company supplies packaging products for a wide variety of industries, its Mexican facility will focus on the automotive sector. Some of its main automotive customers are Audi, Volkswagen, BMW, Bosch, Brose, Hella and Continental.

Lincoln MKZ wins AutoPacific's 2017 Ideal Vehicle Award for the fourth year in a row

Automotive research and consulting firm AutoPacific, Inc. announced the 2017 Ideal Vehicle Awards (IVA), recognizing cars that best meet their owner's expectations and desires. The list includes the Hermosillo-made Lincoln MKZ which outscored the Lexus ES to top the Luxury Mid-Size Car segment for the fourth year in a row. The awards are based on responses from more than 54,000 owners of new vehicles across all major manufacturers. Respondents were asked what changes they would make to their car or truck in 15 key categories, including ride and handling, interior storage, safety features, power and acceleration and technology.
Japan-based cold forging manufacturer Minamida breaks ground in Guanajuato

Japan-based cold forging manufacturer Minamida broke ground in recent days at the Leon-Bajio Industrial Park (PILBA) in Leon, Guanajuato to build a production site. The company plans to invest US$ 1.5 million and hire 30 workers in order to start operations in April 2018, then eventually it will add a US$ 4.5 million investment to become fully operational with a total 180-strong workforce. Minamida is the fourth company to break ground at PILBA, which is the industrial park where global tiremaker Michelin builds a US$ 510 million plant. At the ceremony site, the word was that Minamida will supply bolts, nuts, screws and other cold forging supplies to Mazda.
Delphi teams up with Israel-based Innoviz to expand self-driving technologies

Delphi Automotive PLC announced it has signed a commercial partnership agreement with Innoviz Technologies, a leading Israeli-based company developing LiDAR technology for the mass commercialization of autonomous vehicles. LiDAR, which stands for Light Detection and Ranging, is a remote sensing method that uses light in the form of a pulsed laser to measure variable distances. Innoviz's proprietary LiDAR sensing solutions will be integrated into Delphi's systems to offer a more comprehensive portfolio of self-driving technologies. Innoviz LiDAR technology provides longer-range scanning performance and superior object detection and accuracy.
WABCO wins its largest braking system order from major truck and buses manufacturer

WABCO Holdings Inc., a global supplier of technologies for commercial vehicles, announced that it has entered into a new long-term agreement with a major global manufacturer of trucks and buses, which is headquartered in Europe. WABCO will supply the customer's vehicle brands with its breakthrough modular braking system platform (mBSP). This major contract represents WABCO's single largest order ever for supply of mBSP, a technology that enables vehicle makers to flexibly equip their global truck and bus platforms with either anti-lock braking (ABS) or electronic braking systems (EBS) anywhere in the world, saving development and production resources.
China's Great Wall confirms interest in acquiring FCA's Jeep brand

According to Automotive News, Great Wall Motor's President, Wang Fengying, plans to contact Fiat Chrysler Automobiles to discuss acquiring the Jeep brand. "With respect to this case, we currently have an intention to acquire. We are interested in (FCA)," an official at Great Wall Motor's press relations department told Reuters. He declined to give his name and gave no further details. Two people familiar with the matter said Great Wall Motor had asked for a meeting with FCA to make an offer for all or part of the group. FCA manufactures the Jeep Compass SUV at the Toluca Assembly Plant, where it also builds the Dodge Journey and the Fiat 500.
