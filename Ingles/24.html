7 February, 2017
Chinese automaker joins Carlos Slim to build SUVs in Mexico

Mexico's Giant Motors, an auto manufacturer partially owned by Carlos Slim, and China's Anhui Jianghuai Automobile (JAC Motor) unveiled plans to invest over US$ 212.4 million in a Mexican car plant. The factory will begin with a capacity of 11,000 vehicles annually, with output ultimately rising to 40,000. The initial phase of the project is expected to create 1,000 jobs. JAC Motor's SUV S2 and SUV S3 would be the first vehicles to be assembled, but the facility may also produce electric cars.
Mercedes-Benz efforts to build cars in Mexico remain on schedule: CEO

Even if the joint effort between Daimler and Nissan to build cars over a shared platform is still under review, the US$ 1 billion plant they build together in Aguascalientes, Mexico, is scheduled to start rolling out Nissan's Infiniti models this year and Mercedes-Benz compact vehicles will follow in 2018, said CEO Dieter Zetsche. "I believe that NAFTA will continue to exist," Zetsche told reporters after his year results conference. Daimler makes almost a third of its revenue in the NAFTA region.
Auto sales slowed in January over U.S. market; improvement expected

U.S. auto sales slowed in January, but analysts remain confident that low gas prices and tax refunds will increase demand for new vehicles in the coming months. January sales fell 1.8 percent to 1.14 million vehicles, according to Autodata. General Motors, Toyota, Fiat Chrysler and Ford all reported declines, while Honda, Nissan, Hyundai and Volkswagen saw sales gains.
Mexican car sales increased during January

In Mexico, light vehicle sales totaled 123,260 units last month, a 3% hike compared to January 2016, according to the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA). Nissan remains the biggest seller over the Mexican market with a 23.4% share, down from 25% reported last December. Volkswagen and GM followed with 15.8% and 14.9% shares, respectively. FCA ended with 7.8%, followed by Honda (6.9%), Toyota (6.5%), Ford (6.3%), Kia (4.7%), Mazda (3.9%) and Hyundai (2.6%).
Canadian auto dealers achieve all-time record sales for January

Canadian car and light truck sales rose 2.2 percent in January over last year to an all-time record for the month, DesRosiers Automotive Consultants said. Total sales in Canada climbed to 110,945 light vehicles in January, up from 108,572 in the same month last year, DesRosiers said, beating the previous record-setting January sales of 110,266 vehicles in 2002.
FCA's Saltillo facility builds its 3 millionth Pentastar V-6 engine

Six years after FCA Mexico's Engine Plant 2 started operations in Saltillo, Coahuila, the 3 millionth Pentastar V-6 rolled out of the production site. The Saltillo facility produces three different versions of the Pentastar. Since first introduced, the engine has replaced several six cylinder models ranging from 2.7 to 4.0 liters all over the Fiat Chrysler Automobiles portfolio.
GM's Bolt EV to enter Mexican market in Summer

General Motors will introduce the electric car Bolt EV in the Mexican market on second half of 2017. It will be GM's third fully-electric model to enter since the Spark EV and Volt are already sold in the country. The Bolt EV will feature 383 kilometers (238 miles) autonomy, long enough to drive from Mexico City to Acapulco, said the company.
GM will add down time at Lordstown, Ohio, plant

Despite a recent jump in Chevrolet Cruze sales, General Motors is adding several weeks of down time to the Lordstown, Ohio, plant's 2017 schedule "to align production with market demand," the automaker said. GM sold 19,949 Cruzes sold in the U.S. last month compared with 14,362 in January 2016, a 38.9% increase.
Bosch agrees to pay US$ 327 million in dieselgate-related settlement

German automotive supplier Bosch entered into a US$ 327.5 million settlement agreement with civil suit claimants in the U.S. relating to the company's involvement in the Volkswagen diesel emissions fraud scandal. Bosch "neither acknowledges the facts as alleged by the plaintiffs nor does Bosch accept any liability" with its entrance into the settlement agreement, said the manufacturer.
Ternium Mexico develops dual phase steel for automotive industry

Mexican steelmaker Ternium Mexico has developed a dual phase steel at its Churubusco mill in Monterrey, with the goal of supplying the automotive industry. According to Ternium, the new steel will have the ability to make autos lighter and safer by combining two different phases or microstructures, the company said in a press release.
