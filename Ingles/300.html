GM has a positive outlook for 2020

General Motors Mexico sees 2020 as a positive and promising year: it's expecting a turning point towards the growth of domestic sales and says that it's prepared to meet the stricter rules marked by the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA). The president and general director of General Motors Mexico, Francisco Garza, assures that with USMCA's entry into force, Mexico strengthens itself and remains attractive for the arrival of new automotive suppliers.

Kia Motors Mexico reiterated its commitment to Conalep

Kia Motors México maintains its commitment to the development of the country and Mexican youth, because of this, it's strengthening the agreement with Colegio Nacional de Educación Profesional Técnica (Conalep) to benefit students and alumni of this institution. On top of the efforts made since the beginning of this agreement in 2018, Kia will support Conalep with two Kia Soul units and tools that will aid students in their studies, by getting acquainted with the brand's advanced technology.

Renault opens new dealership in Baja California Sur

Due to the sales increase of 18.4% that Renault achieved in 2019, the brand is expanding its presence in the country with the opening of a new dealership in Los Cabos. The French automaker now has 65 dealerships in its distributor network. The Renault Los Cabos dealership is located in a 1,850 square meters lot with construction of 1,134 square meters and will have a showroom where the clients will be able to look at the most recent models from the French brand, a service center, an after-sale service space as well as a used car space.

DiDi will add electric and hybrid vehicles to its fleet

DiDi, the Chinese mobility firm, will add 200 electric and hybrid vehicles to its Mexico operations. With these units, the company is creating its first fleet of this type in Latin America. Juan Andres Panama, director of operations for DiDi Mexico said that these vehicles will be added to their current fleet in partnership with Drive the future and Engie with the models 2020 Renault Zero and BYD's e5. Panama added that traveling in one of these vehicles will have the same cost as riding in a conventional vehicle.

Ford Technology Center: the opportunity to live the Ford experience

Ford recently inaugurated the Ford Technology Center initiative, which consists of a technology center that will be located at several points within the country and it hopes to allow users to live the technological experience they would get from a Ford vehicle. Some of the technologies the customer can get to know are FordPass, FordPass Connect and Co-Pilot360, which are available in some of the brand's models.

Lamborghini says it will not produce vehicles in Hidalgo

Automobili Lamborghini said that it has no intention of producing vehicles in Mexico after various media published that the Italian company would be making an electric vehicle in the state of Hidalgo. Through Lamborghini Mexico, Automobili Lamborghini informed that: "it recently found out that Jorge Antonio Fernandez Garcia met with Hidalgo's Coordinating Business Council (CCE) to discuss the development and production of Lamborghini brand vehicles. Lamborghini has no knowledge of any type of business relations with entities established in Latin America or with Mr. Garcia and has no intention to produce vehicles in Mexico."

Toyoda Gosei plant will soon begin operations in San Luis Potosi

With a US$30 million investment, the plant has finished construction and will begin operations in the next few days. Toyoda Gosei Automotive Sealing Mexico has another plant in Mexquitic, also in San Luis Potosí. Tominaga San, president of the new plant located in the municipality of Venado, San Luis Potosi said that the investment for this new plant was of US$30 million and that it will generate up to 900 direct jobs when it begins operations. Toyoda Gosei makes automotive seals for Toyota, Ford, Nissan and Honda.
