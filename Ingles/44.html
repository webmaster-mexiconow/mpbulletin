North America's auto industry needs NAFTA, says Scotiabank

A note by Canadian investment bank Scotiabank considers NAFTA the agreement that enabled the North American auto sector to outperform the global industry. Any new restriction to the free flow of vehicles and parts among the three countries would have a negative impact on their economic activity, including potential loss of 2 million jobs, says its Global Auto Report. According to Carlos Gomes, Senior Economist and Auto Industry Specialist, more than 92% of all auto industry shipments from the U.S. are now destined to other NAFTA countries. This integration has boosted productivity and enhanced the region's competitiveness, says the Scotiabank's report.
Sales of hybrid, electric cars boost 323% in Mexican market during January

Mexican auto dealers sold 593 hybrid and full electric vehicles in January 2017, a 323.6% increase compared to same month of 2016, according to latest figures from AMIA. Most of those units, 564 vehicles, feature a hybrid powertrain, while only the 29 remaining were full electric, says the report. Mexico City is where 42% of sales took place with 249 units sold, 239 hybrids and 10 full electric cars. The Toyota Prius Hybrid remained as leader in sales with 384 units sold, due in part to a program in Mexico City which offers financial help to taxi drivers who get rid of obsolete units.
Mercedes Concept A Sedan revealed in Shanghai

Mercedes-Benz revealed the new entry-level sedan that will go on sale as part of the German automaker's upcoming 8-model-strong fourth-generation compact car portfolio with the unveiling of the Concept A Sedan at the Shanghai auto show. Set to reach the U.S. by early 2019, the production version of the new 4-door concept has been conceived to challenge the likes of the Audi A3 and next-generation BMW 1-Series sedans. The unit strongly hints at the design of the new A-Class sedan which company officials had indicated would be assembled in Aguascalientes, Mexico, but those plans are now under review.
Velvac Holdings sold for US$ 39.5 million; deal includes a plant in Reynosa

Chicago private equity firm Prospect Partners announced it has completed the sale of Wisconsin-based Velvac Holdings Inc. to The Eastern Company for US$ 39.5 million. Velvac designs and manufactures proprietary vision technology for commercial vehicles at a production facility located in Reynosa, Tamaulipas. Velvac products are used in heavy and medium duty trucks, motorhomes and buses. It is a Tier 1 supplier of mirrors and camera-enabled vision systems, which make up about two-thirds of its revenue. Velvac sells about 45,000 camera-enabled units each year. Its 2016 revenue totaled US$ 60.3 million.
Chinese autoparts manufacturer looks for land to build factory in Mexico

Chinese auto parts manufacturer Huizhou Toneluck Electro-Mechanics revealed plans to set up a plant in Mexico aimed to expand its sales in North America. The company plans to spend an initial US$ 7.3 million on the new factory. Huizhou Toneluck aims to buy land in Mexico by the end of this year and construction taking place in 2018. Commercial operations are scheduled to start in 2019 expecting annual sales of US$ 43.5 million. The company is a major global manufacturer of car door locks and electrical switches with annual sales of US$ 87 million.
Japan-based Usui International opens second plant in Guanajuato

Japanese firm Usui International opened its second manufacturing plant in Puerto Interior, Guanajuato, to supply tubing solutions for automakers located in the Bajio region. La company invested US$ 8 million in this facility, its third in North America, to manufacture tubing solutions for brakes, fuel, power steering and cooling systems. Usui started operations in Guanajuato in 2014 with a US$ 10 million plant. Toyota, Mazda, Hino Motors and Volkswagen, are some of its main customers.
Chevrolet Cruze production to be scaled back

Production of the Chevrolet Cruze at the General Motors Complex in Lordstown, Ohio, will be scaled back in mid-July to adjust with demand, leading to an unknown loss of jobs that will likely be determined over the next several weeks. The plant will continue to operate on two shifts, but fewer Cruzes will be produced each day. GM officials said there's a 99-day supply of Cruzes. Analysts say a healthy inventory level for compact cars is a 60-day supply.
Schaeffler technologies receive two awards for "Contribution to Excellence"

Schaeffler Group was recently honored with two Automotive News PACE (Premier Automotive Suppliers' Contribution to Excellence) Awards. Schaeffler's electromechanical active roll stabilizer and automatic transmission one-way clutch assembly received top honors at the annual event. To serve the North American automotive market, Schaeffler operates development centers in Troy, Michigan; Fort Mill, South Carolina; Wooster, Ohio; and Puebla, Mexico; as well as manufacturing facilities in: South Carolina; Missouri; Ohio; Ontario, Canada; Puebla and Irapuato, Mexico.
Delphi taps three firms to enhance its connected cars, self-driving businesses

Delphi announced partnerships with three different companies aimed to develop systems that can process and transmit data faster in order to enhance its connected cars and self-driving businesses. The companies involved are Valens, an Israel-based leader in signal processing capability; Rosenberger, a high frequency connectivity company specialized in high-speed data transmission, and Otonomo, a developer of data marketplaces for automakers. Delphi will offer services from all three companies as a part of a connected car ecosystem.
GM to add more than 1,100 jobs to expand self-driving operations in California

General Motors announced that it will add more than 1,100 new jobs and invest US$ 14 million in a new research and development facility for Cruise Automation in San Francisco. Cruise Automation and GM engineers are currently testing more than 50 Chevrolet Bolt EVs with self-driving technology on public roads in San Francisco; Scottsdale, Arizona; and metro Detroit. The Cruise Automation team plans to move into the new space by the end of the year. California's Office of Business and Economic Development allocated an US$ 8 million tax credit for this expansion.
