Despite decline in auto production, Magna posts record sales, profits

Canadian auto parts manufacturer Magna International Inc posted a record US$ 561 million net income for the second quarter, an increase of 1% compared to same period of 2016. The company also reported all-time quarterly record sales of US$ 9.68 billion (up 3%). Sales in North America, the company's biggest market, increased just 0.4% to US$ 5.37 billion. Sales from operations in Mexico totaled US$ 1.42 billion in the second quarter and US$ 2.75 billion for the first half of 2017, up 9% and 6.6% respectively. The growth was achieved despite North American and European light vehicle production declining in second quarter by 3% and 1%, respectively.
Cummins opens Research & Development center in San Luis Potosi

Almost a year after laying the foundation stone, the Cummins Mexico Research and Development Center officially opened, a project that seeks to create new alternatives in the remanufacturing of engines, officials said at the ribbon-cutting ceremony. Right next to its manufacturing plant in San Luis Potosi stands this Research & Development Center equipped with state-of-the-art technology, so that its employees develop new efficient and sustainable solutions for the transportation industry. The company created 30 high-paying jobs and invested US$ 10 million in the 26,900 square-feet facility and a wide range of tools, including a 3D printing.
Nucor acquires St. Louis Cold Drawn, including a production site in Nuevo Leon

Nucor Corporation announced a deal to acquire St. Louis Cold Drawn, Inc., a manufacturer of cold drawn steel bars that mainly serve the U.S. and Mexican automotive industry. St. Louis Cold Drawn, Inc. employs 125 people in two plants located in St. Louis, Missouri, and Apodaca, Nuevo Leon, with a combined annual capacity of 200,000 tons. The Apodaca site, opened in 1999, has more cold finishing capability and capacity than any other in Mexico. The cold finished steel bars are used in electric motor shafts, shock absorbers & MacPherson struts, bearings, screw machine parts, stabilizer bars among other applications. Financial terms of the deal were not disclosed.
Nexteer's net profit up 20% boosted by launch of new products, operating efficiency

Nexteer Automotive, a manufacturer of steering and driveline, reported a net profit of US$ 179.7 million for the six-month period ended June 30, 2017, an increase of 20.7% over the same period in 2016, as a result of successful launch of new programs, improved product line mix, improved operating efficiency and cost competitiveness, said the company in its first half 2017 report. The Michigan-based company with two manufacturing plants in Juarez and one in Queretaro reported total revenue of US$ 1.97 billion, an increase of 2.6% over the same period in 2016. In the first half of 2017, the company launched of 15 new product applications for different customers.
Lear inaugurates harnesses production facility in Coahuila

Lear Corporation officially opened its 46th manufacturing plant in Mexico a month after the facility started its first phase of operations in Matamoros, Coahuila, to manufacture wire harnesses. Just half of the 300,000 square-feet facility is occupied, but the company expects to operate the plant at full capacity by the end of this year. Around 1,200 workers were recruited for the first phase of operations, a figure Lear expects to increase to 3,000 when the plant reaches its intended production rates. During the last four years the company opened 12 plants in the country. The auto supplier estimates its Mexican workforce will total 56,000 employees by the end of the year.
Thailand-based Indorama Ventures acquires DuraFiber plant in Queretaro

Indorama Ventures Public Company Limited (IVL), a Thailand-based global chemical producer, announced an agreement to acquire DuraFiber Technologies (DFT) Mexico, in a move to expand its reach in the automotive sector on the back of rising demand for lightweight, high-performance materials. The facility is the only domestic tire cord fabric producer in Mexico with products approved by major global tire companies. The plant, located in Queretaro, manufactures cord fabrics and industrial textiles that are used in a wide range of applications including reinforcement for conveyor belts, hoses, single-ply roofing, tents, airbags, seat belts, safety harnesses and ropes.
Strattec fiscal year results affected by startup costs of new Mexican facility

Wisconsin-based locking system manufacturer Strattec Security Corp. reported US$ 108.4 million in net sales during fiscal fourth quarter and year ending July 2, 2017, a flat result if compared to the US$ 108.3 million from same period of 2016. Net income was US$ 1.8 million in fourth fiscal quarter, more than triple if compared to US$ 584,000 in the prior year quarter. Gross profit margins were 13.5% in the current year quarter compared to 14.0% in the prior year quarter. The decrease in gross profit margin in part attributed to startup costs associated with a new facility in Leon, Guanajuato, said the company. Strattec also operates three plants in Juarez, Chihuahua.
Chinese automakers show interest on a potential purchase of FCA

Representatives of Chinese automakers over the last couple of weeks have sent delegations to Auburn Hills, Michigan, looking for a potential purchase of Fiat Chrysler Automobiles with at least one of them making a formal offer at a small premium over its market value, Automotive News reported. The offer was rejected for not being enough. A source also said FCA executives have traveled to China to meet with their counterparts. It's unclear which Chinese automakers are pursuing FCA, but different sources have pointed to Dongfeng Motor Corp., Great Wall, Zhejiang Geely Holding Group or FCA's current partner in China, Guangzhou Automobile Group.
There's evidence that Uber, Lyft services reduce car ownership

In areas where Uber, Lyft and other on-demand ride services operate, consumers may buy fewer cars and even take fewer trips, according to a new study by the University of Michigan, Texas A&M and Columbia University. The research took place after voters of Austin, Texas, blocked a ballot measure that ended up pulling Uber and Lyft drivers out of the city. The suspension provided frame for an experiment to measure changes on travel behavior. A strong 41% of those surveyed turned to their own vehicle to fill the lack of services, 9% actually bought an additional car for this purpose. The average monthly frequency of trips decreased from 5.65 to 2.01, a 68% drop.
VW's on its way for another year at the top

Volkswagen is on course to retain its crown as the world biggest seller for a second year in a row, according to an analysis by JATCO Dynamics. The report, which just counts cars, SUVs and light trucks, leaving out heavy trucks and buses, states that VW has sold 5,092,247 vehicles from January through July, up 1.3% compared to same period of 2016. The alliance Renault-Nissan comes second to 4,650,954 units barely above Toyota with 4,647,521 vehicles. The rest of the list goes as follows: GM - 3,878,426; Ford - 3,092,299; Hyundai/Kia - 3,114,652; Honda - 2,509,365; FCA - 2,415,458; PSA Peugeot Citroen - 1,957,180 and Daimler - 1,340,537.
