Vehicle financing falls 25.6% during the first seven months of 2020

During the first seven months of 2020 there were 377,765 cars (new and used) acquired through financing, which meant a decrease of 25.6% when compared to the same month of 2019, according to numbers from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). According to its last report on the total financing of light vehicles, 319,636 new units were acquired through financing. At the same time, 58,129 used units were financed during the first seven months of 2020.

JAC opens three JAC Stores in the country

JAC said that, in the last three months, it has generated more than 190 jobs in the country thanks to the opening of 3 new JAC Stores which are located in the cities of Durango, Puebla and most recently, Hidalgo, which headquarters the brand's assembly plant in Mexico. Auto parts' FDI represents 89.8% of foreign investment for manufacturing of transport equipment

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) for the manufacturing of parts of automotive vehicles added US$159.7 million in the first semester of the year, which represents an annual increase of 19.2%, detailed the Ministry of Economy. During this year, in the year's first quarter this segment of FDI added US$130.6 million, while during the second quarter it only added US$29.2 million. In contrast, in 2019 the first quarter registered US$131 million and the second one only US$3 million. Between January and June, auto parts' FDI represents 89.8% of foreign investment for manufacturing of transport equipment, which added US$177.8 million in this period.

Mexican auto parts exports to the United States register growth of 1.2%

Mexican auto parts exports to the United States registered a growth of 1.2% in July, at US$5.1 billion, which reflected its first increase after most of the plants were forced to close due to the COVID-19 pandemic, according to data from the Census Bureau. Within Mexico's automotive sector, automotive parts are the most important segment in sales to the United States market, taking up 47.3% of participation in July, which is almost represented by both the car segment (24.1%) and the heavy vehicles segment (28.6%).

The automotive industry loses almost 62,000 jobs during the year's first semester

The automotive industry lost about 62,000 jobs during the year's first semester, after the suspension of labor due to the COVID-19 pandemic, of which 55,000 were from auto parts manufacturing plants, while the rest are in car manufacturing and sales dealerships. Oscar Albin, president of the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA), said that from January to June of 2020 a fall of 6% in employment within the sector was registered, since it went from 867,000 to 812,000 job sources in the sector when compared to the same period of last year.

ReNeuvo Group invests US$46 million in Aguascalientes

The Canadian group dedicated to the recycling of tires, plastics and thermoplastics for the automotive and aerospace industry, ReNeuvo Group, evaluated several locations in Mexico to decide on its first international location, like Queretaro, Hidalgo or Guanajuato, but decided on settling in Aguascalientes. This is one of the first investments in the state after the USMCA's entry into force, and will see an investment of US$46 million, which will generate 500 direct jobs mid-term on 9 plants. Four of these plants will begin operations in the project's first three years, each one with a specific activity.
