Bulletin #1
10 November, 2016
Audi delays technology endeavours to cut costs

Due to heavy financial losses at Volkswagen Group because of emissions scandal known as the "Dieselgate", Audi will delay its technology endeavours to cut costs, company officials told Reuters in recent days. It's not clear yet how this measure will impact Audi's plans for its new Mexican facility, where the German group would make hybrid or electric versions of the Q5.
Brembo opens plant in Nuevo Leon, announces new US$ 95 million investment

As Italian brakes manufacturer Brembo inaugurated its US$ 36 million aluminum calipers plant in Mexico. The company also announced it will invest an additional US$ 95 million to build a new foundry for cast iron discs located next door to the calipers plant. The facility is located in Escobedo, in the northern State of Nuevo Leon, and is one of the company's most modern and cutting edge facilities. It features state-of-the-art manufacturing processes that combine high efficiency with the latest technically integrated solutions, Brembo said in a press release.
Nissan sets end of production date for the Tsuru

Nissan confirmed the end of production of the Nissan Tsuru in Mexico by May 2017 because Government requirements for air bags and brakes, which Tsuru doesn't have. The company expects to continue its Mexican sales and production leadership despite ceasing production of the iconic sedan, which has accomplished 2.4 million sales to date. A special commemorative version of the Nissan Tsuru will be sold beginning March 2017, with only 1,000 units available.
Hyundai Dymos to supply seats for a three-wheel vehicle manufacturer

Elio Motors, Inc., the startup vehicle manufacturer planning to launch a three-wheel car that will get up to 84 mpg with a set US$ 7,300 base price, announced Hyundai Dymos as its seating manufacturing partner. Hyundai Dymos was launched by Hyundai Motor Group in 1994. In addition to Shreveport, the company has North American operations in West Point, Ga., San Luis Potosi, Mexico, Monterrey, Mexico and Farmington Hills, MI. The company will set up production capability onsite at Elio Motors' Shreveport, La., manufacturing facility.
Automotive seats maker Tachi-S to built Research Center in Mexico

Japan-based automotive seats manufacturer Tachi-S announced a US$ 10 million investment to built a Research and Development Center where the company will test products made by its Mexican and Brazilian subsidiaries. The facilities will be located in Aguascalientes and will start operations by early 2017 aimed to provide technical support and certification to seats made in Mexico and Brazil for assemblers such as Nissan and Honda.
Bosch opens engineering center, announces US$ 78 million investment

To strengthen its presence in Mexico, the Bosch Group just set two milestones in the country: at its existing site in San Luis Potosi, the global supplier of technology and services has opened a new US$ 10 million powertrain engineering center. At the same time, its local automotive technology manufacturing capacity will be increased. Between 2015 and 2018, Bosch will invest roughly US$ 78.5 million in expanding several locations, including facilities in Cuautitlan Izcalli, Aguascalientes and Queretaro.
Quickstep Holdings and Futuris Automotive to develope car seat 50% lighter

Australia-based companies Quickstep Holdings and Futuris Automotive Group are joining forces with for the development and production of carbon fibre seat backs that will be as much as 50 per cent lighter than conventional units . Quickstep Holdings is a Sydney-based company with links to aerospace and defence. Futuris Automotive Group is a global parts supplier active in the U.S., Mexico, China, Thailand and Australia. Futuris Mexican plants are located in Piedras Negras and Ciudad Acuna, Coahuila.
Scania teams up with McNeilus to built concrete mixer trucks

Scania teamed up with McNeilus to showcase its first concrete mixer trucks for the Mexican market, at the Mexican Association of Concrete Industries' congress in Cancun. The truck displayed at the congress, one of the most important events in the concrete industry's calendar, was a P 310 C6x4HZ model. Scania is providing the chassis, while the vehicle has an eight cubic metre mixer drum that has been manufactured by McNeilus in Mexico.
Aeromexico to open daily flight between Monterrey and Detroit

As Mexican auto industry continues its expansion, Aeromexico announced its new daily direct flight between Monterrey and Detroit, starting on January 10, 2017. This will be the second option for automotive executives, since Delta Air Lines already offers the same daily connection. This new route will be operated with Embraer 190 aircraft with 99 passenger seats, including 11 in Clase Premier, Aeromexico's Business Class.
Goodyear's new Mexican plant might start ahead of schedule

The production at the first Goodyear factory in San Luis Potosi might be ready ahead of schedule, as there are reports favorable advancements in the building's construction, 90% in infrastructure and 30% in equipment, assured David Gomez, General Director of Goodyear in the State during press conference. US$550 million are being invested in the plant and 1,000 direct jobs will be generated by 2018. The current plan aims to start operations by mid-2017 with 500 employees. There are 300 workers currently hired, out of which 113 were sent to plants in countries such as Brazil, China, Canada, the U.S. and Slovenia, for their training process.
