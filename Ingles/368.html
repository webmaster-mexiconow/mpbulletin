Daimay Automotive inaugurates second plant in Coahuila

Daimay Automotive Interior's second plant was inaugurated in the FINSA Industrial Park in Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila, with an investment of US$86 million. In addition, the first stone of what will be its third plant was laid at the same event. Victor Guillermo Villarreal Sosa, field director of Daimay, said that this investment generates more than a thousand jobs and is achieved despite the adversity of the pandemic to the labor environment that has managed to establish itself in the entity. The main components that Daimay manufactures are visors, headboards, armrests, and ceiling consoles, among other pieces.

Uber allows drivers to know the trip's final destination before it starts

Before accepting each trip with the Uber app in the country's capital, drivers will receive on the same screen information about the destination and additional data. This new feature is intended to provide more information, and thus greater peace of mind, to the thousands of driving partners who use the app to generate revenue. This feature adds to security technology innovations also available for all travel in the country such as the ability to record audio for a security report, PIN code verification and RideCheck technology.

Automotive companies continue investing in Mexico despite the pandemic

Toyota, Ford, Nissan, and Volkswagen have invested more than $2 billion in their plants in the country to begin producing new models, despite the pandemic. Toyota announced the investment of US$170 million to expand the production of the Tacoma in Guanajuato. Ford invested US$1 billion, according to data from the government of Sonora, to modernize its plant in Hermosillo. For its part, Nissan invested US$244 million to start production of the newest generation of its Sentra compact sedan. Volkswagen invested US$600 million investment to start production at the Puebla plant of a new medium-size SUV, to be marketed under the name Taos.

Mabuchi Motor Mexico will expand its operations in Aguascalientes

Mabuchi Motor Mexico will expand its operations at the end of this year in Aguascalientes with the construction of a new plant, which will be located in the San Francisco IV Industrial Park, in the municipality of De Francisco de los Romo. This new plant will produce electric motors for glass lifts, mirrors and keys for vehicles that are currently manufactured in Vietnam. Mabuchi Motor is one of the leading companies in the electric motor market, which has among its main clients OEMs such as: Honda and Mazda, as well as companies from other sectors.

Audi will produce the Q5 Sportback in Mexico

The Audi Q5 Sportback will be produced in the San Jose Chiapa plant in Mexico and will be launched to the international market in the first half of 2021. Hispano Suiza announces its arrival to Mexico

Hispano Suiza, a Spanish brand of luxury hypercars, announces its arrival to Mexico with a pair of extremely exotic electric models. The Carmen uses two electric motors to drive the rear axle with a total of 1,005 hp, enough to reach 100 km/h in less than 3 seconds. The first Hispano Suiza dealer in Mexico arrives in collaboration with Grupo Sanvi in response to the behavior of the luxury market in our country. The price of the Carmen starts at 1.5 million € (MXN$39.2 million) and will be available for delivery from April 2021.

Vehicle exports from Mexico to the United Arab Emirates increase 103.9% in 2020

During the first eight months of this year Mexico exported 7,835 new cars and light trucks to the United Arab Emirates, which meant an annual increase of 103.9% in contrast to the 3,843 units sent during the same period last year, according to data from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (Inegi). The United Arab Emirates became Mexico's sixth largest automotive export destination with a 0.5% share during the January-August period of this year.

Queretaro's manufacturing of transport equipment reported drop of 27%

Manufacturing of transportat equipment reported the most significant drop in production value during April and May, as part of the early impacts of the pandemic. From January to July alone, the production value of this branch in the state accumulated $1.7 billion, a drop of 27% from the same period last year, when the value of this manufacturing amounted to $2.303 billion.

The automotive industry's recovery will be evident in the last quarter of the year: INA

As a result of the situation brought about by COVID-19 and the impact it had on the automotive industry during the first part of the year, it is estimated that production in 2020 will reach 14 million units in North America. According to Oscar Albin, Executive President of the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA), the recovery of the automotive industry in the last quarter of the year will be evident and this will have a favorable impact on the auto parts sector, with the expectation of reaching 16 million units level in North America once again in 2021.
