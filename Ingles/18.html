Bulletin #18
17 January, 2017
Mexico's auto industry ends 2016 with all-time records

Mexican auto exports and production rose in December to end 2016 with new record high figures. Auto production rose 8.8% in the final month of 2016 to 242,495 units, while exports were up 4.8% to 216,645 vehicles. For the year, output climbed 2.0% to 3,465,615 units as exports picked up 0.3% to 2,768,268 vehicles.
Infiniti unveils potentially autonomous crossover soon to be built in Mexico

Infiniti unveiled a concept model for the redesigned 2018 QX50, the compact crossover that Nissan's luxury brand plans to produce at its Mexico facility. "This car is going to get autonomous-drive functions as the next step," Infiniti President Roland Krueger said at the premiere.
Nissan halts joint development with Daimler; Mexican plan will go on

Nissan will a halt joint development program of luxury cars with Daimler's Mercedes-Benz. A Mexican plant in the works in Aguascalientes, Mexico, will go on, but profitability from the project will be hurt. Sources involved with the program told Infiniti brand was not performing well enough to absorb Mercedes technology costs.
GM reveals the all-new 2018 Terrain; shifts production to Mexico

General Motors Co. introduced the all-new 2018 Terrain whose production will be shifted from Canada to Mexico in order to expand production of the Ontario plant's Chevrolet Equinox. GM's Equinox and Terrain will go on sale as redesigned models in the first quarter and this summer, respectively, as small SUVs are a booming segment of the market and a big moneymaker for the company. That's why the automaker decided to increase output from one factory to three -adding the vehicles at two existing facilities in Mexico, according to a GM spokesman.
FCA to shift Ram heavy duty production from Mexico to U.S.

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles announced a US$ 1 billion investment to retool and modernize the Warren Truck Assembly Plant (Michigan) to produce the all-new Jeep Wagoneer and Grand Wagoneer, and the south plant of the Toledo Assembly Complex (Ohio) to build an all-new Jeep pickup truck. These actions are planned to be completed by 2020. The investment in Warren will enable the plant to produce the Ram heavy duty truck, which is currently built in Mexico. The facility located in the northern state of Coahuila will remain manufacturing other Ram versions.
BMW remains 'absolutely' committed to new Mexican plant

The German premium segment automaker BMW revealed the company is "absolutely" committed to the factory the group builds in San Luis Potosi, Mexico, despite President-elect Donald Trump trade policies. "Construction is progressing in order to meet the scheduled 2019 production start and the BMW 3 Series Sedan will be built at the plant from 2019 onwards," the company added.
Volkswagen unveils the new built-in-Mexico 2018 Tiguan

Volkswagen chose to unveil the built-in-Mexico all-new 2018 Tiguan at the North American International Auto Show as a kick off a big year for the brand in America. "The new Tiguan demonstrates how we plan to give American customers the usability and versatility they demand without sacrificing style or Volkswagen's trademark driving dynamics," company officials said at the presentation.
Mazda's Mexican plant will remain a core manufacturing base: CEO

Mazda Motor Corp. said that its Mexican plant will remain a core manufacturing base, unaffected by trade policies of U.S. President-elect Donald Trump. It is desirable for NAFTA to be maintained for the whole auto industry, Mazda CEO Masamichi Kogai said at a New Year press conference in Hiroshima where the company is headquartered.
No reason to reconsider Mexican plant: Michelin President

Michelin is not rethinking plans for a new US$ 475 million plant in Mexico despite pressure by President-elect Donald Trump on companies to steer manufacture operations to the United States, the tiremaker said on the sidelines of the annual Detroit auto show. "The decision to build the factory in Mexico was made some years ago, and we aren't reconsidering it," Michelin President Jean-Dominique Senard said.
Taiwan-based Hwang Yu Group builds production facility in Jalisco

Taiwan based company Hwang Yu Group, dedicated to the automotive sector, started the construction of its plant at the industrial park "Colinas del Lago", in Jalisco. The investment is valued at US$ 7 million and it will generate 200 jobs. The company produces steering, suspension and brakes components.
