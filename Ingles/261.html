XTremer version of the X-Trail arrives to Mexico

Nissan X-Trail XTremer arrives to Mexico to offer a special version of the popular SUV for those who enjoy adventure and sportiness, which offers a bold outside design and an extreme and daunting spirit. The special edition Nissan X-Trail XTremer has been adapted to be sold in the Mexican market on the Advance degree of two rows, with Oxford gray bodywork and distinctive features that give it that sporty look.
Mitsubishi hopes to sell 25,000 units by the end of 2019

Mitsubishi hopes to sell 25,000 units by the end of 2019, which would represent an increase of 44% with respect to 2018. This ambition is not to be taken lightly, since it's backed up by their actual numbers. In the first seven months of the year, the company's sales grew 26%, while those of the general industry decreased 6.6%. Jorge Vallejo, general director of the company in Mexico, trusts that this dynamism has occurred because of the company's portfolio combined with consumer guarantees.

Volkswagen Mexico exported 122,887 Tiguan units

The Tiguan, produced in Cuatlancingo's Volkswagen plant is the most exported vehicle by the Mexican terminal automotive industry, according to information provided by the company. During the January-July period, Volkswagen Mexico reported the production of a total of 266,496 vehicles in their plant, of which it exported a total of 260,739 in the same period; 122,887 of them were Tiguan units. This last number represents a 27.8% growth in comparison with the shipments of this model during the first seven months of 2018.
Plasma Automation Group will invest US$2.5 million in Leon expansion

Plasma Automation Group will invest more than US$2.5 million to grow their 4,000 square meters plant in Leon, Guanajuato. Ernesto Sandoval, the group's director, said that with this investment the company will be able to increase their high precision machined components, laser cutting systems, high-definition plasma, engineering, and metal-mechanic design and construction. These products cater mainly to the aerospace and automotive sector.
ANPACT asks to modify the NOM-044

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) informed that around the country 20% of the service stations do not have ultra-low sulfur diesel (ULSD). Due to the scenario that forces them to produce vehicles with EUROVI/EPA10 without a 100% availability of the ULSD in the country, the producers unitized in ANPACT, asked for the NOM-044 to be modified so that the currently valid technology EUROV/EPA07 can coexist with EUROVI/EPA10 until there's a 100% availability of ULSD in the country.
Minghua of Mexico announced the investment of US$31 million in Puebla

The Chinese capital company, Minghua of Mexico, dedicated to the production of plastic pieces for the automotive industry, announced the investment of US$31 million for the construction of a storage center in the municipality of Huejotzingo, which will generate 250 new jobs. The complex covers an area of 10,000 square meters, and in it the sequenced assembly and shipment of painted plastic injection pieces will be carried out to supply Volkswagen Mexico.
Bosch will implement a dual education program in Jalisco

Bosch and the government of Jalisco united to implement the dual education system, which will promote the development of qualified technicians, who will not only guarantee quality processes, but also bolster new information technologies. Guadalajara is one of the places in which Bosch has a greater growth in technological activities which require IT professionals, which is why it established the goal of training coders to meet the sector's demand in the next few years.
Impro made a US$120 million investment in San Luis Potosi

Impro laid the first stone and commemorative plate for its complex located in the WTC 2 Park, in which it's making a US$120 million investment. The Asian capital company is dedicated to the manufacturing of high-precision forging components for cars, planes, medical equipment and construction machinery. Their arrival will create 2,500 jobs in a three year period. San Luis Potosi's minister of Economic Development, Gustavo Puente Orozco, noted that the company is the seventh largest forging manufacturer of independent investment in the world and China's most important.
Peugeot is looking to close 2019 with the sale of more than 10,000 units

With a double digit growth for the first semester of the year, French automaker Peugeot is betting on the Mexican market. The brand is looking to close 2019 with record sales above 10,000 units. Igor Dumas, CEO of Peugeot in Mexico, affirmed that the 14% accumulated growth with 6,209 units means that the company is in line with its 2016-2021 business plan in Mexico to double its sales. He said that the challenge is to reach 1% market participation in domestic sales, from the 0.5% with which they began three years ago. For this year, there's the prediction of reaching a participation of 0.8%.
