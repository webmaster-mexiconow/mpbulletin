Bulletin #29
23 February, 2017
Mexican automotive trade surplus reached US$ 62.9 billion in 2016

In 2016, Mexican automotive trade surplus reached US$ 62.9 billion, a 1.2% decrease compared to 2015 and first fall since 2009, according to figures from Banco de Mexico, the country's central bank. Auto exports totaled US$ 113.3 billion in 2016, a 1.03% decrease from previous year. Meanwhile imports reached US$ 50.4 billion, 0.85% less than 2015.
Rassini reports 49% growth in 2016 earnings

Mexico-based auto supplier Rassini, S.A.B. de C.V., reported earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortization (EBITDA) for the fiscal year 2016 of US$ 159.5 million, a 49% increase from US$ 107 million during the previous year. According to its unaudited financial results, Rassini's revenue rose to US$ 817 million, 27% more than US$ 644.8 million in 2015. Its net income increased to US$ 71.5 million, a 62% rise from the US$ 44.2 million in the previous year.
Automotive sector creates the best paid jobs in 4 out of 6 Mexican regions

Human capital firm Adecco Mexico revealed that automotive salaries are among the highest compared to other sectors in four out of six regions in Mexico. According to its report, auto industry pays higher than others in Central (Bajio), Northeast, Western Coast and Southeast areas. The automotive sector was outpaced by other industries only in Mexico City and the Northwest region. The study analyzed 58,857 profiles among 18 different sectors over the six regions.
Queretaro's automotive investments tripled in 2016

Foreign direct investment related to the automotive sector more than tripled during 2016 in Queretaro, from US$ 89.9 million in 2015 to US$ 293.5 million last year, revealed state authorities. According to Marco Antonio del Prete Tercero, secretary of Sustaintable Development in Queretaro, automotive production is expected to increase from US$ 65 billion in 2016 to US$ 75 billion in 2017.
Flex acquires AGM Automotive; deal includes a Queretaro plant

Flex, formerly Flextronics, announced a definitive agreement to acquire AGM Automotive, a global supplier of automotive interior components and systems, including overhead console systems, interior lighting, electronic components and textile flooring solutions. AGM is headquartered in Troy, Michigan, with additional facilities in the U.S., Mexico (Queretaro), Costa Rica, Austria and China. Flex operates six manufacturing sites in Mexico, located in Chihuahua (2), Jalisco (2), Tamaulipas and Aguascalientes. Financial terms of the deal were not disclosed.
Yusen Logistics opens US$ 13 million facility in Guanajuato

Yusen Logistics opened a 53,820-square-foot logistics center near Celaya, Guanajuato, to provide services to its automotive customers, including OEMs and tier 1 and tier 2 suppliers, creating 139 jobs. The US$ 13 million facility is located on a 17-acre site in the Amistad Industrial Park in the Bajio region, one of the largest automotive clusters in Latin America. Some of Yusen customers in the Bajio region are Mazda, Honda, Toyota, THK Rythm, Yutaka and Matsujo, among others.
Cummins revenues, profits fall in 2016

Diesel engines and solutions manufacturer Cummins reported revenues for the full year 2016 of US$ 17.5 billion, 8% lower than 2015. Revenues in North America decreased 12% and international sales decreased 2% mainly due to foreign currency movements. Earnings before taxes for the year was US$ 2.0 billion or 11.4% of sales, compared to US$ 2.1 billion or 10.9% of sales in 2015. Cummins operates manufacturing sites in Ciudad Juarez and San Luis Potosi.
Mexican-Canadian joint venture to supply power for San Luis Potosi industrial park

Canadian developer ATCO partnered with Mexican counterpart RANMAN Energy to supply an innovative power generation solution in the rapidly developing World Trade Centre Industrial Park in San Luis Potosi, Mexico. The project, which includes a high-voltage substation, five modular natural gas-fired electricity generating units and an associated distribution network, will be completed in two phases. The amount of investment remains undisclosed.
Four 2017 Honda vehicles named "Best Family Cars of 2017" by Kelley Blue Book

Four 2017 Honda models, the CR-V, Pilot, Odyssey and the built-in-Mexico HR-V were each named as Best Family Cars of 2017 by the experts at Kelley Blue Book. The survey focused on safety, value, reputation, comfort and convenience, cargo versatility, technology, and fit of various rear-facing and forward-facing child safety car seats when choosing the vehicles that best meet the needs of today's families.
Mexico City retains crown of 'Most Traffic Congested City in the World'

GPS and navigation solutions provider TomTom released the results of the TomTom Traffic Index 2017, the annual report detailing the cities around the world with the most traffic congestion. Mexico City once again takes the top spot with drivers expecting to spend an average of 66% extra travel time stuck in traffic anytime of the day (7 points up on last year), and up to 101% in the evening peak periods versus a free flow, or uncongested, situation - adding up to 227 hours of extra travel time per year.
