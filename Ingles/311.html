BM of Mexico invests US$10.1 million in Tlaxcala

The Italian company BM moved a plant from Ciudad Juarez to Tlaxcala with an investment of US$10.1 million and will generate 350 new jobs. Industrias BM of Mexico is an Italian company with 75 years in the automotive market, and is dedicated to the production of grills, lateral posts, back spoilers, consoles and dashboards for clients like Volkswagen and Audi. Alberto Barcella, president of Industria BM Group was present and said that they decided on Tlaxcala due to the state government's trust, availability and collaboration.

Santander and Mazda Mexico join forces to offer financing

Santander Mexico joined forces with the automotive company Mazda Motor Mexico so that the bank can become the brand's financial arm starting July. In a press release, they indicated that through this agreement, Mazda Motor Mexico clients will have financing from the Spanish bank through an automotive credit, Super Auto Santander. With almost 15 years in the Mexican market, Mazda Motor Mexico currently holds seventh place in vehicle sales within the country.

Mexico produced 326,183 units in February

Mexico produced 326,183 light vehicles in February 2020, an increase of 4.5% when compared to February of last year, informed INEGI. In particular, Mexican light vehicle exports registered a year-to-year decrease of 1.9% in February, at 266,035 units. Among the main producers were: General Motors (74,560 units and an increase of 21.1%), Nissan (59,601 units and a decrease of 6.7%) and FCA Mexico (51,376 units and an increase of 27.7%).

Heavy vehicle sales drop 22.6% in February

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), informed that during the month of February, wholesale sales of heavy vehicles reached a total of 2,019 units, which compared to the 2,608 registered in February 2019, represented a fall of 22.6%. During said month, heavy and commercial vehicle retail sales reached 2,324 units which, compared to the 3,187 sold ones during the same month of 2019, meant a decrease of 27.1%.

New vehicle financing drops 3.6%

Mexicans are acquiring less and less credits for the purchase of vehicles, data which is in line with the drop in new vehicle sales. From the 104,832 new units sold during the first month of this year, 68.8% of them were acquired through financing or 72,198 vehicles, which at the same time reflected a drop of 3.6%, according to data from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA).

The 2020 Dodge Durango arrives to Mexico

The new 2020 Dodge Durango arrives to Mexico, which for this year model is expanding its product line to offer the Toruing, GT and R/T versions. "SUV demand in Mexico has had an important growth in the last decade which is why by realigning the Durango portfolio with new versions, is an opportunity so that our clients can have more options, configurations and more attractive prices," said Jesus Gallo, director of Dodge in Mexico.

CARS from Daimler Commercial Vehicles begins operations in Mexico City

A new Authorized Spare Parts and Service Center (CARS) from Daimler Commercial Vehicles Mexico (DVCM) began operations in Mexico City together with Camiones Rivera. CARS is one of the after sales service proposals from DVCM, who offers spare parts and solutions for Mercedes-Benz buses and Freightliner trucks. With this, DVCM is consolidating its service and distribution network, with which it's reaching more than 80 locations strategically located throughout the country.
