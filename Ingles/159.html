GM unveils the new Chevrolet Blazer to be built in Ramos Arizpe

Chevrolet confirmed that is bringing back the iconic Blazer nameplate except that technically it's not an SUV, but a mid-size, five-passenger crossover that will fit between the Equinox and the third-row seat Chevy Traverse. The unit will be powered by an inline 193 horsepower 2.5L 4-cylinder engine or an optional 305 horsepower 3.6L V-6 with torque enough to tow up to 4,500 pounds. Both will come with a 9-speed automatic transmission and optional all-wheel drive. As MexicoNow previously reported, the pre-production units have already rolled out of the Ramos Arizpe assembly plant, in the Northern state of Coahuila, with production scheduled to begin by the end of year. Nemak reaches 3-million parts milestone in its new division for electrified vehicles

Monterrey-based Nemak, a manufacturer of aluminum components for the automotive industry, announced that it has produced more than three million parts in its structural and electric vehicle components (SC&EV) business, marking a key milestone in the implementation of its strategy for this segment. Nemak is leveraging its portfolio of casting technologies as well as its expertise in secondary processes to deliver high-value-added enhancements, which are required for the proper functionality of SC/EV components. Battery housings have one of its fastest-growing product lines, these include providing advanced design and cast-in cooling solutions, which are critical for crash resistance and battery performance, respectively. Despite higher growth rates, Brazil auto production still behind Mexico's

Despite a 12.1% hike in automotive production during the first five months of 2018, Brazil is still behind Mexico's output volume, which increased just 3.9% in the same period. Such data suggests that Mexico will once again remain leader in Latin American car production, although there have been some improvements in the Brazilian auto sector, where the economy grew in 2017 for the first time after two years of recession. Global automakers manufactured in Mexico a record figure of 1.6 million vehicles in the first five months of the year, despite an 8.9% drop in domestic sales. Different case is that of Brazil with 1.17 million units manufactured, but a 17% boost in domestic sales. More details here: bit.ly/2KiuERa
Vesta opens industrial park in Puebla for suppliers of Volkswagen, Audi

The industrial real estate developer Vesta inaugurated in recent days the Vesta Park Puebla I industrial park, located in the municipality of Huejotzingo, Puebla, after just over two years of construction work and a US$50 million investment. The development will focus on serving companies in the automotive sector, especially suppliers of Volkswagen and Audi. The complex consists of six buildings distributed in 100,000 square meters and already has three tenants: the Germany-based Pfinder KG, specialized in anticorrosive chemicals; Air International, an Australian manufacturer of thermal systems that will supply solutions for Volkswagen and Ford; as well as the services firm CJ Logistics. DENSO in talks to manage all of Toyota's electronic component operations

Toyota Motor Corporation and DENSO Corporation announced that they have agreed to begin talks toward consolidating the core electronic component operations of both companies within the DENSO electronics business unit. Currently, advanced development and mass-production development for Toyota's electronic components, as well as the production of such components, are conducted by both companies. Some of DENSO's manufacturing operations in Mexico that could benefit from this consolidation are the facility in Apodaca, Nuevo Leon, which manufactures instrument clusters, among other products, and a plant in Reynosa, Tamaulipas, that makes audiovisual products and other electronics components. Despite delays, plans to manufacture BAIC cars in Mexico still stand

The Chinese automaker BAIC (Beijing Automotive Industry Corporation) announced that it postponed to the end of the year the announcement of an approximately US$2 billion investment for the construction of its first manufacturing plant in Mexico. However, "the plan keeps going and the amounts are standing," the director of the firm in Mexico, Jimena Saenz, told Alto Nivel in recent days. The industry is experiencing a period of uncertainty that is temporary, but not the plans and confidence that BAIC has in Mexico, "which is undoubted, as well as the commitment," said the official. In the meantime, the company keeps on with talks for the installation of the plant with authorities from several states. More details here: bit.ly/2KlkItt
Calsonic Kansei shifts two production lines from Tennessee to Mexico

Japan-based automotive components manufacturer Calsonic Kansei announced it will shift two production lines from Shelbyville, Tennessee, to Mexico, a move that will result in the loss of 56 jobs, local media reported. Citing two employees as source, the Shelbyville Times Gazette reports that employees were notified about the decision, which is expected to be completed by February, during a "pre-shift" meeting recently. The two production lines involved manufacture automotive air conditioning parts, the evaporator line and the condenser line. Calsonic operates two manufacturing facilities in the Mexican state of Aguascalientes, but only one of those is dedicated to thermal systems.
ZF subsidiary shifts production from Canada to Mexico

A TRW Automotive production plant in Tillsonburg, Ontario, will shut down by the end of the year, after shifting all of its operations to Mexico, a spokesman recently confirmed to local media. The facility, which employs 71 workers, began production in 1979 and remained in operations after TRW was acquired by German manufacturer ZF Group. The plant has been dedicated to production of a wide range of linkage suspension and cast products, including upper/lower control arms, stabilizer links, ball joints, inner/outer ends, hubs, rotors, extenders and differential carriers. The company did not offer any detail regarding the Mexican facility where the operations will be placed.
