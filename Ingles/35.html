For Audi, a second Mexican plant is not a discarded idea

Audi could build a second plant in Mexico, revealed El Sol de Puebla in recent days. According to the newspaper, company officials are vaguely working on the idea of a "mirror plant" inside the San Jose Chiapa complex to build the A3 sedan. The report surfaced the same day Audi's CEO Rupert Stadler said in at the company's annual conference the Puebla facility is about to start building a hybrid version for the Q5. Stadler also revealed a full electric Q5 featuring Audi's e-tron technology is on the way, without commenting further details.
Japan automakers offer small pay hikes aware of Trump policies towards Mexico

Major Japanese automakers notified their labor unions they will offer smaller pay-scale hikes than last year -the second straight yearly fall- amid uncertainty surrounding U.S. President Donald Trump's protectionist stance on imports such as cars made in Mexico and elsewhere. Toyota Motor Corp. offered a raise of US$ 11.47 (1,300 yen) a month, down from last year's US$ 13.23 (1,500 yen). When including the increased allowance for childrearing, the total amount rises to US$ 21.17 (2,400 yen). Nissan Motor Co., offered a pay-scale hike of US$ 13.23 (1,500 yen), half the raise from last year.
Chinese automaker reconsiders plan to build assembly site in Mexico

The possibility of the United States imposing tariffs or a border tax on goods made in Mexico has Great Wall Motors considering building its plant in the U.S. instead, said general manager Wang Fengying. China's biggest maker of SUVs already has a research center in Los Angeles where it is preparing to build U.S.-certified versions of its Haval SUV for sale in 2020, Wang said.
Recession in Brazil helps Mexico lead auto production in Latin America

The recession in Brazil has helped Mexico take the leading position of auto production in Latin America, according to data released by the automotive industry associations of both countries, Brazil's Anfavea and Mexico's AMIA. Brazil produced 2.1 million vehicles in 2016, while Mexico produced 3.4 million units. Mexico's domestic sales hike of 18.6%, to 1.60 million units, helped to take over the ranking.
Falken, Yokohama to supply tires on Jeep Compass in North America

Fiat Chrysler Automobiles has selected tires from Sumitomo Rubber North America and Yokohama Tire Corp. as OE fitments on selected versions of the 2017 Jeep Compass, the car maker's compact four-wheel-drive, four-door hatchback built in Mexico. FCA has chosen SRNA's Falken Wildpeak H/T 01A2 as an OE fitment on the Compass Trailhawk and the all-season Yokohama Geolandar G055 as a fitment on the Compass's Latitude and Limited versions.
Schaeffler Group's automotive division reports growth in revenue, earnings

German auto parts supplier Schaeffler increased its revenue in 2016 to approximately US$ 14.20 billion (EUR 13.3 billion) from US$ 14.09 billion (EUR 13.2 billion) in the previous year. At constant currency, this represents a growth rate of 3.4%. Earnings before financial result and income taxes (EBIT) before special items reached US$ 1,814.5 million (EUR 1,700 million) up from US$ 1,788 million (EUR 1,676 million) in 2015. Schaeffler operates three manufacturing sites, one in the state of Guanajuato and two more in Puebla as well as a distribution center in Mexico City.
Magna joins Ford to develop carbon fiber composite subframe

In pursuit of lower vehicle weight to reduce emissions and improve fuel efficiency, Magna and Ford developed a prototype carbon fiber composite subframe which reduces mass by 34% compared to a stamped steel equivalent. By replacing 45 steel parts with two molded and four metallic parts, the prototype achieves a dramatic 87% reduction in the number of parts. The subframe is the result of a research and development project between both companies to use carbon fiber-reinforced composites in chassis applications.
Chinese firm Ningbo Asiaway Machinery plans investment in San Luis Potosi

China-based Ninbo Asiaway Machinery, a company specialized in aluminum and zinc die cast manufacturing, plans to install a production plant in San Luis Potosi, revealed Gustavo Puente Orozco, State Secretary of Economic. Government officials and Ninbo executives plan to announce next month the exact location, investment figures and how many projected jobs this new endeavor will create.
Michelin to start second phase of Guanajuato plant at the end of March

By the end of March Michelin will start the second phase of its Guanajuato plant construction at the Leon-Bajio Industrial Park. The French firm is scheduled to start operations in 2019 after a US$ 510 million investment. Current work is focused on building the platform, which will clear the way to erect the rest of the structure. A conflict between hopper truck operators threatened to delay the program, but officials assured the situation is being handled accordingly.
Ford issues safety recall for 6,000 Mustang vehicles in North America

Ford is issuing a safety recall for certain 2017 Ford Mustang vehicles to inspect left-hand door handle spring that may have been built with a misoriented return spring, which may cause the door to unlatch during a side impact, increasing the risk of injury in a crash. Ford is not aware of any accidents or injuries associated with this issue. Affected vehicles include certain 2017 Ford Mustang vehicles built at Flat Rock Assembly Plant, January 13, 2017 to January 31, 2017. There are approximately including 5,470 in the U.S., 20 in Mexico and 266 in Canada.
