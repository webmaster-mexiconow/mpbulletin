Hyundai Motor of Mexico is offering an extended guarantee and credit extension to its clients

As support before the emergency caused by COVID-19, Hyundai Motor of Mexico is expressing its solidarity with its clients by offering two additional benefits. In the case of the extended guarantee, the automotive company has decided to extend the coverage of service guarantee up to July 31st, 2020 if it expires between April 1st and June 30th. Hyundai Motor of Mexico in collaboration with BBVA, created a support program for its clients in the country, with an extension of up to four months in the payment of their automotive credit.

Honda of Mexico extends halt of operations until April 30

Honda of Mexico said that it will extend the halt of operations until April 30 in both of its automotive plants in Mexico, due to the impact caused by the coronavirus. Honda's installations are located in Celaya, Guanajuato and El Salto, Jalisco. "Honda of Mexico informs that as the market evolves in regards to the COVID-19, it will continue to evaluate the conditions and adjust its production operations in the country as necessary," said the brand.

General Motors will produce masks in Mexico

General Motors of Mexico announced that at the end of this month it will begin the production of 9 million surgical level 1 masks in its manufacturing complex in Toluca in the State of Mexico. A part of these masks will be destined for donation to public hospitals located in Mexico City, State of Mexico, San Luis Potosi, Guanajuato and Coahuila, states where the company has operations and the other part will be used for employees and distributors.

The automotive industry will recover in 2022: IHS Markit

Specialized consulting firm IHS Markit expects that the global automotive industry will recover in 2022. "After a grim 2020 when deep cuts are expected, 2021 should offer some growth, although we won't see the levels before COVID-19, which is likely to materialize in 2022 and beyond," said the consulting firm in a report. In this way, it's expected for advanced research projects to be affected in 2020 and 2021 in comparison with general product development activities.

Toyota premieres new microsite to protect its clients

Toyota Mexico put into operation the microsite #YoMeQuedoEnCasa, whose main objective is to protect clients that seek to acquire a new vehicle, by carrying out the whole purchase process from the comfort of their home. On top of carrying out this process remotely and receiving the vehicle at their home, users will enjoy attractive incentives in different models, which will change month to month. Toyota of Mexico is contemplating to continue with this project for at least the next 3 months in the 94 sales points from their distributor network.

Metalsa and Nemak have enough liquidity to overcome the economic slowdown

The risk rating firm, Standard & Poor's (S&P), confirmed the ratings and maintained a stable perspective for Metalsa and Nemak, since both Mexican auto parts providers have financial flexibility, as well as a healthy liquidity during 2020, which will allow them to overcome the current economic slowdown caused by the COVID-19 health emergency. Additionally, both auto parts companies have started a contraction strategy which includes the reduction of 20 to 40% in investment spending and the suspension of all non-essential output cash flows.

New vehicle imports see a decrease of 16.2% in the year's first quarter

In 2020's first quarter 18,814 vehicles entered the country, number which is 16.2% less to that reported in the same period of last year, being the biggest negative variation since 2009, on top of being the lowest volume in 5 years, according to data from INEGI. Asia represented 57.8% of Mexico's total new vehicle imports in the first three months of 2020; the participation last year was of 59.5%. The importation of vehicles from the United States and Canada, Mexico's main commercial partners, fell 19.9% between January and March of this year.
