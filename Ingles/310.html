Nissan Mexicana will create a Technological Innovation Center in the country

Nissan Mexicana announced the creation of a new Technological Innovation Center in our country, which will be located in the installations of the brand's new corporate building in Mexico City, informed Luciana Herrmann, director of Corporate Communication for the brand. Although she didn't give any more details for this project, the director said that it's a bet on Nissan's part to digitalize internal systems in the development of new technologies.

The Sierra AT4 CarbonPro arrives to national territory

GMC announced the start of sales in Mexico of the Sierra AT4 CarbonPro, a special limited edition of 100 units in metallic silver of the new generation of this pickup, equipped with the industry's first carbon fiber box. GMC Sierra AT4 CarbonPro is equipped with the connectivity system and personalized OnStar assistance, as well as a Wi-Fi hotspot to connect up to 7 devices at the same time and remote connectivity with the vehicle through the mobile app MyGMC App. It will be available starting this month and will have a price of MXN$1,162,900.

Toyota commercializes 7,844 units in February

Toyota managed to commercialize 7,844 units in February, number with which it covered its monthly sales volume. During February, the five vehicles with the biggest sales volume were RAV4 with the sale of 1,346 units, Hilux with 871, Corolla with 780, Avanza with 750 and Yaris SD with 728 units, representing approximately 57% of total sales for the company this month. As for hybrid sales, there were a total of 1,069 commercialized units, which represented about 14% of total sales for the company in February.

Lincoln opens new Boutique Lincoln Puebla

Lincoln Mexico is continuing with its strategy and now, the distributor Lincoln located in the "city of angels" has been completely renewed to join the brand's latest trend with the name: Boutique Lincoln Puebla. Vehicles sales experienced their first increase in 32 months

Total vehicle sales in the internal market registered in February of 2020 their first positive variation in little over a year. In Mexico there were 104,328 commercialized vehicles, which implied an annual growth of 0.32% (in February of 2019 there were 103,989 sold vehicles). This result meant an increase from the negative trend followed by the automotive industry for almost three years now. The brand dominating the national market is Nissan, 20 out of 100 purchased cars in the country are from the Japanese brand.

Daimler Trucks Mexico qualified as Great Place to Work

Daimler Trucks Mexico managed to occupy the 10th place within the Great Place to Work ranking in the category of companies with more than 5,000 collaborators. This certification recognizes the good practices of companies so that the people working for them can do their work in optimal conditions. "Occupying the 10th place in Great Place to Work in Mexico 2020 reflects the effort, passion and commitment of every single person that forms part of the Daimler Trucks Mexico family," said the company in a press release.

Supply chain in the Bajio will be affected because of the COVID-19

Supply chains, mainly those for the automotive industry, in the Bajio will be affected because of the coronavirus. The director of Analysis and Trading Strategy of Financial Group Monex, Carlos González Tabares, highlighted that the participation that Chinese companies have in the production processes of the companies operating in the region, and in the country, is high. González mentioned that while, we can't yet know the total effects that the pandemic will have in the local and national economy, it's estimated that at least the first two quarters of this year will be tough.
