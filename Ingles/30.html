27 February, 2017
Bridgestone to keep NAFTA operations unchanged

Bridgestone has no plans to adjust its operations in response to President Donald Trump's policies, Masaaki Tsuya, CEO of the world largest tire maker, said during a briefing before Japanese media. The company has 19 tire-related plants in North America, 15 in the U.S., three in Mexico and one in Canada. Tsuya said, "If NAFTA changes, Mexico could still be used as an export base targeting the South American market, which is still growing."
Daimler Trucks promotes Saltillo plant Manager

Daimler Trucks North America announced a series executive appointments to its aftermarket and operations areas, effective June 1, 2017. Among those appointments is Sungsoo Lee's, currently Plant Manager of Saltillo Truck Manufacturing, as General Manager, Operations Planning & Quality Management, Daimler Trucks North America. In his new position, Lee will be responsible for directing and leading the activities of Customer Application Engineering, Quality Management, Six Sigma and Production Planning.
Dana Inc. earnings increased 29% in 2016

Dana Inc., a power-technologies and driveline supplier, announced its adjusted earnings increased 29% in 2016, before interest and taxes, to US$ 166 million, up from US$ 129 million in 2015. Dana benefited from higher sales volume, favorable foreign currency effects and improved performance in the commercial vehicle segment. Dana Inc., formerly Dana Holding Corp., is headquartered near Toledo, Ohio. Over the NAFTA region Dana operates 23 facilities, 12 locations in U.S., six in Mexico and five in Canada.
Japanese airline ANA launches route to Mexico City

All Nippon Airways (ANA) began running flights between Narita Airport near Tokyo and Mexico City, targeting business travelers at Japanese companies, particularly automakers. Despite the recent shift into protectionist policy in the U.S., particularly towards Mexico, "there's been no change in reservation trends," ANA's President Osamu Shinobe said at the opening ceremony. The maiden flight to Mexico City was about 97% full, according to a press release.
Toyota again named the 'Most Admired' Auto Company by Fortune

Toyota was named by Fortune as number 34 of the "World's Most Admired Companies" and number 1 Auto company for the third consecutive year in its annual ranking. Fortune's survey measures nine attributes considered critical to a company's global success, including quality of products and services, social and environmental responsibility, global competitiveness, and value as an investment, among others.
Nidec plans to expand steel press machinery output for Mexican market

Japanese manufacturer Nidec announced plans to expand its industrial press machinery business with an eye on growing demand for high-tensile automotive steel in the U.S. and Mexico. The company will build a plant in Ohio in order to supply both markets. As automakers demand to reduce car weight, companies are turning to light-weight, high-tensile steel. Nidec plans to build servo presses that offer greater versatility in pressure and speed than hydraulic models and are better suited to work with thin steel of very high strength.
Chrysler recalls nearly 75,000 cars for loose bolts

Fiat Chrysler says it is recalling about 75,000 cars in the U.S., Canada and Mexico, because driveshaft bolts can come loose on all-wheel drive models, increasing the risk of a crash. The automaker says affected cars include 2014-2017 Dodge Charger and Chrysler 300 vehicles equipped with all-wheel drive. Chrysler says it will notify owners, and dealers will replace all eight front driveshaft bolts for the cars, free of charge. The recall is expected to begin March 31.
Magna warns about protectionism in North America in 4Q brief

Canadian auto supplier Magna International said increased protectionist measures in North America could pose a substantial risk to the company in the future. CEO Don Walker, speaking during the company's fourth-quarter conference, said any border-adjustment tax would negatively impact the entire industry. Magna's Mexican operations make up 14% of total sales, news agency Reuters estimates.
Visteon record performance allows strong 2016 financial results

Visteon Corporation reported full-year sales of US$ 3,161 million, a decrease of US$ 84 million compared to 2015, primarily attributable to the sale of an interiors European facility in 2015. In 2016, global vehicle manufacturers awarded Visteon new business of US$ 5.4 billion, executing a record 59 product launches. Gross margin was US$ 131 million, compared with US $118 million a year earlier. Adjusted EBITDA was US$ 98 million, compared with US$ 83 million in 2015. The improvement was driven by increased production volumes, new business and favorable currency.
Former Honeywell Technologist is named President of Delphi Electronics and Safety

Delphi Automotive PLC named David Paja as President of Electronics and Safety (E&S). "David brings an outstanding track record of technology innovation and operational success to Delphi, having led some of Honeywell International's most successful and complex businesses," said Kevin Clark, Delphi president and CEO. "With his strong background in Internet of Things technologies we look forward to his leadership in expanding our fastest growing business," he added.
