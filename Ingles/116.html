GM leads Mexican auto exports in 2017; Nissan ranks first in production volume

In 2017, General Motors (GM) increased its production volume in Mexico by 14.6% to 805,758 cars, pickups and SUVs, ranking as second biggest auto manufacturer in the country, but also as the automaker with the highest export volume with 693,782 units, a 28.6% hike compared to 2016. Nissan retained its title as the main automaker in Mexico by volume with 829,262 vehicles, but ranked third as exporter with 468,900 units, behind GM and Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA). Meanwhile, FCA Mexico ranked third in production with 638,653 units and second in exports to 599,490 vehicles, which represented hikes of 39.1% y 35.2% respectively.
Volkswagen production in Mexico up 11.2% in 2017; exports hike 15.6%

Volkswagen Mexico ended 2017 with increases of 11.2% in production and 15.6% in exports, totaling 461,248 manufactured units and 386,122 vehicles sent abroad respectively. Of the models produced by the German automaker in Puebla, Mexico, the Jetta led production volume with 205,149 units, ranking second passenger car with the highest manufacturing and export volume in the industry. The Golf, Beetle and Tiguan models are also assembled in this facility. Volkswagen was ranked as the fourth largest automotive company in the country in both production volume and exports. However, it is worth noting that the company operates just one production facility in the country. The assembly plant reached its production peak in 2014 with 475,121 cars.
Audi confirms it exceeded production goal at Mexican plant by 5.7%

In 2017, Audi assembled 158,550 Q5 SUVs during its first full year of operations at the new plant in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla, the company confirmed in a statement. This volume exceeds by 5.7% the company's 2017 production goal, which was 150,000 units. Earlier this month a union leader revealed to local media that Audi workers in Puebla were granted an additional day off in January for fulfilling the company's goal. The Puebla-made vehicle was Audi's best-selling model in the United States during 2017 with 57,640 units, which represented a 16.3% hike. Last year Audi sold a total 226,511 cars and SUVs in the U.S. market, so basically, one in four vehicles was a Q5.
Kia releases renderings of the third-generation Forte sedan

Ahead of the global debut of the all-new 2019 Kia Forte sedan at the upcoming North American International Auto Show in Detroit, Kia Motors revealed design renderings of the compact sedan, which current version is built at its Mexican facility located in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon. The third-generation Forte retains its sporty image, thanks to a number of sleek and dynamic styling cues inspired by the Stinger fastback sport sedan. What is most noticeable is that the Forte has evolved from a cab-forward design to one that is decidedly swept-back. Creases in the hood lend to the Forte's muscular appearance and distinctive design traits on the front fascia. The front clip is flanked by a more dynamic headlamp design and layout.
Sales of electrified cars in Mexico hiked 34.6% during October

After four months in a row of decreases, sales of electrified vehicles in Mexico grew 34.6% during last October to 985 hybrid and electric cars, according to the latest figures from the AMIA. Such sales comprised 967 hybrid units and 18 full electric cars. From January through October, Mexican auto dealers sold 8,268 electrified cars, representing a 32.8% hike compared to the same ten-month period of 2016. Nearly half of October sales in Mexico, a 44.6%, took place in the country's capital due to environmental regulations that grant benefits for those who owned this type of drivetrain. Second place on sales volume is the neighboring State of Mexico with a 13.3%. Residents in the State of Mexico are subject to same regulations when entering Mexico City.
Ford urges 2,900 Ranger pickup owners to stop driving after new Takata death

Ford Motor Co. announced it had confirmed a second death in an older pickup truck caused by a defective airbag inflator of Takata Corp, urging 2,900 owners in North America to stop driving immediately until they can get replacement parts. The automaker said it confirmed in late December that a July 2017 crash death in West Virginia in a 2006 Ford Ranger was caused by a defective Takata inflator. It previously reported a similar death in South Carolina that occurred in December 2015. The new recall affects 2,900 vehicles. These include 2,700 in the United States and nearly 200 in Canada. Mazda Motor Corp is expected to conduct a similar recall for some 2006 Mazda B-Series trucks, which were built by Ford and are similar to the Ranger.
There's common ground in NAFTA's regional content issue, says Mexican official

Ildefonso Guajardo, secretary of Economy, said there is a common ground on a key objective of the U.S. in the NAFTA talks, arguing that the strengthening of regional content standards for automobiles offers an opportunity to achieve a victory for the three nations. " The traceability list for some automotive components needs to be reconsidered in light of new technologies," Ildefonso Guajardo said Tuesday during a meeting of Mexican diplomats in Mexico City as reported by El Financiero. The United States has proposed raising the so-called rules of origin from the current 62.5% to 85% of NAFTA content and requiring a new minimum content of 50% of the US. Guajardo did not explicitly mention a minimum percentage of regional content higher in his description of a reinforced rule.
Mexico-made Nissan NP300 Frontier named “Best Pick Up” by Brazilian press

The Nissan NP300 Frontier version produced at the CIVAC plant in Cuernavaca, Mexico has been recognized again, now as the "Best Pick Up" at the prestigious "The Elegies" (Os Escolhidos) awards, organized by the specialized press in Brazil. With the latest award, Nissan NP300 Frontier has already been recognized eight times by the specialized press as the best pickup of the year. Those awards are: Abiauto, Auto Esporte, Carpress, Car Magazine Brazil, Robb Report, Los Elegidos, L'Auto Preferita and Car and Driver Brasil - 10Best. The Nissan NP300 Frontier pickup features a 166-horsepower engine and 178 lb-ft. of torque. Today the pickup is sold in 33 markets and is already the best-selling vehicle of the brand in South America.
German manufacturer Mann+Hummel opens its second plant in Queretaro

The Mann+Hummel Group, a German manufacturing company specialized in filtration components, inaugurated its second plant in Queretaro and third overall in Mexico. The company invested US$ 15 million and recruited 150 workers to start up operations at the Queretaro Industrial Park. By the end of year total workforce at the plant will double, state government officials said in a statement. Mann+Hummel plans to manufacture 15 million filters a year at the new facility. Other production sites are located in Queretaro and Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila.
Lohr Group invests US$ 46 million to manufacture car transporter systems in Tlaxcala

Lohr Group, a French manufacturer of car transporter systems, announced plans to build a manufacturing plant in Tlaxcala state to supply products for both North and South America's markets. Lohr's Mexican subsidiary acquired a 70,000 square-feet greenfield in the municipality of Calpulalpan, where it plans to invest US$ 10.8 million and recruit 182 workers in order to start operations by the end of year. The company expects to make an additional US$ 35.6 million investment over the next five years to expand operations. Once the plant becomes fully operational its total workforce will be 240 employees while production capacity will be 400 units a year. Approximately 85% of the production will be exports.
