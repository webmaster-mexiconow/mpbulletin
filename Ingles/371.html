The 2021 Escalade will start its commercialization in mid-November

Cadillac announced that in mid-November it will begin selling the 2021 Escalade in Mexico. The fifth generation of this iconic luxury SUV has been completely redesigned and incorporates innovative technologies that are pioneers in the industry and stand out in its segment. The 2021 Cadillac Escalade features a new standard 6.2L V-8 engine with 425 hp and 460 lb-ft of torque that includes variable valve timing, stop/start and Dynamic Fuel Management technologies, which balance performance with significant efficiency.

Delta Tecninc invests US$5.3 million in Querétaro

With an investment of US$5.3 million, the Catalan company Delta Tecnic, specialized in the production and distribution of color concentrates and raw materials for plastics, cables, inks, paints and cosmetics, is expanding its international activity with the opening of a new plant in the municipality of Pedro Escobedo, in Querétaro. From this plant, the company will supply its products throughout the American continent, with a focus on the automotive industry. This new plant is expected to begin operations either at the end of 2020 or in the first quarter of 2021.

Mexico gains share in the U.S. automotive market

Mexico gained market share in the U.S. automotive import market from January through August 2020, which decreased, however, by 28.8% when compared to the same period of 2019. According to data from the Department of Commerce, Mexico's share of total U.S. automotive imports climbed from 36.2% in the first eight months of 2019 to 36.5% in the same period of 2020. Mexico is the second largest supplier of passenger vehicles to the U.S. market, the largest supplier of buses, trucks and special purpose vehicles, and the largest supplier of auto parts.

1,766 green vehicles were sold during July

During the seventh month of 2020, 1,766 ecological vehicles were sold in Mexico, which represented a reduction of 14.60% as compared to the 2,068 units placed during the same month of 2019, according to figures from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). The three states with the highest demand for "green" vehicles during July of this year were Mexico City (484), Jalisco (183) and the State of Mexico (264); together, the three states account for 52.71% of the total number of green vehicles sold.

J.D. Power gives recognition to BMW as the best rated luxury brand

On March 27th of this year, J.D. Power announced BMW as the best rated luxury brand in its Mexico 2020 Sales Satisfaction study. "We are very grateful for this recognition given by J.D. Sales, production and export of heavy vehicles exhibit falls during September

According to information published by INEGI, the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT) announced that, during the month of September, a total of 2,059 units were sold wholesale, which means a drop of 23.2% compared to the same month last year. Production of heavy vehicles totaled 13,523 units, which compared to the 16,942 units manufactured during the same month in 2019, represents a decrease of 20.2%. Exports during September fell 15.6%, with a total of 12,139 heavy vehicles, 2,239 units less than those exported during September 2019.
