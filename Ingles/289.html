Heavy vehicle sales decrease in November

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) informed that, during the month of November, the wholesale sale of heavy and commercial vehicles reached a total of 2,170 units, which compared to the 3,994 registered in November 2018, represent a decrease of 45.7%. During said month, the retail sales of heavy and commercial vehicles reached 3,026 units which, compared with those commercialized during the same month of 2018, mean a drop of 19.5%.

Julian Electric invests US$10 million in Coahuila

Julian Electric is a manufacturing company that produces cables and harnesses for the automotive industry, particularly heavy vehicles. The end of the construction of this new plant is projected for September 2020, and saw an investment of US$10 million and will create 450 direct jobs. Kenny Julian, CEO of Julian Electric, said that in Coahuila, in Saltillo, they found skilled and productive labor, which makes the region strong, and played a hand in their motivation to decide on this zone for their new plant.

AMIA adjusts their estimates for this year downwards

After consecutive months in the drop of vehicle production and exports, the automotive industry is slowing down as a result of the contraction of world markets, which is why the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA) is adjusting their estimates downwards for this year. "It's going to be difficult to reach the numbers we had established in our predictions, we think we might reach a number close to 3,757,000 produced vehicles in 2019, with an exportation of 3,357,000 vehicles, which would imply a fall close to 4% this year for production and a fall of 2.7% in exports," said Eduardo Solis, president of AMIA.

AMDA trusts that the extension of the decree that regulates the importation of vehicles will be extended

The National Automotive Dealer Association (AMDA) hopes for the Ministry of Economy to extend the decree that regulates the permanent importation of used vehicles that's been in effect since July 2011 and that expires on December 30th. Guillermo Rosales, deputy director general of the association, said in conference that this decree is fundamental so that there's a control over the importation of used vehicles, which affects the internal market.

Volkswagen Mexico expects 2020 to be a stable year production and sales wise

The Volkswagen Mexico plant expects 2020 to be a stable year in both production and sales, since despite the contraction in the domestic market, exports were positive, expressed Mauricio Kuri Curiel, director of Corporate Communication and Government Relations. He commented that the production of a new truck in Puebla by the German automaker, located in the municipality of Cuautlancingo, implies that there's quality and this means that they're always striving for new projects.

KIA Motors Mexico donated cars and transmissions to technical schools in Nuevo Leon

To bring students from the country's north east closer to the latest technology, KIA Motors Mexico donated cars and vehicle transmissions to technical schools and universities of the region. The donated vehicles are two family vehicles KIA Sedona and five sedans KIA Optima, which formed part of the company's fleet. On top of that, 122 KIA Forte transmissions will also be donated so that students may use them in their programs.

2,466 hybrid and electric vehicles were sold during September

With information published by INEGI, the Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA) revealed the sales report for hybrid and electric vehicles. According to the numbers published by INEGI, during September 2019 the sale of hybrid and electric vehicles was of 2,466 units, 48.6% above that registered for the ninth month of 2018. The sales were made up by 26 electric cars, 101 plug-in hybrids and 2,339 hybrids.

The limited edition Wolfsburg of the Jetta arrives to Mexico

One of every five compact sedans sold in Mexico this year was a Volkswagen Jetta. To keep boosting sales, the German automaker is releasing the Wolfsburg Edition in our country, a limited edition charged with an extra dose of equipment and a distinguished appearance. Its equipment is based on the Comfortline version. The rest of its configuration keeps the same features characteristic to the Jetta range. It's only available with an automatic six speed transmission. The Volkswagen Jetta Wolfsburg Edition is already available in the brand's distributors in Mexico and its price is of MXN$380,890.

Continental inaugurated new Human Relations Center in Silao

For the technology company Continental the most important thing is both the personal and professional development of their workers. Because of this, the Human Relations Center within the installations of the FIPASI Silao Plant was inaugurated; with this project, in which around MXN$25 million were invested, Continental is preparing to keep and grow the talent of both its current and future workers. In this plant, which houses the Human Relations Center, there's around 1,800 people working and is dedicated to the production of diverse tire speed sensors, motors and transmissions for automobiles.
