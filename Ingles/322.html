Nissan Mexicana names Jose Carlos Dominguez as senior director of Human Resources

Nissan announced the naming of Jose Carlos Dominguez Silva as senior director of Human Resources of Nissan Mexico, to further strengthen the company's leadership team in our country. In his new position within Nissan Mexicana, Jose Carlos Dominguez Silva is responsible of leading the change in the Japanese company's organizational culture and maximizing the talent of its collaborators under Nissan's vision of moving people to a better world. Carlos Dominguez reports to Jose Roman, president and general director of Nissan Mexico.

Heavy vehicle exports and production drop in February

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), reported that during February, the production of motor transport vehicles reached a total of 12,323 units, representing a decrease of 24.3%, when compared to the same month of last year. Likewise, in February there was a total export of 10,500 units, which meant a decrease of 23.0% in relation to the same month of 2019. During the year's first two months, there were 25,637 produced units, which represents a decrease of 21.4% Accumulated exports reached a total of 22,161 units, which meant a drop of 19.6%.

Ford Explorer's sixth generation is available in our country

In 2020, Ford Explorer is turning 30 and its sixth generation was presented at the beginning of last year worldwide and it's finally available in Mexico. All available versions in our country have a capacity for seven passengers and only the high performance ST version includes integral traction. The engine powering the XLT and Limited versions is a turbocharged 2.3 liter EcoBoost, capable of generating 300 hp and 310 lb-ft. With the Explorer ST, Ford's emblematic SUV enters the Performance family. Its engine is a V6 3.0 liter EcoBoost, which generates 400 hp and 415 lb-ft.

FCA Mexico donates 100,000 face masks to public health institutions

Continuing FCA's initiatives and programs to support relief efforts related to the COVID-19, FCA Mexico Foundation donated 100,000 surgical face masks to public health institutions in the states of Coahuila, State of Mexico and Mexico City, where FCA Mexico has a presence. This donation is part of FCA's relief plan being carried out in several regions throughout the world, but particularly in North America. In Mexico, the first of these actions was the delivery of groceries to vulnerable families.

It's urgent for the heavy vehicles sector to be defined as essential: ANPACT

Miguel Elizalde, president of the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), indicated that it's urgent to define this industry as "essential" in Mexico, considering that, in contrast, the United States government already has classified cargo and transport companies as such, as well as their entire value chain, support processes and services, which by not applying similar measures in the country will affect the manufacturing rhythm of companies within the sector in North America.

The automotive sector hopes to restart operations at the beginning of May

A little over a month of announcing a halt in operations, the country's automotive sector is readying itself to go back; the estimated date for vehicle production to get back on track is May 4. "The dates we have are on May 4 for practically all companies, only Nissan is aiming for the 3rd. They're all set on the 4th, however, there's a proposal from the United States government where it asks the automotive industry to return a few days before they return," commented Rolando Alanís, president of Guanajuato's Automotive Cluster.

Audi and Estrella Roja donate transport for medical personnel

Puebla's Governor, Luis Miguel Barbosa Huerta, confirmed that Audi and Estrella Roja will donate for a month 40 units to transport medical personnel from their homes to their place of work and vice versa. Audi will lend 21 units to the cause, while Estrella Roja will contribute 19 buses. This measure originated from the intention of ensuring the safety and integrity of medical personnel; in this way they seek to avoid physical attacks toward them, or some form of discrimination.

Ford will produce 100,000 face shields in Chihuahua

American automaker Ford announced the start of production of 100,000 face shields in its Chihuahua plant to support Mexican society in light of the COVID-19 pandemic. The first 20,000 will be donated to government health institutions in the states of Chihuahua, Sonora, Guanajuato and State of Mexico, regions where Ford has a manufacturing presence. The other 60,000 will be delivered to the federal government with the purpose of being distributed between health personnel, doctors and specialists dealing with patients diagnosed with COVID-19 in the front lines.

Kia releases microsite to protect its customers during the contingency

Kia Motors Mexico, through its distributor network, is offering its clients a new option to reserve any of the brand's models, with the goal of ensuring their purchase without ever leaving home as an alternative during the contingency. Through the microsite apartatukia.com, clients can chose and reserve any of the brand's vehicles. The registration process is very easy, since they only ask for some personal data; once in the microsite clients can choose the model, version and color.

Concamin urges for the restart of operations to happen in par with the United States and Canada

The Confederation of Industrial Chambers (Concamin) raised its voice and urged Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador's government to consider the global chains such as the automotive, aerospace and electronic as essential to restart operations and for these to be homologated with its commercial partners in the United States and Canada, as soon as possible, since there's the risk of breaking value chains in North America. Eduardo Solis, representative of Concamin in value chains, alerted that in the automotive branch, "speaking of broken chains means that a company in the United States might not be able to open due to the lack of Mexican parts."
