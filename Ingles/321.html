Profeco recalls Fiat 500 vehicles

The Federal Consumer Prosecution (Profeco) alerted on the recall effectuated by Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA Mexico) in regards to 2012-2019 Fiat 500 vehicles, commercialized by the company because of transmission failures. "This is due to the fact that in some of the vehicles, the cable selector in the transmission shift could suffer a premature wear in the hub, which could cause the vehicle to not make the correct transmission shifts," explained Profeco in a statement.

Citio will begin operations in Mexico City

Citio, the innovative and pioneering sustainable mobility company in Mexico and Latin America will operate through its Swap & Go model. The service will give access to a lithium battery exchange network with more than 50 different stations within Mexico City, which will also work as recharging points in strategic roads. Together with Italika, this start-up will solve the sustainable mobility problem for all those people that are looking to travel comfortably, in less time and accessibly.

Coparmex asks AMLO to consider the automotive industry as an essential sector

Gustavo de Hoyos, president of the Mexican Employers' Association (Coparmex) urged President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador to carry out the agreement that would allow the country's automotive industry to restart operations in a coordinated manner with the rest of the world. The business leader asked the General Health Council to publish an agreement in the Federal Official Gazette, designating the automotive industry settled in Mexico, including its supply chain and commercialization as "essential sector of the economy" during the health emergency caused by the coronavirus pandemic.

For more information click here: https://bit.ly/3czCXpm
The automotive industry should restart operations on par with the Unites States

Manuel Montoya, president of Mexico's Automotive Industry Cluster Network, said that the United States will restart its plant activities in May, which is why it's asking for auto parts from our country. "The American plants have announced that they will restart operations in May, because in the United States the automotive industry is considered essential. Then they will begin producing vehicles and are asking for auto parts plants to begin operations so that they can give support, because if the parts don't arrive they can't carry out their activities," he said.

For more information click here: https://bit.ly/2XSohOe
Queretaro joins call for the automotive industry to be considered as essential

The automotive industry is one of the branches of the economy that has suffered the most because of the impact caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, due to the halt experienced by the sector throughout the world. Thus, both in Queretaro and throughout the country, it's being requested for the automotive industry to be considered as essential, since it's considered non-essential as of right now, which is why it needs to halt operations during the duration of the health emergency, indicated the president of the Queretaro branch of the Mexican Employers' Association (Coparmex), Lorena Jimenez Salcedo.

For more information click here: https://bit.ly/3cyoERV
Lincoln of Mexico is offering support to its clients during the pandemic

Rosangela Guerra, director of Lincoln Mexico, highlighted that the company is supporting its clients through this quarantine through Ford Credit programs. The first one is for clients with Lincoln financing, and it consists of postponing the vehicle payment is for 3 months. The second one is a financing plan for those interested in acquiring a new vehicle from the brand, who will be able defer their first payment by 90 days.

The Mercedes AMG A 45 S arrives to Mexico

The 2020 Mercedes-AMG A 45 arrives to Mexico with the world's most potent turbo four cylinder engine, which with 2.0 liters it's capable of generating 421 hp and 368 lb-ft. It works with a dual-clutch eight speed automatic transmission and an integral traction system 4MATIC+, to reach 100 km/h in only 3.9 seconds. The price for the Mercedes-AMG A 45 in Mexico is of MXN$1,269,900.
