Audi will deliver documentation to the government of Puebla to be audited

In view of the closure of the Audi plant in Puebla by the City Council of San Jose Chiapa, due to the debt of various taxes, the company said it will deliver the required documentation and open its doors to be audited at the time that the authorities decide. In a statement, the automaker said that it is a company that practices values and human rights. In addition, in order to maintain a prosperous relationship, the company is in permanent contact with federal, state and municipal authorities; the application received from the municipal government has been reviewed and evaluated by the company.

Hyundai presents the 2021 Hyundai Creta

The 2021 Hyundai Creta will arrive to Mexico in the coming weeks and uses a new platform, shared with Kona and Seltos. Thanks to this new architecture, it is able to extend its wheelbase and increase its size up to 4.3 meters long. The 2021 Hyundai Creta range in Mexico is composed of four versions: GL, GLS, GLS Premium and Limited Turbo. Starting from the most accessible version aluminum wheels, automatic headlight start, electrical equipment, second row A/C ducts, tire pressure monitor, infotainment with screen from 8″, four speakers and compatibility with Android Auto and Apple CarPlay are included.

Ford asks federal government for stability and investment security

Hector Perez, CEO of Ford Mexico, requested the federal government to provide stability and investment security to automotive industry entrepreneurs in light of the new rules established in the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA), the constant blockades of railroad tracks and the growing insecurity that prevent the normal flow of goods, since they affect the reactivation of the industry and generate uncertainty for new investors. According to the executive, the USMCA represents investment opportunities for Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers of auto parts and inputs, as automakers seek to increase the value of regional content (RVC).

SUV and pickup manufacturing will be the engine of the Mexican automotive industry

The new SUV and pickup truck manufacturing facilities in Mexico, among which Ford's Bronco Sport and VW's Taos stand out, will be positioned as the engine of the automotive industry in the coming months and will lead to the expected growth of Mexican exports, mainly to the U.S. market, anticipated Guido Vildozo, managing partner of IHS Markit's North American light vehicle sales. Due to the investments made by the automotive industry in Mexico over the past decade, the companies began to replace their production lines for compact and sub-compact vehicles in the segment of greater demand in the United States, such as SUVs and pickups.

Kenworth del Bajio celebrates its fifteenth anniversary

Kenworth del Bajio, is celebrating on October its fifteenth anniversary in one of the areas with greater economic boom in our country, and therefore, key in cargo transport. The history of this dealership goes back 50 years, when it used to be part of Kenworth del Centro. It was on October 11, 2005 when Kenworth del Bajio emerged as an independent dealership, forming a new strategic arm of the BACE group. Today, Kenworth del Bajio has presence in the states of Guanajuato, Queretaro and San Luis Potosi through 7 cities, with a portfolio that exceeds 3,000 active clients in all its business units.

The Mexican automotive industry will face several challenges in post-pandemic recovery

The high dependency on the United States for vehicle exports, lack of qualified labor, lower sales in the domestic market and political uncertainty are the main risks that the Mexican automotive industry will face after the pandemic. Francisco Bautista, leading partner of EY's Advanced Manufacturing and Mobility Segment, said that dependence on U.S. automotive exports is a risk for Mexico because the chain is highly integrated to that market, but if sales in that country do not rise once the pandemic passes, the sector will be greatly affected.

Assembly of the new Volkswagen Taos begins in Puebla

Upon presenting the first assembly of the Taos to the world from the Puebla plant, Volkswagen de Mexico reported that it invested US$400 million to begin production of its fourth vehicle on the Mexican platform and bet on the vehicle segment that is growing internationally. Steffen Reiche, President of Volkswagen Mexico, informed that the Taos reaches the segment of compact SUVs that is growing worldwide, in the midst of the pandemic and the economic crisis, and that it already has a 30% share of sales in the international market.
