Mexican car production, export reach record high in February

Mexico's production and export of cars reached a record high in February, rising 11.1% and 9.7% respectively year-on-year. AMIA data shows the country produced a total of 301,475 light vehicles and exported 240,909 of them, both figures are the highest for this month ever. In the first two months of this year combined, Mexico produced 580,017 cars and exported a total of 452,591, up 7.7% and 4.5% respectively compared to figures from the same period of 2016.
No plans to move production out of Mexico, Renault-Nissan CEO reiterates

Renault-Nissan has no plans to move production out of Mexico despite threats of tariffs on trade between the U.S. and Mexico, CEO Carlos Ghosn told CNBC in a recent interview. "I'm not so worried because frankly I think NAFTA is so much in the interest of all parties," said Ghosn. "We are used to dealing with different countries, political change, agreement change, and this is not an exception," he added.
BMW production chief not worried about Trumps policies on Mexico

Despite warnings from President Trump, BMW Group said it will stay committed to its plans to build a plant in Mexico. "We need free world trade," BMW CEO Harald Krueger told the CAR-Symposium automotive congress in Bochum. Meanwhile, production manager Oliver Zipse emphasized that Mexico has free trade agreements with 45 countries - and if the U.S. had to be removed from the list - there were still 44 countries left for the export of the vehicles built in San Luis Potosi.
Aeromexico opens Monterrey-Korea flight

To satisfy the demand and due to the increase in passenger traffic, mainly for automotive business, between Monterrey, Nuevo Leon and South Korea, Aeromexico is starting a direct flight to Seoul. The destination is expected to start operations this upcoming May 25th. According to official data, South Korea is the second country to bring the most FDI into the State, with 15%, after the U.S., who contributed 50%.
Patent infringement case could impact on Ford's Hermosillo plant

Paice, a developer of hybrid vehicle technology, announced that the U.S. International Trade Commission (ITC) has instituted a formal investigation into patent infringement claims against Ford. If Paice prevails, all imports of Ford's hybrid cars, including the Fusion and Lincoln MKZ models built in the Hermosillo plant, would be banned from entering the United States.
Mexico will supply Jeep Compass for Europe and Middle East

Jeep's new Compass model will be supplied to Europe from up to three different plants, starting from the middle of this year. Company chief Mike Manley said right hand drive versions for the UK and Australia will be supplied from India, while Mexico plant is now up and running and ready to start supply of left hand drive models for the rest of Europe and Middle by mid-year. Additional capacity will come out of Brazil if required.
FCA executive for Mexico, recognized as Pinnacle Professional for fifth year in a row

May Leng Yau-Patterson, Head of World Class Manufacturing Mexico Operations for Fiat Chrysler Automobile, has been recognized by Continental Who's Who as Pinnacle Professional in the Automotive industry for the fifth year in a row. She is responsible for leading and coaching plant managers at the assembly, engine and stamping plants throughout Mexico in the implementation of methodologies that focuses on eliminating waste, increasing productivity, and improving quality and safety in a systematic and organized way.
Zoltek to expand carbon fiber production in Mexico

Zoltek Companies Inc. will embark on a large-scale capacity expansion of carbon fiber production at its El Salto, Jalisco, Mexico facility starting in 2017. The added capacity will effectively double Mexico's capacity to more than 10,000 tons of carbon fiber per year. Rapidly growing demand for industrial grade carbon fiber for use in automotive applications is a key driver in the decision to expand, said a company's official.
Cooper Tire relocates offices to Guadalajara

Cooper Tire de Mexico has relocated its offices from El Salto to a new location in Guadalajara within the Los Arrayanes industrial park. The new 22,000-square-foot office spans two floors and houses the sales, marketing, customer service, logistics and finance teams, which serve Cooper's customers throughout Latin America.
GM will lay off 1,100 at Michigan plant after production shift

General Motors will lay off 1,100 workers in May at its Lansing Delta Township assembly plant in Michigan as it shifts production of a vehicle to Tennessee. The largest U.S. automaker last year added 800 jobs at its Spring Hill, Tennessee plant to build a new version of the GMC Acadia SUV. The Lansing plant will continue building the Buick Enclave and Chevrolet Traverse after it retools for a month, GM spokesman Tom Wickham said in an emailed statement.
