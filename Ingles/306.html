Ford donates eight manufacturing robots to schools in Sonora

Ford donated eight high-precision manufacturing robots to universities and a high school in Sonora, with the objective of generating professionalization opportunities for young people that are seeking to work in the automotive industry, through tools that allow them to develop new abilities. The eight delivered robots come from the bodywork division of the Hermosillo plant, in which they carried out autonomous subassembly processes like: vision systems, seal applications, handling tool and welding.

Audi Mexico will reduce its CO2 emissions by 75%

Audi Mexico announced that the electric requirement for production will be covered in its entirety with energy coming from renewable sources. Audi's plant in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla will reduce its CO2 emissions by 75%. The company will cover all of its electric requirements for the production of the Audi Q5 with renewables sources, as the first step towards a production free of CO2 for 2025, within the framework of the MISSION:ZERO strategy.

The BMW X3 xDrive30e arrives to Mexico

As part of BMW Group's electrification strategy, the German brand is releasing in Mexico the new BMW X3 xDrive30e. This vehicle was presented during the CBMM Niobium Mexico City E-Prix from the FIA ABB Formula E Championship. It has a four cylinder gas engine with BMW's TwinPower Turbo technology and an electric motor integrated to the Steptronic 8 speed transmission that allows for an acceleration from 0 to 100 km/h in 6.1 seconds, reaching a maximum speed of 210 km/h. The combined system generates 292 hp and 410 Nm.

Brose invests US$188 million in Queretaro

Brose, German automotive company, materialized investments for US$188 million in the Brose Airport plant, where it will generate 1,100 jobs. The capital to be used in the company's third plant in the state-located in the Aerotech, Colon industrial park-is part of the company's expansion project in the state. The director of the Brose Mexico plant in Colon, Miguel Angel Castillero, explained that this installation is born out of the need to support the supply requirements of its clients in the region, generating the first plant in America that manufactures back seats.

Electro mobility Law will be presented for our country

The senator and minister of the Commission of Urban Development, Land Management and Living, Indira Kempis, announced that the advances of what will be the proposal of the first Electro mobility Law in Mexico will be presented soon. "We have worked during a year, months of work, with my experience and experts in the matter, to release this draft in which we're proposing the existence of infrastructure, laws and incentives for electro mobility in Mexico," she said. Kempis added that experts as well as academics in the matter were involved in the making of the text.

Heavy vehicles production and exports reach historic numbers in 2019

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), informed that, during 2019, the production of heavy vehicles reached a total of 198,100 units which compared to the 176,794 produced in 2018, represent an increase of 12.1%. At the same time, they informed that heavy vehicle exports registered a total of 168,265 units, which compared to the 147,727 exported units during 2018, represents a growth of 13.9%. These numbers are better than those seen in 2015, year in which production and exports of heavy vehicles were at an all-time high.

Bimbo Group continues to encourage sustainable mobility

Under the premise of a sustainable mobility and an investment of US$150 thousand Bimbo incorporated to its fleet 100 electric delivery units from Moldex's Vekstar brand, a Bimbo Group subsidiary, which have 100% Mexican technology and engineering, every unit will avoid the emission of 3.4 tons of carbon dioxide (CO2) a year and will have a reusable lithium battery. In addition to these vehicles, 41 hybrid vehicles were also added to the fleet, being the first of their kind to be integrated to replace part of the company's supervision fleet in Mexico City.
