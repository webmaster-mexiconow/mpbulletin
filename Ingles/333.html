The Mexican automotive industry returns to operations

According to the stipulations and recommendations released by the federal government and the Ministry of Health, the automotive industry is already considered as an essential activity and has started the restart of its activities in a staggered manner. The companies that began the gradual restart of their operations are: Fiat Chrysler, Ford, BMW, Honda, Nissan, and Toyota, all under strict health standards due to the coronavirus and under the need to reactivate production chains with the United States before the USMCA's entry into force.

BMW's Electric Corridor celebrates its first anniversary

During May of last year, BMW Group Lain America inaugurated its first electric corridor in Mexico with an initial extension of 412 kilometers and 6 fast charging stations, it being without cost and multiplatform (adaptable to hybrid and electric vehicles of any brand). With this, the company once again demonstrated its commitment to the electrification projects in the region and strengthened its position as a leader promoter of sustainable mobility. In January 2020, the first expansion of this corridor was announced, with the start of operations of the "Supercarga Capillita" station located in the state of Puebla.

GM reduces Chevrolet Aveo's line in Mexico

The Chevrolet Onix is one of General Motor's strongest bets in Mexico for the next few years. Which is why, the 2020 Chevrolet Aveo yields before the arrival of its older sibling with a reduction in its product line in our country. From now on, the Chevrolet Aveo will no longer be available in the LZT version and the automatic version is out of the portfolio. The line, will then be comprised only of the LS and LY versions with a manual transmission. The Chevrolet Aveo LS and LT will not undergo any equipment changes.

Daimler Trucks Mexico bets on connectivity

Daimler Trucks Mexico presents its integral connectivity strategy made up by Enlace Freightliner 2.0 and the E-CAS, with which it seeks to support the safety and profitability of clients and operators. Enlace Freightliner 2.0 is an electronic tool installed in vehicles to connect them to the internet, with the goal of increasing fleet profitability by strengthening their maintenance, operation, logistic and safety. The E-CAS brings constant accompaniment to clients, through unit and fleet performance analysis.

Clients prioritize brand reputation when buying a vehicle: J.D. Power

According to the study Automotive Consumer Trust in Mexico (CCAM) from consulting firm J.D. Power, carried out during the COVID-19 contingency, it was concluded that to actually buy a new vehicle, brand reputation keeps being the first thing consumers look at with 18%, efficiency in gas consumption comes in at second place with 13%, followed by good vehicle quality and previous experience with the brand, with 11% for each one, according to the results of the study's fourth wave.

Puebla will be home to ANPACT's 2021 Expo Transporte

Expo Transporte ANPACT 2021 will be carried out in the Exhibitor Center Puebla, in the city of Puebla spanning the days 6, 7 and 8 of October, 2021. In its past edition, Expo Transporte ANPACT 2019 had more than 46,800 attendees from 30 countries, had a total of 445 expositors and occupied more than 70,000 m2 in its total exhibition area. In the 2021 edition, the event which reunites in Mexico the auto transport industry worldwide will be centered in offering transport and mobility solutions.
