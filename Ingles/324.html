North America's automotive industry gets ready to restart operations

Mexico, the United States and Canada are currently preparing for North America's automotive industry restart of operations, which are restricted or halted due to the COVID-19 pandemic, informed the Ministry of Economy. "The Mexican government is working in a close, coordinated and permanent manner with authorities in the United States and Canada in order to establish the criteria, guidelines, protocols and conditions that shall be observed to allow and successfully transit into the reopening of productive activities of North America's automotive industry," informed the Ministry of Economy in a press release.

Volkswagen of Mexico adjusts restart of operations dates

Volkswagen of Mexico informed that, following up on the established dates for the temporary halt of manufacturing activities in its plants in Puebla and Guanajuato announced to conclude on April 30 because of the coronavirus pandemic, there will be an adjustment to the previously established calendar for the restart of operations to May 18 for the Puebla plant with dates to be announced for the engine plant established in Silao, Guanajuato; the restart will remain subject to official authorizations that might be released.

For more information click here: https://bit.ly/2SnndOG
Automotive companies will have an extension to comply with USMCA's origin rules

Luz Maria de la Mora, under secretariat of Foreign Trade of the Ministry of Economy, informed that the governments of the three countries are currently in the process of defining the instructions and other operation rules so that North America's automotive industry can comply with the USMCA's rules once it goes into effect. "We're also working in the design of the alternative rules of transition which will allow vehicle assemblers to access longer deadlines to meet the new origin rules," she said.

Return to operations will have to be gradual: AMIA

The return to operations before the health emergency caused by the COVID-19 will have to be gradual for North America's automotive industry and will obey client demand and the new hygiene and health protocols, assured Fausto Cuevas, general director of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA). The director exposed that the phases of the pandemic in North America are different and because of this the return won't be simultaneous, which is why there's still a high level of uncertainty as to how they will restart operations.

Face mask production begins at General Motors' Toluca plant

General Motors of Mexico announced that it formally began the production of level 1 surgical face masks in its manufacturing complex in Toluca in the State of Mexico. Starting this week and during the next six months, General Motors will produce approximately 1.5 million monthly face masks until it reaches 9 million. Of the total of those 9 million masks, one part will be destined for donation to public hospitals located in Mexico City, State of Mexico, San Luis Potosi, Guanajuato and Coahuila, states where the company has operations and another part will be used for employees and distributors.

Kia Motors Mexico donates personal protection equipment in Nuevo Leon

Kia Motors Mexico's president, Jong Kun Lee, announced the donation of personal protection equipment, material which is essential for doctors, nurses and health professionals treating patients affected by COVID-19. "We want to help them have the tools to successfully stop the threat of the coronavirus, with 30,000 biosecurity suits with a hood and 10,000 face shields to be distributed among different public hospitals," he indicated.

Audi will extend the halt of operations to May 31

Audi announced to its workers that before the health contingency caused by the coronavirus, they will be extending the halt of operations for another month, until May 31, time in which employees will receive 60% of their pay. Originally the company's halt of operations was going to last until May 3rd. Through a release directed to workers, the brand's union indicated that there's not yet conditions to return to work due to the fact that the health emergency has been extended into May 30th.
