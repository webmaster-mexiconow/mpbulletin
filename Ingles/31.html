Bulletin #31
2 March, 2017
Nemak opens US$ 200 million plant in Nuevo Leon

Monterrey-based automotive supplier Nemak opened a US$ 200 million manufacturing site to produce aluminum parts in Garcia, Nuevo Leon. The facility will manufacture cylinder blocks and transmission cases for automakers such as Ford, General Motors, Audi and Volkswagen using a high pressure die casting (HPDC) process. On full capacity, the plant and its 610 workers can produce up to 2.2 million parts a year.
Ford starts hiring process for transmission plant in Guanajuato

Ford Motor Company started the hiring process for its new transmission plant scheduled to start operations by the end of this year in Irapuato. At least 300 candidates attended a job fair organized by state labor officials, who helped the job applicants fill the required paperwork, while Ford personnel conducted interviews.
AK Tube opens US$ 10 million plant in Queretaro

U.S.-based steel tube manufacturer AK Tube opened a US$ 10 million plant in Queretaro, its first facility abroad. The new site will manufacture tubes for exhaust systems. Over the next three months the facility will hire up to 70 workers to process around 13,000 tons of tube yearly. The company has targeted assembly plants in Saltillo, Monterrey, Guadalajara, Puebla and Toluca as part of its business plan, according to Ed Urbaniak, AK Tube president.
Grupo Simec reports strong 2016 earnings; new plant in the works

Mexican steelmaker Grupo Simec reported net earnings of US$ 266 million in 2016, compared with a US$ 163 million net loss in 2015. The rebound is attributed to higher domestic sales and increasing steel prices. Simec, a subsidiary of Mexico's Industrias CH, announced in 2015 plans to build a US$ 600 million special bar quality steel plant with a yearly capacity of 600,000 tons aimed for the automotive industry. Production is expected to begin this year.
Vitro completes acquisition of Pittsburgh Glass Works for US$ 310 million

Monterrey-based Vitro announced it completed the acquisition of Pittsburgh Glass Works' (PGW) automotive glass business from LKQ Corporation for US$ 310 million, free of cash and debt. PGW operates seven manufacturing plants, two satellite facilities and two float glass furnaces in the U.S. It also has one manufacturing plant in Poland and owns an equity participation in two joint ventures, one each in Mexico and China.
Toyota grants Contribution Award to Mexican auto supplier Metalsa

Metalsa, a supplier of structural components for light and commercial vehicles, was recognized with the Regional Contribution Award from Toyota Motor Corporation (TMC) for the outstanding contribution to safety, quality, delivery and support provided from the Apodaca Plant in Nuevo Leon, Mexico. TMC Chairman, Takeshi Uchiyamada, presented the award to Leopoldo Cedillo, Metalsa CEO, during the 2017 Annual Global Suppliers Convention in Nagoya, Japan. Metalsa was the only Mexican supplier to be recognized during this event.
Built-in-Toluca new Jeep Compass is put to test in Texas

As Fiat Chrysler prepares the new Jeep Compass arrival at U.S. dealers in late March, automotive media journalists are gathering in San Antonio, Texas to conduct tests on the built-in-Toluca SUV. So far reviews have been positive, like one from Motor Trend by Erick Ayapana, who concluded that it will be a "standout" in the compact SUV segment.
Ildefonso Guajardo to talk NAFTA, US relationship before Detroit Economic Club

Ildefonso Guajardo Villarreal, Mexico's secretary of Economy, will speak March 3 at an event hosted by the Detroit Economic Club at the MotorCity Casino Hotel, to give his country's perspective on the North American Free Trade Agreement and the trade relationship with the U.S. The talk comes after President Donald Trump has repeatedly expressed interest in renegotiating NAFTA.
Wilbur Ross approved by Senate as Trump's commerce secretary

The U.S. Senate voted 72-27 to confirm billionaire Wilbur Ross as Trump's commerce secretary. Known as the "King of Bankruptcy," Ross made a fortune by working to resurrect failing companies in the auto, steel and textile industries, but has also been criticized for shipping jobs overseas at some of those struggling companies. During the hearing, he said its firm International Automotive Components had "no choice" but to move some jobs to Mexico at the request of a major customer.
Plant manager of BorgWarner in Jalisco to receive Women in Manufacturing Award

The Manufacturing Institute, a Washington, DC-based organization dedicated to improving manufacturing, will grant a Women in Manufacturing STEP Ahead Award to Skotti Fietsam, Plant Manager at BorgWarner Morse Systems, a facility located in El Salto, Jalisco. STEP stands for science, technology, engineering and production. The award honors women who demonstrate excellence and leadership at all levels of the manufacturing industry.
