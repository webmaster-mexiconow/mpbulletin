Alternative sales methods gave vehicle sales some relief during May

Quick consumer adaption for the purchase of vehicles online and via phone allowed light vehicle distributors to see a better landscape in light vehicle sales during May, despite the fact that sales floors remained closed due to the COVID-19 pandemic, informed Guillermo Rosales, director of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). This circumstance made it so that vehicles could only be commercialized through a combination of remote sales channels (telephone attention, social media, and websites) and to your door services.

Heavy vehicle sales drop during May

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), informed that, during the last month of May, wholesale sales of heavy vehicles reached a total of 677 units, which compared to the 4,682 registered in May of 2019, represents a fall of 85.5%. At the same time, during May, the retail sales of heavy vehicles reached 1,282 units which, compared to the 3,947 sold during the same month of last year, meant a fall of 67.5%. Miguel Elizalde, executive president of ANPACT, commented that "the fall in sales corresponds to a lower demand derived from the economic effects of the COVID-19 pandemic."

Audi Mexico will restart operations on June 15

Audi Mexico will gradually restart its production operation on Monday June 15 in San Jose Chiapa. The company is in positive dialogue with the authorities from the State of Puebla and with the Ministry of Economy. This has led to the decision to gradually restart operations in the San Jose Chiapa plant on June 15, with 30% of the workforce to begin the production of the Audi Q5 in one turn. Meanwhile, the administrative areas will keep working under the Mobile Working scheme to minimize the risk of contagion.

Most of the automotive companies in the country are not ready to comply with the USMCA

The United States, Mexico, Canada Agreement (USMCA) will go into force on July 1st and will involve some challenges for the Mexican automotive industry. In particular that regarding the new automotive origin rules with which, all companies that desire to export to one of the countries in the treaty will have to comply. Eduardo Solis, member of the Board of the Confederation of Industrial Chambers (Concamin), expressed in a press conference that only 30% of the automotive industry companies will be able to comply with these new rules.

Mexico produces 22,119 vehicles in May

In May there were 22,119 automobiles produced in Mexico, 93.7% inferior to that registered in the same period of last year, informed the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This drop was caused by the suspension of operations in assembly plants across the country, since they weren't considered as an essential activity by the federal government in the face of the COVID-19 pandemic. Audi and Volkswagen were the only two companies that didn't produce a single vehicle during the year's fifth month. While exports in the first five months of the year were of 822,246 units, 42.2% less to that reported in the same period of 2019.

Daimler Trucks restarts operations in North America

After almost three months in which the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic suspended manufacturing activities, Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA) has reopened its nine manufacturing plants in North America. The June 1st reopening of the assembly plants of Saltillo and Santiago in Mexico marked the start of a complete reactivation of operations and the start of the resupply of commercial vehicles for operators worldwide. The Saltillo and Santiago plants are strong nodes in DTNA's production network and help maintain the production of trucks to export to the 35 countries in which DTNA sells commercial vehicles.

Heavy vehicle producers will have to reprogram their supply chains to comply with the USMCA

Last minute changes in the norms about the application of the origin rules in the automotive sector in the new commercial agreement between the United States, Mexico and Canada will make it harder to comply with the obligations in the regional purchase of steel and aluminum. The compliance with these norms, published last Wednesday, is vital to enjoy no tariff in the USMCA, which will entry into force on July 1st. Miguel Elizalde, president of ANPACT, estimated that it will be "a great challenge" for the industry to make the necessary adjustments in such a short time and in the midst of the coronavirus pandemic, although he recognized that there were certain administrative facilities given for the transition.
