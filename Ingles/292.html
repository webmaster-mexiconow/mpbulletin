The Chevrolet Onix will be exported to 38 countries

The subcompact assembled by General Motors in Mexico will cover the Mexican market, as well as countries from Central and South America, Africa and the Middle East. After a few days of the start of production of the subcompact Onix, General Motors Mexico announced that an 80% of what is produced will be destined for the Mexican market while the rest will be exported to 38 countries, without including North America. The Onix’s exportation will begin in February to cover markets like Central and South America, Africa and the Middle East.

Conventus Polymers will create a Mexican subsidiary in Guadalajara

Conventus Polymers has established a Mexican subsidiary in Guadalajara and will open in our country like Conventus Polymers of Mexico. They added that Conventus has been making business in Mexico for several years and that the Mexico expansion is part of the brand’s business strategy to grow in key geographical regions. John Jorgensen, president of Conventus Polymers, said through a press release: “Mexico makes a strategic sense for several reasons, including our additional commitment to key multinational OEMs.”

Despite GM strike, the automotive company will reach the production of 280,000 vehicles in San Luis Potosi

Despite the strike experienced by General Motors in the United States during October, that stopped the production lines of several plants in the countries, the automotive company will reach a production of 280,000 automobiles this year in its San Luis Potosí complex. In conference, Hector de Hoyos, director of the complex, said that this is in line with their projection made at the beginning of 2019, which is similar to that achieved last year. “We are working at 100% capacity, we’re the only complex together with one in Canada that didn’t stop working, which is why we’ll meet orders in Mexico and abroad,” he highlighted.

JAC keeps betting on Mexico, this time with the release of the JAC J7

JAC is continuing with their expansion in Mexico. After announcing a handful of electrical models and completing its SUV segment, the Chinese firm is eyeing the compact sedan segment, where the JAC J7 will face the Volkswagen Jetta, Toyota Corolla, Mazda 3 and company. The engine in the JAC J7 for Mexico is a four cylinder turbocharged 1.5 liter engine, capable of generating 148 hp and 154 lb-ft. All of the information regarding pricing and versions will be released once the release fate is near, it is expected for the second semester of 2020.

Mazda will produce its 1 million vehicle in February of next year

In February 2020, Mazda’s manufacturing plant headquartered in Salamanca, will reach the production of its 1 millionth vehicle, after six years of starting operations on February 26, 2014. This was made known by Miguel Ángel Barbeyto Gómez, president and general director of Mazda Motor of Mexico, who said that in the 14 years of operations in the country, the company has evolved in a consistent manner and that in 2019 it will manage to sell 60,000 vehicles, volume which will allow it to cement itself in the seventh position among the main new car companies in the country.

Mazda Mexico has sold 500,000 units in our country

Mazda will celebrate 15 years in Mexico next year. As a prelude for the celebrations, the Japanese brand is celebrating a milestone in our country with the delivery of its 500,000 units. The car in question was a gray Mazda 3 Hatchbak, which was delivered without charge to its owner. The delivery of this unit coincided with the welcome cocktail celebrated because of the re-inauguration of the Mazda Americas distributor, located in Guadalajara. Mazda began operations in Mexico in October 2005. It currently has 59 distributors in our country.
