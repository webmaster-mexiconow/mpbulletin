Bulletin #13
29 December, 2016
GM tools up Ramos Arizpe plant to build second generation Equinox

The Ramos Arizpe General Motors complex will start assembling the second generation of its Equinox utility vehicle by mid-January 2017 and the necessary adjustments to the production line are being made to this effect, sources revealed to local newspapers in the State of Coahuila, where the plant is located. This new SUV will be built in the Cadillac SRX platform, which is assembled in this facility.
Vitro acquires automotive business from Pittsburgh Glass Works

Mexican glassmaker Vitro SAB announced it agreed to buy the automotive-glass manufacturing operations of Pittsburgh Glass Works, a subsidiary from LKQ Corp. for US$ 310 million, in order to expand its presence in the U.S. market. Vitro's acquisition includes seven manufacturing plants, two satellite facilities and two glass furnaces in the U.S.; a glassmaking plant in Poland; and stakes in two joint-ventures in the U.S. and China.
GM to lay off 1,300 workers, halt assembly lines in five plants

General Motors announced it will lay off almost 1,300 workers beginning March 6, as it cuts one of two production shifts at the Detroit-Hamtramck Assembly plant that builds the Chevrolet Volt and Impala, Cadillac CT6 and Buick LaCrosse, due to lower demand on sedans. Before cutting the second shift, GM plans to stop work at the plant for three weeks in January. It also is scheduling three weeks of downtime at Fairfax Assembly in Kansas City, Kansas, two weeks at the Lansing Grand River plant in Michigan and one week in Lordstown, Ohio, and Bowling Green, Kentucky.
Fiat Chrysler to cut minivan, car production in Canada

Fiat Chrysler will halt production at its two Canadian plants to align supply with demand. The Windsor and Brampton facilities will be down from January 3 to 6. The roughly 9,000 hourly workers at those sites will be on temporary layoff. The Brampton Assembly plant produces the Dodge Charger and Challenger as well as the Chrysler 300 for Fiat Chrysler, while the Windsor Assembly facility produces the automaker's minivans, including the all-new Chrysler Pacifica.
Ford will close Kansas City truck plant for a week

Ford Motor Co will close its Kansas City, Missouri pickup truck and van plant for a week in early January to match production and demand, the automaker announced via press release. The plant makes F-150 pickup trucks and Ford Transit vans. Ford also closed the Kansas City plant for a week a couple of months ago.
GM reveals new 9-speed transmission built in San Luis Potosi

General Motors revealed details about a new built-in-San Luis Potosi nine-speed automatic transmission that improves fuel economy about 2% and will be offered as an option on 10 different models by the end of 2017. The gearbox is already features on 2017 Chevrolet Malibu midsize sedans with 2.0-liter turbocharged engines. It will be available on Chevrolet Cruze compact with diesel engines early in 2017, and on the 2018 Chevrolet Equinox compact SUV later next year.
VW starts to build its big-size SUV at Chattanooga facility

Despite early chatter that it could be built in Mexico, Volkswagen started production at its Chattanooga plant for the Atlas SUV, the biggest vehicle VW has ever made in America. The new unit, which will be sold on dealer lots next year, began rolling off the assembly line in the Tennessee facility after a US$ 900 million upgrade.
Delphi, Mobileye to reveal their self-driving-car technology at CES 2017

Advancements in self-driving-car technology developed by Mobileye and Delphi Automotive will be revealed at the Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas next month. Mobileye, a developer of advanced driver-assistance technology, joined efforts with auto supplier Delphi in August after it parted ways with Tesla Motors in July.
Bosch to add 500 jobs in Juarez plants during 2017

Germany-based Robert Bosch plans to hire 500 workers next year for its manufacturing sites in Ciudad Juarez. The company has a catalogue of more than 50 products manufactured in this city, some of them include: sensors, distance control units, anti-blocking system devices, electrical power steering control, sunroof motors and window lifters. Officials said new products might be manufactured soon in those facilities.
Mexico expands import quota on sheet steel from South Korea

The Mexican government made a preliminary decision on recent days to increase its import quota regarding cold-rolled steel sheets imported from South Korea by 40,000 tons in 2017 and by 60,000 tons in 2018, to add a total of 590,000 tons per year. This measure is expected to contribute to stabilize operations of South Korean manufacturing facilities in Mexico such as POSCO Mexico, Hyundai Motor Company and Kia Motors, since most of South Korea's cold-rolled steel sheet exports to Mexico are used by those companies.
