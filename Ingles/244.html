Magna and Coahuila explore possibilities for the creation of a research center

Don Walker, Magna's international general manager, told the business delegation headed by Governor Miguel Angel Riquelme Solis that the company's experience in Coahuila is a benchmark for its corporate operations and investments at the global level. Therefore, the proposal to build a technological research center, which would be carried out by the government of Coahuila, is of great interest, Walker said. Riquelme Solis also announced that, in the near future, the state authorities, the education, business and labor sectors will sign a strategic agreement that will keep the performance of Coahuila's workforce at its highest level.
Isuzu Mexico opens a dealership in Oaxaca

With the aim of providing a better service to all customers in the city of Oaxaca, Isuzu Motors Mexico inaugurated a new dealership. In this dealership, located in the junction of the main access roads to the state capital, in addition to selling Japanese vehicles, better after-sales service will be offered to all Isuzu customers based in Oaxaca. Prior to the ribbon cutting, several personalities who attended the inauguration gave a message to the people that attended the event. The first to speak was the president of Isuzu Motors of Mexico, Koichi Yoda, who took the stand to thank the state of Oaxaca for opening its doors to Isuzu.
Metal Technologies Components invests US$67 million in SLP

During the inauguration of the US$67 million Metal Technologies Components plant, the governor of San Luis Potosi, Juan Manuel Carreras Lopez, indicated that this is an example of strong bilateral relations. In addition, he thanked the executives of this company for demonstrating their interest and trust in Mexico, especially in the entity. This plant, which already employs 280 people, will produce nodular and gray iron for the automotive, truck and agricultural sectors and is part of a network of US companies with operations in Indiana, Michigan, Minnesota, and Tennessee.
Nissan shows how electric vehicles contribute to noise pollution reduction

Considering that traffic noise is second among the environmental threats with the greatest impact on public health, Nissan conducted a sound measurement experiment to raise awareness on how electric vehicles can help reduce said risk from growing. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), prolonged exposure to high levels of environmental noise, such as traffic noise, can cause adverse health effects, such as high blood pressure, coronary artery disease, hearing loss, and heart attacks.
Hyundai Accent leads sales within the brand

Hyundai Motor of Mexico closed May off with a total of 3,644 sold vehicles; thus, increasing the brand's participation within the Mexican automotive market in 2019. The Accent model, in its sedan and hatchback version, is the company's best-selling vehicle, with a total of 848 sold vehicles in May. The Accent's sales represent 24.8% of Hyundai Motor Mexico's total sales so far into 2019. In that sense, Hyundai Motors Mexico sold 4,573 Accent vehicles from January to May 2019.
General Motors' San Luis Potosi plant commemorates World Environment Day

General Motors' San Luis Potosi Complex has stood out since the beginning of its operations, in 2008, when it comes down to taking care of the environment and its natural resources. For this reason, the automotive brand carried out activities during June so as to commemorate the World Environment Day, and it did so by planting 500 maguey trees at the Gogorron National Park and by collecting waste for its correct disposal alongside residents of the Carranco, Bledos, San Vicente Lagoon, Rosario, and Socavon communities.
Sinaloa adds four Mercedes-Benz buses to its tourist transportation repertoire

Mercedes-Benz Buses and its distributor Cytson, alongside AllAbordo, added four new buses to the tourist transport repertoire in the state of Sinaloa. Said buses will travel on Los Mochis, Guasave, Culiacan, and Mazatlan routes. The Merdedes-Benz vehicles operate with an OC 500 RF 1943 chassis, a Marcopolo Paradiso 1350 bodywork with a capacity for 42 people, GPS and air conditioning. All of them have a state-of-the-art BlueTec 5 low emission engines and a Safety Bus safety system.
