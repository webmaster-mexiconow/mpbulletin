BMW San Luis Potosi plant has built pre-production models

After an image circulated around different San Luis Potosi media outlets in recent days, of what was allegedly the first BMW 3 Series sedan manufactured in the Mexican plant, the communication area of the company confirmed to local media that it was in fact a pre-series or pre-production model. According to officials, they are currently testing both production processes and the result of these, which is a small batch of vehicles that are also tested to detect possible glitches before the final product reaches the market. More details here: bit.ly/2QSPLwu
Volkswagen halts Jetta production in Puebla for two weeks

From October 8 until October 21, the all-new Jetta's production line will idle at the Volkswagen plant in Puebla, union leaders revealed to local media. The halting has two purposes, they said: to adjust vehicle inventories in the main countries in which it is marketed, but also to carry out a tooling process so that, starting January, the Tiguan SUV will also be produced in the same line. Such changes will be necessary to maintain a constant production avoiding the need to cut off staff at the plant once the production of Golf and Beetle comes to an end next year, union representatives said. More details here: bit.ly/2A9ZhWr
Audi plant in Puebla ends two-week stoppage

As the shutdown of operations began at the Volkswagen plant, another concluded at the Audi facility. Union leaders said that the halting, from September 24 to October 8, was meant to retool the assembly line in order to build the upcoming 2019 models of the Q5 SUV. It is worth noting that Audi recently announced that the Q5 would be the first vehicle in the catalog to debut its new hybrid powertrain, which will be available for the 2019 model year. However, union representatives declined to comment on whether the changes had anything to do with the introduction of this new variant. More details here: bit.ly/2A9ZhWr
Less than six months after opening, Midori expands its new plant in San Luis Potosi

Japanese manufacturer Midori Auto Leather revealed it's already expanding its second plant in San Luis Potosi, which was inaugurated at the beginning of May. Company officials said to local media that the new expansion will house a production line of leather in order to serve a new General Motors program. According to the administrative manager, Armando Humara, since its opening, the second plant has only made cutting work with the leather manufactured at the other facility. However, with the start of a new project for GM they saw the need to produce their own raw material. More details here: bit.ly/2yhOUP1
Nissan adds a second Mexico-made model to the Midnight Edition lineup

Nissan Motor Company announced that it chose the NP300 Frontier pickup manufactured in Mexico to expand its Midnight Edition offer of vehicles. It is the second Mexico-made model that joins this range after the Nissan Kicks crossover. The Midnight Edition lineup is complemented by the Murano and Pathfinder SUVs, as well as the Maxima and Altima sedans. The NP300 Frontier LE Midnight Edition is built at the Nissan CIVAC plant located in Cuernavaca, Morelos, where it has been manufactured for more than three decades, while the Nissan Kicks rolls out of the A1 plant, one of the two assembly facilities located in Aguascalientes, Central Mexico. More details here: bit.ly/2NBgsnj
HELLA partners with Faurecia to develop tech for tomorrow's vehicle interiors

The Germany-based lighting and electronic solutions manufacturer HELLA and the French technology automotive supplier Faurecia announced a partnership aimed to develop technologies for tomorrow's vehicle interiors. For this purpose, HELLA will supply innovative products in the field of interior lighting to match Faurecia's expertise as a complete system integrator for vehicle interiors. It's not a crazy idea to say that some of the final products that might result from this joint work could be manufactured in Mexico in the near future, if not that some of the development could also take place in the country, since both companies operate production plants and technical centers here. More details here: bit.ly/2PwdtxY
Ford readies layoffs amid global restructuring plan

A few weeks after Ford Motor Company announced that steel and aluminum tariffs will cost them US$1 billion in profit, the Dearborn, Michigan-based automaker revealed that a global restructuring plan is in the works to revamp its managing operations. However, the company made clear that the measure does not seek to mitigate losses due to tariffs, but is part of a previously planned restructuring effort to make the company's operations more dynamic worldwide. The plan would cost around US$25.5 billion and would affect only salaried employees, while those jobs directly related to manufacturing will remain intact. More details here: bit.ly/2yBgV3i
Apodaca-based Metalsa expands operations in Kentucky

Apodaca-based structural components supplier Metalsa announced that, due to an expansion at one of its plants in Elizabethtown, Kentucky, it will approximately recruit an additional 250 workers to the nearly 2,000 it already employs in the region. The Elizabethtown complex is one of Metalsa's largest operations worldwide. It supplies frames and other structural components for the iconic Ford pickup trucks which are built at the assembly plant nearby in Louisville, Kentucky. In fact, earlier this year Ford recognized Metalsa as a top-performing global supplier because of its support to this program. More details here: bit.ly/2A78JK8
Santa Catarina-based Katcon Global to open its fifth manufacturing plant in China

Santa Catarina-based manufacturer of exhaust systems Katcon Global announced it will open its fifth manufacturing plant in China at the beginning of 2019, Nuevo Leon media reported. The facility, located in the city of Xiangtan, will open 150 job positions in order to manufacture 450,000 exhaust systems per year, increasing the company's global output capacity by 11%. Katcon CEO Carlos Turner highlighted in a press conference the potential of the Chinese automotive market, referring that the growth rates of vehicle production are greater than those of Europe and North America, places where the company also operates plants. More details here: bit.ly/2A78JK8
Grupo Surman unveils plans to build Bajaj motorcycles at two facilities in Mexico

Grupo Surman, one of the largest automotive dealers in the country, announced that it will install two motorcycle assembly plants of the Indian brand Bajaj through its Motodrive subsidiary to supply the domestic market. In a conference, Olaf Sarabia, CEO of the subsidiary, said the first plant will have a capacity of 50,000 units per year. "This plant will be located in an industrial park in Toluca (State of Mexico). We just finished the construction work; the tooling process will start in the coming days," he explained. The second factory will be in the north of the country, but the precise place is yet to be defined. San Luis Potosi and Durango are the states in mind. It will have a similar or greater capacity than that of Toluca. More details here: bit.ly/2QMVJyB
