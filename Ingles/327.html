Heavy vehicles sales drop 76.9% in April

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), informed that, during the month April, the wholesale sale of heavy vehicles reached a total of 893 units, number which compared to the 3,865 units registered in April of 2019, represents a fall of 76.9%. At the same time, during April, the retail sales of heavy vehicles reached 1,072 units which, compared to the 3,840 commercialized during the same month of 2019, meant a decrease of 72.1%.

The SEAT Ateca changes to Triptonic transmission in our country

The 2020 line of the SEAT Ateca in Mexico premiered integral 4DRIVE traction and six speed DSG transmission in September of last year, but the Volkswagen Group plant in the Czech Republic, where the Ateca is produced, stopped making said combination recently. Because of this, the Spanish SUV is modifying its specifications for our country, without changing its year-model. The 2020 Ateca keeps its turbo 1.4 liter engine of 150 hp and 184 lb-ft, but it's now using a Triptonic eight speed transmission and frontal traction.

Kia keeps fifth place in the national market during April

Due to recent events and with a market fall of 64.5% in comparison to the same month of last year, Kia Motors Mexico closed April with 2,103 sold units, result with which it keeps its fifth place in the national market. At the close of the month, the most sold model is the Kia Rio, model which is proudly Mexican since it's produced in the brand's Pesqueria plant in Nuevo Leon, selling 1,011 units in its sedan and hatchback versions. The Kia Seltos placed second, only 4 months after its release in the Mexican market, by selling 335 units.

Automotive production and exports fall in April

During April vehicle production in Mexico fell to minimal levels. Due to the decree made by health authorities, non-essential activities, like vehicle assembly, have been suspended since March 31 and will be until May 30. Which is why, vehicle assembly reached 3,722 units, while in the same month of 2019 a total of 300,106 units were assembled. This meant a decrease of 98.8% in total production. AMIA reported that a total of 27,889 units were sent abroad during this month; in April of last year exports reached 284,431 units. The fall in vehicle exports was of 90.2%.

Return of the automotive industry expected for May 17: Concamin

The automotive industry will be one of the first to open in the country's restart of economic activities along with a lot of branches of its supply chain, said Francisco Cervantes, president of the Confederation of Economic Chambers of Mexico (Concamin). "On May 17 they will begin opening, as long as the health curve won't be affected, but one of the segments and seeing the regional activity that we have with the United States and Canada is the automotive and derived from this industry there's a lot of industries that are included like the textile, leather coverings, tires, auto parts, etc.," he said.

It's expected for Aguascalientes automotive plants to restart operations on May 18

Several automotive plants in Mexico will reopen between mid-May and June 1st once the contingency caused by the pandemic is over, as announced by representatives of the automotive sector. In declarations to Radio Formula, the president of the Automotive Cluster of Aguascalientes, Cuitláhuac Pérez, informed that "unofficially" they expect to reactivate the region's automotive plants on Monday May 18. Perez explained that the two main companies settled in Aguascalientes, which are Nissan and Mercedes-Benz, will not release their intent to restart operations until this week.
