Toyota recalls 2020 Hiace Diesel vehicles

The Federal Consumer Protection Office (Profeco) informed of the recall effectuated by Toyota of Mexico to its 2020 Hiace Diesel vehicles. The recall is due the fact that there's the remote possibility that the clap that connects the combustible pump with the rail injectors could not be tightened according to specifications. With the passing of time, a combustible rail with a tightening outside of specification could loosen due to the vehicle's vibration. If this were to happen, a combustible leak could happen, which could stop the engine.

Authorized distributors must increase client satisfaction: J.D. Power

Jair Montes de Oca, account manager at J.D. Power of Mexico, commented that one of the main findings of the CSI-LT 2020 study was that, from the 2,854 people polled, most think that authorized distributors offer a more expensive service, when compared to independent shops. This result showed that the only annual difference in the service of both is of MXN$351. Authorized distributors need to learn how to capture that market and that will be achieved through greater client satisfaction. Since, for example, quality of service is what is most evaluated by the Mexican client. It's followed by customer service and then installations.

Toyota will continue boosting distance sales

Toyota Mexico has boosted its online sales platform during the last few months of this year marked by the health crisis, to protect the health of all those people who sought to acquire a new vehicle. The automotive company said that of the 43,549 sold vehicles during the year's first seven months, 50% were sold distantly. The microsite will change its name to Impulso Toyota with the objective of supporting those possible car buyers to acquire a unit, and they will be accompanied through digital channels during the entire purchasing process.

Mazda launches Mazda Financial Services and aims for 6% in market participation

Mazda officially launched Mazda Financial Services, in alliance with Santander Bank Mexico, with which the Japanese automaker aims to reach 6% in market participation in the commercialization of light vehicles in the medium term. Miguel Barbeyto, president of Mazda Motor Mexico, said that with the financial arm they pretend to go from 4.6% the mark possesses in the market in 2020, to 6% where 50% of its sold units would be sold through this mechanism, under an advanced digital platform with credits to a fixed rate of 6.75%.

JAC takes over the fifth part of the electric vehicle market in the country

In less than six months, Chinese brand JAC became the brand with the widest range of electric vehicles in Mexico and the second one with the biggest market participation. Between January and June, there's been 195 sold electric vehicles from six different brands. In this period JAC, commercialized in Mexico by Giant Motors Latin America, almost obtained a fifth part of the market, according to INEGI. JAC's strategy was a change in discourse. Instead of basing its financial offer on the sales price, the client is told about a total cost of ownership.

Jose Zozaya is named president of AMIA

Jose Zozaya, former general director of Kansas City Southern of Mexico (KCSM) was named as the new executive president of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA). With this naming he is replacing Eduardo Solis, who left the position at the beginning of this year. In a press release, AMIA's board of directors said that with his naming the sector is reaffirming its commitment to Mexico's growth, with the automotive sector and with the objective of boosting growth and the welfare of Mexican families.
