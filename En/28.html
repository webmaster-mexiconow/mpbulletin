Ford CEO: Two new Mexican facilities will start operations this year

Ford confirmed it has invested US$ 2.5 billion in two production sites scheduled to start operations by the end of year. The company spent US$ 1.1 billion to build a facility within its Chihuahua complex to produce a new gasoline-powered engine, and US$ 200 million more to expand its existing I-4 and diesel engines production, creating 1,800 jobs total. In addition, Ford spent US$ 1.2 billion to build a transmission plant within the premises of a facility in Irapuato used by transmission supplier and partner Getrag. The project will create 2,000 jobs.
Michigan officials, executives on a three-day trade mission to Queretaro

Michigan Lt. Gov. Brian Calley in leading a three-day trade mission to Mexico focusing on opportunities for 21 Michigan companies. The mission arrived on Sunday, February 19 and will return on Wednesday, February 22. During the trip, Calley will meet with automotive and aerospace companies during a supply chain-oriented business forum in Queretaro. He also will meet with executives from TREMEC Corporation and Rassini Brakes, two Mexican companies with operations in Michigan.
GM to idle Lordstown, Ohio, plant for three weeks in March

The General Motors Assembly Complex in Lordstown, Ohio, will be idled for three weeks next month as part of the company's effort to align production of the Chevrolet Cruze with consumer demands. Production at the facility, where the Cruze sedan is made, is scheduled to stop the weeks of March 13, 20 and 27. Those dates are subject to change, however, and it remained unclear whether additional time would be added to the schedule.
Standard Motor Products shifts operations from Nogales to Reynosa

Standard Motor Products announced it has begun the task of relocating the General Cable wire assembly operation from Nogales, Mexico to an existing assembly facility in Reynosa, Mexico. The company plans to complete this move by the end of first quarter 2018, and expect substantial synergies when the integration is complete.
SEAT Mexican sales increased 16% in January; delivered 2,700 cars

SEAT's worldwide sales achieved the best January results since 2003. The brand delivered 32,300 cars in the first month of 2017, which represents a 16.5% increase over the same period last year. In Mexico, the Spain-based automaker saw a 16.3% increase and total sales of 2,700 cars.
Ford to invest US$ 200 million in new wind tunnel complex

Ford will invest US$ 200 million in a new aerodynamic testing complex that will house a next-generation rolling road wind tunnel and state-of-the-art climatic chamber to advance improvements in fuel economy. The complex will sit on 13 acres next to Ford's current Driveability Test Facility in Allen Park, Michigan. Construction starts this year.
Cooper-Standard reports record sales in fourth-quarter 2016

Michigan-based automotive supplier Cooper-Standard Holdings Inc. reported a fourth-quarter net income of US$ 31.1 million on record sales of US$ 875.4 million in 2016, compared with US$ 21.7 million on sales of US$ 854.4 million in the fourth quarter of 2015, a year-over-year net income jump of 43%. The company reported a full-year 2016 net income of US$ 139 million, representing a 24% increase from 2015's net income of US$ 111.9 million. Cooper-Standard operates eight plants and a distribution center in Mexico.
New GM contract in Canada, conditioned to restore hundreds of jobs

General Motors will have to restore hundreds of jobs being cut at a southern Ontario assembly plant to reach a new contract with the factory's workers this fall, the president of Canada's largest autoworkers union said. The automaker is cutting 625 jobs at its plant by the end of July as it phases out production of two current generation vehicles and moves some work to Mexico. Securing jobs will be key to a collective agreement to replace the one expiring in September, said Unifor president Jerry Dias.
Auto suppliers seek opportunity as industry shifts to mobility

Auto suppliers seek opportunity as industry shifts to mobility Three Michigan's top auto supplier executives spoke to the Detroit Economic Club in a meeting about mobility and their place in it. David Dauch, chairman and CEO of American Axle & Manufacturing, said the company is diversifying its products for use in electric vehicles and other alternative systems; Matt Simoncini, president and CEO of Lear Corp., is investing in startups that focus on software it can integrate into seats; and James Verrier, president and CEO of BorgWarner Inc., is doubling down on propulsion technologies for use in autonomous and connected cars.
GM plans to build, test thousands of self-driving Bolts in 2018

General Motors plans to deploy thousands of Chevy Bolt self-driving electric cars in test fleets in partnership with ride-sharing affiliate Lyft Inc, beginning in 2018, sources familiar with the automaker's plans told Reuters news agency. It is expected to be the largest such test of fully autonomous vehicles by any major automaker before 2020. The vehicles will be tested as a ride-sharing fleet in several states, one of the sources said.
