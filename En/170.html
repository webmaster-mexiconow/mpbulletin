US auto sales down 3.7% in July due to lower discounts

Most major automakers reported lower sales in the U.S. market for July after dealerships restrained incentives for the first time in 55 months. Average incentives per vehicle dropped US$230 compared with a year ago, according to Ford. AutoNews estimates U.S. auto dealers sold 1.36 million new vehicles last month, a 3.7% decline from July 2017. From January through July auto sales totaled 9.98 million cars and light trucks, which is a figure 1.1% higher than those recorded during the same seven-month period of 2017. The seasonally adjusted, annualized rate (SAAR) of sales for July fell to 16.73 million, the lowest result since the 16.58-million rate in August 2017, when Hurricane Harvey disrupted sales. Auto sales in Mexico drop 6.4% in July

Auto dealers sold 114,312 new vehicles in Mexico last month, 6.4% less cars than those sold in July of 2017. This is the fourteenth month in a row with decline in sales. Sales from January through July totaled 795,011 units, which represents an 8.1% drop compared to the same seven-month period of 2017, according to figures from the Mexican Association of Auto Dealers (AMDA). "Auto dealers keep facing adverse conditions due to the loss of purchasing power by consumers of subcompact cars and rising interest rates," said Guillermo Rosales, head of AMDA. The association estimated its members would market 1.45 million units in 2018. More details here: bit.ly/2vgtccL
Auto sales in Canada fall for the fifth straight month

In Canada, auto sales fell for the fifth straight month in July. With total sales for the month at 175,317 light vehicles and a 3.6% decline, it was the largest percentage slide so far this year. Light truck sales declined 1.1% and passenger cars dropped 9.0%. Year-to-date sales are now down 0.7% to 1,211,994 units with light truck sales increasing 3.3% against a 9% decrease in passenger car sales, according to DesRosiers Automotive Consultants. Ford Motor Company saw its total vehicle sales in Canada rose 0.9% in July and remained the top seller with 27,918 vehicles sold. Meanwhile, General Motors sales fell 2.4% to 25,226 vehicles. The largest sales decrease for the month was posted by FCA, down 33.3%. More details here: bit.ly/2Mb0y2U
Mexico's auto production will stagnate by 2020, IHS Markit predicts

Mexico's manufacturing industry will reach its peak production by 2019, with 4.2 million units, and will stagnate to around 4 million by 2020. This will put the country in harmony with the rest of North America–one of the few regions where the industry automotive is contracting. This is the forecast that the senior manager of Sales of Light Vehicles in America from the consultancy firm IHS Markit, Guido Vildozo, made during the Mexico Outlook 2018 seminar, hosted by the Anahuac University, as reported by Mexican media. The U.S. market will be impacted by the imposition of tariffs on steel and aluminum, which will increase the price of vehicles and, consequently, sales will be affected, according to Vildozo. Honda plant in Celaya to resume operations until mid-November

Honda will resume assembly operations at its plant in Celaya, Guanajuato, until mid-November, due to the damages caused by the June 28 flood, said the Japanese automaker in a statement. The company explained that it has not dismissed any of its workers and does not contemplate making any layoffs. "Our expert teams have completed an investigation of the damage to the tooling and equipment at the auto plant, which produces the Honda Fit and HR-V, and we now expect to resume auto and engine production in Celaya in mid-November," said the company. Production of the refreshed 2019 Honda HR-V is already underway at another Honda plant in El Salto, Jalisco, about 160 miles west of Celaya. More details here: bit.ly/2OyfPwq
Chinese suspension manufacturer BWI unveils US$30 million expansion in Chihuahua plant

BeijingWest Industries (BWI), a Chinese manufacturer of brake and suspension systems, currently works on a US$30 million expansion at its manufacturing facility in the city of Chihuahua, state capital, local authorities revealed in a press conference attended by Craig Diem, the company's Global Director of Manufacturing Operations. Along with the expansion, 100 job positions will be opened by the end of the year, increasing the plant's total workforce to 700 employees. The company manufactures suspension systems, including passive shock absorbers and air suspension products. Its customer portfolio includes GM, Ford, Land Rover and BMW. KIA officially introduces the all-new 2019 Forte sedan

The Korean automaker KIA presented its new Forte 2019 sedan, which is manufactured in the plant located in the municipality of Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon, and will be available at Mexican auto dealerships starting August 9. The Forte model is the third best seller under the KIA brand and the first model to be produced at the company's Mexican facility, said Horacio Chávez, general director of Kia Mexico, during the presentation of the unit in Cancun. By the end of 2018, the total production at the Nuevo Leon plant will be 314,000 units: 153,000 Forte cars (83,300 units from the next-gen version and 69,700 vehicles of the outgoing variant); 96,000 KIA Rio and 65,000 Hyundai Accent. J.D. Power ranks three Mexico-made VW vehicles among the most appealing cars

Volkswagen announced that the outgoing version of the Jetta has been awarded the most appealing Compact Car in the J.D. Power 2018 Automotive Performance, Execution and Layout (APEAL) Study. This is the fifth time that the sedan built in Puebla, Mexico, has ranked highest in this hotly contested category, which is comprised of 18 vehicles for 2018. Both the Golf and Tiguan, also built at the Puebla assembly plant, ranked second in their respective categories, and the Atlas was ranked fourth in the Midsize SUV segment. Overall, Volkswagen improved three rank positions to 18th overall in the Study, both above the industry average and outpacing the industry year-over-year improvement average. More details here: bit.ly/2O1H55o
NAFTA: Deal on rules of origin 'getting close'

The United States and Mexico are getting close to a deal on the key issue of autos content rules at talks to renew the NAFTA trade pact, Mexican and Canadian officials said on Wednesday. Guillermo Malpica, head of the trade and NAFTA office for the Mexican government, also said the United States had "started showing more flexibility last week" on autos content and other topics at the negotiations, which have dragged on for almost a year. A Canadian official added that negotiators are making progress on auto content rules, and endorsed the concept of linking those rules to improving workers' wages. Malpica also said that negotiators "are getting close" to an agreement on the so-called autos rules of origin.
Tesla Model 3 outsells small and mid-size luxury cars, says report

The ramp up in Model 3 production is enabling Tesla to outsell small and midsize luxury car sales in the U.S., according to data by CleanTechnica. In the second quarter of 2018, Tesla produced just over 53,000 vehicles, doubling its output compared to the same quarter last year. For the first time, Model 3 production, of 28,578 units, exceeded combined Model S and X production which totaled 24,761 vehicles with deliveries to customers reaching 40,740 for the quarter. With total estimated sales for July amounting to 16,000 vehicles, the closest individual model to Tesla's mass-market endeavor is the Mercedes C-Class, with July sales estimated at just 6,029 units.
