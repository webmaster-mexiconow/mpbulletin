The automotive sector will close 2019 with record surplus

Despite the fact that activity for Mexico’s automotive industry slowed down in the last few months of the year, the sector’s attraction of currency will result in a surplus up to 7% higher in 2019 with respect to the previous year, because Mexico sells vehicles with a higher added value, assured Eduardo Solis, president of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA). This means that the trade balance could close in between US$85 billion and US$89 billion at the close of 2019, mentioned the representative.

Queretaro’s automotive industry is worried because of the steel content

Before the last agreements made on the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA), the local automotive industry is vigilant of the implications that the new origin rule to incorporate steel and aluminum products will have, as well as the implementation of controversy solution panels. The director of the Automotive Cluster of Queretaro, Daniel Hernandez Camacho, explained that there’s a concern over the process that would imply meeting the percentage of North American regional content of steel and aluminum, which is why there’s still the need to precisely know how it’ll be implemented.

PACCAR announced new plant director and deputy director general

Renato Villalpando, general director of PACCAR Mexico, announced the following organizational changes: Carlos Ayala will take on the position of commercial deputy director general in PACCAR México. Carlos received a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from CETYS University, and began working at PACCAR in 1985. Alejandro Novoa will assume the position of plant director in PACCAR Mexico. Alejandro began working in PACCAR in 2003 and his most recent position was of commercial deputy director in PACCAR Mexico.

Hyundai Elantra’s update reaches Mexico

The Hyundai Elantra’s update has been among us for only a year, but the South Korean brand keeps perfecting its offer, this time with novelties on a mechanical level. The 2020 model, which already has a price in Mexico, is replacing the six speed automatic transmission for a new continuously variable speed, called IVT. The 2020 Hyundai Elantra will be available in Mexico with prices ranging from MXN$297,000 to MXN$395,700.

FCA, Toyota and Volkswagen were the three brands that recalled their products the most in 2019

During 2019 there was an average of a thousand daily recall calls for vehicles in Mexico, which meant that 371,846 vehicles were considered in 72 fast automotive alerts emitted by the Federal Consumer Protection Office (Profeco), of which every 9 out of 10 belong to units from FCA, Toyota and the Volkswagen Group. Between the three firms there’s a participation of 93% total of recalled units.

Simulator to train trucking conductors is inaugurated

The School of Arts and Crafts of Cuautitlan Izcalli became the headquarters of the first Training Center for Federal Trucking, from the Institute of Training and Coaching for Industrial Work, by having a classroom equipped with a simulator that will allow for the promotion of accident prevention culture and social responsibility. Jaime Rebollo Hernandez, general director of the organism highlighted that this type of training will favorably impact the region’s economic activity, by improving the performance of trucking companies.

Kia Motors Mexico achieves sales growth in 2019

Kia Motors, the South Korean brand, found the formula to get through a complex year in vehicle sales, despite the contraction of the new vehicle internal market. This year represented the second consecutive year with falls for the automotive industry, whose sales in the internal market contracted 8%, where brands like Nissan, General Motors and Volkswagen lost market share. Kia was one of the seven car selling brands in the medium sector that achieved growth in the industry during this year.
