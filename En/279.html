November 8th, 2019
Chinese electric bus company will invest US$150 million in Durango

Ramon Davila Flores, minister of Economic Development of Durango, said that a Chinese company will invest US$150 million to install an electric bus assembly plant in the state. He detailed after a reunion with the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) that the investment agreement would be consolidated in 15 days with the company, whose name he wasn't able to reveal due to an agreement, this investment is expected to create 500 jobs in its first phase.
Car sales fall 8.92% in October

October of this year in Mexico saw the sales of 107,094 new light vehicles, which translates to a fall of 8.92% in respect with the same month of last year, informed the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). By releasing the progress of the internal market sales according to administrative registers of the light vehicle automotive industry, INEGI detailed that, from January to October of this year a total of 1,062,487 units have been sold, meaning 7.65% less than in the same period of last year.
The internal tractor, heavy and special vehicle internal market should be incentivized

Deputy Jose Salvador Rosas Quintanilla (PAN) urged the ministries of Economy and Finance and Public Credit to incentivize the internal market of tractors, heavy and special new vehicles, with the objective of renewing fleets and meeting quality standards, attached to commercial treaties like the USMCA. Additionally, he suggested the strengthening of quality controls for imports of these type of vehicles coming in from the United States.
FCA Mexico sold 5,126 units in October

FCA Mexico reported the sales of 5,126 units in October, 165 more than the 4,961 registered last month and 20 more than those registered in September. RAM sold 2,053 vehicles in October, an increase of 9% over last year. Dodge placed 1,410 vehicles; Attitude was the brand's most sold vehicle with 835 units. On the other hand, Jeep sold 1,131 units, 4% more when compared with October of last year; FIAT sold 477 units. Lastly, Chrysler placed 44 vehicles and Alfa Romeo sold 11 units.
Toyota looks to Corregidora in search of human capital and suppliers

Before the upcoming opening of the Toyota plant (planned for the end of this year in Apaseo el Grande, Guanajuato) the company is seeking human capital and suppliers in the municipality of Corregidora, Queretaro-which neighbors the new plant. The minister of Sustainable Development of Corregidora, Ricardo Alegre Bojórquez, said that the automotive company has asked for information on both companies that would be added to their supply chain, as well as specialized profiles.
Anuncia BMW Group cambios en su equipo directivo en México

BMW Group San Luis Potosi Plant announced a change in its Board: Hermann Bohrer, current president and CEO of BMW Group San Luis Potosi plant is retiring, leaving Jörg Willimayer in his place. During his stay in San Luis Potosi, Bohrer managed the placement of the first stone followed by the construction and installation of the plant, finishing with the start of production of the BMW 3 Series. Starting January 1st, 2020, Jörg Willimayer, current senior vice president of Purchasing and Supplier Network of BMW AG, will start as president and CEO of the BMW Group plant in San Luis Potosi.
Senate rejects the regularization of chocolate cars

Senators continued to say no the transitory article that the Chamber of Deputies added to the 2020 Federal Revenue Law, with which they sought to regulate chocolate cars currently in the country. During Tuesday evening, the upper house voted with three votes in favor, 108 against and zero abstentions for the fifteenth transitory article, with which it is definitely ruled out.
AMIA proposes the establishment of kiosk operated by SAT to regularize chocolate cars

The Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) reaffirmed their willingness to have a conversation with deputies and senators to propose solutions that will resolve the issue of vehicles imported illegally, also called "chocolate." The proposal that AMIA would seek to present is that through offices or kiosks operated by SAT, the illegally imported vehicles would be able to pay taxes for their internment and thus they would regularize their situation; by doing this they would also be able to start the process of getting plates in Mexico.
Automotive production and exports decrease in October

The Mexican automotive industry reported the biggest reduction in production for an October since the existence of the register of the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI) that has data starting 1988, while it had the biggest decrease in exports for a similar month since 1992. In October 311,150 vehicles were assembled in Mexico, 16.3% less than the number registered in the same period of 2018, informed INEGI. Regarding exports to other countries, during the tenth month of the year a total of 252,292 units were exported, 19.5% less than that registered for the same month of last year.
With the approval of the USMCA Mexico could attract up to 20% of auto part investments

With the approval of the United States–Mexico–Canada Agreement (USMCA), Mexico could attract at least 20% of the new investments that Japan, Germany, South Korea and China will route to North America, to substitute imports to the region, said Oscar Albin, president of the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA). These countries are the main auto parts exporters, after Mexico, to the North American region, for a quantity of US$85 billon. However, some parts of the product that is now sent from outside the region will have to be produce within it.
