Employment in Mexico's automotive industry reached record high in 2017

In 2017, the Mexican automotive industry employed 839,571 workers both for the assembly of vehicles and for the production of auto parts, which represented an increase of 6.7% compared to a year before, according to the most recent data from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (Inegi). This jobs figure is the highest in the history of the sector and represents more than double the 408,100 positions that the industry had in 2009, when it reached its lowest during the last decade due to the international financial crisis. Mexico's first Specialized Car Terminal formally begins operations

Seaports operator SSA Mexico announced that its new Specialized Car Terminal (TEA), located in the Port of Lazaro Cardenas, Michoacan, formally initiated operations with the arrival of the first two car carriers, contributing for the first time through this infrastructure to solve the needs of the automotive industry in the Mexican Pacific. Last week, two vessels of the shipping company K Line, Helios Highway and Hyperion Highway, arrived at the port with 2,420 and 2,767 units on board, respectively. The ships transported cars of the Toyota, General Motors, Suzuki, Daimler, Honda, Hino, Isuzu and Nissan brands, as well as heavy machinery. More details here: bit.ly/2G9oDUJ
Grupo Industrial Saltillo to invest US$ 116 million this year, 60% budgeted for Mexico

Grupo Industrial Saltillo S.A.B. de C.V (GIS), a Mexican industrial conglomerate serving automotive, construction materials and houseware segments, announced it plans to invest during 2018 up to US$ 116.6 million, of which 60% -about US$ 69.6 million- will be allocated towards its Mexican operations. Such resources will be mostly focused on increasing productivity and profitability, said the company in a filing to stock market regulators. The automotive division will count on US$ 69.4 million of which US$ 30.5 million will be budgeted for Mexican operations, while the remaining will be allocated towards the company's facilities in Europe and Asia. More details: bit.ly/2Glo1Pt
Ontario-based manufacturer Linamar to invest US$ 50 million in Durango facility

Ontario-based manufacturer Linamar plans to invest US$ 50 million to revamp and expand its manufacturing site located in Gomez Palacio, Durango, announced the governor, Jose Aispuro Torres, who met with directors of the company. According to the official, a large part of the investment will go towards introducing new machinery with state-of-the-art technology, while another smaller amount will be used to expand the facility. Linamar has five plants in Mexico, located in the municipalities of Nuevo Laredo, Tamaulipas; Gomez Palacio, Durango; Torreon and Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila. In this last location the company operates two manufacturing complexes. More details here: bit.ly/2pBUgPY
India-based Sakthi Automotive Group builds US$ 40 million plant in Durango

India-based Sakthi Automotive Group recently broke ground in Durango to build its first plant in Mexico. An investment of US$ 40 million and the opening of 600 job positions is expected, according to a statement from the state Government. The company will set up operations in the Durango Logistic and Industrial Center, located in the state capital, to manufacture aluminum components for braking and suspension system. Sakthi Automotive Group specializes in aluminum casting and machined parts for brakes and chassis and has presence in 14 countries. Some of its global customers are General Motors, Ford, BMW and Volkswagen. More details: bit.ly/2DQjNtC
Audi scores a resounding success with armored SUV

The launch of the armored version of the Audi Q5 in Mexico has been a resounding success. Of 250 units sold that it had set as goal for this year, the company already has 155 orders, the German automaker revealed to Expansion. "It's a project that was developed in Germany especially for the Mexican market and other countries in Latin America," said Jorge Ochoa, manager of Armored Vehicles at Audi Mexico. For US$ 96,600, customers will get an armored vehicle with thermoformed ballistic steel, which withstands impacts of up to a Magnum 357. "It's a standard armor level for situations like an assault in Mexico City," Ochoa said. More details: bit.ly/2DS8UHT
Mexico's heavy vehicle production increases 4.6% in January; exports hike 15.5%

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT for its acronym in Spanish), announced that the production of heavy vehicles last January was 9,838 units, which compared to 9,407 vehicles in January 2017, represented an increase of 4.6%. Of this production, 410 units correspond to passage, which means a fall of 36.7% in that category. On the other hand, during the month of January, the export of heavy vehicles reached a total of 8,179 units, which compared to 7,083 vehicles in the same month of 2017, represented a growth of 15.5%. Nissan slows factory output as US demand drops

Nissan is slowing production at several assembly plants, including those in Mexico, to keep the supply of vehicles on U.S. dealers' lots in check after being caught off guard by weaker consumer demand. Japan's second-largest automaker is aiming to reach a 50-60 day supply of cars and trucks in August, when dealers start transitioning to sell 2019 model-year vehicles, Dan Mohnke, U.S sales chief for the Nissan brand, said to Bloomberg during the National Automobile Dealers Association's annual convention in Las Vegas. Nissan reached 86 days of inventory as of Sept. 1 last year, and the company increased incentive spending and shipped more cars to rental companies and other fleet customers to help bring that number down.
Volkswagent to unveil a midsize pickup truck concept this week in New York

According to AutoNews, Volkswagen is set to unveil a midsize pickup truck concept this week at the New York Auto Show. The German automaker will jump into the midsize pickup segment with a U.S.-made truck that could be built in Tennessee, since it would share platform with the Atlas SUV. The model could enter the production phase "relatively soon" if the feedback at the show will be positive, AutoNews said. Earlier this year VW trademarked the Amarok name in the United States, which is same name that it uses for a pickup that is sold in other markets around the world, but AutoNews sources said it will be marketed under a different nameplate.
Twin Cities Auto Show in Minneapolis names the Infiniti QX50 Car of the Show

The Twin Cities Auto Show announced the all-new, Mexico-made 2019 Infiniti QX50 as this year's Car of the Show. The luxury mid-size crossover with world-first technologies, convinced the judges with its standout design and enhanced interior space, on an entirely new platform. The Twin Cities Auto Show is a production of the Greater Metropolitan Automobile Dealers Association (GMADA), an organization consisting of more than 130 new car and truck franchised dealers in the Greater Minneapolis and St. Paul area. This year the show displayed more than 600 vehicles from 36 foreign and domestic brands. The Infiniti QX50 will arrive to U.S. dealerships over the next weeks.
