GM weighs on bringing back the Chevrolet Blazer; could be built in Mexico

Automotive specialized site WardsAuto revealed General Motors is expected to bring an all-new midsize CUV to market in the U.S. under the Chevrolet brand next year tentatively called the Blazer. The five-passenger unit would use the same platform as the 3-row Traverse, but will offer a more accessible option to consumers. "The Blazer's primary competition would be the Ford Edge and it would be built at GM's Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila, assembly plant," says WardsAuto's report.
Mexico's auto imports reach record high in first quarter 2017

For the first time, Mexico's auto imports surpassed the 200,000-unit milestone in a three-month period. According to AMIA, imports totaled 217,525 vehicles in first quarter 2017, up 12.6% compared to same period 2016. During the period that ended March 31, 42% of units sold over the Mexican market were assembled in the country, while the rest 58% came from abroad. Imports from Asia have grown 50.8%, while those from U.S. and Canada increased 16.9% during the first quarter.
Judge approves US$ 2.8 billion criminal penalty for Volkswagen

A judge approved a US$ 2.8 billion criminal penalty for Volkswagen in the U.S. for cheating on diesel emissions tests, in a scheme involving 580,000 diesel cars programmed to turn on pollution controls during testing and off while on the road. Since the September 2015 disclosure that VW intentionally cheated for at least six years, the company has agreed to spend up to US$ 25 billion in the United States to address claims from owners, environmental regulators, states and dealers and to make buy-back offers.
AK Steel develops two new lines of high-strength automotive steel

AK Steel announced that demonstration samples of its two new types of Advanced High Strength Steel have now been delivered to multiple automotive customers for evaluation. The AK Steel NEXMET family of steel products enables automotive companies to design and manufacture lighter-weight vehicles that are capable of meeting increasingly stringent emissions regulations and passenger safety requirements. The new NEXMET 1000 and 1200 products were designed specifically for use in automotive structural components.
Mazak to open customer support center in Queretaro

Japan-based machine tool manufacturer Mazak announced it will open in the coming months a new customer support center in El Marques, Queretaro, to enhance levels of localized service. The new building will house applications and service technicians, offer training to customers and demonstrate Mazak machine tool and automation technologies, including those specifically geared toward the automotive industry. The facility encompasses nearly 6,000 square feet, including training space and a 3,200-square-foot showroom that will house some of Mazak's most advanced machining systems.
VW to invest US$ 300 million in charge stations network

Electrify America, a wholly owned subsidiary of Volkswagen Group of America, is set to invest US$ 300 million in a network of hundreds of electric-vehicle charge stations in 11 different urban markets, including facilities along popular highway corridors. It's the first stage of a deployment meant to boost demand for electric vehicles. Construction is expected to begin in the second quarter of 2017 to complete work by 2019. The project promises "the most advanced charging technology ever deployed," with an estimated 15 to 20-minute charging time for the next generation of electric vehicles.
GM grants innovation award to Magna's laser-based process

Canadian automotive supplier Magna International Inc. has won a General Motors Supplier of the Year Innovation award for its unique process of laser cutting and welding front fascias for the 2017 Chevrolet Camaro ZL1. Magna leaders accepted the award at General Motors' 25th annual Supplier of the Year ceremony in Orlando, Florida. Magna's laser cutting and welding process provides greater flexibility when making lower-volume parts and enables weight savings by using a thinner-walled component. Magna also won a 2016 Innovation Award from the Society of Plastics Engineers for this process.
Honda invests US$ 124 million for advanced wind tunnel in Ohio

Honda today announced a new investment of US$ 124 million to establish a multifunctional aeroacoustic wind tunnel facility to advance vehicle innovation and enhance the world-class testing facilities at the Transportation Research Center (TRC), in East Liberty, Ohio. The groundbreaking is slated for the late summer of 2017. The aeroacoustic wind tunnel facility will have space for four secure and confidential customer bays, providing the opportunity for use by customers other than Honda.
Growing skepticism on self-driving technology, J.D. Power study finds

More consumers are becoming skeptical of self-driving vehicles even as they increasingly want semiautonomous safety technology, a J.D. Power and Associates study found. Each age group J.D. Power surveyed, except for those born between 1977 and 1994, showed less trust in autonomous technology than they did in last year's survey. The number of respondents in Generation Z, those born between 1995 and 2004, who said they "definitely would not" trust self-driving technology rose by 11% points to 22% compared with last year's study. Meanwhile, 81% of Baby Boomers say they "definitely" would not trust self-driving technology, up from 77% in 2016. J.D.
VW plans to take on Tesla with four affordable models

Volkswagen AG is gearing up to take on Tesla Inc. with its namesake brand planning to roll out four affordable electric vehicles in the coming years and the Audi unit debuting an upscale battery-powered crossover it aims to introduce in 2019. To make its electric vehicles more viable, the German company has made "huge progress" in reducing production costs, Christian Senger, head of the VW electric-car project, said at the Shanghai International Auto Show, where the nameplate unveiled its first battery-powered crossover. The models will be partly developed in China and also include a mid-size sport utility vehicle, a hatchback and a sedan.
