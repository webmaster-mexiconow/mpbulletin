Mexico's auto production increases 7.7% in September, exports up 15%

Last month, Mexico's automotive industry built 307,174 cars and light trucks, a 7.7% increase compared to the 285,344 units assembled in the ninth month of 2016 and the highest figure for a month of September ever, according to data from AMIA. From January through September, auto plants established in Mexico have built 2,829,761 vehicles, a 9.8% raise compared to the first nine months of 2016, the AMIA added. Exports also reached new heights, during September 270,899 units were shipped to foreign markets, that's 15% more than those exported in the same month of 2016. For the first nine months of 2017 exports totaled 2,287,896 vehicles, an increase of 11.5%.
Sources: Volkswagen Puebla plant tools up to build the T Roc compact SUV

Volkswagen's Puebla assembly plant is already in the tooling process to build the new T Roc compact SUV, which was revealed in Frankfurt early this year, Mexican automotive news site Al Volante reported citing unnamed sources. According to Al Volante sources, it's only a matter of weeks for the first T Roc pre-production prototypes to roll out of the plant. The production model will be present again at Los Angeles Auto Show by early December, where VW will confirm its arrival to the U.S. market, said Al Volante. Also, production of the Beetle is scheduled to cease by the end of 2017. Arguably this closure will help to increase output capacity for the all-new Tiguan.
Next-gen Mazda 3 to feature spark-less ignition engine

Mazda Motor Corporation announced it will unveil a new product concept model at the 2017 Tokyo Motor Show. Mazda will also display the SKYACTIV-X next-generation engine, which is the first gasoline unit capable of spark-less ignition. The model, a compact hatchback that resembles the Mazda 3, "adopts SKYACTIV-Vehicle Architecture, next-generation technologies that apply a human-centered design philosophy for optimal functionality, and is powered by the SKYACTIV-X," said the company. The Mazda 3 sedan and hatchback is assembled in Guanajuato, where there's also an adjacent engine production facility. However, there's still to be a confirmation by Mazda that both the new Mazda 3 and the SKYACTIV-X engines will be manufactured in Mexico.
Mizayaki Seiko opens US$ 11 million plant in Jalisco

Mizayaki Seiko, a specialty steel bars and wire manufacturer, officially opened its first plant in Mexico located at the Colinas de Lagos industrial park in the municipality of Lagos de Moreno, Jalisco. The Japan-based company invested US$ 11 million and recruited 50 workers to manufacture cold drawn bars for automotive applications. The company will provide such products to auto suppliers that build components for automakers like Toyota, Honda and Mazda. This the second Japanese manufacturer recently opening operations at Colinas de Lagos. In early September, Yasunaga opened a US$ 10 million facility to manufacture connecting rods.
Japanese coil spring manufacturer NHK Spring expands in Guanajuato

Japanese coil spring manufacturer NHK Spring broke ground last week at its plant located in the municipality of Irapuato, Guanajuato, to build a new facility where the company will invest US$ 6.8 million in order to expand operations. La company expects to double its workforce from current 250 to 500 strong once the new premises become fully operational. This NHK Spring plant supplies components to original equipment manufacturers such as Nissan, Mazda and Ford, as well as auto suppliers like Rassini. However, at the ground-breaking ceremony word got out this facility will also supply parts for the new Infiniti assembly plant under construction in Aguascalientes.
Round four of NAFTA talks begins with difficult demands to fulfill

The fourth round of talks to renegotiate the NAFTA began Wednesday in Arlington, Virginia with contentious new U.S. demands threatening to collapse the process. The new round promises to be difficult, with U.S. Trade Representative Robert Lighthizer announcing early that talks would be extended by two days to October 17. Business groups see several U.S. proposals that could result in deadlock, including a sunset clause that would require the trade pact to be renewed in five years, minimum U.S. content in cars, and major changes in dispute-resolution mechanisms.
U.S. content in Mexican autos higher than argued: AMIA

The Mexican Automotive Industry Association (AMIA) said U.S. content in vehicles made in Mexico is far higher than Trump administration have argued. Last month, the U.S. Commerce Department said an analysis of OECD trade in value-added data found U.S. content in automotive imports from Mexico fell to 18.1% in 2011 from 26.5% in 1995. U.S. content in automotive imports from Canada declined to 26.4% from 34.9% over the same period. However, AMIA's president Eduardo Solis said the group's own analysis found that U.S. content in Mexican parts ranged between 37% and 39.5%. In the case of Canadian cars, the U.S. content varied from between 48% to 52%.
Strike at Canadian GM plant puts pressure on Chevrolet Equinox inventory

It's been four weeks since the strike started at the CAMI Assembly plant in Ingersoll, Ontario, and the stock of the Chevrolet Equinox SUVs is drying up despite efforts to ramp up production in Mexico. According to the Automotive News Data Center, U.S. inventory dropped to 43,453 vehicles, or a 41-day supply, at the beginning of October. Estimated inventory stood at an all-time high of 74,400 units, or a 66-day supply, in June. Since the June peak, Equinox inventory has dropped on average about 10,300 units per month. "That's going to be hard on Equinox to compete on such a limited quantity in such a hot segment," said Edmunds analyst Jessica Caldwell.
GST AutoLeather, an auto supplier with seven plants in Mexico, files for bankruptcy

GST AutoLeather, Inc. a Michigan-based developer and manufacturer of automotive leather interior trim solutions, announced that it has filed a voluntary petition for relief under chapter 11 of the United States Bankruptcy Code in the United States Bankruptcy Court for the District of Delaware. The company employs around 3,000 workers at seven production facilities in Mexico, three are located in Arteaga, Coahuila; one in Nuevo Laredo, Tamaulipas and three other plants in Leon, Guanajuato. GST AutoLeather has obtained a commitment from its senior secured lenders for a US$ 40 million debtor-in-possession facility, the proceeds of which will be used to fund ongoing business operations.
Bendix opens logistics center in its Acuna campus

In response to dramatic, prolonged growth at its Acuna, Coahuila, manufacturing campus, Bendix Commercial Vehicle Systems LLC has established the Acuna Logistics Center to help improve materials flow, inventory control, and cost savings. The company's Acuna operation had previously relied on several satellite warehouses for receiving and distribution. The logistics center is the fourth building on the expanding Acuna campus. Comprising 109,000 square feet, is located 1.5 miles from Bendix's Acuna Plant 1, and is staffed by 38 employees, a number which is expected to grow to meet increased need.
