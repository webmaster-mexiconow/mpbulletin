Auto parts production expects fall of 24% in 2020: INA

The Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA) estimated that at the close of 2020 auto parts production will see a turnover of US$74.7 billion, which represents an annual fall of 24% before the US$97.8 billion from 2019. According to Oscar Albin, president of INA, the auto parts industry lost US$23 billion in production during 2020's first semester due to the halt in vehicle production both in Mexico and the United States, as a health measure to prevent the spread of the pandemic caused by COVID-19.

Heavy vehicle sales recover during June

Retail sales for heavy vehicles in Mexico managed to recover during June to levels seen before the pandemic, after experiencing two complicated months; nonetheless, companies' commercialization during the sixth month of the year presented a fall of 46% when compared to the same month of 2019. Miguel Elizalde, president of the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), informed that in June there were a total of 2,183 sold vehicles through retail and 1,985 wholesale sold vehicles, which meant an improvement compared to April and May, but it was due to "back orders," which would mean that 2020 will close with a 20% fall.

Chihuahua entrepreneurs ask to open automotive production at 100%

Entrepreneurs from Chihuahua City considered that Ford Motor Company's position on the unsustainability of the production of the plant located in the capital is a "wake-up call" for the automotive sector in the state to start operating at 100% capacity. Chihuahua's plant is operating at 50% of its capacity, in compliance with the mandate established by the State Government as the territory is at an orange light due to registered cases of coronavirus.

Ford invests US$1.1 billion in Hermosillo

With an investment of US$1.1 billion, Ford Motors Company will now produce the Bronco Sport SUV in its Hermosillo, Sonora plant in substitution of its Fusion and MKZ models, which will leave the assembly line on July 30, informed Jorge Vidal Ahumada, the state's minister of Economy. Over the course of a year, the American automaker prepared to adapt its Sonora plant to the new United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) requirements.

It's necessary to incentivize financing for the acquisition of heavy vehicles

If financing for the acquisition of heavy vehicles is incentivized, especially for small and medium carriers, the recovery in bus and truck demand could go back to 2019 levels and even be advanced to 2021, assured Miguel Elizalde, president of the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT). If the acquisition of advanced technology vehicles is stimulated, this would help carriers do more with less, thus inhibiting price raises in products, given the operative and logistic efficiency.

INEGI will publish the Administrative Register of the Heavy Vehicles' Automotive Industry

Starting in July, the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI) will be publishing the production, sales and exports data for heavy vehicles from the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT) members. INEGI and ANPACT, in association with AMDA presented the Administrative Register of the Heavy Vehicles' Automotive Industry. Miguel Elizalde, president of ANPACT, commented that the signing of this agreement is an effort on behalf of INEGI and ANPACT of offering clear and transparent information that supports a healthy competition environment.

Mexico surpasses Canada and the United States in quality: J.D. Power

A study carried out recently by consulting firm J.D. Power reveals that the country is positioning itself in North America by the quality of the vehicles it produces. For the first time, Mexico has surpassed Canada and United States in this rubric, according to the opinion of American consumers. In J.D. Power's quality ranking, Mexico took ninth place globally, with an average of 36 errors or defects reported by every 100 vehicles. Canada comes in at 13th place with 39 reported defects and the United States in 15th with 41 problems for every 100 vehicles.
