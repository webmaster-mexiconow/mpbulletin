Heavy vehicle production and exports increase in October

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) informed that, during October, production of cargo vehicles reached a total of 17,391 units, which represents an increase of 2.7% when compared to the same month of last year. At the same time, in October, there was a total of 14,573 units exported, which meant an increase of 9.6% in relation to the same month of 2018.

KIA Mexico unveils the specifications of the KIA Seltos

The long awaited KIA Seltos already has specifications for Mexico. The vehicle will be offered in three versions, all of them imported from India: EX, EX Pack and GT Line. The first two have an atmospheric 1.6 liter engine of 121 hp and 112 lb-ft, associated to a six speed automatic transmission. The EX version can be equipped with a manual six speed transmission. The KIA Seltos GT Line will be the only one with a 1.4 liter turbo engine, capable of generating 138 hp and 179 lb-ft, and will be available only with an automatic double clutch seven speed transmission.

AMDA proposes a package of proposals to strengthen the internal market

The Mexican automotive industry will present a package of proposals to the federal government to reactivate the internal market, among which there's fiscal stimuli and eliminating vehicular tenure throughout the country. Additionally, there's a call to ensure stricter customs inspections to fight the entry of illegal vehicles, bigger vehicular control through the creation of a unique license plate and simplifying the law against money laundering.

The decree that regulates the definite importation of used vehicles is extended

Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador's government determined to extend through to December 31, 2020 the program that regulates the definite importation of used vehicles coming from abroad and into the country. Consequently, vehicles coming from the United States and Canada can be imported with any antiquity year without paying a tariff, as long as they present an origin certificate that endorses the car's manufacturing in North America and a certificate of polluting emissions.

Mazda Mexico manages to increase sales by 3.4% in 2019

Mazda Mexico's sales increased 3.4% in 2019, when compared to 2018. The following information comes from Mazda North America's monthly sales report, in which it points out that, in 2019, Mazda sold 60,081 vehicles in Mexico, which results in the aforementioned increase of 3.4%. In December 2019, however, Mazda sold 5,906 vehicles, which means a decrease of 2.5% in comparison with the same month of 2018.

Queretaro's automotive industry closes 2019 with good results

The local automotive industry reported an optimal and atypical close of 2019, which contrasted with years that are characterized by closing production cycles and thus a slower activity. The automotive companies located in the state showed bigger optimism, with open production lines and even staggered shutdowns and not total ones, said the director of the Automotive Cluster of Queretaro, Daniel Hernandez Camacho. The cluster's director commented that at the close of the year there was an increase in production demand in the state's industry.

Toyota ends 2019 with 105,663 sold units

Toyota Motor Sales of Mexico ended the year with a total of 105,663 sold units, which places it as the fourth seller in the national automotive industry. As of December, Toyota closed its fourth best month of 2019 by placing 11,321 vehicles. "2019 meant a continuous improvement, due to the challenges that we had to face in Toyota and the automotive sector in general. The challenge was great, especially in the year's first semester; however, Toyota was able to meet the needs of its clients and close the year in a good way," commented Tom Sullivan, president of Toyota Motor Sales of Mexico.
