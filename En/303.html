BMW expands EV Corridor with new charging station in Puebla

After announcing in 2019 the start of operations for the EV open Corridor BMW Puebla-CDMX-San Luis Potosi (SLP), BMW Group Latin America is keepings its promise with the new station "Supercharge Capillita" in Puebla, which allows users to drive from Puebla's Zocalo to SLP's Zocalo, thus making this corridor the biggest in Latin America. It's important to highlight that the new "Supercharge Capillita" station offers free and multiplatform service, which is to say, it adapts to hybrid and electric units from different automotive brands.

Toyota sold 8,126 units in January

Toyota met their monthly goal by selling 8,126 units. In this way, the Japanese brand occupies the fourth place within the Mexican automotive industry, with a market participation of 7.8%. During January, the 5 vehicles with a highest sales percentage were RAV4 with 1,229 units, Hilux with 1,059; Corolla sold 963 units, Avanza 712 and Hiace 666, representing more than half of the company's total sales, with almost 57%.

Kia sold 7,754 units during the first month of the year

Kia managed to sell 7,754 units, which means the South Korean brand occupies 5th place in the national market, with a market participation of 7.4%. The top vehicles were Kia Rio with 3,020 placed units; followed by Forte in second place with 1,481; Sportage with 1,059, while the biggest surprise of this period came from Seltos with 823 sold units, being the Kia Motors Mexico model with the most sold units on its release month. Rounding out the top five is the iconic Soul, with 639 units.

Toyota in Apaseo el Grande is officially inaugurated

The Japanese automaker officially inaugurated Toyota Motor Manufacturing Guanajuato (TMMG) installed in Apaseo el Grande, Guanajuato. "We're very excited to welcome you to Toyota's new home in Guanajuato, our manufacturing plant in Apaseo el Grande represents a key part for North America," expressed Francisco Garcia, president of TMMG during his speech in the inauguration ceremony. The complex has the highest technology and innovation for the manufacturing of the Tacoma, a high-quality vehicle that will meet the demands of the North American and local markets, with the honor of being produced in Mexico.

320,495 vehicles were produced in January

During January 320,495 units were produced in Mexican territory, which represents a fall of 4% in annual comparison, informed the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). This is the first fall presented by the industry in a similar month in 11 years. This reduction responds to a lower demand outside the country, due to the fact that only 228,184 vehicles were sent abroad, 5.8% less than that reported in the first month of 2019. This is the lowest number reported for the external sector in January since 2009, year in which the world faced an economic crisis.

Toyota will invest US$247 million in Guanajuato

Japanese automaker Toyota announced that it will invest US$247 million in Guanajuato, as part of its commitment to the entity, said Luis Lozano, legal and institutional relations director for the brand in Mexico. In conference with the inauguration of the plant in Apaseo el Grande, the director said that these are in addition to those used for the construction of the assembly plant. He commented that there isn't a determined project in which this capital might be used yet, nor are they subject to a set schedule.

Auto transport industry demands modification to the NOM-044

Representatives from organizations such as Concamin, Coparmex, Canacar and Canapat, as well as ANPACT, AMDA, AMTM, INA and Conatram made a joint call to Semarnat to modify the NOM-044, with the purpose of having the standards EPA7/EuroV and EPA 10/EuroVI in new engines to coexist, until Pemex can guarantee the supply of ultra-low sulfur diesel (ULSD) throughout the country. "We're facing an inconsistency. On the one hand, the heavy vehicles automotive industry is being obligated to have cleaner environmental technology in 10 months; while 81% of cities within the country don't have an obligation of supplying ULSD," explained Miguel Elizalde, executive president of ANPACT.
