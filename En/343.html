62,837 new vehicles were commercialized during June

During June of 2020 there were a total of 62,837 commercialized new vehicles in the Mexican market. This sales level meant a slight recovery of almost 50% when compared to the previous month of May when 42,028 units were sold. Despite this increase, the level is significantly low to that registered before the pandemic. In June the fall in sales was of 41% in comparison to the same month of 2019, adding another fall. In the year's accumulated sales, from January to June there's been a total of 436,445 sold units, a level 31.9% lower in annual comparison.

General Motors Mexico is recognized as a Socially Responsible Company

General Motors of Mexico received the Socially Responsible Company (ESR) certification from the Mexican Center for Philanthropy (Cemefi) and the Alliance for Corporate Social Responsibility in Mexico (AliaRSE), as a result of the actions it carries out in favor of its work team and society. FCA sold 3,909 units during June

This first semester RAM placed itself as the most sold FCA Mexico brand, thanks to the versatility offered by its vehicles, which work for day to day work as well as for lifestyle, without sacrificing performance. The value of labor content is important in the automotive sector origin rules

The uniform regulations of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) detail the way in which the Value of Labor Content (VLC) in the automotive sector rules of origin will be measured. Currently the VLC is of 30%, which consists of at least 15 percentage points of spending in materials and workforce with higher salaries, no more than 10 percentage points of spending in technology and no more than 5 percentage points in the establishment of high salaries. This clause will be in force starting July 1st 2020 to June 30th 2021.

The USMCA is a challenge, but one the automotive industry is ready to face

The automotive industry is ready to meet the USMCA's new requirements even when it is still facing a complex global situation, as expressed by the leaders of light and heavy vehicles and auto parts producers associations. Oscar Albin, INA's president, highlighted that the automotive industry, with the agriculture sector, were the big winners of the now extinct NAFTA, and now they will be for the USMCA, "I don't see any problems, we have the conditions, because the automotive industry cannot change in the blink of an eye."

BRP will invest US$136 million in Juarez

Bombardier Recreational Products, announced the first investment in Mexico of the era of the trade agreement between the United States, Mexico and Canada (USMCA). The Canadian firm will build a plant for the production of 4-wheeled vehicles in Ciudad Juarez, Chihuahua. In addition, the company announced that around US$136 million will be invested in this project. José Boisjoli, president and CEO of the company, said that despite the pandemic, demand for their products has remained strong and even exceeded last year's figures for the same period.

Toyota obtained an 8.5% market share

Toyota thanked its clients for their continuous trust and for the fact that they keep choosing their vehicles, since June contributed to the brand reaching a participation share of 8.5%, with which it remains in the fourth place within the industry. The challenge faced by Mexico and the world is big, which is why the Japanese brand has adjusted its production to the country's new demand. At the same time, it thanks its customers for choosing their products, since they reached the sale of 5,428 units during June.
