Trump considers 25 percent tariff on imported cars

President Donald Trump has instructed the Department of Commerce "to consider initiating an investigation" to determine whether automotive imports "threaten to impair the national security" of the United States, the White House said in a statement last night. Such probe could result in 25 percent tariffs on imported vehicles. A source with knowledge, Commerce Secretary Wilbur Ross's strategic thinking told DailyMail that steel, not the global automobile trade, is at the center of the controversy. According to the report, Trump is using the threat of tariffs to persuade the companies to use more American steel. More details here: bit.ly/2J0hAlZ
KIA celebrates two years of building cars in Mexico; readies production of next-gen Forte

Korean automaker KIA Motors recently completed two years manufacturing cars at its plant located in the municipality of Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon, with a production of 417,000 vehicles to date. KIA began operations on May 16, 2016, at its Mexican plant, which currently produces one vehicle every 53 seconds. The Forte and Rio models, as well as the Hyundai Accent, are manufactured in the facility, all of them in sedan and hatchback versions. At the ceremony, the president of the plant, Woo Yoel Park, revealed that the Pesqueria facility will start production of the all-new 2019 KIA Forte in July.
BMW taps fellow German developer BayWa to build solar farm for its San Luis Potosi plant

BMW has signed a long-term power purchase agreement with Germany-based BayWa for the development of a photovoltaic (PV) farm, to supply 252 MWh of power per year to the new manufacturing facility that the automaker currently builds in San Luis Potosi. The deal was closed for a total of US$ 165 million. The energy will be generated by a solar plant that will be constructed, operated and maintained by BayWa inside the BMW premises. This project will cut BMW’s CO2 emissions by approximately 208 tons annually, in line with BMW’s worldwide sustainability principles to continue reducing its carbon footprint worldwide. More details here: bit.ly/2J3ehKY
Ford honors Mexican manufacturer Metalsa as top-performing global supplier

Mexico-based manufacturer Metalsa was recognized as a top-performing global supplier for Ford Motor Company at the 20th annual Ford World Excellence Awards, the company revealed in a statement. Only 30 companies were selected as finalists from thousands of Ford suppliers globally. Metalsa was presented with a Special Recognition Award for the "Exemplary Launch of the 2018 Expedition and Navigator Full Frame" by Ford Motor Company's Joe Hinrichs, executive vice president and president, global operations, and Hau Thai-Tang, executive vice president, product development and purchasing. More details here: bit.ly/2LqQBys
Canadian manufacturer Magna, with 32 plants in Mexico, posts record quarterly profit

Canadian automotive supplier Magna International Inc. reported better-than-expected first quarter results on a record profit of US$ 660 million or US$1.83 per share for the quarter, up 14.4 percent from US$577 million, or US$ 1.51 per share a year ago. Revenue totaled US$10.8 billion, up 21.3 percent compared with US$8.9 billion in the same quarter last year. Given the latest results, the company raised its full-year sales target to a range between US$40.9 billion and US$43.1 billion, up from its previous forecast range of US$39.3 billion to US$41.5 billion, and 2018 net income of US$2.4 billion to US$ 2.6 billion, slightly higher than its previous projection of US$2.3 billion to US$2.5 billion. New York-based ITT Corp. opens US$ 60 million plant in Guanajuato

New York-based diversified manufacturer ITT Corporation announced it has officially began production of highly engineered brake pads in a new plant located at the Guanajuato Puerto Interior industrial park, in the municipality of Silao, Guanajuato. Through its subsidiary ITT Motion Technologies, the company invested US$ 60 million and opened 300 job positions in order to start production with an output capacity of 1.7 million pieces per year, which will gradually increase to 20 million in the next couple years. Tunisian cable manufacturer COFICAB targets Ciudad Juarez to set up its third plant in Mexico

Four years after establishing operations in Mexico, Tunisian cable and wire manufacturer COFICAB Group will invest US$ 50 million to open its third plant in the country, this time in Ciudad Juarez, Chihuahua, state officials announced. This new facility, in addition to those that COFICAB already operates in the cities of Durango, state capital, and Silao, Guanajuato, will begin operations in September to provide raw material to automotive harness manufacturers in the region. The plant will be located in the Intermex Sur Industrial Park, where it will open 300 jobs positions to manufacture three million kilometers of cable annually with expected sales of US$ 250 million. More details here: bit.ly/2IWG8MC
Taiwanese manufacturer Hiroca to begin production at Guanajuato plant by fourth quarter

Hiroca Holdings Ltd., a Taiwanese auto parts manufacturer based in China said last week its new plant in Mexico is scheduled to begin production in the fourth quarter of this year. The company plans to invest US$ 4 million in a joint venture with Israel-based Arkal Automotive CS Ltd. to build a facility in Guanajuato, which would produce plastic automotive components for major car brands, including Volkswagen AG and Daimler AG, Hiroca said in a statement on Friday last week. The capacity expansion project is in line with Hiroca’s business strategy to attract more global brand clients, especially electric vehicle developers, by providing lightweight car parts.
Carbures signs deal to build seven testing lines for steering systems in Queretaro

Carbures, a Spain-based designer of engineering solutions, announced it has signed a EUR 1.6 million (about US$ 1.88 million) contract with a Tier 1 automotive supplier in Mexico to provide seven testing lines for electronic steering systems. The testing lines will be manufactured at the Carbures plant located in Queretaro, to be delivered ready and equipped with its own engineering and first-of-its-kind software, which allows quality control of steering systems at high production rates. GM adds new four-cylinder turbocharged engine to the next-gen Chevrolet Silverado

Chevrolet is adding a four-cylinder turbocharged engine -that can run on two cylinders -to its all-new 2019 Silverado pickup truck, in hopes of topping the 22 mpg (combined rating) of two variants of the Ford F-150. Standard on LT and RST trims, the new engine delivers an SAE-certified 310 horsepower and 348 lb-ft of torque, for 22 percent more torque than the 4.3L V-6 it replaces. Developed specifically for truck applications, the new 2.7L Turbo inline four-cylinder engine delivers peak torque from 1,500 to 4,000 rpm. The next-gen Silverado with the 2.7L Turbo delivers 0-60 mph performance in less than seven seconds and weighs 380 pounds less than the current Silverado with the 4.3L V-6.
