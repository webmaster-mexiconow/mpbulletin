Kia is extending its Kia Promise campaign through to August

"Kia Promise" is a coverage campaign launched on a global level to bring benefits to vehicles commercialized by Kia Motors Mexico and its distributors throughout the country. The benefits apply for clients whose guarantee expired between March 23 and June 30 of 2020 or whose service appointment was programmed for said period. The program extends coverage for these vehicles until August 31st, 2020 and clients will be able to carry out maintenance service in any of the brand's models until this date, without losing the guarantee.

Ford recalls F-150 models

Ford recalled 8,053 trucks sold in Mexico from the model F-150 from the years 2014 and 2017, equipped with a 3.5 liter EcoBoost engine. In a press release, the company highlighted that this was because of a mistake in the master cylinder of the braking system that might generate a change on the ride and the sensation that the braking pedal needs more effort," said the company.

Volkswagen will apply more than 100 hygiene measures for the restart of operations

Volkswagen of Mexico has presented a report on the effects the COVID-19 pandemic has had on its personnel in and out of their plants. The report said that: "we've successfully implemented sanitary protocols in the interior of our plants in Puebla and Silao on which there's been around 5% of the average personnel every day, during the days that's operations have been halted, to carry out basic surveillance, maintenance and design work and the implementation of more than 100 hygiene measures without having knowledge of a single contagion case on the inside of the factories."

Magna restarts operations in Mexico

Auto parts company Magna International informed that it restarted its operations in Mexico where it operates 33 auto parts production plants. "All of our plants in Mexico have received the approval of the government to restart production and substantially every single one is operative," said the company. Magna International is the biggest auto parts producer in Mexico and one of the biggest 10 private employers in the country. Although it supplies parts to all the main OEMs, most of their sales are for six clients: General Motors, Ford, Fiat Chrysler, Daimler, BMW and Volkswagen.

Barbosa issued a decree to stop the restart of operations of Puebla's automotive industry

Miguel Barbosa, governor of Puebla, published a decree that establishes that there's no conditions to reopen the automotive industry on June 15, as previously expected. This is the second decree issued by Barbosa, to postpone the restart of activities for the automotive sector, on top of the construction sector, of the entity. The first decree was published on May 22 with the goal of stopping the reopening of the Volkswagen and Audi plants and about a hundred manufacturers of auto parts projected for June 1st.

Daimler Trucks' plant in Saltillo has a new general manager

After 36 years in the company, George Ferro, general manager of Daimler Trucks' Manufacturing Plant in Saltillo, is retiring in October. George began his career in Chrysler in 1984 in Canada, and in 2007, he entered Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA) as director of Operations of the Trucks Sterling plant in St. Thomas, Ontario. Jane Rosaasen, the current general manager of Trucks' Manufacturing Plant in Mt. Holly, will assume the general manager position for Trucks' Manufacturing Plant in Saltillo.

Volkswagen and Audi will not restart activities on June 15

Neither Volkswagen nor Audi will restart operations on Monday, June 15 as informed to their respective workers' unions. Although both companies have announced that they will restart activities at this date, although the date was changed again after the decree emitted last Friday by the state government, in which it establishes that there's no health conditions in the entity for their return, although it didn't set sanctions in case of breach. In the meanwhile, companies will carry out activities to restart operations.
