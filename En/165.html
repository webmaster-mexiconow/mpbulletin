Honda: production in Celaya will resume until highest quality is ensured

Honda de Mexico announced that it will take several weeks to determine when its auto plant in Celaya, Guanajuato, can resume Fit and HR-V production. Operations on site were suspended on June 28 due to the overflow of a river nearby. As a result, production of the Honda Fit and HR-V in Celaya has been suspended for the month of July. The Honda transmission plant located in the same premises resumed production effective July 9, said the company in a statement. "Our commitment is always to provide our customers with high quality products, and production will not resume until we can ensure the highest levels of quality for all parts and products," says the document. Toyota plant in Guanajuato will triple production capacity in first six years

Nine months after Toyota announced that its Guanajuato plant would produce Toyota pickups instead of the Corolla sedan, the Japanese automaker confirmed that the manufacturing complex is already 40% complete and on schedule to open by the end of 2019. Previously the company had established a production capacity of 100,000 units per year, but that figure is expected to change. Guillermo Diaz, Operations Director of Toyota Motor Sales of Mexico, informed in a press conference that, in a first phase, the plant will assemble 100,000 units and will generate 1,100 jobs. But in a second phase, the production will rise to 200,000 units and later it will reach a capacity of 300,000 light trucks, all this before 2025. More details here: bit.ly/2KYch8l
Production of new Mercedes-Benz A-Class starts in Finland; China and Mexico are next

The production ramp-up for the new Mercedes-Benz A-Class has reached Finland, three months after it began in April at the Mercedes-Benz plant in Rastatt, Germany, and was followed by the plant in Kecskemét, Hungary, in May. Mercedes-Benz production partner Valmet Automotive last week started production of the new A-Class in its plant in Uusikaupunki, the third location in the five-plant global production network that also includes facilities in China and Mexico. Before the end of this year, the COMPAS (Cooperation Manufacturing Plant in Aguascalientes) plant in central Mexico will join the production network. More details here: bit.ly/2uqBSwB
Toyota plant in Guanajuato will be 'able to build nearly any vehicle'

Toyota de Mexico President Mike Bafan said in interview to AutoNews that the assembly plant the Japanese automaker currently builds in Guanajuato will feature enough flexibility to make nearly anything that Toyota executives ask for. The facility scheduled to open in late 2019 will complete some form of logistic triangle to supply Tacoma and Tundra pickup trucks to the North American market from three plants: Tijuana, San Antonio and Guanajuato, while paving the way for new products. "I want to see the look on their face when they say, 'Mike, we've decided to go from truck to car,' or 'We've come up with this thingamajig. Can you do it down there?' I want to say, 'Yes, we can do it.' We can do anything," added Bafan. More details here: bit.ly/2NOzgR3
Mexico will end 2018 as fifth biggest auto parts manufacturer worldwide: INA

In recent years, Mexico has established itself as the fifth producer of auto parts in the world, with expected annual sales of US$90 billion in 2018, said Oscar Albin Santos, executive president of the National Auto Parts Industry (INA) in recent days. According to the official, the Mexican auto parts industry consists of approximately 1,300 factories that employ 820,000 people. Of these plants, 35% are constituted with Mexican capital, while 90% of the 880 foreign capital plants are managed by Mexican executives. Based on these figures, Mexico ranks as the fifth producer of auto parts, after China, the United States, Japan and Germany. More details here: bit.ly/2zHO2pV
Canadian manufacturer Martinrea opens its second plant in Guanajuato

Canadian metal auto parts manufacturer Martinrea International opened its second plant in the state of Guanajuato, and tenth overall in the country. The 14,800-square-meter facility is located in the Guanajuato Puerto Interior industrial park in the municipality of Silao. The company invested US$30 million and opened 200 job positions in order to start operations in the facility which houses five production lines of 5,900 ton stamping machines, 23 assembly cells and 80 robots. Once fully operational, the plant will have capacity to manufacture 35,000 pieces each day. Some of the automakers the company supplies from Mexican facilities are: FCA, Ford, Nissan, Infiniti, General Motors, Volkswagen, Mercedes Benz and BMW. More details here: bit.ly/2NNH9Gm
HBPO to open two new plants in Mexico by the end of year, despite uncertainty of NAFTA

HBPO, a supplier of front-end modules, revealed to Wards Auto that its plans to open two new assembly plants in Saltillo and Aguascalientes remains unchanged despite the uncertainty regarding the renegotiation of NAFTA. The joint venture between Hella, Behr and Plastic Omnium, already operates plants in Puebla, Toluca and San Jose Chiapas. The Puebla plant also increased production of plastic components 50% earlier this year with the addition of a third injection-molding press. With the two new plants in Mexico fully operational, the company hopes its annual revenues to top US$1 billion by 2022, said a top executive cited in the article. More details here: bit.ly/2NfnRZG
Mercedes Benz introduces full-electric buses to the Mexican market

Mercedes Benz Buses announced that in the next few months it will introduce 100% electric units to the Mexican market, with the capital of the country being the first location where these vehicles will enter into operation. The Director of Sales, Marketing and Post Sale of Mercedes Benz Buses, Raul Gonzalez, revealed that the company is about to conclude the talks with the government of Mexico City, to replace with buses from the eCitaro line the current electric transport known as trolleybuses. Gonzalez said that the buses will have an average of 150-kilometer autonomy and the recharge will be carried out in the yards at night, although some infrastructure will also be developed to recharge the units quickly. Bosch, Daimler to deploy pilot program for automated driving in California

Bosch and Daimler are speeding up the development of fully-automated and driverless driving (SAE Level 4/5) in the city, and for that end, both companies have chosen California as the pilot location for the first test fleet. In the second half of 2019, Bosch and Daimler will offer customers a shuttle service with automated vehicles on selected routes in a Californian metropolis. The pilot project will demonstrate how mobility services such as car sharing, ride-hailing and multi-modal platforms can be intelligently connected to shape the future of mobility. In addition, the partners have decided on the U.S. technology company Nvidia as the supplier of the artificial intelligence platform as part of their control unit network. More details here: bit.ly/2LokUFL
Self-driving cars don't need oversight yet, US regulator says

Heidi King, deputy administrator of the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, said in an interview to Bloomberg that it's premature to regulate the self-driving vehicles being tested by companies such as General Motors Co. and Waymo LLC. "At this point the technology is so nascent I don't think it is appropriate today to regulate. It's not there yet, but each and every day we are open to identifying when the time is right," she said. King's comment reaffirms the agency's commitment to a hands-off approach to rule for autonomous vehicles that safety advocates have criticized for being too lax, especially since an Uber Technologies Inc. self-driving SUV struck and killed a pedestrian in Phoenix, Arizona, last March.
