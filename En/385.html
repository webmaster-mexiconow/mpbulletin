Automotive production in Queretaro experiences

November has represented one of the best manufacturing records, after the impact of the pandemic, since it equals, and in some cases exceeds, the production level that took place in March, before activities stopped, said the president of Queretaro's Automotive Cluster, Renato Villaseñor Mendoza. Some companies in the cluster, he stated, are already operating at 100% of their capacity, resuming pre-pandemic production; however, there are also cases where the average fluctuates between 75 and 95% of the capacity of each company.

Ford Mexico is recognized in the Employers for Youth 2020 ranking

Ford de Mexico was recognized in the Employers for Youth 2020 ranking, which highlights the best companies for young professionals in Latin America, placing the company in ninth place. With this recognition, the company reaffirms its commitment to continue moving the country and boosting local talent by providing the necessary tools to foster the growth of young people and Mexican society. Currently, Ford de Mexico has approximately 10,000 employees, of which more than 70% are young people between 20 and 35 years of age.

Peugeot Landtrek to boost brand sales in the medium-size pickup segment

The French manufacturer, Peugeot, recovers its presence in the mid-size pickup segment not only in Mexico but also in Latin America. Since 1979, with its 504 model, the brand had no presence in this market. Peugeot's Executive Vice President, Jean Philippe Imparato, commented that the launch of the new Peugeot Landtrek has three objectives: to develop the brand outside of Europe, to increase sales volumes in the commercial vehicle segment where 77% of sales are pick-ups, and to strengthen the brand's reputation. In Mexico, 80% of sales in the commercial vehicle segment are pickups, and within this segment, 62% are concentrated in medium-size pickups, which is why Landtrek is present.

Biden's administration to boost Mexico's electric vehicle manufacturing

The administration of U.S. President-elect Joe Biden will give a major boost to the use of electric cars, since his government policy will focus on reducing pollutant emissions, said Brenda Rogel Salgado, partner of the firm Hogan Lovells. For his part, Juan Francisco Torres-Landa, partner of Hogan Lovells, noted that since Mexico is the sixth largest producer of electric vehicles in the world and the fourth largest exporter, if the country begins to promote the consumption of electric cars in the United States, "there will be no turning back" and these types of vehicles will have to be manufactured in Mexico.

Lamborghini celebrates its 10th anniversary in our country

The Murciélago, Gallardo, Aventador, Centenario, Huracan and Urus models were the first that Lamborghini commercialized upon its arrival to Mexico 10 years ago, through the distributor located at Insurgentes Sur 1608. Impregnadora del Bajio invests US$1.8 million in Silao

Impregnadora del Bajio is a local company that has managed to be included in the production chain of the region's assembly plants through the impregnation of casting parts to eliminate pores and leaks, which generates savings and benefits to the environment by not having to re-melt the aluminum. The plant is located in Las Vías II Industrial Logistics Park, and had an investment of US$1.8 million. In addition, the SME revealed that it plans to make a second investment in 2021, to manufacture the first totally Mexican impregnation machine, as well as to grow its operation with three shifts.

The rule of law is needed to attract FDI: AMIA

The President of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA), Jose Zozaya, stated that Mexico is among the 15 countries that continue to attract investment. In these times of crisis, the struggle to attract Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) is fierce, and this is when the country should be shown to have a rule of law that provides security, and infrastructure. One of the factors that is undermining the promotion of investment is the closure of the railroads for more than 5 months and the taking of roads, without the intervention of the authority.
