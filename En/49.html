Bulletin #49
8 May, 2017
Auto sales decrease 3.3% in Mexican market during April

Mexican auto dealers sold 114,477 light vehicles in April, a 3.3% fall compared to 118,407 units sold during same month from 2016, Mexican Auto Dealers Association (AMDA) revealed. Sales for the first four months of 2017 totaled 492,725 cars and trucks, up 5.8% when compared to the 465,733 vehicles sold over the same period of 2016.
Ford sales decline 7.2% in the U.S. market during April, but Lincoln MKZ is up 24.8%

Ford Motor Company's overall U.S. April 2017 sales declined 7.2% compared to a year ago, with 214,695 vehicles sold. Concerning built-in-Mexico models, the oval logo company sold 3,329 Ford Fiestas, 25.4% less compared to same month of 2016. The Ford Fusion joined the fall with 16,697 cars sold, down 19.5%. The Lincoln MKZ was Ford's only built-in-Mexico car to increase its sales in April to 2,658 units, up 24.8% compared to last year. Total MKZ sales from January to April reached 9,301 vehicles, also a 7.1% increase.
GM sales dip 5.8% in the U.S. market, but Trax deliveries are 44.3% up

General Motors reported April sales of 244,406 crossovers, trucks and cars, a 5.8% dip. In April, Chevrolet dealers delivered the first 4,500 redesigned 2018 Chevy Equinox compact crossovers with the 1.5L turbo engine. Silverado sales decreased 19.68% to 40,154 units, GMC Sierra sales decreased 15.25% to 17,400 units. GM builds both pickups in Mexico and in the U.S. Sonic sales decreased 67.76% to 1,645 units. On the bright side the Chevrolet Trax sales increased 44.32% to 6,509 units and the Cruze sales hiked 50.62% to 21,317 units. GM builds the Trax in San Luis Potosi, the Cruze rolls out of plants in Mexico and in the U.S.
FCA post a 7% fall on sales; demand for the Dodge Journey remains strong

FCA US reported sales of 177,441 units, a 7% decrease compared with sales of 190,071 units in April 2016. Sales for the redesigned Jeep Compass totaled 3,520 units during the second month since it started arriving to auto dealerships. The Dodge Journey extended the strong performance from last month and sold 8,401 units, a 72% hike compared to April 2016. Meanwhile, the Fiat 500 sales fell 20% to 1,201 units. FCA builds the Fiat 500, Compass and Journey models in Toluca. Sales for the Ram series increased 8% to 43,321 units, the company builds light pickups in Mexico and the U.S.
Audi Q5 leads the company to its 76th straight monthly record in the U.S.

Audi of America's April sales increased 5% to 18,711 vehicles, a record. Demand was strong across the product lineup, with the Q5, Q7 SUVs and the A4 sedan leading the volume gains. It was the best April sales result for Audi in the U.S., marking the brand's 76th straight monthly record. The new built-in-Puebla Q5 compact SUV led the gains with 5,028 vehicles sold, an increase of 26%.
Toyota sales drop 4.4%; Tacoma deliveries also fall but the Yaris' are up 15%

Toyota Motor North America reported U.S. April 2017 sales of 201,926 units, a decrease of 4.4% from April 2016 on a volume basis. The midsize pickup Tacoma, which is built in San Antonio and Baja California, Mexico, saw sales fall 6.1% to 17,006 vehicles, in April. For the year, Tacoma sales are down 3.5% to 60,499 units. The Yaris iA, which is built by Mazda in Guanajuato, was the only Toyota car model to post an increase in April to 3,479 units, up 15.1% compared to last year. Year-to-date sales for the Yaris totaled 12,314 units, up 18% compared to the 10,432 vehicles from the same four-month period of 2016.
Honda sales fall 7%, but the built-in-Mexico HR-V sets new record

American Honda Motor Co., Inc. reported April sales of 138,386 Honda and Acura vehicles, a decrease of 7.0% from year-ago results. However, the built-in-Mexico compact crossover HR-V set a new record, rising 22.4% on sales of 8,527 vehicles. The compact hatchback Fit did not perform so well, totaling 4,871 units, 5.1% down compared to same month 2016. Over the first four months, sales for the HR-V totaled 28,223 units and 16,773 for the Fit, which represented increases of 25.5% and 0.8% respectively.
Nissan reports 1.5% decrease in U.S. sales, but Sentra's deliveries are up 5.8%

Nissan Group announced total U.S. sales for April 2017 of 121,998 units, a decrease of 1.5% over the prior year. Concerning built-in-Mexico models, Nissan sold 8,322 vehicles of its Versa compact car, including sedan and the hatchback version known as Versa Note, a 9.2% decrease compared to April 2016. Year-to-date sales for the Versa totaled 39,397 units, down 13.7%. As for the Nissan Sentra 20,255 units were sold in April, up 5.8%. Year-to-date sales totaled 71,669 vehicles, 12.7% down compared to first four months of 2016.
Volkswagen sales increase 1.6% in the U.S. during April led by the Golf with a 40% hike

Volkswagen of America reported sales of 27,557 units delivered in April 2017, a 1.6% increase over April 2016. Concerning built-in-Mexico models, the whole Golf family saw a strong performance with monthly sales of 7,187 vehicles, up 40.7%. On the other hand, the Jetta saw a 12.1% decrease to 9,398 vehicles in April. VW sold 1,497 Beetle cars in April, meaning an increase of 27.8%. Sales for the Tiguan SUV totaled 2,939 units in April, 22.7% less than the 3,804 vehicles the previous year.
Mazda reports a 7.8% decrease on U.S. sales; Mazda3 deliveries sink 44%

Mazda North American Operations reported April U.S. sales of 24,164 vehicles, representing a decrease of 7.8% versus April of last year. Year-to-date sales through April are up 2.6% versus last year, with 93,235 vehicles sold. Sales for the Mazda3 totaled 5,980 units in April, down 44.2%, while year-to-date sales reached 26,586 vehicles, 19.1% less than same four-month period of 2016. The company combines sedan and hatchback sales for the Mazda3 in its report, but only the saloon version is built in Salamanca, Guanajuato.
Kia sales drop 5.6%, but Forte deliveries reach record figure

Kia Motors America announced April sales of 53,358 units in the U.S. market, 5.6% less than the 56,508 vehicles the year before. Meanwhile its sister brand Hyundai totaled 63,050 vehicles, an overall increase of 1.3%. The Kia Forte, built in Nuevo Leon, reported the highest monthly sales total in company history for the family of compact cars with 10,496 units, a 7.7% increase compared to the 9,740 the year before. On the opposite side, the Kia Rio saw sales drop to 1,463 units, 42% less than the 2,523 vehicles from a year ago. The Hyundai Accent, also built in Nuevo Leon, reported 6,951 units sold, 46.2% less than the 12,933 vehicles from April 2016.
