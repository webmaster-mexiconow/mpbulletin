By the end of fiscal year, Infiniti cars will be produced in Mexico: Nissan VP

Nissan is going ahead with plans to build premium-segment cars in Mexico despite U.S. President Donald Trump threats to fix a "big border tax" on imported vehicles, said Nissan Vice President Joji Tagawa last week during a conference. "We are on track, in line with the schedule and by the end of the fiscal year our new Infiniti cars will be produced there," Tagawa said.
Mexico exports to U.S. twice as much auto parts as cars

On 2016, Mexico's auto parts exports to U.S. reached US$ 55.8 billion, that's 136% more than US$ 23.6 billion value of Mexican assembled cars exported over the same year, according to latest figures from the Department of Commerce. Mexico consolidated as the main auto parts supplier to the U.S. market with a 37.8% market share, followed by China with 11.9%.
Built-in-Mexico Ford Fiesta ST gets Kelley Blue Book Award

Due in part to a low Kelley Blue Book Fair Purchase Price and low maintenance costs, the 2017 Ford Fiesta ST, built in Cuautitlan, Mexico, has received the Kelley Blue Book 2017 5-Year Cost to Own Award for sporty compact cars. Fiesta ST's competitive EPA rating of 26 mpg in city, 33 mpg in highway and 29 mpg combined also contributed to its win. Fiesta ST is equipped with a standard six-speed manual transmission and a 1.6-liter 16-valve Ti-VCT turbocharged direct-injection EcoBoost four-cylinder engine.
Tristone Flowtech invests in Mexico after 44% sales growth in NAFTA region

Frankfurt-based Tristone Flowtech Group revealed in its annual briefing that a global sales growth of 9% in 2016 versus prior year was driven by an increase in the NAFTA region of 44%. Last December the company started production at a new plant in Delicias, Mexico with engine and battery cooling applications. The new facility operates over a 16,500 square-meter surface, twice as big as the existing plant at the same location. An additional US$ 6.4 million investment in manufacturing equipment is planned for 2017 at the same plant.
Nissan sales jump 6.2% in U.S. market during January; Mexican sales drop 5.7%

Nissan Group announced total U.S. sales for January 2017 of 112,319 units, an increase of 6.2% over the prior year and a January record. Over the Mexican market sales reached 28,829 units, 5.7% less than 30,568 vehicles sold in January 2016. The Japanese automaker remains as biggest seller in Mexico for 92 months straight with a 23.4% market share.
Built-in-Mexico Q5 SUV leads Audi's sales boost during January

Audi's January sales over the U.S. market increased 11% to a record of 13,201 vehicles. Both SUVs and sedans performed well, with the Q3, Q5 SUVs and the A4 and A6 sedan models leading the volume gains. The previous January record was set in 2016 with 11,850 vehicles sold. January results were bolstered by strong demand across the SUV lineup, increasing 22% over last January. The built-in-Mexico Q5 was the volume leader with 3,636 vehicles sold, an increase of 35%.
MotorWeek grants Drivers' Choice Award to made in Mexico 2017 Ford Fusion

The 2017 Ford Fusion, built in Hermosillo, Mexico, has won the 2017 MotorWeek Drivers' Choice Award for Best Family Sedan. The adaptive cruise control with stop-and-go functionality and Pre-Collision Assist with Pedestrian Detection were among 20 driver-assist technologies available across the Fusion lineup that caught drivers' attention. Every year, MotorWeek assesses new cars, trucks and SUVs, judging vehicles from a buyer's point of view and taking into account such factors as trends, innovation, value and reliability.
Kia's new markets may offer solutions to U.S. tariffs

Kia Motors Corp. sales in Mexico folded by five from a year earlier in 2016, suggesting a possible solution to a proposed U.S. tax, market observers told The Korea Herald. In 2016, Kia Motors sold 58,112 vehicles in Mexico, up 427.3% from a year earlier. During January, sales again climbed 85.9% to 5,780 cars in January. Analysts also pointed out that overall sales in Central and South American countries jumped 22.7% to 196,938 cars last year.
Continental expands facility in Juarez, creates 300 jobs

Germany-based auto supplier Continental expanded one of its facilities in Juarez. The Continental plant 1, part of the powertrain division, manufactures 23 main products and dozens of variants. Nowadays, the company employs 2,000 workers in its two Juarez facilities and the expansion created 300 new vacancies. The amount of the investment was not disclosed.
Toyota sales drop 11.3% in U.S. market during January

Toyota Motor North America reported its U.S. January 2017 sales of 143,048 vehicles, a decrease of 11.3% from January 2016 on a volume basis. Toyota division posted January sales of 127,476 units, down 9.2% on a volume basis. Lexus posted January sales of 15,572 vehicles, down 25.6% on a volume basis. In Mexico, the automaker surpassed for the first time 8,000 units sold in a January month, a 4.5% increase compared to first month from 2016.
