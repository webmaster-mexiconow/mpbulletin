Nissan invests US$244 million in the Sentra's production

Nissan informed that it invested US$244 million to start the production of the eight generation of its compact sedan Sentra, in the Aguascalientes plant better known as A2. Nissan informed in a video press conference that most of this amount, 74% of it, was used for the development and production of the new generation engine, while 26% was used for the refurbishment of the production line for the manufacturing of the vehicle. The vehicle's eight generation, but the fifth for Mexico, will have a new four cylinder 2.0 liter engine.

Nissan will cut 200 jobs in Aguascalientes

Nissan informed that it will cut 200 jobs in its Aguascalientes plant to make operations sustainable in the midst of the coronavirus pandemic. Nissan, which also has factories in Morelos, in the center of the country, said that it is ready to meet the requirements established in the new commercial agreement for North America (USMCA), which will entry into force on July 1st. On a global scale, Nissan joins the list of multinational companies that have announced personnel cuts.

Mexico and Brazil agree on heavy vehicle trade starting on July 2023

Mexico and Brazil agreed on having free trade for heavy vehicles starting on July 2023, after a transition period of three years, with which they postponed since the start that was originally planned for July 1st, informed the Ministry of Economy. The agreement came to be thanks to the conversations between the Ministry of Economy and Brazil’s Ministries of Foreign Relations and Economy, to the protection of the Agreement of Economic Complementation No. 55 (ACE 55).

Kia Motors Mexico manufactures its first million units

After four years and a month of production, the Kia Motors Mexico plant reached its first produced million units. A white Forte with complete equipment for the American market, was the million unit produced in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon, by proud Mexican hands. Currently, Kia produces in its Mexico plant the Forte, Kia Rio and Accent models for more than 50 countries. The Kia Motors Mexico plant began production on May 16, 2016 and it took it a little over four years to reach a million produced units.

FCA plant in Toluca obtains the Gold Award from J.D. Power in manufacturing quality

Out of 75 plants in the American continent, the assembly plant in Toluca won the Gold Award in manufacturing quality, while the brand's assembly plant in Belvidere, Illinois won bronze. FCA's general performance increased by five places to reach fourth place between the 15 companies evaluated by J.D. Power. "These results reflect the relentless attitude of our team," said Mark Champine chief quality officer of FCA North America. Toluca and Belvidere are home to the Jeep brand SUVs, which produce the Compass and Cherokee, respectively.

JAC opens new distributor in Durango

JAC, the brand commercialized and assembled in Mexico by Giant Motors Latin America, announced the opening of JAC Store Durango, the brand's 33rd distributor, which welcomes Grupo Elfer as part of JAC's family. With this opening, the brand adds more than a thousand direct jobs and about 4,000 indirect ones in the country. JAC Store Durango will commercialize the brand's complete range of combustion vehicles, as well as the X200, the brand's commercial vehicle ideal for the last mile market.

Kia Motors Mexico plant gets sixth place in J.D. Power's initial quality study

The vehicles produced in the country were once again recognized by J.D. Power's Initial Quality Study (IQS) in the Unites States. Forte won first place in the compact vehicle category, while Kia Rio won third place in the subcompact vehicle category. Additionally, the Kia Motors Mexico plant kept sixth place in initial quality between the assembly plants in the American continent, showing that excellence in product quality is a constant for the Korean brand.
