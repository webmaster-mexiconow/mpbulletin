Green vehicle sales increase 12.33% during the January-May period

In this year's January-May period there was a total of 8,817 green vehicles sold in Mexico, which meant an increase of 12.33% when compared with the 7,849 sold units during the same period of 2019, according to numbers from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). From the total of green units sold in the accumulated from January to May of this year, hybrid vehicles are the ones with the most sales, by having a participation of 88.72% in the market. On the other hand, plug-in hybrid units obtained a market participation of 9.8%

Used vehicle imports into Mexico decrease 20%

During the year's first semester 62,753 used vehicles imported from the United States entered Mexico, which meant a decrease of 20% in contrast with the same period of last year, according to numbers from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). In the year's sixth month 8,235 used vehicles originating from the United States entered the country, a decrease of 37.5% when compared to the 13,171 units that entered the country during the same month last year.

Heavy vehicle exports keep their level, but decrease in sales continues

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), based off information published by INEGI in the Administrative Automotive Register of Heavy Vehicles and the sales number shared by its associates, made known that in July, being the first month on which Mexico exports with the new USMCA rules, there were a total of 10,019 exported units, which meant an increase of 0.4% in relation to the 9,983 units exported during the previous month. During the year's first seven months, wholesale sales added a total of 11,874 vehicles, 56.3% less than the 27,170 sold units during 2019.

Clients look to independent shops once their guarantee expires: J.D. Power

The combination of the expiration of their guarantee and the continuous increase in service costs is pushing clients away from service centers in Authorized Distributors and pushing them toward independent shops, according to J.D. Power's Customer Service Index- Long Term 2020. The study shows that, general satisfaction in owners of a vehicle that's 3 to 12 years old is 40 points higher (in a scale of 1000 points) in Authorized Distributors than in independent shops and that more than half (59%) of said vehicles are serviced in independent shops.

Toyota Mexico adjusts its sales forecast to 72,500 units

Toyota Motor will not reach its goal of selling more than 100,000 units during 2020 in our country due to lower vehicle demand and the impact caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, which is why the commercialization was adjusted downwards by 31.4% in respect to 2019. The Japanese automaker expects the sale of 72,500 vehicles compared to the 105,663 ones it sold in the Mexican market during 2019. During the first seven months of 2020, the automotive company commercialized 43,549 units, from which 49% have been sold through the internet, while the remaining 51% have been commercialized physically.

Electric vehicles will be free from tariffs: SE

The Ministry of Economy (SE) will authorize the exemption from tariffs to new imported electric cars and trucks, a measure that has already been agreed on by the federal government. The change will happen through a modification in the Law of General Taxation regarding Imports and Exports, in which the import of new electric vehicles for the transport of people (light and heavy) and new electric vehicles for the transport of merchandise will be exempted.
