KIA Motors Mexico sold 8,211 units in October

The South Korean brand managed to sell 8,211 units in October, thus placing itself in fourth place on a national level, with a market participation of 7.7%, which demonstrates the trust that the Mexican consumer has on its products. The total sales of KIA this year mean an improvement of 1.6% respect to the same 10 months of last year and are partly due to the proudly produced in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon, KIA Rio, which sold 3,024 units.
AMDA expects new car sales to keep dropping in 2020

The automotive industry expects the drop in new car sales in Mexico will continue in 2020, with a tendency to a deeper fall and one that could place itself in 1 million 270,000 units, around 30,000 less units with respect to those expected to be sold at the end of this year. Guillermo Rosales, deputy director of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) affirmed: "the decrease in sales will continue as a result of the growing uncertainty around economics and insecurity, as well as the loss of consumer trust."
Acquiring a chocolate car can have criminal and economic consequences

As part of the actions carried out by the Government of Mexico to verify the correct compliance of fiscal obligations and foreign trade, the Tax Administration Service (SAT) carried out an operation in 3 car lots in Tijuana where illegal vehicles are allegedly sold, resulting in the seizure of of 15 cars and 7 trucks. It should be mentioned that a person that acquires a vehicle that entered the country illegally might be subject to consequences that go from the administrative to criminal, which is why citizens are advised against the purchase of such vehicles.
JAC sales grow 55.2% in October

At the closing of the January to October 2019 period, JAC Mexico reported a sales growth of 66.8% when compared to the same period of last year thanks to the six vehicles currently offered in Mexico: the J4 sedan, the SUV family Sei2, Sei3, Sei4 and Sei7; and the Frison T6 pickup. On the other hand, the growth for the month of October was of 55.2% in comparison with the same month of last year. The vehicles that stood out where the small SUV Sei2 and the brand's medium sized pickup, Frison T6.
FedEx Express Mexico adds 494 vehicles to its fleet

FedEx Express in Mexico announced the incorporation of 494 new vehicles that will improve the brand's domestic service. The investment is part of the company's strategy to boost its operational and service performances, as well as supporting the country's economic competitiveness. With this investment, the company will have a fleet of approximately 1,800 units in service for the closure of the year; one of the most important seasons due to the increase of commercial activity.
Chevrolet's Onix will have three versions in Mexico

There will be three versions of the Chevrolet Onix in Mexico, all of them with a turbo engine. The LS and LT will use a 1.0 liter engine, capable of generating 114 hp and 120 lb-ft, with an option of a manual transmission of 5 speeds or an automatic one of six speeds. At the top of its range, called Premier, it will be replaced by an engine of 1.2 liters of 130 hp and 141 lb-ft. with an automatic transmission of six speeds. There's an estimated price range of MXN$270,000 to MXN$340,000, to compete against Nissan Versa, Volkswagen Vitrus, KIA Rio, Mazda 2 Sedan, among others.
A perfect storm formed to affect vehicle production and exports in October

Eduardo Solis, president of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) said that a series of factors became the "perfect storm" to generate the negative production and exports numbers experienced by the country in October. "On top of the platform changes of some companies that caused them to report zero, there was also the low demand in markets such as the United States, destination in which exports decreased 18.6% in the year's tenth month," commented the industrial representative.
