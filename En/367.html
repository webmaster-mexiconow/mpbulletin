Production of the Taos in Puebla will contribute to investment in the state: Canacintra

The production of the brand's new SUV model, the Volkswagen Taos, for 2021 in the Puebla plant, will help maintain the 90,000 jobs that are generated in the auto parts sector, but will also force companies to invest to adjust production lines and comply with the supplier, considered the National Chamber of the Transformation Industry (Canacintra). The local president of the organization, Gabriel Covarrubias Lomelí, said that the investments of the auto parts companies will be necessary, which is normal every time Volkswagen decides to bring one of its models to its plant in Puebla to then export it to other markets.

Ford and Mastretta Bikes collaborate in launching electric bicycle cabs

After finishing the production of the Mastretta MXT sports car in 2014 and entering the electric bicycle business, brothers Daniel and Carlos Mastretta have entered a new niche with Ford: electric bicycle cabs. The vehicle is designed to make short trips within the neighborhoods, of up to one kilometer (such as a trip to the supermarket, school or the nearest subway station), with an average cost of 20 to 50 pesos. The price of each unit is MXN$40,000. Now, the Mastretta's company is working on financing mechanisms to facilitate the purchase of the units.

US$52 million are invested in Durango

The companies Siete Leguas Automotive and Tecopress SPA will invest US$52 million in Gómez Palacio, Durango, which will generate 900 new jobs in the municipality. "This investment allows us to offer 600 direct jobs, plus those generated indirectly thanks to the confidence of José Juan Marcos González, president of the Board of Grupo Siete Leguas, in investing US$17 million in Gómez Palacio," said municipal president Marina Vitela Rodríguez during the inauguration of the company. She also took the opportunity to inform about the startup of the new Tecopress SPA high pressure aluminum injection plant, whose US$35 million investment will generate 300 jobs in the municipality.

Jörg Willimayer will leave BMW Group San Luis Potosi Plant's presidency

Jörg Willimayer, current president and CEO of BMW Group San Luis Potosi Plant, will leave the position on October 1st to continue his career within the group in Germany, thanks to his great experience in BMW's global production network. During his stay in San Luis Potosi, Willimayer led important milestones for the company such as the start of production of plug-in hybrid vehicles at the Mexican plant; the production and export outside the Americas region of the first BMW 3 Series vehicles to countries such as Japan, Russia, Australia and Germany; the production of the 50,000th unit.

Honda of Mexico recalls Honda and Acura models

Honda de Mexico, through the Federal Consumer Protection Agency (Profeco), announced a recall for its HR-V, BR-V, City, Insight and Accord models, as well as for the Acura NSX, ILX and RDX due to the possibility that the pump impeller may expand under certain temperature and usage conditions. According to the Japanese firm, this situation could cause the flow of fuel to decrease, resulting in the engine not starting or stopping unexpectedly. In total there are 15,291 units involved.
