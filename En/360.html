Queretaro's automotive industry operates at 70%

After the halt that the automotive industry had due to the health contingency caused by COVID-19, automotive companies in the state maintain an average of 70% of their production capacity. The recuperation will depend on the course set by the industry's global market, estimating that at the close of 2020 productive capacity could reach 80%, said the president of Queretaro's Automotive Cluster, Renato Villaseñor Mendoza. The recovery will be gradual, with the expectation that 2023 will be the year in which pre-pandemic levels of production will be reached again.

FCA México reported the sale of 3,317 units in August

For a fourteenth consecutive month RAM continues to be FCA's number one brand in terms of sales. FCA Mexico reported in August the sale of 3,317 units. RAM sold 1,300 units; RAM 700 sold 520 units and RAM Light Duty sold 343 units. Dodge reported in August the sale of 1,033 units. On the other hand, Jeep sold 651 units. CUPRA creates app so that clients may complete the purchasing process from their phones

The coronavirus pandemic made vehicle distributors accelerate their digital sales channels. For example, CUPRA's distributor in the Roma colony in Mexico City has designed an app that allows clients to tour the sales floor, board the vehicle, configure it and pay for it through their phone, without the intervention of a salesperson. "It's possible to complete the purchasing process in just one day," explained Leyre Aizpurua, Business Manager of CUPRA Garage and who was in charge of the project. The app's development was conducted by Grupo Autofin.

New vehicle sales continue recovering during August

After reaching historically low levels in April due to the confinement, during August automotive companies in the country sold 77,092 units, according to AMIA's report and disseminated by INEGI. Although the advances in car sales are modest, with this result there's now four consecutive months of recovery, following a trend that points to bigger recovery. In comparison with the new vehicles sold last month (72,897) the August sales levels implied a growth of 5.8%.

The United States, Mexico and Brazil reach agreement on steel exported to the US

The commerce authorities of the three countries reached an agreement that allows to avoid new tariffs on Brazilian and Mexican steel exported to the United States. Robert Lighthizer, head of the Office of the United States Trade Representative mentioned the agreement reached with minister of Economy, Graciela Marquez, as a constructive commitment. The new agreement allows Mexico to protect its steel industry by closely monitoring exports, and for Brazil there's a reduced export tariff.
