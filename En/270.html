INA hopes to create offices abroad to attract investment

The National Auto Parts Industry (INA) is looking to, hand in hand with federal authorities, fulfill in different countries the tasks previously done by ProMexico in terms of attracting investments, revealed their executive president, Oscar Albin Santos. Albin Santos detailed that they have carried out meetings with the Federal Government to propose the creation of these offices, in countries with a specific stake in the sector, like Japan, South Korea, Germany, China and the United States.
Audi e-tron arrives to Mexico

Audi presented its first 100% electric vehicle for our country in an event in Mexico City. The Audi e-tron will be available for purchase as of the first week of October and it will have a price of MXN$1,919,000 and will be offered in a single version. Within the Audi e-tron's defining characteristics we can highlight the 400 kilometer range and power equivalent to 408 horse power.
First CUPRA distributor will be found in Mexico City

The Spanish brand is putting its finishing touches on its premiere in our country, which will happen by the end of the year, coupled with the big news that Mexico will be the first country in the world to have an exclusive CUPRA distributor. This new distributor, so called flagship, will be found in Mexico City, and will be exclusive for CUPRA, although internally it will be linked to SEAT. Some SEAT dealerships will also have a space dedicated to CUPRA.
Auto parts sector in Nuevo Leon contributes with 32% of the manufacturing total in the state

The local auto parts sector contributes with 32% of the manufacturing total in the state of Nuevo Leon, a 5% growth when compared with two years ago, when it represented 27%, according to Americo Garcia, deputy minister of Industry, Trade and Services of the state's Ministry of Economy and Labor. "It's very important in terms of exports too; it represents almost 43% of Nuevo Leon's total exports. It's very important to keep promoting it," mentioned Garcia.
Toyota and GMC are among the best in dealership service

Dealer service is increasingly relevant to keep customer loyalty and according to a study from consulting firm JD Power, the brand in the sales segment with best service is Toyota, and GMC is the best in the luxury segment. In the evaluation about Customer Service Index (CSI) there were a total of 21 evaluated brands through 6,904 interviews with clients that have vehicles that are between one to three years old.
