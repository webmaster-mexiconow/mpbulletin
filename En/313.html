The BAIC X30 arrives in Mexico

The BAIC X30 is already available in Mexico. This is the sixth SUV commercialized by the Chinese brand in our country, and will compete with Renault's Duster and JAC's Sei 3. It has an atmospheric 1.5 liter engine, capable of generating 114 hp and 100 lb-ft, and it's available with a manual five speed transmission or an automatic four speed one. As for security, it has two airbags in the front. Both versions (manual and automatic) have ABS, EBD and electronic stability control. Its prices in Mexico range from MXN$313,900 for the manual version to MXN$329,900 for the automatic one.

Mexican auto parts exports face a complicated landscape

Mexican auto parts exports will be affected by the closing of assembly plants in the United States. In 2020, auto parts exports will be influenced by the effects of the coronavirus and probably in a positive way, by the bigger reginal content stipulated in the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA), if the latter goes into force in June, as programed by the American government.

Porsche has a new general director for Mexico

Porsche of Mexico officially presented its new general director, Camilo San Martin González, who after more than 20 years of experience working for Volkswagen Group, is joining the operations from the Stuttgart brand in our country. San Martin is replacing Luis Carlos Henry Arroyo, who served as general director starting in 2018. Porsche Mexico's new general director seeks to implement new processes, bring innovative ideas and hopes to increase client fidelity and passion for the brand.

Heavy vehicle production and exports fall in January

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), informed that, during January, the production of heavy vehicles reached a total of 13,314 units, which represents a fall of 18.5%, when compared to the same month of last year, At the same time, in January, there were a total of 11,661 exported units, which meant a decrease of 16.3% in relation to the same month of 2019. The fall in both production and exports responds to a decrease in demand from the United States, our main export destination.

10 automotive companies stop operations in the country

So far, 10 of the 12 companies that produce vehicles in Mexico will partially close their plants due to the impact of COVID-19, which dragged their auto parts suppliers to facility closings, given the low demand for units worldwide and the closing of borders. General Motors, Nissan and Volkswagen, as well as BMW announced that they would join the partial closure of their facilities in Mexico. Starting this Monday, automakers such as Toyota, Honda, Audi and General Motors will suspend their work, while Ford and FCA have already implemented this measure since last week.

COVID-19 is the third strike to move suppliers to North America

For Oscar Albin, president of the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA), the shortage of components in automotive plants, after the collapse of the global supply chains caused by the coronavirus COVID-19, is the third strike to reduce dependency on China as a supplier of electronic components. In 2019, Mexico purchased US$8,000 in Chinese auto parts, which represented a 14.5% of total imports. This is the scope of opportunity. However, Albin considers, that although some investments have arrived to Mexico because of the pandemic, most of the new projects will disembark in the United States.

AMDA asks for emergency measures before the standstill of productive activities

Through a letter sent to the chief executive, the National Automotive Dealer Association (AMDA), warned that companies in general will have a cash flow problem to face their commitments like tax payments, payroll, credits and suppliers, which is why it's necessary to implement economic emergency measures to reduce the loss of jobs. They're asking for the extension in the provisional tax payment, immediate deduction to investment, as well as differing in six partialities the payment in the ISR's definitive statement in the fiscal year 2019 to legal persons.
