The crisis could help Mexico attract auto parts investments

The export of auto parts from Mexico to the United States, exceeds in 1.3 times the value of the shipments abroad of light cars and in 5 times the entries for the export of trucks and tractors, according to data from the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA). Now, with the COVID-19 pandemic and the USMCA's entry into force, the opportunities for greater investment attraction to manufacture auto parts in Mexico increase, because owners are looking to reduce costs and the country has an opportunity.

Automotive industry proposes fiscal modifications to encourage vehicle sales

The automotive industry proposed a series of fiscal modifications that seek to encourage the sale of vehicles and reactivate the Mexican economy, in which 100% deductibility is proposed for green cars and a 50% increase in deductibility for combustion units. The automotive sector proposed to the legislators that Article 34, Section Four of the Income Tax Law (ISR) be amended so that hybrid and electric vehicles, as well as vehicles with friendly technologies, may be deducted up to 100% in one year.

Nissan innovates with the Around View Monitor

The Around View Monitor is inspired by the "eagle eye", this technology allows the driver to park or back up more easily and safely. The Around View Monitor is a system that consists of four exterior cameras located, one in the front, two on the sides and one in the rear, which allow for 360-degree peripheral vision. In Mexico, in addition to the X-Trail, the recently introduced Nissan Sentra, Nissan Versa and Nissan Altima have this technology.

General Motors celebrates its 85th anniversary in Mexico

General Motors celebrates eight and a half decades of operations in Mexico. It has become the main producer and exporter of the sector in the country. Since 1937, when it opened its first manufacturing plant, GM has produced more than 15 million vehicles and almost 30 million engines at its four manufacturing complexes. "We will continue to grow with Mexico, with its industry, its economy and its people; these are only the first 85 years," said Francisco Garza, President and CEO of General Motors of Mexico, Central America and the Caribbean.
