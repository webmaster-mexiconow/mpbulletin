ArcelorMittal to provide material for the Mexican automotive industry

After having generously invested in Mexico, the steelmaker ArcelorMittal plans to increase its productive capacity by 32.5% by next year, so as to replace imports and serve the automotive industry, electrical appliances and construction, explained its general director, Victor Cairo. The steelmaker will go from producing 4 million tons to 5.3 million hot roll (rolled steel) and rods for the automotive industry by May 2020.
Kenworth Mexicana, ready to comply with the NOM-044-2017

Luis Fernando Reyes, commercial director of Kenworth Mexicana, a subsidiary of PACCAR Incorporated, announced that the company is prepared to comply with the new standards that, as of July 2019, will come into effect through the NOM-044-2017 of the Secretariat of the Environment and Natural Resources (SEMARNAT). "Our sales force and service technicians are certified in the new technologies and products associated with the changes in emissions," said Fernando Reyes.
The new rules of origin of the T-MEC will be a challenge for the industry: AMIA

The executive president of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA), Eduardo Solis, said that the new rule of origin proposed by the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) is complex and difficult for the industry. "The new rule of origin of the USMCA means a very important challenge for some brands in our country. The periods of transition may not be enough to adapt to the new provisions," said Solis.
Ikano invests US$170 million in Coahuila

The coordination achieved by the governor of Coahuila, Miguel Angel Riquelme Solis, between the state and municipalities, and the disposition of the mayors of Ramos Arizpe and Saltillo, managed to attract an investment of US$170 million, which will generate 2,000 jobs. The project will be developed throughout the next three to five years for Ikano Industry to operate its plant at a 100%. "Starting a factory in a fast growing country like Mexico, and becoming part of the dynamic environment in Ramos Arizpe, is a fantastic opportunity for us," the company mentioned.
Nissan Mexicana and BMW Group Mexico reach the 500 installed chargers milestone

Nissan Mexicana and BMW Group Mexico celebrated the installation of 500 charging stations of their joint network in Mexico. Since the beginning of their alliance in 2015, the companies have made an investment of MXN$100 million, which represents a great achievement for the pioneering brands of electric mobility in Mexican territory. The alliance between Nissan Mexicana and BMW Group Mexico was signed with the aim of promoting the development of charging infrastructure for electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids.
Grupo Hyunbo invests US$8 million in new plant located in Queretaro

The governor of Queretaro, Francisco Dominguez Servien, inaugurated Hyunbo Group's new plant, which belongs to a Korean company located in the municipality of El Marques. In that sense, said plant contributes to the automotive development of the entity. "A company with more than five years in our country that decides to open its new plant in our state with an investment of US$8 million and 200 new jobs of high specialization; its arrival adds power to a key sector of our economy, the auto parts industry," he said.
Daimler's Saltillo plant celebrates 10 years of operations

Jeff Allen, vice president of operations of Daimler Trucks North America, said that Coahuila has unparalleled conditions for companies that want to invest and grow in Mexico. In the framework of the celebration of the Tenth Anniversary of Daimler's Saltillo Plant, Allen recalled that, when the company decided on Coahuila as the best destination for their investment, they trusted the decision, but now, 10 years later, they're able to confirm that Coahuila has incomparable conditions for companies that want to invest and grow in Mexico.
Giant Motors Latin America doubles up its production in Hidalgo

The Mexican automaker, Giant Motors Latin America, announced that it will double up the production of cars of the Chinese brand JAC in Mexico at its plant located in Ciudad Sahagun, Hidalgo. In a statement, Elias Massri, president and CEO of the company, said that this was all due to the acceptance of the models that are being currently distributed, and in preparation for the arrival of its two new models that will launch during the last quarter of 2019: the SUV JAC SEI 4 and the pickup Frinson T8.
Henkel opens its Vehicle Repair Training and Applications Center in Mexico City

A total of 1,500 powertrain and bodywork training courses for employees of workshops, service areas, mechanics, and repairers, are scheduled to be taught at the Vehicle Repair Training and Applications Center (VRM TAC) that Henkel Mexicana put into operation in the capital of the country. Javier Marron, commercial director for Henkel's Industrial Division in Mexico and Latin America, pointed out that the operation of this center will contribute to maintenance technicians incorporating updated technologies that contribute to raising the quality of their work.
Volkswagen Mexico is recognized as one of the Best Places to Work

Volkswagen Mexico was included, for the third time, in the list of Best Places to Work in Mexico made by the Great Place to Work Institute (GPTW). The German carmaker occupied the 12th position in the select group of 25 GPTW-certified firms operating in the country, with a workforce of more than 5,000 employees, standing out as the only one dedicated to the production of light vehicles. "Volkswagen is a brand recognized for the quality of its vehicles, but behind every car we deliver to our customers there is an excellent team of men and women," said Jesus Sobero, director of Volkswagen in Mexico's personnel.
