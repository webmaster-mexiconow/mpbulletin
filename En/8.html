Bulletin #8
12 December, 2016
U.S. auto sales on track for a new record

Low gasoline prices, Black Friday deals and postelection confidence helped pull November U.S. auto sales out of their recent slump and increased the chances that 2016 could set a record. Total U.S. sales rose 4 percent for the month to 1.38 million units. That makes it the highest November on record, beating the previous record of 1.32 million set in 2001, according to Kelley Blue Book.
Ford teams up with BlackBerry to develop automated vehicles

BlackBerry signed a deal to work directly with Ford Motor Co to expand the carmaker's use of its QNX secure operating system, as the automaker looks to develop automated vehicles. The deal with Ford is the first BlackBerry has done directly with a major automaker, though it currently sells its technology to auto industry suppliers.
AHMSA invests US$ 444 million in equipment to supply auto industry

Altos Hornos de Mexico (AHMSA) signed an agreement with UK-based Primetals Technologies to acquire US$ 444 million in equipment, the Mexican steelmaker announced in a press release. According to AHMSA, the new equipment will help increase production while meeting the specifications automotive industry demands.
Saab will return to auto industry, but not in the traditional sense

Sweden-based Saab Group, which abandoned its automotive enterprises on 2012 to focus on its aerospace businesses will return as a supplier, the company announced in a press release. Saab revealed that radar systems, computers and software featured on its aircraft are being studied hoping they can be packaged and sold off to automakers that do not have the capital to develop their own.
Jeep plans off-road Trailhawk variant of retooled Compass

The redesigned 2018 Jeep Compass, due in U.S. dealerships next year, will offer a new off-road ready trim level but largely keep its styling cues cribbed from the larger Jeep Grand Cherokee. The unit, which is to be built for North America in Toluca, Mexico, will be powered by a 2.4-liter I-4 engine mated to either a nine- or six-speed automatic transmission, depending on its configuration. A six-speed manual will also be available.
Mecaplast acquires Key Plastics; deal includes two plants in Chihuahua

European auto supplier Mecaplast Group has agreed to acquire Michigan-based Key Plastics LLC in a bid to expand its global presence. Key Plastics operates 12 manufacturing facilities worldwide, including two plants in Chihuahua city. The company produces automotive interior, exterior and under-the-hood plastic components with annual sales of about US$ 415 million, which $295 million are made in the NAFTA region. It has about 3,500 employees worldwide. Financial terms of the agreement were not disclosed.
Toyota reveals new C-HR subcompact crossover

Working to rebalance its U.S. portfolio and production to get a 50-50 car-truck sales, Toyota revealed the aggressively styled C-HR at the Los Angeles Auto Show. The subcompact crossover fills a big hole in Toyota's lineup, and executives say it's likely to make a big splash beginning in the spring.
Ford recalls 650,000 Fusions, Lincoln MKZs to fix seat belts

Ford Motor Co. recalled approximately 650,000 vehicles in North America with faulty seat belt anchor pretensioners. The automaker said the affected part in 2013-16 Ford Fusions and 2013-15 Lincoln MKZs could separate during a crash and may not properly restrain a driver or passenger. The units were built at the Flat Rock, Michigan, and Hermosillo, Sonora plants.
Joint venture headed by Kobe Steel opens wire plant in Guanajuato

A joint venture of Shinsho Corporation's Kobe Steel Ltd. (Kobelco) and four other companies held an opening ceremony onsite to mark the start of production of automotive steel wire in Mexico. The supplier's plant situated is in the Santa Fe Industrial Park in Silao, Guanajuato, Mexico. It processes steel wire rod into cold wire used to make automotive fasteners and cold-forging products shipped to auto parts makers in Mexico.
Bosch sells its emission test equipment business

Sweden-based Opus Inspection has signed an agreement to acquire the emission test equipment business from Bosch Automotive Service Solutions, Inc. In 2015, the business generated over US$ 5 million in revenues. Operating profit margins over the past three years varied between 10 – 15 percent. The purchase price is approximately US$ 1.5 million.
