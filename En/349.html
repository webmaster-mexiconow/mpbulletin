Chevrolet revealed the new Tracker's engine

Chevrolet revealed that the new Tracker will arrive to Mexico with an efficient turbo engine, to offer its clients a compact SUV that stands out for its dynamic driving with great performance and fuel efficiency both in the city and on the highway. Chevrolet Tracker will be equipped with an efficient 1.2 liter turbo engine, which generates a potency of 130 horsepower and 140 lb-ft of torque. The engine is coupled to a transmission that can be either a five speed manual one or a six speed automatic one.

Mercedes-Benz Buses doubles its efforts to keep Mexico moving

Mercedes-Benz Buses understands how important it is to have availability of parts, lubricants and special parts which is why it has the backup of the International Daimler Parts Distribution Center (CIDPD), in San Luis Potosi. "The CIDPD team has had to double their efforts during this pandemic, since buses have to be on top condition always, which is why even in hard times the team was reorganized, taking care of health and safety measures so that our clients, operators and carriers, could have access to spare parts every day, 24/7," explained Raul Gonzalez, director of Sales, Marketing and After Sales of Mercedes-Benz Buses.

Light vehicle production in Mexico would place at 2.9 million units in 2020

Even with the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA), it will take Mexico six years to recover the light vehicle manufacturing of 3.8 million units reached in 2019, lost due to the effects of the coronavirus pandemic, coincided automotive sector specialists. After the crisis caused by the COVID-19 pandemic and low demand already presented by the automotive industry, at the close of 2020 it's expected for light vehicle production in Mexico to reach 2.9 million units, a decrease of 24% when compared to 2019, estimated Guido Vildozo, light vehicle sales director of IHS Markit.

Volkswagen of Mexico asks collaborators to find alternative solutions to salary increase

Volkswagen of Mexico called its thousands of workers in the state of Puebla to consider alternate solutions to the salary increase demands which it faces with the goal of maintaining stability, when it anticipates a greater than 24% annual fall in domestic sales due to the crisis caused by the coronavirus. The weakened automotive company is facing a collective contract negotiation in which workers are asking for a 12% increase in salaries, which if not successful, would trigger a strike starting on August 18, said the union to local media.

The automotive industry's post pandemic recovery is extremely important: AMIA

Through a virtual panel organized by the Mexican Institute of Executives in Foreign Trade (IMECE), Fausto Cuevas, general director of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA), said that the industry generates about 3.8% of the national GDP, which is why the post pandemic recovery is very important. AMIA's general director also said that in terms of production and exports, during the year's first semester they saw falls of 40.8 and 41.7% respectively, "the recovery will take us a couple of years," he highlighted.

Worldwide automotive production will see a decrease of 3.5 million units: IHS Markit

IHS Markit estimates that after the closure of automotive plants in recent months to avoid the spread of COVID-19, at the close of the year there would be a total of 71 million produced vehicles, a decrease of 3.5 million units, mainly affecting the Chinese, United States and European markets. Guido Vildozo, light vehicles sales director of IHS Markit, said in a virtual conference that the recovery of vehicle production will reach 2019 levels of production by the end of 2023 or that it might even take more time.

Daimler Trucks Mexico bets on connectivity

With the incorporation of the telematics tool Enlace Freightliner 2.0, Daimler Trucks Mexico ratifies itself as a company that provides solutions that contribute to transport companies elevating their business' profitability. This tool is unique in its kind and integrates telematics, tracking and mechanisms that allow to carry out diagnosis on the vehicle's powertrain for better control. Abimael Aranda Portillo, director of market development and product portfolio of Daimler Trucks Mexico, informed that Enlace Freighliner 2.0 is installed as original equipment in every New Cascadia with 36 moths of service and it's also offered for M2 and 360 models with geolocation.
