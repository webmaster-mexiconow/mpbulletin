Vehicle production and exports recover slightly during June

Vehicle production and exports in Mexico fell again during June, although they did moderate their fall when compared to the previous two months. During the year's sixth month, the assembly plants in the country manufactured 238,946 units, a 29.25% less than in June of 2019, while exports decreased 38.79% to 196,173 vehicles, according to data published by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). In the previous two months, both exports and production suffered annual falls of more than 90%.

Queretaro's automotive industry is working to meet the new content value rules

After stopping production for over two months, Queretaro's automotive industry sees an opportunity for growth in the entry into force of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA). Renato Villaseñor Mendoza, president of Queretaro's Automotive Cluster, said that the local industry is working in generating strategies to meet the new regional content rules, which would help offset the stop experienced by the sector because of COVID-19.

Mexican vehicles have a 12.83% share of the American market

During the first semester of 2020 in the United States there were a total of 6,429,034 sold light vehicles, which meant a fall of 23.5% in contrast to the same period of 2019 when 8,401,189 sold vehicles were reported, according to numbers from Ward's Automotive Reports. In the US the commercialization of vehicles coming from Mexico had a market participation of 13.83% with 825,221 sold units during the first six months of 2020, however, this meant a fall of 39.8% when compared to the same period of last year when 1,370,759 Mexican vehicles were sold.

Used vehicle imports and automotive financing contract

By the fifth month of 2020, 237,219 zero kilometer units were sold through some of the main three credit options offered in Mexico, 83,598 less when compared to May of last year, which meant a contraction of 26.1%, as explained by Guillermo Rosales Zárate, deputy director general of AMDA. As for imported used vehicles to Mexico, he highlighted that between January and May of this year there's been 54,990 interned units, 10,319 less, which means a decrease of 15.8%. "Despite the decrease, this number meant 14.7% of the total number of vehicles sold in the reference timeframe," he said.

Kia keeps its fifth place in the Mexican market

Despite the fact that the health contingency hasn't passed, Kia visualizes a more stable environment as far as sales go, since at the close of June, it projected positive results with 4,550 sold units in Mexico, thus reaffirming its fifth place position in the market with an accumulated market participation of 7.3%. Of the 4,450 sold units last month, 16% derived from the brand's official micro site: apartatukia.com, which began operating on April and where customers can set aside a vehicle for themselves with only MXN$2,000.
