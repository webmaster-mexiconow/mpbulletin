Ford relies on Mexico to deliver first pursuit-rated hybrid police car

Ford Motor Co., the leading police vehicles seller in the United States, has launched the industry's first pursuit-rated hybrid police car. The hybrid sedans will be built in Hermosillo, Sonora, and modified at the Chicago Assembly Plant. The new vehicle will be the first hybrid sedan certified by police agencies featuring full pursuit capabilities. Police Responder Hybrid Sedan customers could see nearly US$ 3,900 a year in potential fuel savings per vehicle, said the company in a press release.
Eaton, Cummins join forces to build transmissions in San Luis Potosi

Eaton and Cummins Inc. announced an agreement to form a joint venture aimed to develop and manufacture automated transmissions for heavy-duty and medium-duty commercial vehicles. The joint venture, named Eaton Cummins Automated Transmission Technologies, will begin in the third quarter and the transmissions will be made in San Luis Potosi, Mexico, where both companies operate production facilities. Under the terms of the agreement, Eaton will receive US$ 600 million in cash from Cummins for 50% stake in the joint venture.
GM to idle multiple plants to retool for upcoming models

General Motors plans to idle multiple North American plants for a total of 10 weeks later this year to retool for upcoming models, including redesigned pickups. The downtime is in addition to GM's annual summer plant shutdown period in early July. Chevy dealers began selling the third-generation Equinox in March, and GM is preparing to launch new versions of the Chevy Traverse, GMC Terrain and Buick Enclave by fall. GM builds pickups in Fort Wayne, Indiana and Silao, Guanajuato for the NAFTA market.
Suzuki Garphyttan inaugurates US$ 12.7 million plant in Guanajuato

Swedish-Japanese firm Suzuki Garphyttan opened in recent days its first Mexican production facility to supply oil tempered wire for several automotive applications. The plant is located at the Opcion Industrial Park in the municipality of San Jose Iturbide, Guanajuato. The company invested US$ 12.7 million and recruited 47 workers for the first phase in this operation. The 13,000 square-meters facility will be able to manufacture 6,000 metric tons of wire annually in the first stage and is expected to double its capacity by 2020, after an additional US$ 5 million investment.
Volkswagen deliveries rise in March; extends warranty for Tiguan and Atlas SUVs

The Volkswagen Passenger Cars brand delivered 557,400 vehicles worldwide in March, an increase of 2.5% compared to same month of the previous year. Deliveries in the U.S. ran at 27,600 vehicles, 2.7% higher than the previous year. In order to boost sales, VW just announced the 2018 Tiguan and Atlas SUVs in the U.S. market will come with a transferrable, six-year/72,000-mile bumper-to-bumper warranty. The rest of the industry averages three-year/36,000-mile bumper-to-bumper warranties.
France-based Lacroix acquires stake in Firstronic to supply customers in Mexico

Lacroix Electronics, a France-based electronic manufacturing services (EMS) provider, announced it acquired a stake in Firstronic LLC, based in Michigan. The alliance will provide support for customers of Lacroix in North America, in particular for automotive equipment vendors who have factories in the United States and Mexico. "The acquisition will provide the flexibility and responsiveness of a regional player with factories in Grand Rapids, Michigan and Juarez, Mexico," said the company in a press release.
Delphi subsidiary Hellerman Tyton starts operations in Nuevo Leon

UK-based Delphi subsidiary Hellermann Tyton started operations in recent days at a production facility after a US$ 10 million investment. The manufacturer of cable management solutions built a plant which will be dedicated to plastics injection to produce harnesses for car engine cables. Once the plant is working at 100% of its installed capacity, it will uphold a maximum of 700 direct jobs. The plant will be located into the Vynmsa Industrial Park, in the municipality of Escobedo, with a production area of 10,000 square meters, with expectations to grow to 20,000 in 2018.
Mazda sales increase 4.9% in US market during March

Mazda North American Operations reported March U.S. sales of 24,549 vehicles, representing an increase of 4.9% versus March of last year. With an equal amount of selling days in 2017 as in 2016, first quarter sales are up 6.8% versus last year, with 69,071 vehicles sold. Sales for the Mazda 3 sedan and hatchback, which is built in Salamanca, Guanajuato, totaled 7,137 units in March and 20,606 over the first quarter, 2.3% and 6.9% decreases respectively. Mazda Motor de Mexico reported March sales of 4,061 vehicles, a flat result versus March of last year.
Nissan, Cuernavaca plant union agree 4.5% wage raise

Autoworkers for the Nissan auto plant in Cuernavaca, Morelos, in Mexico walked off the job on April 4 to press for wage increases, according to local media. The workers' union, Sitnissan, called for a 5.5% raise, while the company offered 3% with a bonus of 1,750 pesos (US$ 93.76), which it later raised to 3,000 pesos (US$ 160.73). The next day, Nissan raised its offer to 4%. After an assembly vote of the nearly 3,800 workers the next day, Sitnissan countered with 4.5%, which management accepted. Workers returned to work Monday, April 10.
"Customers would revolt," auto dealers warn against border tax

Auto dealers reunited in a forum at the New York International Auto Show warned against President Donald Trump border tax proposal, saying it would hike vehicle prices to the detriment of U.S. consumers. The tax could particularly affect vehicles made in Mexico and shipped to the U.S. "If the net result (of a border tax) increases prices by as much as US$ 1,700, which is one figure I've seen, it would create an economic calamity like we've never seen before. And customers would revolt," NADA President Peter Welch said.
