Audi to build electric cars at all plants, including Mexico

Pressured by labor unions to allocate electric car projects to Germany, Audi revealed it will build electric vehicles at all its plants across the world, including Mexico, news outlet Reuters reported. "In future, electric cars will roll off the line in all of our plants," CEO Rupert Stadler told a gathering of 7,000 workers at Audi's base in Ingolstadt. The company previously revealed plans to include more than 20 electrified vehicles in its catalog before 2025, with about a dozen designed to be battery-only. The automaker, one of Volkswagen's most profitable brands, last year awarded production of its first mass-produced electric sport-utility vehicle to a plant in Brussels.
Automotive exports from Nuevo Leon to reach more than US$ 12 billion in 2017

Automotive exports from Nuevo Leon state are expected to reach more than US$ 12 billion in 2017, with the United States as the main destination, said Manuel Montoya, director of the state Automotive Cluster, who added the figure would mean a new record high, boosted in great part by the arrival of the new Kia assembly plant. There's currently around 200 automotive companies with operations in Nuevo Leon, whose exports totaled US$ 11.5 billion during 2016, Montoya added. "We have had a relatively good year, industry exports this year have grown 19% because of Kia, but even if we remove Kia from the calculation, exports have grown by 6% or 7%," he said.
Detroit Big Three to lose leadership of North American auto production in 2017: HIS

The Detroit big three automakers will make less than half of vehicles produced in North America this year for the first time ever, according to Joe Langley, an analyst with IHS Markit. General Motors Co., Ford Motor Co. and Fiat Chrysler Automobiles NV will make a combined 8.6 million vehicles in the region this year, Langley said at a briefing in Detroit. That compares with 8.7 million to be produced by Tesla Inc. and manufacturers based outside the U.S. The gap will widen further in the years ahead, with the Detroit Three seen making 8.1 million units in North America in 2024, compared with 9.8 million by other carmakers, IHS Markit said.
Eaton Cummins joint venture unveils its first automated transmission

Eaton Cummins Automated Transmission Technologies has unveiled the first automated heavy-duty transmission to be introduced as part of the newly formed joint venture between Eaton and Cummins. The new Endurant 12-speed automated transmission is the lightest, most efficient 1,850 lb.-ft. capable heavy-duty transmission, the companies said in a joint statement. Designed for linehaul applications where weight savings and efficiency can add to a fleet's bottom line, Endurant weighs up to 105 pounds less than competitive automated manual transmissions. The transmissions developed by the joint venture are manufactured in San Luis Potosi.
Officials claim progress as they wrap up third round of NAFTA talks

Mexico, the United States and Canada wrapped up the third round of talks to renegotiate NAFTA this Wednesday in Ottawa with some issues resolved, but too much work remains to be done in order to get a preliminary deal by the end of the year, as planned. Mexico's Secretary of Economy, Ildefonso Guajardo, said there would be "substantial challenges" in the next round. One of the issues experts believe to be the thorniest is the rules of origin. U.S. Trade Representative Robert Lighthizer said the United States would "hopefully" present draft text on the matter during the next round scheduled to begin in Washington on October 11.
New leadership arrives to Audi Puebla facility

After Audi successfully consolidated the production start-up in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla, Andreas Lehe will now manage, starting October 1st, the duties of Production and Logistics in the plant. Klaus Peter Körner, who holds the position until now, will return in late September to Germany as stipulated in his contract. Audi Mexico is steadily advancing its goal of producing 150,000 Audi Q5 this year. So far in 2017, 100,000 SUVs have already rolled out in San Jose Chiapa. Alfonso Dintner, president of Audi Mexico, said: "Andreas Lehe, a renowned expert with more than 17 years of experience in leadership roles, will bring valuable knowledge to Mexico."
Korean auto supplier Dongkook builds US$ 26 million plant in Nuevo Leon

Dongkook Industrial, a Korean auto parts maker, will start production at a new Mexican plant in the second half of next year to supply components to Kia Motors. The company plans to invest US$ 26.3 million to finance the construction and operations of the new facility. Earlier in April, Dongkook's auto parts affiliate Kabul International Group kicked off construction of the factory in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon. Dongkook projects the facility to be completed by November. Following the test-run of the factory, it plans to start production in the second half of 2018, rolling out parts such as crash pad, garnish and fog and head lamps.
India-based UNO Minda Group builds US$ 15 million plant in Mexico

The UNO Minda Group, an India-based Tier 1 global automotive supplier, announced it is expanding its manufacturing capacity with a new US$ 15 million facility in Mexico for horns. The plant is under construction in Queretaro, where the company already owns a small operation in through Clarton Horns, a Spain-based manufacturer acquired in 2013, which supplies horns for the Volkswagen Atlas. The facility will be developed in two stages. During its first phase it will supply horns for Volkswagen, specifically for the Tiguan, Polo and New Jetta, while in its second phase it will meet the needs of Ford and GM in Mexico and the US. The facility will be operational by April.
Mexico-based automotive frame manufacturer Metalsa invests US$ 36.5 million in Kentucky

Mexico-based automotive frame manufacturer Metalsa Structural Products Inc. announced it will add 113 jobs at its Owensboro, Kentucky facility with a US$ 36.5 million expansion to produce a new line of stamped and welded components. The expansion will include robotic welding cells, assembly line robots, infrastructure and building expansions to increase the facility's square footage to accommodate the new production line and additional warehouse space. The expansion began this summer. Company leaders expect production to begin in March 2019. The company based in Apodaca, Nuevo Leon, currently employs in Kentucky about 2,700 people at three plants.
Delphi now plans to spin-off its electrification, automated driving division

Delphi Automotive PLC announced that Aptiv and Delphi Technologies will be the names of the two highly-focused, publicly-traded companies that will result from the company's previously announced spin-off of its Powertrain Systems segment. Aptiv, comprises the Electronics & Safety and Electrical/Electronic Architecture businesses, will focus on accelerating the commercialization of active safety, autonomous driving, enhanced user experiences and connected services. The powertrain business will carry the name Delphi Technologies, as it focuses on enabling advanced vehicle propulsion through combustion, software and controls and electrification.
