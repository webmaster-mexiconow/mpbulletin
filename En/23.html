Bulletin #23
2 February, 2017
There's no second thoughts on Mexican plant: Toyota CEO

Toyota's CEO for North America, Jim Lentz, said the company is not having second thoughts about plans to build a new assembly plant in Guanajuato for Corolla small-car production, despite President Donald Trump threats of a 20% border tax on Mexican imports. A change of plans is impossible in the near-term because of the complexity involved to rearrange the supply chain, Lentz told a small group of reporters at the National Automobile Dealers Association convention in New Orleans.
Sanhua Holding Group plans US$ 35 million expansion in Coahuila

China-based Sanhua Holding Group announced a US$ 35 million expansion plan at its facility, located in Ramos Arizpe, Coahuila, where the company manufactures refrigeration and air conditioning control components. Two new production lines will add 300 workers to the 500 already employed at the facility. Sanhua arrived to Coahuila two years ago, as part of the supply chain for Kia's factory, located in the neighbor state of Nuevo Leon.
Hirschvogel builds US$ 30 million plant in Queretaro

German firm Hirschvogel Automotive Group started the construction of its ninth plant in the world and its first in Mexico, with a US$30 million investment. The facility is located in the Municipality of San Juan del Rio, Queretaro, and it has a 115,000 square meters surface. The first stage, machining, will be ready to produce its first sample pieces in April this year, and the second, installation of forging machines, will start producing pieces in 2018.
GM, Honda join efforts to build a hydrogen fuel cell factory

General Motors and Honda have formed a joint venture to produce hydrogen fuel cell systems at a GM battery pack plant near Detroit. The venture will create 100 jobs. GM and Honda are splitting the US$ 85 million investment of the project. The joint company will have a board of directors, including three executives from each brand with a rotating chairperson.
VW overtakes Toyota as global best-selling automaker

Toyota Motor Corp. lost its title as the world's best-selling automaker to Volkswagen Group, ending the Japanese company's four-year reign, as demand for its flagship Camry sedan waned in the U.S. and sales in China expanded at a slower pace than the overall market. Toyota's global sales, including its Lexus, Daihatsu and Hino brands, rose 0.2 percent to 10.2 million vehicles in 2016. That fell short of VW Group's record 10.3 million cars, trucks and buses, a 3.8 percent gain.
Plan for US$270 million Mexican steel plant remains on track: JFE Holdings

Japanese firm JFE Holdings said its plans to build a US$ 270 million steel plant in Mexico with U.S. company Nucor remain on track, although it is closely watching U.S. policy after President Donald Trump threatened to ditch the NAFTA. JFE and Nucor announced in June last year a joint-venture to build a plant in Mexico to supply automakers in the country. The factory is expected to produce 400,000 tons of galvanized sheet steel annually after completion in 2019.
BMW chief defends free trade and Mexican facility amid Trump threats

BMW Chief Executive Officer Harald Krueger defended the importance of free trade and its Mexican plant during a conference in Germany, responding to President Donald Trump suggesting he'll push for tariffs to protect American jobs. The official pointed out that BMW's U.S. plant, located in Spartanburg, South Carolina, is the German manufacturer's largest worldwide and exports 70% of its annual production.
Mexican manufacturer Vistamex opens production site in Guanajuato

Mexican plastic injection manufacturer Servicios Vistamex started operations in recent days at the Amistad industrial park located in Apaseo El Grande, Guanajuato. The company, which currently supplies parts for automotive and appliances sectors, invested US$ 9.45 million in the new 4,000 square meters' facility and hired 280 workers. Some of Vistamex customers are Valeo, Magna, Norgren, Sanko Gosei, Winsor Mold Group, Fujikiko, Hella, ACS, Alpha, Mabe, and General Electrics.
Nissan Mexico achieves breaking-record year in production, sales

Nissan Motor announced its production and sales final figures for 2016, which included breaking record numbers in both fields for its Mexican subsidiary. During 2016 production increased 3.1% year-on-year to 848,086 units, meanwhile sales increased 15.6 percent year-on-year to 403,286 units.
Automotive Lighting to move exhaust component production to Mexico

Automotive Lighting LLC plans to invest US$ 35 million for a new production facility in Michigan in a project that it says will create 362 jobs over five years, state authorities announced. The current exhaust work done at the Michigan plant will transfer to a facility in Mexico. Automotive Lighting owns three production sites in Mexico. Two plants in Juarez, Chihuahua, and one more in Tepotzotlan, state of Mexico.
