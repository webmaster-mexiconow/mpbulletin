Next-gen VW Jetta to debut at the Detroit Auto Show in January

The next-gen 2019 Volkswagen Jetta will debut at the 2018 North American International Auto Show in Detroit in January and is expected to go on sale during the spring of next year, Hinrich Woebcken, CEO of Volkswagen North America, revealed last weekend. According to a report by Motor Trend, the Jetta will be available in the sporty R-Line version within the first three months of its launching and the GLI model will arrive later within the first year. The model will be built in Puebla, Mexico, using the flexible MQB platform, which have allowed that facility to produce models based on this architecture without the need of significant investments.
Ford completes testing for its pursuit-rated hybrid police car

Production units of Ford's pursuit-rated hybrid Police vehicle are starting to roll out of the company's Chicago Assembly Plant where they're modified for law enforcement use after being shipped from Hermosillo, Sonora, Mexico. After completing a series of tests by the Michigan State Police, the vehicle first ever Police Responder Hybrid Sedan is now available for sale to law enforcement agencies across the United States. Testing took place at the Grattan Raceway and included a 32-lap pursuit simulation that tests durability of the powertrain, suspension, brakes and tires. The vehicle is expected to get 38 miles per gallon, including 40 mpg during driving.
New Faurecia plant in Guanajuato begins trial production runs

A new Faurecia plant in San Jose Iturbide, Guanajuato, has started trial production runs aiming to begin formal operations before this year ends, Periodico am reported. A ribbon-cutting ceremony is scheduled in the first quarter of 2018. The company's 15th plant in Mexico will feature industry 4.0 components, which will make it the most modern factory of this group in the country, revealed Francisco Maciel, general director of Faurecia Mexico during a tour by the facility along with representatives of the French Embassy. This location will be focused on the manufacture of exhaust systems (clean mobility), with an initial investment valued at US$ 48 million.
Japanese manufacturer Moriroku Technology inaugurates plant in Guanajuato

Six months after it began operations, the Japanese manufacturer Moriroku Technology inaugurated its production facility located in Guanajuato Puerto Interior industrial park, where the company produces interior plastic components for the Volkswagen Tiguan. Back in December 2015, Moriroku Technology broke ground to build the plant which started operations last March. During the ceremony, company officials revealed plans to invest US$ 20 million and the recruiting of 190 workers in order to begin its first phase of production. According to the officials, an expansion is already in the works aimed to supply parts for Honda.
Spotted next-gen Ram 1500 marks the end of an era for pickup production in Mexico

Spy photographers came across a 2019 Ram 1500 completely undisguised after winds in Michigan blew the cover off the next-generation pickup truck. Production is due to commence in January. FCA confirmed in April 2016 that it would shift production out of the current locations of Warren Truck in Michigan and Saltillo in Mexico into Sterling Heights in Michigan. The Warren Truck and Saltillo plants will supposedly continue building the outgoing Ram 1500 to be sold as a cheaper model. Combined production capacity of 200,000 units is reportedly planned for 2018 before dropping to 65,000 in 2019. Production would cease by 2020 as those plants are retooled for other vehicles.
NAFTA negotiations to extend into next year

Top U.S., Mexico and Canada officials agree that talks to revise the North American Free Trade Agreement will go into next year amid disagreements over how best to change the deal. In a joint statement following the fourth round of talks, American, Canadian and Mexican officials said "significant conceptual gaps" will change the negotiating timeframe. "New proposals have created challenges and ministers discussed the significant conceptual gaps among the parties," says the joint statement released at the close of the fourth rounds of talks Tuesday. Mexico will host the fifth round of talks on November 17-21.
Toyota executive: U.S. seeks 'impossible' changes to NAFTA

The changes President Donald Trump is seeking in NAFTA negotiations will be "impossible" for Mexico and Canada to agree to because they conflict with trade agreements they have with other nations, a Toyota executive said on Wednesday. "The issues are impossible for our neighbors to accept. They have other free trade agreements they must comply with," said Leila Afas, Toyota director of international public policy in Washington, D.C. "This is a critical time to reverse what is happening," Afas said, acknowledging that many U.S. companies have built their supply chains around the 22-year-old trade agreement.
Union ratifies contract at GM's Equinox plant in Canada

Workers at General Motors' CAMI plant in Ingersoll, Ontario, voted to accept an agreement ending a four-week-long strike. The union released results showing 85.9% of its membership accepted the four-year contract, with 78.7% of skilled trade workers agreeing to the terms. The union secured 2% raises in the first and fourth years of the contract, ratification bonuses and additional bonuses paid in each December of the contract. What the deal does not include, however, is a guarantee from GM that Ingersoll jobs wouldn't be sent to the carmaker's two plants in Mexico. Those plants also produce the company's top selling Chevrolet Equinox.
Ford to recall 1.34 million trucks in North America for door latch fix

Ford said it would recall 1.34 million 2015-17 Ford F-150 and 2017 Ford Super Duty trucks in North America to add water shields to side door latches at a cost of US$ 267 million. The automaker said the safety recall is due to a frozen door latch or a bent or kinked actuation cable in the affected vehicles, that may result in a door not opening or closing. Dealers will install water shields over the door latches and inspect and repair door latch cables if needed. Ford has now recalled more than 5 million vehicles for varying door latch-related issues since 2016, but the company said the issue in the new recall is different from prior ones.
Magna joins BMW, Intel and Mobileye to bring self-driving tech into the market

Canadian auto parts manufacturer Magna International announced it has joined the BMW Group, Intel and Mobileye, an Intel company, on a mission to deliver a self-driving, flexible vehicle platform by 2021. As a Tier 1 technology integrator, Magna will be responsible of helping automakers industrialize and customize the domain controller designed by BMW Group, Intel and Mobileye. Magna established its first Mexico facility in Puebla in 1991. Currently, the company operates 30 manufacturing sites and a Research Center in Mexico. Together all facilities employ more than 29,000 workers.
