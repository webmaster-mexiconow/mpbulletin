The 2021 Chevrolet Tahoe and Suburban will be available in Mexico

Chevrolet announced that the new 2021 Tahoe and Suburban, which were made known worldwide on December of last year, will be available in the Mexican market during the third quarter of 2020. The new generation of these iconic big SUVs was completely transformed to offer greater interior capacity and load volume, as well as innovative technologies and exclusive characteristics for a unique driving experience in its segment.

Light vehicle production in Mexico would fall 23% in 2020

The health contingency caused by the COVID-19 and the economy will hit North America's automotive industry production in more than 30% in 2020, by assembling approximately 12 million units, estimated Guido Vildozo, IHS Markit's director of light vehicles in North America. The automotive sector specialist explained that the production of light vehicles in Mexico would fall 23% in 2020 when compared to 2019, to locate itself below 3 million units.

Companies from State of Mexico's Automotive Cluster contribute with ventilators

Aware of the need to collaborate on the necessary actions to counteract the negative effects the COVID-19 is causing in the Mexican population, Elisa Crespo, executive president of the Automotive Cluster of the State of Mexico, detailed the initiatives undertaken by the cluster's companies. They are contributing with ventilators designed for the intense care area, whose development is in charge of the Engineering and Industrial Development Center (CIDESI), and where the Automotive Cluster of the State of Mexico is supporting companies with parts and components at a moment in which demand is very high.

AMDA and Jalisco's Ministry of Transportation sign agreement

Jalisco's Ministry of Transportation and Jalisco's chapter of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), signed a mutual collaboration agreement. The agreement deals with procedures, promotion of available technological elements in vehicles to decrease the risk of injuries and, in general, the promotion of road safety culture. As part of the agreement, the Ministry of Transportation will support AMDA Jalisco in the management of procedures for their associates, in what refers to the sales of new and used cars while their registry procedure with the Ministry of Public Credit are completed.

The Mexican automotive industry is under pressure to restart operations

The governments of Mexico, the United States and Canada are engaged in dialogue regarding the restart of the regional manufacturing industry, with most pressure placed upon the automotive industry. In the United States the restart of 13 automotive plants is planned between the 4th and 11th of May, while Mercedes-Benz began production on April 27. The challenge is on how to maintain supply chains for industries deemed essential in the United States during the COVID-19 pandemic, without endangering Mexican workers.

Volkswagen of Mexico will see a gradual restart of operations

The return to vehicle production at Volkswagen of Mexico will be gradual and in accordance with the times determined by the government along with the administrations of the United States and Canada, said Mauricio Kuri, the brand's director of Corporate Communications and Government Affairs. Kuri stressed that the company is maintaining a dialogue with Andrés Manuel López Obrador's government, and they recognize that they are working to start up the industry. "The important thing is that we are recognized as a priority industry on which hundreds of thousands of small and medium enterprises depend," he said.

Alternative Staging Regime extends compliance with the automotive rule of origin by two years

The United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) allows to give two additional years to the compliance with the automotive rule of origin, under certain conditions and requirements, indicated the Ministry of Economy. However, this won't replace any other origin rules or any provisions in the general applications for said merchandise. The agreement lets vehicle producers know the requirements to get an extension in the compliance with the origin rules for the automotive sector within USMCA's framework. Once these requirements are met, companies may present petitions to use an Alternative Staging Regime.
