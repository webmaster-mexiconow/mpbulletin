Michigan-based auto supplier Inteva opens in Guanajuato its sixth Mexican plant

Michigan-based Inteva Products, LLC, a global tier one automotive supplier, opened a new 300,000-square-foot manufacturing site in Silao, Guanajuato. This is Inteva's sixth site in Mexico, other facilities in the country are located in Matamoros, Juarez and Puebla. The Guanajuato plant currently employs 100 men and women, with plans to hire 500 additional workers over the next months. The US$ 38.5 million facility will manufacture interior systems such as door and instruments panels, but Inteva plans to expand its capabilities to manage production of all four of its product lines which also include roof systems, closure systems, motors and electronics.
Mexican camshaft manufacturer Arbomex achieves National Prize for Exports

Mexican machined and cast products manufacturer Arbomex received in recent days the National Prize for Exports, the highest award for Mexican enterprises that successfully operate in the global market. At the ceremony, President Enrique Peña Nieto acknowledged that companies such Arbomex represent a role model. Arbomex is a leading manufacturer and exporter of high added value products such as camshafts, crankshafts, auxiliary shafts, wheel hubs, cylinder heads, flywheels and plates. Among other recognitions, the company recently achieved the Award for Excellence by Mazda Mexico, and Fiat Chrysler Automobiles' Best Supplier of the Year Award.
Japanese auto suppliers have established 1,100 operations in Mexico: Jetro

Japanese automotive companies have installed about 1,100 operations in Mexico due to the growth of this industry in the country, including sales offices, logistic warehouses and manufacturing sites, said the director general of Japan External Trade Organization (Jetro), Tadashi Minemura, during the closing of the first Automotive Industry Business Meeting of ProMéxico-Jetro. The meeting was attended by 17 buyers, including BMW, Ford, General Motors and ZF, among others, and more than 67 automotive supplier companies established in Mexico.
Korean auto supplier Kolon Glotech expands operations in Coahuila

South Korea-based Kolon Glotech, a supplier of textile and leather components for automotive interiors, opened its second facility in Saltillo, Coahuila, to produce seat covers. The company invested US$ 8 million and expects to recruit 500 workers once the plant is fully operational, according to a statement by the state government. Kolon Glotech started operations in Coahuila last year with a US$ 12 million investment to supply parts for General Motors, but the company found new business opportunities with the arrival of a Kia assembly plant to the neighbor state of Nuevo Leon.
Bendix builds laboratory to support educational opportunities in Coahuila

Ohio-based heavy-truck component manufacturer Bendix, in collaboration with the CECyTEC - a local technical vocational school - and the Mexican government, recently broke ground for an industrial production laboratory to increase educational opportunities for youth in Acuna, Coahuila. The new 2,500-square-foot state-of-the-art industrial production lab is part of a two-initiative plan. Bendix Commercial Vehicle Systems will also provide a dual education apprenticeship opportunity for select CECyTEC students, while delivering practical training at the company's Acuna campus.
RECARO showcases four new lines of performance seats at Frankfurt Auto Show

Global leader of performance car seats, RECARO Automotive Seating showcases an extended portfolio at the 67th International Auto Show (IAA) from September 12 - 24, in Frankfurt, Germany. The Adient's subsidiary is revealing the all-new modular RECARO Performance Seat Platform (RPSP) for OEM customers and a limited edition. RECARO Automotive Seating operates a manufacturing facility in Lerma, State of Mexico, that supplies performance seats to several automakers in North America. Some models featuring RECARO seats are Chevrolet Camaro, Ford Mustang, Focus ST, Fiesta ST, Fiat 500 and Nissan Sentra Nismo, among others.
DENSO receives Masters of Quality Supplier Award from Daimler Trucks

DENSO International America, one of the world's largest automotive technology, systems and components suppliers, earned a 2016 Masters of Quality Supplier Award from Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA). It is the highest distinction DTNA gives to its suppliers. DENSO operates six production facilities in Mexico. Three plants are each located in the cities of Apodaca, Guadalupe and Salinas Victoria, Nuevo Leon; two plants are each in Silao and Irapuato, Guanajuato, and one in Frontera, Coahuila. Daimler Trucks has two manufacturing plants located in Santiago Tianguistenco, State of Mexico, and Saltillo, Coahuila.
Honeywell, Lear team up to develop software for autonomous cars

Honeywell and Lear Corporation announced the two companies are collaborating to provide automotive software technology and infrastructure solutions to address threats associated with emerging connected and autonomous vehicle development. The companies are focused on pairing Honeywell's intrusion detection technology software and security operations centers with Lear's automotive electrical distribution systems and connected gateway expertise. They have developed an effective system to identify and validate software commands and data generated by more than 100 million lines of code governing modern vehicle operation.
Houston area residents rush to auto dealers to replace around 1 million damaged vehicles

Hurricane Harvey may have done more vehicle damage than any storm in U.S. history, destroying as many as 500,000 autos, according to Cox Automotive. Car-market researcher Black Book estimates the toll was even greater, saying up to 1 million might have to be replaced. While Harvey dragged on auto sales in August, the stocks of carmakers including General Motors Co. and Ford Motor Co. have rallied amid expectations that post-storm replacement demand could boost deliveries this fall and into early 2018. The mix of vehicles consumers are likely to want should be favorable - Texas is a major market in the U.S. for pickups and SUVs.
Chinese automaker BAIC still considering a formal assembly plant in Mexico

Chinese automaker BAIC revealed it's still considering to build a complete assembly plant that includes stamping process and paint shop by 2020. The company operates an assembly line in Veracruz which just finishes pre-assembly vehicles that arrive in containers from overseas. Company officials said governors from six Mexican states have met with corporate executives in China to offer premises and benefit packages in order to have the plant installed at their jurisdictions. Among the states interested in the project are Nuevo Leon, Quintana Roo, Sonora, Morelos, Hidalgo and Guanajuato.
