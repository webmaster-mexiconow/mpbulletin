30 January, 2017
Mazda's Mexican plant reaches 500,000 units milestone

Mazda's vehicle number 500,000 (a machine gray metallic Mazda 3) rolled out of its Mexican facility. This is the first time the milestone is achieved at a Mazda plant in less than three years, said company officials. The production site began operations in February 2014 after a US$ 770 million investment. The plant currently employs more than 5,200 workers to build the Mazda 2 and Mazda 3 models, as well as Skyactiv engines.
Ford spent US$ 200 million on cancelled Mexican plant

Ford Motor Company took a nearly US$ 200 million hit from costs related to canceling the US$ 1.6 billion small car plant in Mexico, according to its fourth-quarter report. The Dearborn, Michigan, automaker posted a US$ 783 million net loss compared with a US$ 1.9 billion profit from a year ago. The loss was due largely to a US$ 3 billion noncash adjustment of pension obligations and a US$ 199 million charge for halting construction of the Mexican factory.
Panasonic switches production to auto parts at Nuevo Leon facility

A Panasonic plant located in Apodaca, Nuevo Leon, will stop making vacuum cleaners shifting production to car seat warmers and other automotive parts, according to Japanese media. Only a few of its 500 or so workers are expected to lose their jobs to the change. The vacuum cleaner production will move to a facility in Malaysia while the auto parts work is scheduled to start in April.
Bill Forge Global opens US$ 12 million plant in Guanajuato

India-based Bill Forge Global started operations in Celaya, Guanajuato, at the Cuadritos Industrial Park, with an initial investment of US$12 million, and expectations to escalate to US$30 million and generating over 75 jobs. Bill Forge Global manufactures a variety of cold, warm, hot forged and machined components for the automotive sector. General Motors, GKN Driveline, Ford, Honda, Bosch, Wabco, Fiat, NSK and Mahindra are some of its main customers.
Ford recalls nearly 8,000 F-150 trucks after instrument panels go dark

Nearly 8,000 2017 Ford F-150 trucks are being recalled after the automaker learned the instrument panels can go dark and fail to provide warning lights, messages and chimes. Ford says about 6,792 of the trucks are in the U.S., 1,057 are in Canada and 125 are located in Mexico. All the trucks were built between October 4, through November 23, 2016, and no accidents or injuries have been reported due to instrument panel failures.
GM cuts 625 jobs at Ontario plant, shifts some production to Mexico

General Motors is cutting 625 jobs at its assembly plant near London, Ont., as it shifts some production to Mexico. About 3,100 workers assemble the Chevrolet Equinox and GMC Terrain crossovers at this facility. Terrain production, however, is scheduled to be shifted to a plant in Mexico in July.
Currency rate lowers Strattec's cost of Mexican operations

Strattec Security Corporation, manufacturer of automotive locks and ignition switches, reported diluted earnings per share of US$ 0.11 for the fiscal second quarter ended January 1, 2017, compared to US$ 0.93 in the prior year quarter. The decrease was in part attributed to additional costs incurred to improve quality of production processes and start up their new Guanajuato facility, which were partially offset by a more favorable Mexican Peso to U.S. Dollar exchange rate.
U.S. auto workers to get among the biggest bonus checks ever

Workers at U.S. car factories are reaping the benefits of increasing demand for high-margin pickups and sport-utility vehicles, with union employees expected to take home among the biggest bonus checks ever. Ford Motor's 2016 payout of US$ 9,000 is among the highest in its 114-year history. Fiat Chrysler will pay out on average US$ 5,000, the highest bonuses since the company's 2009 bankruptcy. Checks from GM -which is reporting its results on Feb. 7- will likely top the $11,000 paid last year.
New Mexico targets Takata, auto makers over faulty air bags

New Mexico Attorney General Hector Balderas is suing Japanese manufacturer Takata and a long list of automakers in connection with the sale of cars with dangerous air bag inflators. The state is seeking civil penalties for each defective air bag that entered the New Mexico market and penalties for each day the manufacturers misrepresented the safety of their products. The lawsuit does not document any deaths or injuries in New Mexico.
Hyundai Motor's profit tumbles for the 12th time straight

Hyundai Motor Co. posted its 12th straight year-over-year decline in quarterly profit, hit by weak car demand and higher costs from strikes in South Korea and sales incentives in the U.S. The auto maker said net profit in the October-December period was US$ 918 million. The continuing profit decline comes as sedans such as the midsize Sonata fell out of favor with consumers, who prefer larger SUVs that have become cheaper to drive as fuel prices fall.
