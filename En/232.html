Bulletin #232
April 8th, 2019
MAN Truck & Bus commences operations in Queretaro's new production line

With an investment plan of US$5.7 million for this year, the heavy vehicle company MAN Truck & Bus Mexico began the operation of line two at its new plant in Queretaro. Governor Francisco Dominguez Servien said that, with this project, MAN Truck & Bus, of the Volkswagen Group, provides employment to more than 300 people within the state, which proves the conditions of certainty and confidence that the entity possesses. The plant represents an investment of more than US$1.7 million and specializes in the production of chassis for buses with Euro EEV technology.
Volkswagen Mexico increases its production by 14.3% in March

Volkswagen Mexico increased its production by 14.3% in March after its vehicle plant in Puebla manufactured 37,434 units during said month of 2019, meaning, its production increased by 4,684 in comparison to March of 2018. Likewise, in the accumulated months of January to March, the production at Puebla's plant increased by 40.7%, as it registered 110,116 produced vehicles. Said plant manufactured 78,219 vehicles during the first trimester of last year.
Production of Mexican vehicles increased 4.77%, exports hiked 1.96%

The production and exports of Mexican automobiles increased by 4.77% and 1.96% in March, respectively, compared to what was reported in the same month of last year. Information from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI) shows that in the third month of the year, 346,873 units were assembled in the country, which represented a clear increase of 4.77%, compared to the same period of 2018. On the other hand, in March of this year, 323,631 automobiles were sent abroad, representing a 1.96% increase.
KIA Motors Mexico grows 9.7% in March compared to 2018's numbers

KIA Motors Mexico has scored a new sales record, as achieved its best March by delivering 8,404 units, reinforcing its fifth place in the country with a market share of 7.2%. This figure represents a growth of 9.7% compared to the same period of the previous year; in addition, its sales figures are still 9% higher than last month, which helps the company maintain its growth streak. The sales were led by the KIA Rio, produced at the Pesqueria plant, Nuevo Leon, with 3,005 units, followed by the Forte with 1,525 vehicles in its sedan version and 113 vehicles in its hatchback version.
Acura grows 20% during the first quarter of 2019

The Japanese brand of luxury vehicles and subsidiary of Honda, Acura, presents an accumulative annual growth of 20% during the first trimester of the year, placing the brand in the fourth position within the premium segment in Mexico. Sales during the first trimester of 2019 registered a total of 531 units, and a significant increase of 37% in sales during the month of March compared to last year. With the recent launch of the all-new RDX, and the seven-passenger MDX SUV, Acura reinforces its position in the premium segment of the automotive industry.
UFI Filters invests US$25 million in Coahuila

The governor of Coahuila, Miguel Angel Riquelme Solis, announced the arrival of UFI Filters, which brings an investment of US$25 million and the generation of 250 new jobs. The state leader welcomed and thanked the Italy-based automotive company for having determined to bring their investments to Coahuila, settling in the municipality of Ramos Arizpe. The company is to begin operations in June. UFI Filters develops filters and modules for cars, specializing in Formula 1 vehicles.
Oerlikon Balzers opens US$4 million plant in Queretaro

With a US$4 million investment, Oerlikon Balzers opened its second plant in Queretaro, and the third one in the country. The Swiss company produces solutions against the wear and tear suffered by surfaces of the automotive, aeronautical, food and metal mechanic sectors. It uses sustainable production methods, including PPD (Pulsed Plasma Diffusion) technology, unique in Latin America. During the event, Marc Desrayaud, president of the Business Unit of Oerlikon Balzers Industrial Solutions, acknowledged the work of the state government, and reaffirmed his confidence in the workforce's professionalism and in Queretaro's economic development.
Mitsubishi's Mexican sales hike by 39.3% in March

Mitsubishi Motors Mexico reported that, by the end of March, its sales grew 39.3% with the delivery of 1,711 units, unlike the 1,228 vehicles that were sold in the same month of last year. Ahkin Vazquez, commercial director of Mitsubishi Motors Mexico, stated that Mirage orders increased by 98.83%, L200 by 78.76%, Mirage G4 by 41.86% and the Outlander model by 50.67%. In March of this year, Jorge Vallejo, president and CEO of the company, presented the work plan "New era Mitsubishi 2019", which aims to sell more than 25,000 units in 2019, 70% higher than the sales reported in 2018.
Heavy vehicles sales increase in the first quarter: ANPACT

The National Association of Bus, Truck and Tractor Truck Producers (ANPACT), reported that, during this year's March, the wholesale sale of heavy vehicles reached a total of 3,928 units, which compared to those registered in March of 2018, represent an increase of 16.6%. During the same month, retail sales reached 3,890 units, an amount that represents an increase of 17.2%.
Mazda and Mercedes-Benz are the automotive companies that satisfy the Mexican customer the most

Mazda and Mercedes-Benz were the brands with the highest ranking of satisfaction of the Mexican customer throughout the purchase of cars, where the delivery of the unit was the most important factor in the sale process, explained Gerardo Gomez, director of J.D. Power. When presenting the Customer Satisfaction Study of J.D. Power in Mexico 2019, Gomez said that, for the second consecutive year, the Japanese brand was placed at the top, with a score of 881 out of 1,000 points, as to how happy the customer was throughout the purchase of a vehicle. While Mercedes-Benz, in the luxury segment, reached a score of 888, followed by BMW with 881 points.
