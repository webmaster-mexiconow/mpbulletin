Report: Ford plans to shift production of Fusion sedan to China

Ford plans to shift production of midsize Fusion and Mondeo sedans out of Mexico and Spain, respectively, in 2020 and move it to China, Reuters reported citing three inner sources. While Ford didn't deny its intentions to shift production to China, the company said there's no plans to export such models from China to North American and European markets. "Fusion/Mondeo are an important part of the Ford car lineup. We will have more information to share about the next Fusion/Mondeo at a later date," Ford said in a statement. Ford Fusion monthly sales totaled 14,901 vehicles in November, down 15.1%, while year-to-date sales fell 22.1% to 192,179 units.
Volkswagen releases sketches of the all-new 2019 Jetta ahead of its debut in Detroit

Ahead of its official debut at the 2018 Detroit Auto Show in January, Volkswagen released sketches of the all-new 2019 Jetta which give an idea of what the next generation of the popular sedan will look like. So far, it is known that the vehicle will finally debut the popular MQB architecture shared with Golf, Tiguan and even the Arteon, which means a major improvement in terms of handling and comfortability. It will have a turbo 1.4-liter engine with 150 horsepower and 184 lb-ft. paired to either an eight-speed automatic or a six-speed manual transmission. The unit will get post-collision braking standard, with optional blind spot monitoring, rear parking assist and adaptive cruise control. Also standard, it'll feature LED front and rear exterior lighting.
Audi Q5 earns Best Buy, Best Auto Tech awards from Kelley Blue Book

The 2018 Audi Q5 has been named winner of the 2018 Kelley Blue Book Best Buy in the luxury SUV category, in addition to receiving the 2018 Kelly Blue Book Best Auto Tech Award in the luxury category. The annual Best Buy Awards from Kelley Blue Book recognize the top new model-year vehicle choices available in the U.S. market. The third annual Kelley Blue Book Best Auto Tech Awards honor models with the most advanced infotainment, convenience and active driver assistance features at a great value to car buyers. "This wasn't an easy choice. There were several excellent contenders in this category, including the stellar Mercedes-Benz GLC. But key factors gave the Q5 the advantage," said KBB about the Puebla-made SUV.
Latin NCAP grants four stars in occupant protection to Nissan Kicks

The Latin New Car Assessment Programme (NCAP) published its eighth and last round of results for 2017 this week with encouraging results for the Nissan Kicks. The model achieved four stars for each Adult Occupant Protection and Child Occupant Protection. The compact SUV, with two airbags as standard equipment, showed a just unstable structural and footwell area performance in the frontal crash test that explains the weak chest protection while in the side impact protection it showed adequate to good protection being enough in the overall results for four stars in Adult Occupant Protection. Also, the lack of passenger airbag disconnection switch and ISOFIX marking not meeting Latin NCAP requirements, explain the four stars in Child Occupant Protection.
Germany-based manufacturer Henkel to increase production in Guanajuato

Germany-based chemical products manufacturer Henkel received the proper permits from Mexican environmental authorities to conduct expansions in order to add a new production line at its facility in Salamanca, Guanajuato, El Economista reported. Due to increasing demand in the auto industry, the company is investing US$ 8 million to produce an automotive sealer which it currently imports from the United States. The Salamanca plant of Henkel currently produces paints, adhesives, liquids, ferric, powders, emulsified, reduced, oxidizers and other types of sealants for a diverse range of applications. The company operates six plants in Mexico, four of them located in the state of Mexico, one in Nuevo Leon and the Guanajuato site.
Fagor Arrasate opens facility in Queretaro to supply metal-forming machinery

Fagor Arrasate, a Spain-based manufacturer of metal-forming machine tools, invested US$ 5.3 million in Queretaro to setup a facility to supply stamping systems and presses to manufacturers all over Latin America. "The start-up of this plant represents the logical consequence to the progressive growth of orders in Mexico, and will allow Fagor Arrasate to consolidate its important presence in the Mexican market and NAFTA," said the company in a statement. According to its executives, Mexico currently is Fagor third biggest market just behind China and Germany. Some of its customers in the country are Volkswagen, Gestamp, Metalsa and Thyssenkrupp, among others.
Ternium confirms US $1 billion investment in Nuevo Leon

Italian-Argentine conglomerate Techint Group confirmed it will invest US$1 billion to set up a new steel mill in Nuevo Leon through its subsidiary Ternium. The hot rolling mill will be developed as part of an expansion of Techint's industrial center in the municipality of Pesquería, where the consortium first started operations in 2013. Expected to be operational in the second half of 2020, the new plant will have an annual production capacity of 3.7 million tonnes of steel and manufactured products to meet demand in the automotive industry as well as the home appliance, machinery, energy and construction sectors. CEO Paolo Rocca presented the plan to President Enrique Pena Nieto in a private meeting in Mexico City before making it public.
Lear Corp. acquires Israel-based, self-driving tech developer EXO Technologies

Lear Corporation announced that it has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire Israel-based EXO Technologies, a developer of GPS technology providing high-accuracy solutions for autonomous and connected vehicle applications. "The acquisition will provide Lear with a differentiated technology to significantly improve GPS accuracy and reliability, thereby enhancing vehicle safety and enabling autonomous driving," said Matt Simoncini, Lear's president and CEO. EXO has developed core technology that addresses the need for high-accuracy positioning in a vehicle. Its proprietary technology works with existing GPS receivers to provide centimeter-level accuracy anywhere on the globe without the need for terrestrial base-station networks.
Sales of heavy trucks fall for third month in a row during November

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT) reported that wholesale sales of commercial vehicles fell 32.1% during November, compared to the same month of 2016, totaling 3,226 units sold. Retail sales also recorded a decline of 18.2% with 3,750 units delivered. November was the third consecutive month with a drop in monthly sales, a trend that began last September. ANPACT reported that wholesale sales during the first 11 months of 2017 reached a total of 36,848 units, which resulted in a decrease of 5.5%. The year-to-date retail sales totaled 37,007 units, a volume that shows a decrease of 3.4% compared to the 38,299 units sold from January through November 2016.
Orders for Tesla's electric semi-truck keep growing

Tesla's recently-revealed electric semi-truck is drawing everybody's attention, it seems, with orders for the vehicles coming from Walmart, DHL, Anheuser-Busch, and Canadian grocery chain Loblaws. Now PepsiCo tops the list with the largest pre-order yet: a fleet of 100 electric trucks reserved before production starts in 2019. The forthcoming units will run between US$ 150,000 and US$ 180,000, depending on range, with a fancy "Founders Series" of semis coming in at US$ 200,000. While those numbers are a higher than a traditional diesel truck, operating them may save companies US$ 250,000 at a million miles of use, according to Tesla.
