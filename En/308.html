AMDA expects light vehicle sales to drop between 3 and 4% in 2020

The sales perspective for light vehicles in 2020 in the country continues to be negative; the commercialization of light vehicles is expected to fall between 3 and 4% to place 1,272,000 units, estimated Guillermo Rosales. The deputy director general of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) said that this drop is explained by the decrease in buyers' income. "On the other extreme, there's buyers that have a bigger acquisitive power, that do have the possibility to undertake financing to acquire vehicles, but they're being cautious to acquire a long-term commitment," he added.

Car and truck production captured US$3.9 billion in FDI

Mexico attracted US$3.9 billion in Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in the production of cars and trucks in 2019, which meant a year-to-year increase of 18% and a record. With these results, car and truck production became the productive segment that received the most FDI last year, surpassing multipurpose banking (US$3.7 billion) and auto parts production (US$3 billion). The Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) estimates that Mexico will become the fifth vehicle producer worldwide in 2021 due to the new players in original equipment manufacturing.

Meor invests US$18 million in Pesqueria

With an investment of US$18 million and 1,000 new jobs, directives from Meor, a real estate investment fund with presence in Mexico and abroad, inaugurated the Logis All plant which will supply automotive parts to Kia in Mexico and other countries. This plant is located in the Asia Pacific Park industrial park, in the municipality of Pesqueria. TAIGENE invests US$45.7 million in Guanajuato

Taiwanese company TAIGENE, whose name means Taiwan Generators, has more than 50 years of history in the production of electric motors and it now arrives to Mexico with its first plant in Latin America located in Parque Colinas de Leon in Leon, Guanajuato. With an investment of US$45.7 million, the electric motor plant for the automotive industry will begin operations in March. TAIGENE will produce electric motors for direction systems, windshield wipers, seats, fans, air pumps and A/C systems for cars from brands like GM, Ford, FCA, BMW and others.
