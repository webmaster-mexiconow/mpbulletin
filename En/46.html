Tesla follows Audi's strategy in Mexico to meet production deadline

Tesla CEO Elon Musk faces criticism for skipping testing phases to launch its Model 3 sedan by a self-imposed volume production deadline of September. According to a report by Reuters, Musk is using computer simulations to help Tesla in advancing straight to production tooling, a technique first used by Audi which allowed it to launch production at its Mexican plant 30% faster. In fact, an Audi executive involved in the Mexican plant, Peter Hochholdinger, is now Tesla's vice president of production, says the Reuters report.
Draft order on NAFTA shakes foreign exchange markets

Several media outlets reported that a draft version of an executive order to withdraw the U.S. from NAFTA went under review and would be unveiled by the end this week, citing White House officials as sources. After the news spread, the U.S. dollar gained 1.87% versus the Mexican peso at 19.20 per dollar. The U.S. currency rose 0.25% versus the Canadian dollar at 1.36. Later that day president Donald Trump on conference calls with leaders from Mexico and Canada, said the U.S. would not pull out of the Free Trade Agreement "at this time".
Daimler net profit doubles during first quarter

Daimler reported its net profit doubled during first quarter 2017 on the back of record sales and revenues. The company's unit sales were 10% above last year at 754,300 vehicles, with revenues up by 11% to US$ 42.32 billion. Its earnings before taxes were 87% above prior-year level at US$ 4.37 billion, with net profit doubled to US$ 3.05 billion. Mercedes-Benz Cars' unit sales increased by 14% to 568,100 vehicles in the first quarter. However, first quarter sales by Daimler Trucks of 94,000 vehicles were 11% lower than in the prior-year period. Daimler builds heavy trucks in Santiago Tianguistenco, State of Mexico, and Saltillo, Coahuila.
Takata defective airbags inflators make way to used auto parts market

A driver of a 2002 Accord was seriously injured after a faulty Takata air bag inflator ruptured during a crash in Las Vegas, Nevada. Honda said the inflator had not been installed by a dealer but likely was a salvaged part from a junk yard. The Japanese automaker has purchased more than 60,000 salvaged Takata Corp air bag modules in an effort to prevent similar incidents. Meanwhile Takata strongly urged all consumers to check a government website to see if their inflators have been recalled. The victim, 18, suffered a puncture wound to her trachea in a rear-end crash.
Bolt EV owners in the US drive 4.5 million miles in three months

Chevrolet reported that 3,492 Bolt EV owners in the United States have driven a cumulative 4,570,300 miles as of April 2, 2017 since the vehicle went on sale last December. These all-electric miles have resulted in more than 175,000 gallons of fuel saved based on the average EPA-estimated 26 mpg for 2017 vehicles in the U.S. The average Bolt EV owner drives approximately 53 miles per day, "demonstrating the vehicle's versatility as a daily driver and road trip-ready crossover" said GM in a statement. The Bolt EV will be available in Mexico by summer.
Higher sales boost Rassini's earnings 28% during first quarter

Mexican suspension and brake components manufacturer Rassini reported a strong performance during the first quarter, with sales for 4,880 million Mexican pesos, approximately US$ 263.8 million at an 18.5 pesos per U.S. dollar exchange rate, which represented a 28% hike. According to its unaudited financial results, earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortization reached around US$ 54 million (998 million pesos), also a 28% growth, driven by higher sales, an enhanced product mix in the NAFTA region and a permanent control over the cost structure.
Honda begins production of 2018 Odyssey minivan in Alabama

More than 1,500 associates at Honda Manufacturing of Alabama (HMA) celebrated the start of mass production of the all-new 2018 Honda Odyssey minivan, slated to go on-sale this spring at Honda dealerships nationwide. HMA has been the exclusive manufacturer of this vehicle in America over the past seven years. The plant also manufactures the Pilot SUV, the Ridgeline pickup truck, the Acura MDX luxury SUV and the V-6 engines that power each vehicle. The 2018 Odyssey is the second generation to be designed and developed in the United States.
U.S. auto industry accelerates automation in 2016

The U.S. manufacturing industry installed 31,500 robots during 2016, out of those a new record of approximately 17,500 units went to the automotive sector, meaning auto plants acquired 43% more robots last year compared to 2015. In the last seven years, the operational stock in the auto industry increased by about 52,100 units, from 74,900 to about 127,000. In comparison, China installed about 90,000 industrial robots last year, according to preliminary results published by the Statistical Department of the International Federation of Robotics (IFR).
Three built-in-Mexico Nissan models awarded for ‘Best Fleet Value in America'

Three models built by Nissan in Mexico were honored at the twelfth annual edition of ‘Best Fleet Value in America' awards granted by Vincentric, a data analysis firm specialized in auto industry. Nissan Versa S Plus took the Best Fleet Value Subcompact award while the Versa Note S Plus was named Best Fleet Value Subcompact Hatchback, both models won their categories for second year in a row. The Nissan NV200 S received the Best Fleet Value Small Commercial Cargo Van merit for the fourth time. Nissan builds the awarded units at a production facility in Cuernavaca, Morelos, established in 1966.
Waymo starts self-driving test with real life customers in Phoenix

Google's self-driving technology company, Waymo, is giving hundreds of Phoenix-area residents access to autonomous vehicles, in one of the largest and most public efforts yet to put ordinary people inside cars of the future. Waymo isn't just offering the occasional ride, the company said. Instead, participants accepted into its "early rider program" and their immediate family members will use either a self-driving SUV or minivan as their primary or secondary vehicle at no cost. The vehicles will have safety drivers to monitor rides at all times.
