The automotive industry will gradually return to operations

Since the government gave the green light to the restart of operations of the automotive, aerospace, mining and construction industries starting this week, as long as their safe distancing and labor protection protocols are approved by federal authorities, the automotive sector informed that activities will be gradual, at a work rhythm of 20% and with strict measurements to prevent infections. The Bajio Region declared itself ready to give the step with the entry of technicians and engineers to turn on plant engines and fire up the robots.

The Ford Fusion will say goodbye to production lines in July

The American brand announced that it would stop selling sedans and hatchbacks in North America to bet everything on SUVs and it wasn't kidding. After saying goodbye to the Fiesta, Focus and Taurus, Ford Fusion's end to production has come. According to a report accessed by Ford Authority, the brand's Hermosillo plant will stop producing the Fusion in two stages. On June 5th the last unit meant for police forces will come out of production, while on July 21st the last Fusion for public sales will be produced.

Mitsubishi offers sanitization service in thanks to those who help combat COVID-19

Mitsubishi Motors of Mexico is thanking doctors, paramedics, nurses, the police force, firefighters, hospital and clinic cleaning personnel, medical services drivers and private services (DiDi, Uber and Cabify) and giving them a courtesy sanitization service for their vehicle. In recognition of the work they're doing for the country, Mitsubishi Motors is offering this benefit to any person that is currently performing any of these jobs, with the intention of contributing to society's well-being.

The guidelines to restart operations are already available

Construction, mining and manufacturing of transport equipment (among which automotive production and exportation are included) companies in Mexico will have to implement sanitary measures and conduct the "Self-assessment of the Health Security Protocol," which is made up of 60 questions to determine if work centers have the Health Secutiry Guidelines in the Work Place, to be able to restart their operations after the temporary halt taken as a measure to prevent the spread of COVID-19.

Puebla's Governor thinks it'll be difficult for the automotive industry to restart on June 1st

Due to the worst moment being lived in Puebla in regards to the rise in COVID-19 cases, there's no conditions for the automotive industry to restart on June 1st, assured governor Miguel Barbosa Huerta. Although the Federation determined that this sector would be able to join on May 18 by being considered as an essential activity alongside construction and mining in the country, the state leader said that, based on its attributions, the Volkswagen of Mexico and Audi plants, as well as the 150 suppliers will not renew operations on the expected date.

Queretaro's automotive industry will operate at between 60 and 70% of its capacity

The state's automotive industry will start at in between 60 and 70% of its operative capacity; due to a lower level production projection due to the fall in the worldwide automotive market, informed the president in Queretaro of the Mexican Confederation of Business Owners (Coparmex), Lorena Jimenez Salcedo. She exposed that they've approached members of Queretaro's Automotive Cluster, stating that about 350 Tier 1 and Tier 2 companies will gradually restart operations.

Ford is awaiting approval to resume operations

Ford submitted to the federal government all of its health protocols in production, administration and car sales in Mexico, which is why it's only waiting for the authorities' approval to begin assembling vehicles in the country. Lucien Pinto, marketing and sales director for the company in the country, said that as soon as they manage to pass all of the requirements, they will immediately resume production. Ford has stamping and assembly plants in Hermosillo and Cuautitlán, while additionally producing engines in its Chihuahua plant and transmissions in Irapuato.
