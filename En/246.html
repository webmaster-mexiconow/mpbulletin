Harman inaugurates the second stage of its Advanced Engineering Center with US$12 million

With an investment of around US$12 million, Harman inaugurated the second stage of its Advanced Center of Engineering in Queretaro. The extension consists of the installation of a pilot factory, warehouse services, as well as validation and testing laboratories, and advanced manufacturing engineering so as to test the performance of its products. Harman's senior vice president of global operations, Daniel Jonas, highlighted the company's growth in the manufacturing sector, as well as the competitiveness offered by the state.
Manuel Montoya to lead the National Cluster Network of the Automotive Industry

Faced with changes within the international environment, such as the US-China trade war and the Agreement between Mexico, the United States and Canada (USMCA), which modifies aspects in the automotive industry, nine clusters from different Mexican regions created the National Cluster Network of the Automotive Industry, whose objective is to develop local suppliers. Manuel Montoya Ortega, general director of the Automotive Cluster of Nuevo Leon and president of the National Network of Clusters of the Automotive Industry, recalled that this effort dates back to 2016 "and now the network is constituted as a civil association".
Mayra Gonzalez to leave CEO position at Nissan Mexicana

Nissan Mexicana has announced changes in its management team, while celebrating an unprecedented milestone of 10 consecutive years of leadership in sales within the Mexican market. Effective July 1, Jose Roman, current corporate vice president, was named president and CEO of Nissan Mexicana, replacing Mayra Gonzalez, who is to assume the position of division general manager of Nissan Motor Co. in Japan. "Nissan is fortunate to have an established global leader like Jose Roman returning to Mexico to maintain our position as leaders," said Jose Valls, president and chairman of Nissan North America.
Transportes Arlequin acquires 10 Nuevo Cascadia models

Daimler Trucks Mexico announced the sale of 10 vehicles of the Nuevo Cascadia model to the company Transportes Arlequin, an action that demonstrates the great acceptance of Mexican transporters to Daimler Truck's portfolio addition. "It's an amazing feeling for us to have the trust of clients such as Transportes Arlequin; we have built a strong commercial alliance, to the point of forging a fleet constituted of Freightliner vehicles by 90%," said Flavio Rivera, president and CEO of Daimler Vehicles Commercial Mexico.
Mexican automotive exports to the United States grow 19.8% in May

Mexico registered exports of products worth US$41,825 million in May, a year-on-year increase of 6.7%, driven by the automotive sector, the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI) reported. In particular, automotive external sales climbed at an annual rate of 16%, its best performance in the last seven months. In greater detail, automotive exports to the United States in May rose by 19.8%, the highest dynamism since August 2014.
Vetelia to enter the automotive sector with an electric vehicle

Vetelia transferred its operations to Queretaro from Leon, as it found the aeronautical supply there to be something it needed to make its dream come true: design its first electric car prototype. The company mainly produces bicycles and it has been able to significantly develop its electric car prototype project. "Larger companies started making a bicycle and ended up making cars," said Emilio Sosa Solis, CEO of the company. The goal Vetelia has for its electric car is for it to offer speed no greater than 150 kilometers per hour, with potency to go from zero to 100 kilometers within seven seconds.
At least 28 US governors request a rapid ratification of the USMCA

A total of 28 US governors asked the United States Congress to "quickly" ratify the Treaty between Mexico, the United States and Canada (USMCA). In a letter sent to the leaders of the House of Representatives and the Senate, they stressed that they support smart and strategic trade agreements that promote economic growth in their states and expand opportunities for their workers, farmers, and manufacturers. "As executive directors of our states, we urge Congress to quickly approve the USMCA so that American workers can begin to take advantage of the benefits of better trade with our neighbors in North America," they said.
Ford gives Q1 certification to SEG Automotive Mexico's Plant Lerma

Ford Mexico gave the Q1 certification to SEG Automotive Mexico's Plant Lerma after two years of constant work in which the company's personnel was a key factor for said achievement, said Ruben Chavez, electrical site manager-supplier technical assitance (STA). During the ceremony, Luis Chacon, vice president of operations for SEG Automotive Mexico, said that one of the company's objectives, when Plant Lerma started operating, was for them to obtain the highest level of recognition as one of Ford's Tier 1.
Vehicle manufacturers in Mexico seek to comply with the USMCA

The car manufacturers installed in Mexico are interacting with the government of the country in search of ways to comply with the rule of origin required by the renewed free trade agreement, the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) reported. The president of AMIA, Eduardo Solis, described the new rule of the Treaty between Mexico, the United States and Canada (USMCA) as "very hard" and "very complex", as it increases the regional threshold of automotive content from 62.5% to 75%. In addition, he warned that compliance could be difficult for some companies.
Metalsa wins tender to collaborate with Ford's F-150 electric truck

The Mexican autoparts firm Metalsa is working on the development of chassis for electric cars of the Ford and Rivian brands, said Leopoldo Cedillo, general manager of the company. "We won the tender to work on the chassis for the F-150 electric truck (Ford), and we are also helping Rivian, which is a startup in the United States," said Cedillo. Moreover, he explained that the automotive industry is migrating towards electric mobility, which is why they're investing in innovation and development for these kinds of vehicles.
