It's necessary to reactivate the internal market: ANPACT

Miguel Elizalde, president of the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), informed that the assembly of 12,866 units was achieved, which represents a fall of 30.4%, when compared with the 18,498 assembled ones during the same month of 2019. "To reach a recovery in production it will be necessary to reactivate the internal market which requires a legislative framework that incentives fleet renewal, as well as accelerating procedures such as the registration of new units," he commented.

Uber helps Public Health Authorities in the fight against COVID-19 with new portal

Uber announced the release of a new portal that will allow Public Health Authorities in Mexico to obtain information on accounts, trips and deliveries made through its platform, to help them track contact and infection prevention for COVID-19. The portal is free to use, and exclusive for Public Health Authorities within the framework of the health emergency. Previous to sharing any information, Uber will be in charge of verifying the origin of every request made through the portal and will respond according to the required information.

August interrupts the upturn of the heavy vehicles industry

Heavy vehicle production in Mexico interrupted in August the upturn it had been maintaining from June with the reactivation of the economic activities before the "new reality" due to COVID-19, despite the fact that exports keep exhibiting an increase. Miguel Elizalde, president of the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractors (ANPACT), informed that the assembly of units fell 30.4% annually to 12,866 units. According to the August report from the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI), heavy vehicle exports continued at their improvement rhythm during the eight month of the year when compared to July, with exports for 11,160 units, 11.4% more than the previous month.

Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles begins selling the 2020 Crafter

Mexican vehicles have a participation of 13.95% within the American market

A total of 8,980,925 light vehicles were sold in the United States during the first eight months of 2020, which meant a decrease of 21.5% when compared to the 11,436,371 sold units during the same period of 2019, according to numbers from Ward's Automotive Reports. In said period, vehicles of Mexican origin had a participation of 13.94% by adding 1,252,437 units, however, this was a decline of 31.1% when compared to the same period of last year when 1,818,948 Mexican vehicles were sold.

Toyota invests US$800 million in the development of new technologies

Japanese automaker Toyota will create a global investment fund with US$800 million to develop new autonomous driving or artificial intelligence technologies. The fund, named Woven Capital, will begin operations in 2021 and will be managed by a subsidiary of Toyota's research and development institute (TRI-AD) and will be based in the United States, according to a press release from the Japanese company. The objective of the new fund will be to invest in "emerging companies that are working in innovative technologies like autonomous mobility, artificial intelligence, data analysis, automatic learning, connectivity and smart cities," explained Toyota.
