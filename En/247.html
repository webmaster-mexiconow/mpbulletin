BMW Group Financial Services Mexico and Latin America appoints new CEO and CFO

BMW Group Financial Services Mexico and Latin America tapped Matthias Schlesiger as its new CEO so as to replace Harald E. Schüll, who was in charge of the company's operations for nine years. On the other hand, Dagmar Mutter will be the new CFO of BMW Group Financial Services Mexico and Latin America. Both executives will assume their new positions as of July 1, 2019. BMW Group Financial Services is BMW Group's financial division and is recognized as a leader in financial services due to its wide range of options, opportunities, and financing products within the automotive sector.
The USMCA could fortify automotive exports: Jetro

The negotiation of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) was a "great success" for Mexico, as it guarantees automotive exports of up to 2.6 million vehicles per year, said Kimihiko Inaba, general director of the Japan External Trade Organization (Jetro). "Mexico can ensure its base position of automotive exports to the United States," he added. And this not only benefits Japanese companies established in Mexican territory, but many American manufacturers that operate in the country as well.
Cristina Perkins assumes the marketing manager position at Toyota Motor Sales México

Toyota made official one of the most representative changes within the company in Mexican territory. In this sense, the firm announced that Cristina Perkins is to work as the marketing manager of Toyota Motor Sales Mexico. Cristina has an extensive experience within the automotive industry, as she has led multiple departments in Toyota such as the sales, consumer experience, dealerships and, of course, marketing departments.
As of today, the NOM-044 becomes compulsory

On July 1, 2019, the NOM-044, a norm that entered into force on January 1 of this year, formally becomes compulsory. This means that only trucks and tractors equipped with engines that comply with EPA 07 or Euro V emission technologies, as well as EPA 10 and Euro VI, will be sold in Mexico. In addition, there is a second phase of the NOM-044 which demands that, as of January 2021, trucks and tractors can only be imported and produced with the EPA 10 and Euro VI technologies.
This is how Queretaro is preparing for the new Toyota plant

Queretaro's municipal authorities, entrepreneurs, and universities are preparing for the imminent opening of the Toyota assembly plant in Apaseo el Grande, Guanajuato, which is expected to be completed by early 2020. On the one hand, they are getting ready to train people that the supplying companies will demand and, on the other hand, they are promoting the arrival of industrial parks and hotels to the municipality. The municipality of Corregidora in Queretaro, adjacent to Guanajuato, works with the Technological University of Corregidora (UTC), companies, and other institutions so as to certify and train the personnel that will eventually be required.
Beat is recruiting drivers in Nuevo Leon

Beat, one of the main apps for private transportation in Latin America and the fastest growing in the region, announced that, starting July 1st, it is to open the doors of its Driver's Center in the city of Monterrey, Nuevo Leon, which means that drivers wishing to join Beat will be able to do so. During its first month of operations, Beat obtained a growth of 60% a week, while they had seven times more trips than in their first 30 days by the second month. Moreover, the progress continues, as double-digit growth continues to be registered month after month.
