Alejandro Mondragon is Scania Mexico's new general director

Scania Mexico announced a change in the company, Alejandro Mondragon will be succeeding Enrique Enrich as general director, while he will undertake new responsibilities in the Swedish company. Mondragon began his career as commercial trainee in Scania Mexico in 2005 and due to his performance and professionalism, became director of Service in Mexico in 2013. Alejandro is an industrial engineer with a degree from the National Technological Institute of Mexico, and has an MBA from the Universidad del Valle de Mexico and a Management Program from the Stockholm School of Economics.

Volkswagen of Mexico designs respiratory assistant to help treat COVID-19 patients

Steffen Reiche, president of the Board of Volkswagen of Mexico, presented through a videoconference to several press outlets a prototype of "electric emergency respiratory assistant" developed by the German brand, which is destined to treat patients with COVID-19, and which is awaiting approval from Federal Commission for Protection Against Health Risks (COFEPRIS), to then be distributed to different hospitals in the country. Once certified, the ventilators made by Volkswagen will be sent to Mexican hospitals, beginning with those in Puebla and Guanajuato.

Canacintra in Queretaro asks to consider the automotive industry as essential

Industrialists from Queretaro are asking the federal government to decree the productive sector as essential; due to the fact that they have started to generate new work protocols to be implemented once they return to productive activities. The president of the Queretaro chapter of the National Chamber of Industry (Canacintra), Jorge Rivadeneyra Díaz, exposed that in coordination with state authorities they are studying sanitary protocols, from using personal protection equipment, such as gloves and face masks, to scattering personnel in several working periods and avoiding large crowds.

Vehicular leasing sees increase of 5% in the first quarter of 2020

The Mexican Association of Vehicle Leasers (AMAVe) reported 224,225 units administered by partners, which means an increase of 5% when compared to the closing of 2019's fourth quarter. 73% of these units are pure leasing, scheme which saw a growth of 8%. However, one of the most important growths was financial leasing with 33.7% when compared to the closing of the fourth quarter of last year. On the other hand, the daily rent segment was affected the most by the COVID-19 pandemic, with a fall of more than 5% when compared to the fourth quarter of 2019.

New vehicle sales take a plunge in April due to COVID-19

During April of this year 34,903 new vehicles were sold in Mexico, which implied the lowest sales volume reported since 2007. The automotive industry was specially affected by the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. In this fourth month we can observe an immediate plunge in the sale of cars, only from March to April sales fell 60.1%, according to numbers from the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA) and reported by INEGI. The sales vehicle in April also meant a fall of 64.6% in relation to that registered in April of last year.

Aguascalientes readies itself to reactivate the state's automotive sector

The state governments of Aguascalientes, Queretaro, San Luis Potosi, Guanajuato and Jalisco are preparing strategies to reactivate the automotive sector, since it's fundamental for the region's economic growth. This was informed by the governor of Aguascalientes, Martín Orozco Sandoval, but he also assured that it is being handled through federal authorities to restart the activities of vehicle assemblers that employ and sustain thousands of families throughout the country. He also considered that for Aguascalientes it's necessary to restart this sector as soon as possible, since it represents 19% of the state's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and generates about 120,000 jobs.

The Chevrolet Trackers arrives to Mexico

Chevrolet announced the incorporation of a new model to its van portfolio in Mexico. Chevrolet Tracker is an urban style SUV which will offer technological, security, connectivity and efficiency innovations. "Chevrolet Tracker will arrive to lead a transformation in the SUV segment, because it offers the highest value proposal in its segment," commented Jorge Plata, director of Marketing for General Motors of Mexico. "This model is based on current trends of the highest valued characteristics by clients, a modern, athletic and urban design, high safety and technological innovation standards, as well as total connectivity and efficient performance."
