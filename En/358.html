The 2020 Land Rover Defender is now available in Mexico

The 2020 Land Rover Defender is now available in Mexico, with a futuristic appearance marked by the iconic square traces of its predecessor, present in the silhouette and the headlights. For Mexico, the 2020 Defender's range is made up by three versions: 110, 110 First Edition and 110 X. In all cases the engine will be a turbo six cylinder 3.0 liter engine, which with a micro hybrid system that is capable of generating 400 hp and 405 lb-ft.

Distance commercialization grows thanks to the pandemic

The closing of new vehicle distributors as a measure to prevent the spread of COVID-19 became a strong engine for the transition to digital sales. During the months of April, May and June on which confinement took place, the automotive industry managed to sell almost 140,000 vehicles, almost exclusively through remote channels. This is a meaningful number because it was achieved without there being physical sales points and/or physical exhibitions.

Despite the pandemic the automotive industry generates surplus of US$28.1 billion

Between January and June 2020, the automotive industry generated an export value of US$47.5 billion, which meant the loss of 34.6% of its income when compared to the same period of 2019, according to data from INEGI. In case of imports, Mexico registered in 2020's first semester an entry of products for this sector with a value of US$19.3 billion, 32.5% less than that reported in the same period of 2019. The automotive trade balance between January and June of this year had a surplus of US$28.1 billion, 36% beneath that registered in the same period of 2019.

AMDA, Cesvi and AMIS offer tools to validate vehicle information

The Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) in collaboration with Mexico's Experimentation and Road Safety Center (Cesvi Mexico) and the Mexican Association of Insurance Institutions (AMIS) have concentrated their efforts in offering tools to validate vehicle information. The vehicle information validation, through the VIN will allow vehicle buyers and sellers from AMDA's distributor network to validate the correct composition of the serial number, as well as consulting with the insurance sector that said vehicle is not linked to any illegal activities.

The Mexican automotive sector will recover after the fall: AMIA

The Mexican automotive sector will recover after the general fall of 30% this year, affirmed Jose Guillermo Zozaya, AMIA's executive president. AMIA reported a monthly recovery of 23% and an annual increase of 0.7% in vehicle production during July. However, it faces an annual deficit of more than 800,000 vehicles in production and bigger than 743,000 in exports for this year. However, after the start of the "new normality" on June 1st, Zozaya considered that there's signs of recovery, although he doesn't yet have an annual forecast.

Volvo's plug-in hybrid XC40 Recharge PHEV arrives to Mexico

The Volvo XC40 Recharge PHEV, already has a price in Mexico. Its mechanic configuration is made up by a turbo 1.5 liter three cylinder engine, capable of generating 180 hp. The Volvo XC40 Recharge PHEV is available for presale in Mexico, with a price of MXN$999,000.
