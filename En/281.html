Financing for used cars increases, while it decreases for new vehicles

Financed sales for used cars are starting to have an upward trend among Mexican consumers, due to the decreasing purchase power for a durable good, distributors reported that credit used cars achieved a double-digit growth (12.1%), while credit for new cars decreased 10.4% between January and September of this year. Used cars have gained market participation in comparison to new financed cars, situation that presents itself during times of economic crisis, and above all due to the uncertainty of Mexico's economic future, explained Guillermo Rosales, director of AMDA.
SUV Frison T8 arrives to Mexico

JAC confirmed the arrival to Mexico of the Frison T8, the brand's new pickup, that along with the Frison T6 completes JAC's pickup line. "Very soon the JAC Frison T8 will be the daily ally of families that enjoy adventure and are looking for an SUV," commented Elias Massri, president of the Board and CEO of Giant Motors Latin America. The heart of the new JAC Frison T8 beats thanks to a Turbo 2.0L engine that, thanks to a six speed manual transmission generates 190 horsepower and 213 lb-ft torque.
Heavy vehicle sales fall in October: ANPACT

ANPACT reported that, during October, wholesale sales registered a fall of 30.5% while retail sales decreased 19.8%. As for wholesale sales, the sales of cargo units registered a fall of 16.4%, however, the sales of passenger units suffered an even bigger fall of 70%, product of a fall of 74.9% in the sales of class 7 buses during said month. Retail sales in cargo and passenger units registered a fall of 14.1 and 38% respectively.
Allgaier Group will invest US$11.6 million in new Puebla plant

After eight years of opening their first plant in Puebla, Allgaier Group, German maker of stamping tooling for car bodies will invest US$11.6 million in a second factory, which will generate 81 direct jobs and 100 indirect ones. The objective of this new project, which will be built in the municipality of Amozoc, is to attend the Volkswagen's new SUV model and Audi Mexico's Sport version of the Q5 SUV, according to what Ulrich Martin, CEO of the company, said.
Bimbo added 34 MAN tractor trucks to its fleet

Bimbo Group, one of the most important conglomerates in our country, evaluated several flat tractor trucks and after their good performance decided to strengthen their fleet with 34 new MAN TGX 26.480 units. "We're thankful for the trust Bimbo Group has given us by acquiring these units. We just finished giving their operators the preventive maintenance training so the units can always be on the road and to guarantee both the satisfaction with our product as well as the expected performance for their operation," commented Miguel Vallejo, commercial director of MAN Truck & Bus Mexico.
Digit Automotive invests US$40 million in San Luis Potosi

Chinese automotive company, Digit Automotive, made an investment of US$40 million, thus generating 80 direct jobs and will dedicate itself to produce metallic pieces for car seats. The company is located very near to the San Luis Potosi BMW plant. The inaugurated plant started operations in May 2019 and began construction in 2017 with an investment of US$40 million and expects to grow in the next few years by almost MXN$3 billion and to generate 200 direct jobs.
Nissan Mexico inaugurated new corporate offices in Mexico City

Under the brand's slogan "Innovation that Excites", Nissan inaugurated their new corporate building located in Mexico City. The design of the Nissan Mexico offices is inspired in the concepts of speed, movement and innovation and has a space of 16,900 square meters distributed in 11 levels to reach a height of 135 meters. Jose Roman, president and general director said: "We're very happy to be in our new home because we have prepared an environment that encourages collaboration and creativity that help generate innovative ideas through the lens of Nissan Intelligent Mobility."
Mexico will keep being attractive for automotive supplier investment: EY

In the midst of economic volatility, Mexico will keep being an attractive destination for automotive investments and before the ratification of the USMCA and other commercial agreements, it should approach China to strengthen the supply chain, recommended Randall Miller, global manufacturing leader of EY. He assured that Mexico will face great business opportunities for automotive manufacturing, maybe not from OEMs, but rather from the arrival of new suppliers because of the commercial war between the United States and China.
KIA Motors Mexico names Yangseop Ghil as new vice president

KIA Motors Mexico announced the incorporation of a new vice president for the South Korean brand in Mexico: Yangseob Ghil, who will be replacing Ike Kwon, assuming the charge starting November 11. Ike Kwon worked in KIA Motors Mexico since December 2018. On his part, Yangseob Ghil arrives to Mexico after representing the brand as president of KIA Iberia, Spain, where he worked for two years. Ghil has a 28 year track-record with KIA, occupying diverse charges in the European continent.
Volkswagen's Tiguan leads automotive production in Mexico

The Tiguan, produced in the brand's Puebla plant, led October manufacturing both in its segment as well as in all variants made in the more than 20 plants located within Mexico. With 187,826 produced units in the accumulated of the months of January to October, Volkswagen Tiguan led production in the Mexican light vehicle automotive industry.
