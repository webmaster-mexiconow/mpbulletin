Audi Mexico receives Top Employer award

Audi Mexico obtained the Top Employer 2020 award for the first time, this award is given by Top Employers Institute, organization which offers a certification program that allows an evaluation of organizations and to better their work conditions, accelerating the impact of Human Resources strategies for the enrichment of the workplace. Qualities such as trust, pride of belonging and cooperation come hand in hand with the certification obtained by Audi Group in San Jose Chiapa.

Heavy vehicles sales drop in January

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), informed that, during January wholesale sales of heavy and commercial vehicles reached a total of 1,494 units, which compared to the 2,120 registered in January of 2019 represents a fall of 29.5%. During said month, retail sales of heavy and commercial vehicles reached 2,388 units, which compared to the 2,491 sold ones during the same month of 2019, meant a decrease of 18.8%.

Automotive credit decreases 9.2% in 2019

The Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA) said that during 2019, financial companies registered 790,823 sold new vehicles, which represents 80,501 less units when compared to the annual period of 2019. Automotive financing for new vehicles closed with red numbers during 2019, by decreasing 9.2% when compared to 2018, which in turn represents the most drastic fall for the sector; in contrast, credit sales for used vehicles increased 14% when compared to last year.

Honda of Mexico sold 6,645 units in January

During January, Honda of Mexico sold a total of 6,645 units, thus maintaining sixth place in sales in the national market with a market participation share of 6.3%. According to data from the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA), during the first month of this year the Honda CR-V achieved sales for 2,095 units. As for the popular Honda HR-V, which is produced in the company's Celaya plant for several markets, it sold 1,545 units, keeping third place between the most sold vehicles in its segment in our country.

Mexico will surpass Germany in auto parts production toward 2021: INA

With the growth rate achieved by the auto parts industry and the boom that will come from the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA), Mexico is on the road to becoming the fourth producer of automotive components and parts in the world toward 2021 and surpassing Germany, as forecasted by the National Auto Parts Industry (INA). Alberto Bustamante Gonzalez, director of Foreign Trade and Standardization of INA, projected a positive landscape for the Mexican industry with the USMCA's entry into force and the goal of US$102 billion in production for this year, of which more than 90% will be focused in North America.

General Motors awarded Ternium with the Supplier Excellence Award

General Motors (GM) awarded Ternium with the Supplier Excellence Award in the company's Industrial Center in Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon. Ternium steel is present in every recently Mexico made GM model. The Supplier Excellence Award is the accolade given by the automotive company to its providers once they've shown excellence and commitment to the quality of their products as well as those who have high-quality standards in service and safety in their plants.

Mazda in Guanajuato reaches the production of one million units

Mazda reached the production of one million units produced in Guanajuato. "This achievement wouldn't have been possible without the clients that purchase vehicles made in this plant, as well as suppliers, government and collaborators. My deepest thanks to every person supporting us," declared Akira Marumoto, president of Mazda. The company, hailing from Japan is part of the 300 companies from this country installed in Guanajuato, adding to a community of more than 3,200 Japanese people and representing Guanajuato's main commercial partner.
