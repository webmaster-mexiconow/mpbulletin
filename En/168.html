Mexico-made Mercedes Benz A-Class to be unveiled at Paris Motor Show in October

Mercedes-Benz announced that the new A-Class sedan will be presented to the general public for the first time at the Paris Motor Show (October 4 to 14, 2018), just a few weeks before its official launch at the end of year. Production will start in Aguascalientes, Central Mexico, later this year at the COMPAS (Cooperation Manufacturing Plant Aguascalientes) joint-venture, a facility built in cooperation with the Renault-Nissan Alliance, where Nissan manufactures the Infiniti QX50 SUV. Mercedes-Benz already presented the A-Class L Sedan at this year's Auto China in Beijing in April. Semiconductor manufacturer Infineon readies US$40 million expansion in Tijuana

German semiconductor manufacturer Infineon Technologies AG will invest US$40 million over the next four years to expand its production capacity in the border city of Tijuana, Baja California, state authorities announced. The project was unveiled during a meeting held in Germany by a delegation led by Governor Francisco Vega de Lamadrid with executives of the company including Alfred Hoffmann, Joerg Spiegel and Jirko Lohse, corporate vice presidents of different areas. Infineon has been operating in Tijuana for 17 years under the name Rectificadores Internacionales S.A. de C.V. as a manufacturer of power management technology including integrated circuits, circuit devices, power systems and other components. More details here: bit.ly/2JPdSrH
Spain-based Bergé to handle logistics for Audi at Puebla plant

Madrid-based company Bergé Infraestructuras y Servicios Logisticos (BISL) announced it was awarded a contract for logistics activities at the Audi plant in the municipality of San Jose Chiapa, Puebla, in Central Mexico. This new award involves the management of all inbound processes of said facility, from the entrance of maritime containers and trailers into the plant to storage activities in the different buildings of the manufacturing complex. This new contract awarded to Bergé in Mexico, with the direct employment of approximately 70 people, adds to the logistic activities that the company has been developing since 2013 at the Volkswagen plant, also in Puebla, with about 130 employees. Sales of electrified vehicles in Mexico jump more than 50% during April

Last April, auto dealers in Mexico sold 1,382 electrified vehicles, a figure that represents a skyrocketing 50.9% jump compared to same month of 2017. The monthly figure comprises of 21 full-electric cars and 1,361 hybrid units, according to the latest data available from the Mexican Auto Industry Association (AMIA). From January through April, sales of electrified vehicles totaled 4,946 units, that's 68 full-electric and 4,878 hybrid cars. Such figures also represent a double-digit increase of 45.3% compared to the same four-month period of 2017. Almost half of sales of electrified vehicles in the country have taken place in Mexico City and the neighboring state of Mexico, with 35.5% and 14.4% of deliveries respectively. Presses manufacturer Schuler Group sets up in Queretaro its third service location in Mexico

Germany-based Schuler Group, the world's largest manufacturer of presses and other metal forming technologies, announced it has opened a third service location in Queretaro, central Mexico, as part of the strategy to expand its presence in North America. Mexico and Canada official still optimistic on NAFTA deal despite tariff tensions

Mexican and Canadian officials are optimistic they can reach a trilateral trade deal with the U.S. in the next several months, even amid sticking points on car production, an automatic expiration clause and President Donald Trump's threats to impose tariffs on foreign vehicles. The three partners will work to land an agreement before the U.S. midterm elections in early November, Mexican Economy Minister Ildefonso Guajardo said yesterday after a meeting with Canadian Minister of Foreign Affairs Chrystia Freeland in Mexico City. While some of the negotiations take the shape of meetings between just two nations, and the U.S. has expressed a preference for bilateral deals, Mexico and Canada remain committed to a trilateral agreement, they said.
General Motors cuts its full-year earnings forecast due to tariffs

General Motors lowered its earnings forecast for the year following a second-quarter profit decline due indirectly to the Trump administration's tariffs on steel and aluminum, CFO Chuck Stevens said during a conference call. Although the company sources more than 90% of its steel and most of its aluminum domestically, the tariffs are causing domestic suppliers to raise prices of the materials. The increased pricing represents a majority of an expected US$1 billion impact in added costs to GM's business operations this year, up from an earlier estimate of about US$500 million. The automaker said earnings before interest and taxes dropped 13% to US$3.2 billion compared with a year earlier. GM lowered its guidance on free cash flow for the year from the mid-US$5 billion range to US$4 billion.
Ford profits drop almost 50% due to disruption in pickup production

Ford's second-quarter earnings plunged 48% to US$ 1.1billion, hence the company lowered its 2018 earnings projections, citing a disruption in the production of its popular F-150 pickup truck and heavy losses in China during the quarter. The Dearborn, Michigan, automaker lowered its full-year forecast due to challenges overseas and said CEO Jim Hackett's global restructuring efforts could result in charges totaling US$11 billion over the next three to five years. The company downplayed Trump's tariffs on steel and aluminum, however, its CFO Bob Shanks acknowledged the books took a tariff hit in the quarter and expects a bigger impact later this year.
Nissan Leaf celebrates four years of presence in the Mexican market

Nissan announced that its electric vehicle LEAF completed this month four years since its launch in Mexico, with 480 units marketed to date, which has prevented the emission of more than 3,000 tons of CO2 to the atmosphere. The Japanese automaker said that the owners of Leaf cars in Mexico have traveled 17 million kilometers, which is equivalent to 33 trips from the earth to the moon. The charging infrastructure for Nissan LEAF has also been growing, which has led the company to operate the largest network in the country, including charging stations in its 230 Mexican dealerships and 50 additional recharging centers installed in public spaces of Mexico City, Guadalajara and other cities. Sergio Marchionne, the CEO who saved Fiat and Chrysler, dies at 66

Sergio Marchionne, the executive who turned Fiat and Chrysler around before combining the companies as a profitable business, died yesterday at 66. He was the CEO of Fiat when it bought Chrysler out of bankruptcy in 2009 following a US government bailout. The companies merged about four years ago. As the global auto industry was shaken by the financial crisis in the late 2000s, there was serious doubt that American icon Chrysler would survive. But under Marchionne's leadership, the company eventually emerged as a debt-free, profitable global automaker. In 2017, FCA sold 2 million vehicles in the United States, more than double its sales in the dark days of 2009. Globally, FCA sold 4.7 million cars last year.
