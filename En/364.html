Concamin and the government of Puebla work together to create automotive Innovation Center

The Mexican Chamber of Industry (Concamin) and the Municipal Government of Puebla signed a Collaboration Agreement which will allow for the creation of an Industrial Innovation Center for the automotive industry. This strategic alliance will allow for the design and the promotion of actions oriented toward increasing production in OEM companies and Tier 1, 2 and 3 companies, as well as micro, small and medium enterprises within the industrial sector. Another agreement was signed between Concamin, Mexico's National Technological University and the municipality of Puebla that anticipates the needed actions for the support of the Center's operation.

China sees investment potential in Mexico

The president of the Commerce and Technology Mexico-China Chamber, Aurea Amapola Grijalva, assured that the Asian country sees a potential to invest in Mexico, especially in the automotive, electric, photovoltaic, telecommunications, clean energies, mining, petroleum and gas industries; however, she said that the country has to take steps to attract more investment from China. "Lately we have welcomed companies that are mostly from the auto parts industry; the second rubric is electric industries; third place, the photovoltaic industry and green energy; in fourth, telecommunications and lastly, companies interested in mining, the petroleum sector and gas," she commented.

Carlos Santiago is the new general director of Planta Santiago

Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA) announced Carlos Santiago as the new general director of the production plant in Santiago Tianguistenco, State of Mexico. In his new position he will lead all the truck manufacturing operations and direct support functions from the plant. According to DTNA, Carlos is an Electric Engineer from the Polytechnic School of the University of Sao Paola and has a wide knowledge of the manufacturing processes of South America's and Europe's automotive industry.

Daimler Trucks Mexico chooses to maintain a close relationship with carriers

With the retail sale of 763 trucks last August, Daimler Trucks Mexico (Freightliner) accumulated, during eight consecutive months of 2020, the invoicing of 4,903 new class 4 to 8 vehicles and tractors. Daimler Trucks Mexico reported a contraction of 36% in sales compared to the same period of last year. In light of such scenario, Daimler Trucks Mexico implemented various marketing actions that, in essence, seek to offset such negative trend and, in turn, seek to maintain a close relationship with the carriers in the various segments where Freightliner trucks and tractors compete.

Toyota invests US$170 million to increase its capacity in Guanajuato

Toyota has decided to reinvest US$170 million in Mexico to increase the capacity of its Guanajuato plant, which started production of the Tacoma pickup in December 2019, to supply the North American region. The company has also added 500 additional jobs to the 1,000 initially projected for the complex. The Guanajuato complex, which currently has the capacity to produce 100,000 units per year, will assemble 138,000 Tacomas starting in January 2022, once the additional investment has been fully exercised.

JAC Sei7 Pro will arrive to Mexico during the year's last quarter

JAC, the brand assembled and marketed in Mexico by Giant Motors Latin America, confirmed that sales for their new SUV called "Sei7 Pro" will begin during the last quarter of the year. This new member of JAC's portfolio will integrate key elements of the brand's new product family DNA, in addition to equipping a complete interior with great ergonomics and comfort. The JAC Sei7 Pro engine will be a 1.5L direct injection turbo with DCT transmission, i.e. double clutch, features that will help it generate 165 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque.

Peugeot plans to launch several models for the Mexican market

The launch of the new Peugeot 2008 in Mexico was only the beginning of several launches that the French firm has scheduled for our country this season. The first will be Landtrek, the pickup truck with which Peugeot intends to strengthen its position in the work vehicle category. Mexico will be the first country to receive it. The next launch will be the Peugeot 208, one of the French firm's most anticipated models. Its new generation will arrive to our streets during the first half of 2021. Updates on the 3008 and 5008 models will also be seen during the first half of the year.
