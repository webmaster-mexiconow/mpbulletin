Light vehicle sales in Mexico drop 10.4% in April

The sale of light vehicles in Mexico in April of this year suffered a fall of 10.4% compared to the same month of last year, reported the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). In this sense, in April 2019, 98,105 new light vehicles were sold in Mexico, which, compared to 109,534 in the same month of last year, resulted in the aforementioned drop of 10.4%.
This is how India and Mexico are going to strengthen commercial ties

After the bilateral commercial relationship between Mexico and India reached US$10.2 billion in 2018, a growth of 240% in the last decade. In this sense, officials from both countries announced the launch of the India-Mexico Business Chamber, a private organization with which they seek to enhance the flow of goods and investments. "The result of our cooperation is satisfactory, but we still have space to grow," said Graciela Marquez, secretary of Economy.
Volkswagen's former employees are struggling to find jobs in other companies

Rey Garcia Avendaño, leader of the Independent Union of Workers of the Automotive Industry (Sitiavw) reported that, after leaving Volkswagen, and when seeking jobs in different companies, the former employees of the German automaker are discriminated against and hardly manage to get hired. The leader said that this is a historical phenomenon that doesn't happen that often. Nevertheless, he was questioned about whether the 1,003 workers who have just left the factory, through voluntary retirement, will join other companies within the automotive sector or if they'll enter a different industry.
Nissan plants receive PROFEPA'S certificates of clean industry

The Federal Attorney for Environmental Protection (PROFEPA) delivered three certificates of clean industry to managers of Nissan Mexicana. In this sense, the PROFEPA recognizes Nissan's compliance with current environmental regulations and the implementation of good operating and engineering practices. The Japanese automaker in Mexico received the tenth renewal of the Aguascalientes plant certificate and the second renewal of the Aguascalientes A2 plant due to multiple factors, such as the use of 65% clean energy in the A1 plant.
CLAUT Edomex signs agreement with educational institutions for the automotive sector

The State of Mexico's Automotive Cluster (CLAUT EdoMex), headed by Maria Elisa Crespo Ferrer and the Automotive Education Node, represented by Joaquin Labastida Diaz, general director of the Technological Superior Studies of Santiago Tianguistenco (TEST), signed a specific collaboration agreement with the objective of carrying out actions in favor of higher education. The other academic entities belonging to the node are the Technological Superior Studies of Coacalco, the Polytechnic University of the Valley of Mexico, among others.
Vitro reports US$79 million of EBITDA in the first quarter of 2019

The Monterrey-based glass producer, Vitro, reported that in the first quarter of 2019 it had an operating flow (EBITDA) of US$79 million, 22.9% lower than that registered in the same period of last year. In its financial report sent to the Mexican Stock Exchange (BMV), the company highlighted that this was due to a decrease in the flow of the flat glass business by 27.8% due to a higher operating cost. This was also combined in a reduction of 10.4% in its packaging business and a rearrangement in the volume of sales of automotive glass.
The new Volvo S60 will arrive in Mexico this August

From its first generation, the Volvo S60 has distinguished itself by being a classy sedan with coupe-like influences and an ample interior space; in addition, for being a driver-centered car with a sporty driving style backed by its safety features. The all-new Volvo S60 will be produced in South Carolina, United States and, the version that will be available for its purchase in Mexico will be an Ignite T5 version with 254 hp and a torque of 258 lb-ft with AWD traction.
MAN Truck & Bus Mexico grows 48% during the first quarter of 2019

During the first quarter of the year, MAN Truck & Bus Mexico reported a 48% growth in sales in the cargo vehicle segment due to the demand of the Delivery family trucks (VW models 6.160, 9.170 and 11.180), equipped with Cummins engine Euro V classes 4, 5 and 6. Likewise, the sales performance of the Workline segment 15.190 and 17.230, Constellation 17.280 and 24.280 classes 7 and 8, as well as the TGX 26.480 tractor unit were praised as well.
Companies within the sector are going for eco-friendly technology: ANPACT

Based on the work of more than ten years between representatives of the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources (SEMARNAT) and specialists of the 14 companies affiliated to the National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), progress towards the implementation of cleaner technologies for the manufacture of engines and heavy vehicles is being made. Because of this, Miguel Heberto Elizalde Lizarraga, president of ANPACT, assured that companies in the sector are ready to move towards the implementation of new technologies that favor the environment.
KIA Motors Mexico grows 9.7% in March 2019

After obtaining the best March sales in its history in the country, KIA Motors Mexico closed the first quarter of 2019 in a leading position within the industry with high export levels and a strong support for its social programs. The Korean automaker sold 8,404 units in March, the second best monthly figure since it started operating in the country. This figure represented a surprising growth of 9.7% compared to the same month of 2018.
