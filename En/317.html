April 7th, 2020
Kia plant in Pesqueria will suspend operations

Kia Motors Mexico informed that it will temporarily stop vehicle production in its Pesqueria, Nuevo Leon plant starting on April 6, following the federal government's recommendations, due to the health emergency declared to avoid the spread of coronavirus. They don't yet have a date in which they might go back into production, and this will be subject to the federal government's instructions. During the time in which operations are suspended, the plant and its installations will receive maintenance and deep cleaning.

Honda is extending the suspension of operations in its North American plants

Honda Motor Co informed that it's extending the suspension of all production in its automotive plants in the United States and Canada until April 10, while in Mexico it will do so until April 13, in response to coronavirus. Honda of Mexico detailed in a press release that that this is adding on to the temporal halt of operations of its Celaya, Guanajuato plant. As for Honda El Salto, in the state of Jalisco, the company informed that productive operations will halt from April 4th to the 13th.

For more information click here: https://bit.ly/2UNEy4W
Car sales will see their worst year this 2020: AMDA

Mexico will live the worst year in history for car sales due to the COVID-19 pandemic, since it's expecting a general contraction of 26% without yet having a support plan from the federal government, said the deputy director general of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), Guillermo Rosales Zarate. In March, the decrease was of 50% and the levels for April are expected to be below 90%, which is why he urged to take actions that will impede the consequences of these drops to be even more "painful."

For more information click here: https://bit.ly/3dVVU7r
ANPACT asks for heavy vehicle sector to be kept in operation

The National Association of Producers of Buses, Trucks and Tractor Trucks (ANPACT), asked the federal government to specify in a legal document that the manufacturing and importation of heavy vehicles and auto parts, as well as the sale and registration of the aforementioned, to be considered as an essential sector and to allow the industry to keep working and as such meet the delivery of orders with clients that are attending to the national and international COVID-19 emergency. Even when federal authorities have published two decrees on essential sectors, "there are still ambiguities," which causes uncertainty in businessmen before the urgency of delivering required units or parts to their clients.

For more information click here: https://bit.ly/3aO1GWp
JAC is celebrating its third anniversary in Mexican soil

JAC, the automotive brand sold and assembled by Giant Motors Latin America, is celebrating its third year of presence in Mexico accumulating more than 1,000 direct and indirect collaborators; 31 JAC Stores in the country with 19 distributor groups; 10,000 assembled units; 11 commercialized vehicles, of which 8 have a combustion engine and 3 are totally electric and all can be quoted and sold through the brand's webpage jac.mx.

Kia commercializes 6,995 units in March

Kia Motors Mexico informed that it experienced a light decrease in its March results, reaching 6,995 sold units, number with which the South Korean brand maintains the 5th place in sales, reaching 7.9% in market penetration. The most sold car of the automotive company in Mexico was the proudly made in Mexico Kia Rio, which in its HB and sedan versions added 3,050 sold units in March. In second place came the new Kia Seltos, with 1,300 sold units.

For more information click here: https://bit.ly/2RiaZpZ
Volvo Buses stops production in the State of Mexico due to COVID-19

All activities related to the production of vehicles destined for the massive transport of people, which are carried out in a plant in Tultitlán, State of Mexico, were suspended by Volvo Buses, in adherence to the health emergency declared by the federal government's General Health Council. There's not any information available at which time operations will restart at the plant. Said measure is meant to add to the actions to contribute in reducing the spread of the pandemic generated by the coronavirus COVID-19.
