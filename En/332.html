Nissan Mexicana will gradually go back into operations

In Nissan, the workers' safety is always the highest priority. Starting on March 17, there's been an implementation of a series of proactive measures to prevent the spread of the coronavirus COVID-19, including the temporal suspension of production in the installations beginning on March 25. Nissan announced that it's preparing to gradually restart its operations under strict safety measures to protect the health and well-being of its workers. Nissan has already met all the protocols required to restart operations and will proceed according to what authorities indicate.

The Mexican automotive industry asks to allow the reopening of the sector in Puebla

The Mexican automotive industry, represented through the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA), the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA), the Mexican Association of Automotive Parts Manufacturers (INA), the Automotive Cluster Center Zone (Clauz), as well as the Mexican Confederation of Industrial Chambers (Concamin) made a call to the government of Puebla, headed by Luis Miguel G. Barbosa Huera, to together, find solutions and collaborate to urgently reactivate the state's economy with the goal of promoting the development and social well-being of the state's inhabitants in this unprecedented situation.

States in the Bajio are carrying out joint work with Michigan's government

For the states in the Bajio, the automotive sector represents one of the cornerstones of the manufacturing and exports industry; which is why these states are jointly working on the reactivation of this industry, expected for June 1st. The close relationship present in the supply chain between Mexico's and the United States' automotive industry, will be a guiding principle for the reactivation of this sector. Governors from the Bajio region are carrying out joint work with the government of Michigan, United States, in the face of the supply dependence and the plans to restart operations.

Toyota is preparing to restart operations in the country

Toyota said that it's preparing for the gradual restart of operations and ordering its operations in the states of Baja California and Guanajuato, applying protocols and health safety measures to prevent its workers from being infected with the coronavirus COVID-19. "This stage contemplates the implementations of protocols and health safety measures, as well as training activities and production tests that allow us to adapt to the new standards," explained the company, without giving any dates.

Kia returns to gradual operations in Pesqueria

After two months in which Kia Motors Mexico suspended vehicle production in its Pesqueria plant, and after meeting all the requirements for the protocols and guidelines established by the federal government, the South Korean company is beginning operations in a partial manner. It is worth mentioning, that for the return of the new essential industries such as the automotive, mining and construction, the Mexican Social Security Institute (IMSS) will have to validate the Active Health Safety Protocol.

Audi Mexico helps the health sector combat the pandemic

Audi Mexico is helping the health sector of the state of Puebla with various activities to help combat the COVID-19 pandemic. On top of donating surgical material like masks, gloves, hats, boots and other inputs, the company is also using its technological equipment to produce face masks that will be used by the plant's workers. The delivery of said inputs to health institutions began during May and will continue until July. Audi Mexico designed a project to produce, in collaboration with two local suppliers, 40,000 washable face masks that will be donated to the residents of the municipalities neighboring the plant.

Puebla's automotive industry is expected to return on June 15

The state government confirmed that the state's economic reactivation will begin on June 15 in a gradual manner with those activities considered as essential and some non-essential ones, applying health safety measures to suppress the increase in COVID-19 cases. The state's minister of Economy, Olivia Salomon Vibaldo, explained that in the case of the automotive and construction industry they will ask for them to restart operations with only 30% of the workforce in staggered turns to prevent agglomerations.
