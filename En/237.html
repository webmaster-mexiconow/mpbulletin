ANPACT agrees with the inclusion of transport and mobility in the National Development Plan 2019-2024

The National Association of Bus, Truck and Tractor Truck Producers (ANPACT) considers a step in the right direction that the transportation of cargo and passengers are part of the National Development Plan (PND) 2019-2024. Ever since 2000, ANPACT has fought to have the motor transport sector included in the PND. The inclusion of this important industry of the country's economy and its consideration in the objectives of the fundamental axes for development are steps in the right direction.
Toyota exceeds monthly sales goal in April

Toyota Motor Sales Mexico has exceeded its monthly sales goal by having sold 7,245 vehicles throughout the country, which allowed the company to remain in fourth place within the Mexican automotive market. The Hilux model sold 1,623 units, Yaris Sedan sold 1,252, Sienna sold 677, Hiace sold 597, and RAV4 sold 406 units. When it comes to the sale of vehicles with hybrid technology, 647 units were sold.
Audi announces the manufacture of the first hybrid premium car produced in Mexico

Audi Mexico's Smart Factory located in San Jose Chiapa, Puebla, is advancing at full speed with the production of the Audi Q5. Since the beginning of its production, the company has reached the figure of 400,000 manufactured units. And now, Audi Mexico has announced the manufacture of the first premium hybrid car in Mexico, which will be exported to North America and Europe.
Nissan Altima, one of the best vehicles for 2019

The new Nissan Altima was recently recognized as one of the "12 Best New Cars for 2019" according to the automotive website Autotrader.com. This renovated sedan stood out from the rest as it is an attractive vehicle for clients seeking sophistication, expressiveness, and sportsmanship.
Industries in Queretaro are affected by delays in border crossings

The automotive and aerospace industries are among the industries in the state with the highest degree of affectation in the face of delays in the border crossings of the United States. The president of the Commission of Foreign Trade of the Queretaro business center of the Employers' Confederation of the Mexican Republic (Coparmex), Sergio Nava, explained that, in general, all companies that have export operations to the neighboring country have been affected to some degree.
Nemak to produce electrical components in Nuevo Leon

The high-tech aluminum automotive components company began, on its campus in Garcia, Nuevo Leon, the construction of a plant that will produce and assemble just over 200 parts of batteries for fully electric cars. The pieces will be integrated into something known as "battery housings", which will be manufactured right there with a very special aluminum alloy and of the highest resistance, explained Armando Tamez Martinez, general manager of Nemak.
Luz Elena Jurado, the new managing director of Volvo Trucks and Mack Trucks Mexico

Luz Elena Jurado was appointed as the new managing director of Volvo Trucks and Mack Trucks in Mexico so as to replace Martin Saenz, Volvo Trucks reported in a press release. The new managing director, who held the position of managing director of Volvo Financial Services in Mexico since 2010, "will be part of the management team of Volvo Trucks North America and will report to Peter Voorhoeve, president of Volvo Trucks North America," the company explained.
