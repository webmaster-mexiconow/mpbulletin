Flex invests US$14 million in Ciudad Juarez

The company Flex, expert in battery harnesses, decided to expand one of its plants in Ciudad Juarez, Chihuahua. This US$14 million investment will generate 1,000 jobs. Flex commented that the expansion will be of 5,574 square meters and the creation of a validation laboratory. Additionally, the laboratory will allow the company to validate the harnesses' design and manufacturing work, process which was previously carried out in the plants located in the United States. On top of that, a master's degree between Flex and the Technological Institute of Ciudad Juarez (ITCJ) was consolidated for the collaborators of the manufacturing company.

JAC Sei4, the first one to receive the Black Series treatment in Mexico

JAC, which is assembled and commercialized in Mexico by Giant Motors Latin America, announced the arrival and start of sales of the 2021 JAC Sei4 Black Series. The brand's first special edition is limited to 8 units, and will be available online through www.jac.mx at a price of MXN$419,000 which includes 2 years of free insurance on top of the benefits of being part of the "Black Series" club. The JAC Sei4 Black Series' engine, shared with the Active and Connect versions, is a 1.5 turbo charged one with a CVT transmission, which generates 145 horsepower and 154 lb-ft of torque.

Marcopolo presented the 2020 Senior, the first midibus made 100% in Mexico

The first midibus made 100% in Mexico by Marcopolo, "2020 Senior," is "more robust, safe and efficient," due to the fact that it integrates high resistance steel, and was presented to directives and executives from transport companies, which gathered at the firm's plant in Nuevo Leon. Paulo Andrade, general director of Marcopolo Mexico, informed that the 2020 Senior's bodywork (with 9.5 meters of length, 3.2 meters of height and 2.4 meters of width, and a capacity of 31 seats and two doors or 33 seats with one door) can adapt itself to any chassis developed for the nine ton passenger vehicle segment in Mexico.

Vehicle imports decrease 57.3% in May

During May of 2020, 6,019 used vehicles entered the country legally, which meant a decrease of 57.3% when compared to the same month of last year when 14,099 units entered the country, according to numbers from the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). According to Guillermo Rosales, AMDA's deputy general director, this fall was a result of the closure of the border as a sanitary measure from the American government to stop the spread of the pandemic generated by COVID-19; another factor was the devaluation of the peso before the dollar.

The USMCA is both a challenge and an opportunity for Queretaro's automotive industry

The state's automotive industry, which has an auto parts vocation, sees both challenges and opportunities in the implementation of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA). The president of the Automotive Cluster of Queretaro, Renato Villaseñor Mendoza, said that the new treaty will imply the challenge of gradually increasing the level of regional content during the next four years, until it reaches 75%. He added that the challenge of meeting the new origin rules could be accompanied by the attraction of foreign investment projects that may opt to install themselves in the entity.
