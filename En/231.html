Claudio Ariel de la Peña, the new Sales director of Daimler Trucks Mexico

As of April 15th, Claudio Ariel de la Peña Sierra will be the Sales director of Daimler Trucks Mexico. He will be replacing Luis Vega, who was recently named Sales and International Service director for Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA). Claudio obtained an engineering degree at the Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education and has had a long career at Daimler, where he has held various positions since 1997. His achievements include 36% growth in sales in the central and southern region of the country during 2018, which contributed to consolidate the company's leadership in Michoacan, Veracruz, Colima, Puebla, Yucatan, Tabasco and Chiapas.
Nissan boosts electric mobility across Latin America

Nissan, the world leader in sales of electric vehicles with over 70 years of Japanese heritage in this area, ever since the launch of the Tama model in 1947, continues to detonate electrification across Latin America. In 2018, Nissan commissioned the study "The Future of Mobility in Latin America", carried out by the think tank C230. It was observed in the study that said region has opportunities to accelerate the adoption of electric vehicles (EVs), as 80% of the respondents were willing to acquire an EV and 75% considered that the cost of recharging is less than the cost of purchasing conventional fuels.
The American automotive industry warns Donald Trump about closing the border with Mexico

The main automobile manufacturers in the United States warned that the closure of the border with Mexico, as threatened by President Donald Trump, will cause serious problems for the sector. Auto Alliance, which represents some of the world's leading carmakers and produces 70% of the vehicles sold in the United States, explained that said measure should be avoided. The group, which includes companies such as BMW, Fiat Chrysler (FCA), Ford, General Motors (GM), Mercedes-Benz, Toyota, the Volkswagen group and Volvo, reiterated that US factories depend on parts and components manufactured in Mexico in order to produce vehicles.
Sales of light vehicles decreased 1.5% in March

The sales of light vehicles in Mexico in March of this year were of 117,122 units, and when compared to the 118,907 sold vehicles in the same month last year, there's a clear decrease of 1.5%, reported the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). According to the INEGI's report, 332,013 vehicles were sold from January to March of this year, which represented a decrease of 1.7% when compared to the same period in 2018, when 337,869 vehicles were commercialized. In the third month of 2019, Nissan ranked as the brand with the most sales within the domestic market, with a total of 25,434 sold units; followed by General Motors, which delivered 18,288 vehicles; and Volkswagen, which sold 11,533 units.
Mexican banks offer more attractive loans than automotive brands

Automotive brands have weakened the loans they grant, which has resulted in banks and auto-finance companies "accelerating" as an option to boost the purchase of vehicles, said Guillermo Rosales, deputy general director of the Mexican Association of Automotive Distributors (AMDA). Information from Jato Dynamics shows that, in January of 2019, automotive brands reduced their volume of credits by 12.2% compared to the first month of the year before. In contrast, banks and financing companies rose by 9.6% and 1.5%, respectively. Rosales said that the schemes that banks and financing companies are able to offer are more attractive than those of the automotive brands.
Hyundai Motor Mexico talks about the NHTSA investigation

In response to the announcement that the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) decided to open new investigations on Hyundai vehicles, the offices of Hyundai Motor Mexico (HMM) have reported the following: the research carried out by the NHTSA covers 3 million vehicles in total, including Hyundai and KIA models marketed in the United States. In this sense, no Hyundai vehicles distributed in Mexico include this revision. The investigated vehicles in question are the Santa Fe and Sonta models in their 2011 through 2014 versions; however, these versions were not commercialized in Mexico.
Joyson Safety Systems relocates to Nuevo Leon

Due to the labor dispute that took place in Matamoros, Tamaulipas, the manufacturer of safety systems for the automotive industry, Joyson Safety Systems, decided to reinstate its operations in Nuevo Leon, informed Roberto Mattus Rivera, general director of the National Council of the Maquiladora and Export Manufacturing Industry (Index) Matamoros to El Economista. Adalberto Núñez Ramos, undersecretary of Labor of Nuevo Leon, confirmed in an interview that the Chinese manufacturer of steering wheels and safety belts will be installed in the municipality of Guadalupe. Joyson Safety Systems was the first maquiladora that announced its exit from Matamoros in February, after liquidating 550 workers.
Exco Technologies invests US $ 25 million in Queretaro

Exco Technologies Limited announced an investment of US$25 million as part of its expansion in Queretaro, which will represent the generation of 100 highly specialized jobs. The Canadian firm specializes in the design, construction and development of molds for the automotive industry. The confirmation of the project was made around the tour that a delegation of state authorities carried out in Canada. During the mission, the governor, Francisco Dominguez Servien, participated in the Seminar of the Automotive Industry by AA, where he promoted Queretaro as a destination of opportunities.
The Mexican automotive sector is in debt to the domestic market

The debt of the national automotive industry is with the domestic market, as it has not managed to reverse the downward trend, said Eduardo Solis, president of the Mexican Association of the Automotive Industry (AMIA). The director warned of the need for incentive and financing strategies to attack the problem. "Our outstanding debt is with the domestic consumption and we have to focus our agenda on the domestic market. The forecast of production, sales to the public and export of the industry will be announced next month, but the domestic market is falling and we have to do something when it comes to incentives and financing," Solis stressed.
Mazda and Mercedes-Benz are the automotive companies that satisfy the Mexican customer the most

Mazda and Mercedes-Benz were the brands with the highest ranking of satisfaction of the Mexican customer throughout the purchase of cars, where the delivery of the unit was the most important factor in the sale process, explained Gerardo Gomez, director of J.D. Power. When presenting the Customer Satisfaction Study of J.D. Power in Mexico 2019, Gomez said that, for the second consecutive year, the Japanese brand was placed at the top, with a score of 881 out of 1,000 points, as to how happy the customer was throughout the purchase of a vehicle. While Mercedes-Benz, in the luxury segment, reached a score of 888, followed by BMW with 881 points.
